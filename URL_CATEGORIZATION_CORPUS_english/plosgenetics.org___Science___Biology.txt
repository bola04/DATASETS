        <h2 class="title">
         <a alt="A component of the TOR (Target Of Rapamycin) nutrient-sensing pathway plays a role in circadian rhythmicity in Neurospora crassa" href="/plosgenetics/article?id=10.1371/journal.pgen.1007457">
          A component of the TOR (Target Of Rapamycin) nutrient-sensing pathway plays a role in circadian rhythmicity in
          <i>
           Neurospora crassa
          </i>
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="blurb">
         <p>
          Using the fungus
          <i>
           Neurospora crassa
          </i>
          as a model organism, Lalanthi Ratnayake and colleagues discover a gene that is important for maintaining rhythms that continue without the known feedback loop. They further find that this gene codes for a protein that was already known to be important in helping cells to adjust their growth rate to adapt to varying availability of nutrients.
         </p>

        <h2 class="title">
         <a alt="A conserved role for Syntaxin-1 in pre- and post-commissural midline axonal guidance in fly, chick, and mouse" href="/plosgenetics/article?id=10.1371/journal.pgen.1007432">
          A conserved role for Syntaxin-1 in pre- and post-commissural midline axonal guidance in fly, chick, and mouse
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="blurb">
         <p>
          Syntaxin-1 is a core factor in tethering synaptic vesicles and mediating their fusion to the cell membrane at the synapse. Thus, Syntaxin-1 mediates neurotransmission in the adult nervous system. Here, Ros, Barrecheguren, Cotrufo et al show that Syntaxin-1 is also involved in axonal guidance in the CNS of vertebrates and invertebrates during nervous system development.
         </p>
        </div>

        <h2 class="title">
         <a alt="Opposing roles of microRNA argonautes during Caenorhabditis elegans aging" href="/plosgenetics/article?id=10.1371/journal.pgen.1007379">
          Opposing roles of microRNA argonautes during
          <i>
           Caenorhabditis elegans
          </i>
          aging
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="blurb">
         <p>
          In
          <i>
           C. elegans
          </i>
          nematodes, two highly related Argonautes (ALG-1 and ALG-2; Argonaute-Like Genes) play largely overlapping roles in the miRNA pathway during development. In this study, Antti P. Aalto, Ian A. Nicastro and colleagues report that the activities of these two Argonautes diverge in aging animals, as loss of ALG-1 shortens lifespan, while loss of ALG-2 extends it.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>

        Image credit: Antti P. Aalto, Ian A. Nicastro and colleagues

       </a>

         <h2 class="title">

           An intronic enhancer of
           <i>
            Bmp6
           </i>
           underlies evolved tooth gain in sticklebacks
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="blurb">
          <p>
           Understanding how traits evolve in nature remains a fundamental goal in biology. Threespine stickleback fish offer a powerful system to address this question. Using genetic and genomic data, Phillip A. Cleves and colleagues map a genomic region regulating evolved tooth gain to an intronic region of
           <i>
            Bone Morphogenetic Protein 6 (Bmp6).
           </i>
          </p>
