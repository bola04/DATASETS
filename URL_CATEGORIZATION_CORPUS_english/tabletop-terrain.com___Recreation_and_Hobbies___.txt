               </a>
               Last week saw me speed painting a Monster Truck for my Warden team, because it was the final race of the first
               <a href="https://ospreypublishing.com/gaslands" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/ospreypublishing.com/gaslands']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Gaslands
               </a>
               Circuit Out West at my
               <a href="http://tcownz.blogspot.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/tcownz.blogspot.com/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                local gaming club
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               We had eight players set up on two tables and it was absolute mayhem. One of the club founders Greg put a nice write up on
               <a href="http://tcownz.blogspot.com/2018/06/racers-start-your-engines.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/tcownz.blogspot.com/2018/06/racers-start-your-engines.html']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                the TCOW blog
               </a>
               with plenty of action photos.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsTrophy.png" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/download/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsTrophy.png']);">
                <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2960" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 184px) 100vw, 184px" src="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsTrophy-184x300.png" srcset="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsTrophy-184x300.png 184w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsTrophy.png 600w" width="184"/>
               </a>
               The Warden monster truck is based on the
               <a href="https://play.hotwheels.com/en-us/shop/productdetail?id=10001373" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/play.hotwheels.com/en-us/shop/productdetail?id=10001373']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Grave Digger Hot Wheel
               </a>
               and is probably the last vehicle I’ll add to this team. It came apart really easily as it’s only held together with a single rivet. Extra detailing was added with old 3D printer rafts (the window mesh), plasticard (the giant ram) and a handy
               <a href="http://www.greenstuffworld.com/en/inicio/348-2x-rivets-rubber-molds.html?search_query=rivet+mold&amp;results=3" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.greenstuffworld.com/en/inicio/348-2x-rivets-rubber-molds.html?search_query=rivet+mold&amp;results=3']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                rivet making set
               </a>
               I ordered from
               <a href="http://www.greenstuffworld.com/en/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.greenstuffworld.com/en/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Green Stuff World
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               I also put together a trophy for the Gaslands circuit, which is largely based on this
               <a href="https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2055033" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.thingiverse.com/thing:2055033']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">

               </a>

               <a href="http://tcownz.blogspot.com/2018/06/racers-start-your-engines.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/tcownz.blogspot.com/2018/06/racers-start-your-engines.html']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                TCOW write up
               </a>
               .
              </p>

               I’m busy planning up a second GCOW three race circuit as well, as there’s a few stock scenarios we haven’t tried yet. I’m also trying to come up with at least one original scenario idea, as well as tweak Death Race a little.
              </p>

             <div class="entry-content">
              <p>
               <a href="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2.png" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/download/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2.png']);" target="_blank">
                <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2940" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2-300x300.png" srcset="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2-300x300.png 300w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2-150x150.png 150w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2-768x765.png 768w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2-60x60.png 60w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2-50x50.png 50w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/gaslandsVehicleTemplate2.png 800w" width="300"/>
               </a>
               The three race
               <a href="https://gaslands.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/gaslands.com/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Gaslands
               </a>
               circuit I’m running at my
               <a href="http://tcownz.blogspot.com/2018/06/zombie-drivers.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/tcownz.blogspot.com/2018/06/zombie-drivers.html']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                local gaming club
               </a>
               had another round last weekend, and I tried out these new vehicle template I designed and printed beforehand.
              </p>
              <p>
               They were inspired by other Gaslands dice templates available on
               <a href="http://www.thingiverse.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.thingiverse.com/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Thingiverse
               </a>
               , but I wanted a much more compact design that I could combine with the standard vehicle templates you can download from
               <a href="http://www.gaslands.com" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.gaslands.com']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Gaslands.com
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               My original design is
               <a href="https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2931432" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.thingiverse.com/thing:2931432']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                available on Thingiverse here
               </a>
               and will print very quickly on any reasonable 3D printer with a 0.4mm nozzle. It’s designed to be used with the good old Games Workshop red and black dice they used to sell in tins. I have always had terrible luck while rolling these dice in games, so they’ve been re-purposed as tracking dice. You also need at least standard D10 dice to track hull points. You can cross off the hull points on the paper sheet of course, but since Gaslands has a respawn mechanism the D10 is meant to replace doing that.
              </p>
              <p>
               In play I found them pretty useful as they don’t take up a lot of space on the table and combine all the vehicle stats, activation, current gear, and hazard tracking, as well has let you count the ammo for up to four weapon systems.
              </p>

               </a>

               <a href="https://ospreypublishing.com/store/osprey-games/osprey-wargames" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/ospreypublishing.com/store/osprey-games/osprey-wargames']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">

               </a>
               series.
               <a href="https://gaslands.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/gaslands.com/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">

               </a>
               is a game of vehicular combat, designed primarily to be used with
               <a href="https://hotwheels.mattel.com/shop" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/hotwheels.mattel.com/shop']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">

                Mattel
               </a>
               .
              </p>

               I plan to review Gaslands once I have a few more games under my belt – but for the time being I’ve dusted off my collection of Hot Wheels cars from the
               <a href="/archives/2015/04/02/1838/outrider-customised-hot-wheels/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                last game of vehicular combat
               </a>
               I was playing.
              </p>
              <p>
               Gaslands is more of a team based game, so I’ve customised a few more Hot Wheels from the collection to create my first team. Fifty points is the recommended starting team in Gaslands, so that’s what these guys are. Three middleweight cars, one armed with a three shot rocket launcher, one armed with a ram and rear facing machine guns, and one armed with forward facing machine guns and a rear oil sprayer.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/hotWheelsPrisonCarsRear.png" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/download/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/hotWheelsPrisonCarsRear.png']);">

This team is a Warden team, meaning the drivers are all felons. They’re driving prison cars, so the doors are sealed, windows barred and they enter and leave the car via the locked roof hatch.

               <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broad_arrow" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broad_arrow']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">

               </a>
               – as they’re easier to paint than the more common striped uniforms. After some mistakes with Army Painter ‘Fire Lizard’ orange, I found Citadel’s ‘Fire Dragon Bright’ (inspired paint names) covers a lot better. These cars have been stripped, primed, base coated Fire Dragon Bright, ink washed, highlighted with the base coat, sponged with grey and varnished. The metals are just Vallejo Gunmetal Grey with Silver highlights.
              </p>
              <p>
               I’m looking forward to seeing how they fare in a Gaslands death race!
              </p>

                <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2886" height="181" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/nukaColaTankPainted-300x181.png" srcset="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/nukaColaTankPainted-300x181.png 300w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/nukaColaTankPainted-768x464.png 768w, http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/nukaColaTankPainted.png 1024w" width="300"/>

               I’m continuing work on my post apocalyptic table for
               <a href="http://worldsendpublishing.com/this-is-not-a-test/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/worldsendpublishing.com/this-is-not-a-test/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                This is Not a Test
               </a>
               . I’m trying to improve the vertical possibilities of the table by adding some height and climbing surfaces to game over. TnT’s rules are fairly brutal, requiring a lot of game time to make unaided climbs – so I’m also printing and painting lots of simple ladders to make getting vertical easier.
              </p>
              <p>
               I’ve created a set of 3D printed platforms, that can be printed with legs or with angle brackets. Both variations are shown in the photos. The platforms with legs are the right height to rest against the large number of shipping containers I’ve already assembled and painted. They’re the diving board looking platforms on the right of the photo. The platforms without legs have 45 degree pieces that help them to rest on free standing terrain, and they’re shown on the left between the tanks.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="http://www.tabletop-terrain.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/nukaColaTankPainted2.png" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/download/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/nukaColaTankPainted2.png']);">

               </a>
               The platforms work with a clip system that involves a some work with a needle file to get a good fit. I deliberately left them a little tight so there’s a friction fit between the pieces before I glued them. The design was inspired by some similar pieces I found on
               <a href="https://www.thingiverse.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.thingiverse.com/']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Thingiverse
               </a>
               , that were bit lacking in surface design for my tastes, and somewhat tricky to print on the Anet A8. My designs are simplified for ease of printing, and only require light rafting for the joining pieces. They’re also available on
               <a href="https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:2764321" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.thingiverse.com/thing:2764321']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Thingiverse here
               </a>
               .

              <p>
               The tanks and some of the other terrain is constructed from very old Urban War ‘Hexagon’ kits. These are still available from a
               <a href="http://www.urbanwar.nl/store.php?sitemap_id=62&amp;subprodcat=15" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackPageview', '/out/www.urbanwar.nl/store.php?sitemap_id=62&amp;subprodcat=15']);" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                few places online
