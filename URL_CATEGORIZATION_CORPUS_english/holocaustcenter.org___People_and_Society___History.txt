            <div class="sectionA">
             <div class="gutter">
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
                <strong>
                 Michigan Summer Seminar
                 <br/>
                 on Holocaust Education:
                </strong>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
                <strong>
                 <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
                  An Interdiscplinary Inquiry
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: 18pt;">
                  on Teaching the Holocaust
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </strong>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
                <strong>
                 <em>
                  Monday, July 9 through Saturday, July 14
                 </em>
                 <br/>
                 <img alt="Michigan Remember" height="225" src="https://www.holocaustcenter.org/image/rock.jpg" style="margin: 5px 10px; border: 1px solid black;" width="400"/>
                </strong>
               </span>
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="font-size: 10pt;">
                <strong>
                 <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                  Studying, Learning &amp; Teaching:
                  <br/>
                  Critical Lessons in Holocaust Education
                 </span>
                </strong>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               The Michigan Summer Seminar on Holocaust Education will ask teachers to assume the dual role of student and teacher during the week-long seminar. Participants will deepen Holocaust and genocide content knowledge, while also exploring and sharing teaching strategies that meet the 2016 Michigan mandate on genocide and Holocaust education and have long-lasting impacton students.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <strong>
                The program offers the following areas of focus and experience:
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               - Testimony from Holocaust survivors
               <br/>
               - Presentations by content specialists on a comprehensive range of topics
               <br/>
               - Work with primary documents and teaching resource materials
               <br/>
               - Immersion into the pedagogy of genocide, antisemitism, and social justice
               <br/>
               - Use of media and literacy tools and skills in presenting historical fact, context, and relevance
               <br/>
               - Sessions held at a world-class Holocaust museum
               <br/>
               - Professional collaboration with colleagues from across Michigan
               <br/>
               - Opportunity for inquiry, instructional design, connection, and reflection
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Held at the Holocaust Memorial Center Zekelman Family Campus in Farmington Hills
               </li>

              <h1 style="text-align: center;">
               The True Wartime Escape
               <br/>
               of Margret &amp; H.A. Rey
              </h1>
              <h4 style="text-align: center;">
               On view April 11 through July 5, 2018
              </h4>
              <p>
               <img alt="Margret and H.A. Rey" height="162" src="https://www.holocaustcenter.org/image/Reys-1.jpg" style="margin: 5px 10px; border: 1px solid black; float: left;" width="225"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Curious George was able to go off on his adventures thanks to the fascinating, harrowing and largely unknown wartime escape from the Nazis by German-born Jews, Margret and H.A. Rey, who created Curious George.
              </p>
              <p>
               The Curious George books are beloved by children of all ages around the globe with George’s stories translated into many languages, including Afrikaans, Chinese, Danish, French, German, Japanese, Portuguese and Swedish.
              </p>
              <p>
               Author Louise Borden, an award-winning children’s book author, heard about the Reys’ escape from the Nazi invasion and “just like the Reys’ famous little monkey,” Borden was curious. She was “intrigued by the story of Margret and H.A. Rey’s flight from Paris on bicycle in June 1940.”
              </p>
              <p>
               Over the course of several years, Borden spoke, wrote and emailed with people in England, France, Germany and Portugal who had known Margret and H.A. Rey. Borden traveled to many small towns and cities based upon the addresses found in letters and work diaries the Reys’ wrote in 1936 to 1940 when they lived in Paris.
              </p>
              <p>
               Borden’s research culminated in her book
               <em>
                The Journey that Saved Curious George
               </em>
               which inspired the exhibit.
              </p>
              <p>
               The exhibit features photographs of the Reys, illustrations by Allan Drummond, and several keepsakes, including H.A.’s copious notes in his pocket diary detailing their four-month wartime escape from Paris to New York. It also highlights several amazing artifacts from the Holocaust Memorial Center’s archives and Detroit area survivors.
              </p>
              <p>
               H.A. and Margret arrived in New York on October 14, 1940. The following year, Curious George, formerly named Fifi, was published. H.A. and Margret became U.S. citizens in 1946.
               <img alt="New York: Curious George is Published" height="200" src="https://www.holocaustcenter.org/image/New-York-Curious-George-is-Published.jpg" style="margin: 5px 10px; float: right;" width="200"/>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Join us for one of the exciting programs featuring
               <em>
                The True Wartime Escape
               </em>
               exhibit!  Learn more about these opportunities, admission, and RSVP, by
               <a href="https://www.holocaustcenter.org/programs">
                CLICKING HERE
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               The exhibit is open Sunday through Friday and is free with museum admission or membership. For more information about the exhibit, please call 248-553-2400.
              </p>
              <p>
               The exhibit is recommended for ages 12 and older.
              </p>

              <h1 style="text-align: center;">
               What Could Have Been
              </h1>
              <p style="text-align: left;">
               What could these people have become? What could they have accomplished? Invented? Or simply celebrated? We will never know. But, we know what we can do now. Tell the world about them and educate every generation about the horrors of genocide.
