            <div class="entry-content">
             <p>
              The Port Moody Station Museum is hosting a series of talks focused around their exhibition of Rudolf Koch sketches.
              <br/>
              Koch was a famous typographer and the museum, displays for the first time in America, his Great War sketches.
             </p>
             <p>
              On
              <span class="Object" id="OBJ_PREFIX_DWT21_com_zimbra_date" role="link">
               May 24
              </span>
              Dr Dorothee
              <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-3914 alignleft" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 232px) 100vw, 232px" src="http://alcuinsociety.com/content/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/image1-232x300.jpg" srcset="http://alcuinsociety.com/content/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/image1-232x300.jpg 232w, http://alcuinsociety.com/content/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/image1-791x1024.jpg 791w" width="232"/>

             </p>

             </p>

             </p>

             <p>
              Underwood &amp; Underwood was a much-used photographic agency in New York that went out of business, and they sold off their collection of photographic prints. Subsequently a few of New York’s newspapers closed their doors, and photographs from their morgues became available, so she had her pick of those. The result is that she has a collection of photographs reflecting the current events of the day—criminals being arrested, May West leaving jail, Wrong Way Corrigan returning from his misdirected airplane flight to Ireland, The Duke of Windsor—photographs that are intensely interesting if one is old enough to remember the news from those days.
