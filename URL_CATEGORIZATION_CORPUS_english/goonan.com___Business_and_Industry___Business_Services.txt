   <p align="center">
    <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif" size="5">
     Kathleen
 Ann Goonan burst into prominence with
    </font>
    <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif">
     <a href="http://www.goonan.com/queencityjazz.html" style="text-decoration: none;">
      <font size="5">
       <strong>
        <i>
         QUEEN
 CITY JAZZ
        </i>
       </strong>
      </font>
     </a>
     <font size="5">
      <i>
       .
      </i>
      "Spectacular," said
      <strong>
       <i>
        Locus
       </i>
      </strong>
      <i>
       .
      </i>
      "The most important debut novel I've seen this year." It was a finalist
 for the 1998 British Science Fiction Award.
     </font>
     <a href="http://www.goonan.com/bonesoftime.html" style="text-decoration: none;">
      <font size="5">
       <strong>
        <i>
         THE
 BONES OF TIME
        </i>
       </strong>
      </font>
     </a>
     <font size="5">
      , her widely acclaimed second novel, became a
 finalist for the Arthur C. Clarke Award in 2000. Her third novel
     </font>
    </font>
    <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif">
     <a href="http://www.goonan.com/mississippiblues.html" style="text-decoration: none;">
      <font size="5">
       <strong>
        <i>
         MISSISSIPPI
 BLUES
        </i>
       </strong>
      </font>
     </a>
     <font size="5">
      won immediate praise from
      <strong>
       <i>
        Publisher's Weekly
       </i>
      </strong>
      and made her "...
      <strong>
       a major voice in the field
      </strong>
      ."
     </font>
     <a href="http://www.goonan.com/crescentcityrhapsody.html" style="text-decoration: none;">
      <font size="5">
       <b>
        <i>
         CRESCENT
 CITY RHAPSODY
        </i>
       </b>
      </font>
     </a>
     <font size="5">
      , a Nebula finalist, according to
      <strong>
       <i>
        Publisher's
 Weekly's
       </i>
      </strong>
      starred review is "...as
 intellectually demanding yet emotionally satisfying as Duke Ellington's
 best..."
     </font>
    </font>
    <font face="Times New Roman, Times, serif" size="5">
     In
 another starred review
     <strong>
      <i>
       Publisher's Weekly
      </i>
     </strong>
     says of
     <strong>
      <i>
      </i>
     </strong>
     <b>
      <i>
       <a href="lightmusic.html" style="text-decoration: none;">
        LIGHT
 MUSIC
       </a>
      </i>
     </b>
     <strong>
      <i>
       ,
      </i>
     </strong>
     also a Nebula finalist
     <i>
      <strong>
       ,
      </strong>
     </i>
     "Exuberant...
 This classic novel of ideas, with state of the art technology as its
 subject, remains the work of a powerful imagination with a superior
 command of language."
     <a href="http://www.peterstraub.net/" style="text-decoration: none;">
      Peter Straub
     </a>
     writes
 "
     <strong>
      <i>
       <a href="inwartimes.html" style="text-decoration: none;">
        IN WAR TIMES
       </a>
      </i>
     </strong>
     is a novel of great historical reach -- from the Battle of the Bulge to
 the Kennedy assassination and beyond -- and profound ambition,
 expressed with an unmistakable ease of execution and a master's
 sureness of touch. Kathleen Goonan has come through to the kind of
 control that makes every startling fresh development, and this novel
 bristles with astonishing moments of development, seem inevitable. Not
 only does Goonan know that Charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie changed
 the world, she understands that every vision of the future conceals a
 deep yearning for one's own specific past. That's real wisdom."
    </font>
