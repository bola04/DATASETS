     <p>
      <strong>
       Pop-Up-Advertising
      </strong>
      <br/>
      Pop-up ads or popups are a form of online advertising on the World Wide Web intended to increase web traffic or capture email addresses. It works when certain web sites open a new web browser window to display advertisements. The pop-up window containing an advertisement is usually generated by JavaScript, but can be generated by other means as well.
      <a href="http://www.pc-safety.org/articles/pop-up-ads.html">
       Click here for the full article
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Phishing Scammers
      </strong>
      <br/>
      Internet hackers and scammers are becoming increasingly talented and skillful at new forms of ways to scam money out of people online. Gone are the days when a few hackers in Nigeria would send out millions of emails promising the user millions from some king, more advanced hackers are using phishing to scam billions each year.
      <a href="http://www.pc-safety.org/articles/phishing.html">
       Click here for the full article
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Spyware/Adware and Malware
      </strong>
      <br/>
      Spyware is computer software that is installed surreptitiously on a personal computer to intercept or take partial control over the user's interaction with the computer, without the user's informed consent.
      <a href="http://www.pc-safety.org/articles/spyware-adware-malware.html">
       Click here for the full article
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Trojan Horses
      </strong>
      <br/>
      When most people hear the name Trojan Horse they immediately think about the siege on Troy in ancient times and the way the Greeks managed to get inside the city of Troy, this same method is now being used to infiltrate our personal computers. A Trojan horse in computer terms is a program that installs malicious software while under the guise of doing something else.
      <a href="http://www.pc-safety.org/articles/trojan-horses.html">
       Click here for the full article
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Internet Eraser
      </strong>
      <br/>
      I'm not here to ask questions about what is on your machine or what you have been surfing for on the internet, but you may have problems when other people browse on your machine. Just like your car or yourself a computer needs a regular clean out to help with speed and security. The best method for this is to clean up your history from search engine toolbars and the saved history within your machine by using an Internet Eraser.
      <a href="http://www.pc-safety.org/articles/internet-erasers.html">
