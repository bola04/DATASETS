              <strong>
               What is Capitalism?
              </strong>
             </h1>
             <p>
              Capitalism is a
              <strong>
               social system
              </strong>
              based on the principle of
              <strong>
               <a href="category/rights/">
                individual rights
               </a>
              </strong>
              . Politically, it is the system of
              <strong>
               laissez-faire
              </strong>
              (
              <strong>
               <a href="category/freedom/">
                freedom
               </a>
              </strong>
              ). Legally it is a system of
              <strong>
               objective law
              </strong>
              (
              <strong>
               <a href="category/law/">
                rule of law
               </a>
              </strong>
              as opposed to rule of man). Economically, when such freedom is applied to the sphere of production its result is the
              <strong>
               <a href="category/trade/">
                free-market
               </a>
              </strong>
              .
             </p>

              features commentary on political, economic and cultural issues from a pro-capitalist perspective.
             </p>

        <div class="et_pb_section et_pb_section_3 et_section_regular">
         <div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_2">
          <div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_3_4 et_pb_column_4 et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough">
           <div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_4 et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_text_align_left">
            <div class="et_pb_text_inner">
             <h1>
              <strong>
               Ten Principles of Capitalism
              </strong>
             </h1>
             <p>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               1. Capitalism is the moral ideal
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               Capitalism is the moral social system as it leaves the individual free to be virtuous — to pursue the good — by acting by reason.
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “The moral justification of capitalism does not lie in the altruist claim that it represents the best way to achieve ‘the common good.’ It is true that capitalism does—if that catch-phrase has any meaning—but this is merely a secondary consequence. The moral justification of capitalism lies in the fact that it is the only system consonant with man’s rational nature, that it protects man’s survival qua man, and that its ruling principle is: justice.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/ethics/">
               EXPLORE THE
               <strong>
                ETHICS OF CAPITALISM &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               2. Individualism regards the individual (not the collective) as a sovereign being
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               Individualism is not opposed to one living in society as a
               <em>
                trader
               </em>
               ; it is opposed to one living as a
               <em>
                slave
               </em>
               .
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “Individualism regards man—every man—as an independent, sovereign entity who possesses an inalienable right to his own life, a right derived from his nature as a rational being. Individualism holds that a civilized society, or any form of association, cooperation or peaceful coexistence among men, can be achieved only on the basis of the recognition of individual rights—and that a group, as such, has no rights other than the individual rights of its members.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/individualism/">
               EXPLORE THE CONCEPT
               <strong>
                INDIVIDUALISM &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               3. The trader principle is the basis of all moral relationships
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               Individualism is based on the principle of trading value for value for mutual benefit (as opposed to coercion and compulsion.)
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “The principle of
              <em>
               trade
              </em>
              is the only rational ethical principle for all human relationships, personal and social, private and public, spiritual and material. It is the principle of
              <em>
               justice
              </em>
              .” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/trade/">
               EXPLORE THE
               <strong>
                TRADER PRINCIPLE &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               4. Freedom is the absence of physical coercion
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               In a political context, freedom does not mean “freedom” from want, need, and the laws of reality.
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “Since knowledge, thinking, and rational action are properties of the individual, since the choice to exercise his rational faculty or not depends on the individual, man’s survival requires that those who think be free of the interference of those who don’t. Since men are neither omniscient nor infallible, they must be free to agree or disagree, to cooperate or to pursue their own independent course, each according to his own rational judgment. Freedom is the fundamental requirement of man’s mind.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/freedom/">
               EXPLORE THE MEANING OF
               <strong>
                FREEDOM &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               5. Individual rights define one’s freedom of action in a social context
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               All rights are rights to
               <em>
                freedom of action
               </em>
               : the right to those actions necessary to rationally support one’s life — so long as one does not violate the equal rights of others.
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “‘Rights’ are a moral concept—the concept that provides a logical transition from the principles guiding an individual’s actions to the principles guiding his relationship with others—the concept that preserves and protects individual morality in a social context—the link between the moral code of a man and the legal code of a society, between ethics and politics. Individual rights are the means of subordinating society to moral law.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/rights/">
               EXPLORE THE CONCEPT
               <strong>
                INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               6. The initiation of physical force is the only way to violate rights
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               The individual can only be stopped from acting rationally by the initiation of physical force (including fraud).
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “It is only by means of physical force that one man can deprive another of his life, or enslave him, or rob him, or prevent him from pursuing his own goals, or compel him to act against his own rational judgment. The precondition of a civilized society is the barring of physical force from social relationships—thus establishing the principle that if men wish to deal with one another, they may do so only by means of
              <em>
               reason
              </em>
              : by discussion, persuasion and voluntary, uncoerced agreement.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/force/">
               LEARN WHY THE INITIATION OF
               <strong>
                PHYSICAL FORCE
               </strong>
               IS EVIL
               <strong>
                &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               7. Government’s purpose is to protect individual rights
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               Government’s job is to ban the initiation of physical force from all relationships, making all relationships voluntary.
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “The only proper purpose of a government is to protect man’s rights, which means: to protect him from physical violence. A proper government is only a policeman, acting as an agent of man’s self-defense, and, as such, may resort to force
              <em>
               only
              </em>
              against those who
              <em>
               start
              </em>
              the use of force.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/government/">
               EXPLORE THE NATURE OF
               <strong>
                GOVERNMENT &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               8. Government actions are regulated so that individual rights can be protected
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               Capitalism is based on a rule of objective law founded on the principle of individual rights, as opposed to a “rule of men.”
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “All laws must be
              <em>
               objective
              </em>
              (and objectively justifiable): men must know clearly, and in advance of taking an action, what the law forbids them to do (and why), what constitutes a crime and what penalty they will incur if they commit it.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/law/">
               EXPLORE THE CONCEPT OF
               <strong>
                OBJECTIVE LAW &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               9. A free-market is an economic system based on individual rights
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               A free-market bans
              </strong>
              <strong>
               physical force, fraud, and coercion from all economic relationships, resulting in
               <em>
                voluntary cooperation
               </em>
               between buyers and sellers (trade), and
               <em>
                peaceful competition
               </em>
               between buyers and between sellers.
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “In a free economy, where no man or group of men can use physical coercion against anyone, economic power can be achieved only by
              <em>
               voluntary
              </em>
              means: by the voluntary choice and agreement of all those who participate in the process of production and trade. In a free market, all prices, wages, and profits are determined—not by the arbitrary whim of the rich or of the poor, not by anyone’s “greed” or by anyone’s need—but by the law of supply and demand. The mechanism of a free market reflects and sums up all the economic choices and decisions made by all the participants. Men trade their goods or services by mutual consent to mutual advantage, according to their own independent, uncoerced judgment.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/economics/">
               AN INTRODUCTION TO THE SCIENCE OF
               <strong>
                ECONOMICS &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h2>
             </h2>
             <h2>
              <strong>
               10. Capitalism is the system of progress; statism is the system of plunder
              </strong>
             </h2>
             <h5>
              <strong>
               The alleged failures of capitalism are actually caused by the success of statism.
              </strong>
             </h5>
             <p>
              “A statist is a man who believes that some men have the right to force, coerce, enslave, rob, and murder others. To be put into practice, this belief has to be implemented by the political doctrine that the government—the state—has the right to
              <em>
               initiate
              </em>
              the use of physical force against its citizens. How often force is to be used, against whom, to what extent, for what purpose and for whose benefit, are irrelevant questions. The basic principle and the ultimate results of all statist doctrines are the same: dictatorship and destruction. The rest is only a matter of time.” — AYN RAND
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: right;">
              <a href="category/history/">
               LEARN OF CAPITALISM’S
               <strong>
                HISTORY OF PROGRESS
               </strong>
               <strong>
                &gt;
               </strong>
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
