         <div class="content_left">
          <h2>
           Poetry
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span class="youtube">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="344" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/JvNFWwojtu8?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark" title="YouTube video player" type="text/html" width="425">
            </iframe>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <em>
            I’ve just finished reading The Day The Grass Came. I am overwhelmed, thrilled, lit up. Virile, vital, virtuosic. Stupendous.The words demand to be spoken out loud; and the underlying connections forging the whole piece into one great organic entity are profound and muscular. The earth moves.
           </em>
           <strong>
            Simon Callow
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The visceral intellectualism with which he attacks his subjects is exemplified in The Day the Grass Came which defines an ambitious work — and triumphs. The shorter poems are just as impressive……………………
           </em>
           <strong>
            Melvyn Bragg
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Prizewinner in Arvon(2ce), Peterloo (2ce), &amp; Bridport competitions, Leo Aylen has published 9 poetry collections; been published in 100 anthologies, and had 100 poems broadcast on BBC R3 &amp; R4, as well as on radio and television in Britain, Europe, America, and Africa.
          </p>
          <p>
           He has held a Distinguished Visiting Professorship at McMaster University, Ontario, and has been Poet in Residence at Fairleigh Dickinson University, New Jersey; Poet in Residence at the Orange Tree Theatre, Richmond; Cecil Day Lewis Fellow; Royal Literary Fund Fellow..
          </p>
          <p>
           Leo has been sole subject of 3 American nationwide TV programs. (CBS).
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="content_right">
          <h2>
           Film
          </h2>
          <p>
           <span class="youtube">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="344" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/uadSqylwsBk?wmode=transparent&amp;fs=1&amp;hl=en&amp;modestbranding=1&amp;iv_load_policy=3&amp;showsearch=0&amp;rel=1&amp;theme=dark" title="YouTube video player" type="text/html" width="425">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Leo Aylen’s biographical documentary – Dynamo: a life of Michael Faraday – was quite excellent. Faraday’s passion for investigation and his genius for perceiving the essentials of a problem were vividly communicated.  This was a genuine popular scientific educator.
           </em>
           <strong>
            Maurice Richardson    THE OBSERVER
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            From the first scrape of the plane on the white-wood coffin to the final laconic account of the body stiffening in death, Leo Aylen’s The Death of Socrates was strangely gripping…………..
           </em>
           <strong>
            THE DAILY MAIL
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           As a writer-director he has made 15 films for TV (BBC &amp; ITV), and
           <br/>
           been nominated for a BAFTA award for
           <em>
            <strong>
             The Drinking Party
            </strong>
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           He has worked as a screenwriter in Hollywood. He co-wrote
           <em>
            <strong>
             Gods and Generals
            </strong>
           </em>
           (Warner Bros 2003, starring Robert Duvall, Mira Sorvino, Jeff Daniels, directed by Ronald Maxwell, Executive Producer Ted Turner).
          </p>
          <p>
           He created
           <em>
            <strong>
             Chaos &amp; Pattern
            </strong>
           </em>
           , a 5-film series of educational documentaries on science &amp; engineering (2007).
          </p>
          <p>
           See film page for
           <em>
            <strong>
             Gods and Generals
            </strong>
           </em>
           , and more
           <em>
            <strong>
             Chaos &amp; Pattern
            </strong>
           </em>
           clips.
          </p>
