           <h1 class="title">
            Welcome to PWAC Toronto Chapter
           </h1>
          </header>
          <div class="post-content box mark-links">
           <p>
            For information about joining PWAC, please visit our national organization’s website,
            <a href="http://www.pwac.ca">
             www.pwac.ca
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <hr/>
           <h2>
            <span style="color: #ff9900;">
             UPCOMING EVENTS
            </span>
           </h2>
           <div>
            <p class="Listing__section__title">
             <strong>
              Special Event with Partner Organizations
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p class="Listing__section__title">
             <strong>
              New Directions in Self-Publishing
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Tue, March 27,  7:30 PM–9:30 PM
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              Room 610,
              <a href="http://map.utoronto.ca/building/154" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Health Sciences Building
              </a>
              ,155 College St, Toronto, ON M5T, Canada
             </strong>
             <br/>
             <strong>
              (Enter through front doors, take elevator to sixth floor, and turn left).
             </strong>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p class="Listing__section__title">
            <img alt="pexels-photo-76942" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-1515" height="150" src="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/pexels-photo-76942-150x150.jpeg" width="150"/>
            Self-publishing has come a long way since print-on-demand services first surfaced in the late 1990s. Driven by technological change, shifting reading habits, and proliferating formats, self-publishing is a constantly evolving space where success can be elusive, but where the odds improve with detailed knowledge of the industry, investments in professional editing and design, and a willingness to adapt to changing conditions.
           </p>
           <div class="Description__container Description__container--open">
            <div class="Description__body">
             <p>
              Join
              <a href="https://www.editors.ca/branches/toronto/index.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Editors Toronto
              </a>
              and the Professional Writers Association of Canada’s Toronto chapter (PWAC) on March 27 for a panel discussion about the current state of the industry. Led by four seasoned professionals whose work has involved them in all areas of self-publishing, from POD, hybrids, and ebooks to crowdfunding, audiobooks, and online reading and writing communities, this event is sponsored and hosted by the
              <a href="https://learn.utoronto.ca/courses-programs/creative-writing" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Creative Writing Program
              </a>
              at the School of Continuing Studies, University of Toronto, and is geared to writers, editors, other publishing industry professionals, and students. Register online.
             </p>
             <p>
              ABOUT OUR PANELLISTS:
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1516" style="width: 150px">
              <img alt="Stephanie Fysh" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-1516" height="150" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" src="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/sfysh-150x150.jpg" srcset="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/sfysh-150x150.jpg 150w, http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/sfysh-300x300.jpg 300w, http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/sfysh-768x768.jpg 768w, http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/sfysh-1024x1024.jpg 1024w" width="150"/>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               Stephanie Fysh
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.stephaniefysh.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               <strong>
                Stephanie Fysh
               </strong>
              </a>
              is a Toronto-based freelance editor of fiction and nonfiction for publishers and independent authors. Alongside freelancing, Fysh taught editing at Ryerson University for over a decade, was co-coordinator of the Publishing certificate for many of those years. She will speak about her 20 years as a freelance editor and especially about her work with independent authors of fiction, specializing in fantasy, science fiction, YA, romance, and erotica. Fysh, who has also worked with hybrid publishers on projects that range from harrowing memoirs to comedy and who still edits the occasional textbook, will discuss what it takes to bring a project from development to layout-ready manuscript.
              <strong>
               <br/>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1517" style="width: 150px">
              <img alt="Nina Munteanu" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-1517" height="150" src="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/nina-munteanu-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               Nina Munteanu
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.ninamunteanu.ca/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                Nina Munteanu
               </a>
              </strong>
              is an award-winning Canadian ecologist and internationally published author of eight novels, including
              <em>
               Darwin’s Paradox
              </em>
              and
              <em>
               The Splintered Universe Trilogy
              </em>
              , award-winning short stories, essays, and nonfiction books. As an editor, Munteanu has worked extensively for traditional publishers and self-published authors. A frequent contributor to
              <i>
               Amazing Stories
              </i>
              and other science fiction publications, Munteanu teaches writing at George Brown College and the University of Toronto. Her latest book is
              <i>
               Water Is…The Meaning of Water
              </i>
              , a scientific study and personal journey as limnologist, mother, teacher and environmentalist, which was featured in
              <em>
               The New York Times
              </em>
              in 2016. Munteanu will speak about rising professional standards in self-publishing, and shifting opportunities and responsibilities facing editors.
              <strong>
               <br/>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1518" style="width: 150px">
              <img alt="Meghan Behse" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-1518" height="150" src="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/MeghanBehse_highres-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               Meghan Behse
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/meghan-behse-06b13540" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               <strong>
                Meghan Behse
               </strong>
              </a>
              has been working in alternative publishing for nearly eight years. She began her career as an editor for Colborne Communications – editing everything from financial reports to board games – and soon became the company’s vice president. In 2011, Meghan was intimately involved in bringing the hybrid publishing model to Canada through her work launching and later operating
              <a href="http://www.iguanabooks.ca/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Iguana Books
              </a>
              . As publisher, she steered Iguana through a new world of digital and print-on-demand publishing while experimenting with unique royalty arrangements and funding models, including crowdfunding for book projects. Meghan is currently the president of
              <a href="https://www.publaunch.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               PubLaunch Inc.
              </a>
              , an online marketplace and crowdfunding platform where writers, readers, and publishing professionals join forces to publish books. She’ll focus on strategies for using emerging technologies to overcome obstacles faced by writers within the alternative publishing industry, particularly in Canada.
              <strong>
               <br/>
              </strong>
             </p>
             <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1519" style="width: 150px">
              <img alt="Mark Leslie Lefebvre" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-1519" height="150" src="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/MarkLeslieLefebvre_LaurenLang-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
              <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
               Mark Leslie Lefebvre
              </figcaption>
             </figure>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.markleslie.ca/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                Mark Leslie Lefebvre
               </a>
              </strong>
              is an author of fiction and non-fiction, a professional speaker, a digital publishing advocate and a bookseller with more than a quarter century of experience in writing, publishing, and bookselling. He self-published his first book in 2004, was an early adopter of Amazon’s Kindle Direct Publishing program, and he created Kobo Writing Life, a self-publishing tool that now represents 1 in every 5 book sales in Kobo’s global catalog. With more than a dozen traditionally published and self-published books to his name, (including
              <i>
               Haunted Hospitals
              </i>
              ,
              <i>
               Macabre Montreal
              </i>
              , and
              <i>
               A Canadian Werewolf in New York
              </i>
              ) Mark remains optimistic about the growing possibilities for writers and publishers in this exciting new digital age. He’ll explain what prompted him to self-publish his first book, “ten years before any self-respecting writer would admit to such a foul thing,” and discuss why it’s important to understand the publishing business before deciding which solutions in a hybrid publishing world work best. Lefebvre will also tackle the big-picture question: “What is currently wrong with self-publishing and how can we work together to fix that?”
             </p>

            <h2>
             <span style="color: #ff9900;">

            <p>
             <b>
              Seminar
             </b>
             <br/>
             <b>

            </p>
            <p>
             <b>
              Advanced Interviewing Skills for Writers
             </b>
             <img alt="digitalrecorder" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-1415" height="150" src="http://www.pwactoronto.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/digitalrecorder-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Monday, January 29, 2018, 7 to 8:30 p.m.
             </b>
             <br/>
             <b>
              Miles Nadal Jewish Community Centre,750 Spadina Avenue, Rm 218
             </b>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-weight: 400;">
              Whether you’re a journalist, a corporate writer or a ghostwriter, you need to know how to ask tough questions and get good
             </span>
             <span style="font-weight: 400;">
              answers
             </span>
             <span style="font-weight: 400;">
              —
             </span>
             <span style="font-weight: 400;">
              preferably without alienating your interview subject. Writers share this need with other professionals, such as lawyers and psychologists. At this panel presentation, you’ll get practical tips from writers and other professionals who are experts at getting people to open up and share information.
             </span>

Please RSVP to Carolyn Camilleri, VP Special Events and Networking, at

             <span style="font-weight: 400;">

             <b>
              Wednesday, October 25, 2017, 7 to 8:30 p.m.

Sue Bowness, freelance writing instructor and PWAC Toronto Chapter blog editor, will share an overview of how freelance writers earn a living, including different types of content you can write and their pay rates. Get tips from accountant Sunny Widerman about money management for freelancers. Karen Luttrell, marketing strategist and PWAC Toronto Chapter president, will walk you through the basics of developing a marketing plan for your new freelance writing business.

              </a>
              <br/>
              Monday, March 27, 7 to 8:30 pm
              <br/>
              Miles Nadal Jewish Community Centre, Room 318
             </b>

            <p>
             If you earn your living as a freelancer, it’s essential to have great client relationships. This event will help you take good care of those all-important relationships and yourself too. Topics include building rapport with new clients and securing repeat work, the elements of a good contract, how to get paid when they’re not paying, and how to fire a bad client.
            </p>

              <br/>

              <br/>
              Miles Nadal Jewish Community Centre, Room 318

            <p>
             Dealing with deadlines doesn’t have to be stressful. Learn self-care strategies and wellness tips that can fit into your busy schedule. Trade your tension for healthy mind and body strategies that will help you feel better and boost your productivity. Speakers include experts in ergonomics, fitness, and nutrition.

               Going Global: How to Find &amp; Work with International Clients
              </a>
              <br/>
              Thursday, November 24, 7 – 8:30 pm
              <br/>
              Miles Nadal Jewish Community Centre, Room 318
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             We’re called the Professional Writers Association of Canada, but our international markets are expanding as English becomes the default language of business and academia in more and more countries. In this panel discussion, you’ll learn how to find, pitch to, and nurture clients outside of domestic markets. We’ll also cover the practicalities of working internationally, such as getting paid, tax implications, and required paperwork (such as the W-8BEN form for American clients).

Key topics include self-promotion for introverted writers, adopting new vs. traditional self-promotion tactics, creating your own website/blog vs. hiring a professional, and more.
