         <h3>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Featured_Article:_.22Wiebe.2C_Katie_Funk_.281924-2016.29.22">
           Featured Article: "Wiebe, Katie Funk (1924-2016)"
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p>
          <a href="/index.php?title=Wiebe,_Katie_Funk_(1924-2016)" title="Wiebe, Katie Funk (1924-2016)">
           (Read full article)
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <div class="thumb tright">
          <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
           <a class="image" href="/index.php?title=File:Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg&amp;filetimestamp=20180519121427&amp;">
            <img alt="" class="thumbimage" height="372" src="/images/thumb/4/4c/Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg/300px-Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg" srcset="/images/thumb/4/4c/Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg/450px-Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg 1.5x, /images/thumb/4/4c/Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg/600px-Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
           </a>
           <div class="thumbcaption">
            <div class="magnify">
             <a class="internal" href="/index.php?title=File:Wiebe-Katie-Funk.jpg&amp;filetimestamp=20180519121427" title="Enlarge">
             </a>
            </div>
            <i>
             Katie Funk Wiebe, late 1980s. Photo courtesy Katie Funk Wiebe collection, Centre for Mennonite Brethren Studies, Winnipeg, Manitoba
            </i>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          Author, biographer, editor, columnist, and essayist, Katie Funk Wiebe was also known as a speaker, preacher, pioneer, prophet, feminist, teacher, mentor, and historian. In 2000,
          <i>
           The Mennonite
          </i>
          named her among the top 20 Mennonites with “the most powerful influence on life and belief of the…Mennonite Church in the 20th century.”
         </p>
         <p>
          Katie Funk was born 15 September 1924, in Laird,
          <a href="/index.php?title=Saskatchewan_(Canada)" title="Saskatchewan (Canada)">
           Saskatchewan
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/index.php?title=Canada" title="Canada">
           Canada
          </a>
          to Jacob Funk (11 July 1896-7 May 1986) and Anna (Janzen) Funk (11 March 1895-5 March 1994). They were Mennonite immigrants from
          <a href="/index.php?title=Ukraine" title="Ukraine">
           Ukraine
          </a>
          . The fourth child and third daughter in a family of five children, Katie grew up in Blaine Lake, Saskatchewan, where her father managed the local grocery store.
         </p>
         <p>
          While Katie called herself a Christian, she reflected later that she wasn’t really a disciple until she had a profound encounter with God at the age of 19 just before she left for
          <a href="/index.php?title=Winnipeg_(Manitoba,_Canada)" title="Winnipeg (Manitoba, Canada)">
           Winnipeg
          </a>
          to attend
          <a href="/index.php?title=Mennonite_Brethren_Bible_College_(Winnipeg,_Manitoba,_Canada)" title="Mennonite Brethren Bible College (Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada)">
           Mennonite Brethren Bible College
          </a>
          (MBBC). When Katie began working for a student publication, she met its new editor, Walter Wiebe (3 October 1918-17 November 1962), a student from
          <a href="/index.php?title=Yarrow_(Chilliwack,_British_Columbia,_Canada)" title="Yarrow (Chilliwack, British Columbia, Canada)">
           Yarrow
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/index.php?title=British_Columbia_(Canada)" title="British Columbia (Canada)">
           British Columbia
          </a>
          . They were married on 21 August 1947 in the
          <a href="/index.php?title=Central_Mennonite_Brethren_Church_(Saskatoon,_Saskatchewan,_Canada)" title="Central Mennonite Brethren Church (Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, Canada)">
           Saskatoon Mennonite Brethren Church
          </a>
          .
         </p>

         <h3>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Our_Mission">
           Our Mission
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p>
          The Global Anabaptist Mennonite Encyclopedia Online provides reliable, freely-available English-language information on Anabaptist-related congregations, denominations, conferences, institutions and significant individuals, as well as historical and theological topics. Secular subject articles from an Anabaptist perspective and full-text source documents are also included.
         </p>

For questions of that type, please contact a local genealogical society, or check with a Mennonite historical library or archives.
