              <a href="cg5/">
               <b>
                CG-5 Mount Improvements
               </b>
              </a>
              <br/>
              The CG-5 German equatorial mount is a Chinese-made clone of the well-known Vixen Great Polaris (GP) mount.
              <a href="cg5/">
               <img align="RIGHT" alt="CG-5 Refurbishing" border="0" height="90" src="cg5/images/cg5_120.jpg" vspace="5" width="120"/>
              </a>
              Although the CG-5 is quite popular, many examples suffer from sticky movements in both the right ascension and declination axes.
              <b>
               Astronomy Boy
              </b>
              shows you how to smooth out the motions of your CG-5.
              <br/>
              <p>
               <a href="barndoor/">
                <b>
                 Build a Barn Door Tracker
                </b>
               </a>
               <br/>
               A barn door tracker (also known as a Haig or Scotch mount) is a tracking camera mount that lets you get started in astrophotography, even if you don't own a telescope. Check out
               <b>
                Astronomy Boy's
               </b>
               barn door, along with all the information you need to build your own.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="eyepieces/">
                <img align="RIGHT" alt="Homemade Eyepiece" border="0" height="115" src="eyepieces/images/finished_100.jpg" width="100"/>
               </a>
               <a href="eyepieces/">
                <b>
                 Homemade Eyepieces
                </b>
               </a>
               <br/>
               If you're looking for a build-it-yourself astronomy project, but constructing an entire telescope seems too complex, how about making your own eyepieces? It's surprisingly easy to do if you have some basic woodworking skills and a few common tools.
              </p>

              The stuff of myths and legends, the constellations of the night sky tell stories as old as mankind. As these photos demonstrate, it's easy to make beautiful constellation portraits with simple equipment.

                <b>
                 SAA 100 Deep Sky Catalog
                </b>
               </a>
               <br/>
               The most famous catalog of deep sky objects is the one compiled by Charles Messier in the 18th century. Now, participants in the sci.astro.amateur newsgroup have nominated a new list of the 100 best non-Messier objects for amateur telescopes, the
               <a href="saa/">
                <b>
                 SAA 100
                </b>
               </a>
               .
              </p>

               <b>
                any
               </b>
               part of the sky.
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="halebopp/">

                <b>
                 Comet Hale-Bopp
                </b>
               </a>
               <br/>
               The winter and spring of 1997 brought a bright comet to our skies. As these photos show, it was easy to capture beautiful images of Comet Hale-Bopp on film without complicated equipment. Enjoy the pictures, and be ready when the next bright comet comes our way!
