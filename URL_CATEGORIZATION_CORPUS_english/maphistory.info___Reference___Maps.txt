      <h1>
       Map History / History of Cartography
      </h1>
      <h2>
       THE Gateway to the Subject
      </h2>
      <p>
       Old maps, early maps—
 non-current maps in general—are the subject of this site. Whether you are an academic,
       <a href="family.html">
        family historian
       </a>
       , collector, teacher, parent or surfer - WELCOME! Use this site to find the worthwhile
       <b>
        <i>
         free
        </i>
       </b>
       information about old maps, both on the web and in the real world. The site's 120 'pages' offer comment and
 guidance, and over 6,500
       <i>
        annotated
       </i>
       links selected for relevance and quality. As the information is logically
 organised in a directory structure, you should easily find what you want. For an overview, check out
       <a href="sum.html">
        What the site is about
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Follow the links to the left; or use the
       <a href="mapsindex.html">
        Index
       </a>
       (and perhaps find unexpected things as well); or explore via the
       <a href="sitemap.html">
        Sitemap
       </a>
       to
 see the full range.  In addition, you can search across the
       <i>
        whole
       </i>
       of this site:
      </p>
      <center>
       <form action="http://search.freefind.com/find.html" method="GET" target="_self">

      <p>
       Please note that there are no pictures of maps on this site (but see
       <a href="webimages.html">
        <b>
         Map image sites
        </b>
       </a>
       for thousands of annotated links to them). Alternatively,
       <a href="webtexts.html">
        <b>

 easy access to the growing body of
       <i>
        free

        updated continuously
