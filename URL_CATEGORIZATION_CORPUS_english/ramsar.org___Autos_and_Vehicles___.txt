               <a>
                About Ramsar
               </a>
              </h2>
              <div class="field field-name-field-stacked-block field-type-field-collection field-label-hidden">
               <div class="field-items">
                <div class="field-item even">
                 <div class="stacked-block-right">

                 <div class="field field-name-field-content field-type-text-long field-label-hidden">
                  <div class="field-items">
                   <div class="field-item even">
                    <p class="intro-resume">
                     The Convention on Wetlands, called the Ramsar Convention, is an intergovernmental treaty that provides the framework for national action and international cooperation for the conservation and wise use of wetlands and their resources.
                    </p>
                    <p class="about-ramsar-figures" style="margin-top: 16px;">
                     Number of Contracting Parties:
                     <strong>
                      170
                     </strong>
                    </p>

                     <strong>
                     </strong>
                     <span style="font-family: proxima_nova_ltsemibold;">
                      2,314
                     </span>
                    </p>
                    <p class="about-ramsar-figures">
                     Total surface of designated sites:
                     <strong>
                     </strong>
                     <span style="font-family: proxima_nova_ltsemibold;">
                      245,614,112 ha
                     </span>
                    </p>

      <div class="row-fluid view view-home-featured-site view-id-home_featured_site view-display-id-block view-dom-id-65243384982ef60d88d7ed58f4ebe358" id="home-country-feature-site-container">

       <div class="span5 country-feature-right">
        <div class="row-fluid country-feature-site-content">
         <div class="span12">
          <div class="country-feature-site-text-container">
           <div class="country-feature-site-title">
            North, Middle &amp; East Caicos Islands
           </div>
           <div class="country-feature-site-country">
            United Kingdom
           </div>
           <div class="country-feature-site-date">
           </div>
           <div class="country-country-feature-site-description-site-body">
            North, Middle &amp; East Caicos Islands. 27/06/90; Turks &amp; Caicos Islands; 58,617 ha; 21°45'N 071°45'W. Nature Reserve. Extensive coastal site including prime examples of the main wetland habitats found on the Islands. Consists of intertidal algal flats and submerged banks, salt ponds, mangrove swamps, lagoons, sinkholes, and marshland. The site provides important feeding areas for waterbirds, habitat for the endangered sea turtle, and nursery grounds for fish. Flamingos feed and breed in the salt ponds and offshore limestone islands act as refuges for iguanas. The area is important for Dendrocygna arborea, probably numbering fewer than 1,000 pairs in the Caribbean. Tourism is an important industry in the islands. As of 2002, a management plan is in development with community support under a Darwin Initiative project. Ramsar site no. 493.
           </div>
