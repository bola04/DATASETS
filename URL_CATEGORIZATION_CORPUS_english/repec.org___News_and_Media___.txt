   <h2>
    <a name="general">
    </a>
    General principles
   </h2>
   <font size="4">
    RePEc
   </font>
   (
   <font color="#FF6633" size="4">
    Re
   </font>
   search
   <font color="#FF6633" size="4">
    P
   </font>
   apers in
   <font color="#FF6633" size="4">
    Ec
   </font>
   onomics) 
 is a collaborative effort of hundreds of volunteers in
   <a href="http://repec.org/docs/RePEC_co.html">
    99 countries
   </a>
   to enhance the dissemination of research in Economics and related sciences. The heart of the
 project is a decentralized bibliographic database of working papers, journal articles, books, books chapters  and
 software components, all maintained by volunteers. The collected data are then used in various services that serve the collected metadata to users or enhance it.
   <p>
    So far, over 1,900 archives from
    <a href="http://repec.org/docs/RePEC_co.html">
     99 countries
    </a>
    have contributed about 2.5 million research pieces from 3,000 journals 
 and 4,600 working paper series. Over 50,000 authors have registered and 
 75,000 email subscriptions are served every week. See below on how you 
 can be part of this initiative.
   </p>

   <h2>
    Adding bibliographic information to RePEc
   </h2>
   The basic principle is that publishers index their content themselves 
 into RePEc. They host the metadata on their http or ftp site, following 
 the
   <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/p/rpc/rdfdoc/guildp.html">
    Guildford Protocol
   </a>
   , which indicates how the metadata archive should be structured. Then, the syntax of the metadata template syntax is guided by
   <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/p/rpc/rdfdoc/redif.html">
    ReDIF
   </a>
   , the Research Documents Information Format.
   <p>
    If you intend to contribute information about your publications to RePEc, you may read the above documents or use these
    <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/stepbystep.html">
     step-by-step instructions
    </a>
    or
    <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/templates.html">
     sample templates
    </a>
    . The same instructions apply for commercial publishers or research institutes.
   </p>
   <p>
    RePEc archive maintainers may also make good use of the
    <a href="http://econpapers.repec.org/check/">
     template syntax and link checker
    </a>
    , of
    <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/maintain.html">
     tips and tricks
    </a>
    and the
    <a href="http://econpapers.repec.org/archiveFAQ.htm">
     FAQ
    </a>
    .
   </p>

   <h2>
    Volunteers
   </h2>
   RePEc is entirely based on the contributions of volunteers: Maintainers of RePEc archives, editors at
   <a href="http://nep.repec.org/">
    NEP
   </a>
   and
   <a href="https://mpra.ub.uni-muenchen.de/">
    MPRA
   </a>
   , and
   <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/team.html">
    those who run
   </a>
   the various RePEc services. If you want to get involved check out our
   <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/volunteers.html">
    volunteer opportunities
   </a>
   or contact any member of the
   <a href="https://ideas.repec.org/team.html">
    RePEc team
   </a>
   .
   <p>
    RePEc emerged from the
    <a href="http://netec.mcc.ac.uk/">
     NetEc group
    </a>
    ,
  created in 1992, which received support for its WoPEc project between 
 1996-1999 by the Joint Information Systems Committee (JISC) of the UK 
 Higher Education Funding Councils, as part of its Electronic Libraries 
 Programme (eLib). RePEc was created in June 1997 to decentralize the 
 work done by WoPEc and thus make it independent of grant needs. RePEc is
  then guaranteed to remain free for all parties.
