                    <a class="style3" href="/functional_inspection.cfm">
                     OBD II (On-Board Diagnostics II - Functional Inspection)
                    </a>
                   </b>
                   is a on-board self-diagnostic smog check computer; required for all California vehicles manufactured after 1996. OBDII vehicles have the capability to diagnose virtually every smog check component that can affect emission results. Smog check components such as the oxygen sensor (O2), catalytic converter (CAT), engine coolant temperature (ECT) sensors as well as several others are inspected by the smog technician during the
                   <a href="/visual_inspection.cfm" target="_blank">
                    visual inspection
                   </a>
                   and are checked by the OBDII computer for proper operation and to ensure fuel is being burned thoroughly. If a smog check problem or malfunction is detected, the OBD II computer will illuminate a warning light on the vehicle instrument panel known as the Check Engine light or Service Engine Soon light, and record an
                   <a href="/trouble-codes.cfm">
                    OBDII trouble code
                   </a>
                   . Very often you may not notice an engine problem. This is due to the OBDII computer operating under pre-programmed data rather then real time. The problem exists but the engine ignores the defective sensors input and provides its own manufacturer programmed data.

Smog stations are required to have OBDII trouble code retrieval tools or scanners on hand.
