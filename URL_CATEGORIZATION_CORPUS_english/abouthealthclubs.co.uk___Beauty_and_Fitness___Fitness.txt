        <h1>
         Health Clubs
        </h1>
        <p>
         As UK health clubs increase, so public awareness about personal lifestyle and fitness increases. Finding gyms or your local fitness club is not always easy. With over 3 million people now have a gym membership! So how do you go about joining one and finding the right
         <a href="/consumer/gym-membership.html">
          gym membership
         </a>
         ?
         <br/>
         <br/>
         There is a range of health club operators in the market - from small individually run operations to Fitness First, LivingWell and David Lloyd Health clubs' of this world. We know the choice can become a little confusing - especially if you are new to fitness.
        </p>
        <p>
         With abouthealthclubs.co.uk, we want to help you find the right club for you - so we've got some gym articles, some fitness information and advice on examining what you want from a health club and why you want to join.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         We also work closely with two leading chains to offer you the chance to find out more about them in particular
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Please select one of the links above to help you start on the road to finding a new health club (or if you are more a spa-type person check out our
         <a href="http://www.spadeals.co.uk" target="_blank">
          spa deals
         </a>
         websites for the latest offers and vouchers. If you simply want to search for your nearest health clubs uk wide, please use our health clubs directory search or for the best fitness clubs in the UK (including local authority based clubs).
         <br/>
         <br/>
         There's over 5,700 health clubs out there, so good luck finding one that's right for you. And remember please see a doctor before you start any new training and ensure that when you join, you ask for a full personal fitness programme from the fully qualified instructors. This could help to save your life!
        </p>
        <p>
         Finally, we would just like to offer you a brief piece of advice about finding
         <a href="http://www.onelifestyle.co.uk" target="_blank">
          Personal Trainer
         </a>
         in your local area. We believe that if you are serious about your new lifestyle and getting fit, why not look to get a personal trainer for a night every week to help motivate you and ensure you are working out correctly.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Try the UK's largest popular
         <a href="http://www.nrpt.co.uk" target="_blank">
          Personal Trainers
         </a>
         team. Remember this is the only place where they check for you whether they hold first aid and insurance certificates. But never meet for the first time in a private place.
        </p>
