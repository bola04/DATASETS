          <a href="http://plastik.hu/2018/05/31/ura-zelda/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Ura Zelda">
           Ura Zelda

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          I think I finally lost my mind.
         </p>
         <p>
          I mean I liked the Zelda games, started and played most of them, but as things are, I never truly finished them. There were a couple of exceptions though: I almost maxed out the Minish Cap (but didn‚Äôt have notes and a Kinstone fusion was missing), then truly maxed out Oracle of Seasons and Ages. It was a very definitive moment in my gaming life. Yet, when Majora‚Äôs Mask came in 2015, I stopped playing it at some point. I also stopped playing Wind Waker HD in 2013. This is the usual gamer, you start something, then you don‚Äôt finish it.
         </p>
         <p>
          However.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, however. When Twilight Princess HD came along in 2016, I completed it. Not only that, I maxed it out along with the new Cave of Shadows dungeon (which is just waves of different enemies, really). It was fun and a great experience. You know, the kind where it‚Äôs hard and you need to retry the part over and over and over and over again. But you finally succeed and it‚Äôs AMAZING!
         </p>
         <p>
          Then it was time for me to revisit Wind Waker HD. I picked up my old savegame from three years back, and despite the fact that I‚Äôd forgotten everything, I got back into it and not only did I finish it, but I maxed it out. That meant completing the game once again, from beginning to end, with all minigames, figurines, and everything. To my standards it was a crazy ride, but a very satisfactory one.
         </p>
         <p>
          After this I picked up Majora‚Äôs Mask 3DS and did the same feat: not only did I complete the game, but I once again maxed it out, all hearts, sidequests, fishing holes are now done. (Note: in the mean time Breath of the Wild came along and I started playing it on the Wii U and stopped.)
         </p>
         <p>
          After Majora‚Äôs I had to decide where I wanted to go next.
         </p>
         <p>
          The obvious choice was Breath of the Wild on the Switch. I even bought the game, but yet something was telling me my Zelda destiny is somewhere else. In 2011 I bought Ocarina of Time 3DS and completed it (and even maxed it out, but as I see now a final heart piece is missing). The game contains the Master Quest also which I‚Äôve always wanted to play, but never did. So I thought maybe I should play that next. But if I did, I had to play Ocarina, in this new game mode, which is harder, from scratch. Something wasn‚Äôt right.
         </p>
         <p>
          I then realized my destiny: I need to replay and max out a fresh copy of Ocarina of Time, so it all comes back to me, and when that is done, I need to do experience Master Quest. I mean, we‚Äôre talking about one of the best video game of all time, right?
         </p>
         <p>
          So here I am, at 41 years of age, replaying a fucking game from 2011, which I have completed almost to the fullest, and when that‚Äôs done, I‚Äôll replay it one more time where the whole gameworld is inverted and dungeons are even harder. And oh, yes, did I mention there is also a fishing minigame that needs to get completed?
          <a href="https://zelda.gamepedia.com/Boss_Challenge" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           And a boss challenge mode and a boss gauntlet?
          </a>
         </p>

         <h3>
          <a href="http://plastik.hu/2018/05/19/notes-on-how-to-finish-the-fishing-minigame-in-majoras-mask-3ds/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Notes on how to finish the fishing minigame in Majora‚Äôs Mask 3DS">
           Notes on how to finish the fishing minigame in Majora‚Äôs Mask 3DS
          </a>
         </h3>
         <span class="entry-meta">
          <time datetime="2018-05-19-23-45" itemprop="published">
           <a href="">
            # 2018.05.19. 23:45
           </a>
          </time>
          <span class="entry-categories ">
           category:
           <a href="http://plastik.hu/tag/gamez/" rel="category tag">
            gamez
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          After 90 hours of net gametime I can finally claim the 100% completion of the 3DS version of Majora‚Äôs Mask.
         </p>
         <p>
          Bought this game back when it was released in 2015, put around 25-30 hours into it, then ended up not playing it any more. Things are changing and I‚Äôm back in business: this year I picked it up again, played and played until it was all smooth again for my fingers then completed it to the fullest.
         </p>
         <p>
          This means fishing out the two fishing holes that are exclusive to the 3DS version. Here is my guide that I feel is worth sharing because the available guides don‚Äôt really mention these ‚Äì or parts were news to me.
         </p>
         <p>
          First, the guides from around the net!
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì
          <a href="https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/boards/877120-the-legend-of-zelda-majoras-mask-3d/71247029" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Gamefaqs
          </a>
          (mainly used this one)
          <br/>
          ‚Äì
          <a href="http://www.ign.com/wikis/the-legend-of-zelda-majoras-mask/Fishing" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           IGN guide
          </a>
          <br/>
          ‚Äì Zeldadungeons
          <a href="https://www.zeldadungeon.net/wiki/Swamp_Fishing_Hole" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Swamp
          </a>
          and
          <a href="https://www.zeldadungeon.net/wiki/Ocean_Fishing_Hole" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Ocean
          </a>
          holes with fish pictures (useful)
          <br/>
          ‚Äì
          <a href="https://touriantourist.blogspot.se/search/label/Majora's%20Mask" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Tourian Tourist‚Äôs guide
          </a>
          (scroll down to 2016)
          <br/>
          ‚Äì Videos:
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zCmFL_yrJDU" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Swamp Fishing Hole Guide
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SYd8HKpv0S8" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Ocean Fishing Hole Guide
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are my notes:
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì First, I suggest doing the fishing holes last, when you have all the masks. It‚Äôs not compulsory, but since you‚Äôll need to use various masks to catch the fish, it‚Äôs worth getting the masks, then do the holes in a focused effort.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì Start with the swamp hole which is the smaller of the two. When you enter, just explore the area first from underwater as a Zora, identify the parts and observe the fish in the water.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì The idea of this minigame is to cast your line in the direction of the fish. Let the bait sink or float, reel slowly until the fish notices it. When there is a bite, quickly press ‚ÄúA‚Äù and use the analog pad to drag towards you. It requires some effort to reel them in and usually some direction change opposite from where the fish is trying to swim. If you hear the reel clicking sound it means, it‚Äôs getting closer to you. Often times the fish jumps and gets further away. Patience.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì By the hole entrance there are 11 drawings of the fish with hints how to catch them. The drawings are not very good and pretty captain lorez, so I suggest using Zeldadungeons excellent PNG‚Äôs instead. I also suggest using a spreadsheet with the list of the fish to mark where to focus next.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì By the counter there is a board with all your catch. This is how you can tell what you‚Äôd achieved. (If only Nintendo would‚Äôve put a completion marker on the savegame!)
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì Catch the common fish first that don‚Äôt require a mask to catch. This is a good warm-up to understand the mechanism.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì Some of the fish require that you wear a certain mask. The idea is that you go relatively close to the fish you want to catch and put the mask on. Of course, the fish needs to have been spawned in the pool, so confirm that first, then wear the mask. You‚Äôll hear a chime which means the fish is now ‚Äútriggered‚Äù. When this occurs there is a splash in the surface vertical to where the fish swims. This way you can identify on the surface in which direction you need to cast your line. Note: some trigger is shorter, some longer. If trigger goes off, you need to take off your mask and put it back on again.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì Workflow is the following: select a fish from the list you want to catch. Enter the hole, switch to Zora Link and try to find the fish underwater. This might seem daunting at first, but after a while it‚Äôll get easier. A new set of fish spawns when you enter the hole. If you can‚Äôt find the fish you‚Äôre looking for, exit the hole and re-enter. If you see your fish, go and buy the rod and go to a convenient place from where you can cast your line in the direction of the fish. Use the mask, observe the chime, then search for the splashes on the water surface. If you switch to camera mode you can fine tune the direction where you‚Äôll cast your line. If you don‚Äôt have the trigger or run out of time, the fish won‚Äôt bite. Once you have a catch, exit the hole and save.
         </p>
         <p>
          ‚Äì This way each fish will cost 50 rupees. To make money, just collect it outside the hole while you‚Äôre working on getting the spawn. For instance, if you exit the Swamp Hole, go to the target shooting next door. Do not enter the room, instead, climb up to the higher ledge where there is two bush. They usually contain 3 x blue and 1 x red rupees. Every time you enter this area you can harvest the money. Very easy to get the 50 rupees for the fishing rod session. Same applies to the ocean, but you need to kill the Like Likes on the beach. Use night time to harvest more.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tips for fish I found a little harder to catch:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Sweet Ranchfish
          </strong>
          ‚Äì short trigger time and always spawns by the waterfall pond. Enter the hole go there, see if there is a fish, if not, repeat. Once you see it, buy the rod, walk there and play Epona‚Äôs song. You‚Äôll hear the chime then you can cast the line. Be quick with the cast.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Fragrant Reekfish
          </strong>
          ‚Äì wear the Mask of Scents and enter the hole. The purple fog above the surface of the water will tell you where the fish is.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ferocious Pirarucu
          </strong>
          ‚Äì swims around the pillar, easy to notice. Then just have a smaller fish bite your lure and this will trigger the Pirarucu. Similar strategy applies to the fish that bites on smaller fish.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Colossal Catfish
          </strong>
          ‚Äì on the final day switch to Deku Link, hop to the pillar, switch to Goron Link and ground pound. If you hear the chime, locate the surface and cast the line. If you don‚Äôt, exit the hole and re-enter.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ninja Flounder
          </strong>
          ‚Äì go to one of the left pillars, wear the mask and listen to the chime. If you don‚Äôt get the chime, immediately exit, and re-enter the room. Once you get the chime, it will require some patience to locate from above (as it is unseen with the Zora mask).
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Nuptuna
          </strong>
          ‚Äì hard to spot because it looks like a common fish, wear the Couple‚Äôs Mask and listen to the chime.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Great Fairy Fish
          </strong>
          ‚Äì this worked for me: have all 11 fish on your roster. Once you do, start a new cycle, slow the time, and catch fish for the first two days. It will be boring and long, but don‚Äôt get discouraged. When the third day starts, immediately go out and save. Come back to the hole with the Fairy‚Äôs Mask on your head and check if it shimmers when you enter the hole (this is to check if there is a Fairy Fish around). If there isn‚Äôt, re-enter. If there is, swim as a Zora to the crystal cave on the top right portion of the area. The Great Fairy Fish will be hard to spot, but from the right hand side there is a little way to look inside. Do this process until you spot the fish and it‚Äôs not later than 11:00 PM on the last day. If it is later, reload your savegame and repeat (you‚Äôll need the time for this fish). Once you see the Great Fairy Fish, buy a pass, go to the closest pillar and wait until the Fairy Fish bait swims relatively close to the cave. Put on your Fairy‚Äôs Mask to get a fresh chime, cast your lure and try to get it to bite. When there is a bite, let it swim towards the cave. you need to let the reel go and direct the fish by pulling the rod. Eventually the Great Fairy Fish will come out of the lair and eventually it‚Äôs going to bite. Patience is needed for this, because it will be a long fight.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-33617" sizes="(max-width: 1200px) 100vw, 1200px" src="http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/big_fairy_fish.jpg" srcset="http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/big_fairy_fish.jpg 1200w, http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/big_fairy_fish-300x206.jpg 300w, http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/big_fairy_fish-768x526.jpg 768w, http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/big_fairy_fish-1024x701.jpg 1024w" width="100%"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          I don‚Äôt believe it either. Have fun! üôÇ
         </p>

           How to play Commodore 64 games on your modern Mac
          </a>
         </h3>
         <span class="entry-meta">
          <time datetime="2018-03-04-14-06" itemprop="published">
           <a href="">
            # 2018.03.04. 14:06
           </a>
          </time>
          <span class="entry-categories ">
           category:
           <a href="http://plastik.hu/tag/8-bit-today/" rel="category tag">
            8 bit today
           </a>
           <a href="http://plastik.hu/tag/c64/" rel="category tag">
            c64
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Bought a couple of games for the Commodore 64.
          <a href="https://www.knightsofbytes.games/samsjourney" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Sam‚Äôs Journey
          </a>
          seems a very nice Warioland/Mario based platformer with very interesting graphics. Here is how to play it on the Mac.
         </p>
         <p>
          You need to download
          <a href="http://vice-emu.sourceforge.net" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           VICE
          </a>
          for the Mac. The latest version is 3.1 and
          <a href="http://www.zimmers.net/anonftp/pub/cbm/crossplatform/emulators/VICE/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           you want to grab the
          </a>
          Cocoa version called
          <code>
           vice-macosx-cocoa-x86_64-10.12-3.1.dmg
          </code>
          . Create a folder in your
          <code>
           /Applications
          </code>
          and dump everything in there.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can now start the emulator by clicking on
          <code>
           x64.app
          </code>
          . Here is what you need to set up in order to play from the keyboard. First, go to ‚ÄúSettings‚Äù and check ‚ÄúSave settings on exit‚Äù. Now set up the joystick emulation. Go to ‚ÄúSettings ‚Äì Joystick‚Äù and then ‚ÄúKeyset‚Äù and select ‚ÄúKeyset B‚Äù. This is a very confusing UX, but what you need to do is press a button that you‚Äôd like to map the joystick to and then set it to a direction. Middle one is the fire button. I use the left Alt for fire and the cursor keys for directions. You don‚Äôt need to set the NE, NW, SE, SW directions. Here is what it looks like:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-33606" sizes="(max-width: 894px) 100vw, 894px" src="http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/joy_settings_vice-1-894x1024.png" srcset="http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/joy_settings_vice-1-894x1024.png 894w, http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/joy_settings_vice-1-262x300.png 262w, http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/joy_settings_vice-1-768x880.png 768w, http://plastik.hu/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/joy_settings_vice-1.png 1192w" width="596"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Games usually use the joystick port 2, so I set Joystick #2 to Keyset B. (Don‚Äôt use the default Keyset A because it maps to regular keyboard characters and you won‚Äôt be able to type. Just set it up like the screenshot above.) Quit VICE, restart it and make sure that your joystick emulation parameters are correctly set.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now, it‚Äôs time to load a game. Go to ‚ÄúFile ‚Äì Attach Disk Image‚Äù and point to the disk image into disk unit 8. In the C64 screen type the famous

          The franchise starts to look like what Battlefield/Assassins Creed are in video games or any of the superhero movies: year after year, predictably, something new comes along, with even better graphic and even more ‚Äútests‚Äù as to what
          <em>
           will
          </em>
          predictably work with a wide demographic. In Rogue One this is so clear, it just makes me sad.
         </p>
         <p>
          I do not feel the same with Nintendo, for instance. I have good faith that the next Zelda game, Breath of the Wild, will have a lot of interesting concepts and entertainment that will provide a lot of excitement and gameplay hours. Nintendo has always put the core gameplay first. It has got to work. It has got to be fun. I could also name Pixar, whose movies traditionally are all exceptionally well written and funny.
         </p>
         <p>
          Rogue One, on the other hand, didn‚Äôt want to be a good Star Wars movie. Sure, you would think that everything was there, the dogfights, practical effects, all the references to the originals, but if you discount those what will you have? Shallow characters, weak story and bad directing.
         </p>
         <p>
          Take the last shot for example. Darth Vader is two corridors away, slaying down rebels while they pass along the laserdisc with the plans, while Leia is chill as fuck looking out the window? And I supposed to believe that this very vessel, that Darth even saw depart, will come back in the opening scene of the New Hope and Vader and Leia act as if none of this would have happened? Come on.
         </p>
         <p>
          I also have doubts whether telling the story of Rogue One could work in a 130 minute format. I could see this work in the animated series, but here, in such a limited time, trying to convey so much, is just too challenging. I failed to see characters, I only saw costumes.
         </p>
         <p>
          I‚Äôm not saying it wasn‚Äôt worth seeing, but what I do say is that I had hard time to connect with this movie. I feel it was bad precedent with the franchise and Disney is on a great track to level it with the bland superhero movies.
         </p>
