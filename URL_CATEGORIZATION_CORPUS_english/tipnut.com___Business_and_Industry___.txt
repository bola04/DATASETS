       <h3>
        Frequently Asked Questions
       </h3>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Where do you find all your tips?
        </strong>
        I've been collecting vintage homemaking magazines, books/cookbooks and craft patterns for over 20 years, I love this stuff! The internet has given me an opportunity to compile all this information into a handy resource that anyone can review.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Are you legit or just someone seeking to be a big blog celebrity?
        </strong>
        I'm a pretty private person and don't publish photos of myself or my personal information...if I was trying to "fake it till I make it" so I can be a "celebrity", I'm doing a pretty poor job of things ;). I'm genuinely legit and am content to share tips and tricks, keeping that the sole focus here. In other words, no I am not trying to become the next Martha Stewart or Heloise or big name blogger.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         How did you come up with the idea for this website?
        </strong>
        At the time I started, there really wasn't anything like it. There were a small number of craft blogs that featured projects occasionally and a few recipe blogs, but nothing that did both plus creative homemaking tips (cleaning, gardening, household, etc.). Initially I wanted a place where I could organize my collection of information but soon saw an opportunity to share all the nifty things I found online. This allows me to extend a bit of goodwill and support to other bloggers plus give readers something fun to check out...win win for everyone!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         The setup here is unusual...Is this a blog or a website or what?
        </strong>
        I consider it a hybrid of both. It started out as a daily blog but over the years things got a tad messy and overwhelming. What's the use in having a site full of tips if they're scattered all over the place? I've been working for over a year now to condense the site into fewer pages so that you're viewing all the like-tips on a single page whenever possible. Give me another year to finish cleaning up and I think I'll have the most organized, visitor-friendly tips/crafty site on the web!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         How many people write here?
        </strong>
        Just me, that's it. You can read more on
        <a href="http://tipnut.com/about/">
         my About page here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
