      <h1>
       Welcome to GeneWatch UK
      </h1>
      <p>
       GeneWatch UK is a not-for-profit group that monitors developments in genetic technologies from a public interest, human rights, environmental protection and animal welfare perspective. GeneWatch believes people should have a voice in whether or how these technologies are used and campaigns for safeguards for people, animals and the environment. We work on all aspects of genetic technologies - from GM crops and foods to genetic testing of humans.
      </p>

      <h2>
       Topics
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-566989">
         GM insects
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Read the GeneWatch
         <a href="http://www.genewatch.org/article.shtml?als[cid]=567356&amp;als[itemid]=577516">
          press release
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.genewatch.org/uploads/f03c6d66a9b354535738483c1c3d49e4/Failed_in_the_field_fin.pdf">
          briefing
         </a>
         showing new evidence of failures of Oxitec's genetically modified (GM) mosquito trials, including documents recently released as a result of Freedom of Information requests in the Cayman Islands. Read the articles in the
         <a href="http://www.miamiherald.com/news/local/community/florida-keys/article211305309.html">
          Maimi Herald
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://www.caymancompass.com/2018/05/14/emails-reveal-gm-mosquito-program-impact-was-overstated/">
          Cayman Compass
         </a>
         and
         <a href="https://caymannewsservice.com/2018/05/genewatch-gm-mosquitoes-failed/">
          Cayman News Service
         </a>
         .
 The Cayman Islands has now abandoned plans to roll out GM mosquito releases across Grand Cayman, but has announced a new local trial in the West Bay area, in which GM mosquito releases will be combined with spraying and other interventions. Read the articles in the
         <a href="https://www.caymancompass.com/2018/05/21/release-of-genetically-modified-mosquitoes-to-resume-this-month/">
          Cayman Compass
         </a>
         and
         <a href="https://caymannewsservice.com/2018/05/mrcu-signs-new-600k-deal-with-oxitec/">
          Cayman News Service
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-575432">
         New Genetic Engineering Techniques
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The UK Government has allowed the first field trial of gene edited crops, arguing they should not be regulated under legislation covering GM crops. This means that in future they could be grown without any prior assessment of the risks or labeling for consumers. Read the
         <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/science/2018/05/22/exclusive-gene-edited-super-crops-grown-uk-first-time/">
          Telegraph
         </a>
         article and the
         <a href="https://www.gmfreeze.org/press-releases/defra-issues-a-free-pass-on-new-gm-technique/">
          GM Freeze press release
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Read the
         <a href="https://www.euractiv.com/section/agriculture-food/opinion/the-eu-needs-to-speak-up-to-avoid-backdoor-gmos-on-our-plates/">
          article
         </a>
         by Friends of the Earth Europe on the importance of regulating "GM 2.0" and the
         <a href="https://www.euractiv.com/section/agriculture-food/news/eu-seeds-industry-no-sense-to-label-products-from-new-breeding-techniques/">
          report
         </a>
         on how industry aims to try to avoid labeling these new GM foods.
        </p>
        <p>
         Read the
         <a href="http://tacd.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/TACD-Resolution-new-genetic-engineering-techniques_with-appendix_7-September.pdf">
          resolution
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://tacd.org/new-policy-resolution-on-consumer-concerns-about-new-genetic-engineering-techniques/">
          press release
         </a>
         from the Transatlantic Consumer Dialogue (TACD).
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-405258">
         GM Crops and Food
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Court of Justice of the EU (CJEU) has confirmed the right of civil society organisations to submit legal cases concerning the health risks of genetically engineered plants.The case was brought by TestBiotech, following rejection of a complaint by TestBiotech and GeneWatch UK, which raised concerns about assessment of the health impacts of GM crops with altered oil content. Read the
         <a href="https://www.testbiotech.org/en/node/2169">
          TestBiotech press release
         </a>
         and the report in the
         <a href="https://www.courthousenews.com/eu-environmental-challenge-to-monsanto-soybeans-revived/">
          Court House News Service
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Syngenta's RoundUp Ready GA21 maize is the only remaining GM crop in the commercial
         <a href="http://www.genewatch.org/uploads/f03c6d66a9b354535738483c1c3d49e4/20151012_GMP_EU_cultivation_pipeline.pdf">
          pipeline
         </a>
         for EU cultivation that would be suitable for growing in England or Flanders (Belgium): areas of northern European countries which grow maize and have not used the
         <a href="http://ec.europa.eu/food/plant/gmo/authorisation/cultivation/geographical_scope_en.htm">
          opt out
         </a>
         . This crop is awaiting approval by the EU.
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-566699">
         International DNA databases
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Read the report by the Forensic Genetics Policy Initiative:
         <a href="http://dnapolicyinitiative.org/report/">
          Establishing Best Practice for DNA databases
         </a>
         . This report was developed using an innovative consultative approach. The final report reflects input from civil society groups around the world from a human rights perspective.
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-569340">
         A DNA database in the NHS?
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Google subsidiary DeepMind has been given
         <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/health-38055509">
          access to NHS patient data
         </a>
         from millions of Londoners, without patients' knowledge or consent. Read the 2013 GeneWatch UK
         <a href="http://www.genewatch.org/uploads/f03c6d66a9b354535738483c1c3d49e4/DNAinNHS_GWbriefing_fin.pdf">
          report
         </a>
         about how the Government plans to share NHS medical records and genetic information from every patient with companies like Google.
        </p>
        <p>
         For more information on sharing of NHS medical records, visit
         <a href="https://medconfidential.org/">
          Medconfidential
         </a>
         .
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-396520">
         Genes and Marketing
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Read the GeneWatch UK
         <a href="http://www.genewatch.org/uploads/f03c6d66a9b354535738483c1c3d49e4/IVDReg_GWbrief_fin.pdf">
          briefing
         </a>
         about the EU's In Vitro Diagnostics (IVD) Regulation. The new regulation will protect patients and consumers by requiring companies to provide evidence that genetic tests and computer algorithms correctly diagnose or predict disease, before they can be marketed. However the regulations will not come into force until 2022.
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-566959">
         GM fish
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         In the USA, the FDA has approved
         <a href="http://www.fda.gov/NewsEvents/Newsroom/PressAnnouncements/ucm473249.htm">
          GM salmon
         </a>
         for use as food: the first time a GM animal has been allowed into the food chain. The GM salmon eggs will be produced by the company AquaBounty in Canada, shopped to Panama for growing in an on-land facility, and then shipped as dead fish products to the US market, where they are not required to be labelled as GM. Environmentalists are concerned about the impacts on wild salmon if the GM fish escape, including if GM fish eggs are accidentally or deliberately diverted to fish farms elsewhere in the world. The Center for Food Safety has
         <a href="http://www.centerforfoodsafety.org/press-releases/4131/fda-approves-first-genetically-engineered-animal-for-human-consumption-over-the-objections-of-millions">
          stated
         </a>
         it will bring a legal case against the decision.
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/sub-396422">
         GM and Cloned Mammals
        </a>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Guardian
         <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/science/2015/dec/23/uk-couple-await-birth-of-two-clones-of-dead-dog">
          reports
         </a>
         that a UK couple have cloned their dead dog.
        </p>
       </li>
      </ul>
