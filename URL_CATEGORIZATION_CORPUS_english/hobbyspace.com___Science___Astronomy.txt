             <tr align="center" valign="top">
              <td class="textMain">
               <p>
                <span class="header3Text">
                 Blue Origin and Virgin Galactic 
                           Fly Reusable Rockets to Suborbital Space
                </span>
               </p>
              </td>
             </tr>
             <tr align="center" valign="middle">
              <td align="left" class="textMain" valign="top" width="53%">
               <table border="1" bordercolor="#CCCC99" cellpadding="4" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
                <tr align="center" valign="top">
                 <td class="textMain">
                  <table border="0" width="100%">
                   <tr align="left" valign="top">
                    <td class="textMain" colspan="2" height="0">
                     <p>
                      Jeff Bezo's
                      <a href="http://www.blueorigin.com" target="_d">
                       Blue 
                                       Origin
                      </a>
                      company and Richard Branson's
                      <a href="http://www.virgingalactic.com" target="_d">
                       Virgin 
                                       Galactic
                      </a>
                      are in the final stretch of 
                                       a race more than a decade long to be the 
                                       first to provide routine rides to suborbital 
                                       space on rocket powered vehicles.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Blue Origin has now test flown the reusable
                      <a href="https://www.blueorigin.com/technology" target="_d">
                       New 
                                       Shepard
                      </a>
                      vertical takeoff and landing 
                                       vehicle several times. The flights have 
                                       been uncrewed but each one has tested various 
                                       key systems and improvements in the vehicle, 
                                       which includes a liquid hydrogen/liquid 
                                       oxygen powered booster and a crew capsule 
                                       that separates from the booster after the 
                                       engine shuts down and returns for soft landing 
                                       via parachutes. Here is a video of highlights 
                                       of a test flight on April 29, 2018 at Blue 
                                       Origins West Texas facility:
                     </p>
                     <p align="center">
                      .
                      <iframe allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/h6_RvniifL8" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                      .
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      And here is a replay of the entire live 
                                       webcast of the test:
                     </p>
                     <p align="center">
                      .
                      <iframe allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/ZUV53Nn3PhA" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                      .
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      New Shepard reached 107 kilometers (351,000 
                                       feet or 66.5 miles) in altitude. The goal 
                                       is to begin flying company employees by 
                                       the end of 2018 and paying passengers in 
                                       2019. The company already is flying science 
                                       and technology research payloads for paying 
                                       customers.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Meanwhile, Virgin Galactic has been test 
                                       flying
                      <a href="http://www.virgingalactic.com/mission/" target="_d">
                       VSS 
                                       Unity
                      </a>
                      , the second
                      <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SpaceShipTwo" target="_d">
                       SpaceShipTwo
                      </a>
                      vehicle built after the first one was destroyed 
                                       in an
                      <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SpaceShipTwo#October_2014_crash" target="_d">
                       accident 
                                       in 2014
                      </a>
                      . The
                      <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scaled_Composites_White_Knight_Two" target="_d">
                       WhiteKnightTwo
                      </a>
                      aircraft carries Unity to about 15 kilometers 
                                       (50,000 ft) and then releases it. Unity 
                                       fires its hybrid rocket motor, powered with 
                                       liquid nitrous oxide and solid fuel. After 
                                       reaching apogee, the wings of the SS2 fold 
                                       upwards putting the vehicle into a V configuration 
                                       that allows for high stability during reentry 
                                       into the atmosphere (designer Burt Rutan 
                                       called this "feathering" after 
                                       the feathers in a badmitton birdie). :
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Here is a video of highlights of the second 
                                       flight in which Unity's motor was fired. 
                                       (Previous to the powered tests, Unity did 
                                       a series of drop glide flights that tested 
                                       various systems.) The vehicle with two pilots 
                                       reached 34.9 km (114,500 feet) and a speed 
                                       of Mach 1.9 before gliding back to the Mojave 
                                       Air and Space Port for a safe landing.
                     </p>
                     <p align="center">
                      .
                      <iframe allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/AIzx6X3ub2I" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                      .
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      And here Branson talks about the significance 
                                       of the test and about preparation for riding 
                                       himself on one of the early flights to space:
                     </p>
                     <p align="center">
                      .
                      <iframe allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Eq54aOx6gwU" width="560">
                      </iframe>
                      .
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Branson talks of Unity reaching space (reportedly 
                                       the company uses 80 km as the
                      <a href="https://hobbyspace.com/Rocketry/Advanced/records.html#SpaceBounds" target="_d">
                       borderline 
                                       to space
                      </a>
                      rather than the more common 
                                       100 km) by the end of 2018. Commercial rides 
                                       would begin in 2019. Virgin has over 700 
                                       customers who have put down deposits on 
                                       the $250,000 price for tickets to ride on 
                                       Unity. Two other SpaceShipTwo vehicles are 
                                       under construction to provide for weekly 
                                       flights from
                      <a href="http://www.spaceportAmerica.com" target="_d">
                       Spaceport 
                                       America
                      </a>
                      in New Mexico.
                     </p>
                     <p align="center">
                      ===
                     </p>

                      Follow the latest developments in this 
                                       NewSpace era with a subscription to
                      <a href="http://www.newspacewatch.com" target="_d">
                       NewSpace 
                                       Watch
                      </a>
                      .
                     </p>
