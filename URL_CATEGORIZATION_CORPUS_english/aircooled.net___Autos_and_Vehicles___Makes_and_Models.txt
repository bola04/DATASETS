               . We even have a nice
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Moresa-Piston-Cylinder-Set-85-5mm-x-69mm-Cast-p/311-198-069fd.htm" target="_blank">
                Mexican 85.5mm P&amp;C
               </a>
               set if your purse strings are tight!
              </p>
              <p>
               With that out of the way, know that most MPG engines use a heavy pulley and flywheel, but we believe in going the other way. Heavy flywheels take power to speed up, we want to speed up the car not the flywheel! So we are going to use a lightened flywheel. This engine uses this
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Forged-Lightened-Flywheel-FORGED-13lb-8-Dowel-p/light-flywheel-ec.htm" target="_blank">
                13lb 8 Doweled Flywheel,
               </a>
               you simply do not need a more expensive or stronger flywheel for THIS engine. You can also use any other
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/DPR-Chrome-Vanadium-200mm-Lightened-Flywheel-p/dpr-light-type-1-flywheel.htm" target="_blank">
                8 doweled flywheel
               </a>
               (because the cranks above are 8 doweled) a 10lb or a
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/DPR-Chrome-Vanadium-200mm-Stock-Weight-Flywheel-p/dpr-8-dowel-type-1-flywheel.htm" target="_blank">
                8 Doweled 18lb Stock Weight Flywheel
               </a>
               .
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Chromoly-Gland-Nut-Washer-Economy-36mm-Head-p/031-2236.htm" target="_blank">
                Chromoly Gland Nut,
               </a>
               and run a stock clutch, nothing fancy.
              </p>
              <p>
               You‚Äôll find that the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/4140-Forged-Chromoly-76mm-Stroker-Crankshaft-p/002-4176vw.htm" target="_blank">
                76mm crank
               </a>
               and rods, ‚Äúhits‚Äù the case. Don‚Äôt freak out! Being the adventurous type, we just have to think a little bit and work through our minor problems in our engine adventure. The clearancing is so minor, you can do this in your garage, or even the back patio (if you time it so the wife is gone! üòâ ).
              </p>
              <p>
               Here is a photograph of a case half that has been clearanced for a 76mm crank and rods. You will find similar when you put bearings on the your crankshaft and set it in place in the case to check, it WILL HIT in these spots. Make SURE that when you are doing this you push the crankshaft all the way toward the flywheel. When the flywheel is on it will pull the crank this way, if you have the flywheel off the crank can ‚Äúfloat‚Äù to a position it will never be run in, and you‚Äôll be clearancing places you don‚Äôt need to, and will end up clearancing twice as a result of your error!
              </p>
              <p>
               Simply mark the spots that hit with a sharpie and take the crank back out. Make sure the crankshaft is pushed all the way toward the flywheel. When the flywheel is bolted up it will hold it in this location,¬† you do not want to clearance the wrong places because the crankshaft floated toward the pulley (where it isn‚Äôt when the engine is assembled and running)! Now, how you clearance the case is up to you. You can use a Dremel but that will take some time because the Dremel is puny. The easy way to do this clearancing job is to grab a hand held grinder with a 4 or 5‚Ä≥ wheel, and work over the areas that you identified with the marker. Don‚Äôt worry about making it pretty YET, just lay the metal on the case back until the crankshaft and connecting rods clear. You‚Äôll probably have the crankshaft in and out 10 times. Remember while you do this to wear safety glasses because of the flying chips, AND also remember that you are grinding on a magnesium case. Magnesium is extremely flammable, so make sure you keep the grinder moving, don‚Äôt work on one spot for too long, it will get too hot.
              </p>
              <p>
               Once the crank is rotating in the case without issue, do the other case half. Once that one is done put the connecting rods on the crank, and lay it back into the case. The trick here is to only clearance what you have to. When the engine is actually assembled, the small end of the connecting rod will be held ‚ÄúCentered‚Äù by the piston and cylinder. But we don‚Äôt have that, and if we did we couldn‚Äôt see, so now what? Here‚Äôs our trick!
              </p>
              <p>
               RUBBER BANDS: Put these over the outsides of the 4 head studs, but slipped through the small end of the connecting rod. This trick will center the small end of the rod while you slowly rotate the crankshaft to check for rod/case contact. You need .030‚Ä≥ of clearance. Check and grind until it rotates without interference. If it hits on 1 cylinder, you can assume it‚Äôs going to hit on all 4, so clearance all 4 the same way in the same relative locations. When it clears smooth out the sharp edges from your work, and that the case had before you got it! You don‚Äôt have to clean it yet, we are still in the ‚ÄúMock Up‚Äù stage.
              </p>
              <p>
               Now put the P&amp;Cs on (you don‚Äôt need the rings for this part of the job, and measure your deck height. You want your deck height to be between .040-.070‚Ä≥. You‚Äôll need
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Barrel-Shims-90-5-92mm-Type-1-p/90.5-92mm-barrel-shims.htm" target="_blank">
                85.5mm Cylinder Base Shims
               </a>
               for the 85.5mm and
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Piston-Cylinder-Set-88mm-x-69mm-Slip-In-p/vw8800t1k.htm" target="_blank">
                88mm Slip In P&amp;Cs
               </a>
               . selecting the thickness that gets our deck height in our .040-.070‚Ä≥ range. If you don‚Äôt have a deck height tool, you can use this
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Deck-Height-Measuring-Tool-Deluxe-p/deck-height-tool.htm" target="_blank">
                Deck Height Measuring Tool
               </a>
               . You can use a dial indicator or calipers if you are careful too! Remember, you want a deck height of .060-.070‚Ä≥. It will likely be smaller than this when you mock it up, do some quick math and figure out which set of
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/1600cc-Barrel-Shims-1300-1600cc-Set-of-4-p/1600cc-barrel-shims.htm" target="_blank">
                85.5mm Cylinder Base Shims
               </a>
               you need to put between the case and cylinders to get there. Smaller deck heights are better if you have a choice.
              </p>
              <p>
               Next, do some quick math to figure your compression ratio out, we are going to build this engine at a target CR of 9:1 for a really efficient use of the fuel! For example, a 1745cc engine with .060‚Ä≥ deck height needs 46cc combustion chambers in the heads, as one combo to achieve 9:1 Compression Ratio. If we have .070‚Ä≥ deck height, we need 44.5cc in the heads for the same CR. We will make the heads match the engine and deck height, since we are doing the heads last. Your goal is to just get the engine‚Äôs deck height between .040-.070‚Ä≥. Get the deck height within range, then we will make the heads have the chamber volume we need to get the CR dialed in.
              </p>
              <p>
               Now you can take the engine back apart and drop the
              </p>
              <p>
               Crankshaft
               <br/>
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Crankshaft-Install-Kit-With-Drive-Pinion-Type-1-p/t1-crank-install-kit-w-pinion.htm" target="_blank">
                Crank Gears, Keys, Spacer, Snap Ring
               </a>
               <br/>
               Connecting Rods
               <br/>
               Flywheel
               <br/>
               Pressure Plate
               <br/>
               Crank Pulley
               <br/>
               Pistons
              </p>
              <p>
               Off at the balance shop. We need this thing to PURR, vibration is wasted energy. This is money well spent! Remember at $5/gallon the paybacks will be swift. When your parts come back, clean everything and start final assembly. Use
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Curil-T-Engine-Sealant-75ml-Tube-p/curil-t.htm" target="_blank">
                Curil-T
               </a>
               for the case sealant, and
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/DIRKO-RTV-Sealant-Silicone-p/dirko.htm" target="_blank">
                Dirko
               </a>
               for the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/1600cc-Barrel-Shims-1300-1600cc-Set-of-4-p/1600cc-barrel-shims.htm" target="_blank">
                cylinder base shims
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               For our camshaft, there are not a lot of good choices for a MPG cam. We feel that most cams are designed for ‚Äúpower‚Äù instead of MPG. The biggest issues we have are that most aftermarket cams have too much valve lift to be ‚Äúefficient‚Äù. Lift is for power, NOT MPG. We want a gentle cam, with a very mild lift, so we can use soft valve springs. This reduces friction, and is super easy on parts. Fortunately, CB Performance has just the cam we want! We have chosen the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Eagle-Type-1-Camshaft-2231-Grind-CB2231-p/cb2231.htm" target="_blank">
                CB2231 Camshaft
               </a>
               , it has the duration we want, and has very moderate valve lift. Basically ANY really mild cam will work fine.
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/SCAT-C20-Type-1-Camshaft-1-1-or-1-25-Rockers-20003-p/20003.htm" target="_blank">
                SCAT C20 Camshaft
               </a>
               is also a candidate. You could also use the CB 2229, 2230, or
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Eagle-Type-1-Camshaft-Cheater-Cam-Grind-CB2280-p/cb2280.htm" target="_blank">
                2280 ‚ÄúCheater Cam‚Äù.
               </a>
               Another great grind is the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Engle-W90-Type-1-Camshaft-1-1-or-1-25-Rockers-p/e6090.htm" target="_blank">
                Engle W-90,
               </a>
               which is a grind lower than the W-100. The
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Eagle-Type-1-Camshaft-Cheater-Cam-Grind-CB2280-p/cb2280.htm" target="_blank">
                CB 2280 Cheater Cam
               </a>
               is what was used in the CB Mileage Motor engine article in HVWs (which we had never read until this article was half written).
              </p>
              <p>
               The use of these cams lets us use
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Valve-Spring-1200-1600cc-Engines-113-109-623C-p/113-109-623c.htm" target="_blank">
                stock valve springs
               </a>

               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Chromoly-Valve-Spring-Retainers-Set-of-8-p/chromoly-retainers-vw-set-8.htm" target="_blank">
                Bugpack Chromoly Retainers

               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Chromoly-Valve-Spring-Retainers-Economy-Set-of-8-p/ac109463wb.htm" target="_blank">
                Economy Chromoly Retainer Set
               </a>
               for those with a really tight budget!
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/SCAT-Swivel-Ball-Style-Valve-Adjusters-20118-p/20118.htm" target="_blank">
                Courier Valve Adjusters
               </a>
               or
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Elephant-s-Feet-Valve-Adjusters-8-p/adj-elephants-ft-8.htm" target="_blank">
                Elephant‚Äôs Feet
               </a>
               are also recommended but not required, you can use
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/8mm-Valve-Adjusting-Screw-Each-043-109-451-p/043-109-451.htm" target="_blank">
                stock adjusters
               </a>
               , even
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Hardened-Lash-Caps-for-8mm-and-9mm-Valves-8-p/lash-caps-8mm.htm" target="_blank">
                lash caps
               </a>
               are optional because of our conservative valve train! The only reason we can do this is we are only lifting .330‚Ä≥ at the valve, so we can also use stock push rods! The Gentle Valve Train also allows us to use
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/CB-Performance-Super-Stock-Rockers-1-1-1-Ratio-p/cb1684.htm" target="_blank">
                1.1:1 Ratio Stock Rocker Arms,
               </a>
               or retain your stock arms and add
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/SCAT-Bolt-On-Rocker-Shaft-Kit-20129-p/20129.htm" target="_blank">

               </a>
               which are completely optional and certainly not necessary for a mild build like this. You must get it out of your head that you need ‚Äúmore valve lift‚Äù, so forget 1.25 or 1.4/1.5 ratio rockers.
              </p>
              <p>
               You can also either use a
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/ACN-Billet-Straight-Cut-Cam-Gears-Steel-p/acn-straight-cuts.htm" target="_blank">

If you don‚Äôt want every last .2mpg, don‚Äôt worry about these cam gear selections, just use a stock or basic bolt-on cam gear, it‚Äôs really not a big deal.
              </p>
              <p>
               For our oiling system, use
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Schadek-21mm-Oil-Pump-8mm-Pump-Studs-111-115-107-p/111-115-107akbr.htm" target="_blank">

               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Aircooled-Net-Full-Flow-Filter-Kit-VW-Type-1-p/full-flow-kit-t1.htm" target="_blank">
                Full Flow Filter Kit for Type 1
               </a>
               is always a good idea, to keep the dirt out and keep this thing running for years with minimal wear.
              </p>
              <p>
               For cylinder heads we are using our
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/ACN-Stocker-Plus-Dual-Port-Cylinder-Heads-35-X-32-p/l3-heads-pair.htm" target="_blank">

               </a>

               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Chinese-Fuel-Injection-Dual-Port-Cylinder-Head-p/043-101-355ck.htm" target="_blank">
                Stock Cylinder Heads,
               </a>
               don‚Äôt be afraid to take them apart and give a quick ‚Äúfluff and buff‚Äù with a die grinder and sanding wheel. Just smooth the edges up. Clean it, then re-assemble and lube. A good valve job is a wise move, if you can‚Äôt do it bring it to a head guy or machinist that can!
              </p>
              <p>
               Single Port Head Owners: follow the same cam guidelines above, knowing you will be running either the stock carburetor, or dual 1bbl carburetors. Single ports are great torquey reliable engines, and they will drive like a different engine if you put a better flowing exhaust, and a set of dual 1bbl carburetors on there.
              </p>
              <p>
               For the exhaust system, in previous articles you had a lot of options, but for this engine you do not. Because we are going for efficiency, we MUST have a
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/VW-Sedan-Bus-Street-Header-1966-74-1300-1600cc-p/street-header-t1.htm" target="_blank">

               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Single-Quiet-Pack-Muffler-for-2031-Beetle-Bus-Ghia-p/3122-55-3122.htm" target="_blank">
                Single Quiet Pack Muffler
               </a>
               , or our personal favorite the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Hide-Out-Muffler-Hideaway-Type-1-Street-Headers-p/street-hideaway.htm" target="_blank">
                Hide-a-Way Muffler.
               </a>
               HOWEVER, if you are going to use a center mount carburetor (stock, or Progressive), you must get our
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Pre-Heat-Modification-to-VW-Headers-Labor-p/exhaust-preheat-modification.htm" target="_blank">

               </a>
               to the header. We need as much heat to the intake manifold as we can get. By doing this we‚Äôll be able to jet the carburetor LEAN, knowing that all liquid fuel is vaporized, and the cylinders won‚Äôt know they are lean! It will actually burn it instead of unburned fuel making it‚Äôs way to combust in the exhaust system. Heat on the intake is your friend! If you have dual carburetors, the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Pre-Heat-Modification-to-VW-Headers-Labor-p/exhaust-preheat-modification.htm" target="_blank">
                Pre-Heat Modification
               </a>
               m modification is not needed on the
               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/VW-Sedan-Bus-Street-Header-1966-74-1300-1600cc-p/street-header-t1.htm" target="_blank">

               <a href="//vwparts.aircooled.net/Dual-Weber-ICT-Carb-Kit-All-Engines-Redline-p/redline-dual-ict.htm" target="_blank">
