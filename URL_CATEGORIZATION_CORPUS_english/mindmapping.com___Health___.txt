           <h1 class="heading main-heading">
            Mind Mapping
           </h1>
           <p class="mb50">
            This page is meant to inform you about Mind Mapping and provide ideas that can help you create and benefit from Mind Maps
           </p>
           <h2 class="heading secondary-heading">
            What is Mind Mapping?
           </h2>
           <hr/>
           <img alt="mind mapping" class="img mb30" src="img/maps_en.jpg"/>
           <p>
            Mind mapping is a highly effective way of getting information in and out of your brain. Mind mapping is a creative and logical means of note-taking and note-making that literally "maps out" your ideas.
           </p>
           <p>
            All Mind Maps have some things in common. They have a natural organizational structure that radiates from the center and use lines, symbols, words, color and images according to simple, brain-friendly concepts. Mind mapping converts a long list of monotonous information into a colorful, memorable and highly organized diagram that works in line with your brain's natural way of doing things.
           </p>
           <p>
            One simple way to understand a Mind Map is by comparing it to a map of a city. The city center represents the main idea; the main roads leading from the center represent the key thoughts in your thinking process; the secondary roads or branches represent your secondary thoughts, and so on. Special images or shapes can represent landmarks of interest or particularly relevant ideas.
           </p>
           <p>
            The great thing about mind mapping is that you can put your ideas down in any order, as soon as they pop into your head. You are not constrained by thinking in order. Simply, throw out any and all ideas, then worry about reorganizing them later.
           </p>
           <p>
            There are many great
            <a href="https://www.matchware.com/mind-mapping-software" target="_blank">
             mind mapping software
            </a>
            programs out there to help you organize your thoughts and then automatically export them to an easy-to-read, ordered list.
           </p>
           <p>
            While it is absolutely possible to mind map the old-fashioned way with a pen and paper, why not take advantage of the technology age and save yourself some much needed time?
           </p>
           <p class="mb50">
            The
            <a href="mind-map.php">
             mind map
            </a>
            is the external mirror of your own radiant or natural thinking facilitated by a powerful graphic process, which provides the universal key to unlock the dynamic potential of the brain.
           </p>
           <h3 class="heading secondary-heading">
            The Five Essential Characteristics of Mind Mapping:
           </h3>
           <hr/>
           <div class="row">
            <div class="col-md-8">
             <ul class="image-list">
              <li>
               The main idea, subject or focus is crystallized in a central image
              </li>
              <li>
               The main themes radiate from the central image as 'branches'
              </li>
              <li>
               The branches comprise a key image or key word drawn or printed on its associated line
              </li>
              <li>
               Topics of lesser importance are represented as 'twigs' of the relevant branch
              </li>
              <li>
               The branches form a connected nodal structure
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="col-md-4">
             <img alt="mind mapping" class="img" src="img/mind-mapping-branches.png"/>
            </div>
           </div>
           <header>
            <h3 class="heading secondary-heading">
             How to Make a Mind Map
            </h3>
           </header>
           <hr/>
           <ul class="big-bullets mb50">
            <li>
             <span>
              Think of your general main theme and write that down in the center of the page. i.e. Food
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span>
              Figure out sub-themes of your main concept and draw branches to them from the center, beginning to look like a spider web i.e. Meats, Dairy, Breads
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span>
              Make sure to use very short phrases or even single words
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span>
              Add images to invoke thought or get the message across better
             </span>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span>
              Try to think of at least two main points for each sub-theme you created and create branches out to those
             </span>
            </li>
           </ul>
