           <div class="field-content">
            <p>
             Medicine Doesn’t Work If Patients Don’t Take It
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="views-field views-field-field-issue-blurb">
           <div class="field-content">
            <p>
             And yet, nonadherence not only persists, but the costs of more effective medications makes the problem worse. As our cover story notes,
             <a href="https://www.managedcaremag.com/archives/2018/6/when-cost-medications-keeps-patients-taking-them">
              nonadherence impairs and even shortens life
             </a>
             , and that’s toxic for a health system that has trouble controlling costs as it is.
            </p>
            <p>
             Mainly, though, this issue is about managing diabetes. Our story looks at how
             <a href="https://www.managedcaremag.com/archives/2018/6/nevada-pushes-transparency-diabetes-drug-prices">
              Nevadan lawmakers are pushing a bill that requires more transparency from drugmakers
             </a>
             . As other states mull similar action, pharma companies push back.
            </p>
            <p>
             We  also look at
             <a href="https://www.managedcaremag.com/archives/2018/6/blood-sugar-control-limbo-how-low-go">
              new guidelines from the American College of Physicians that recommend less-aggressive blood sugar targets
             </a>
             . Other professional groups think that’s a terrible idea.
            </p>
            <p>
             Meanwhile, researchers have proposed a new taxonomy that
             <a href="https://www.managedcaremag.com/archives/2018/6/dividing-diabetes-cluster-instead-types">
              divides diabetes into clusters instead of types
             </a>
             .
            </p>
           </div>
