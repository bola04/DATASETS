          <a href="http://www.pplp.org/2018/05/04/june-6th-hoot-featuring-kate-rushin-and-christopher-clauss/" rel="bookmark">
           June 6th Hoot featuring January G. O’Neil and Patrice Pinette
          </a>
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p id="June-6">
          <strong>
           January Gill O’Neill and Patrice Pinette
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-546 alignleft" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 200px) 100vw, 200px" src="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/January-ONeil-Photo-Credit-Rachel-Eliza-Griffiths-200x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/January-ONeil-Photo-Credit-Rachel-Eliza-Griffiths-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/January-ONeil-Photo-Credit-Rachel-Eliza-Griffiths.jpg 427w" width="200"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          January Gill O’Neil is the author of two poetry collections,
          <i>
           Misery Islands
          </i>
          and
          <i>
           Underlife
          </i>
          , published by CavanKerry Press. A third collection,
          <i>
           Rewilding
          </i>
          , will be published by CavanKerry Press in fall 2018. She is the executive director of the Massachusetts Poetry Festival, an assistant professor of English at Salem State University, and board of trustee member with the Association of Writers and Writing Programs (AWP) and Montserrat College of Art. A Cave Canem fellow, January’s poems and articles have appeared in the Academy of American Poet’s Poem-A-Day series, American Life in Poetry, Garrison Keillor’s The Writer’s Almanac,
          <i>
           American Poetry Review, New England Review
          </i>
          , and
          <i>
           Ploughshare
          </i>
          s, among others.
         </p>
         <p>
          <i>
           Misery Islands
          </i>
          was selected for a 2015 Paterson Award for Literary Excellence. Additionally, Mass Center for the Book chose
          <i>
           Misery Islands
          </i>
          as a Must-Read Book for 2015 and it won the 2015 Massachusetts Book Award.
         </p>
         <p>
          Previously, January was a senior writer/editor at Babson College. She earned her BA from Old Dominion University and her MFA at New York University. She lives with her two children in Beverly, Massachusetts.
         </p>

           <strong>
            <span class="il">
             Patrice
            </span>
            Pinette

           is inspired by alchemy between the arts. She collaborates with artists and musicians in workshops, readings, and exhibits. She teaches creative writing, literature, and eurythmy, which embodies the dance of language, in Waldorf high schools, CFA’s Renewal Courses, and at Antioch University New England in the Healing Arts in Education program.

         <h2 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">

           May 2nd Hoot featuring Suzanne Rancourt, Cheryl Savageau, and Marie Francis
          </a>
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-417 alignleft" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 253px) 100vw, 253px" src="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/cropped-EagleVoicephoto-2_56_04-PM-253x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/cropped-EagleVoicephoto-2_56_04-PM-253x300.jpg 253w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/cropped-EagleVoicephoto-2_56_04-PM-768x909.jpg 768w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/cropped-EagleVoicephoto-2_56_04-PM-865x1024.jpg 865w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/cropped-EagleVoicephoto-2_56_04-PM.jpg 1017w" width="253"/>
          <strong>
           Ms. Rancourt
          </strong>
          is Abenaki/Huron descent, born and raised in the mountains of West Central Maine currently residing in the Adirondack Mountains, NY. A multi-modal artist, she has work appearing, in
          <i>
           Dawnland Voices 2.0 #4
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Northern New England Review
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Bear Review
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Three Drops Press
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Snapdragon Journal, mgversion2&gt;datura
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Sirsee, Slipstream
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Muddy River Poetry Review
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Ginosko, Journal of Military Experience, Cimarron Review
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           Callaloo
          </i>
          , numerous anthologies, translations, and text books.
          <i>
          </i>
          Her book,
          <b>
           Billboard in the Clouds
          </b>
          was the winner of the Native Writers’ Circle of the Americas First Book Award.  Ms. Rancourt is a USMC and USA veteran.
          <br/>
          <img alt="" class=" wp-image-494 alignright" height="291" sizes="(max-width: 194px) 100vw, 194px" src="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/Cheryl-3-1-200x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/Cheryl-3-1-200x300.jpg 200w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/Cheryl-3-1-768x1151.jpg 768w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/Cheryl-3-1-683x1024.jpg 683w" width="194"/>
          <b>
           Cheryl Savageau
          </b>
          is the author of three collections of poetry,
          <i>
           Mother/Land, Dirt Road Home,
          </i>
          which was a finalist for the Paterson Poetry Prize and nominated for a Pulitzer Prize, and
          <i>
           Home Country
          </i>
          .  She has won Fellowships in Poetry from the National Endowment for the Arts and the Massachusetts Artists Fellowship Program, and has been twice nominated for Pushcart Prizes.  Her children’s book,
          <i>
           Muskrat Will Be Swimming
          </i>
          , was a Smithsonian Notable Book and won the Skipping Stones Award.  She currently edits the online journal
          <i>
           Dawnland Voices 2.0
          </i>
          and teaches writing workshops.  Her memoir,
          <i>
           Out of the Crazywoods,
          </i>
          is forthcoming in 2018.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-401 alignleft" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/FB_IMG_1475812385961-300x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/FB_IMG_1475812385961-300x300.jpg 300w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/FB_IMG_1475812385961-150x150.jpg 150w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/FB_IMG_1475812385961.jpg 692w" width="300"/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Marie N. Francis
          </strong>
          is a full-blood Passamaquoddy. The youngest of five children, Marie was raised on the Pleasant Point reservation. She was born with eye and heart problems as well as dyslexia. She struggled in school for years while her mother fought to get appropriate academic support for her. These struggles did not affect her willingness to try, though. Her siblings describe her as “always willing to try or help or stand up to bullies.” Marie attended Landmark School, lettered in soccer, participated in drama, and created the peer leadership program that is still going strong today. Marie has spent the last 20 years working for the City of Portland, where she has participated in numerous trainings, including Nonviolent De-escalation and Google Guide. Outside of work, Marie has participated in many sports including bowling (where she was nationally ranked) and professional arm wrestling. She has run sound boards, organized benefit concerts, tried out for Fear Factor, and participated in The Equinox Petroglyph Project. Marie is a published poet and photographer. Her work can be found in Dawnland Voices, both in the anthology and volume 2 of the magazine. Her photography work can be found on display in various venues in Portland, Maine.

        <header class="entry-header">
         <h2 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          <a href="http://www.pplp.org/2018/03/22/20th-anniversary-poetry-gala/" rel="bookmark">
           20th Anniversary Poetry Gala!
          </a>
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.3sarts.org/performances/performance/PPLP:-Poetry-Gala?performanceid=2928">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-608" height="602" sizes="(max-width: 365px) 100vw, 365px" src="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Copy-of-Poetry-Gala-poster3-2-621x1024.jpg" srcset="http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Copy-of-Poetry-Gala-poster3-2-621x1024.jpg 621w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Copy-of-Poetry-Gala-poster3-2-182x300.jpg 182w, http://www.pplp.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Copy-of-Poetry-Gala-poster3-2-768x1266.jpg 768w" width="365"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Time flies when you’re building community, especially in a community as wonderful as Portsmouth.
         </p>
         <p>
          We hope you will join is in celebrating with an evening of poetry, music, prizes and dancing to mark the 20-year anniversary of the Portsmouth Poet Laureate Program (PPLP) April 13 at 3S ArtSpace.
         </p>
         <p>
          The event, emceed by current Portsmouth Poet Laureate Mike Nelson, will feature readings by many of PPLP’s past laureates and New Hampshire Poet Laureate Alice Fogel, as well as poetry activities, raffle drawings and hors d’oeuvres with a cash bar.
         </p>
         <p>
          The evening will conclude with a live-music dance party. “We really see this as an opportunity for the whole Portsmouth and Seacoast arts community to get together and celebrate, and we hope to see many of our friends from other local arts organizations come and enjoy food, drink and camaraderie,” said Marina Buckler, co-chair of the PPLP Board of Trustees.
         </p>
         <p>
          Portsmouth resident Nancy Moore Hill and a group of poets and friends founded PPLP–as the organization is commonly known–in 1998, with a mission to build community through poetry. Every two years, the group appoints a new Portsmouth Poet Laureate who receives a stipend and support for a new, community-building project.
         </p>
         <p>
          “PPLP is one of the oldest municipal laureate programs in the country that actually provides a stipend and support for the laureate,” said Bill Burtis, co-chair of the PPLP Board of Trustees.
         </p>
         <p>
          Music and hors d’oeuvres start at 6 p.m., with poetry and performances starting at 7.
         </p>
         <p>
          In addition to raffle prizes, silent auctions will be held for rare poetry books signed by the authors.
         </p>
         <p>
          Reserve your spot at the Gala through
          <a href="http://www.3sarts.org/performances/performance/PPLP:-Poetry-Gala?performanceid=2928">
           the event page
          </a>
          .
         </p>
