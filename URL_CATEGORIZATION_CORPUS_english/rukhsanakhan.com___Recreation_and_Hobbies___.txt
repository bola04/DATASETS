      <span class="text_normal">
       My name is Rukhsana Khan (pronounced
       <a href="http://www.teachingbooks.net/pronounce.cgi?aid=1539">
        ruk-SA-na 
 		kon
       </a>
       you can hear me say it at this link) and I'm a children's author and storyteller.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       My books have won a number of awards, one of them was even chosen by the 
 		New York Public library as one of the
       <a href="http://www.nypl.org/childrens100" target="_blank">
        100 Greatest 
 		Children's Books in the Last 100 Years!
       </a>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I visit around eighty 
 		schools a year, all over Canada, the U.S. and internationally, and I meet thousands of 
 		students from kindergarten to high school.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       People say I have a down-to-earth style of presentation.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       My presentations go from light-hearted fun for primary children to 
 		serious issues like teen suicide, loss, abandonment and child 
 		refugees. But even my more serious presentations are laced with humour 
 		and are age appropriate.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Similarly, my books run the range from
       <em>
        <a href="books/sillychicken.html">
         Silly Chickens
        </a>
       </em>
       and
       <a href="books/bigredlollipop.html">
        <em>
         Big Red 
 		Lollipops
        </em>
       </a>
       to a motherless girl abandoned in a marketplace in Kabul 
 		(
       <a href="books/wantingmor.html">
        <em>
         Wanting Mor
        </em>
       </a>
       ). My stories have all depended on what demanded to be 
 		written at the time I was writing them.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       You can get a taste of my presentation style from the youtube clips in 
 		the
       <a href="about/videos.html">
        Videos Section
       </a>
       .
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I am available for
       <a href="http://www.rukhsanakhan.com/presentations/skypepresentations.html">
        Skype
       </a>
       visits. Please 
 		email me: rukhsana @ rukhsanakhan.com for more information and rates.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       You can check out my
       <a href="http://www.blog.rukhsanakhan.com">
        blog
       </a>
       for my thoughts on life 
 		and writing.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       You can read the
       <a href="about/testimonials.html">
        testimonials
       </a>
       to find out what others have said about 
 		me.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       You can check out some of my television appearances, radio interviews, conferences I've presented at and  
 		profiles of me both online and in print here:
       <a href="http://www.rukhsanakhan.com/about/profiles-appearances.html">
        profiles &amp; appearances
       </a>
       .
       <br/>
       <br/>
       And you can see some of the places my work has taken me in my
       <a href="about/photogallery.html">
        photo 
 		gallery
       </a>
       .
       <br/>
       <br/>
       When I was a kid growing up in the small town of Dundas, Ontario, I 
 		always dreamed of being an author. I feel blessed that my dreams have come true.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       And now, at a time when there is so 
 		much conflict in the world, I hope my stories help create some 
 		cross-cultural understanding.
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </span>
