             Apple have been reprimanded by Australia's Federal Court over
             <a href="https://www.buzzfeed.com/joshtaylor/apple-fined-9-million-for-error-53-on-iphones-and-ipads?utm_term=.kcvaKXB6y#.doXpNj2qy" target="_blank">
              illegally denying warranties
             </a>
             .
             <i>
              Apple was taken to court by the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) after customers were unable to use their iPhones or iPads following an update, and were shown an 'error 53' warning.  When the customers took their devices to Apple, the company's staff told at least 275 customers in Australia from 2015 to 2016 that the devices were no longer eligible to be repaired or replaced because the device had previously been repaired by a non-Apple repairer.  ACCC commissioner Sarah Court said in a statement that this was in breach of Australian consumer law.
             </i>
             If this happened to you, look into it, you might get a refund.

             <br/>
             Gamers are up in arms over the discovery of a widespread tracking program
             <a href="https://www.pcgamesn.com/red-shell-spyware" target="_blank">
              called "Red Shell"
             </a>
             , included in lots of games.
             <i>
              The information collected includes operating system, browser version number, IP address (anonymized through one-way hashing), screen resolution, in-game user id, and font profiles. Red Shell promises that only device information is collected on your computer, with nothing personally identifiable included in their tracking.
             </i>
             Discussion and more info in
             <a href="https://forums.overclockers.com.au/threads/spyware-red-shell-found-in-lots-of-pc-games.1247239/" target="_blank">
              this thread
             </a>
             .
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Fifteen more websites are to be
             <a href="https://www.smh.com.au/business/companies/internet-providers-ordered-to-block-15-torrent-streaming-sites-by-federal-court-20180618-p4zm6b.html" target="_blank">
              blocked in Australia
             </a>
             , thanks Axe.
             <i>
              On Tuesday, Federal Court justice John Nicholas agreed, ordering the sites be blocked for a period of three years, after which Foxtel can apply to have the orders extended.  ISPs must take "reasonable steps to disable access" to the list of websites within 15 business days and redirect internet users to a page advising the site has been blocked because it facilitates copyright infringement.  Foxtel will pay the compliance costs of blocking the websites, calculated at $50 per domain name.  In his judgment, Justice Nicholas said each website appeared to be principally designed to provide copyrighted material free of charge, demonstrating a "flagrant disregard by the operators of the rights of copyright owners".
             </i>

              WHO said classifying gaming disorder as a separate addiction will help governments, families and health care workers be more vigilant and prepared to identify the risks. The agency and other experts were quick to note that cases of the condition are still very rare, with no more than up to 3 per cent of all gamers believed to be affected.
