                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://musingsfortheopenminded.blogspot.com/2016/10/exposed-brother-of-hillarys-campaign.html">
                       EXPOSED: Brother of Hillary's Campaign Chairman is Registered Foreign Agent of Saudi Government
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1494250271115333585" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <br/>
                      SOURCE:
                      <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/medea-benjamin/hillary-clinton-the-podes_b_11779826.html" target="_blank">
                       Huffington Post
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <img class="image__src" src="http://i.huffpost.com/gen/4638116/images/n-HILLARY-CLINTON-628x314.jpg"/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="image__credit js-image-credit" style="width: 628px;">
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       JOSH EDELSON via Getty Images
                      </div>
                      <iframe style="display: none;">
                      </iframe>
                      <div class="share-bar-highlight js-highlight hidden" style="left: 277.22px; top: -75px;">
                       <a class="share-bar-highlight__twitter js-highlight-share-button" data-beacon='{"p":{"lnid":"twitter","mnid":"entry_text","plid":"http://www.huffingtonpost.com/medea-benjamin/hillary-clinton-the-podes_b_11779826.html"}}' data-type="twitter" href="https://www.blogger.com/null">
                       </a>
                       <a class="share-bar-highlight__facebook js-highlight-share-button" data-beacon='{"p":{"lnid":"facebook","mnid":"entry_text","plid":"http://www.huffingtonpost.com/medea-benjamin/hillary-clinton-the-podes_b_11779826.html"}}' data-type="facebook" href="https://www.blogger.com/null">
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div id="photo-strip">
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       If I told you that Democratic Party lobbyist Tony Podesta, whose brother John Podesta chairs Hillary Clinton’s presidential campaign, is a
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":1,"plid":"exclusive-hillary-clinton-campaign-bundler-is-directly-lobbying-for-saudi-arabia/"}}' href="http://dailycaller.com/2016/04/20/exclusive-hillary-clinton-campaign-bundler-is-directly-lobbying-for-saudi-arabia/" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         registered foreign agent
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       on the
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":2,"plid":"saudi-arabia-continues-hire-politically-connected-american-lobbyists-public-relation-firms/"}}' href="https://theintercept.com/2015/10/05/saudi-arabia-continues-hire-politically-connected-american-lobbyists-public-relation-firms/" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         Saudi government’s
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       payroll, you’d probably think I was a Trump-thumping, conspiratorial nutcase. But it’s true.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       The lobby firm created by both Tony and John Podesta in 1988 receives
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":3,"plid":"http://www.alternet.org/election-2016/podesta-group-pay-play"}}' href="http://www.alternet.org/election-2016/podesta-group-pay-play" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         $140,000 a month
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       from the Saudi government, a government that beheads nonviolent dissidents, uses torture to extract forced confessions, doesn’t allow women to drive, and bombs schools, hospitals and residential neighborhoods in neighboring Yemen.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       The Podesta Group’s March 2016
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":4,"plid":"http://www.fara.gov/docs/5926-Short-Form-20160311-306.pdf"}}' href="http://www.fara.gov/docs/5926-Short-Form-20160311-306.pdf" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         filing
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       , required under the Foreign Agents Registration Act of 1938, shows that Tony Podesta himself oversees the Saudi account. At the same time, Tony Podesta is also a top campaign contributor and bundler for Hillary Clinton. So while one brother runs the campaign, the other brother funds it with earnings that come, in part, from the Saudis.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       John and Tony Podesta have been heavyweights in DC insider politics for decades. John Podesta served as President Bill Clinton’s chief of staff, founded the influential DC think tank Center for American Progress (which regularly
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":5,"plid":"136324/release-with-saudi-reforms-underway-next-president-has-chance-to-reframe-the-u-s-saudi-relationship/"}}' href="https://www.americanprogress.org/press/release/2016/04/25/136324/release-with-saudi-reforms-underway-next-president-has-chance-to-reframe-the-u-s-saudi-relationship/" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         touts Saudi “reforms”
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       ), and was counselor to President Obama. Tony Podesta was dubbed by the
                       <em>
                        New York Times
                       </em>
                       as “
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":6,"plid":"136324/release-with-saudi-reforms-underway-next-president-has-chance-to-reframe-the-u-s-saudi-relationship/"}}' href="https://www.americanprogress.org/press/release/2016/04/25/136324/release-with-saudi-reforms-underway-next-president-has-chance-to-reframe-the-u-s-saudi-relationship/" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         one of Washington’s biggest players
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       “ whose clients “are going to get a blueprint for how to succeed in official Washington.”
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       The brothers seem to have no problem mixing their roles into the same pot. Tony Podesta held a Clinton campaign
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":7,"plid":"https://www.yahoo.com/news/hillary-moneyman-highlights-new-saudi-connection-194828485.html"}}' href="https://www.yahoo.com/news/hillary-moneyman-highlights-new-saudi-connection-194828485.html" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         fundraiser
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       at his home featuring gourmet Italian food
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":8,"plid":"https://www.yahoo.com/news/hillary-moneyman-highlights-new-saudi-connection-194828485.html"}}' href="https://www.yahoo.com/news/hillary-moneyman-highlights-new-saudi-connection-194828485.html" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         cooked by
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       himself and his brother, the campaign chairman. The fundraiser, by the way, came just days after Tony Podesta filed his Saudi contract with the Justice Department, a contract that included an initial “project fee” payment of $200,000.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       The Saudis hired the Podesta Group in 2015 because it was getting hammered in the press over civilian casualties from its airstrikes in Yemen and its crackdown on political dissidents at home, including sentencing blogger Raif Badawi to ten years in prison and 1,000 lashes for “insulting Islam.” Since then, Tony Podesta’s fingerprints have been all over Saudi Arabia’s advocacy efforts in Washington DC. When Saudi Arabia executed the prominent nonviolent Shia dissident Sheikh Nimr al-Nimr, causing protests throughout the Shia world and inflaming sectarian divisions, The New York Times
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":9,"plid":"world/middleeast/shiite-cleric-gained-in-status-as-a-rivalry-deepened.html?_r=0"}}' href="http://www.nytimes.com/2016/01/05/world/middleeast/shiite-cleric-gained-in-status-as-a-rivalry-deepened.html?_r=0" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         noted
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       that the Podesta Group provided the newspaper with a Saudi commentator who defended the execution.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       The Podesta-Clinton-Saudi connection should be seen in light of the recent media exposes revealing the taudry pay-to-play nature of the Clinton Foundation. Top on the list of foreign donors to the foundation is Saudi Arabia, which contributed between $10 million and $25 million.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       What did the Saudis get for their largesse and access? Wikileaks revealed a 2009 cable by then Secretary of State Hillary Clinton saying: “More needs to be done since Saudi Arabia remains a critical financial support base for Al Qaeda, the Taliban, Lashkar e-Tayyiba and other terrorist groups.” Instead of sanctioning the Saudis, Clinton did the opposite: She authorized enormous quantities of weapons to be sold to them. On Christmas Eve in 2011,
                       <a data-beacon='{"p":{"mnid":"entry_text","lnid":"citation","mpid":10,"plid":"saudi-christmas-present/"}}' href="https://theintercept.com/2016/02/22/saudi-christmas-present/" target="_hplink">
                        <span style="color: #2e7061;">
                         Hillary Clinton and her closest aides celebrated
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       a massive $29.4 billion sale to the Saudis of over 80 F-15 fighter jets, manufactured by Boeing, a company which coincidentally contributed $900,000 to the Clinton Foundation. In a chain of enthusiastic emails, an aide exclaimed that it was “not a bad Christmas present.” I’m sure the Yemenis at the receiving end of the Saudi bombings would not be so enthusiastic.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="content-list-component mt-paragraph text">
                       The Clintons have said that if Hillary Clinton gets elected, the foundation will stop taking foreign donations. But what about no longer taking campaign contributions from people who are paid by the Saudi government to whitewash its image? The Podesta Group should be blacklisted from contributing to Clinton’s campaign until they drop the monarchy as a client and return their ill-gotten gains. If Hillary Clinton wants to be a meaningful symbol for human rights and women’s empowerment, her campaign must live up to the values she claims to represent, and this would be one step in the right direction.
                      </div>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://musingsfortheopenminded.blogspot.com/2016/10/wikileaks-deniers-new-breed-of.html">
                       WikiLeaks Deniers: The New Breed of Conspiracy Nut
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3041610603285205580" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Caitlin Johnstone -
                       <a href="http://www.inquisitr.com/3606764/wikileaks-deniers-the-new-breed-of-conspiracy-nut/" target="_blank">
                        Inquisitr
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/CrGrnbCUIAMEsjX.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="640" src="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/CrGrnbCUIAMEsjX.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       Flat-Earthers. Young Earth creationists. Holocaust deniers. And now, WikiLeaks deniers.
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       Remember when kooky conspiracy theories used to be under the sole jurisdiction of poorly-designed websites with bloodstained Illuminati symbols in the background? Now you see them being peddled on mainstream news stations, spouting convoluted, contradictory messages that go all over the map, from
                       <a class="external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7DcATG9Qy_A" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        this delusional lunatic
                       </a>
                       babbling on CNN about how reading WikiLeaks is illegal, to
                       <a class="external" href="https://twitter.com/adamjohnsonNYC/status/785736619550765056" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        this
                       </a>
                       pants-on-head psychotic madman from MSNBC going on a manic 37-post Twitter rant about how Trump is collaborating with the Russians to deliver doctored leaks, basing his fantastical claims on nothing but his own wild imagination. “It’s the Russians! It’s the Russians!”
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="ym" id="ym_1240382767387490741" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       Somehow in the fevered hallucinations of these yammering bedlamites, it’s possible for the leaked documents to be (A) illegal to read, (B) completely fake, and (C) stolen by Russian hackers, all at the same time.
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       It’s hard to blame them for losing the plot at this point, though. I mean, the defenders of the establishment are really backed into a corner these days. Nobody’s buying their old “but Trump!” schtick anymore, it’s common knowledge that the corporate media is at the whim of the ruling elites, it’s undeniable that DNC violated their own
                       <a class="external" href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/2989759-Impartiality-Clause-DNC-Charter-Bylaws-Art-5-Sec-4.html" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        Charter
                       </a>
                       by colluding against Sanders, and the
                       <a class="external" href="https://wikileaks.org/podesta-emails/emailid/1120" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        Pied Piper email
                       </a>
                       confirmed that they rigged the Republican primaries as well. This has left them with no recourse but to either put on their tinfoil hats and blame the whole thing on an elaborate conspiracy by the Kremlin or to espouse the even nuttier position of attempting to cast doubt upon the unassailable integrity of Julian Assange and WikiLeaks. Or admit they were wrong, but how often does that happen?
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       WikiLeaks has an untarnished, decade-long record of 100 percent perfect vetting of their sources, and everyone knows it. That’s why after the DNC leaks dropped, nobody bothered trying to deny anything; the DNC issued Bernie Sanders an apology, the officials involved tendered their letters of resignation, and the Democrats immediately began raving about Russian hackers to try and make everyone forget the whole thing. Assange
                       <a class="external" href="http://www.motherjones.com/politics/2010/04/wikileaks-julian-assange-iraq-video?page=3" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        told
                       </a>
                       <em style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                        Mother Jones
                       </em>
                       in 2010 that a full interdisciplinary panel of experts is set to the task of vetting each leak and investigating each source, and if there’s doubt, it’s thrown out. Assange has poured far too much effort, wisdom, and expertise into maintaining the integrity of his establishment for anyone to legitimately cast doubt upon it. They can try, but they’ll just sound demented.
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>

                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       It’s been funny watching the talking heads on the news sweat and stammer as they realize their complicity in a coordinated assault on American democracy is gradually being exposed. Watch
                       <a class="external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1m9YroW7VN8" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        this unfortunate dingbat
                       </a>
                       squirm as Glenn Greenwald brings up the revelation that CNN helped prep Hillary Clinton for a question on one of their debates. Guess what channel he works for?
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       Speaking of Greenwald, he’s written
                       <a class="external" href="https://theintercept.com/2016/10/13/on-wikileaks-journalism-and-privacy-reporting-on-the-podesta-archive-is-an-easy-call/" style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; color: #444444; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank">
                        an article worth reading
                       </a>
                       about another one of the screwball tactics these WikiLeaks deniers have been employing: pretending that the leaks are just normal political stuff and not newsworthy or interesting. Greenwald explains that whether or not a leak is “shocking” or “Earth-shattering” is irrelevant to its significance and importance; what matters is how it contributes to the goal of painting a picture for the public of what’s really going on in their world. I agree that this is a valid point, but I hasten to add that these revelations which WikiLeaks has been bringing to light are indeed shocking; no amount of establishment gaslighting is going to make me believe that a presidential candidate having the ability to control the media coverage of the election and manipulate both the Republican and Democratic primaries is something normal and uninteresting.
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       “The lady doth protest too much, methinks.” That’s a quote from some boring Shakespeare play, but it’s also something people say when someone’s resisting something with a frantic intensity that reveals their true intentions. When you hear all the talking heads and Correct-the-Record shills loudly trying to get you to look anywhere else but where you’re looking, the lady doth protest too much. Look there, because you may be sure it’s where the truth is.
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                       Trust yourself, dear reader. Don’t doubt yourself just because confident-sounding people tell you to believe them in an authoritative tone. Be true to what you know is true, and never,
                       <em style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
                        never
                       </em>
                       let them trick you into thinking you’re the crazy one when you’ve got all the facts on your side. Remember:
                      </div>
                      <div style="border: 0px; box-sizing: border-box; font-family: Montserrat, Arial, 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
