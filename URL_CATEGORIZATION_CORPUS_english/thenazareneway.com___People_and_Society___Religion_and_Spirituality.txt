               <a href="Philo's%20Rules%20for%20Allegory.htm">
                Philo's 
 						Rules for Enigmas
               </a>
              </font>
              Philo's rules for enigmas 
 						can be used to identify and interpret coded messages 
 						hidden within scripture. It works for both the Old 
 						Testament and the New Testament.
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="Wisdom%20the%20Goddess.htm">
                Wisdom the Goddess
               </a>
              </font>
              <span lang="EN">
               Excerpts from
               <i>
                Wisdom of Solomon:
               </i>
               “Wisdom…
              </span>
              a
              <b>
               Holy
              </b>
              and disciplined
              <b>
               Spirit…
              </b>
              <span lang="EN">
               a 
 						kindly
               <b>
                Spirit…She
               </b>
               hastens to make
               <b>
                Herself
               </b>
               known.” Thanks to
               <i>
                Wisdom of Solomon
               </i>
               , the secret 
 						of the Trinity’s “Holy Spirit” is revealed:
               <b>
                She is 
 						the Goddess.
               </b>
              </span>
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="Allegory%20101.htm">
                Enigmas 101
               </a>
              </font>
              (Taken from a series of six lessons shared with the Essene Holy 
 		Communions Group). It can be demonstrated that Luke wrote his gospel 
 		using Philo's "Rules for the Allegorical Interpretation of Scripture." 
 		They are an easily followed set of rules that lead to "The Truth" Luke 
 		promised to provide to Theophilus.
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="MaryMagdaleneSinnerorQueen.htm">
                Enigmas 202
               </a>
              </font>
              The gospel writer Luke used Philo's "Rules for 
 						Enigmas" to tell the story of "Mary Magdalene, The 
 						Black Madonna." (Essay also listed under "Of Women and 
 						the Holy Order" below)
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="MaryMagdaleneSinnerorQueen.htm">
                Mary Magdalene:  Sinner, Queen, or Both?
               </a>
              </font>
              Luke's gospel 
 		interpreted enigmatically leads to the historical identity of Mary 
 		Magdalene.
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <a href="I%20AM%20THAT%20I%20AM.htm">
               I AM THAT I AM
              </a>
              <i>
               Asherah
              </i>
              was a major
              <b>
               <i>
                <a href="http://www.statemaster.com/encyclopedia/Semitic" style="color: #336699; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none; text-line-through: none">
                 <span style="color:windowtext">
                  Semitic
                 </span>
                </a>
                <a href="http://www.statemaster.com/encyclopedia/Mother-goddess" style="color: #336699; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none; text-line-through: none">
                 <span style="color:windowtext">
                  mother goddess
                 </span>
                </a>
               </i>
              </b>
              ,
              <span lang="EN">
               worshipped in ancient
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Israel" style="color: #336699; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none; text-line-through: none" title="Kingdom of Israel">
                <span style="color:windowtext">
                 Israel
                </span>
               </a>
               as the consort of El 
 		and in
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Judah" style="color: #336699; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none; text-line-through: none" title="Kingdom of Judah">
                <span style="color:windowtext">
                 Judah
                </span>
               </a>
               as the
               <a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/consort" style="color: #336699; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none; text-line-through: none" title="wikt:consort">
                <span style="color:windowtext">
                 consort
                </span>
               </a>
               of
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yahweh" style="color: #336699; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none; text-line-through: none" title="Yahweh">
                <span style="color:
 windowtext">
                 Yahweh
                </span>
               </a>
               .
              </span>
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <a href="Mary%20Magdalene%20Great%20Mother-Goddess%20Queen.htm">
               <font size="4">
                Mary Magdale
               </font>
               <span lang="EN">
                <font size="4">
                 ne:
                </font>
                Great Mother-Goddess Queen
               </span>
              </a>
              <i>
              </i>
              Enigmas were the Nazarenes' 
 		only hope of saving their history from destruction at the hands of their 
 		enemy, Saulus, aka "The Apostle Paul."
              <span lang="EN">
              </span>
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <a href="serapistnw.htm">
               <font size="4">
                Serapis
               </font>
              </a>
              The crucifixion, some biblical investigators now suggest, was a 
 		reenactment of the ancient Rite of Osiris, the sacred drama presented 
 		annually throughout Egypt.
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="Lucifer%20Satan%20or%20Goddess.htm">
                Lucifer: 
 		Satan or Goddess?
               </a>
              </font>
              Luc is Latin for "light"; ferre is Latin 
 		for "bearer." The planet Venus was called the
              <i>
               Light Bearer
              </i>
              because it appears in the morning sky just before the sun rises.
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="The%20Gospel%20of%20Paul.htm">
                The Gospel of Paul
               </a>
              </font>
              (Based on a series of four lessons shared 
 		with the Essene Holy Communions Group.)
              <font size="4">
              </font>
              Paul 
 		claimed conversion and named himself an "Apostle." Luke's 
 		enigmatical 
 		message is that Paul lied. Paul was an infiltrator, a spy, and, as 
 		Thomas Jefferson asserted, the great corrupter of the doctrine of Jesus 
 		the Nazarene.
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <font size="4">
               <a href="Micah's%20Prophecies.htm">
                Micah's Prophecies
               </a>
              </font>
              More important, perhaps, than Jesus' birth in 
 						Bethlehem was Micah's prophecy about his consort,
              <i>
               The 
 						Magdalene.
              </i>
             </p>
            </li>
            <li>
             <p align="left">
              <a href="Locusts%20or%20Pancakes.htm">
               Locusts or Pancakes:
              </a>
              <i>
              </i>
              How the transposition of 
 						the Greek word for "pancakes" with the Greek word for 
 						"locusts" erroneously reported that John the Baptist ate 
 						locusts.
             </p>
            </li>
           </ul>
          </center>
         </div>
        </div>
       </blockquote>

      <p align="left">
       <font size="4">

        </a>
       </font>
       <i>
       </i>
       Commemorating the events that led 
 		to the arrest and execution of Jesus the Nazarene. During this week we 
 		are reminded to reflect upon the meaning of Jesus' death. An event that 
 		took place nearly two millennia ago at a place which still remains the 
 		epicenter of religious and political violence today.

        </a>
       </font>
       The political and economic reasons 
 		for dealing with Jesus. The "conspiracy" against Jesus had been building 
 		for at least 3 years, and sources record seven instances of official 
 		plotting against him, two efforts at arrest, and three assassination 
 		attempts before this time. This intrigue was no spur of the moment idea.
      </p>

     <li>
      <p align="left">
       <font color="#800000" size="4">
        <a href="http://www.thenazareneway.com/details_history_of_crucifixion.htm">
         Details and History of Crucifixion
        </a>
       </font>
       Crucifixion was never 
 		performed for ritual or symbolic reasons; it is an ancient method of 
 		execution in which the victim was tied or nailed to a large wooden cross 
 		(Latin: crux) and left to hang until dead.
      </p>
     </li>
     <li>
      <p align="left">

 		"out of the cross.

         INRI: The Inscription Explained
        </a>
       </font>
       Pilate had an inscription 
 		written and put on the cross. It read, 'Jesus of Nazareth, the King of 
 		the Jews.' Many of the Jews read this inscription, because the place 
 		where Jesus was crucified was near the city; and it was written in 
 		Hebrew, in Latin, and in Greek.
      </p>

      <p align="left">

        </a>

 		Testament story as an historical account of an actual event central to 
 		their faith. Others generally view the account of the resurrection of 
 		Jesus as fictional to varying degrees.

      <p align="left">
       <font color="#800000" size="4">

        </a>
       </font>
       Aramaic Bible researchers claim 
 		that the traditional "forsaken" interpretation is a mistake in the 
 		Aramaic scribing that was transferred to later transcriptions. According

        <i>

        </i>
       </b>

         Vicarious Atonement
        </a>
       </font>
       The Crucifix is the emblem and symbol of 
 		the Son of God, not because Jesus shed his blood upon the cross for the 
 		sins of man, but because the Christ is crucified perpetually so long as 
 		sin remains.
      </p>

      <p align="left">
       <a href="serapistnw.htm">
        <font size="4">
         Serapis
        </font>
       </a>
       The crucifixion, some biblical investigators now suggest, was a 
 		reenactment of the ancient Rite of Osiris, the sacred drama presented 
 		annually throughout Egypt. These dramas told the story of the dying and 
 		revived god, Osiris, and his goddess, Isis, the Magna Mater. With a goal 
 		of peaceful coexistence between Jews and Romans, Caesar Augustus joined 
 		the Nazarenes to create a "Messianic Osiris" to satisfy both religions. 
 		The "crucifixion" that occurred during the annual Passover celebration 
 		incorporated Judaism's favorite holiday with the Roman's favorite 
 		festival.
       <a href="serapistnw">
       </a>
      </p>
