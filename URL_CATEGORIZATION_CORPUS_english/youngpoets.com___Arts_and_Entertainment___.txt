        <h3>
         National Schools Project Page
        </h3>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://youngpoets.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/Projectpage.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-119" height="331" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" src="http://youngpoets.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/Projectpage.jpg" srcset="http://youngpoets.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/Projectpage.jpg 500w, http://youngpoets.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/12/Projectpage-300x198.jpg 300w" title="Projectpage" width="500"/>
           </a>
           We want kids excited about writing!
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The National Schools Project, initiated in 1994, is designed to share our youth’s talents with others, supply a national audience for student writing, and provide a publishing opportunity for young poets. Schools around the nation are invited to participate in the project by submitting their students’ most creative poetry. A panel of educators reviews the poetry submitted and selects poems to be published based on a list of literary criteria (check
         <a href="http://youngpoets.com/project-description/" title="Project Description">
          Project Description
         </a>
         ). The authors of poems selected are notified individually and written parental permission gained to print their poetry. There is no charge to the student for publication, and a free copy of the book is sent to the participating school library. Additionally, any school can request a complimentary copy at any time.
        </p>
        <p>
         The project employs high school and college students who work around their school hours and extra-curricular activities. The project sponsors fundraisers at area high schools and gives awards to schools.  Here is a list of the
         <a href="http://youngpoets.com/2011-school-winners/" title="2011 School Winners">
          2017 Poetry School Winners
         </a>
         .
        </p>
