         <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/walking-routes-on-national-trails">

         <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/canal-walks">

         </a>
         , challenging climbs on our
         <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/contentnew/mountain-walks">

        <h2 class="panel-title">
         Latest Walking Routes
        </h2>
       </div>
       <div>
        <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="table table-hover">
         <tr class="ViewRow" onmouseout="javascript:className='ViewRow'" onmouseover="javascript:className='highlight'">
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 25%">
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/conwy-mountain-walking-route">
            Conwy Mountain
           </a>
          </td>
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 15%">
           3 miles (5.2 km)
          </td>
          <td class="hidemob" style="width: 55%">
           Enjoy fabulous views over Conwy Bay and the Welsh mountains on this climb to Conwy Mountain (Mynydd y Dref).
           <br/>
           You can follow a section of the
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/north-wales-path-walking-route">
            North Wales Path
           </a>
           from the town up into the hills. Starting at Conwy Suspension Bridge head north along the Conwy Estuary to Bodlondeb Woods. Follow the trail round the edge of the woods before turning south towards Cadnant Park. Trails then climb west to the mountain summit which stands at a height of 244 m (801 ft). Here you will find Neolithic Hut Circles and the Iron Age hillfort of Castell Caer Seion. There's also lots of pretty heather and magnificent views over the town to Great Orme and the mountains of the Snowdonia National Park.
           <br/>
           There are a number of footpaths you can follow around the hill top. At the western end you will find the Sychnant Pass where the car park offers an alternative start point for exploring the area.
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr class="ViewRow" onmouseout="javascript:className='ViewRow'" onmouseover="javascript:className='highlight'">
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 25%">
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/leominster-walking-route">
            Leominster
           </a>
          </td>
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 15%">
           3 miles (4.5 km)
          </td>
          <td class="hidemob" style="width: 55%">
           Explore this attractive Herefordshire town and enjoy riverside trails along the River Lugg.
           <br/>
           The walk starts in the town centre at the noteworthy Grange Court. The timber-framed building was built in 1633 by John Abel, and moved to its present location in 1859. It is extravagantly decorated with carvings, including mermaids, angels, animals, flowers and grotesque people.
           <br/>
           The route then follows public footpaths north past Leominster Priory which dates from the 13th century. You cross the River Kenwater before picking up riverside trails on the eastern side of the River Lugg.
           <br/>
           Follow the trails south to Eaton Hall where you cross the river and follow the trails back into the town centre.
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr class="ViewRow" onmouseout="javascript:className='ViewRow'" onmouseover="javascript:className='highlight'">
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 25%">
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/bircher-common-walking-route">
            Bircher Common
           </a>
          </td>
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 15%">
           2 miles (3.8 km)
          </td>
          <td class="hidemob" style="width: 55%">
           Explore this area of lowland heath on this pleasant walk near
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/leominster-walking-route">
            Leominster
           </a>
           . There's miles of footpaths to follow across the common with lovely views over the surrounding Herefordshire countryside from the high points.
           <br/>
           Woodland trails can also be found in the adjacent Oaker Coppice and Croft Wood.
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr class="ViewRow" onmouseout="javascript:className='ViewRow'" onmouseover="javascript:className='highlight'">
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 25%">
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/berrington-hall-walking-route">
            Berrington Hall
           </a>
          </td>
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 15%">
           1 miles (2.25 km)
          </td>
          <td class="hidemob" style="width: 55%">
           Explore the 250 acre parkland of this fine Georgian mansion near
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/leominster-walking-route">
            Leominster
           </a>
           . The hall can be reached on foot by following the long distance Herefordshire Trail from the town or you can park in the on site car park.
           <br/>
           In the 'Capability' Brown designed park there are lovely gardens with fountains, a Ha-ha and a variety of plants and flowers. There's also the pretty Berrington Pool, peaceful woodland trails and nice views of the surrounding farmland and the Welsh mountains.
           <br/>
           After your stroll you can enjoy refreshments in the National Trust's tea room.
           <br/>
           You can virtually explore the parkland using the google street view link below!
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr class="ViewRow" onmouseout="javascript:className='ViewRow'" onmouseover="javascript:className='highlight'">
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 25%">
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/pin-mill-walking-route">
            Pin Mill
           </a>
          </td>
          <td class="ViewLabel" style="width: 15%">
           2 miles (2.8 km)
          </td>
          <td class="hidemob" style="width: 55%">
           Enjoy woodland trails and riverside paths on this delightful walk in Suffolk. There's wonderful views over the River Orwell where you can see lots of little boats at the charming hamlet of Pin Mill. The area is also great for flora and fauna with pretty heather and yellow gorse to see as you make your way through the area.
           <br/>
           The walk starts from the Pin Mill car park, just to the north of the village of Chelmondiston. From here you can pick up the
           <a href="http://www.gps-routes.co.uk/routes/home.nsf/RoutesLinksWalks/stour-and-orwell-walk-walking-route">
            Stour and Orwell Walk
           </a>
           to take you along the River Orwell. The trails take you through the attractive pine woodland of the Cliff Plantation before returning to the car park.
           <br/>
           After your walk you can visit the splendid Butt and Oyster pub in Pin Mill. The pub dates from the 17th century and features in the Swallows and Amazons book series by Arthur Ransome, who patronised the inn himself.
