         Madness takes its toll. Please have exact change.

            <p>
             <span class="let-j">
              J
             </span>
             ohn Dryden observed in 1681 that
 "
             <em>
              Great wits are sure to madness near allied, And thin partitions do their bounds divide,
             </em>
             "
 echoing the observation made centuries ago by the philosopher Aristotle
 that madness and genius seem to go hand in hand.
 In this cultural tradition, we often believe that the mental illnesses of
 great poets are simply a condition of their own artistic genius.
            </p>
            <blockquote id="bq0">
             <div id="q0">
              <span class="bq-quote">
               <span class="bqs">
                “
               </span>
               We poets in our youth begin in gladness; but thereof comes in the end despondency and madness.
               <span class="bqe">
                ”
               </span>
              </span>
              <span class="bq-source">
               ~ William Wordsworth ~
              </span>
             </div>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             Why might creative people be prone to such dementia?  Perhaps it
 is the fact that their subject is the world of experience itself,
 reflected through their creations.  Perhaps in some cases it is the
 unceasing pressure and stress that is the nature of celebrity.
 In their attempt to tell the truth as they see it, poets may find pain,
 yet their craft itself might offer one way to lend them power over that pain.
            </p>
            <blockquote id="bq1">
             <div id="q1">
              <span class="bq-quote">
               <span class="bqs">
                “
               </span>
               The lunatic, the lover, and the poet,
               <br/>
               Are of imagination all compact.
               <span class="bqe">
                ”
               </span>
              </span>
              <span class="bq-source">
               ~ William Shakespeare ~
              </span>
             </div>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             The interest in the controversial aspects a poet's life itself can
 sometimes draw attention away from their creations.
 There is a general tendency for the sensational and pathological to
 attract heightened notice by the general public.
 This may not always be a negative thing, however, as it can generate
 more interest in a poet's work.
            </p>
            <p>
             These pages present stories about and works by several widely admired
 poets who courted emotional disaster throughout their lives.
 Sometimes their struggle was transformed into brilliant creations.
 Sometimes the pain was simply too overwhelming, and they succumbed to it.
 The tales of their lives demonstrate that often tormenting relationship
 between pain and creativity.
            </p>
