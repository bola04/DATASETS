               </abbr>

                common
               </abbr>

               </abbr>

               <abbr title="Common to all; pertaining to the whole body of citizens (in this case, the entire nation); not limited in application (such as to a single area, class, or person).">
                general
               </abbr>

                Welfare
               </abbr>
               , and
               <abbr title="To safeguard or protect; to make certain.">
                secure
               </abbr>
               the Blessings of
               <abbr title="Freedom from unjust restraint or control; freedom to do as one chooses within the bounds of just laws.">
                Liberty
               </abbr>
               to ourselves and our
               <abbr title="Children; future generations.">
                Posterity
               </abbr>
               , do
               <abbr title="To appoint, decree, or enact; to set in order.">
                ordain
               </abbr>
               and establish this
               <abbr title="An established form of government; a system of basic rules and principles that determine how a state or nation is organized and governed. (A constitution is of higher authority than any law enacted by a legislature. See II.1.8; VI.2-3.)">
                Constitution
               </abbr>

                </abbr>
