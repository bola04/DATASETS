       <h2 align="Left">
        <a name="News">
         News and Comments
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p>
        I was surprised to see how many minerals on this site fluoresce under longwave,
 	but failed to fluoresce under my old shortwave. This week I checked all the
 	minerals in inventory for longwave fluorescence and posted them with longwave
 	UV preview images.
       </p>
       <p>
        To remind you, ever since I was a beginning mineral collector back in 1968
 	I always owned only a shortwave (254 nm) ultraviolet light. The conventional
 	wisdom was that most of the good fluorescent minerals would fluoresces best
 	under shortwave ultraviolet light. Every mineral (70,000+) posted to this
 	site over the years was checked for fluorescence under shortwave
 	ultraviolet. The gallery of
        <a href="fluoresc.htm">
         Fluorescent_Minerals
        </a>
        were all illustrating only shortwave fluorescence.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now I acquired a new longwave (365 nm) ultraviolet light.
       </p>
       <p>
        If you want to acquire a reasonably priced longwave light, I recommend the
 	flashlight-type that look like a high-powered LED flashlight, but has a 365
 	nm longwave light in it. I purchased mine from Patrick Bigos of Midnight
 	Minerals
 	(
        <a href="https://www.midnightminerals.com/">
         https://www.midnightminerals.com
        </a>
        )
 	in southern Maine. The longwave light was $60 with rechargeable battery
 	and charger. A bargain!
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         John H. Betts, 6/19/2018
        </i>
       </p>

       <h2>
        Pledge to My Customers:
       </h2>
       <p>
        Customer satisfaction is my primary goal. I pledge:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         New minerals will be added to this site every week.
        </li>
        <li>
         All minerals offered will be naturally crystallized specimens - no fakes,
 	    no dyes,  no irradiation, no crystals glued on matrix.
        </li>
        <li>
         Only the exact item pictured on the site will be shipped - no substitutes.
        </li>
        <li>
         Photographs will accurately represent each item - no exaggeration, enhancement
 	    or retouching flaws.
        </li>
        <li>
         Mineral species and localities will be accurately listed on labels with each
 	    specimen.
        </li>
        <li>
         All items will be shipped via US Postal Service, usually the next business
 	    day after the order is finalized.
        </li>
        <li>
         Each mineral will be individually wrapped and carefully packaged to prevent
 	    damage during shipping.
        </li>
        <li>
         All historic labeling, if available, will be included with each mineral.
        </li>
        <li>
         Returns are gladly accepted. No questions asked, no explanation necessary.
        </li>
        <li>
         All customer information will be kept confidential and never shared or sold.
        </li>
        <li>
         I will continue to freely share my knowledge with others and contribute
 	    to the study and appreciation of minerals.
        </li>
        <li>
         All mineral specimens have a lifetime guarantee: if the mineral species was
 	    incorrectly identified, I will refund the cost of the original purchase &amp;
 	    shipping, the cost return shipping.
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <i>
         John H. Betts
        </i>
        , owner
        <br/>
        Mineral dealer since 1989, mineral collectors since 1967
