       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Don Tate: Author and Illustrator
        </h1>
        <div class="undertitle">
        </div>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Hello, thank you for visiting my website. I am the illustrator of more than fifty critically acclaimed trade and educational
         <a href="http://squarebearstudio.com/setups/test3/books/">
          books for children,
         </a>
         and the author of two award-winning titles.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of the books I’ve illustrated include
         <a href="https://dontate.com/the-cart-that-carried-martin/">
          The Cart That Carried Martin
         </a>
         , (Charlesbridge);
         <a href="https://dontate.com/books/hopes-gift/">
          Hope’s Gift
         </a>
         , (Penguin); and
         <a href="https://dontate.com/books/the-amazing-age-of-john-roy-lynch/">
          The Amazing Age of John Roy Lynch
         </a>
         , (Eerdmans, 2015), a
         <a href="http://www.txla.org/new-tba-list-released">
          Texas Bluebonnet Master List selection
         </a>
         , 2016-17. The most recent book that I’ve illustrated is called
         <a href="http://www.charlesbridge.com/products/whoosh-lonnie-johnsons-super-stream-of-ideas">
          Whoosh! Lonnie Johnson’s Super-Soaking Stream of Inventions
         </a>
         , (Charlesbridge, 2016); a Junior Library Guild selection that has already garnered two starred reviews.
        </p>
        <p>
         I am the author of
         <a href="https://dontate.com/books/it-jes-happened-when-bill-traylor-started-to-draw/">
          It Jes’ Happened: When Bill Traylor Started To Draw
         </a>
         (Lee &amp; Low Books, 2102), an Ezra Jack Keats
         <a href="http://www.ezra-jack-keats.org/ejk-book-award/2013-book-award/" target="_blank">
          New Writer Honor
         </a>
         winner; and
         <a href="https://dontate.com/books/poet-the-remarkable-story-of-george-moses-horton/">
          Poet: The Remarkable Story of George Moses Horton
         </a>
         (Peachtree, 2015).
         <em>
          Poet
         </em>
         is an
         <a href="http://www.ezra-jack-keats.org/h/2016-ejk-book-award-ceremony-highlights/">
          Ezra Jack Keats Book Award winner,
         </a>
         recipient of a
         <a href="http://www.christophers.org/release---awards-2016">
          2016 Christopher Award
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.texasinstituteofletters.org/news/2015-winners-news-release.pdf">
          winner of the Texas Institute of Letters
         </a>
         book awards, and was recently honored with a
         <a href="http://www.writersleague.org/2015BookAwardWinnersFinalists">
          Writers’ League of Texas book awards
         </a>
         , and an SCBWI
         <a href="https://www.scbwi.org/2016-crystal-kite-awards-announced/">
          Crystal Kite, 2016
         </a>
         . My next authored and illustrated picture book, Strong As Sandow: How Eugen Sandow Became The Strongest Man on Earth, will publish in 2017. Also, please look for my short stories for middle grade students,
         <strong>
          Whiz Kid
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          Dance Like You Draw
         </strong>
         , in
         <a href="http://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/317386/been-there-done-that-school-dazed-by-mike-winchell/9780448486741/">
          Been There, Done That: School Dazed
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition to writing and illustrating, I
         <a href="https://dontate.com/school-visits/">
          enjoy presenting at elementary schools
         </a>
         , reading and writing conferences, book and literary festivals.
         <a href="https://dontate.com/contact/">
          Please contact me
         </a>
         for more information.
        </p>
        <p>
         I am a founding host of the
         <a href="http://thebrownbookshelf.com/" target="_blank">
          The Brown Bookshelf
         </a>
         –a blog dedicated to books for African American young readers; and a member of the #
         <a href="http://weneeddiversebooks.tumblr.com/" target="_blank">
          WeNeedDiverseBooks
         </a>
         organization, created to address the lack of diverse, non-majority narratives in children’s literature.
        </p>
       </div>
