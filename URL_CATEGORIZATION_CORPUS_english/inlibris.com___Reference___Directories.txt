   <h2>
    Welcome to Inlibris
   </h2>
   <p>
    With nearly
    <strong>
     54.000 links
    </strong>
    ,
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    is maybe the biggest directory of literary resources on the Internet.
   </p>
   <p>
    Search
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    for etexts, writers, agents, books, ezines, prizes, publishers, workshops, genres, dictionaries, bookstores, libraries, hypertexts and other literary and language- or book-related sites.
   </p>
   <p>
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    is the
    <strong>
     first directory
    </strong>
    that is able to repeat for you a search defined by a previous, unknown user (
    <strong>
     Another user’s search
    </strong>
    service). This service selects at random words that were typed at
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    by a previous user.
   </p>
   <p>
    With the
    <strong>
     10 literary pages at random
    </strong>
    service, you’ll find interesting literary information, not limited by your own questions. But the results will probably be unrelated. In contrast, repeating
    <strong>
     another user’s search
    </strong>
    , you’ll get sites with something in common.
   </p>
   <p>
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    allows you to define a search of pages with a word or more than one. If you type more than one,
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    will show you pages that have
    <strong>
     all
    </strong>
    your words.
   </p>
   <p>
    To limit the result of your search to the more relevant addresses,
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    will only show you the pages that have the words you typed
    <strong>
     in the title, the description or address
    </strong>
    .
   </p>
   <p>
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    will ignore terms that are too common, like articles or some prepositions. It will ignore punctuation and accents, too.
   </p>
   <p>
    <strong>
     Inlibris
    </strong>
    is
    <strong>
     PDA-friendly
    </strong>
    .
