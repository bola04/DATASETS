       <div class="panel-heading">
        <h3 class="panel-title">
         Stumbling Blocks! New Bible Study for 2018
        </h3>
       </div>
       <div class="panel-body">
        <p>
         <a href="Stumbling_Blocks_Bible_Study.htm">
          <img alt="Stumbling BLocks bible study" class="pull-left img-responsive" height="212" src="Images/stumbling-blocks-sm.gif" style="margin-right:10px" width="150"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The journey of faith, like that of life is often littered with stumbling blocks. Most Christians would probably admit to coming across the occasional pothole on their journey of faith, stumbling now and then, needing a little assistance, a hand to hold.
        </p>
        <p>
         It can be helpful to think about these stumbling blocks and how they happen, in order that we might better help others who are struggling in their own journeys.
        </p>
        <p>
         “The beginning of wisdom is found in doubting; by doubting we come to the question, and by seeking we may come upon the truth.” (Pierre Abelard)
        </p>
        <p>
         Available as a pdf download for your small group Bible study, or for use by individuals.
        </p>
        <p>
         Check out the Bible Study and download a sample study
         <a href="Stumbling_Blocks_Bible_Study.htm">
          with this link
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         For more info on all the Bible studies available on the website, head to
         <a href="shop.htm">
          this page
         </a>
         !
        </p>

       <div class="media-body">
        <h4 class="media-heading">
         Triumph &amp; Tragedy - Women in the Bible
        </h4>
        <p>
         In this Bible study, we’re looking at four Old Testament women who have a common connection with King David; three (Michal, Abigail and Bathsheba were wives and the fourth, Tamar, was his daughter).
        </p>
        <p>
         The stories of these four women are intertwined at times, and include other members of the extended family, some in a helpful role, others very definitely not!
        </p>
        <p>
         It is possible to see them as stories that would fit nicely into the soap operas that form part of our TV viewing, and there is also great tragedy, violence, intrigue and betrayal to add to the mix - but then, this is the Old Testament!
        </p>
