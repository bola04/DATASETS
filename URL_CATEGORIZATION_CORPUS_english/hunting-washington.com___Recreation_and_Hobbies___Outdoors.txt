        <h3>
         Welcome to Hunting Washington
        </h3>
        <p>
         Come in and join us in the
         <a href="/smf/index.php" title="washington hunting forum">
          Washington hunting forum
         </a>
         to talk
                                 about some of your own experiences and share some of your photos with other hunters.
        </p>
        <p>
         Washington state is situated in the northwest corner of the lower 48 states bordering British Columbia, Idaho, Oregon and the Pacific Ocean.
                                 Washington state hunters have a diverse set of ecosystems including deserts, dense forests, craggy mountains, lowland rain forests, wide open valleys, canyons, big rivers and rolling hills.
                                 All providing good habitat for plenty of game animals.
        </p>
        <p>
         Complimenting the variety of Washington's terrain are some decent hunting opportunities including big game such as the following (click links to visit boards in the forum)
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,2.0.html">
          elk
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,33.0.html">
          black bear
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,3.0.html">
          mule deer, blacktail deer, whitetail deer
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,17.0.html">
          shed hunting,
         </a>
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,12.0.html">
          moose, mountain goat and big horn sheep.
         </a>
         There are many avid varmint, predator and small game hunters who pursue
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,6.0.html">
          bobcats, coyotes, fox and the like
         </a>
         .
                                 The waterfowl hunting is usually very good in several areas of the state for
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,8.0.html">
          waterfowl such as ducks, canadian geese and snow geese
         </a>
         .
                                 Upland bird opportunities for hunters provided are
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,7.0.html">
          turkey
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php/board,22.0.html">
          pheasant, plenty of grouse, chukar and quail
         </a>
         .
                                 Washington hunters are encouraged to participate in our
         <a href="http://hunting-washington.com/smf/index.php">
          hunting forum
         </a>
         and contribute to our community.
                                 The purpose and intent of this website is to make an internet location where Washington focused hunters can share adventures, ideas and coordinate activities with like minded individuals.
                                 There are lot of forum members who partake in archery, muzzleloader and modern firearms hunting. Including a broad base of users from across the state. We even have a trapper's board, a taxidermy section and a chat room.
        </p>
        <p>
         Several outfitters, guides, taxidermists and other businesses have sponsored our forum to help keep this resource alive. We like to thank these individuals and businesses for their support.
                                 We hope you consider using one of these businesses for your needs.
        </p>

       <h3>
        Washington For Wildlife
       </h3>
       A new organization seeking to increase membership and committee members. Please visit their site at
       <a href="http://washingtonforwildlife.org" target="_blank">
        http://washingtonforwildlife.org
       </a>
       . The purpose of Washington for Wildlife is as follows:
       <br/>
       "To protect Washington's hunting, fishing, and outdoor heritage; to foster local programs enhancing habitat, wildlife, and outdoor activities in Washington State; to hold regulating agencies accountable as the stewards of our wildlife;
                             to ensure that science used in wildlife management is both valid and reliable; to work with other organizations in the furtherance of stated goals; and to fight legal and legislative efforts to take our rights and freedoms provided under the Washington State Constitution and the United States Constitution."
