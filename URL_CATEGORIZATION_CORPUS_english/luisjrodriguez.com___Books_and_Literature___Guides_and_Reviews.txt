            <h2 class="headline">
             About Luis J. Rodriguez
            </h2>
            <div id="content">
             <div class="intro" id="intro">
              <div class="text-content">
               <div style="font-family: Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; word-spacing: normal; letter-spacing: normal; white-space: normal; float: right; margin: 0px 0px 15px 25px;">
                <img alt="Headshot.jpg" class="img-circle" height="189" src="https://d3n8a8pro7vhmx.cloudfront.net/luisjrodriguez/pages/10/attachments/original/1407263381/Headshot.jpg?1407263381" style="float: right;" width="309"/>
                <p>
                 <strong style="word-spacing: normal;">
                  Welcome
                 </strong>
                 to the website of Luis J. Rodriguez--from 2014-2016, the official Poet Laureate of Los Angeles. For Luis poetry is soul talk, a prophetic act, a powerful means to enlarge one's presence in the world.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Luis is also a novelist/memoirist/short story/children's book writer as well as a community &amp; urban peace activist, mentor, healer, youth &amp; arts advocate, husband, father, grandfather, and great-grandfather.
                </p>
                <p>
                 He has 15 books in all genres, including the best-selling memoir, "Always Running, La Vida Loca, Gang Days in L.A." His latest memoir is the sequel, "It Calls You Back: An Odyssey Through Love, Addiction, Revolutions, and Healing." His latest poetry book is "Borrowed Bones" from Curbstone Books/Northwestern University Press. Luis is founding editor of Tia Chucha Press, now in its 28th year, and co-founder/president of Tia Chucha's Centro Cultural &amp; Bookstore in the San Fernando Valley. And he's co-convener of the Network for Revolutionary Change.
                </p>
               </div>

                Luis is dedicated to a clean, balanced, abundant, cooperative, healing world. No more capitalist private property relations, exploitation, war, or inequities. "In essential things, unity; in nonessential things, liberty. In all things compassion."
               </p>
