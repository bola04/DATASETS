        Welcome to senescence.info!
       </h1>
       <p>
        <strong>
         An educational and information resource on the science of aging
        </strong>
       </p>

   <hr size="1" width="20%"/>
   <blockquote>
    <p align="justify">
     "The knowledge that every ambition is doomed to frustration at the hands of a skeleton have never prevented the majority of human beings from behaving as though death were no more than an unfounded rumor."
    </p>
    <p>
     <em>
      Aldous Huxley
     </em>
    </p>
   </blockquote>
   <hr size="1" width="20%"/>
   <p align="justify">
    <a href="aging_definition.html">
     Aging
    </a>
    , or senescence, is the major cause of
    <a href="death_and_aging_fears.html">
     suffering, disease, and death
    </a>
    in modern times. With the worldwide graying of the population, we are now aging for a longer period of time than ever before.
    <a href="gerontology.html">
     Gerontology
    </a>
    , and its sub-field biogerontology in particular, is the science that studies the aging process to prevent age-related disease and degeneration, preserve health, and prolong human life.
    <strong>
     senescence.info
    </strong>
    aims to highlight the
    <a href="gerontology_tutorials.html">
     importance of research on aging
    </a>
    and give an overview of current knowledge on the
    <a href="gerontology.html">
     biology and genetics of aging
    </a>
    , including
    <a href="antiaging_science.html">
     anti-aging therapies
    </a>
    ,
    <a href="aging_models.html">
     models
    </a>
    and
    <a href="aging_theories.html">
     theories of aging
    </a>
    . The most ambitious, even if distant, goal of gerontological research is to make aging optional, to develop a
    <a href="aging_cure.html">
     cure for aging
    </a>
    , and the social implications of a radical increase in human lifespan due to scientific breakthroughs are also
    <a href="immortal_society.html">
     discussed
    </a>
    .
   </p>
   <p align="justify">
    <strong>
     senescence.info
    </strong>
    is aimed at a vast audience and includes
    <a href="gerontology_tutorials.html">
     tutorials
    </a>
    for the non-specialist. Though of course my personal points of view are more prevalent, I try to mention different perspectives, even if I do not agree with them. Although
    <strong>
     senescence.info
    </strong>
    is not peer-reviewed, it is used as a reference by scientists and students in the field and recognized as a major knowledge and information website on the aging process; as evidence of its academic standing,
    <strong>
     senescence.info
    </strong>
    has been highlighted in
    <em>
     Science
    </em>
    (
    <strong>
     307
    </strong>
    :187),
    <em>
     Aging Cell
    </em>
    (
    <strong>
     8
    </strong>
    :65) and
    <em>
     Nucleic Acids Research
    </em>
    (
    <strong>
     41
    </strong>
    :D1027).
   </p>

       <h2>
        <a href="http://genomics.senescence.info">
         Genomics of Aging
        </a>
       </h2>
       <blockquote>
        <p align="justify">
         I am the principal investigator of the
         <a href="http://pcwww.liv.ac.uk/~aging/">
          Integrative Genomics of Ageing Group
         </a>
         at the
         <a href="http://www.liv.ac.uk">
          University of Liverpool
         </a>
         in the UK. Amongst other
         <a href="http://pcwww.liv.ac.uk/~aging/research.html">
          projects
         </a>
         , we maintain the
         <a href="http://genomics.senescence.info">
          Human Ageing Genomic Resources
         </a>
         , a series of tools and databases aimed at understanding the genetic basis of human aging.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <p align="justify">
           <a href="http://genomics.senescence.info/genes/">
            GenAge
           </a>
           is a curated database of genes related to aging in humans and in model organisms.
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p align="justify">
           <a href="http://genomics.senescence.info/diet/">
            GenDR
           </a>
           is the first database of dietary restriction-associated genes.
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p align="justify">
           <a href="http://genomics.senescence.info/longevity/">
            LongevityMap
           </a>
           is the first database of human genetic variants associated with longevity.
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p align="justify">
           <a href="http://genomics.senescence.info/species/">
            AnAge
           </a>
           is an aging-oriented animal database featuring over 4,000 species.
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p align="justify">
           <a href="http://human.ageing-map.org/">
            The Digital Ageing Atlas
           </a>
           is the first centralized collection of aging changes and pathologies.
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </blockquote>
