         <h2>
          HNS Guide:
          <a href="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/guides/defining-the-genre/">
           Defining the Genre
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="small-thumb thumb-link">
          <a href="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/guides/defining-the-genre/" title="Read guide: Defining the Genre">
           <img alt="" class="attachment-list size-list colorbox-26 wp-post-image" height="100" src="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/images1-67x100.jpg" title="" width="67"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p>
          There are problems with defining historical novels, as with defining any genre. When does ‘contemporary’ end, and ‘historical’ begin? What about novels that are part historical, part contemporary? And how much distortion of history will we allow before a book becomes more fantasy than historical?…

        The Society

      <div class="box box-side">
       <div class="section">
        <h5>
         What is the HNS?
        </h5>
        <p>
         We are a literary society devoted to promoting the enjoyment of
         <a href="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/guides/defining-the-genre/" title="Defining the Genre">
          historical fiction
         </a>
         . We are based in the USA and the UK but we welcome
         <a href="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/directory/">
          members
         </a>
         (who can be readers or writers) from all round the world. Through our
         <a href="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/magazines/">
          print magazines
         </a>
         , conferences, website, social media and through the dynamism of our membership we help bring the excitement of these novels to the widest audience. Find out about
         <a href="https://historicalnovelsociety.org/members/">
          joining us
         </a>
         here.
        </p>
