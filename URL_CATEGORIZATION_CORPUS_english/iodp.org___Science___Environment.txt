           The
           <em>
            JOIDES Resolution
           </em>

           <strong>
            <a href="https://www.southampton.ac.uk/oes/research/staff/lcmn.page">

            </a>
           </strong>

           <a href="/boards-and-panels/science-evaluation-panel" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
            Science Evaluation Panel
           </a>
           . Lisa will replace outgoing co-chair Professor Ken Miller effective April 1, 2019 for a three-year term.
          </p>

           <strong>
            ECORD Summer School 2018
           </strong>

           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.marum.de/en/education-career/ECORD-training/ECORD-Summer-Schools/ECORD-Summer-School-2018.html">
             Apply
            </a>
           </strong>
           by May 25.

           </strong>

            </a>
           </strong>

          <h2>
           <a href="http://iodp.tamu.edu/scienceops/expeditions/brothers_arc_flux.html">
            Now Sailing: Brothers Arc Flux
           </a>
          </h2>
          <p>
           This
           <em>
            JR
           </em>
           expedition to the Brothers submarine volcano in the southern part of the Kermadec Arc will explore this magma-derived hydrothermal system, including fluid-rock interactions, the distribution of metals/metalloids and their pathway from depth to the seafloor, and the nature of microbial life in this extreme environment.
          </p>
          <h3>
           <a href="http://iodp.tamu.edu/scienceops/expeditions/hikurangi_subduction_margin.html">
            Recently Completed (May 2018): Hikurangi Subduction Margin
           </a>
          </h3>
          <p>
           This
           <em>
            JR
           </em>
           expedition to study slow slip events (SSE - episodic movements of the plate interface) along the northern Hikurangi subduction margin off New Zealand's North Island retrieved samples from the subducting plate and from primary thrust faults in the accretionary wedge. Borehole observatories that will continue to monitor the slip source area were installed at two sites in the frontal thrust and upper plate.
          </p>
          <h3>
           <a href="http://www.jamstec.go.jp/chikyu/e/nantroseize/expedition_380.html">
            Recently Completed (February 2018): NanTroSEIZE Front Thrust Long-Term Borehole Monitoring System
           </a>
          </h3>
          <p>
           This expedition aboard
           <em>
            Chikyu
           </em>
           installed a long-term borehole monitoring system (LTBMS) in NanTroSEIZE hole C0006, complementing two existing nearby subseafloor observatories as part of the Nankai Trough Seismogenic Zone Experiment (NanTroSEIZE). The LTBMS will connect to Japan's DONET undersea cabled network, sending pressure, strain, and seismic data to the mainland in real time.
          </p>
