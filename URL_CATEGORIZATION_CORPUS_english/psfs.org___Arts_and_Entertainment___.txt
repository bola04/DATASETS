               Once again, we present the Annual Hugo Review (NOT Predictions!) Panel.  Our intrepid band of readers lead by Terry Sisk Graybill, will read all of the fiction nominees (or as much as possible, given this year’s candidates) and viewed all of the dramatic presentations in order to be able to present you with their impressions of which candidates are worthy (or not) of a Hugo Award.  Come see and hear the panel discuss, agree and disagree on what they think of all of the nominees, along with their nominees for Best in Show and Dragon the (the item that dragged on the longest). While the panel’s track record is not always matched by that of the actual electorate, you will be able to hear about books and stories you may have missed that were published during 2017, and still have time to cast your vote for the Hugos to be awarded at this year’s World Science Fiction Convention (Worldcon 76), assuming you are a member or joined before the deadline. More information about the Hugo Award can be found at

            The Philip K. Dick. Award
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           PSFS is proud to sponsor the Philip K. Dick award in association with the Philip K. Dick Trust. The Philip K. Dick award is presented to an author best representing the spirit of innovative science fiction in the paperback marketplace. Films produced from Philip K. Dick's prolific output include Blade Runner, The Minority Report and The Adjustment Bureau. Guaranteed to always be interesting reads, more information about current nominees and past winners can be found at
