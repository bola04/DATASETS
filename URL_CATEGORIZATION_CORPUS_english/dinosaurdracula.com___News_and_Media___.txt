           <header>
            <h2>
             <a href="http://dinosaurdracula.com/blog/random-action-figures-44/">
              Five Random Action Figures, Part 44!
             </a>
            </h2>
           </header>
           <div class="the_excerpt">
            <p>
             To keep this edition of
             <strong>
              <a href="http://dinosaurdracula.com/blog/tag/five-random-action-figures/">
               Five Random Action Figures
              </a>
             </strong>
             on-brand for Cruel Summer, I will only feature figures that have some tangential tie to the season. Enjoy!
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <img alt="" class="border aligncenter size-full wp-image-33277" height="804" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/5-3.jpg" srcset="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/5-3.jpg 640w, http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/5-3-382x480.jpg 382w" width="640"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 14pt; color: #ff0000;">
              <strong>
               <img alt="" class="alignright size-full wp-image-33270" height="170" sizes="(max-width: 170px) 100vw, 170px" src="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/1.png" srcset="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/1.png 170w, http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/1-150x150.png 150w" width="170"/>
               Mike, The Sewer Surfer!
              </strong>
             </span>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
              <strong>
               Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (1990)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             This screwball Michelangelo was from a set of
             <em>
              TMNT
             </em>
             figures that gave our heroes intense new costumes, and even new occupations. (Raph became an astronaut, Don became a sneaky detective… you get the picture.)
            </p>
            <p>
             Since every kid who collected
             <em>
              TMNT
             </em>
             toys started with the original Turtles, it made sense for Playmates to keep redressing them. To their credit, these “extra” Turtles weren’t cheap cash-grabs with rudimentary facelifts. Each had a mold all its own, and they were littered with so many details that the first wave of figures seemed positively antique by comparison.
            </p>
            <p>
             Just look at all of the little touches on Mikey. Seaweed! Baby sharks! Gnarly crabs! Hell, I’ve had this guy for 30 years, and I’m only now noticing the octopus on his foot.
             <a class="more-link" href="http://dinosaurdracula.com/blog/random-action-figures-44/#more-33268">
              Read More…
             </a>

            <h2>
             <a href="http://dinosaurdracula.com/blog/horror-movie-popcorn-scenes/">
              5 Horror Movie Popcorn Scenes!
             </a>
            </h2>
           </header>
           <div class="the_excerpt">
            <p>
             I love popcorn! I love popcorn
             <em>
              so much
             </em>
             that I get extraordinarily excited when it turns up in movies.
             <em>
              Especially
             </em>
             horror movies, where it often becomes a prop in bizarre murders involving alien clowns or unreasonably toxic spiders.
            </p>
            <p>
             Below: Five scenes from horror movies that feature popcorn. I know what you like to read about.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <img class="border aligncenter size-full" src="http://i288.photobucket.com/albums/ll197/dinosaurdracula/2018/ezgif-2-8d4428ace0_zpsdrj3cucu.gif"/>
             <br/>
             <img class="border aligncenter size-full" src="http://i288.photobucket.com/albums/ll197/dinosaurdracula/2018/ezgif-2-19dcdd07ff_zpsbdtpoevz.gif"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 14pt;">
              <strong>
               <span style="color: #ff0000;">
                Scream!
               </span>
               (1996)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             During
             <em>
              Scream’s
             </em>
             <strong>
              <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G3lSvJ5RXKA" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
               perfect opening scene
              </a>
             </strong>
             , Casey (Drew Barrymore) plays along with a “harmless” prank call that soon leads to her death. That’s the too-simple summary, because it’s hard to me to go into particulars when all I wanna talk about is THAT JIFFY POP.
            </p>
            <p>
             While on the phone, Casey’s shakes an adorable Jiffy Pop pan over the stove. You know, the one that creates a big tinfoil “brain” of popcorn. I’ve cooked Jiffy Pop a hundred times, and it never ceases to amaze me. I can’t blame Casey for her poor sense of foreboding during that call, because when
             <em>
              I’m
             </em>
             making Jiffy Pop, it’s all I’m able to focus on, too.
            </p>
            <p>
             Jiffy Pop has been around since 1959 and certainly didn’t need pub from
             <em>
              Scream
             </em>
             to survive, but I’m still convinced that it’s
             <em>
              this scene
             </em>
             that made Jiffy Pop such a thing with people of my generation. Hell, even 20+ years later, it’s still cashing residuals.

            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <img alt="" class="border aligncenter size-full wp-image-33097" height="853" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/6-2.jpg" srcset="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/6-2.jpg 640w, http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/6-2-360x480.jpg 360w" width="640"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              The Super Mario Bros. 3 Happy Meal
             </strong>
             , from 1990, was definitely one of those occasions. Remember, Nintendo toys
             <em>
              in general
             </em>
             were scarce back then. Even when they did pop up, the results could be
             <strong>
              <a href="http://dinosaurdracula.com/blog/nintendo-trophy-figures/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
               pretty offbeat
              </a>
             </strong>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             (Given how white hot Nintendo was at the time, I’ve never understood why they didn’t make a bigger push for toy store merch.)
            </p>
            <p>
             This Happy Meal was the perfect storm.
             <em>
              Super Mario Bros. 3
             </em>
             had
             <em>
              just
             </em>
             come out, and it was all kids were talking about. Our interest in these toys wasn’t casual at all. We absolutely would’ve gobbled ‘em up even if they were sold as regular retail items without bonus cheeseburgers.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <img alt="" class="border aligncenter size-full wp-image-33098" height="740" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/7-1.jpg" srcset="http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/7-1.jpg 640w, http://dinosaurdracula.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/7-1-415x480.jpg 415w" width="640"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             That the toys were so good by Happy Meal standards was almost immaterial. Ronald could’ve written “KOOPA” on plain brown bags, and so long as Nintendo stamped it, we woulda been there. In a sense, it’s
             <em>
              surprising
             </em>
             that they put so much effort into this set. (Not that I’m complaining!)
            </p>
            <p>
             There were four toys in all, or five if you count the “Under 3” exclusive, which in this case was a stoic Raccoon Mario without any of those pesky choking hazards. The “core four” are shown above, each with a unique mold and action feature.
             <a class="more-link" href="http://dinosaurdracula.com/blog/super-mario-bros-3-happy-meal/#more-33091">
              Read More…
             </a>
            </p>
