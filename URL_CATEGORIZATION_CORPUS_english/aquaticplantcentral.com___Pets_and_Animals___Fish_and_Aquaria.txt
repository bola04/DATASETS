The sample I brought home has grown much larger since then.<br /> <br /> <img src="http://i13.photobucket.com/albums/a276/auroraborealis12/Fish/Collecting_Trips/South%20Carolina%202014/Ludwigiacfalata_zps3fa8e33e.jpg" border="0" alt="" onload="NcodeImageResizer.createOn(this);" /><br />
 My favorite find was this stoloniferous Ludwigia, tentatively IDed as L. alata. I plan to flower it in the greenhouse and see if that's actually correct.<br /> <br />
 Alas, I couldn't stay there forever, so we packed up at the end of the week and started driving back home.<br /> <br />
 Not that that stopped me from ditch diving some more! somewhere in North Carolina, we stopped at a Chik-fil-A for lunch. It was a rainy, overcase sort of day, and a ditch out front was flooded. I poked around a bit, and found myself another prize:<br /> <img src="http://i13.photobucket.com/albums/a276/auroraborealis12/Fish/Collecting_Trips/South%20Carolina%202014/Gratiolavirginiana_zps5e2089a0.jpg" border="0" alt="" onload="NcodeImageResizer.createOn(this);" /><br />
 Some Gratiola virginiana was growing amidst the grass!<br /> <br />
