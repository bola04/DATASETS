     <p class="pageTitleText">
      <img alt="" height="32" src="/images/flourish_beechLeavesRight.gif" width="44"/>
      The Elvish Linguistic Fellowship
      <img alt="" height="32" src="/images/flourish_beechLeavesLeft.gif" width="44"/>
     </p>
     <p class="pageSubtitleText">
      A Special Interest Group of the
      <a href="http://www.mythsoc.org/" title="Mythopoeic Society">
       Mythopoeic Society
      </a>
     </p>
     <p class="pageDescriptionText">
      The Elvish Linguistic Fellowship (E.L.F.) is an international organization devoted to the scholarly study of the invented languages of
      <a href="http://www.tolkiensociety.org/tolkien/" title="About Tolkien">
       J.R.R. Tolkien
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p class="pageDescriptionText">
      The primary activity of the E.L.F. is carried out in the pages of its two print journals,
      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/VT/" title="Vinyar Tengwar">
       <span class="workTitle">
        <span class="foreignText">
         Vinyar Tengwar
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      (available by
      <a href="VT/index.html#Subscriptions_anchor">
       subscription
      </a>
      ) and
      <a href="http://www.eldalamberon.com/parma22.html" title="Parma">
       <span class="foreignText">
        <span class="workTitle">
         Parma Eldalamberon
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      , and in its online journal,
      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/Tengwestie/" title="Tengwestie">
       <span class="foreignText">
        <span class="workTitle">
         Tengwestië
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      . The E.L.F. also sponsors the
      <a href="http://groups.yahoo.com/group/lambengolmor/" title="Lambengolmor">
       <span class="foreignText">
        Lambengolmor
       </span>
      </a>
      mailing list.
     </p>

     <p class="bodySubsectionText">
      This 28-page issue features a presentation and analysis of the “Túrin Wrapper”, comprising a set of three untranslated Sindarin texts from the (probably early) 1950s pertaining to the “Túrins Saga”.

        Lulu.com

     <p class="bodySubsectionTitleText">

      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/VT/" title="Vinyar Tengwar">
       <span class="foreignText">

         Vinyar Tengwar
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      format, subscription, and back-issue changes
     </p>
     <p class="bodySubsectionText">
      Due to the vastly increased demands of time required for subscription and back-issue-order fulfillment as readership has grown dramatically, and to the reality of irregular publication dictated by its changed focus on publishing new primary material, and in order to remove format constraints on the size and scope of its contents,
      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/VT/" title="Vinyar Tengwar">
       <span class="foreignText">
        <span class="workTitle">
         Vinyar Tengwar
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      will undergo a format change and a switch to per-issue ordering after the publication of issue 50. Starting with issue 51,
      <span class="foreignText">
       <span class="workTitle">
        VT
       </span>
      </span>
      will be sold on a per-issue basis only, and will be ordered directly from the the online, print-on-demand publisher
      <a href="http://www.lulu.com/spotlight/elf">
       Lulu.com
      </a>
      . Back-issue collections spanning issues 1 thru 50 of
      <span class="foreignText">
       <span class="workTitle">
        Vinyar Tengwar
       </span>
      </span>
      are also available as a series of softcover volumes through Lulu.com (in both perfect- and coil-bound formats), as
      <span class="workTitle">
       The Collected
       <span class="foreignText">
        <span class="workTitle">
         Vinyar Tengwar
        </span>
       </span>
      </span>
      .
     </p>
     <p class="bodySubsectionTitleText">
      <img alt="" class="bullet" src="/images/bullet_beechLeavesDown.gif"/>
      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/VT/VT-index-en.pdf" title="VT Index">
       <span class="foreignText">
        <span class="workTitle">
         Vinyar Tengwar
        </span>
       </span>
      </a>
      Index
     </p>
     <p class="bodySubsectionText">
      Diego Segui has very kindly provided his index of
      <span class="workTitle">
       <span class="foreignText">
        Vinyar Tengwar
       </span>
      </span>
      for publication here, in both
      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/VT/VT-index-en.pdf">
       English
      </a>
      and (the original)
      <a href="http://www.elvish.org/VT/VT-index-sp.pdf">
       Spanish
      </a>
      versions. Diego writes: "This index lists in alphabetical order all words, roots, affixes, etc. belonging to languages invented by J.R.R. Tolkien which appear attested, discussed or mentioned in the journal Vinyar Tengwar (VT). Its purpose is to provide the researcher with quick access to a reference, or to allow focusing on a particular form by checking it against other locations where information can be found."
     </p>
