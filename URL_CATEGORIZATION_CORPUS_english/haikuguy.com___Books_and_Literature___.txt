     I: haiku
    </b>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    My haiku have appeared in journals around the world. Enjoy this random
    <a href="http://haikuguy.com/randomku.php">
     sampling
    </a>
    .
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <img align="middle" alt="Two Ways cover" border="0" height="300" hspace="0" src="twoways.jpg" width="180"/>
    <br/>
    <br/>
    <i>
     On a Sign Pointing Two Ways
    </i>
    is my only stand-alone collection of haiku (a total of 71), published in a bilingual English-Spanish edition by Rompesilencios Ediciones in Cartago, Colombia, in 2016. Spanish translation and introduction by Carlos Alberto  Castrillón; edited by Victoria Eugenia Gómez M. It is not on sale outside of Colombia (at this time).

       Hoa Lu thi tap
      </i>
     </a>
     is Hoang Quang Thuan's book celebrating the thousand-year celebration of Hanoi: Vietnamese poems with English and French translations. I helped Thai Ba Tan polish the English versions. This is available as an e-book for
     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Hoa-Lu-Vietnams-Ancient-ebook/dp/B0096VGOUY/ref=sr_1_1?s=digital-text&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1347021050&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=hoa+lu">

      Nook
     </a>
     .
    </li>
    <li>
     <a href="http://www.nxbgd.vn/nxbgdvn.aspx?lang=vi-VN&amp;page=news&amp;id=194">
      <i>
       Thi van yen tu
      </i>
     </a>
     is a second book by Hoang Quang Thuan that I helped Thai Ba Tan to translate. Its topic is the founding of a new school of Buddhism in ancient Vietnam.
    </li>

       </i>
      </b>
     </a>
     is a bilingual Spanish-English haiku collection by Colombian poet, Fernando López Rodríguez; I contributed the English translations.
