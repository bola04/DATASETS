           5 May 2012 - CrocLog Podcast Episode 10
          </div>
          <div align="left" style="font: 12px Arial; color:#000000">
           The latest CrocLog Podcast is now available from the
           <a href="http://crocodilian.blogspot.com.au/2012/05/croclog-podcast-episode-10.html">
            Croc Blog
           </a>
           site, and you can also find it on iTunes. This episode features an interview with croc legend Rom Whitaker, someone who has been an inspiration for many of us with his devotion to crocodile and snake conservation. We also talk about record-breaking crocodile bite forces, about Cambodia selling off its National Parks, recovery of the American crocodile in Florida, Nile crocodiles attacking elephants, recent crocodile attacks, and a discussion about Swimming with Crocodiles.
          </div>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <div align="left" style="font: bold 14px Arial; color:#000022">
           17 February 2012 - Swimming with Crocodiles
          </div>
          <div align="left" style="font: 12px Arial; color:#000000">
           If you're in the UK, don't miss the two-part
           <a href="http://crocodilian.blogspot.com.au/2012/02/swimming-with-crocodiles.html">
            Swimming with Crocodiles
           </a>
           which starts on BBC2 at 9pm (after Top Gear) on Sunday 19 February and concludes the following week at the same time. I travel to Botswana with UK adventurer Ben Fogle and we go diving with wild Nile crocodiles in the Okavango Delta to investigate their behaviour and sensory abilities from the perspective of a crocodile. In part 2 we consider look at doing the same thing with saltwater crocodiles in Australia. Also, there have been a number of new CrocLog Podcasts posted, the most recent is
           <a href="http://crocodilian.blogspot.com.au/2012/02/croclog-podcast-episode-9.html">
            Episode 9
           </a>
           featuring an update on the work being done in Belize by ACES.
          </div>

           1 July 2011 - A new look
          </div>
          <div align="left" style="font: 12px Arial; color:#000000">
           Welcome to the newly designed front page for crocodilian.com - something that's been long overdue. The entire website is getting a serious shakedown right now, and I'm being assisted in this enormous task by Brandon Sideleau who's a wildlife photographer and crocodile afficionado based in California. The sections you see above will be joined soon by several exciting new ones. One of these is basically content-ready, but the page design needs to be implemented, and we have plenty of material for the others that just needs tidying up. We're still tweaking the design of the front page, so this may not be the final look, but I hope you like it. Bear in mind we do this in our spare time and run entirely on a voluntary basis, so there are limits to our productivity and talents in web design!
          </div>

           1 July 2011 - A new server
          </div>
          <div align="left" style="font: 12px Arial; color:#000000">
           After 14 years of being hosted by Florida Museum of Natural History, the website Crocodilians - Natural History &amp; Conservation is moving entirely to the crocodilian.com servers. That means the old link
           <a href="http://www.flmnh.ufl.edu/cnhc">
            http://www.flmnh.ufl.edu/cnhc
           </a>
           is going to stop working, so if you haven't already done so please update any links directly to the
           <a href="http://crocodilian.com">
            http://crocodilian.com
           </a>
           homepage. Individual page links should be transferrable by changing
           <em>
            http://www.flmnh.ufl.edu/cnhc/...
           </em>
           to
           <em>
            http://crocodilian.com/cnhc/...
           </em>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           My sincere thanks to FLMNH for hosting the main site for all these years.
          </div>

           9 May 2011 - CrocLog Podcast Episode 5
          </div>
          <div align="left" style="font: 12px Arial; color:#000000">
           The latest episode of the CrocLog Podcast (Ep5) is
           <a href="http://crocodilian.blogspot.com/2011/05/croclog-podcast-episode-5.html">
            now available
           </a>
           . We interview John Brueggen, the director of the St Augustine Alligator Farm, about the first fertile gharial eggs laid in the US; we discuss the new Nile crocodile species
           <em>
            Crocodylus suchus
           </em>
           ; we talk about the TV show
           <em>
            Diving with Crocodiles
           </em>
           which involves doing exactly that with wild Nile crocodiles; and we analyse the latest crocodile news from around the world.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           In other news, the attack database is coming along extremely well and we'll be implementing this here soon, we're updating species distribution maps, and a few other things.
