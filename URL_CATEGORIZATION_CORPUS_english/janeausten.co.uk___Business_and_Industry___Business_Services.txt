            <h3 style="text-align: left;">
             Jane Austen in Bath, England
            </h3>
            <p>
             You’ve arrived at one of the best places to find out more about Jane Austen, our favourite author. Jane paid two long visits to Bath towards the end of the eighteenth century, and from 1801 to 1806 Bath was her home. It also provides the backdrop to two of her novels,
             <em>
              Northanger Abbey
             </em>
             and
             <em>
              Persuasion,
             </em>
             and features in her other novels and in the collection of letters to her sister, Cassandra.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="The Jane Austen Centre entrance" class="alignleft wp-image-23214 size-medium" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 293px) 100vw, 293px" src="https://www.janeausten.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Centre-Tickets-293x300.jpg" srcset="https://www.janeausten.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Centre-Tickets-293x300.jpg 293w, https://www.janeausten.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Centre-Tickets.jpg 433w" width="293"/>
             The
             <strong>
              <a href="https://www.janeausten.co.uk/location-and-opening-Bath-England/">
               Jane Austen Centre
              </a>
             </strong>
             , located at 40 Gay Street in Bath, is a permanent exhibition. Situated in an original Georgian townhouse, it tells the story of Jane’s time in Bath, including the effect that living here had on her and her writing.
            </p>
            <p>
             Come and meet our costumed ‘character’ guides, and enjoy being shown Bath’s fascinating history and its associations with Jane Austen and her family. The Centre also now features a specially commissioned
             <a href="https://www.janeausten.co.uk/jane-austen-waxwork/">
              <strong>
               waxwork of Jane
              </strong>
             </a>
             – two years in the making, with the assistance of an internationally-renowned sculptor, an FBI-trained forensic artist and a Bafta and Emmy award-winning costume designer.
            </p>
            <p>
             We have an extensive
             <strong>
              <a href="/the-jane-austen-centre/group-visits/">
               Group Visits programme
              </a>
              ,
