        <h2>
         So What is Linkpendium, Anyway?
        </h2>
        <p>
         Linkpendium is a 10,000,000+ resource directory to everything on the Web about families worldwide and genealogically-relevant
 information about U.S. states and counties.  We cover both free and subscription sites, with a strong emphasis upon free resources
 provided by libraries, other government agencies, genealogical and historical societies, and individuals.
        </p>
        <p>
         We are particularly proud of our unique indexes to online biographies.
        </p>
        <h2>
         How do I use Linkpendium?
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="#search">
           Family Discoverer
          </a>
          search engine at the top of this page covers 2,800,000 of the pages we index, and is the fastest way to
 explore genealogical and family history on the Internet.
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="#jump_city">
           city
          </a>
          or
          <a href="#jump_county">
           county
          </a>
          or
          <a href="#jump_family">
           family surname
          </a>
          navigation tools in the right column let you jump directly to a Linkpendium page of resources.
     This is a faster way to reach the same pages you can find by following the links below.
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="#counties">
           Locality Directory
          </a>
          below lets you follow links to resources for the U.S. states and counties that interest you.
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="#families">
           Surname Directory
          </a>
          below lets you follow links to resources for families worldwide that interest you.  By following the links, you will see many possible variations on your family name.
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           IMPORTANT: The locality and family surname pages include many resources that could not be included in the search engine.
          </i>
          When you are trying to break down a genealogical brick wall, always use the locality
     and family surname pages in addition to the Family Discoverer.  ;)
         </li>
        </ul>
