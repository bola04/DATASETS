         <h1>
          Welcome to Recovery
         </h1>
         <p>
          <strong>
           You  can recover from addiction.
          </strong>
          This website can help you or a loved one overcome addiction. The information is independent and evidence-based, and includes references to  original and authoritative material where possible.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can use this site in a few ways.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Decide if you have an  addiction.
          </li>
          <li>
           Learn about the genetics of  addiction.
          </li>
          <li>
           Read about alcohol, tranquilizers, marijuana, cocaine,  and opioids.
          </li>
          <li>
           Learn relapse prevention  skills that will improve your chances of success.
          </li>
          <li>
           Learn how to help a family member with an addiction.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           You  are not alone.
          </strong>
          Many people have overcome addiction  and are now enjoying a better life. It can be done. You too can do it.
         </p>
         <h2>
          What is Addiction?
         </h2>
         <!-- Bookbar -->
         <div id="bookbar">
          <p align="center">
           <strong>
            <a href="https://www.iwanttochangemylife.org/book.htm">
             Learn More
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="https://www.iwanttochangemylife.org/book.htm">
            <img alt="I Want to Change My Life" border="1" height="200" src="/images/I-Want-To-Change-My-Life-Cover-130x200.jpg" width="130"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p align="center">
           <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/1897572239">
            <img alt="Buy from Amazon" border="0" height="28" src="/images/buy-from-amazon.jpg" width="90"/>
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <!-- Bookbar end -->
         <p>
          A substance addiction meets two criteria:
         </p>
         <ol start="1">
          <li>
           <strong>
            You have difficulty controlling how much you use or how long you use.
           </strong>
           For example, one drink leads to more drinks, or one line of cocaine leads to more.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            You continue to use even though it has negative consequences to your life.
           </strong>
           For example, you continue to drink even though it has hurt your relationships.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Those two criteria define all addictions.  They are true for alcohol and drug addiction, but they're also true for gambling  addiction, eating disorders, and sexual addiction.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           There  are different stages of addictions.
          </strong>
          The late stage is  the non-functioning addict. They've lost their job and have to use every day. It's  what people think addiction is like, but that stereotype is rare.
         </p>
         <p>
          The early stage is the functioning addict. They  still have a job and their relationships are intact, but their life is suffering  because of their addiction. That is the most common scenario. You don't have to  suffer major losses to have an addiction.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The  consequences of addiction get worse over time.
          </strong>
          Addiction  is a progressive disease. It's never easy to quit. But if you've already suffered  negative consequences and don't want them to get worse, there's never a better  time to quit than now.
         </p>
