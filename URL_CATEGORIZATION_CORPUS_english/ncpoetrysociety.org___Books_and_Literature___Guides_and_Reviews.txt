        <h2 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/home/" rel="bookmark">
          Home
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/home/" rel="bookmark" title="6:00 am">
          <span class="entry-date">
           June 22, 2018
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="meta-sep">
          by
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/author/falco/" title="View all posts by David">
           David
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>

         <div class="pf-content">
          <p>
           <img alt="NC_PoetrySociety-SM" class="size-medium wp-image-3057 aligncenter" height="128" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/NC_PoetrySociety-SM-300x128.png" srcset="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/NC_PoetrySociety-SM-300x128.png 300w, http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/NC_PoetrySociety-SM.png 701w" width="300"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you would like to join the Society and receive our monthly e-newsletter and thrice-yearly print newsletter, contact
           <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/membership">
            membership
           </a>
           .  Become part of a state-wide community of poets and poetry lovers!
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Download our
            <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/welcome-to-ncpoetrysociety/2018-ncps-membership-brochure/" rel="attachment wp-att-3072">
             2018 NCPS Membership Brochure
            </a>
            .
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The North Carolina Poetry Society is an inclusive, welcoming community that does not discriminate on the basis of race, gender, sexual orientation, ethnicity, religion, age, political preference, or any other category which has been used to divide human beings from each other and the natural world.  We value diverse voices and variety of expression.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            If you have any questions or comments about the site,
            <a href="mailto:calnordt@aol.com" target="_blank">
             contact us
            </a>
            .
           </em>
          </p>
          <h2 style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            NCPS News &amp; Events
            <br/>
           </strong>
          </h2>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Susan Laughter Meyers Poetry Fellowship
           </strong>
           <br/>
           The submission period for the first annual Susan Laughter Meyers Poetry Fellowship at Weymouth begins on
           <strong>
            May 1
           </strong>
           .  The recipient will be awarded a one-week stay at the Weymouth Center, a $500.00 stipend, publication of a poem in
           <em>
            Pinesong
           </em>
           (NCPS’s annual anthology of award-winning poems), and an opportunity to read or present a program at an NCPS meeting at Weymouth.  For more information and submission guidelines,
           <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/meyers-fellowship">
            click here
           </a>
           .  The final deadline is
           <strong>
            July 31
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            New NC Poet Laureate Named!
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Jaki Shelton Green
           </strong>
           has just been named Poet Laureate of North Carolina, the ninth overall, first African-American, and third woman to serve in this post.  She won the North Carolina Award for Literature in 2013 and was inducted into the state’s Literary Hall of Fame in 2014.  She was also the first Piedmont Laureate in 2009.  She succeeds Shelby Stephenson, who has been a wonderful and much-loved champion of poetry across the state.  Good luck, Jaki, as our new literary ambassador!
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Remembering Rebecca Ball Rust
           </strong>
           <br/>
           Friends, we have lost another leading literary light.  Rebecca Ball Rust, who founded the North Carolina Haiku Society in 1979 and served as president of the North Carolina Poetry Society, passed away in Aiken, South Carolina on April 27, 2018, at the age of 89.  For a more detailed description of her legacy and samples of her work,
           <a href="https://nc-haiku.org/remembering-rebecca-rust/">
            click here
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            2018 Student Contest Winners
           </strong>
           <br/>
           The winners of the 2018 student contests sponsored by the North Carolina Poetry Society have been announced!
           <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/2018-student-contest-winners">
            Click here
           </a>
           to read the results.  We salute the students and their teachers and parents for their good work, and we hope to meet many of them at Awards Day on May 12 at Weymouth.  Heart-felt thanks to our Student Contests Coordinator, Michael Beadle, and to the judges of each contest.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            2018 Adult Contest Winners
           </strong>
           <br/>
           The winners of the 2018 adult contests sponsored by the North Carolina Poetry Society have been announced!
           <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/2018-adult-contest-winners">
            Click here
           </a>
           to read the results.  We salute all the winners and look forward to hearing many of them read at Awards Day on May 12 at Weymouth.  Special thanks to our Adult Contests Coordinator, Chris Tonelli, and to the judges of each contest.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Our Next Meeting on September 15
           </strong>
           <br/>
           The next meeting of the North Carolina Poetry Society will take place on Saturday, September 15, at Weymouth Center for the Arts in Southern Pines.  In the morning, winners of the Randall Jarrell Poetry Prize and Brockman-Campbell Award will read their poetry.  After lunch, poet and journalist Dan Szczesny will lead a workshop on “finding your lyrical voice on the road.”  For a more detailed schedule,
           <a href="http://www.ncpoetrysociety.org/events">
            click here
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Poetry Reading at McIntyre’s Books
           </strong>
           <br/>
           The next poetry reading at McIntyre’s Books, co-sponsored by the NC Poetry Society, will take place at 2:00 p.m. on
           <strong>
            Sunday, June 24
           </strong>
           .  Featured poets will be Chris Abbate, Dorothy Baird, and Beth Copeland.  Fearrington Village is located on 15-501 between Pittsboro and Chapel Hill.  This monthly reading series is hosted by Carolyn and Guy York.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>

          </p>
