         <a class="faa faa-small clearfix" href="/locate-an-attorney">
          <span class="gradient_blue">
           FIND AN ATTORNEY
          </span>
         </a>
         <p>
          The Special Needs Alliance is a national organization comprised of attorneys committed to helping individuals with disabilities, their families and the professionals who serve them. Many of our members have loved ones with special needs; all of them work regularly with
          <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#governmentbenefitprograms">
           public benefits
          </a>
          , guardianships/conservatorships,
          <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#lifecareplan">
           planning for disabilities
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#specialeducation">
           special education
          </a>
          issues. They collaborate with advocates throughout the special needs community to improve quality of life for individuals with disabilities.
         </p>
         <h2>
          SNA Members Help Families
         </h2>
         <p>
          Special needs attorneys have daily experience with the issues that matter to individuals with disabilities– but which are largely unfamiliar to other counsel. They can advise you about:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#governmentbenefitprograms">
            qualifying and applying for appropriate public benefits
           </a>
           ;
          </li>
          <li>
           creating
           <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#specialneedstrust">
            special needs trusts (SNTs)
           </a>
           to protect eligibility for means-tested government programs;
          </li>
          <li>
           availability of local services and supports;
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#specialeducation">
            special education
           </a>
           ;
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#guardianship">
            guardianship
           </a>
           and
           <a href="/frequently-asked-questions#guardianship">
            powers of attorney
           </a>
           .
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="/sna-can-help-families">
           Learn more »
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2>
          SNA Members Help Professionals
         </h2>
         <p>
          Special needs attorneys can offer guidance to the trustees, financial planners and personal injury attorneys who serve individuals with disabilities and their families. They can advise:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           personal injury attorneys on
           <a href="/structured-settlements-and-snts">
            whether or not a special needs trust (SNT) should be created to hold settlement funds
           </a>
           ;
          </li>
          <li>
           structured settlement brokers on the apportionment of personal injury awards and the use of special needs trusts in conjunction with structured settlements;
          </li>
          <li>
           trustees regarding SNT distributions that will not disqualify one for public benefits.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="/sna-can-help-professionals">
           Learn more »
          </a>
         </p>
