        <div class="hidden-lg hidden-xs hidden-md">
         <h3>
          About the 12 Step Program
         </h3>
        </div>
        <p>
         Twelve Step programs are well known for their use in recovering from addictive
                         and dysfunctional behaviors. The first 12 step program began with
         <a href="http://aa.org" target="_blank">
          Alcoholics Anonymous (A.A.)
         </a>
         in the 1930s and has since grown to be the most widely used approach in
                         dealing not only with recovery from alcoholism, but also from drug abuse and
                         various other addictive and dysfunctional behaviors.
        </p>
        <p>
         The first book written to cover the 12 step program was titled
                         "Alcoholics Anonymous", affectionately known as the
         <a href="/references/the-big-book/">
          Big Book
         </a>
         by program members.
                         Following the subsequent extensive growth of twelve step programs for
                         other addictive and dysfunctional behaviors, many additional books were
                         written and recordings and videos were produced. These cover the steps
                         in greater detail and how people have specifically applied the steps in
                         their lives. An extensive chronology and background about the history of
                         A.A. has been put together at
         <a href="http://www.dickb.com" target="_blank">
          Dick B.'s website
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The twelve steps of the program are listed above and
         <a href="/the-12-steps/">
          on the steps page
         </a>
         in generic form.
                         Other groups who have adopted the 12 steps to address their own
                         particular addictive or dysfunctional behavior have
         <a href="/references/12-step-versions/">
          similar ideas, usually with only minor variations
         </a>
         .
                         These steps are meant to be worked
         <em>
          sequentially
         </em>
         as a process of
                         getting rid of addictive behaviors and should result in a
                         growth in freedom and happiness, as outlined in
         <a href="/references/commonly-used/the-promises/">
          the Promises
         </a>
         . The general governing
                         approach for A.A. groups was originally laid out in the
         <a href="/references/commonly-used/the-12-traditions/">
          Twelve Traditions
         </a>
         , and they
                         remain the guiding principles for most 12 step groups today.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         There is a wealth of further information about 12 Step programs in
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/12_steps" target="_blank">
          Wikipedia
         </a>
         ,
                         including a
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_twelve-step_groups" target="_blank">
          list of 12 step groups
         </a>
         , and also from the numerous links in our
         <a href="/forum/index.php?/links/">
          directory of recovery related websites
         </a>
         .
         <br/>
        </p>
