              <h3>
               Free, Online: Gardening for Pollinators
              </h3>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               This free, online course, available April 6-August 3, 2018, shows you how to convert your yard into habitat for native insects, birds, and other pollinators throughout their life cycles--all at your own pace. The course kicks off a season of
               <em>
                free public programs
               </em>
               --including
               <a class="internal-link" href="/learn/pollinate-new-england.html" title="Pollinate New England">
                demonstration gardens and workshops at 12 locations
               </a>
               throughout the region--offered through
               <a class="internal-link" href="/learn/pollinate-new-england.html" title="Pollinate New England">
                Pollinate New England
               </a>
               , the Society's response to the pollinator crisis. The initiative is made possible by a grant from the Institute of Museum and Library Services.
               <a class="external-link" href="https://46858.blackbaudhosting.com/46858/Pollinators">
                Please help us meet the required match
               </a>
               .
              </p>

              <h3>
               Support Pollinate New England
              </h3>
              <p>
              </p>
              <p>
               The New England native sundial lupine
               <span style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34);">
                supports three rare or endangered butterflies--the Karner blue, the Persius duskywing,and the frosted elfin--which, in turn,pollinate this globally threatened plant. If lupine vanishes, at least two of those butterflies likely will too. If the butterflies go, lupine declines. A life-giving dance becomes a death spiral. Not all native plants are rare, but many pollinators are crashing--and
                <em>
                 all
                </em>
                plants (including crops), wildlife, and people are in trouble. Please
                <a class="external-link" href="https://46858.blackbaudhosting.com/46858/Pollinators">
                 support Pollinate New England
                </a>
                to help us support pollinators.
               </span>
              </p>
              <div class="m_7410058563787375931gmail_signature">
               <p style="color: rgb(34, 34, 34);">
                <img alt="Karner blue butterfly" class="image-left" src="/images/blurbs/karner_blue_pixabay.jpg/image_mini"/>

               State of the Plants
              </h3>
              <p>
              </p>

                </a>
               </em>

               <a class="internal-link" href="/support/ways-to-support.html" title="Ways to Support">

               </a>
