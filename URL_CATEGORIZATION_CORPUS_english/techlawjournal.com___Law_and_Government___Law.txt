              <b>
               <a href="http://www.techlawjournal.com/topstories/2016/0613.asp">
                Supreme Court Addresses 
 Enhanced Damages for Patent Infringement
               </a>
              </b>
              .
              <br/>
              6/13. The
              <a href="http://www.supremecourt.gov/">
               Supreme Court
              </a>
              released its unanimous
              <a href="http://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/15pdf/14-1513_db8e.pdf">
               opinion
              </a>
              in
              <u>
               Halo 
 Electronics v. Pulse Electronics
              </u>
              and
              <u>
               Stryker v. Zimmer
              </u>
              regarding the award of enhanced 
 damages for patent infringement under
              <a href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/35/284">
               35 
 U.S.C. § 284
              </a>
              . The Supreme Court rejected the approach announced by the Federal Circuit in
              <u>
               In 
 Re Seagate
              </u>
              , but provided little guidance to the lower courts in its place. However, it wrote this 
 much. First, the District Courts possess discretion. Second, there is no "rigid formula". 
 Third, treble damages are only to be awarded for "egregious cases of misconduct beyond typical 
 infringement".
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.techlawjournal.com/topstories/2016/0607.asp">
                House Judiciary Subcommittee 
 Approves Bill to Undo Chevron Deference
               </a>
               .
               <br/>
              </b>
              6/7. The
              <a href="http://judiciary.house.gov/">
               House Judiciary Committee
              </a>
              's (HJC) Subcommittee 
 on Regulatory Reform, Commercial and Antitrust Law amended and passed HR 4768 
 [
              <a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/114th-congress/house-bill/4768">
               LOC
              </a>
              |
              <a href="https://washingtonwatch.com/bills/show/114_HR_4768.html">
               WW
              </a>
              ], the "Separation 
 of Powers Restoration Act of 2016", by a vote of 12-8. The bill as introduced amends the Administrative Procedure Act (APA) to provide that the judiciary 
 should not defer to an agency's interpretation of statutes. The Subcommittee approved an
              <a href="http://www.techlawjournal.com/docs/2016/hr4768ains.pdf">
               amendment in the nature of a 
 substitute
              </a>
              (AINS) that adds that the judiciary should not defer to an agency's interpretation 
 when it acts under an agency procedure statute specific to that agency. Also, it provides that 
 courts should not defer to an agency's interpretation of its rules.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <a href="topstories/2016/0606.asp">
                Senate Intelligence Authorization Bill Would 
 Reduce PCLOB Oversight
               </a>
               <br/>
              </b>
              6/6. Section 603 of S 3017 
 [
              <a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/114th-congress/senate-bill/3107">
               LOC
              </a>
              |
              <a href="https://washingtonwatch.com/bills/show/114_SN_3107.html">
               WW
              </a>
              ], the "Intelligence 
 Authorization Act Fiscal Year 2017", would limit the authority of the
              <a href="https://www.pclob.gov/">
               Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board
              </a>
              (PCLOB). Its 
 current mission is examining the impact of anti-terror laws and programs on "privacy and 
 civil liberties". This bill would reduce this to examining "privacy and civil liberties 
 of United States persons". The
              <a href="http://www.intelligence.senate.gov/">
               Senate Intelligence 
 Committee
              </a>
              (SIC) approved this bill on May 24, before its introduction on June 6.
             </p>
             <p>
              <b>
               <a href="http://www.techlawjournal.com/topstories/2016/0601.asp">
                FCC Seeks Further Comments 
 on 5.9 GHz Band
               </a>
               <br/>
              </b>
              6/1. The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) released a
              <a href="http://transition.fcc.gov/Daily_Releases/Daily_Business/2016/db0601/FCC-16-68A1.pdf">
               Public 
 Notice
              </a>
              (PN) that requests more comments in its rulemaking proceeding regarding shared use of the 
 5.9 GHz band. At stake is the future use of this band for Wi-Fi and car safety 
 technology.
             </p>
