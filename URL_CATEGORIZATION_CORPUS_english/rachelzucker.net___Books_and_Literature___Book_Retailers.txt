        <img alt="" class="alignleft" height="212" src="http://www.rachelzucker.net/img/zuckerphoto.jpg" width="204"/>
        <strong>
         Rachel Zucker is the author of nine books, most recently, a memoir,
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.spdbooks.org/Producte/9781933996431/mothers.aspx" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.spdbooks.org']);">
           MOTHERs,
          </a>
         </em>
         and a double collection of prose and poetry,
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.wavepoetry.com/products/the-pedestrians" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.wavepoetry.com']);">
           The Pedestrians
          </a>
         </em>
         . Her book
         <em>
          Museum of Accidents
         </em>
         was a finalist for the National Book Critics Circle Award. She received a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship in 2013. Zucker teaches poetry at New York University and is currently delivering a series of lectures on the intersection of poetry, confession, ethics and disobedience as part of the
         <a href="http://bagleywrightlectures.org/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://bagleywrightlectures.org']);" target="_blank">
          Bagley Wright Lecture Series
         </a>
