     <p>
      <span class="title">
       About SFPA
      </span>
      <br/>
      The Science Fiction &amp; Fantasy Poetry Association was founded as the Science Fiction Poetry Association in 1978 to bring together poets and readers interested in speculative poetry. Members voted to rename the organization in 2017. What is sf/f poetry? Everybody has a unique definition. To be sure, it is poetry (we'll leave
      <em>
       that
      </em>
      definition to you), but it’s poetry with some element of
      <em>
       speculation
      </em>
      —usually
       science fiction, fantasy, or horror, though some include surrealism, some
       straight science. SFPA’s founder, Suzette Haden Elgin, shared her thoughts on sf poetry
       in the aptly titled
      <a href="http://www.sfwa.org/members/elgin/SFPoetry.html" target="_blank">
       “About
       Science Fiction Poetry.”
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Examples of speculative poetry abound. Editor's Choice
       poems are linked on the
      <a href="sl/slarchive.html">
       Star*Line archive
      </a>
      .
       Our
      <a href="promos.html">
       Promotions
      </a>
      page offers .pdf samplers.
       The
      <a href="halloween.html">
       Halloween
      </a>
      page
       features the annual SFPA poetry podcast. Visit SFPA's
      <a href="markets.html">
       Markets
      </a>
      to
       find magazines specializing in genre poetry and the
      <a href="links.html">
       Links
      </a>
      for individual
       sf poets’ websites. Our
      <a href="books.html">
       Speculative Poetry
       Books
      </a>
      lists speculative poetry collections and links to booksellers. SFPA’s official journal,
      <a href="starline.html">
       <em>
        Star*Line,
       </em>
      </a>
      publishes
         speculative poetry and is a wonderful way to begin your poetic journey to the
       stars.
     </p>
     <p>
      As well as an annual open contest, SFPA offers yearly awards for speculative poetry: the
      <a href="rhysling.html">
       Rhysling Awards
      </a>
      for individual poems, the
      <a href="dwarfstars.html">
       Dwarf Stars Award
      </a>
      for short-short poems, and the
      <a href="elgin.html">
       Elgin Awards
      </a>
      for genre poetry books and chapbooks, named for the SFPA founder.
     </p>
     <p>
      SFPA also publishes the yearly
      <em>
       <a href="rhysling.html">
        Rhysling Anthology
       </a>
       ,
      </em>
      comprised
       of works nominated for the Rhysling Awards for Best Poems
       of the Year, and the
      <a href="dwarfstars.html">
       <em>
        Dwarf Stars
       </em>
      </a>
      anthology for short-short speculative poems. Visit the
      <a href="rhysling.html">
       Rhysling
      </a>
      and
      <a href="dwarfstars.html">
       Dwarf Stars
      </a>
      pages to order copies. Don’t miss this
       unique compilation of verse from some of the finest poets working in the
       fields of science-fiction, fantasy, and horror poetry!
     </p>
     <p>
      The anthology
      <a href="books/alchemy.html">
       <em>
        The
       Alchemy of Stars
       </em>
      </a>
      features the
       Rhysling Award-winning poems from 1978 to 2004, an introduction by Jane Yolen, and an
       afterword by Suzette Haden Elgin. See the
      <a href="books/alchemy.html">
       <em>
        Alchemy
       </em>
       page
      </a>
      for
       ordering information.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="ra/rhysarchive.html">
       Rhysling
       Archive
      </a>
      contains cover images, tables of contents, and
       editorial information for all the Rhysling Awards and
      <em>
       Rhysling
       Anthologies
      </em>
      since 1978.
     </p>
     <p class="small">
      SPECIAL
       NOTE TO AUTHORS: Inclusion in the
      <em>
       Rhysling Anthology
      </em>
      is by member
       nomination only. Do
      <em>
       not
      </em>
      submit your poems to this anthology. Membership
       in the SFPA is not required for work to be nominated.
     </p>
