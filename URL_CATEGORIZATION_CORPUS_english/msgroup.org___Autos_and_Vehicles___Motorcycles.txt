       <h2>
        Safest Possible Motorcycling
       </h2>
       <div class="dashedline">
        The fundamental message found throughout this site.
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </div>
       Since the vast majority of motorcycle accidents involve some level of 'rider error', 
                 it makes a great deal of sense to strive for making
       <b>
        no rider errors
       </b>
       .
       <br/>
       <br/>
       The way to achieve making motorcycling as safe as possible is to make sure that
       <b>
        <u>
         you
        </u>
        are
        <u>
         never
        </u>
        the cause of an 
                 accident
       </b>
       (yours or for anybody else), that you
       <b>
        avoid
       </b>
       those situations that are demonstrably less 
                 safe than is reasonable, and that you have developed the
       <b>
        skills and judgment
       </b>
       to deal with any other dangerous situations that may occur.
      </div>

      <h2>
       Case Studies
      </h2>
      <div class="dashedline">
       Here are eight real-world 'incidents' that demonstrate the 
 				importance of understanding the fundamentals included in the 
 				Safety Tip articles.
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </div>
      <div class="listitem">
       <br/>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=9">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label61">
          Normal Braking Skill Are Usually Enough
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label62">
         Riding on a freeway when everything, including the weather, is perfect. Then an incident occurs two lanes away. Normal braking skills would have saved this man's life.
        </span>
       </p>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=5">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label50">
          Conspicuity
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label531">
         We drive a green car ... and we kill a motorcyclist.  We didn't see him despite the fact that we have unobstructed vision for 1000 feet both ahead and behind us.  Yep, we turned left in front of him.
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="clearlist">
       </div>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=10">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label532">
          Expert Witness Changes Case Focus
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label52">
         Motorcyclist drives over a drainage grate and is ejected from bike suffering broken bones and road rash as a result. Lack of roadway maintenance?
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="clearlist">
       </div>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=6">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label53">
          Speed Kills
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label54">
         A lady motorcyclist dies.  We want to find out who killed her.  Sometimes you simply have to ask 'what if?'
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="clearlist">
       </div>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=7">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label60">
          Left Turner is not always the bad guy
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label59">
         A lady motorcyclist is paralyzed.  Her fault, but not unless your attorney understands the significance of rider errors. We will demonstrate just how painful that can be to the left turner.
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="clearlist">
       </div>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=1">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label4">
          Target Fixation
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label2">
         A woman rider in Texas, second in a group of three, mid-afternoon, perfect weather, excellent road conditions: in a curve she
         <b>
          steers
         </b>
         her motorcycle across a double line and into an oncoming pickup truck.
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="clearlist">
       </div>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=4">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label1">
          Honda 1800 Goldwing Runaway
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label5">
         Husband and wife mount their Goldwings in their driveway.  Husband leaves first.  Wife suddenly experiences the motorcycle accelerate (on its own).  It launches her across the street and into the curb.  Bike is totalled.
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="clearlist">
       </div>
       <h3>
        <a href="CASESTUDY1.aspx?num=8">
         <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label70">
          Deep Pockets and Hired Guns
         </span>
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <span id="ContentPlaceHolder1_Label71">
         Motorcyclist, afraid that he is going to collide with 18-wheeler, slams on both brakes and promptly ends up on the ground seriously injured. The trucking company is sued.
        </span>
       </p>
