         <h1>
          Research guide
          <span>
           International Humanitarian Law
          </span>
         </h1>
         <div class="content">
          <img alt="International Humanitarian Law - Research Guide International Law" class="alignleft" height="125" sizes="(max-width: 80px) 100vw, 80px" src="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/hum-80x125.jpg" srcset="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/hum-80x125.jpg 80w, https://www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/hum-101x159.jpg 101w" width="80"/>
          <p>
           International humanitarian law, also known as the laws of war or law of armed conflict is the part of public international law that regulates international and non-international armed conflict. International Humanitarian Law consists of the rules applicable during the conflict. These rules also apply to a situation of occupation arising from armed conflict. The International Humanitarian Law rules can be found in both treaties and international customary law.
          </p>
          <a class="more" href="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/research-guides/war-and-peace/international-humanitarian-law/">
           Check our Research Guide on International Humanitarian Law

        <div class="bg">
         <h1>
          News and events
          <span>
           Lay Down Your Arms! in Every Bookcase
          </span>
         </h1>
         <div class="content">
          <p>
           Everyone knows: without Bertha von Suttner the would be no Peace Palace. Her world famous book Lay Down Your Arms! has been instrumental in the creation of the PCA, the establishment of the Peace Palace and the peace movement. It has therefore been a foundation for the message of peace that the Peace Palace still carries out every day. Her work however is not widely read (in Dutch). The Dutch translation of Lay Down Your Arms! Dates back to 1925 and has become virtually unreadable. For that reason, the Peace Palace Library wishes to publish a modern rephrasing of Lay Down Your Arms! in honour of Bertha von Suttners 175th birthday.
           <br/>
           <img alt="Could Trump and Kim agree to a Peace Treaty ending the Korean War?" class="" height="200" src="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/I-3-waffennieder.jpg" style="margin-top:10px" width="276"/>
          </p>
          <a class="more" href="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/2018/06/lay-down-your-arms-in-every-bookcase/">
           Read more

          <h1>
           Bertha von Suttner (1843-1914)
          </h1>
          <img alt="" class="attachment-content-width size-content-width" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 583px) 100vw, 583px" src="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Bertha-von-Suttner-02.jpg" srcset="//www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Bertha-von-Suttner-02.jpg 583w, https://www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Bertha-von-Suttner-02-300x154.jpg 300w, https://www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Bertha-von-Suttner-02-45x23.jpg 45w, https://www.peacepalacelibrary.nl/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Bertha-von-Suttner-02-530x273.jpg 530w" width="583"/>
          <div class="hover">
           <p>
            Bertha Sophia Felicita von Suttner was a 19th century author and peace activist from Austria. Her most famous work, the anti-war novel titled Die Waffen Nieder (Lay Down Your Arms), became an international bestseller and was published in all the major world languages of the time. Upon the success of her novel, Bertha von Suttner quickly rose to prominence within the international peace movement that was gaining ground in Europe and in the United States of America. She founded the Austrian Peace Society, contributed to the establishment of many other pacifist organizations throughout Europe and founded a peace journal, Die Waffen Nieder, which was later succeeded by the journal Die Friedens-Warte.
           </p>
