       </h1>
       <h1 id="WelcometoWifidogsWebSite">
        Welcome to Wifidog's Web Site
       </h1>
       <p>
        The Wifidog project is an open source captive portal solution.  As such, it has three main functions:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         Location-aware delivery of internal or external content
        </li>
        <li>
         Authentication and authorization
        </li>
        <li>
         Centralizaed network monitoring
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        Most of the contributions to its development came from wireless community groups, so this is the model with the most support.  However, Wifidog has an extremely generic design.  As such, it is now used by several other types of organizations.  With relatively small amount of development it can also be used for
        <a class="wiki" href="/wiki/WirelessCommunityModels">
         many other business models
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        You may also be interested in seeing some of the
        <a class="wiki" href="/wiki/Community">
         community wireless groups, WISP's, universities, and private businesses
        </a>
        currently using Wifidog across the globe, and who
        <a class="wiki" href="/wiki/About">
         worked on developing Wifidog
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        If you want to keep up to date on the project we strongly suggest you look at the
        <a href="http://dev.wifidog.org/timeline">
         timeline
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="I have questions or I need help icon" src="/raw-attachment/wiki/WikiStart/wifidog_wiki_help.png" title="I have questions or I need help icon"/>
        <br/>
        If you have a questions about the Wifidog project, they may be answered in the
        <a class="wiki" href="/wiki/FAQ">
         FAQ
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        If not, please read
        <a class="wiki" href="/wiki/Contact%20/%20Support">
         how to get help or report a bug
        </a>
        to increase your odds of getting a satisfactory answer
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="I want to contribute to Wifidog icon" src="/raw-attachment/wiki/WikiStart/wifidog_wiki_contribute.png" title="I want to contribute to Wifidog icon"/>
        <br/>
        If you want to contribute to the development effort of Wifidog, please read the page on
        <a class="wiki" href="/wiki/Contribute">
         how to contribute
        </a>
       </p>
