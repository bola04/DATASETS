         <h2 class="title">
          About John Bellairs
         </h2>
         <div class="entry">
          <p>
           <img alt="John Bellairs" class="image-right-pad" height="200" src="images/John Bellairs.png" width="200"/>
           <strong>
            John  Anthony Bellairs
           </strong>
           (1938-1991) is the author of  the fifteen acclaimed Gothic mystery
           <a href="the_work/index.html">
            novels
           </a>
           in the Lewis Barnavelt, Anthony Monday,  and Johnny Dixon series, including
           <a href="the_work/housecw/index.html">
            <em>
             The House with a Clock in Its Walls
            </em>
           </a>
           (1973),
           <em>
            <a href="the_work/treasureaw/index.html">
             The Treasure of Alpheus Winterborn
            </a>
           </em>
           (1978), and
           <a href="the_work/cursebf/index.html">
            <em>
             The Curse of the Blue Figurine
            </em>
           </a>
           (1983).  Many of his books were illustrated by prominent artist-illustrator Edward Gorey.
          </p>
          <p>
           Bellairs was born in Marshall, Michigan, earned degrees from Notre Dame and the University of Chicago, taught at various Midwestern and New England colleges, and later lived and wrote in Haverhill, Massachusetts.
          </p>
