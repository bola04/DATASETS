       <h2>
        Warum wir YogaKitchen heißen?
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="Yoga Düsseldorf, YogaKitchen, Hatha und Vinyasa Yoga in Düsseldorf Oberkassel " class="rahmen_right" src="http://www.yogakitchen-duesseldorf.de/bilder/yogakitchen-yoga-studio-in-duesseldorf-oberkassel-11.jpg"/>
        Bei uns "kochst" und "backst" Du sprichwörtlich Deinen Körper. Dein geistiger Zustand wird dadurch verändert, Dein Bewußtsein erweitert - Du kommst durch Yoga 
        ganz bei Dir an. Das Rezept ist einfach: Atmen und Bewegen. Danach fühlst Du Dich stärker, ausgeglichener, glücklicher und zufriedener.
       </p>
       <p>
        <cite lang="en">
         "The technique of the physical (yoga) practices can be compared with the process of cooking ... you take a cooking pot on a 
				fire ... as the food approaches its final stages of preparation, our attention shifts to the finer aspects ... until eventually 
				we switch off the heat ... the changes affected in the food are permanent and irreversible in nature. Our body is like a pot that 
				contains our subtle self. The practices are intended for the subtle self and should not be limited to the body ... always remember, 
				we are cooking the food and not the pot..."
        </cite>
        (
        <a href="http://www.yogavillage.org/teacher.html" target="_blank" title="Yogi Manmoyanand, Yogavillage ">
         Yogi Manmoyanand
        </a>
        )
       </p>

        <h2>
         Warum wir YogaKitchen heißen?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Yoga Düsseldorf, YogaKitchen, Hatha und Vinyasa Yoga in Düsseldorf Oberkassel " class="rahmen_right" src="http://www.yogakitchen-duesseldorf.de/bilder/yogakitchen-yoga-studio-in-duesseldorf-oberkassel-11.jpg"/>

         ganz bei Dir an. Das Rezept ist einfach: Atmen und Bewegen. Danach fühlst Du Dich stärker, ausgeglichener, glücklicher und zufriedener.
        </p>
        <p>
         <cite lang="en">
          "The technique of the physical (yoga) practices can be compared with the process of cooking ... you take a cooking pot on a 
 				fire ... as the food approaches its final stages of preparation, our attention shifts to the finer aspects ... until eventually 
 				we switch off the heat ... the changes affected in the food are permanent and irreversible in nature. Our body is like a pot that 
 				contains our subtle self. The practices are intended for the subtle self and should not be limited to the body ... always remember, 
 				we are cooking the food and not the pot..."
         </cite>
         (
         <a href="http://www.yogavillage.org/teacher.html" target="_blank" title="Yogi Manmoyanand, Yogavillage ">
