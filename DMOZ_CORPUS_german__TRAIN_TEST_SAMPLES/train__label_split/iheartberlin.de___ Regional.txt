       <h2>
        <a href="http://www.iheartberlin.de/2018/08/01/the-unique-documentary-about-the-history-of-fusion-festival/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to The Unique Documentary About the History of Fusion Festival">
         The Unique Documentary About the History of Fusion Festival
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="attachment-large size-large wp-post-image" height="427" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/fusion-640x427.jpeg" srcset="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/fusion.jpeg 640w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/fusion-320x214.jpeg 320w" width="640"/>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: right;">
        <span style="color: #999999;">
         Photo: Ole Ukena –
         <a href="http://www.eyeofo.com" style="color: #999999;">
          Eye of Ole
         </a>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-weight: 400;">
         Good old Fusion turned incredible 20 years old last year! And is now looking back on 20 wild years of building the alternative festival dream that we know today. You have never been to the famous Fusion Festival? Then let us explain the magic shortly: The
        </span>
        <span style="font-weight: 400;">
         Fusion grew out of the idea to create a space for all free-spirited individuals, to be what they want and create a society they feel comfortable in. So instead of only focussing on great music and filling big stages, Fusion is famous for creating its alternate reality that fosters love, sustainable living, and compassion. Who do we have to thank for all these years of intense festival partying? Kudos goes out to Kulturkosmos e.V.!
        </span>
       </p>

       <h2>
        <a href="http://www.iheartberlin.de/2018/07/30/who-was-this-christopher-street-guy-my-sisters-first-pride-parade/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Who was this Christopher Street Guy? – My Sister’s First Pride Parade">
         Who was this Christopher Street Guy? – My Sister’s First Pride Parade
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="attachment-large size-large wp-post-image" height="427" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berlin-Pride-CSD-2018-0916-640x427.jpg" srcset="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berlin-Pride-CSD-2018-0916-640x427.jpg 640w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berlin-Pride-CSD-2018-0916-320x213.jpg 320w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berlin-Pride-CSD-2018-0916-768x512.jpg 768w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berlin-Pride-CSD-2018-0916.jpg 1500w" width="640"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Berlin is a crazy bubble in terms of diversity. Nowhere else in Germany are there so many different spaces, places and outlets for the members of the LGBTQI Community. Of course Berlin is not perfect in terms of safety for the said community. We still have harassments of transsexuals on a monthly basis, and I would not always dare to kiss a man at Kotti at night because you never know how the surroundings might react. Despite that, I am more than happy to call Berlin my home, since I feel that no other place in Germany has the same amount of freedom, tollerance and possibilities for people of different sexual orientations or gender identities.
       </p>
       <p>
        The biggest celebration of this diversity is definitely the annual
        <a href="http://www.iheartberlin.de/?s=csd">
         Christopher Street Pride Parade
        </a>
        . Since I came to Berlin at the age of 18, I have been visiting the parade regularly. Some years I remember as stressful because of the heat and too many drinks. Or I felt that the event had turned too commercial, so I decided I’d rather visit an alternative demonstration instead.
        <a href="http://www.iheartberlin.de/2017/07/24/csd-berlin-2017-so-wet-so-good-so-tenga/">
         Other years the weather was a bit tricky
        </a>
        . But nonetheless every parade has had its highlights and stolen kisses to tell of afterwards.
       </p>
       <p>
        My personal highlight of this year was to bring along my little sister to Pride. It was her first big Pride parade and as I wanted to make the experience as memorable as possible, I showed her all the typical Pride rituals.
       </p>

        </a>
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="attachment-large size-large wp-post-image" height="427" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berliner-Kindheiten--640x427.jpg" srcset="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berliner-Kindheiten--640x427.jpg 640w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berliner-Kindheiten--320x213.jpg 320w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berliner-Kindheiten--768x512.jpg 768w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Berliner-Kindheiten-.jpg 1500w" width="640"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        With all of us being so busy with keeping up with our lives and this super fast moving digital society it is awfully easy to forget to take the focus off ourselves and onto others. An old friend of my parents said to me the other day: “You should really ask your mother about your childhood and also her childhood. When she isn’t here anymore, both of your stories will be lost. I have no one to ask about it anymore.” It was a harsh truth that I honestly never considered before. We need to conserve the childhood stories of us, our parents and grandparents, to keep them from disappearing like they never happened.
       </p>
       <p>
        That exact thought moved educationist Johannes Zillhardt, when his lovely 80-year-old neighbor Waldtraud Gasche, used every opportunity to have a chat with Johannes. Of course only because “the TV isn’t working”, or “the heater makes strange noises”, but all she truly wanted was to talk about her childhood to someone. That heartfelt connection started Johannes idea to meet people from Berlin at any age and write up the stories of their past to keep them alive.

       <p>
        <img alt="" class="attachment-large size-large wp-post-image" height="426" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/WATERFALL-14-1-640x426.jpg" srcset="http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/WATERFALL-14-1-640x426.jpg 640w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/WATERFALL-14-1-320x213.jpg 320w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/WATERFALL-14-1-768x512.jpg 768w, http://www.iheartberlin.de/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/WATERFALL-14-1.jpg 950w" width="640"/>

       <p>

To start, we picked some fun and helpful events and workshop for this summer that will get you started and your mind and body back to balance.
