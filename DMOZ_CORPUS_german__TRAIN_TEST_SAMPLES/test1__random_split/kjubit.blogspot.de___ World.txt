                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://kjubit.blogspot.com/2013/12/the-original-han-solo-dl-44-blaster.html">
                       The Original Han Solo DL-44 Blaster from Star Wars have been offered for public auction!!
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7394139304133482765" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://image.invaluable.com/housePhotos/profilesinhistory/79/428979/H3257-L48772199.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" src="http://image.invaluable.com/housePhotos/profilesinhistory/79/428979/H3257-L48772199.jpg"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://image.invaluable.com/housePhotos/profilesinhistory/79/428979/H3257-L48772201.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" src="http://image.invaluable.com/housePhotos/profilesinhistory/79/428979/H3257-L48772201.jpg"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <blockquote class="tr_bq">
                       <span style="font-size: small;">
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Bold'; font-variant: small-caps; font-weight: bold;">
                         Harrison Ford “Han Solo” non-firing DL-44 Blaster from
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-BoldItalic'; font-style: italic; font-variant: small-caps; font-weight: bold;">
                         Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Bold'; font-variant: small-caps; font-weight: bold;">
                         and
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-BoldItalic'; font-style: italic; font-variant: small-caps; font-weight: bold;">
                         Return of the Jedi
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Bold'; font-variant: small-caps; font-weight: bold;">
                         .
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         (TCF, 1980, 1983) Harrison Ford’s charismatic smuggler, Han Solo, is arguably the most popular character in the original
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Star Wars
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         trilogy. The space-scoundrel-turned hero’s persona is irrevocably tied 
 to his blaster pistol. Solo was modeled after the rogue gunslingers of 
 the westerns that influenced creator George Lucas. This non-firing 
 blaster was created for
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         The Empire Strikes Back
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         and was also used in
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Return of the Jedi
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         .
  It would have been used in the majority of scenes that feature Han, 
 with the heavier, live-fire weapon being used for close-up shots. 
 Particularly noteworthy scenes requiring this lighter version are when 
 Darth Vader uses the Force to lasso the blaster out of Han’s hand in
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Empire,
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         and in
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Jedi
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         when
  Han wrestles with a Stormtrooper to regain possession of his blaster 
 during the Rebels’ encounter with Imperial forces on Endor. Based on the
  German issue Mauser C96 pistol, this piece, measuring 11 in. long, was 
 custom made for the film from resin by casting the original hero prop 
 from the first
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Star Wars:
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         A New Hope
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         ,
  it therefore exhibits the same serial number as the hero prop, which is
  thought to no longer exist. The blaster is exactly in its original 
 filming condition and therefore exhibits wear from use, but retains all 
 of the original details, including the flash suppressor and scope (the 
 eye-piece of which is detailed with reflective scotch-lite tape). The 
 added distinction of this particular piece is that it was also likely 
 used by Mark Hamill as Luke Skywalker, as both characters shared the 
 same style of weapon in
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Empire
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         . Accompanied by a letter of authenticity from a noted
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd-Italic'; font-style: italic;">
                         Star Wars
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'BemboStd';">
                         collector. To our knowledge this is the only known example of this type
  of blaster in private hands. This is a truly incredible item of motion 
 picture history and quite possibly the most exciting science fiction 
 weapon to have been offered for public auction.
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'Helvetica Neue'; font-weight: bold;">
                         $200,000 - $300,000
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'Helvetica Neue';">
                         [via
                         <a href="http://www.invaluable.com/auction-lot/harrison-ford-han-solo-dl-44-blaster-from-star-379-c-44e307f3bc" target="_blank">
                          invaluable
                         </a>
                         ]
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: 'Helvetica Neue'; font-weight: bold;">
                         <br/>
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </blockquote>
