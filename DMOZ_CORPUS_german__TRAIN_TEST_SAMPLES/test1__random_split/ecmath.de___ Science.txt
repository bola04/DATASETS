      <div class="col2 bigcopy">
       <b>
        The ECMath - Einstein Center for Mathematics Berlin
       </b>
       establishes a network of excellence initiatives and projects, which include the Research Center
       <a href="https://www.matheon.de" onclick="window.open(this.href); return false;">
        <span class="smallcaps">
         <span class="matheon">
          Matheon
         </span>
        </span>
       </a>
       , 
 					the
       <a href="http://www.math-berlin.de" onclick="window.open(this.href); return false;">
        Berlin Mathematical School (BMS)
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="http://www.dzlm.de" onclick="window.open(this.href); return false;">
        German Center for Teacher Education in Mathematics (DZLM)
       </a>
       , 
 					the
       <a href="http://www.forschungscampus-modal.de" onclick="window.open(this.href); return false;">
        Research Campus Modal
       </a>
       , and several
       <a href="http://www.ecmath.de/onECMath/crc">
        Collaborative Research Centers (CRCs) or Transregional Research Centers (TRs)
       </a>
       .
      </div>
      <div class="col2 bigcopy">
       <b>
        ECMath
       </b>
       supports application-oriented basic research in
       <span class="matheon">
        Matheon
       </span>
       , selected students in BMS, school activities in DZLM, and knowledge transfer from science to industry, thus connecting education, cutting-edge research in pure and applied mathematics and large-scale industrial applications.
      </div>

       <div>
        <h2 style="color:red">
         ISIMM Junior Prize for Matthias Liero
        </h2>
        Matthias Liero is honored by the International Society for the Interaction of Mechanics and Mathematics (ISIMM) with the bi-annual Junior Prize. The award recognizes junior researchers who have made a significant contribution to the combination of mathematics and physics. Matthias Liero is researcher at
        <span class="matheon">
         Matheon
        </span>
        and WIAS and works in the field of non-linear partial differential equations with applications e.g. in photovoltaics and OLEDs.

      <section class="news-box">
       <h2 style="color:red">
        Cluster of Excellence:
        <br/>
        Berlin mathematics successful in preselection
       </h2>
       With their draft proposal “MATH + The Berlin Mathematics Research Center" mathematicians from the three Berlin universities FU, HU, TU and the research institutes WIAS and ZIB took the first hurdle in the current call of the Excellence Strategy of the German Federal and State Governments. MATH + was asked, together with 87 other applicants, to submit a full application by February 2018. MATH + is based on ECMath,
       <span class="matheon">
        Matheon
       </span>
       , BMS, DZLM and Modal. Further information:
       <a href="http://www.universities-berlin.de/en/commonalities/proposals/index.html" target="_blank">
        universities-berlin.de
       </a>
