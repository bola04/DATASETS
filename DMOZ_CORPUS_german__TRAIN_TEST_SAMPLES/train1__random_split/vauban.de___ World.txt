   <h1>
    Former, non-commercial website of Vauban district, Freiburg im Breisgau, Germany
   </h1>
   <p>
    From 1998 to February 20, 2018 this had been the noncommercial website vauban.de .
   </p>
   <p>
    You will find a temporary site with reduced content
    <a href="https://www.freiburg-vauban.de">
     here: www.freiburg-vauban.de
    </a>
   </p>
   <p>
    This free service has been stopped for the following reasons:
   </p>
   <ul>
    <li>
     Massive requirements by the new European data protection laws in effect from May 25, 2018.
     <br/>
     In general, I am an advocate for data protection and privacy but every small website is now being measured by the same standard as big sites like Facebook, Apple and Google - US-american companies are able to evade most of the requirements because they have signed a "Privacy shield" agreement that is not worth the paper on which it might be printed.
    </li>
    <li>
     Since 2 years, my content management system Joomla 2.5 had not be updated
    </li>
    <li>
     My webdseigner had not been capable to make the transition - even paid -
    </li>
    <li>
     Years ago, I did not succeed to get all or many actors on Vauban under the "umbrella" of vauban.de - maybe then the budget and management could have been secured. Now everyone has their own little website.
    </li>
    <li>
     I am not able to pay again 5000 EUR for a renovation and servicing of the website, also not again hundreds of hours of unpaid work.
    </li>
    <li>
     At least in large parts of Europe, passive houses or plus energy houses should be standard for new construction since 20 years now, the existing gas grid and co-generation could solve the storage problem at least until better storage technologies are available. There is a lot of hope: Renewable energies have proved that they are more economic - but even France needs to suffer a "super nuclear accident" before they might "wake up" from nuclear age, - and even in Japan it is not entirely sure if the lesson has been learned. Why bother with progress? Instead of going forward, societies of the world now have to deal with thousands-year old ideologies like the nation state and religion that are not even fully overcome in the so-called modern countries.
    </li>
   </ul>
