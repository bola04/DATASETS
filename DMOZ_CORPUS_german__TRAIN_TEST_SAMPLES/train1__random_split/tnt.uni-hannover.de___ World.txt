    <h1 class="welcome">
     Welcome to the Institut für Informations­verarbeitung!
    </h1>
    <div class="row ">
     <div class=" col-sm-7 col-md-9 ">
      <div class="panel panel-default">
       <div class="panel-heading ">
        <div class="panel-title h4">
         <a data-toggle="collapse" href="#u5b61f7d449b68">
          Who we are and what we do
          <span class="caret">
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="panel-collapse collapse in" id="u5b61f7d449b68">
        <div class="panel-body ">
         <p>
          We are living in the era of information: sharing and sending pictures, videos and multimedia data over the network has become part of our 
 everyday lifes. This demands for information processing algorithms to encode, transmit, enhance and extract meaningful information from 
 multimedia content. At our Institute we conduct cutting-edge research in the fields of audio and video signal processing, computer vision and machine learning. 
 Broadly speaking, this involves designing intelligent coding algorithms to extract relevant information from the data.
         </p>
         <p>
          Humans constantly extract meaningful information from visual data almost effortlessly. It turns out that simple visual tasks such as 
 recognizing persons, detecting and tracking objects or understanding what is going on in the scene are extremely challenging problems 
 for a computer. Training computers to process information as humans do has many potential applications in fields such as communication systems,
 medicine, artificial intelligence, robotics, surveillance, entertainment or sports science. It is therefore our ultimate goal to be able 
 to emulate the human visual system with computational algorithms.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our Institute was founded in 1973 by
          <a class="blue_link" href="http://www.tnt.uni-hannover.de/staff/musmann/">
           Prof. Dr.-Ing. Hans-Georg Musmann
          </a>
          and is part of the
          <a class="blue_link" href="http://www.et-inf.uni-hannover.de/">
           Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
          </a>
          of the
          <a class="blue_link" href="http://www.uni-hannover.de/en/index.php">
           Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Universität Hannover
          </a>
          .
 Today the group is headed by
          <a class="blue_link" href="http://www.tnt.uni-hannover.de/staff/osterman/">
           Prof. Dr.-Ing. Jörn Ostermann
          </a>
          and
          <a class="blue_link" href="http://www.tnt.uni-hannover.de/staff/rosenhahn/">
           Prof. Dr.-Ing. Bodo Rosenhahn
          </a>
          and consists of about 30 researchers from more 
 than seven different nationalities. Our technology is transferred in current and future telecommunication and digital systems, automation and interpretation tasks, remote 
 sensing or medical image analysis. Approximately 70% of our research is funded by industry and national and international research grants.
         </p>
         <p>
          Do you want to join us? We have
          <a class="blue_link" href="./jobs/jobs.php">
           open positions
          </a>
          .
         </p>
