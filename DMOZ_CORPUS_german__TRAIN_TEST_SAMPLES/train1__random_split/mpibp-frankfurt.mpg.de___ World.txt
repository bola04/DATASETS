   <meta content="Analysis of the function of primary and secondary transport proteins (light-powered pumps, transport-ATPases, carriers, antiporters). Stationary and time-resolved electrical or electrophysiological methods in combination with time-resolved fluorescence techniques using voltage-clamp conditions for the determination of membrane protein dynamics (Bamberg). Expression and crystallisation of membrane proteins for structure determination by electron microscopy and x-ray crystallography. High-resolution electron microscopy and image processing of large macromolecular complexes (Kühlbrandt). Interactions of membrane proteins, nucleic acids, lipids and other biomolecules. Develop detailed and quantitative descriptions of key biomolecular processes, including energy conversion, molecular transport, signal transduction, and enzymatic catalysis (Hummer). Investigation of structure and function of membrane proteins in photosynthesis, the respiratory chain and receptors; overexpression, crystallisation and x-ray structure analysis, electrostatic calculations (Michel)." name="abstract"/>
