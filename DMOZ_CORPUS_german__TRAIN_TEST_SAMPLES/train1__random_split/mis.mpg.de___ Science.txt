        <h1 class="">
         Welcome to the MPI MiS
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="ce-textpic ce-center ce-below">
        <div class="ce-bodytext">
         <p>
          Fundamental questions arising from natural and engineering sciences and economics have always inspired mathematicians to search for new mathematical structures and methods. The interaction between mathematics and the sciences forms the central point of research at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences (MiS) in Leipzig.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>

        <header>
         <h2 class="">
          Felix Otto awarded the Luigi Tartufari International Prize for Mathematics (2018-07-25)
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="ce-textpic ce-right ce-intext">
         <div class="ce-gallery" data-ce-columns="1" data-ce-images="1">
          <div class="ce-row">
           <div class="ce-column">
            <figure class="image">
             <img alt="" class="image-embed-item" height="112" src="/fileadmin/_processed_/b/e/csm_foto_Premi_2018_h_87fdfd30ce.jpg" width="150"/>
            </figure>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ce-bodytext">
          <p>
           Professor Felix Otto, director at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences in Leipzig, Germany, has been awarded the Prof. Luigi Tartufari International Prize for Mathematics from the Accademia Nazionale dei Lincei in Rome in the presence of the Italian President. Felix Otto was honored for his numerous contributions to continuum mechanics and to materials science. In particular, his analysis of partial differential equations modelling the flow of a gas in a porous rock or those describing a particle under frictional and random forces, was praised.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            <a href="/institute/presse/news/2018-07-25-otto-tartufari-prize.html">
             Read more &gt;&gt;&gt;
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>

        <header>
         <h2 class="">
          Most popular SIAM SIMA paper by Felix Otto  (2018-07-23)
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="ce-textpic ce-right ce-intext">
         <div class="ce-gallery" data-ce-columns="1" data-ce-images="1">
          <div class="ce-row">
           <div class="ce-column">
            <figure class="image">
             <img alt="" class="image-embed-item" height="107" src="/fileadmin/_processed_/e/4/csm_otto_paper_2018_7b679eb6a5.jpg" width="150"/>
            </figure>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ce-bodytext">
          <p>
           The 1998 paper "
           <a class="external-link-new-window" href="https://epubs.siam.org/doi/abs/10.1137/S0036141096303359" target="_blank">
            The Variational Formulation of the Fokker--Planck Equation
           </a>
           ", authored by our Director Felix Otto together with Richard Jordan and David Kinderlehrer, is the most downloaded paper of the
           <a class="external-link-new-window" href="https://www.siam.org/Publications/Journals/SIAM-Journal-on-Mathematical-Analysis-SIMA" target="_blank">
            SIAM Journal on Mathematical Analysis
           </a>
           . On the occasion of the Journal's 50th volume, Felix Otto authored a short piece for the
           <a class="external-link-new-window" href="https://sinews.siam.org/" target="_blank">
            SIAM news
           </a>
           , offering some insight as to why the paper continues to spark so much interest today. You can
           <a class="external-link-new-window" href="https://sinews.siam.org/Details-Page/the-impact-of-the-siam-journal-on-mathematical-analysis-most-popular-paper-2" target="_blank">
            read his contribution here
           </a>
           .
           <br/>
           <strong>
            <a href="/institute/presse/news/2018-07-23-most-popular-siam-sima-paper-by-felix-otto.html">
             Read more &gt;&gt;&gt;
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>

         <h2 class="">
          Learning to code from the masters: the Macaulay2 Workshop at MPI MiS (2018-07-03)
         </h2>
        </header>
        <div class="ce-textpic ce-right ce-intext">
         <div class="ce-gallery" data-ce-columns="1" data-ce-images="1">
          <div class="ce-row">
           <div class="ce-column">
            <figure class="image">
             <img alt="" class="image-embed-item" height="111" src="/fileadmin/_processed_/7/3/csm_macaulay2_organizers_365d573372.jpg" width="150"/>
            </figure>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ce-bodytext">
          <p>
           Last month, our institute hosted a dedicated Macaulay2 workshop organized by former MPI MiS postdoc Eliana Duarte Gelvez (now at the Otto-von-Guericke Universität Magdeburg), together with Heather Harrington (University of Oxford) and Thomas Kahle (Magdeburg). Around 60 participants from around the world took part in the program, which offered them a unique opportunity to develop their coding skills by getting in touch with the experts.
