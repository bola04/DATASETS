        <strong>
         <font color="#FFCC66" size="+1">
          What is Depression?
         </font>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Clinical depression is more that just feeling "blue" from time to time. It is a serious condition that affects a person's mind, body, and spirit and impacts every aspect of one's life. Depression is one of the most common mental health conditions affecting almost 10% of the population each year.
        <a href="gen_01.html">
         Learn more
        </a>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         <font color="#FF9900" size="+1">
          Are You Depressed?
         </font>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Doctors diagnose depression by the symptoms one experiences. These can include depressed mood, loss of interest or pleasure, feelings of hopelessness and guilt, and problems with concentration, eating, energy and sleeping. Thoughts of death or suicide may also occur.
        <a href="dia_22.html">
         Learn more
        </a>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         <font color="#33CC99" size="+1">
          4 Causes of Depression
         </font>
        </strong>
        <br/>
        Depression can be caused by factors related to one's
        <em>
         biology
        </em>
        (body chemistry and brain function),
        <em>
         genetics
        </em>
        (heredity),
        <em>
         environment
        </em>
        (stress, trauma, etc.), or
        <em>
         medical conditions
        </em>
        (physical problems, disease, etc.).
        <a href="cau_01.html">
         Learn more
        </a>
