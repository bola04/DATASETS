           <h2>
            WORLD TRAUMA SYMPOSIUM
           </h2>
           <div class="content">
            <div class="image">
             <img alt="" data-sfref="[images|OpenAccessDataProvider]92e7a832-fe31-667a-9799-ff0000a338da" image="sfImageWrapper"/>
             <img alt="Home_BoxedFeature_WTS_REBOA" data-displaymode="Original" height="149" src="/images/default-source/home-page-ads-and-sponsors/home_boxedfeature_wts_reboa.png?sfvrsn=fc81cb92_2" title="Home_BoxedFeature_WTS_REBOA" width="320"/>
            </div>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <br/>
             </strong>
             Dr. Megan Brenner, MD, MS,
             <em>
              trauma and vascular surgeon,
             </em>
             will discuss the latest use of the resuscitative endovascular balloon occlusion of the aorta (REBOA) technique and its impact on prehospital trauma care. Learn about technology advances that have enhanced the viability of REBOA and the training needed to effectively implement this technique.
            </p>
            <p>
             Other top speakers will present on pediatric trauma, hemorrhage control, building resiliency to respond to trauma, advanced
             <strong>
             </strong>
             traumatic brain injury detection and management,
             <strong>
             </strong>
             the role of prehospital care in national trauma systems, and much more.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              October 30, Nashville, Tennessee
             </strong>
             <em>
              <br/>
              Held in conjunction with EMS World Expo
             </em>
             <br/>
             Breakfast, lunch and exhibit displays are included. Provides 8 hours of CAPCE-accredited CE, CME or nursing CE.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.naemt.org/events/world-trauma-symposium/wts-program" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              <strong>
               Learn More
              </strong>
             </a>
