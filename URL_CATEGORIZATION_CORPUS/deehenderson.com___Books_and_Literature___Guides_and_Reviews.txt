          coming your way. I’m releasing my first non-fiction book, I’m bringing you a story about combat medic Connie August, and I’m publishing a short-story so I can share a particularly good idea I had last fall.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          I love weaving together mystery, romance, scripture, and people’s lives with God.  That’s been the typical story mix, in various degrees, for all my books.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          For many years I’ve been thinking about Connie August. She’s a combat medic. Her story, however, never really fit in with the military books I was writing, nor with the romantic suspense. She doesn’t have a romance story to share with readers about a guy. Connie’s story is about a romance with God — she is passionately in love with him. And she’s seen things on the battlefield similar to the miracles in scripture. So her story required something different of me. An Unfinished Death is not a romantic suspense
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          . Ryan and Connie are already dating other people. This story also contains more scripture and conversations about scripture than most stories I have written. I wanted to present Connie August in an authentic way, true to who she is.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          Healing doesn’t come to us by our works. Healing is by grace alone. It is a gift from Jesus.
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          That statement likely resonated with you in one of four ways, as: true, false, incomplete, or as something you’ve not heard before.
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          For that reason, for the first time in my career, I’m releasing a non-
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          fiction
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:
  12.0pt;language:EN">
          work along with
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;
  language:EN">
          a
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          story
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          .
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;
  color:red;font-weight:bold;language:EN">
          Healing is by Grace Alone
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;font-weight:bold;language:EN">
          is a look at what the bible says about healing at a depth you may never have heard before.
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          I encourage you to share Healing is by Grace Alone with friends and family who prefer non-fiction. For those who love a good story, the novel
         </span>
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
          An Unfinished Death, presents the subject matter in action.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;
  text-kashida-space:50%;text-align:justify;text-justify:newspaper;text-kashida-space:
  50%">
         <span style="font-size:12.0pt;language:EN">

I’ve always known his story, but I’ve never had a place to tell it before. So in a very brief but poignant moment in An Unfinished Death (lasting only a couple pages), I’m also presenting the rest of Tom’s story in his own words.
         </span>
        </p>

One of the best gifts you can give an author is to share the stories so they continue to be passed around and enjoyed. And if there is a teenager in your family, or the family of a friend, would you make sure they hear about the stories? I am making a special point to see how many girls in high school  and college can discover the O’Malley series. The stories help to show what a good relationship can be like when they are considering who to date. I’d appreciate your help making that happen. Loan your books out, and be a part. It will be fun to hear the feedback.
