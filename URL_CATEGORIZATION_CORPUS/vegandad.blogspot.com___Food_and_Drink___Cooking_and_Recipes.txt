                       <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-8frGSnMM3EY/WyhqFEnfITI/AAAAAAAAD-s/OdsZxJebRIgNIS9mSdHJKdhVeDZMzVPOQCLcBGAs/s640/39587734950_411a7ebbc4_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="color: #274e13;">
                       The key to injera is getting comfortable with fermentation. Just like sourdough bread, injera gets its flavour from wild (as opposed to commercial) yeast. But unlike sourdough, this recipe is much easier to prepare. A few days out on the counter and this batter will be happily bubbling away and ready to be made into Ethiopian flatbread.  It's a great introduction into the world of fermentation! The bread is gluten free but still rolls just like a crepe and is durable enough to scoop up whatever tasty stew you make.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     INGREDIENTS
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Makes one dozen
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     - 3 cups teff flour
                     <br/>
                     - 4 cups water
                     <br/>
                     - 1 tsp baking powder
                     <br/>
                     - 1/2 tsp salt
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     METHOD
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      1. Mix together teff flour and water. Pour into a large, clean glass or ceramic container (I use a 56 oz canning jar). Keep in mind that the batter will expand by 1/3 or more, so make sure you container is big enough. Secure a piece of cloth over the mouth of the container so everything can breathe--I put a cloth napkin over the mouth of the canning jar and secure it with the metal ring.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      2. Let the batter sit for 2 to 3 days, stirring 3 times a day, until better begin to bubble and rise.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      3. Once the batter is bubbly, stir in the baking powder and salt.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      4. Let the batter sit while you heat a 10" non-stick pan over medium to med-hi heat.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      5. Lightly oil the pan. Pour 1/2 cup of batter into the pan and tilt to coat (thin batter with water if necessary). Cook until the injera has bubbles all over, then cover with a lid so the injera can steam for 2-3 minutes. Regulate the heat so the bread does not burn.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      6. Remove the injera to a cooling rack and roll it while warm. Cover with a towel to keep warm. Repeat with the remaining batter.
                     </div>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://vegandad.blogspot.com/2018/04/wagon-wheel-cake.html">
                      Wagon Wheel Cake
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2962823498210965080" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-h7ORocwJ28o/WszAzlFYmXI/AAAAAAAAD8c/zF87SYPPLmMqytvo6qpAKA-YXSn_RnnrACLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_1529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" data-original-height="1204" data-original-width="1600" height="480" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-h7ORocwJ28o/WszAzlFYmXI/AAAAAAAAD8c/zF87SYPPLmMqytvo6qpAKA-YXSn_RnnrACLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1529.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="color: #274e13;">
                       This cake is insane. It's massive, probably too sweet, has 5 separate elements, and is totally awesome!  Of course, my health conscious mother never bought us
                       <a href="http://www.darefoods.com/ca_en/product/wagon-wheels-raspberry/199" target="_blank">
                        Wagon Wheels
                       </a>
                       when my brothers and I were young, and oh, how we jealously pined for them when other kids brought them to school for lunch! When Vegan Mom and I were dating (and not yet vegan) in university, we used to keep ourselves motivated to study through the positive reinforcement of
                       <a href="http://www.darefoods.com/ca_en/product/viva-puffs-raspberry/194" target="_blank">
                        Viva Puffs
                       </a>
                       . This cake evokes all of those memories, and is simply a lot of fun to make and eat.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       INGREDIENTS
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - about 1 1/2 cups raspberry jelly
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        Cake
                       </b>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       INGREDIENTS
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 227g all purpose flour
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 65g cocoa
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 1/2 tsp baking soda
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 3/4 tsp baking powder
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/2 tsp salt
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 1/4 cup soy milk
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/4 cup water
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 cup sugar
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/2 cup canola oil
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 tbsp vanilla
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       METHOD
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <i>
                        Preheat oven to 350 degrees
                       </i>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       1. Grease two 10 inch round cake pans.  Cut out two circles of parchment paper to fit in the bottom of the pans.  Put the parchment papers in the bottom of the pans and grease it.  Flour both pans.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       2. Sift flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt into a bowl.  In a separate bowl, whisk chocolate soy milk and vinegar together.  Add remaining ingredients and whisk well to blend.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       3. Add dry ingredients and gently whisk until blended.  Don't overdo it.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       4. Divide batter evenly between the two pans.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       5. Bake for 22-24 mins, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       6. Cool on wire racks for 10 mins, then flip the pans over the remove the cake.  Remove the parchment paper.  Let them fully cool.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        Graham Cookie Layer
                       </b>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       INGREDIENTS
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 3/4 cup all purpose flour
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 3/4 cup whole wheat flour
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 2 tbsp dark brown sugar
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/8 tsp salt
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/2 tsp cinnamon
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/2 tsp baking powder
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/4 cup vegetable shortening
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/4 cup maple syrup
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 2 tbsp soy milk
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       METHOD
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       1. Mix together dry ingredients. Cut in vegetable shortening with a pastry cutter to the size of small peas.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       2. Add syrup and soy milk and bring together into a ball. Add a tiny splash more soy milk if needed.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       3. Divide dough into two equal pieces. Roll and trim each into a 10" circle on a piece of parchment paper, using the cake pan as a guide. Don't worry, you can trim it more after it is baked.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       4. Place each piece of parchment/dough circle on a baking sheet. Place in the fridge to cool for 30 mins.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       5. Heat the oven to 350 degrees. Bake one the middle and upper rack for 10 mins, rotating and switching the racks after 5 mins. Cookies should dry on the top and only very, very lightly brown. Do not over bake.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       6. Remove from pans (parchment and all) to a cooling rack. When cooled, trim as needed with a small sharp knife using the cake pan as a guide.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        Marshmallow
                       </b>
                       (from
                       <a href="http://plantified.com/gooses-golden-eggs-with-a-lemon-marshmallow-and-lemon-curd-centre" target="_blank">
                        Plantified
                       </a>
                       )
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       INGREDIENTS
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 3/4 cup salt free aquafaba
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/2 tsp xanthan gum
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 tbsp vanilla extract
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1 1/2 cups sugar
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 2 tsp agar powder
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 2 tbsp glucose syrup
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/3 cup water
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       METHOD
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       1. In a large bowl, whisk aquafaba and xanthan gum to stiff peaks with a hand mixer. Whisk in vanilla. Set aside.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       2. Bring the sugar, agar, glucose, and water to boiling in a small saucepan over medium heat. Bring to 250 F, stirring regularly/constantly to keep the mixture from sticking to the bottom of the pan. Be patient. The sugar will quickly jump in temperature, plateau, then rise again. Resist cranking the heat to hurry the process along.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       3. Slowly stream the hot sugar mixture into the aquafaba, constantly whipping it in with the hand mixer. When the sugar mixture is fully incorporated, keep whipping the mixture until it returns to room temperature (more or less--the bottom of the bowl should not feel warm). You can do all this with a stand mixer, but I find that the sugar mixture likes to get caught up around the top of the beater, so using a hand mixer is better).
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        Ganache
                       </b>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       INGREDIENTS
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 1/2 cup light coconut milk
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 200g bittersweet vegan chocolate chips
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       - 2 tbsp margarine
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       METHOD
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       1. Heat coconut milk to bubbling in a small saucepan over medium heat.
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       2. Remove from heat and add chocolate chips and margarine.  Stir until everything is melted and smooth.  Set aside to cool completely.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       TO MAKE THE CAKE!
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       1. Bake and cool the cake. Level the top of the cake, if necessary (i.e. make sure it is flat and not domed).
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       2. Bake and cool the graham cookie layer. Trim to fit on top of the cake (you can do this while the cookie is still slightly warm if you want).
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       3. Place one of the cake layers on a serving platter. Spread 3/4 cup of raspberry jelly on top. Place one of the graham cookies on top of the jam.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       4. Repeat step 3 with the remaining cake, jelly, and graham cookie, and set aside.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-pwC4m6Ur9dc/Ws6yr9AjxEI/AAAAAAAAD80/21QBTuwvLVsWWJ1f3OaprxobHSAf5f5GwCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180407_104313_41296574721_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-pwC4m6Ur9dc/Ws6yr9AjxEI/AAAAAAAAD80/21QBTuwvLVsWWJ1f3OaprxobHSAf5f5GwCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180407_104313_41296574721_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       5. Make the marshmallow. The marshmallow will set fairly quickly, so be prepared. Spread 7/8 of the marshmallow on top of the cake/jam/cookie layer in your serving platter. Put the second cake/jam/cookie layer on top of the marshmallow (making sure the cookie side is down and the cake layer on top). Gently press down and use an icing spatula to smooth the sides. Use the remaining 1/8 of the marshmallow to aid in this endeavour.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cM9PJZkd17w/Ws6yr7SZ5UI/AAAAAAAAD8w/Vf5lzveMewUKinM5AUoYNMVbdjADVTnYQCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180407_112350_41254635022_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cM9PJZkd17w/Ws6yr7SZ5UI/AAAAAAAAD8w/Vf5lzveMewUKinM5AUoYNMVbdjADVTnYQCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180407_112350_41254635022_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       6. Let the marshmallow fully set. I like to put the cake in a cool place for this step because it makes the ganache step easier.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       7. Make the ganache and let cool to room temperature.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       8. Spread a thin layer of ganache over the entire cake, like you would a crumb coat of butter cream. Set aside in a cool place (the fridge) to set.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       9. Use the remaining ganache to cover the cake. Set aside in a cool place to set.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-IzgTGQAG1mM/Ws6yr_Ko9xI/AAAAAAAAD8s/VsH4QD5EpHARBCtIIIBjAZBtTCvvHTUeQCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180407_120723_41298289211_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-IzgTGQAG1mM/Ws6yr_Ko9xI/AAAAAAAAD8s/VsH4QD5EpHARBCtIIIBjAZBtTCvvHTUeQCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180407_120723_41298289211_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>

                   <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-SgnaJmTC0B4/WpDHfleqONI/AAAAAAAAD78/qV-khyVQYrc4MmzjmvmsIeQuSBdCIOYPwCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1493.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://vegandad.blogspot.com/2018/02/english-muffins.html">
                      English Muffins
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1859771329312634767" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-SgnaJmTC0B4/WpDHfleqONI/AAAAAAAAD78/qV-khyVQYrc4MmzjmvmsIeQuSBdCIOYPwCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_1493.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" data-original-height="1067" data-original-width="1600" height="426" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-SgnaJmTC0B4/WpDHfleqONI/AAAAAAAAD78/qV-khyVQYrc4MmzjmvmsIeQuSBdCIOYPwCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1493.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="color: #0c343d;">
                       I love English muffins but I find making them from a batter to be a real pain. It's hard to get quality muffin rings here, it's not easy to make them a consistent size, and it seems to take forever to make them. So, I reworked a Reinhart recipe by significantly upping the liquid and working the dough more like a ciabatta. The end result is a delicious muffin with some respectable air holes in the dough. Not as many as with a batter, but still pretty good. A stand mixer is essential here to get the gluten to develop properly. You also need a griddle, preferably one big enough to hold 8 muffins at a time so you can whip these off in three batches.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     INGREDIENTS (by weight, except where indicated)
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Makes 24. Cut in half if needed.
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     - 1 oz apple cider vinegar
                     <br/>
                     - 27 fl oz soy milk at room temperature
                     <br/>
                     - 1 lb 14 oz bread flour
                     <br/>
                     - 22g sugar
                     <br/>
                     - 16g instant yeast
                     <br/>
                     - 16g salt
                     <br/>
                     - 1.5 oz vegetable shortening
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     METHOD
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      1. Mix the vinegar into the soy milk, then add to the rest of the ingredients in bowl of a stand mixer.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      2. Using the batter attachment, mix together until combined. Increase the speed to med-lo/med and keep mixing until the dough collects around the beater (this will take about 5-7 mins).
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      3. Switch to the dough hook. Knead the dough on med-lo/med speed until it no longer sticks to the sides of the bowl (this will take a few minutes).
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      4. Transfer to a large, well-oiled bowl. With oiled hands, stretch and fold the dough 2-3 times. Let rest for 5 mins and stretch and fold 2-3 times again. Cover and let rise at room temperature.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      5. While dough is rising, oil enough baking sheets to hold 24 muffins.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      6. When dough has doubled in size, shape into 2.5 oz balls. Use oiled hands as necessary, and try not to degas the dough. Place on the prepared sheets.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      7. Cover dough with cling wrap. Place a empty cookie sheet on top of the dough. Gently press down to flatten the dough into pucks. Let the dough rise with the sheet on top. If the sheet is too heavy and seems to be flattening the dough, don't use it. Just keep pressing the dough down with your fingers throughout the rise to keep a puck shape.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      8. While dough is rising, preheat the oven to 350 degrees, and get a griddle heating to around 350 degrees.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      9. Working in batches, cook the muffins on the griddle for about 4 mins per side. These get a great rise on the griddle, so use your fingertips to press and keep them into a puck shape when they first go on the griddle.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      10. Transfer to a baking sheet and bake in the oven for 8 mins. Transfer to a wire rack to cool.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      TIPS:
                      <br/>
                      1. Make sure to pinch the balls of dough tightly to seal the dough together. You need the good dough ball integrity (if that makes any sense) to get these to rise correctly.
                      <br/>
                      2. Don't let the dough get too warm or it will get really hard to work with. Room temperature all the way.
                      <br/>
                      3. Don't let the dough over-rise when shaped into balls. You want to bake these on the way up, so don't let them double.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       WHAT IF I DON'T HAVE A STAND MIXER?!
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      Never fear! You can still make these. It's going to take some muscle but it can be done. To help you. here is a picture essay.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      First, make sure to dissolve the yeast in the liquid before adding it to the flour.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Second, use a strong wooden spoon to bring the dough together. I actually prefer this spurtle because you can muscle through the dough without too much resistance. When it looks like this, let the dough rest for a few minutes. Then get back at it. The idea is to get the dough to gather around the spurtle in a ball. A vigorous circular motion will accomplish this. If you feel the gluten offering too much resistance, let the dough rest for a few minutes and then try again. It should pull away from the sides of the bowl and gather around the spurtle.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-XbuvCh-QuO8/WuTMSWDmlVI/AAAAAAAAD9M/9OWDlPYZqzQab50mYFG2-g3svZMsE900wCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_131708_41476666111_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="900" height="640" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-XbuvCh-QuO8/WuTMSWDmlVI/AAAAAAAAD9M/9OWDlPYZqzQab50mYFG2-g3svZMsE900wCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_131708_41476666111_o.jpg" width="360"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      When you have a fairly smooth dough, transfer it to to a lightly floured surface. You want enough flour to keep the dough from sticking.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-FFZom-bHy-8/WuTMSmzec1I/AAAAAAAAD9U/R51RGk3EDbcpnZN0LtKqjn8CuAb-WHuwQCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_132756_26605261237_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="900" height="640" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-FFZom-bHy-8/WuTMSmzec1I/AAAAAAAAD9U/R51RGk3EDbcpnZN0LtKqjn8CuAb-WHuwQCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_132756_26605261237_o.jpg" width="360"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      With the help of a pastry scraper, stretch and fold the dough like a letter. If you don't know what a letter is or how to fold one, ask your parents (or maybe even your grandparents).
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-m3kDaY-_kTg/WuTMShVN2sI/AAAAAAAAD9Q/Yxpr4K01Xs4pCJU2cBF1SpYJzZijBFMowCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_132843_40763412384_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-m3kDaY-_kTg/WuTMShVN2sI/AAAAAAAAD9Q/Yxpr4K01Xs4pCJU2cBF1SpYJzZijBFMowCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_132843_40763412384_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Rotate the dough 90 degrees and stretch and fold a second time.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Sag2lV6qfWc/WuTMTOWCPVI/AAAAAAAAD9Y/y_V8LAn0oEUUKiUOPvyoUYKM4pvwHPVQACLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_132921_27605722268_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="900" height="640" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Sag2lV6qfWc/WuTMTOWCPVI/AAAAAAAAD9Y/y_V8LAn0oEUUKiUOPvyoUYKM4pvwHPVQACLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_132921_27605722268_o.jpg" width="360"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Transfer the dough to a well-oiled bowl and turn to coat. With oiled hands, stretch and fold the dough  a third time. Let the dough rest for 5 mins.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-l2klpRkEH6k/WuTMTcakGoI/AAAAAAAAD9c/EVveJR1A2PQuYDBlrnA9Vjoj2UTh5lg0wCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_133600_27605858878_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-l2klpRkEH6k/WuTMTcakGoI/AAAAAAAAD9c/EVveJR1A2PQuYDBlrnA9Vjoj2UTh5lg0wCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_133600_27605858878_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      What you are doing is building a gluten structure, and you should feel the dough firm up as you stretch and fold. When you can shape the dough into a ball like what you see below, your stretching and folding is done. Just keep the dough and your hands oiled, and stretch and fold until you reach perfection. Then cover and let rise.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-985IiQSyvcs/WuTMTTQw_iI/AAAAAAAAD9g/YyrzEuaiUowS33g2wrWezK37X7_XFTqlQCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_135823_40584565335_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="900" height="640" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-985IiQSyvcs/WuTMTTQw_iI/AAAAAAAAD9g/YyrzEuaiUowS33g2wrWezK37X7_XFTqlQCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_135823_40584565335_o.jpg" width="360"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Good gluten structure will give you a good rise.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-sF0nhmjElu4/WuTMToi7SfI/AAAAAAAAD9k/9mohdc2LtGADxRZgHRgE8TZWG-u-1i6iACLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_151829_41436657492_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="900" height="640" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-sF0nhmjElu4/WuTMToi7SfI/AAAAAAAAD9k/9mohdc2LtGADxRZgHRgE8TZWG-u-1i6iACLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_151829_41436657492_o.jpg" width="360"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Now it's time to shape some balls of dough! I keep the dough on my fingertips, using my thumb to push the middle of the dough down while the fingers of my other hand bring the sides of the dough up. You should feel the bounciness of the gluten and a firm ball forms and firms up. If the dough gets too sticky, just lightly oil your hands.
                      <br/>
                      <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5G1z57jmu4Y/WuTMUaEchiI/AAAAAAAAD9s/pvzpVfe8GTwFm8BDxiYzxC3MU5ADswhHgCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_152917_40765709994_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;">
                       <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5G1z57jmu4Y/WuTMUaEchiI/AAAAAAAAD9s/pvzpVfe8GTwFm8BDxiYzxC3MU5ADswhHgCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_152917_40765709994_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      The key is to seal the dough into a tight ball. This will give you a good rise.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SjhJAtDcGOc/WuTMTwqu0YI/AAAAAAAAD9o/oGvLeoPuqM4IUHntzH4FHvYB_upJzXIdACLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_152232_27607710798_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SjhJAtDcGOc/WuTMTwqu0YI/AAAAAAAAD9o/oGvLeoPuqM4IUHntzH4FHvYB_upJzXIdACLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_152232_27607710798_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Let rise on a well-oiled piece of parchment per the recipe above.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-WfcY6CJlFZA/WuTMU-ZBW1I/AAAAAAAAD9w/Juz7Tt6HQkEPTTRLSAJ2bwzd_ukc0yUugCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_152946_41479148011_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="900" height="640" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-WfcY6CJlFZA/WuTMU-ZBW1I/AAAAAAAAD9w/Juz7Tt6HQkEPTTRLSAJ2bwzd_ukc0yUugCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_152946_41479148011_o.jpg" width="360"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      A good griddle is your friend.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/--bAN3uXwcL8/WuTMV5J2AUI/AAAAAAAAD94/EVRQbN_ZhLgMrE_8YKYO-BJaE9ofCQAwwCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_162753_41438161832_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/--bAN3uXwcL8/WuTMV5J2AUI/AAAAAAAAD94/EVRQbN_ZhLgMrE_8YKYO-BJaE9ofCQAwwCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_162753_41438161832_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      Flip and bake per the recipe above.
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-U7_j3kiWqMo/WuTMWDaylRI/AAAAAAAAD98/CxE4MkI9rScK9K3PEIM9vahox6TV_RNzQCLcBGAs/s1600/img_20180415_163118_41480241771_o.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="1600" height="360" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-U7_j3kiWqMo/WuTMWDaylRI/AAAAAAAAD98/CxE4MkI9rScK9K3PEIM9vahox6TV_RNzQCLcBGAs/s640/img_20180415_163118_41480241771_o.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>

                  <span>
                   Sunday, December 17, 2017
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-pnuCwtK2yS0/WjaAyNOM2RI/AAAAAAAAD7M/LQsuL6gv9iIAY3pkLxWB-Tu0KpIlSLbJQCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1445.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://vegandad.blogspot.com/2017/12/sweet-potato-challah.html">
                      Sweet Potato Challah
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1531137346996654420" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-pnuCwtK2yS0/WjaAyNOM2RI/AAAAAAAAD7M/LQsuL6gv9iIAY3pkLxWB-Tu0KpIlSLbJQCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_1445.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" data-original-height="1600" data-original-width="1067" height="640" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-pnuCwtK2yS0/WjaAyNOM2RI/AAAAAAAAD7M/LQsuL6gv9iIAY3pkLxWB-Tu0KpIlSLbJQCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1445.jpg" width="426"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="color: #274e13;">
                       I've been meaning to post this recipe for ages. Sweet potato is my new favourite way to replace eggs in enriched bread dough recipes. It makes the final loaf nice and soft, and adds colour to the dough that     mimics the many egg yolks of non-vegan challah. This is a version of Peter Reinhart's recipe from
                       <i>
                        Artisan Breads Everyday
                       </i>
                       , but I use a blender to incorporate the potato into the liquid ingredients. You can let the dough rise in the fridge, then shape and bake the next day as he calls for, but I usually just do everything in the same day because I don't have the time or the fridge space to follow his method. The recipe produces reliable results every time. Trust me: I make at least two recipes a week so the kids have buns for school lunches.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <i>
                      <br/>
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <i>
                       Makes 2 loaves, or 16 buns
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      INGREDIENTS
                      <br/>
                      <i>
                       All measurements are weight, not volume
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 17oz warm water
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 2.5 oz oil
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 4 oz cooked sweet potato (see note* way below)
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 3 oz sugar
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 14 g instant yeast
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 19 g salt
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - 2 lb 3 oz bread flour
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      - soy milk for brushing
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      METHOD
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      1. Place water, oil, sweet potato, sugar, yeast, and salt in a blender. Blend until smooth.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      2. Add liquid to flour in a large bowl and bring into a dough. Knead until smooth.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      3. Shape into a ball and let rise, covered, in a large oiled bowl until doubled in size.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      4. From here, YouTube is your friend. Determine how many braids you want in your loaf (the pic above is a 6 braid) and find a video for how to braid it. Remember that the recipe makes two loaves.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      5. Place braided loaves on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper (ideally both loaves on one big sheet). Brush with soy milk.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      6. Leave to rise, uncovered, in a warm place until almost doubled in size (about 1 hour). Keep brushing with soy milk every 15 mins or so, to keep the dough from drying out and to build up layers of soy milk (this will give the loaf that glossy finish when baked).
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      7. While dough is rising, preheat oven to 350.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      8. Bake for 20 mins, then rotate the pan and bake for another 15-20 mins, until the loaves are evenly browned and sound hollow when thumped on the bottom. A convection oven really helps get an evenly browned loaf.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      A NOTE ON BUNS
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      This is also my go to recipe for buns--it makes 16 buns that I do as a 4x 4 batch bake on a large sheet pan. Brush them with soy milk like the loaves, but bake at 400 for 15-17 mins, rotating the pan half way through. Or, space them apart, slash the tops before baking, and sprinkle with sesame seeds after the last brushing with soy milk (as pictured below).
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-PQ6368o4EBQ/WjaDVDqNKuI/AAAAAAAAD7k/ZWTv8bldjpoujgngZQed_dww03SDfymKwCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_0959%2B%25281%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="1067" data-original-width="1600" height="426" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-PQ6368o4EBQ/WjaDVDqNKuI/AAAAAAAAD7k/ZWTv8bldjpoujgngZQed_dww03SDfymKwCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_0959%2B%25281%2529.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Or do hot dog/sausage buns.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gjySC0NNtCo/WjaBdVVkkiI/AAAAAAAAD7Y/TsZ97YOts4UltYQUR3M0xoXQyg_KA0-fgCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_0780.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" data-original-height="1067" data-original-width="1600" height="426" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gjySC0NNtCo/WjaBdVVkkiI/AAAAAAAAD7Y/TsZ97YOts4UltYQUR3M0xoXQyg_KA0-fgCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_0780.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      *Note: I prick the skin of a sweet potato a few times with a fork, then cook it in the microwave on the potato setting. It's fast and makes for a sweet potato that is not too wet.
                     </div>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://vegandad.blogspot.com/2017/12/crispy-fried-cauliflower-wingz.html">
                      Crispy Fried Cauliflower Wingz
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3525088356924904699" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gu1LaoCOr2s/WiXqVf1jfnI/AAAAAAAAD68/g3-1DyeYRKsHBDBWNl5A3841PC01SbvvQCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_1438.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" data-original-height="1067" data-original-width="1600" height="426" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gu1LaoCOr2s/WiXqVf1jfnI/AAAAAAAAD68/g3-1DyeYRKsHBDBWNl5A3841PC01SbvvQCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1438.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="color: #274e13;">
                       This recipe is perfect for your upcoming holiday party! Or tuck it away until the Super Bowl. An indulgence, to be sure, but you deserve it. I've been meaning to work up a recipe like this since the cauliflower "wings" craze hit the interwebs a while ago, but I never got around to it. These are crispy and flavourful, and remain so even when they are no longer hot. The boys doused theirs in Buffalo hot sauce, while the rest of us stuck to a sweet BBQ sauce. Delicious!
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <span style="color: #274e13;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     INGREDIENTS
                     <br/>
                     - 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Brine
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     - 2 cups cold water
                     <br/>
                     - 1 tsp garlic powder
                     <br/>
                     - 2 tsp onion powder
                     <br/>
                     - 2 tsp smoked or seasoned salt
                     <br/>
                     - 2 tsp paprika
                     <br/>
                     - 1 tsp poultry spice
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Batter
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     - 2 cups all purpose flour
                     <br/>
                     - 1 cup panko crumbs
                     <br/>
                     - 1/2 cup chickpea flour
                     <br/>
                     - 1/2 cup tapioca flour/starch
                     <br/>
                     - 1 tbsp each: onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, Italian seasoning
                     <br/>
                     - 1 tsp white pepper
                     <br/>
                     - 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
                     <br/>
                     - 3 cups plain soy milk (more if needed)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      METHOD
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      1.
                      <i>
                       The day before:
                      </i>
                      mix together the brine ingredients (I use a blender). Pour into a large freezer bag, then add the cauliflower florets. If your cauliflower is very large, you can make a 1.5 recipe of the brine.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      2. Remove as much air a possible so the brine is making maximum contact with the brine. Refrigerate for at least 12 hours, rotating the bag as needed for even brining.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      3.
                      <i>
                       The Day of
                      </i>
                      : drain cauliflower in a colander. Heat oil in a deep fryer to 350 degrees.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      4. While cauliflower is draining, whisk together the dry ingredients for the batter (i.e. flour to white pepper).
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      5. Dredge the cauliflower in the flour mixture in batches until coated. Shake off all excess and place on a baking sheet.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      6. In a separate bowl, whisk together vinegar and soy milk. Add enough of the soy mixture to the remaining flour mixture to make a thickish batter. Add more soy milk if needed.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      7. Add some pieces of cauliflower to the batter. Turn to coat. Leave the cauliflower in the batter for a few minutes to allow the batter to soak into the dredging flour.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      8. Shake off excess batter and transfer to a cooking tray or plate.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      9. Fry in oil, 3-4 minutes per side, until deep golden brown. Make sure your oil is not too hot or the outside will burn before the cauliflower is cooked.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      10. Drain on paper towels and serve while still hot.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      NOTE 1: while one batch is frying, add another to the batter so it can soak. Repeat.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      NOTE 2: add more soy milk to the batter, if needed. The dredging flour will thicken the batter a bit, so just thin it down again.
                     </div>

                       <img border="0" data-original-height="1183" data-original-width="1600" height="472" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tGqe_Oo62Tc/WiXqShSoEcI/AAAAAAAAD64/lJKMcltRYGQN3-llaWVAqgqdbUUwRvK5gCLcBGAs/s640/IMG_1435.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
