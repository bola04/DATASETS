      <h2>
       Welcome
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <strong>
        Society for Gestalt Theory and its Applications (GTA)
       </strong>
       is an international scientific association established for the purpose of promoting the Gestalt theoretical perspective in research and practice.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <strong>
        21th international scientific Gestalt conference
       </strong>
       <strong>
        will be held in Warsaw
       </strong>
       (Poland) in 2019.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Call for paper please click
        <a href="uploads/pdf/pubs/ENG_Call%20for%20Papers%20END.pdf">
         here!
        </a>
        (Registrationform for presentations click
        <a href="uploads/pdf/pubs/Registration%20form%20EN.docx">
         here!
        </a>
        )
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        You will find further information on this page soon.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2017 the GTA held its
       <strong>
        20th international scientific Gestalt conference
       </strong>
       in Vienna, Austria:
      </p>
      <div data-canvas-width="171.94283333333334" style="text-align: center;">
       April 27th – 29th, 2017
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </div>
      <h3 data-canvas-width="217.22166666666664" style="text-align: center;">
       Individual and Group
      </h3>
      <h3 data-canvas-width="356.3533333333335" style="text-align: center;">
       Dynamic Interdependency within Social Situations
       <br/>
       <br/>
      </h3>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.gestalttheory.net/cms/index.php?page=20thGTAcongress">
         Invitation and Conference Information
        </a>
        <a href="http://gth.krammerbuch.at/sites/default/files/articles/Create%20Article/Tagung_engl.pdf" target="_blank" title="20th GTA Gestalt Theory Convention, 2017 in Vienna, Austria">
         <br/>
        </a>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <img alt="Wien Stephansplatz" height="215" src="uploads/images/tagung/Stephansplatz.jpg" style="vertical-align: middle; margin: 5px;" width="400"/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        Vienna, Stephans Cathedral
        <br/>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        *****
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       In May 2015 the GTA held its 19th international Gestalt theory conference in Parma, Italy, hosted by and in cooperation with the
       <a href="http://www.lingue.unipr.it/" target="_blank" title="ALEF">
        Dipartimento di Antichistica, Lingue, Educazione, Filosofia (ALEF)
       </a>
       (Department of Classics, Modern Languages, Education and Philosophy) of the University of Parma, the Dipartimento di Neuroscienze (
       <a href="http://old.unipr.it/arpa/mirror/english/" target="_blank">
        Department of Neuroscience) of the University of Parma
       </a>
       and the Dipartimento di Scienze della Vita (
       <a href="http://dsv.units.it/en/" target="_blank">
        Life Sciences Department) of the University of Trieste
       </a>
       .
       <br/>
       <br/>
       The conference theme was:
       <strong>
        Body, Mind, Expression
       </strong>
