     <h2>
      Early Modern Texts
     </h2>
     <p>
      On this site you will find versions of some classics of early modern philosophy, and a few from the 19th century, prepared with a view to making them easier to read while leaving intact the main arguments, doctrines, and lines of thought.
     </p>
     <hr/>
     <p>
      The site is  now (2018) operated by the text-preparer
      <a href="http://www.earlymoderntexts.com/faqs/bennett">
       Jonathan Bennett
      </a>
      and his
      <span style="white-space: nowrap;">
       Canadian team.
      </span>
     </p>
     <hr/>
     <p>
      <b>
       We are in the process of creating
      </b>
      <a href="http://www.earlymoderntexts.com/audio" target="_top">
       audio books
      </a>
      <b>
       for selected texts.
      </b>
     </p>
     <hr/>
     <p>
      The most recently added texts are
     </p>
     <ul style="list-style-type:none">
      <li>
       <a href="http://www.earlymoderntexts.com/authors/montaigne" target="_top">
        Montaigne's
        <i>
         Essays
        </i>
        , Book II
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="http://www.earlymoderntexts.com/authors/paley" target="_top">
        Paley's
        <i>
         Natural Theology
        </i>
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="http://www.earlymoderntexts.com/authors/mandeville" target="_top">
        Mandeville's
        <i>
         The Fable of the Bees
        </i>
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="http://www.earlymoderntexts.com/authors/hume" target="_top">
        Hume's
        <i>
         Natural History of Religion
        </i>
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      The next will be Voltaire's

       Treatise on Tolerance
      </i>
