            <em>
             In the Time of PrEP

          </div>
          <div class="homecurrentnewsitem w-richtext">

           <p>
            The
            <em>
             BPJ
            </em>
            team is thrilled to announce that the inaugural title in our chapbook series is Jacques J. Rancourt's
            <em>
             In the Time of PrEP
            </em>
            . Rancourt is the author of
            <em>
             Novena
            </em>
            , winner of the Lena-Miles Wever Todd prize (Pleiades Press, 2017). He has held poetry fellowships from the Wisconsin Institute for Creative Writing, the Cit√© Internationale des Arts in Paris, and Stanford University, where he was a Wallace Stegner Fellow. His poems have appeared in
            <em>
             The Georgia Review
            </em>
            ,
            <em>
             Kenyon Review
            </em>
            ,
            <em>
             Missouri Review
            </em>
            ,
            <em>
             New England Review
            </em>
            ,
            <em>
             Ploughshares
            </em>
            , and
            <em>
             Virginia Quarterly Review
            </em>
            , among other journals, as well as in
            <em>
             Best New Poets
            </em>
            . He lives and teaches in the San Francisco Bay Area.
           </p>
