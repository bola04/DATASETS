        <h2>
         Electronic Paper eBook Readers
        </h2>
        <div class="ItemLeft">
         <a href="https://www.the-ebook-reader.com/onyx-boox-m96.html" title="Read Onyx Boox M96 Universe Review">
          <img alt="Boox M96" src="images/m96-home.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Portable reading devices like the
         <a href="https://www.the-ebook-reader.com/amazon-kindle.html">
          Amazon Kindle
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://blog.the-ebook-reader.com/tag/onyx-boox/">
          Onyx Boox
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="https://www.the-ebook-reader.com/pocketbook.html">
          PocketBook
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="https://www.the-ebook-reader.com/kobo.html">
          Kobo eReaders
         </a>
         all use a unique technology called epaper, which aims to mimic the look of ink on printed paper.
        </p>
        <p>
         Epaper is very energy efficient—a battery charge can last up to 2 months—and it is easily readable in bright light, unlike LCD tablets, computers, and phones.
        </p>
        <p>
         And since it isn't backlit, electronic paper is known to minimize eye strain, especially when reading for extended periods.
        </p>
        <h2 class="Clear">
         Color eReaders and Tablets
        </h2>
        <div class="ItemLeft">
         <a href="https://blog.the-ebook-reader.com/category/apple-ipad/" title="Apple iPad">
          <img alt="iPad eBooks" src="images/ipad-ebooks-home.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Ever since the
         <a href="https://www.the-ebook-reader.com/ipad-3.html">
          iPad
         </a>
         made reading on LCD screens popular, an explosion of Android tablets marketed as ereaders have hit the market.
        </p>
        <p>
         Color ereaders and
         <a href="https://blog.the-ebook-reader.com/category/tablets/">
          tablets
         </a>
         are more suited for reading magazines, newspapers, children's books, comics, and web content than dedicated ebook readers with monochrome screens.
        </p>
        <p>
         As technology continues to evolve, tablets and ebook readers may soon merge into one with color display technologies like
         <a href="https://www.the-ebook-reader.com/mirasol.html">
          Mirasol
         </a>
         , Pixel Qi, and
         <a href="https://blog.the-ebook-reader.com/2010/11/09/triton-color-epaper-technology-unveiled-by-e-ink/">
          color E Ink
         </a>
         .
        </p>
