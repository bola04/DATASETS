                  OHRC successfully challenges restrictions to benefits for workers aged 65+
                 </a>
                </h3>
                <span class="rdf-meta element-hidden" content="OHRC successfully challenges restrictions to benefits for workers aged 65+" property="dc:title">
                </span>
               </header>
               <footer class="submitted grid-5">
                May 31, 2018
               </footer>
               <div class="content clearfix">
                <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                 <div class="field-items">
                  <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                   <p>
                    <strong>
                     Toronto
                    </strong>
                    – The OHRC intervened in Talos v Grand Erie District School Board to challenge the provision of Ontario’s Human Rights Code that allowed employers to cut or reduce benefits to workers aged 65 and over. The Human Rights Tribunal of Ontario found that subsection 25(2.1) of the Code, as well as related provisions in the Employment Standards Act and its regulations, amount to age discrimination and violate the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.
                   </p>

         <div class="block-inner clearfix">
          <h2 class="block-title">
           Human rights in Ontario
          </h2>
          <div class="content clearfix">
           <p>
            The Ontario
            <em>
             <a href="/en/ontario-human-rights-code">
              Human Rights Code
             </a>
            </em>
            is a provincial law that gives everybody equal rights and opportunities without discrimination in specific social areas such as jobs, housing, services, facilities, and contracts or agreements.
           </p>
           <p>
            The
            <em>
             Code
            </em>
            ’s goal is to prevent discrimination and harassment because of race, sex, disability, and age, to name a few of the 17 grounds. All other Ontario laws must agree with the
            <em>
             Code
            </em>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Not all unfair treatment and not all harassment is covered by the
            <em>
             Code
            </em>
            . The treatment or harassment must be based on at least one
            <em>
             Code
            </em>
            ground and take place within a social area to be protected. For an explanation of discrimination and harassment, see
            <a href="http://www.hrlsc.on.ca/en/WhatIsDiscrimination.aspx">
             What is discrimination?
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
            If you believe you have experienced discrimination, the Human Rights Legal Support Centre can help you
            <a href="http://www.hrlsc.on.ca/en/node/143">
             determine if what you experienced is protected under the
             <em>
              Code
             </em>
            </a>
            . If you want to take legal steps to address an incident, the deadline is generally one year from the last discriminatory event.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Ontario
            <a href="/en/human-rights-system">
             Human Rights System
            </a>
            is made up of three separate agencies:
           </p>
           <ol>
            <li>
             The
             <a href="/en/about-us">
              Ontario Human Rights Commission
             </a>
             (that’s us) works to promote, protect and advance human rights through research, education, targeted legal action and policy development.
            </li>
            <li>
             The
             <a href="http://www.hrlsc.on.ca/en/Default.aspx">
              Human Rights Legal Support Centre
             </a>
             gives legal help to people who have experienced discrimination under the
             <em>
              Code
             </em>
             .
            </li>
            <li>
             The
             <a href="http://www.hrto.ca">
              Human Rights Tribunal
             </a>
             is where human rights applications are filed and decided.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <p>
            <a href="/en/learning/human-rights-101">
             Human Rights 101
            </a>
            will help guide you through Ontario’s Human Rights System.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
          </div>
