       <h2 class="entry-title">
        <a class="entry-title-link" href="http://talkingtree.org/theft-moqui-marbles-grand-staircase-national-monument/" rel="bookmark">
         Theft of Moqui Marbles in Grand Staircase National Monument
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2017-11-30T12:57:54+00:00">
         11/30/2017
        </span>
        By
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://talkingtree.org/author/cowlishaw/" rel="author">
           Nathan Cowlishaw
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/theft-moqui-marbles-grand-staircase-national-monument/#respond">
          Leave a Comment
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.talkingtreephoto.com/Photography/Recently-Uploaded/i-gzm2kfb/A" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
          <img alt=" " class="aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="https://i2.wp.com/www.talkingtreephoto.com/Photography/Recently-Uploaded/i-gzm2kfb/0/fb27294d/XL/moqui_marbles-XL.jpg?w=1023" style="border: 1px solid black;" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         These round balls are called moqui marbles are natural and take millions of years to erode out of solid Navajo Sandstone – They are hard concretions of iron that formed in the sandstone. After they are exposed to millions of years of sunlight they develop a dark polished patina called
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/desertvarnish/">
          #DesertVarnish
         </a>
         which takes eons to form.
        </p>
        <p>
         Currently, these
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/moquimarbles/">
          #moquimarbles
         </a>
         are under threat because of their value on the
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/blackmarket/">
          #BlackMarket
         </a>
         . They are also popular with the new age tourist trade and they can fetch a pretty penny by the piece. Last I spoke with a BLM Officer, they stated that over 30,000 lbs of these marbles have been stolen from off of Grand Staircase National Monument in Southern Utah. This is truly saddening, because I’ve actually gone to many of the locations where these marbles have gone missing and it destroys and scars the landscape where they were heavily removed. If you’re caught robbing these off of a federal monument, you may as well rob a bank because the consequences are just as severe!
        </p>
        <p>
         While I do not believe that wilderness is virgin, untouched, or pristine – I do think that humans have an innate responsibility as stewards over the land. The people who rip off these moqui marbles do so for a quick buck. The beauty, once changed, is irreplaceable.
        </p>
        <p>
         The image above shows a moqui marble out in the wild in an area where theft has occurred regularly for decades according to sources that live in the nearby community of Escalante,
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/utah/">
          #Utah
         </a>
         . Mostly what I was able to find here were bits of marble in a place that was once filled with shiny black marbles, smooth and baked in Desert Varnish.
        </p>

       <h2 class="entry-title">
        <a class="entry-title-link" href="http://talkingtree.org/voigtlander-heliar-15mm-f4-5-iii-ultrawide-favorite-landscape-lens/" rel="bookmark">
         Voigtlander Heliar 15mm F4.5 III Ultrawide is Favorite Landscape Lens
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2017-11-28T12:10:52+00:00">
         11/28/2017
        </span>
        By
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://talkingtree.org/author/cowlishaw/" rel="author">
           Nathan Cowlishaw
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/voigtlander-heliar-15mm-f4-5-iii-ultrawide-favorite-landscape-lens/#respond">
          Leave a Comment
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.talkingtreephoto.com/Photography/Recently-Uploaded/i-rnmgBDD/A" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
          <img alt=" " class="aligncenter" data-recalc-dims="1" src="https://i1.wp.com/www.talkingtreephoto.com/Photography/Recently-Uploaded/i-rnmgBDD/0/dc57c58e/XL/voigtlander_15mm_3_2-XL.jpg?w=960" style="border: 1px solid black;" title=""/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I’ve had this lens for a few years now and must admit it is one of the best landscape lenses that I have ever owned and had the pleasure of using. The quality of my images easily beat previous wide angles that I have utilized in the past.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want a high quality lens for professional landscape photography – I highly recommend the
         <a href="https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/voigtlander/">
         </a>
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/voigtlander/">
          #Voigtlander
         </a>
         <a href="https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/heliar/">
         </a>
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/heliar/">
          #Heliar
         </a>
         <a href="https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/15mm/">
          #15mm
         </a>
         F4.5 III lens for
         <a href="https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/sony/">
         </a>
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/sony/">
          #Sony
         </a>
         <a href="https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/emount/">
         </a>
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/emount/">
          #EMount
         </a>
         . This is perhaps THEE BEST
         <a href="https://www.instagram.com/explore/tags/wideangle/">
         </a>
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/wideangle/">
          #wideangle
         </a>
         lens that I have owned to date and have been using for a few years now.
        </p>
        <p>
         I was visiting with Stephen Gandy today who is the largest distributors of
         <a href="http://talkingtree.org/tag/cosina/">
          #Cosina
         </a>
         / Voigtlander lenses over at
         <a href="http://cameraquest.com">
          CameraQuest.com.
         </a>
         He runs an invaluable resource on vintage lenses through his website as well. Check it out!
        </p>
        <p>
         I will post a few sample images here that I’ve captured with this lens and will post more down the road.
        </p>
