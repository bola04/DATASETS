     <p>

      <a href="basilicata/matera.html">
       Matera
      </a>
      , the unique volcanic
      <a href="sicily/aeolian-islands.html">
       Aeolian islands
      </a>
      ,  lovely, little-known
      <a href="lombardy/lake-iseo.html">
       Lake Iseo
      </a>
      and the ancient Greek city of Syracuse (modern
      <a href="sicily/siracusa.html">
       Siracusa
      </a>
      ) in Sicily. You'll also find suggestions for interesting excursions in the overlooked area around Rome, with recommended
      <a href="daytrips.html">
       daytrips
      </a>
      including unusual spots like the cave of an emperor at
      <a href="sperlonga.html">
       Sperlonga
      </a>
      and
      <a href="lazio/terracina.html">
       Terracina
      </a>

     <h1>
      Welcome to Italy
     </h1>
     <p>
      Italy - Bella Italia, the Bel Paese - is a country of extremes, with something to offer every visitor: from snow-iced mountains to hot lazy Mediterranean beaches, from unspoilt hill villages to cities that have bustled for 
thousands of years. This website is designed to help you plan a holiday in Italy, or to research Italian vacation options.
     </p>
     <img align="left" alt="Vernazza in the Cinque Terre" height="246" src="liguria/images/vernazza2.jpg" width="180"/>
     <p>
      Italy travel forums on the internet are full of overwhelmed travellers asking 'I have two weeks in Italy, what should I see?' Responses range from: 'Everything: do
      <a href="veneto/venice/index.html">
       Venice
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="milan/home.html">
       Milan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="tuscany/florence/index.html">
       Florence
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="rome/home.html">
       Rome
      </a>
      then
      <a href="naples.html">
       Naples
      </a>
      ,' to 'Spend a fortnight in
      <a href="tuscany/index.html">
       Tuscany
      </a>
      '. 
The best answer, of course, is to do what suits you. Some people want to squeeze in as much as possible for a 
once-in-a-lifetime trip. Others prefer
to explore a culture at a slower pace. On Italy Heaven you can look around, and consider what your dream holiday might be. Learn more about selected
      <a href="destinations.html">
       destinations
      </a>
      , and read advice on travel in Italy to make your stay easier, more fulfilling or more fun.
     </p>
     <p>
      Nowadays planning your own holiday is a viable and economical alternative to booking a package. It's easy to organise your own journey and book online, and Italy Heaven offers you practical tips and assistance, from
      <a href="italyhotels.html">
       advice on choosing a hotel
      </a>
      to
      <a href="itineraries/index.html">
       suggested Italy travel itineraries
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h4>
      Italian cities
     </h4>
     <p>
      Italy is a dream destination for city breaks, with three of the world's most beautiful cities:
      <a href="rome/home.html">
       Rome
      </a>
      , where antiquities bask in the sunshine in a colourful and lively metropolis,
      <a href="tuscany/florence/index.html">
       Florence
      </a>
      , probably the art capital of the world, and unique
      <a href="veneto/venice/index.html">
       Venice
      </a>
      . Those in search of culture or vibrant modernity may prefer the charms of fashionable
      <a href="milan/home.html">
       Milan
      </a>
      or 
stately
      <a href="turin.html">
       Turin
      </a>
      , while the intrepid can dive into the colourful chaos of historic
      <a href="naples.html">
       Naples
      </a>
      .
