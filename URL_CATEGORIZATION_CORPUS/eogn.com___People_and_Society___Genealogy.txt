      <h2 class="post-title entry-title">
       <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/historic-aerials/">
        Historic Aerials
       </a>
      </h2>
      <!-- .post-title -->
      <p class="post-meta">
       <span class="post-author-meta">
        <a class="post-author" href="https://blog.eogn.com/author/eogn/" rel="author">
         Dick Eastman
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .post-author-meta -->
       <span class="post-updated-meta">
        ·
        <a class="updated" href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/historic-aerials/">
         June 20, 2018
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .updated-meta -->
       <span class="post-category-meta">
        ·
        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/category/online-sites/" rel="category tag">
         Online Sites
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .category-meta -->
       <span class="post-comment-link-meta">
        ·
        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/historic-aerials/#comments">
         2  Comments
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .comment-link-meta -->
      </p>
      <!-- .post-meta -->
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Historic Aerials
        </strong>
        claims to be “Home of the most comprehensive database of historical aerial photos of the United States.” Indeed, it is a huge resource.
       </p>
       <p>
        According to information on the Historic Aerials web site:
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left:30px;">
        “Take a ride in our time machine!
        <br/>
        “Nationwide Environmental Title Research has spent the past 20+ years collecting the worlds largest database of historical aerial images and topographical maps of the United States. Our sources include USGS imagery, several private collections, and we are continually acquiring more. All the imagery we collect is painstakingly orthorectified to provide the data in a searchable and precise geo-locatable format. Our web application allows you to quickly and easily search for any parcel of U.S. land and instantly view that same area in any other year where we could obtain an aerial photo.”
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="https://www.historicaerials.com">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23199" data-attachment-id="23199" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":"","orientation":"0"}' data-image-title="historicaerials" data-large-file="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg?w=740?w=512" data-medium-file="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg?w=740?w=300" data-orig-file="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg?w=740" data-orig-size="512,512" data-permalink="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/historic-aerials/historicaerials/" sizes="(max-width: 512px) 100vw, 512px" src="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg?w=740" srcset="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg 512w, https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg?w=96 96w, https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/historicaerials.jpeg?w=300 300w"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        While the basic service is free, the pictures displayed to free users have a rather obnoxious watermark pasted on top of each image. That makes the free images almost useless. Obviously, the web site owners want each user to sign up for a paid account in order to obtain more useful images.
       </p>

      <h2 class="post-title entry-title">
       <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/smallpdf-converts-pdf-files-to-jpg-and-other-formats/">
        SmallPDF Converts PDF Files to JPG and Other Formats
       </a>
      </h2>
      <!-- .post-title -->
      <p class="post-meta">
       <span class="post-author-meta">
        <a class="post-author" href="https://blog.eogn.com/author/eogn/" rel="author">
         Dick Eastman
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .post-author-meta -->
       <span class="post-updated-meta">
        ·
        <a class="updated" href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/smallpdf-converts-pdf-files-to-jpg-and-other-formats/">
         June 20, 2018
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .updated-meta -->
       <span class="post-category-meta">
        ·
        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/category/cloud-services/" rel="category tag">
         Cloud Services
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .category-meta -->
       <span class="post-comment-link-meta">
        ·
        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/20/smallpdf-converts-pdf-files-to-jpg-and-other-formats/#comments">
         One Comment
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .comment-link-meta -->
      </p>
      <!-- .post-meta -->
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        Genealogists seem to use a lot of different documents in their computers and sometimes need to convert from one format to another. For instance, one newsletter reader recently mentioned a need to convert an e-book in PDF format to JPG files. With the right software, this is easy to do.
       </p>
       <p>
        Any number of programs are available that will convert files from one format to another. In this case, the newsletter reader wanted to convert an entire book that was in PDF format to individual JPG files, one file for each page in the book. I suggested using
        <strong>
         SmallPDF
        </strong>
        , a
        <strong>
         FREE
        </strong>
        online service.
       </p>

      <!-- .post-meta -->
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        A Washington state trucker who authorities say was linked by DNA evidence to the 1987 deaths of a young Canadian couple has been charged with two counts of aggravated first-degree murder. William Earl Talbott II, 55, of SeaTac was charged Friday in Snohomish County (Washington) Superior Court. Talbott is charged in the killings of 18-year-old Tanya Van Cuylenborg and 20-year-old Jay Cook.
       </p>
       <p>
        Authorities say they used information from public genealogy websites to pinpoint Talbott as a suspect then arrested him after getting a DNA sample from a cup that fell from his truck. Police say the genealogist used information uploaded by distant cousins to narrow their search to Talbott.
       </p>
       <p>
        You can read more about the case in an article by Caleb Hutton in the
        <em>
         Herald.Net
        </em>
        at
        <a href="http://bit.ly/2JL1Wfo" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
         http://bit.ly/2JL1Wfo
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>

        (+) When is it Time to Hire a Professional Genealogist?
       </a>
      </h2>
      <!-- .post-title -->
      <p class="post-meta">
       <span class="post-author-meta">
        <a class="post-author" href="https://blog.eogn.com/author/eogn/" rel="author">
         Dick Eastman
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .post-author-meta -->
       <span class="post-updated-meta">
        ·
        <a class="updated" href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/15/when-it-is-time-to-hire-a-professional-genealogist/">
         June 15, 2018
        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .updated-meta -->
       <span class="post-category-meta">
        ·
        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/category/plus-edition-article/" rel="category tag">

        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .category-meta -->
      </p>
      <!-- .post-meta -->
      <div class="post-content">
       <p style="padding-left:30px;">
        <em>
         The following is a Plus Edition article written by and copyright by Dick Eastman.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Genealogy research is a fascinating endeavor. After all, your family tree is a puzzle that needs to be solved. In fact, you are literally finding out where you came from. I strongly recommend that anyone with an interest in ancestry do their own research. Indeed, it is fun and challenging.
       </p>
       <p>
        As author of this newsletter, I sometimes field questions from genealogy newcomers — questions like how they can hire someone to research their family tree for a fee. I typically respond with still another question and a comment: “Would you pay someone to play a round of golf for you? While that might complete the objective, you will miss out on the entire experience.”
       </p>
       <p>
        Despite my rather cavalier remark, I will suggest that professional genealogists can be your best friends and assistants after you have started your own genealogy research. Yes, you should do the basics yourself. You should start with yourself and then find information about your parents, your grandparents, your great-grandparents, and so on, as far back as you can possibly go on your own. Researching your own family tree is fun and can be inexpensive. However, when you do hit a “stone wall” and cannot go back any further, it may be time to call in the professionals.
       </p>

       <p style="padding-left:30px;">
        Over 691,000 records have been added to our National Archives’ collection of medical records from the First World War. Images may provide a variety of additional details such as their service history and a description of the wound.

        A Fire at the Aberdeen (Washington) Museum of History Destroys Much of the Museum’s Collection and a Genealogy Society’s Library
       </a>
      </h2>
      <!-- .post-title -->
      <p class="post-meta">
       <span class="post-author-meta">
        <a class="post-author" href="https://blog.eogn.com/author/eogn/" rel="author">

        </a>
       </span>
       <!-- .post-author-meta -->
       <span class="post-updated-meta">
        ·
        <a class="updated" href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/14/a-fire-at-the-aberdeen-washington-museum-of-history-destroys-much-of-the-museums-collection-and-a-genealogy-societys-library/">

       <span class="post-category-meta">

        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/category/current-affairs/" rel="category tag">

       <!-- .category-meta -->
       <span class="post-comment-link-meta">
        ·
        <a href="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/14/a-fire-at-the-aberdeen-washington-museum-of-history-destroys-much-of-the-museums-collection-and-a-genealogy-societys-library/#comments">
         3  Comments

       </span>
       <!-- .comment-link-meta -->
      </p>
      <!-- .post-meta -->
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        Here is a story that every genealogist, archivist, historian, and museum manager never wants to read: On Saturday, a fire destroyed the Aberdeen Museum of History in Kurt Cobain’s hometown of Aberdeen, Washington, which included items from his early life.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23125" data-attachment-id="23125" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":"","orientation":"1"}' data-image-title="Aberdeen Fire" data-large-file="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg?w=740?w=550" data-medium-file="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg?w=740?w=300" data-orig-file="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg?w=740" data-orig-size="550,309" data-permalink="https://blog.eogn.com/2018/06/14/a-fire-at-the-aberdeen-washington-museum-of-history-destroys-much-of-the-museums-collection-and-a-genealogy-societys-library/aberdeen-fire/" sizes="(max-width: 550px) 100vw, 550px" src="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg?w=740" srcset="https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg 550w, https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg?w=128 128w, https://eogn.files.wordpress.com/2018/06/aberdeen-fire.jpg?w=300 300w"/>
       </p>
       <p style="padding-left:30px;">
        <strong>
         NOTE:
        </strong>
        The late Kurt Cobain was an American singer, songwriter, and musician. Born in Aberdeen, Washington, Cobain formed the band Nirvana with Krist Novoselic and Aaron Burckhard in 1987.
       </p>
       <p>
        No one was in the building at the time of the fire, and no injuries were reported. It took 77 firefighters 10.5 hours to suppress the fire. The fire started from within the building, a cause most typically related to heating or electrical malfunctions. At around 9:30 a.m. on Saturday, firefighters from multiple agencies responded to the fire in the Armory Building. It spread through most of the building, and collapsed a majority of the roof. It caused serious damage to the museum and other organizations located inside like the Coastal Community Action Program, the Senior Center, and the
        <strong>
         Grays Harbor Genealogical Society research library
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
