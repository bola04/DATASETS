       <h3 class="basic-title-subtitle">
        After selling winery in Sonoma, Cohn launches new brand from remaining vineyard
       </h3>

        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         Glen Ellen, Calif.
        </em>
        —Bruce Cohn is no stranger to the wine country scene and his name is known to both wine and music lovers across the country. The founder of B.R. Cohn Winery in Sonoma Valley, for 42 years Cohn doubled as the manager for the Doobie Brothers rock group, and his winery was known as much for its classic rock festivals as it was for its estate wines.

        <a href="/news/article/200328/Virginia-Wine-Pioneer-Dennis-Horton-Dies">
         Virginia Wine Pioneer Dennis Horton Dies
        </a>
       </h2>
       <h3 class="basic-title-subtitle">
        First vintner to plant Viognier, brought back Norton and established estate winery
       </h3>
       <div class="teaser-date" content="06.21.2018" itemprop="datePublished">
        Published on 06.21.2018
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="teaser-content">
       <div>
        <div class="newsPicture">
         <img alt="hertz" previewstyle="background-size:90%;background-position:10% auto" src="/content/image/wv/wv-2018-06-21_horton.jpg" welcome=""/>
         <div style="line-height: 0px;">
         </div>
         Dennis Horton and his wife Sharon established their first commercial vineyard in 1989 and opened an estate winery in 1993. Photo source: Southern Foodways Alliance.
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         Gordonsville, Va.—
        </em>
        A leader in the rebirth of the Virginian wine industry, Dennis Horton, died June 19. He was 73.
       </p>
       <p>
        Horton was born on Dec. 1, 1945 and grew up in Hermann, Mo. After serving in the United States Air Force, Horton attended the University of Maryland. He and his wife Sharon moved to Virginia in 1977 and planted a small vineyard at their home in Madison County in 1983.
       </p>
