      <h2>
       Edward Marston
      </h2>
      <p>
       <img alt="Edward Marston" class="alignLeft" height="192" src="./images/content/author3.jpg" width="128"/>
       Author of sixty crime novels, set in seven distinct periods of history. His books have been translated into many languages.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <a href="./domesday.html">
         Domesday Books
        </a>
       </strong>
       explore the crimes and misdemeanours surrounding the compilation of Domesday in the late 11th century.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <a href="./bracewell.html">
         Nicholas Bracewell
        </a>
       </strong>
       series features an Elizabethan theatre company.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <a href="./redmayne.html">
         Christopher Redmayne
        </a>
       </strong>
       mysteries look at the rebuilding of London after the Great Fire of 1666.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <a href="./colbeck.html">
         Inspector Colbeck
        </a>
       </strong>
       series deals with major crimes committed on Victorian railways.
       <strong>
        Inspector Marmion
       </strong>
       , the
       <strong>
        <a href="./homefront.html">
         Home Front Detective
        </a>
       </strong>
       , solves major crimes in Britain during the Great War.
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <strong>
        Soldier of Fortune
       </strong>
       , dashing career soldier and ladies' man,
       <strong>
        <a href="./rawson.html">
         Captain Daniel Rawson
        </a>
        ,
       </strong>
       distinguishes himself at the battle of Blenheim in 1704.
       <strong>
        Drums of War
       </strong>
       takes him on to 1706 where he is given a challenging solo assignment.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Bow Street Rivals
       </strong>
       is a series set immediately after the Battle of Waterloo and follows the adventures of Peter and Paul Skillen, identical twins, who fight against the rising crime wave in London. Their debut,
       <strong>
        <a href="./pubs2015.html">
         Shadow of the Hangman
        </a>
       </strong>
       , was published in January 2015.
      </p>

      <strong>
       Edward Marston
      </strong>
      was born and brought up in Wales. He read Modern History at Oxford then lectured in the subject for three years before becoming a full-time freelance writer. His first historical mystery,
      <strong>
       The Queen's Head,
      </strong>
      was published in 1988, launching the Nicholas Bracewell series.
