     <h1>
      Funny Poems
     </h1>
     <p>
      Peculiar Poetry is a collection of short funny poems  for teenagers and adults by contemporary English poets Patrick Winstanley, Paul Curtis and Max Scratchmann. The poems have been variously described as 'really very funny, and very nearly poems', 'symptomatic of a deeply disturbed mind' and 'laughable'.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Funny Poems of Every Shape and Style
     </h2>
     <p>
      The collection is diverse in both style and subject matter, 
      including clerihews, parodies and shape poems on subjects ranging from alopecia 
      to xenophobia. There is lighthearted poetry for teens about the angst of adolescence 
      and adult poetry, both funny and serious, about love, life and the universe.
     </p>
     <a href="/max/funny/literary-parodies/47par-001-martin-amis.htm">
      <img alt="An illustration from Max Scratchmann's parody 'Making Bovril for Martin Amis'." class="floatrtlp" src="/images/tlp/martin-amis.jpg"/>
     </a>
     <h2>
      Nonsense Verse and Rude Rhymes
     </h2>
     <p>
      Our newest recruit, Max Scratchmann, is a poet with a most individual voice. Max's  initial collection includes clean funny poems, humorous hate poems and  literary parodies and nostalgic poems, but the field in which  Max excels is
      <a href="http://www.nonsense-poems.com/max/funny/nonsense-verse/">
       nonsense verse
      </a>
      . So immerse yourself in Max's parallel universe and prepare yourself the wealth of poems to come.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Short Poems about Love, Death and Sex
     </h2>
     <p>
      Paul Curtis, our first poet in residence, is a writer of rare talent who has contributed  over four hundred funny poems to the site. His contribution includes collections of
      <a href="/paul-curtis/funny/sport-poems/">
       sports poems
      </a>
      ,  twisted funny poems, rude nursery rhymes and
      <a href="/paul-curtis/funny/dirty-poems/">
       dirty poems
      </a>
      <a href="/paul-curtis/funny/dirty-poems/">
       ,
      </a>
      together with his seminal series of short funny poems about love, life, death and sex. Paul is also a prolific writer of
      <a href="http://www.love-poetry.net/">
       romantic love poems
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      Wry and Inscrutable Poems
     </h2>
     <p>
      Patrick Winstanley's poems are wry, whimsical and weird - qualities which lent Peculiar Poetry its name. Patrick's work ranges from  superficially cute and cuddly animal poems, via
      <a href="/funny/family-poems/">
       funny poems about family life
      </a>
      and famous people   (living, dead and indeterminate) to studiedly
      <a href="/funny/rude-poems/">
       rude  poems
      </a>
      about smegma, vaginoplasty and a host of other unmentionables.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Oddly Conventional
     </h2>
     <p>
      Peculiar Poetry has bowed to convention and included poems for the major holidays and high days in the religious and secular calendars. There are sins of omission, but Peculiar Poetry currently offers christmas poems,  halloween poems, funny Mother's Day poems,  Father's Day poems and valentines poems, while our sister
      <em>
       <a href="http://www.love-poetry.net">
        Love Poetry
       </a>
      </em>
      site has an extensive collection of Paul Curtis's romantic valentines poems.
     </p>
     <h2>
      Not for the Fainthearted...
     </h2>
     <p>
      As one reviewer famously put it, 'Peculiar Poetry combines the comic talent of Karl Marx with the intellectual genius of Groucho Marx.' So expect funny poems that are always wry, often witty, sometimes dark or dirty.  Expect the unexpected…
     </p>
