        <p>
         Public Access to Court Electronic Records (PACER) is an electronic public access service that allows users to obtain case and docket information online from federal appellate, district, and bankruptcy courts, and the PACER Case Locator. PACER is provided by the Federal Judiciary in keeping with its commitment to providing public access to court information via a centralized service.
        </p>

       <h3>
        PACER Case Locator
       </h3>
       The PACER Case Locator is a national index for U.S. district, bankruptcy, and appellate courts. A subset of information from each case is transferred to the PACER Case Locator server each night.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       The system serves as a locator index for PACER. You may conduct nationwide searches to determine whether or not a party is involved in federal litigation.
       <br/>
       <a class="more" href="http://pcl.uscourts.gov">
        Search Now

           The more than one million PACER users include attorneys,
           <i>

          </p>

        <div class="AccordionPanelTab">
         <h3>
          How do I access PACER?
         </h3>
        </div>
        <div class="AccordionPanelContent" style="height: 0px; display: none;">
         <div class="AccordionPanelContentContainer">
          <p style="padding-bottom: 10px;">
           First, you will need a PACER account.  Sign up for one using the
           <a href="/pscof/registration.jsf">
            online registration process
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p style="padding-bottom: 10px;">
           Each court maintains its case information locally.  If you know the district or circuit in which the case is filed, you may
           <a href="/psco/cgi-bin/links.pl">
            search that court directly
           </a>
           .  If you do not know where the case is filed, use the
           <a href="https://pcl.uscourts.gov">
            PACER Case Locator
           </a>
           .
          </p>

           <a href="/announcements/general/train.html">

          </p>

       <div class="AccordionPanel AccordionPanelClosed">
        <div class="AccordionPanelTab">
         <h3>
          When can I access PACER?
         </h3>
        </div>
        <div class="AccordionPanelContent" style="height: 0px; display: none;">
         <div class="AccordionPanelContentContainer">
          <p>
           Case information is available 24 hours a day, including weekends and holidays.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           PACER account specialists are available to assist you at (800) 676-6856 between the hours of 8 AM and 6 PM CT Monday through Friday or by email at
           <a href="mailto:" pacer="">
            pacer@psc.uscourts.gov
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="AccordionPanel AccordionPanelClosed">
        <div class="AccordionPanelTab">
         <h3>
          What information is available on PACER?
         </h3>
        </div>
        <div class="AccordionPanelContent" style="height: 0px; display: none;">
         <div class="AccordionPanelContentContainer">
          <p>
           PACER hosts millions of case file documents and docket information for all district, bankruptcy, and appellate courts. These are available immediately after they have been electronically filed. If you are unable to locate the information you are seeking, please contact the local court or the PACER Service Center at (800) 676-6856 between the hours of 8 AM and 6 PM CT Monday through Friday or by email at
           <a href="mailto:pacer@psc.uscourts.gov">
            pacer@psc.uscourts.gov
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="AccordionPanel AccordionPanelClosed">
        <div class="AccordionPanelTab">
         <h3>
          Is all case information available to the public?
         </h3>
        </div>
        <div class="AccordionPanelContent" style="height: 0px; display: none;">
         <div class="AccordionPanelContentContainer">
          <p>
           No. Some case information is protected. Certain personal identifiers are removed or redacted before the record becomes public, including Social Security number, financial account numbers,
					the name of a minor, date of birth, and home addresses in a criminal case. In addition, some documents may be unavailable to the general public, including:
           <br/>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li style="color:#00235D;list-style-type:disc">
            Pre-2003 bankruptcy case documents.
           </li>
           <li style="color:#00235D;list-style-type:disc">
            Criminal case documents older than Nov. 1, 2004.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="AccordionPanel AccordionPanelClosed">
        <div class="AccordionPanelTab">
         <h3>
          How much does PACER cost?
         </h3>
        </div>
        <div class="AccordionPanelContent" style="height: 0px; display: none;">
         <div class="AccordionPanelContentContainer">
          <p>
           Access to case information costs $0.10 per page. The cost to access a single document is capped at $3.00, the equivalent of 30 pages. The cap does not apply to name searches, reports that are not case-specific, and transcripts of federal court proceedings.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           By Judicial Conference policy, if your usage does not exceed $15 in a quarter, fees are waived.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="AccordionPanel AccordionPanelClosed">

            More&gt;&gt;
