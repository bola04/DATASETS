        Jessica Hamrick
       </h1>
      </header>
      <p>
       <img src="/images/badge.jpg" style="float:right; margin: 1em 0em 2em 2em; width: 300px"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Beginning in November 2017, I am Research Scientist at
       <a href="https://deepmind.com/">
        DeepMind
       </a>
       .
I completed my PhD in Psychology at UC Berkeley in Tom Griffiths’
       <a href="http://cocosci.berkeley.edu/">
        Computational Cognitive Science Lab
       </a>
       . Before that, I researched
intuitive physics in Josh Tenenbaum’s
       <a href="http://web.mit.edu/cocosci/">
        Computational Cognitive Science Group
       </a>
       at MIT.
      </p>
      <p>
       I study human cognition by combining probabilistic models from machine learning
with behavioral experiments from cognitive science. My
       <a href="/research">
        research
       </a>
       focuses specifically on understanding mental simulation from a computational,
resource-rational perspective. For details, please see my
       <a href="/publications">
        publications
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Additionally, I am a strong advocate for open source, open access, and open data
in science. I am a core contributor to the
       <a href="http://jupyter.org/">
        Jupyter
       </a>
       project and lead maintainer of
       <a href="https://github.com/jupyter/nbgrader#nbgrader">
        nbgrader
       </a>
       , which is a tool for
creating and grading assignments in the Jupyter notebook. When I have time, I
help out with the occasional
       <a href="http://software-carpentry.org/">
        Software Carpentry
       </a>
       workshop and contribute to a number of other
       <a href="/software">
        open source projects
       </a>
       .
      </p>
