        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          RealityStudio is the web’s leading resource dedicated to William S. Burroughs and the underground press in the 1960s.
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="William S. Burroughs with a copy of Naked Lunch at the Beat Hotel, 1959" src="http://cdn.realitystudio.org/images/biography/1959_life/loomis-dean.william-burroughs-and-naked-lunch-at-beat-hotel.jpg" style="margin-bottom:1em;" title="William S. Burroughs with a copy of Naked Lunch at the Beat Hotel, 1959"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         What’s New
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="bibliographic-bunker/crawdaddy-archive/">
          Crawdaddy Archive
         </a>
         by Jed Birmingham
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="cut-ups-at-60/">
          CUT-UPS@60
         </a>
         is a conference to be held in London and Paris, September 2020, honoring the 60th anniversary of the invention of the cut-up technique. Watch this space for more detail.
        </p>
        <p>
         RealityStudio is proud to make Michael Stevens’
         <a href="publications/road-to-interzone/">
          The Road to Interzone: Reading William S. Burroughs Reading
         </a>
         available as a free PDF download.
         <i>
          Road to Interzone
         </i>
         , a bibliography of Burroughs’ reading, is one of the indispensable reference works on the author.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://realitystudio.org/texts/scientology-revisited/">
          Scientology Revisited
         </a>
         is a rare Scientology pamphlet reproducing one of William Burroughs’ texts from Mayfair magazine.
        </p>
        <p>
         A small archive of
         <a href="biography/giorno-poetry-project-the-aids-treatment-project/" target="_blank">
          correspondence between Giorno Poetry Project and COIL
         </a>
         documenting the literary community’s response to the AIDS crisis.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="texts/linternationale-hallucinex/">
          L’Internationale Hallucinex
         </a>
         , a pamphlet containing French translations of William Burroughs’ “Invisible Generation,” as well as texts by Claude Pélieu and Carl Weissner.
        </p>
        <p>
         With many thanks to Michael Stevens, the listing of
         <a href="texts/burroughs-blurbs/">
          blurbs which William Burroughs furnished for others’ books
         </a>
         is now almost complete.
        </p>
