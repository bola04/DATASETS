    <h1 class="crayon article-titre-17 entry-title">
     Welcome to the Carbohydrate-Active enZYmes Database
    </h1>
    <div class="crayon article-texte-17 texte entry-content">
     <h3>
      The
      <font color="#5CA2A8">
       CAZy
      </font>
      database describes the families of structurally-related catalytic and carbohydrate-binding modules (or functional domains) of enzymes that degrade, modify, or create glycosidic bonds.
     </h3>
     <p>
      Online since 1998,
      <font color="#5CA2A8">
       CAZy
      </font>
      is a specialist database dedicated to the display and analysis of genomic, structural and biochemical information on Carbohydrate-Active Enzymes (CAZymes).
     </p>
     <p>
      <font color="#5CA2A8">
       CAZy
      </font>
      data are accessible either by browsing sequence-based families or by browsing the content of genomes in carbohydrate-active enzymes. New genomes are added regularly shortly after they appear in the daily releases of GenBank. New families are created based on published evidence for the activity of at least one member of the family and all families are regularly updated, both in content and in description.
     </p>
     <p>
      An original aspect of the
      <font color="#5CA2A8">
       CAZy
      </font>
      database is its attempt to cover all carbohydrate-active enzymes across organisms and across subfields of glycosciences. Please let us know if some families have escaped our attention, we will be happy to add them !
     </p>
     <p>
      For a more extensive encyclopedic resource on the particular features of carbohydrate active enzymes, please visit
      <a class="spip_out" href="http://www.cazypedia.org" rel="external">
       <strong>
        CAZypedia
       </strong>
      </a>
      , a web site driven by the scientific community that studies these enzymes.
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Reference for the CAZy database :
      </b>
      In the 2014 database issue of Nucleic Acids Research, we summarized the many changes that have occurred in the CAZy database during the five previous years.
      <br/>
      Read the
      <a class="spip_out" href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24270786" rel="external">
       <strong>
        Abstract
       </strong>
      </a>
      or the full
      <a class="spip_out" href="https://academic.oup.com/nar/article-lookup/doi/10.1093/nar/gkt1178" rel="external">
       <strong>
        paper
       </strong>
      </a>
      .
      <br/>
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       A new tool associated with the CAZy database !
      </b>
      <a class="spip_out" href="http://www.cazy.org/PULDB">
       <strong>
        PULDB
       </strong>
      </a>
      is a database of Polysaccharide Utilization Loci (PULs) in Bacteroidetes.
      <a class="spip_out" href="http://www.cazy.org/PULDB">
       <strong>
        PULDB
       </strong>
      </a>
      displays information on experimentally determined and predicted PULs for a number of Bacteroidetes genomes.
      <br/>
      Read the
      <a class="spip_out" href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/?term=25355788" rel="external">
       <strong>
        Abstract
       </strong>
      </a>
      or the full
      <a class="spip_out" href="http://bioinformatics.oxfordjournals.org/content/early/2014/10/28/bioinformatics.btu716.long" rel="external">
       <strong>
        paper
       </strong>
      </a>
      .
      <br/>
     </p>
     <div class="paragraphe">
      <h3 class="spip">
       Enzyme Classes currently covered
      </h3>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Modules that catalyze the breakdown, biosynthesis or modification of carbohydrates and glycoconjugates:
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="-" class="puce" height="11" src="local/cache-vignettes/L8xH11/puce-cebf5.gif" style="" width="8"/>
       <strong>
        <a class="spip_out" href="Glycoside-Hydrolases.html">
         <strong>
          Glycoside Hydrolases (GHs)
         </strong>
        </a>
        :
       </strong>
       hydrolysis and/or rearrangement of glycosidic bonds (see CAZypedia
       <a class="spip_out" href="http://www.cazypedia.org/index.php/Glycoside_hydrolases" rel="external">
        definition
       </a>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="-" class="puce" height="11" src="local/cache-vignettes/L8xH11/puce-cebf5.gif" style="" width="8"/>
       <strong>
        <a class="spip_out" href="GlycosylTransferases.html">
         <strong>
          GlycosylTransferases (GTs)
         </strong>
        </a>
        :
       </strong>
       formation of glycosidic bonds (see
       <a class="spip_out" href="http://www.cazypedia.org/index.php/Glycosyltransferases" rel="external">
        definition
       </a>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="-" class="puce" height="11" src="local/cache-vignettes/L8xH11/puce-cebf5.gif" style="" width="8"/>
       <strong>
        <a class="spip_out" href="Polysaccharide-Lyases.html">
         <strong>
          Polysaccharide Lyases (PLs)
         </strong>
        </a>
        :
       </strong>
       non-hydrolytic cleavage of glycosidic bonds
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="-" class="puce" height="11" src="local/cache-vignettes/L8xH11/puce-cebf5.gif" style="" width="8"/>
       <strong>
        <a class="spip_out" href="Carbohydrate-Esterases.html">
         <strong>
          Carbohydrate Esterases (CEs)
         </strong>
        </a>
        :
       </strong>
       hydrolysis of carbohydrate esters
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="-" class="puce" height="11" src="local/cache-vignettes/L8xH11/puce-cebf5.gif" style="" width="8"/>
       <strong>
        <a class="spip_out" href="Auxiliary-Activities.html">
         <strong>
          Auxiliary Activities (AAs)
         </strong>
        </a>
        :
       </strong>
       redox enzymes that act in conjunction with CAZymes.
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="paragraphe">
      <h3 class="spip">
       Associated Modules currently covered
      </h3>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Carbohydrate-active enzymes often display a modular structure with non-catalytic modules appended to the enzymes above
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="-" class="puce" height="11" src="local/cache-vignettes/L8xH11/puce-cebf5.gif" style="" width="8"/>
       <strong>
        <a class="spip_out" href="Carbohydrate-Binding-Modules.html">
         <strong>
          Carbohydrate-Binding Modules (CBMs)
         </strong>
        </a>
        :
       </strong>
       adhesion to carbohydrates
      </p>
     </div>
     <div class="paragraphe">
