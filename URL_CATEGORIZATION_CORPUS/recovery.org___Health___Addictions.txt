          <a href="topics/addiction-intervention/">
           addiction intervention
          </a>
          may be necessary. During the beginning phases of recovery, an
          <a href="topics/alcohol-and-drug-detox/">
           alcohol and drug detox
          </a>
          is always necessary to remove any unwanted chemicals from the body, followed by intensive
          <a href="topics/addiction-recovery-therapy/">
           addiction recovery therapy
          </a>
          . The latter may take 28-90 days (sometimes more), but completing the program provides all the tools needed to stay substance-free. It is highly beneficial for those leaving rehab to join a
          <a href="topics/recovery-support-groups/">
           recovery support group
          </a>
          and even consider spending time in a
          <a href="topics/recovery-homes/">
           recovery home
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          What a person learns during rehab and any
          <a href="topics/extended-recovery-programs/">
           extended recovery program
          </a>
          is how to steer clear of drugs and alcohol through
          <a href="topics/relapse-prevention/">
           relapse prevention
          </a>
          , and developing positive, healthy methods of coping to continue living a happy, drug-free life.
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2 data-plugin="toggle-slide" data-target="next" data-type="mobile">
         Recovery Programs and Support Groups
        </h2>
        <div class="toggle-area">
         <p>
          For every addiction, there are recovery programs and support groups to help those looking to kick their habits. Generally speaking, these programs can be grouped into one of two big buckets: those following a 12-step approach, and those that do not. Which you choose will be a matter of personal preference and â€“ to some â€“ whether you believe in a higher power, which 12-step programs typically promote. Either way, these resources can help you stay happy, healthy and social after your course of recovery from drug addiction or alcoholism treatment.
         </p>
