                 </a>
                </b>
               </h3>

              <div class="cms_widget_post_content widget_post_content">
               <i>
                <b>
                 Dungeon World
                </b>
                is the first foray into
                <b>
                 Powered by the Apocalypse
                </b>
                games for many gamers, since it is just a hop, skip, and a jump from something like
                <b>
                 Dungeons and Dragons
                </b>
                or
                <b>
                 Pathfinder
                </b>
                into
                <b>
                 Dungeon World
                </b>

                <b>
                 PbtA
                </b>
                system intact.

               </i>

                 </a>
                </b>
               </h3>
              </div>
              <div class="cms_widget_post_content widget_post_content">
               <i>
                <b>
                 Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes
                </b>
                is so packed with material,
                <a href="http://www.enworld.org/forum/content.php?5264-Opening-Mordenkainen-s-Tome-of-Foes" target="_blank">
                 my first review
                </a>

                <br/>
                <br/>
               </i>
              </div>

             <div class="cms_widget_post_bit widget_post_bit">

                 </a>
                </b>
               </h3>
              </div>
              <div class="cms_widget_post_content widget_post_content">
               <i>
                I've talked more than once about my love of bestiaries. They're my favorite part of any fantasy role-playing game. A well-done bestiary can do more to set the tone, and explain the setting, of a game than anything else. I've also followed along with the releases that
                <b>
                 DriveThruRPG
                </b>
                has been doing of print on demand reprints of vintage
                <b>
                 TSR
                </b>
                games and supplements. Today, these two streams cross in what I am going to call a tale of two bestiaries.
                <br/>
                <br/>
               </i>

                  Drift Into The Sea Of Leaves With The Summerland RPG
                 </a>
                </b>
               </h3>
              </div>
              <div class="cms_widget_post_content widget_post_content">
               <i>
                Overnight a vast forest sprang up ancient and full grown. It devastated the works of man.  Foreboding and somehow alive, the Sea of Leaves is a wild place permeated by the Call, a siren-song that lures the majority of humans into the woods never to return. Survivors know that tight human connections strongly binding communities together provides resistance to the Call. A small number of people, survivors of terrible trauma, are able to resist the Call to journey between settlements and enter the ruined cities. You are one of these damaged people. You are a Drifter.
