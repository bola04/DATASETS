      <i>
       Editor
      </i>

			usage, and style.  With its comprehensive databases, sophisticated analysis routines, and
     <a class="underline" href="javascript:openWindow('../pages/writer-friendly.html',850,650,0,0,0,0,1,0,300,100)" style="font-family:'Verdana','Arial','Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 12px; color: blue;" title="writer-friendly interface">

       writer-friendly interface
      </b>
     </a>
     ,
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     far outperforms other editing programs in the number of problems it identifies and the accuracy of its comments.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     does not base its suggestions on dubious statistical norms.  It does not impose discredited
     <a class="underline" href="javascript:openWindow('../pages/notes_observations.html',850,650,0,0,0,0,1,0,300,100)" style="font-family:'Verdana','Arial','Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 12px;

      <b>
       Zombie Rules
      </b>
     </a>
     or offer haphazard lists of homonyms and synonyms as
     <a class="underline" href="javascript:openWindow('../pages/notes_observations.html',850,650,0,0,0,0,1,0,300,100)" style="font-family:'Verdana','Arial','Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 12px; 
			   color: blue;" title="Zombie Rules and writing enhancements">
      <b>
       writing enhancements
      </b>
     </a>
     .

			    It is not a click-to-fix editing program; 
			  in most cases,
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     points out problems and raises questions rather than imposing answers.  Writers do the 
			  editing and remain the judges of what they want to say.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <a class="underline" href="../pages/comparisons.html" style="font-family:'Verdana','Arial','Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 12px; color: #0000FF" title="See test results">
      <b>
       An extensive comparative study
      </b>
     </a>
     shows that even the best performers among other editing programs correct fewer than 35% of the grammatical errors that a good 
			human editor would find in the test documents.
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     corrects more than 70%.
     <br/>
     (Computer programs 
			cannot find errors that depend not on words but on their meanings.)  Typically, more than 40% of other editing 
			programs comments on a text are miscorrections or mistakes.
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     is wrong less than 10% of the time.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     British and American writers benefit from
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     s extensive knowledge of both major dialects of English.  It finds 
			thousands of writing mistakes specific to each dialect as well as many tens of thousands common to both, and can help writers 
			used to one dialect who want to write in the other.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <i>
      Editor
     </i>
     is flexible: any of its categories of analysis can be turned off or on to suit a particular writers needs 
			  and habits.  And it is affordable at only $55 for the
