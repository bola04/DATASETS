     <section class="block block-views clearfix" id="block-views-faces-of-mathematics-block">
      <h2 class="block-title">
       Frames Of Mind
      </h2>
      <div class="view view-faces-of-mathematics view-id-faces_of_mathematics view-display-id-block view-dom-id-30168725343b42782f9ccda572b97d08">
       <div class="view-header">
        <p>
         <a href="library/frames-of-mind" style="text-decoration:none;">
          This is a collection of short extracts from the film “Thinking Space”, made by Heidi Morstang for The London Mathematical Society
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="view-content">
        <div class="views-row views-row-1 views-row-odd views-row-first views-row-last">
         <span class="views-field views-field-field-picture">
          <span class="field-content">
           <a href="/library/frames-of-mind">
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" height="400" src="https://www.lms.ac.uk/sites/lms.ac.uk/files/styles/faces_of_mathematics/public/KevinBuzzard_sq.png?itok=oAMBK0oy" width="400"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <div class="views-field views-field-body">
          <div class="field-content">
           The film features nine UK-based mathematicians offering insights into their mathematical thinking across a broad range of mathematical research fields.
Through explorations of their various thought processes, the film portrays mathematicians who are grappling with advanced mathematical ideas. We are presented with the concepts of imagination, intuition, and wonder, as well as rigorous mathematical deduction.
Please note that you are not necessarily expected to follow the mathematics being discussed: these extracts are about the mathematicians themselves, and the way they think.
          </div>
         </div>

          <div class="field-content">
