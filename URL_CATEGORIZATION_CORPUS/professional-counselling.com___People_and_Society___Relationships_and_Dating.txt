       <p>
        <b>
         Are you feeling miserable, but you're done with waiting for the overnight miracle?
        </b>
       </p>
       <p>
        Are you fed up hoping and wishing that your relationship or marital issue or personal problems will 'somehow' disappear?
       </p>
       <p>
        Looking for expert
        <b>
         relationship advice
        </b>
        , healthy relationship tips,
        <b>
         mental self-help
        </b>
        articles, or even counselling or therapy?
       </p>
       <p>
        I have over 24 years' experience as a (couple) counsellor. I have helped thousands of individuals and couples for whom loving or 'living' became just too difficult to tackle by themselves (see
        <a href="https://www.professional-counselling.com/testimonials-counselling-coaching-consultative-support.html">
         Testimonials
        </a>
        ).
       </p>
       <p>
        Oh ...  and I've had my fair share of trouble too!
       </p>
       <p>
        Here, on my
        <b>
         relationship help
        </b>
        site, you are my personal client and guest! I am the founder and author, and problem solver of this site. Here you'll find easily accessible and trustworthy information, as well as links to online counsellors who are ready to help you.
       </p>
       <p>
        And... you are amazing for reaching out to find a way to better yourself, and thereby your intimate couple relationship!
       </p>
       <p>
        By the way, I've ditched my (British) English teacher's advice on how to write articles. I write as I talk - as if you're sitting right across from me. :-)
        <br/>
       </p>

       <h2>
        Expert relationship and love advice with a twist
       </h2>
       <p>
        <b>
         I have well over 160 free articles
        </b>
        with
        <em>
         relationship advice
        </em>
        to help you with your relationship problems and mental health. It's my mission to help you increase your sense of happiness.
       </p>
       <p>
        Through my site I'm hoping to make a positive difference in the life of every single visitor here - that includes you.  So here's my promise to you...
        <br/>
       </p>
       <div class="CalloutBox" style="box-sizing: border-box">
        <p>
         <b>
          I will challenge you
         </b>
         to communicate effectively and compassionately for I believe that you will be able to improve your relationship or marriage.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          I will cajole you
         </b>
         to develop your problem solving skills. I know you can increase your personal emotional well-being and relationship happiness. I can’t give you a magic formula that'll make your problems disappear, but my advice will help you figure out how you can start solving your problems.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          I will empathise, soothe and support you
         </b>
         . I know only too well from my professional (and personal) experience what it's like to feel frustrated, terribly hurt, confused, angry or simply disappointed.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          I will educate you
         </b>
         , for I believe that when you know how to meet your essential emotional needs in balance...  ... you won't just survive, but you'll thrive.
         <br/>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <b>
         Whilst you're reading my articles
        </b>
        imagine the two of us are having a conversation. I will have welcomed you and, hopefully, helped you to feel at ease.
       </p>
       <p>
        I'm listening to you intently to understand what you are going through as best as I can.
       </p>
       <p>
        My articles aren't designed to be academic - I aim to provide information that's easy to understand. I'm talking with you soothingly, but I'm direct when I think that it would help.
       </p>
       <p>
        I hope you'll know, and perhaps even feel it in your gut, that I have your best interest at heart - even if you don't always want to hear what I've got to say.
       </p>
       <p>
        I would not want you to feel judged. I would not want to sound harsh. Here, on 'paper' though, I may not always be successful at conveying that. I hope you'll understand me.
