    <h1>
     Ancient Egypt Online
    </h1>
    <img alt="Ta Mery; Beautiful land" height="96" src="images/ta-mery.jpg" style="float: left;" title="Ta Mery; Beautiful land" width="219"/>
    <p>
     Welcome to Ancient Egypt Online; a site dedicated to the culture, language, religion and history of ancient Egypt. The ancient Egyptians did not call their home "Egypt" (that name is based on the Greek pronunciation of the name of Ptah's temple in Memphis - "Hwt-ka-Ptah"), instead they referred to it as Kemet (or Kem - the black land) or Ta Mery (the beautiful land).
    </p>

    <h2>
     Upper and Lower Egypt
    </h2>
    <a href="nomesupper.html">
     <img alt="Ta Shemau; Upper Egypt" height="58" src="images/upper-egypt.jpg" style="float: left;" title="Ta Shemau; Upper Egypt" width="175"/>
    </a>
    <a href="nomeslower.html">
     <img alt="Ta Mehu; Lower Egypt" height="57" src="images/lower-egypt.jpg" style="float: right;" title="Ta Mehu; Lower Egypt" width="150"/>
    </a>
    <p>
     Ancient Egypt was divided into Ta Shemau (
     <a href="nomesupper.html">
      Upper Egypt
     </a>
     ) and Ta Mehu (
     <a href="nomeslower.html">
      Lower Egypt
     </a>
     ). Click on the hieroglyphs to view a map of Upper Egypt or Lower Egypt.
    </p>
    <div style="clear: both;">
    </div>
    <p>
     The division between upper and lower Egypt was retained after the unification of the kingdom in the
     <a href="predynlist.html">
      Pre-dynastic Period
     </a>
     and the pharaoh was often known as the King of Upper and Lower Egypt. This concept of duality is a constantly reoccurring feature of the Egyptian civilisation and was echoed in the pairing of different
     <a href="thegods.html">
      gods and goddesses
     </a>
     to represent upper and lower Egypt.
    </p>

    <h2>
     History
    </h2>
    <p>
     The Egyptian Civilisation survived, in a relativley stable form, for a staggering three and a half thousand years. Find out more about the different periods of ancient Egyptian history and learn about the kings, queens and nobles who shaped this amazing culture on our
     <a href="kingslist.html">
      Ancient Egyptian Timeline
     </a>
     .
    </p>
    <h2>
     Hieroglyphs
    </h2>
    <p>
     The ancient Egyptian hieroglyphic language is both fascinating and beautiful. For many hundreds of years their meaning was lost, but thanks to the hard work of egyptologists hieroglyphs can again be read and enjoyed. Ancient Egypt Online features a full
     <a href="Gardiner-sign-list.html">
      hieroglyphic sign list
     </a>
     along with detailed tutorials to
     <a href="hieroglyphs.html">
      teach yourself hieroglyphs
     </a>
     , and fun
     <a href="hieroglyphs.html">
      quizzes
     </a>
     .
    </p>
    <h2>
     Religion
    </h2>
    <p>
     The ancient Egyptians were polytheistic (they had many gods) and their religion was both complex and fascinating. At Ancient Egypt Online you can learn more about
     <a href="thegods.html">
      Ancient Egyptian Gods and Goddesses
     </a>
     and find out more about mystical symbols such as the
     <a href="ankh.html">
      Ankh
     </a>
     and the
     <a href="eye.html">
      Eye of Horus
     </a>
     .
    </p>
    <h2>
     Pyramids, Temples and Tombs
    </h2>
    <p>
     The
     <a href="great-pyramid.html">
      Great Pyramid
     </a>
     and the
     <a href="great-sphinx.html">
      Sphinx
     </a>
     are perhaps the most famous monuments associated with Ancient Egypt. Yet, numerous other pyramids were constructed during the
     <a href="oldkingdom.html">
      Old Kingdom
     </a>
     and the
     <a href="middlekingdom.html">
      Middle Kingdom
     </a>
     and Egypt also has a wealth of beautiful temples, palaces and tombs. Find out more in our section on
     <a href="monuments.html">
      Ancient Egyptian Monuments
     </a>
     .
    </p>
