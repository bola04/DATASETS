         <blockquote>
          <font size="5">
           <br/>
           <b>
            June 11, 2018
           </b>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Once a year or so, since 2002, I've sent the following e-mail, in some form or other, to
           <a href="http://everyonewhosanyone.com/other.html" target="_blank">
            the main boys and girls
           </a>
           in media and entertainment. That means I've sent the snippy, dismissive, self-satisfied darlings half a million individual e-mails over the last sixteen years. They mostly ended up in spam folders or ignored or weren't "allowed" to be read (what a crock). They've had 500,000 chances to look at an extraordianry movie, TV series and/or work of great literature and none of them did. Pfssh:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           "
           <a href="https://vimeo.com/257514107" target="_blank">
            <i>
             Ginny Good
            </i>
           </a>
           is the best book published anywhere in the world so far this century. It's a 357 page narrative nonfiction love story that won some prizes and got some
           <a href="http://everyonewhosanyone.com/ggrev.html" target="_blank">
            good reviews
           </a>
           ...but a mere
           <i>
            book
           </i>
           you can just
           <i>
            read
           </i>
           is nothing compared to
           <i>
            The Multimedia Video Book of Ginny Good
           </i>
           . It has music and pictures and sound clips that go with the times in the book...from roughly
           <a href="https://vimeo.com/203609750" target="_blank">
            1959
           </a>
           to the beginning of
           <a href="https://vimeo.com/203891166" target="_blank">
            2004
           </a>
           . Nobody's ever done anything like it. Someday, with a few enhancements, it'll be the coolest way to
           <i>
            read
           </i>
           , period. The only way you can get it (and the
           <a href="http://everyonewhosanyone.com/audio/GGch07m.mp3" target="_blank">
            multimedia
            <i>
             audio
            </i>
            book
           </a>
           ) is to have me mail them to you on a flash drive. Send me an e-mail. Give me a mailing address. If you don't sound like a jerk I'll send you a copy of the real book, too.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           "The story takes place mainly in San Francisco in the sixties. It's around the same number of years after I wrote about Haight-Ashbury in 1967 that Tolstoy wrote about Napoleon burning Moscow in 1812 and both books,
           <i>
            War and Peace
           </i>
           and
           <i>
            Ginny Good
           </i>
           , have more to do with the people than the events. It would make an exquisite, award-winning movie and/or TV series. In fact, just as it is, the multimedia video book is better than anything you can currently see on TV or at the movies. No one has paid it much mind. That's okay. How many people can say he or she wrote the best book published anywhere in the world so far this century, turned it into the first multimedia video book ever and came up with a whole new way to read? One. Me.
           <a href="http://grumpyoldbookman.blogspot.com/2005/06/gerard-jones-ginny-good.html" target="_blank">
            Heh
           </a>
           . Long after most every other book or movie or TV series made so far this century is forgotten, the thoughtful beauty of
           <i>
            The Multimedia Video Book of Ginny Good
           </i>
           will be remembered and revered. You can read it (or watch the
           <i>
            voice only
           </i>
           version) on
           <a href="https://medium.com/@gerardjones" target="_blank">
            Medium
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://vimeo.com/user41893142" target="_blank">
            Vimeo
           </a>
           or
           <a href="http://everyonewhosanyone.com/ggcha.html" target="_blank">
            my site
           </a>
           . Thanks. G."
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I might as well have been talking to people in
           <a href="https://vimeo.com/203338456" target="_blank">
            the Andromeda Galaxy
           </a>
           . Who wouldn't want to know about the best book published anywhere in the world so far this century...
           <i>
            and
           </i>
           the only multimedia video book ever made...
           <i>
            and
           </i>
           a whole new way to read? Not the boys and girls in media and entertainment, that's for sure. Oh, well. I'll keep plugging away. The top media and entertainment conglomerates could put all their best nerds in a big room, spend a billion dollars and not come up with a better work of literary or cinematic art than
           <i>
            The Multimedia Video Book of Ginny Good
           </i>
           . Get it and watch it and see. It's free. Wait. What good could anything free be? Which would you rather do, make a magnificent work of literary art, give it away for free, and have nobody pay any attention to it...or make some silly, comic book glop that earns lots of money and everybody goes gaga over? Ask the boys and girls in media and entertainment. They choose making money...and keeping people stupid...at all costs. That's their job. That's what they're paid to do. Dragons make money. Dragons keep people stupid. Authenticity doesn't. It's just worth reading...and writing...and making into movies and TV shows. Ask Kafka. Ask Van Gogh. Ask Tolstoy. Ask
           <a href="https://vimeo.com/202134933" target="_blank">
            Truffaut
           </a>
           . G.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Gerard Jones

            everyone@everyonewhosanyone.com
           </a>
           <br/>
          </font>
         </blockquote>
