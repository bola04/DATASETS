                <h3 class="ContentHeader">
                 To Whet Your Appetite for DUMPLING SOUP
                </h3>
                <br/>
                <div class="sb_content_image_center">
                 <div align="center" class="sb_captioned_image {SMALLIMAGECLASS}" style="max-width: {MAXWIDTH}">
                  <img alt="" height="293" src="./images/ds_spread_2-330-exp.jpg" width="330"/>
                  <div class="sb_caption" style="width:330px;">
                   Copyright Š 1993 Lillian Hsu-Flanders. All rights reserved.
                  </div>
                 </div>
                 <div class="clearer">
                 </div>
                </div>
                <br/>
                <div class="ContentParagraph">
                 "This picture book set in Hawaii tells of seven-year-old Marisa's attempt to make dumplings for her extended family's New Year's Eve party . . . A lively celebration of setting, tradition, and the rich diversity of a loving family." -- ALA Booklist
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 "A thoroughly enjoyable celebration of family warmth and diverse traditions, illustrated with cheery watercolors." -- Ellen Fader, Horn Book Magazine
                </div>
                <br/>
                <div class="sb_content_image_center">
                 <div align="center" class="sb_captioned_image {SMALLIMAGECLASS}" style="max-width: {MAXWIDTH}">
                  <img alt="" height="248" src="./images/IMG_3496-330-exp.jpg" width="330"/>
                  <div class="sb_caption" style="width:330px;">
                   SLURRRRRPP!
                  </div>
                 </div>
                 <div class="clearer">
                 </div>
                </div>
                <br/>
                <div class="ContentParagraph">
                 YANG FAMILY DUMPLING SOUP RECIPE
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 For the dumplings:
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 2 pounds lean ground beef
                 <br/>
                 1 pound ground pork
                 <br/>
                 4 packages bean sprouts
                 <br/>
                 2 medium-sized won bok cabbages
                 <br/>
                 1 pint kimchi (more if desired)
                 <br/>
                 2 blocks tofu
                 <br/>
                 3 stalks green onions, chopped
                 <br/>
                 3 eggs
                 <br/>
                 1 l/​2 T sesame seed oil
                 <br/>
                 3 T vegetable oil
                 <br/>
                 1 tsp black pepper
                 <br/>
                 3 cloves garlic, minced
                 <br/>
                 1 T grated ginger
                 <br/>
                 3/​4 c soy sauce
                 <br/>
                 1 T sugar
                 <br/>
                 4 packages wonton wrappers
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 For the soup:
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 2-4 quarts chicken broth
                 <br/>
                 2 pounds white chicken breast meat, cooked and chopped into 1-inch pieces
                 <br/>
                 green onions as desired for garnish
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 1.  Saute ground beef and pork, lightly seasoning it   with salt and pepper.  Drain off excess oil.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 2.  Parboil bean sprouts, then chop them coarsely    and squeeze out excess water.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 3.  Parboil won bok cabbages, then chop them finely    and squeeze out excess water.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 4.  Drain kimchi, chop it finely, and squeeze out    liquid.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 5.  Squeeze out excess water from tofu.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 6.  In a large bowl, combine all dumpling ingredients  except wonton wrappers, and mix thoroughly.  Let    stand in refrigerator for 1-2 hours.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 7.  Wrap about 1 l/​2 tablespoons filling in wonton    wrapper.  With fingertips, lightly wet edges with    water.  Firmly pinch edges.  Repeat till all filling has been used.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 8.  Place dumplings in slightly salted boiling water,  and cook gently for about 10 minutes.  Drain,then run cold water over cooked dumplings to avoid sticking.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 9.  In a large soup pot, add cooked chicken meat to    broth, and heat.  Add dumplings.  Garnish with  chopped green onions.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 Yield:  about 100 dumplings
                </div>
