       <p>
        Huge, ground-breaking decision from the Supreme Court today! In a 7 to 2 ruling, the Court said
        <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/courts_law/supreme-court-rules-that-warrant-is-needed-to-access-cell-tower-records/2018/06/22/4f85a804-761e-11e8-805c-4b67019fcfe4_story.html?noredirect=on&amp;utm_term=.d01d4a01e1ce">
         a search warrant is needed
        </a>
        for cops to obtain cell site location information from phone companies.
       </p>
       <p>
        The opinion is
        <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/courts_law/supreme-court-rules-that-warrant-is-needed-to-access-cell-tower-records/2018/06/22/4f85a804-761e-11e8-805c-4b67019fcfe4_story.html?noredirect=on&amp;utm_term=.d01d4a01e1ce">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         The case before us involves the Government’s acquisition of wireless carrier cell-site records revealing the location of Carpenter’s cell phone whenever it made orreceived calls. This sort of digital data—personal location information maintained by a third party—does not fit neatly under existing precedents.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
