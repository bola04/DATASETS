      <h2>
       Registration for DCMI 2018 now open
      </h2>
     </a>
     <p>
      We are pleased to announce that registration is now open for the annual Dublinc Core conference to be held in Porto, Portugal, 10-13th September 2018.
     </p>
     <p>
      This year's conference is co-located with TPDL 2018. By registering once for the DCMI conference you will automatically have free access to the TPDL conference programme as well!
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/conference/2018/">
       Click here for more details about DCMI 2018, and how to register for the conference
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      We look forward to seeing you in Porto!
     </p>

     <a class="post-title" href="http://www.dublincore.org/news/2018/2018_05_23-doctoral-consortium/">
      <h2>
       JTDC 2018 - A Doctoral Consortium
      </h2>
     </a>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Update:
      </strong>
      The deadlines for submissions have been revised, so there is still time to submit!
     </p>
     <p>
      New dates:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        July 01, 2018 – Submissions deadline (submissions by email to
        <a href="mailto:jlb@tecnico.ulisboa.pt">
         jlb@tecnico.ulisboa.pt
        </a>
        )
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li>
       July 08, 2018 – Notifications of acceptance
      </li>
      <li>
       September, 10, 2018 – JTDC 2018 workshop session
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      In addition to the main
      <a href="http://www.dublincore.org/conference/2018/">
       DCMI conference (co-located with TPDL) in Porto in September
      </a>
      , DCMI is also collaborating with TPDL to host
      <em>
       JTDC 2018
      </em>
      , described as a "Doctoral Consortium".
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://www.tpdl.eu/tpdl2018/doctoral-consortium/">
       From the TPDL website, which explains this in detail
      </a>
      :
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       The purpose of JTDC 2018 is to provide to doctoral students a friendly and lively atmosphere for sharing research ideas and experience with peers, and receive constructive feedback from senior researchers and scholars. JTDC 2018 follows an important TPDL tradition that aims at giving next generation researchers a chance to present their work to the digital libraries community, this year also extended to the DCMI community.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
