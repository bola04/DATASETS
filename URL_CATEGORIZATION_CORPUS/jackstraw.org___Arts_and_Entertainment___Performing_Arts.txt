    <p class="text">
     <em>
      to drown
     </em>
     is a new media installation that explores the in between states of being. Using the idea of sinking underwater as a metaphor, the aching slowness of light and sound that quietly unfolds and shifts produces a place that is at once both comfortable and unsettling. Inviting the viewer as a disturbance to the space,
     <em>
      to drown
     </em>
     acknowledges the disquiet that comes with having remained in a place for too long.
    </p>

       <p>
        <span class="text1">
         <span class="headline">
          Seattle on the Spot: The Photographs of Al Smith
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="text">
         <br/>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p class="text">
        <strong>
         Through June 15, 2018
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p class="text">
        <span class="text1">
         Jack Straw Cultural Center
         <br/>
         4261 Roosevelt Way NE
         <br/>
         Seattle
        98105
         <br/>
         FREE
        </span>
       </p>
       <p class="text">
        Legendary photographer Al Smith was an explorer and his camera was the  universal key that opened doors and gave him license to go anywhere.
        <em>
         <a alt="http://mohai.org/exhibit/seattle-on-the-spot/" href="http://mohai.org/exhibit/seattle-on-the-spot/" linktype="1" shape="rect" target="_blank" track="on">
          Seattle on the Spot: The  Photographs of Al Smith
         </a>
        </em>
        , organized by Seattle's Museum of  History &amp; Industry (MOHAI), gives visitors a rare peek into Smith's  historically significant collection that chronicles the African-American  community during the mid-20th century in the Pacific Northwest. The exhibit  also provides an entry point into national discussions about the history and  changing character of Seattle and its evolving neighborhoods.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Jack Straw is pleased to host this satellite exhibition of Al Smith's photos  that are not part of the museum exhibition.
       </p>
