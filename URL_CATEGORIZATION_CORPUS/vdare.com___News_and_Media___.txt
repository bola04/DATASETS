         <h2 class="publication-title">
          Said In Spanish: Univision’s Seven “Infallible” Arguments For Speaking Spanish In The United States Are Not Infallible
         </h2>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="col-xl-24 my-2 by-author">
        By
        <a class="custom-orange" href="/writers/allan-wall">
         Allan Wall
        </a>
        on 2018-06-22 19:35:00 -0400
       </div>
       <div class="publication-content col-xl-24 content my-2">
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #993300;">
           <img src="https://vdare.com/public_upload/publication/featured_image/45584/infallible.png" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="888"/>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="color: #993300;">
           See also:
          </span>
          <a href="https://www.vdare.com/articles/in-quebec-aaron-schlossberg-would-be-a-hero-and-the-restaurant-fined-because-quebec-wants-to-survive-as-a-nation">
           In Quebec, Aaron Schlossberg Would Be a Hero And The Restaurant Fined—Because Quebec Wants To Survive As A Nation
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         When Americans are caught on camera telling Spanish-speakers to speak English or go back to Mexico, the Main Stream Media have a field day. Not least among those media: the Spanish-language Univision network. Just recall the
         <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moral_panic">
          Moral Panic
         </a>
         after lawyer
         <a href="https://www.vdare.com/articles/in-quebec-aaron-schlossberg-would-be-a-hero-and-the-restaurant-fined-because-quebec-wants-to-survive-as-a-nation">
          Aaron Schlossberg
         </a>
         told two employees at a restaurant New York City to speak English
         <em>
          to customers.
         </em>
         And Schlossberg isn’t the only American fed up with hearing Spanish, as
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=man+woman+yells+at+stranger+for+speaking+spanish">
          this compilation at YouTube
         </a>
         shows. (Significantly, women seem particularly perturbed.)
        </p>
        <p>
         These videos are made to order for
         <a href="https://www.vdare.com/posts/why-doesnt-univision-put-english-subtitles-on-american-movies">
          Univision
         </a>
         , which presents them as examples of “hate.” The Treason Lobby network recently offered seven “infallible” arguments with which a
         <a href="https://www.vdare.com/posts/univision-run-by-white-men-who-cant-speak-spanish">
          Spanish-speaker
         </a>
         can defend himself from such “attacks.” The arguments are really comments you hear all the time, and they really aren’t infallible. But given that they bear upon the
         <a href="https://www.vdare.com/posts/vdare-com-on-charles-krauthammer">
          National Question,
         </a>
         which isn’t going away any time soon, let’s take a quick look at them [
         <em>
          <a href="https://www.univision.com/noticias/incidentes-de-odio/7-argumentos-infalibles-que-puedes-usar-si-alguien-te-agrede-por-hablar-espanol-en-eeuu">
           7 Argumentos Infalibles Que Puedes Usar Si Alguien Te Agrede Por Hablar Español En EEUU
          </a>
         </em>
         (
         <em>
          Seven Infallible Arguments You can Use if Someone Attacks You For Speaking Spanish In The United States)
         </em>
         , by Angelica Gallon S.,
         <em>
          Univision
         </em>
         , May 25, 2018].
        </p>
        <p>
         Readers get each point in English and Spanish, then, curiously, more detail in Spanish. Maybe the
         <em>
          muchachos
         </em>
         at Univision didn’t want the
         <em>
          gringos
         </em>
         to know too much.
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          <span style="color: #993300;">
           <strong>
            <em>
             “I have a civil right to speak in any language I please in this country. There is no law that prohibits me from speaking Spanish.”
            </em>
           </strong>
          </span>
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         This one contains several sub-arguments provided by a civil rights lawyer.
        </p>
