                  <p>
                   <b>
                    <i>
                     When a mysterious figure appears in Three 
                                Pines one cold November day, Armand Gamache and 
                                the rest of the villagers are at first curious. 
                                Then wary. And finally, watching the unmoving 
                                figure, a pall settles over the pretty Québec 
                                village.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Armand Gamache, now Chief Superintendent of the 
                                Sûreté du Québec, knows something 
                                is seriously wrong. Through rain and sleet, the 
                                figure stands unmoving, staring ahead. An accusation 
                                on the village green. Gamache knows there must 
                                be a purpose behind this odd act.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Yet Gamache does nothing. What can he do? Only 
                                watch and wait. And hope his mounting fears are 
                                not realized.

                                it falls to Gamache to discover if a debt has 
                                been discharged, or levied.

                                for the accused begins in Montreal, Chief Superintendent 
                                Gamache continues to struggle with actions he 
                                set in motion that bitter November, from which 
                                there is no going back. More than the accused 
                                is on trial. Gamache's own conscience is standing 
                                in judgement.

                 <br/>
                 <img alt="" height="3" src="graphics/split_line.gif" width="500"/>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 I'm often asked two questions: should the books be read 
                        in order? And, what is the order?
                </font>
               </font>
               <p>
                <font color="#000000" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 Both 
                          excellent questions. At the risk of appearing to want 
                          you to buy more books let me say that while it's not 
                          necessary to read them in any particular order (they're 
                          designed to be self-standing) there is quite a strong 
                          character development arc. I think you'd enjoy the books 
                          even more if read in order.
                </font>
               </p>
               <p>
                <font color="#000000" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 Now, 
                          here's the order, from the first to the most recent:
                </font>
                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  <font color="#CC6600">
                   STILL LIFE
                   <br/>
                   A FATAL GRACE / DEAD COLD
                   <br/>
                   THE CRUELEST MONTH
                   <br/>
                   A RULE AGAINST MURDER / THE MURDER STONE
                   <br/>
                   THE BRUTAL TELLING
                   <br/>
                   BURY YOUR DEAD
                   <br/>
                   A TRICK OF THE LIGHT
                  </font>
                 </b>
                 <br/>
                 <font color="#CC6600">
                  <b>
                   THE BEAUTIFUL MYSTERY
                   <br/>
                   HOW THE LIGHT GETS IN
                   <br/>
                   <span class="boldbooktitles">
                    THE LONG WAY HOME
                    <br/>
                    THE NATURE OF THE BEAST
                   </span>
                  </b>
                 </font>
                </font>
                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  <font color="#CC6600">
                   A GREAT RECKONING
                  </font>
                 </b>
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  <font color="#CC6600">
                   GLASS HOUSES
                  </font>
                 </b>
                 <br/>
                 <font color="#000000">
                  <br/>
                  Some of the books have different titles, as you might 
                          have noticed. The publishers did this not, as you might 
                          suspect, to be annoying but because they genuinely feel 
                          their readers respond to different titles. I hope it's 
                          not too confusing.
                  <br/>
                 </font>
                </font>
                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 <br/>
                </font>
                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 <img height="3" src="graphics/split_line.gif" width="500"/>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <font color="#000000">
                  And finally, a small note about 
                          the themes in my books. They're inspired by two lines 
                          from a poem by WH Auden, in his elegy to Melville.
                  <i>
                   Goodness 
                          existed, that was the new knowledge/his terror had to 
                          blow itself quite out to let him see it.
                  </i>
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  How powerful is that?
                 </font>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <img height="3" src="graphics/split_line.gif" width="500"/>
                </font>

                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">

                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 My books are about terror. That brooding terror curled 
                          deep down inside us. But more than that, more than murder, 
                          more than all the rancid emotions and actions, my books 
                          are about goodness. And kindness. About choices. About 
                          friendship and belonging. And love. Enduring love.
                </font>
               </p>
               <p>
                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 If 
                          you take only one thing away from any of my books I'd 
                          like it to be this:
                </font>
               </p>
               <p>
                <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
                 Goodness 
                          exists.
