      <span id="articletitle">
       FIBER: Industrial Commodity Prices Ease
      </span>
      <br/>
      <span id="byline">
       by
       <a href="/comment/Moeller.html">
        Tom Moeller
       </a>
       June 22, 2018
      </span>
      <span id="articletext">
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/comment/180622a.png" name="modal">
         <img class="small" src="/comment/180622a.png"/>
        </a>
        <a href="/comment/180622b.png" name="modal">
         <img class="small" src="/comment/180622b.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        The Industrial Materials Price Index from the Foundation for
International Business and Economic Research (FIBER) slipped 0.5% during the
last four weeks, but increased 12.7% during the last year. Industrial materials
prices overall have risen by roughly one-third versus the early-2016 low. A 1.7%
y/y increase in industrial sector output prompted the gain, following a year of
similar growth during 2017.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        Prices in the
        <b>
         miscellaneous
        </b>
        group exhibited the most
strength with a 0.4% gain during the last four weeks and a 13.4% rise during the
past year. Framing lumber prices improved 0.5% last month, up 43.8% y/y. Prices
for structural panels improved 2.9% during the last four weeks and by 40.8%
during the last year. Lower prices for natural rubber offset some of this
strength with a 10.3% decline last month, down 6.6% y/y. In the
        <b>
         metals
        </b>
        sector, prices slipped 0.3% during the last month, but improved 17.7% y/y.
Copper scrap costs strengthened 1.0% m/m and 21.7% y/y, but aluminum prices were
off 3.1% m/m (+17.5% y/y). The cost of steel scrap eased 0.5% m/m, but rose by
more than one-quarter y/y. The cost of lead eased 0.5% over the last four weeks
(+13.3% y/y) and zinc prices rose 0.9% last month (19.6% y/y). In the
        <b>
         textile
        </b>
        group, prices overall eased 0.2% last month (+3.1%y/y). Cotton prices improved
0.5% over the last month (23.9% y/y), but burlap prices fell 2.9% m/m and were
down a like amount during the past year. In the
        <b>
         crude oil &amp; benzene
        </b>
        sector, prices declined 3.2% last month, but rose 19.3% y/y. Crude oil prices
declined 8.6% m/m to an average $65.61 per barrel, but were up by one-half y/y.
Benzene prices rose 0.9% in the last four weeks and 12.6% y/y.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/comment/180622c.png" name="modal">
         <img class="small" src="/comment/180622c.png"/>
        </a>
        <a href="/comment/180622d.png" name="modal">
         <img class="small" src="/comment/180622d.png"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        Further strength in prices overall may be coming. The
consensus forecast for industrial output from the National Association for
Business Economics (NABE) calls for a 3.8% rise in 2018 and 2.8% growth in 2019.
During the last ten years, there has been 50% correlation between the
three-month change in prices and the change in U.S. industrial output.
In addition, factory sector production in the European Union and Japan
continues to improve.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        Commodity price data can be found in Haver's
        <b>
         DAILY,
WEEKLY, USECON
        </b>
        and
        <b>
         CMDTY
        </b>
        databases. The NABE forecast is in the
        <b>
         SURVEYS
        </b>
        database.
       </p>
