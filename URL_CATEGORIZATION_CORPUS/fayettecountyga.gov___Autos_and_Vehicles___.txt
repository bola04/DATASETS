        <h5 align="center">
         Fayette County Fire &amp; Emergency Services announces 2018 Fall Citizens Fire Academy
        </h5>
        <p>
         The Citizens' Fire Academy is a 10-week program designed to educate and inform citizens about the demanding field of fire, emergency services and emergency management. Students will achieve a greater understanding of the inner workings of the department. The academy will be ten weeks in length, one night per week, on
         <strong>
          Thursday's from 6:30 P.M. until 9:00 P.M.
         </strong>
         The instruction includes practical demonstrations that all participants are encouraged to participate in. However, because of the voluntary nature of the program, physical limitations of applicants will not be considered a reason for exclusion from the academy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The next class starts on August 16, 2018.
         </strong>
         Interested participants who wish to sign up for the Citizens Fire Academy should email
         <a href="mailto:tlackey@fayettecountyga.gov">
          tlackey@fayettecountyga.gov
         </a>
         for an application. There are limited seats available and applicants should reside in unincorporated Fayette County or the Towns of Tyrone, Brooks, or Woolsey.
        </p>
        <p>
         "This is a great opportunity to see and feel the rigors of a job in Fire &amp; Emergency Services. The men and women of the department go to great lengths every single day to make Fayette County a safe place to live and visit. Come see what it takes to work in this demanding field." â€“ Fire Chief David Scarbrough
        </p>
