     <h2 align="center">
      New Resources
     </h2>
     <p align="center">
     </p>
     <div align="center">
      <img alt="The Wordsmith Prompts" src="imgs/banner_prompts.jpg"/>
     </div>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
      We have selected a variety of literature texts commonly studied in Grade 9 upwards, and produced for them a series of study tasks based on actual classroom work as well as on past IB, AP and A level assessments.
     </p>
     <p style="text-align: left;">
     </p>
     <p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px 0px 10px; color: #405f5d; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12.727272033691406px;">
      <span style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; color: #ff9900; font-size: small;">
       <em>
        'These prompts are a terrific tool for generating class discussions, creating short answer exams, or longer essay assignments. I have purchased a number of them and am impressed with each one.'
       </em>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px 0px 10px; color: #405f5d; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12.727272033691406px;">
      <span style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; color: #ff9900; font-size: small;">
       <em>
        From our
        <a href="../commendations.php" target="_blank">
         Commendations
        </a>
        section
       </em>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <em>
       Prompts
      </em>
      are not book-length study commentaries like
      <em>
       The WordSmith Guides
      </em>
      . Rather, they are outline resources, consisting of a range of questions and assignments designed to take students into the heart of each chapter of a novel (or each scene of a play, or each poem in a collection) as they have finished reading it. Together they help build up an overview of each of the five SCASI aspects of the whole piece of writing (Setting, Character, Action, Style and Ideas - the fundamental structure of
      <em>
       The WordSmith Guides
      </em>
      themselves).
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
