     <div class="intro">
      <p>
       Welcome to the
       <b>
        Generic Model Organism Database
       </b>
       project, a collection of open source software tools for managing, visualising, storing, and disseminating genetic and genomic data.
      </p>
     </div>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Get_Started">
       Get Started
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Read the
      <a href="/wiki/Overview" title="Overview">
       GMOD overview
      </a>
      for the big picture, or visit
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Components" title="GMOD Components">
       GMOD Components
      </a>
      for a comprehensive list of GMOD tools.  If GMOD looks promising for your needs, consider attending the next
      <a href="/wiki/Meetings" title="Meetings">
       GMOD community meeting
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Get_Support">
       Get Support
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      GMOD support is available from several different sources.
      <a href="/wiki/Support" title="Support">
       Support
      </a>
      introduces each support option (this web site,
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Mailing_Lists" title="GMOD Mailing Lists">
       GMOD Mailing Lists
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Training_and_Outreach" title="Training and Outreach">
       Training and Outreach
      </a>
      activities (including
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Schools" title="GMOD Schools">
       GMOD Schools
      </a>
      ), and the
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Help_Desk" title="GMOD Help Desk">
       GMOD Help Desk
      </a>
      ) and offers guidance on which one is the most appropriate for your question.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Get_Involved">
       Get Involved
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      As an open source project GMOD relies on the
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Membership" title="GMOD Membership">
       donation of time and software
      </a>
      by groups and individuals.  Contribution of new tools, adoption of existing ones, and
      <a href="/wiki/Site_Guidelines" title="Site Guidelines">
       improving the documentation
      </a>
      are all welcome.
      <a href="/wiki/MOD" title="MOD">
       Existing
      </a>
      and potential users are encouraged to provide feedback via
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Mailing_Lists" title="GMOD Mailing Lists">
       mailing lists
      </a>
      or the
      <a href="/wiki/GMOD_Help_Desk" title="GMOD Help Desk">
       help desk
      </a>
      .  You can also attend project
      <a href="/wiki/Meetings" title="Meetings">
       meetings
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>

     </p>
