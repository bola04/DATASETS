            Search Tips
           </span>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <span class="style8 style4">
            (These notes apply to the top search box for access from anywhere on the site)
            <br/>
            <br/>
           </span>

         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          <span class="style4">
           <font color="#FFFFFF">
            <font color="#000000">
             1. 
      Jump directly to a  chapter
            </font>
           </font>
           or verse just by entering it into the box. Try any abbreviation, down to even a single letter... for example try
           <span class="style9">
            J3
           </span>
           or
           <span class="style9">
            H11
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="style4">
           or
           <span class="style9">
            R7 niv
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="style4">
          </span>
          <span class="style4">
           or
           <span class="style9">
            Mt9 KJV
           </span>
          </span>
          <span class="style4">
           .
          </span>
          <span class="style4">
           <span class="style9">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          <font color="#FFFFFF">
           <font color="#000000">
            2. 
      Combine key words, books and chapters as desired ... for example
           </font>
          </font>
          try
          <span class="style9">
           Acts 27 ship
          </span>
          .
         </p>
         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          <font color="#000000">
           3. 
        Use quotation marks to search a specific phrase.
          </font>
          <span class="style9">
           "In the beginning God"
          </span>
          <font color="#000000">
           returns only exact matches in that word order.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          <font color="#000000">
           4. 
        Use a minus sign to skip entries with a particular word.
          </font>
          Try
          <span class="style9">
           Paul an apostle -epistles
          </span>
         </p>
         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          <font color="#000000">
           5. 
        Use
          </font>
          <span class="style9">
           NT
          </span>
          <font color="#000000">
           or
          </font>
          <span class="style9">
           OT
          </span>
          <font color="#000000">
           for a particular testament.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          <font color="#000000">
           6. 
        Combine books and or chapters with key words. Example:
          </font>
          <span class="style9">
           eph 3 faith
          </span>
          .
         </p>
         <p align="justify" class=" style4">
          7. Narrow your search by including any of these sections:
          <span class="style9">
           Law, History, Prophets, Poetry, Gospels, Acts, Epistles, Revelation.
          </span>
         </p>
