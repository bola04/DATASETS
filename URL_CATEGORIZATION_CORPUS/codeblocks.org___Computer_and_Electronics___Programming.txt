              <td class="contentheading" width="100%">
               The open source, cross platform, free C, C++ and Fortran IDE.
              </td>
             </tr>
            </table>
            <table class="contentpaneopen">
             <tr>
              <td colspan="2" valign="top">
               <p align="left" style="clear: right">
                <a href="/screenshots" title="More screenshots">
                 <img align="right" alt="Application screenshot" border="0" height="257" hspace="5" src="/images/gallery/scr1_tiny.jpg" width="312"/>
                </a>
                Code::Blocks is a
                <em>
                 free C, C++ and Fortran IDE
                </em>
                built to meet the most demanding needs of its users. It is designed to be very extensible and fully configurable.
               </p>
               <p align="left">
                Finally, an IDE with all the features
                <em>
                 you
                </em>
                need, having a consistent look, feel and operation across platforms.
               </p>
               <p align="left">
                Built around a plugin framework, Code::Blocks can be
                <em>
                 extended with plugins
                </em>
                . Any kind of functionality can be added by installing/coding a plugin. For instance, compiling and debugging functionality is already provided by plugins!
               </p>
               <p align="left">
                Special credits go to darmar for his great work on the
                <strong>
                 <a href="https://sourceforge.net/projects/fortranproject/" target="_blank" title="FortranProject plugin webpage">
                  FortranProject
                 </a>
                </strong>
                plugin, bundled since release 13.12.
               </p>
               <p align="left">
                We hope you enjoy using Code::Blocks!
               </p>
               <p>
                <em>
                 The Code::Blocks Team
                </em>
               </p>
              </td>
