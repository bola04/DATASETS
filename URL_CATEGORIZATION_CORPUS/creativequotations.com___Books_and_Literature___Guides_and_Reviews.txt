     <h2 style="text-align: center; line-height: normal">
      <font size="4">
       <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight: 700; text-decoration: underline">
        A Brief History of 
	Creative Quotationsons
       </span>
      </font>
     </h2>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        I’m Frank, the creator and webmaster for 				
	CreativeQuotations.com. I’d like to briefly explain how this 				website, and all the websites in the bemorecreative.com family, 				evolved from my personal interest in quotations.
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        My wife and I began our married life working for 				seven years as volunteers in Zaire (now DR Congo) with Mennonite 				Central Committee – she as a nurse and I, first as a teacher, 				and later as a public health manager. By 1981 I had completed a 				Masters in Tropical Diseases at Johns Hopkins and a Doctorate in 				International Health at Tulane Universities. I was then hired by 				USAID to manage a large health-systems development project based 				in Kinshasa. The SANRU Basic Rural Health project which I helped 				to manage developed 100 decentralized health zones to provide 				primary health care for more than 10,000,000 people. That is, 				however, another story told better at http://SANRU.org.
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        In 1986 I wanted to make a calendar Christmas 				gift for my wife using
        <i>
         Print Shop
        </i>
        and my trusty Apple 				//GS computer. I decided to make a weekly calendar with a 				motherly/homey quotation at the top of each page. Browsing 				through a copy of
        <i>
         The Shorter Bartlett's Familiar Quotations,
        </i>
        I found the quotes that I was looking for, but, in the process, 				also became hooked on quotations about creativity. I began 				collecting quotations relating to creativity and soon had enough 				to make a page-a-day calendar featuring creative-related 				quotations.
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        Within the following months I amassed several 				thousand quotations, and began reading more about the creative 				process. I was intrigued by books like “
        <i>
         A Whack on the Side 				of The Head: How to Unlock Your Mind for Innovation
        </i>
        ” by 				Roger Von Oech. However, it was in reading his sequel, “
        <i>
         A 				Kick in the Seat of the Pants
        </i>
        ,” that I made a creative 				connection that my collection of quotations could be used to 				illustrate components of the creativity process. After more 				research, including “
        <i>
         Pathfinders”
        </i>
        by Gail Sheehy I ended 				up adopting a five-step creative process, called Creative 				Quotations, --
       </font>
      </span>
      <b>
       <u>
        <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
         <font size="4">
          F
         </font>
        </span>
       </u>
      </b>
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        oraging,
        <b>
         <u>
          R
         </u>
        </b>
        eflecting,
        <b>
         <u>
          A
         </u>
        </b>
        dopting,
        <b>
         <u>
          N
         </u>
        </b>
        urturing, 				and
        <b>
         <u>
          K
         </u>
        </b>
        nuckling Down. Those five terms, while 				admittedly a bit out of the ordinary, spell out the word 				“FRANK,” and become my way of personalizing the creative 				process.
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center; line-height: normal">
      <a href="http://bemorecreative.com/gif/F.gif">
       <img border="0" src="http://bemorecreative.com/gif/F.gif"/>
      </a>
      <img border="0" src="http://bemorecreative.com/gif/R.gif"/>

      <img border="0" src="http://bemorecreative.com/gif/K.gif"/>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:
justify;line-height:normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        I credit my ten years of "survival" as a project 				manager in Africa in part to this personalization of the 				creative process, and its application to creative project 				management. I also found that I could logically link almost any 				of my collected quotations with one of those five components. I 				also found that given a dozen or so quotations from a famous 				person that I could usually create a meaningful set of Creative 				Quotations by that person which illustrated, directly or 				indirectly, their thoughts on the creative process – from 				“Foraging” to “Knuckling Down,” e.g.,
        <i>
         <a href="http://creativequotations.com/one/47.htm">
          Creative 				Quotations from Abraham Lincoln
         </a>
        </i>
        .
       </font>
      </span>

     <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        In 1991 our family was evacuated from Zaire due 				to political and economic instability. At that time I began 				working as a freelance consultant in International Health. I 				also began looking for opportunities to share Creative 				Quotations to a larger audience that might appreciate insights 				from famous creative people -- “
        <i>
         The wisdom of the wise and 				the experience of the ages may be preserved in quotations.
        </i>
        (Isaac D'Israeli).
        <a href="http://creativequotations.com">
         CreativeQuotations.com
        </a>
        came to be as a result of that 				effort, and is intended to challenge people readers to think 				creatively by looking for connections between quotations and the 				creative process -- “
        <i>
         Creativity is the power to connect the 				seemingly unconnected
        </i>
        (William Plomer).
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:
justify;line-height:normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        The Internet has provided me with the medium to 				not only share my quotation collection as “
        <a href="http://creativequotations.com">
         CreativeQuotations.com
        </a>
        ”, 				but also to expand to a broader platform of 				“BeMoreCreative.com.” That eventually led to numerous other 				Creative Quotational websites. I’ve thematically combined 				quotations on a selected topic with online links to products 				related to that topic. The
        <a href="http://creativehardwarestore.com">
         CreativeHardwareStore.com
        </a>
        , for example, was inspired by the 				quotation that
        <i>
         “Anyone can look for history in a museum. The 				creative explorer looks for history in a hardware store”
        </i>
        --Robert Wieder.
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
     <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:
justify;line-height:normal">
      <span style="font-family: 'Times New Roman',serif; color: black">
       <font size="4">
        I invite you to browse 
	CreativeQuotations.com (or any BeMoreCreative.com websites) while “
        <i>
         thinking, working and 				living more creatively.
        </i>
        ”  And keep on the lookout for 				connections along the way that may inspire your personal 				creativity!
       </font>
      </span>
     </p>
