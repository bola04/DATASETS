   <h3 align="center">
    Help
   </h3>
   <p>
    For many years the Locomotive &amp; Railway Historical Society of Western Canada has been holding for ultimate disposition, when appropriate, two ex-Canadian Pacific Canadian Locomotive Company-built Fairbanks- Morse diesel locomotives;
specifically, Model CPB 16-4 ex-BC Rail Nos. RCC 3 &amp; 4, nee CPR Nos. 4455 &amp; 4456.
   </p>
   <p>
    I have been in contact with both Fairbanks-Morse Engines (successor to Fairbanks-Morse Locomotives) and Queens University Archives (Canadian Locomotive Company) seeking a source of F-M erecting drawings, specifically C-Line B unit side
elevations of the lower side plating (including peripherals; e.g., door hardware details) and upper-grille drawings to enable replication thereof.  Neither contact has been aware of a source of the  drawings availability if, indeed, they
were even preserved. - Jim Lanigan
   </p>
   <p>
    If anybody can help, let me know and I will pass to Jim.
   </p>

   <h3 align="center">
    A Note to persons searching for Employee Records
   </h3>
   <p>
    I do not nor do most railroad historical societies have any ex-railroad
personnel records in their archives. Those records are most likely lost. It
is recommended that you contact the
    <i>
     U.S. Railroad Retirement Board
    </i>
    ,
which you can contact at 844 North Rush, Chicago, IL 60611 (312) 751-4500.
They also have a Web page at
    <a href="http://www.rrb.gov/geneal.html">
     The
U.S. Railroad Retirement Board
    </a>
    .
   </p>
