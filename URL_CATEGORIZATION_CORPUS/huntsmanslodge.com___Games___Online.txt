       <header class="entry-header">
        <h2 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         <a class="entry-title-link" href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/40307/legion-really-quite-good/" rel="bookmark">
          Legion is Really Quite Good
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2016-09-07T15:48:30+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          September 7, 2016
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/author/craig/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Garwulf
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/40307/legion-really-quite-good/#comments">
           12 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="fooboxshare_post_id" type="hidden" value="40307"/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide.jpg">
          <img alt="hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40310" height="675" sizes="(max-width: 1200px) 100vw, 1200px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide.jpg 1200w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/hunter_with_grimtotem_spirit_guide-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1200"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Despite the less than welcomed hunter changes Blizzard force fed us for Legion, I am really enjoying this expansion so far. I mean…I am really, REALLY enjoying it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although
         <a href="http://us.battle.net/forums/en/wow/topic/20748905201?page=4#post-73">
          I was up in arms over our shattered class
         </a>
         , after some debate and soul-searching, I decided that starting this expansion on any class other than hunter would just feel…well…
        </p>
        <p>
         wrong.
        </p>
        <p>
         The hunter is strong in me. I still can’t bring myself to enjoy Blizzard’s melee hunter class fantasy or their Tarzan spec, but I am experiencing some satisfaction as a Marksman. While not terribly complicated to play, it is a frenetic spec with a bit of a skill cap, and when played correctly, the damage is oh-so-nice.
        </p>
        <p>
         I do miss my traps, however.
        </p>
        <p>
         And Master’s Call.
        </p>
        <p>
         But I am learning to adapt.
        </p>
        <p>
         I’m still feeling a wee bit squishy, though not as bad as before, and MM is maybe a little bit too cleave-happy, but it’ll be my spec of choice until Blizzard revisits Beast Mastery and makes it into a spec I can enjoy once again.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love, love, love the artifact weapon system. I had my doubts at first, but wow…they did a bang-up job with this, IMO. Weapon progression has that rewarding feel that I haven’t experienced for some time, and treasure-hunting for that sweet, sweet artifact power is a blast.
        </p>
        <p>
         And my god, is this game pretty now. The detail which was added to the environments, and the little things like the current actually moving you when you’re in a rapidly moving stream, adds a whole new layer of depth.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance.jpg">
          <img alt="wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40311" height="675" sizes="(max-width: 1200px) 100vw, 1200px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance.jpg 1200w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/wow_legion_improved_graphics_draw_distance-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1200"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Also, the artwork and quest lore is far superior to anything I’ve seen from this game in some time. The questing experience has been truly immersive.
        </p>
        <p>
         I’d say that apart their disappointing approach to “reinventing” the hunter class, Blizz knocked it out of the park with this expansion, from a leveling standpoint anyway. I have been enjoying the leveling experience so much, that I’ve been taking my time – making sure that I stop to smell the roses. So much so, in fact, that I still haven’t hit max level.
        </p>
        <p>
         I’ve also been overhauling my UI, which has slowed me down as well, but with all of these changes, I’ve found it necessary to adapt my interface to the new and simpler, yet proc-happy play-style.
        </p>
        <p>
         All in all, Legion has been a sheer pleasure so far and I’m hopeful that Blizzard will listen to our cries and work towards a compromise with the hunter community – introducing updates and changes that will help to fix some of what they’ve wronged. Until then, I am going to…
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/keep_calm_and_hunter_on.jpg">
          <img alt="keep_calm_and_hunter_on" class="size-full wp-image-40308 aligncenter" height="750" sizes="(max-width: 450px) 100vw, 450px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/keep_calm_and_hunter_on.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/keep_calm_and_hunter_on.jpg 450w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/09/keep_calm_and_hunter_on-180x300.jpg 180w" width="450"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         How are you guys enjoying Legion? Which spec have you found to be most pleasing? Let us know in the comments.
        </p>

        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/legion_invasion.jpg">
          <img alt="legion_invasion" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40293" height="530" sizes="(max-width: 1400px) 100vw, 1400px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/legion_invasion.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/legion_invasion.jpg 1400w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/legion_invasion-300x114.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/legion_invasion-768x291.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/legion_invasion-1024x388.jpg 1024w" width="1400"/>

        <p>
         Now that I’ve had about two weeks to get used to Patch 7.0.3, I wanted to offload some of my thoughts and opinions concerning the pre-Legion patch.
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Good
        </h3>
        <p>
         The transmog catalog is fantastic. I really dig that they went the Diablo 3 route with transmog, and the extra bag space is a godsend. This was a really nice quality-of-life addition to the game.
        </p>
        <p>
         The demon invasions are awesome! They can seem a little grindy after awhile, but holy cow do they make leveling and gearing alts easy! I may very well have 2 shiny new 100s before Legion hits, and possibly a 3rd. I’ll be really happy to have options once the expansion arrives, which I’ll allude to in a bit.
        </p>
        <p>
         The new graphics rendering and extended draw distance are a welcome change. I just built a new PC which is able to take advantage of the higher game settings and boy does Azeroth look pretty when I’m soaring above it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Demon Hunters! I’ve only played one in the time it took me to level him to 100, but I must say, they are pretty fun. Tons of mobility, awesome skill animations, lasers! However, I tend to prefer the old classics myself, so it’s doubtful I’ll be on him too much in the upcoming expansion. There’s already enough of them running around anyway. So much purple in my raid frames and in my Recount.
        </p>
        <p>
         There may be a few other bits I forgot, but those are some things that come to mind when I think of reasons to be excited about 7.0.3.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/flying_over_orgrimmar.jpg">
          <img alt="flying_over_orgrimmar" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40292" height="1080" sizes="(max-width: 1920px) 100vw, 1920px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/flying_over_orgrimmar.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/flying_over_orgrimmar.jpg 1920w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/flying_over_orgrimmar-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/flying_over_orgrimmar-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/flying_over_orgrimmar-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1920"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Bad
        </h3>
        <p>
         The continual pruning of class abilities in an effort to simplify gameplay. I’m getting really tired of this, and more so than any other patch in WoW history, 7.0.3 has utterly gutted many class specs.
        </p>
        <p>
         And my god…Blizzard…what the hell have you done to the hunter class?! I’ve endured every major class overhaul since pre-BC, but this latest patch has left my hunter a shadow of his former self.
        </p>
        <p>
         I was most excited to try out survival since I was one of the oddballs who actually enjoyed having melee abilities back in the day. I played as survival until I could afford a gladiator pike and then I was done. It is an interesting spec, but to me it seems like a novelty. I have a hard time thinking it a viable choice above other melee classes and
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          it certainly does not feel like a hunter
         </span>
         . Hunters are a ranged class and if any of the 3 specs merit a melee-only play style, it should be beast mastery.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know that survival used to be the talent tree with the melee abilities, but in my opinion, “strictly melee” does not jive with the identity of the specialization. Nets, traps, grenades, etc… all those things fit in well with what I would consider the identity of the survival class, but the fighting side-by-side with your loyal pet business… that’s BM all the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         Blizzard completely pooped on the class when they came up with their version of hunter “class fantasy”. I truly feel like MM and BM tuning were deferred until the very end during their class overhauls and they simply ran out of time and ideas. Survival seems a little clunky to me, but it’s at least engaging and interesting to play.
        </p>
        <p>
         Beast Mastery still feels reasonably powerful in PvP, PvE DPS is OK from what I’ve experienced, but overall, I am not happy with it. The rotation doesn’t feel smooth anymore, and I’m not crazy about becoming a “summoner”. I never embraced Dire Beast as a talent and I’m bummed that it’s now a core baseline ability that I’m forced to use.
        </p>
        <p>
         Beast Mastery should be about the bond between a hunter and his pet. It should not be about me summoning some random animal out of thin air every few seconds or pulling a pack of turtles, scorpions, owls, bears, and alligators out of my butt every 3 minutes. I never liked Stampede either, and now it’s pretty much a must-have talent for Beast Mastery. The other talents in the 100 tier are pretty disappointing – leaving us with Stampede as the only viable option at present. Way to give us choices, Blizzard.
        </p>
        <p>
         A Murder of Crows I can deal with as a summoning spell. It’s a fun talent and I think it fits well with the Beast Mastery class fantasy, but as BM, I did always prefer Blink Strikes in that tier.
        </p>
        <p>
         Marksmanship I tried last, and wow, it was not for me. First off, I can’t see huntering without a pet, and MM without Lone Wolf just seems inadequate damage-wise. Furthermore,
         <s>
          without Master’s Call, there’s little need for a pet as MM, for PvP anyway
         </s>
         . For PvE content, I seriously doubt there’s any benefit to taking any talent in that tier besides Lone Wolf.
        </p>
        <p class="note">
         After a bit more research, i.e., patch note “catch-up”, I realized that not all of the pet abilities got scrapped. Some pets have still retained abilities which are advantageous in PvP. I had mistakenly thought all special abilities were dumped for non-exotic pets, but now I see that many pets still possess slows, snares, healing debuffs, etc. Even without Master’s Call, having the added PvP debuff or utility, and Roar of Sacrifice, edges out the DPS boost from Lone Wolf (for PvP). Before realizing this, I queued several BGs as MM using Careful Aim and found that DPS was still very powerful without Lone Wolf, and having RoS did help quite a bit in terms of survivability.
        </p>
        <p>
         To me, MM just feels weird now. It’s traditionally been my least favorite of the three hunter specs,
         <em>
          but I never didn’t like it
         </em>
         . Now I just flat out don’t dig it, which is too bad since it’s clearly the top producer right now in terms of DPS. But if I don’t enjoy it, I won’t play it. I may give MM another pass, but for right now, I just don’t know… I’m so confused right now when it comes to my future huntering.
        </p>
        <p>
         Shocking as it may sound, I’ve even flirted with the idea of maining another class for Legion.
        </p>
        <p>
         That thought is hard for me to fathom, but that’s how broken I feel the class is at the moment. I almost want to avoid hunter in protest.
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Ugly
        </h3>
        <p>
         I’m saddened over the continued homogenization of our hunter pets. For many of us, our furry, scaly or slimy sidekicks are what gave our class its unique appeal.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pets are what initially drew me to the hunter class. The unique meta-game of hunting down and taming beasts for their skills, skins and unique abilities…that was always such a turn on for me. In vanilla, it was so fascinating to learn about the different pet types, with their varying attack speeds and other traits. I actually enjoyed the thrill of taming a pet just so I could learn a new skill level to teach my other pets. It was work, but it was rewarding gameplay and something that really defined playing a hunter.
        </p>
        <p>
         It may seem weird to some of you, but I also used to enjoy cooking food for my pet(s) and making sure they stayed happy and fed. Things like the care and feeding of pets set hunters apart and furthered our class identity. Being a hunter meant you had a sidekick to nurture, level and train, which provided more work, but for many of us, that’s why we chose the class.
        </p>
        <p>
         When Blizzard first began down the path of pet parity, I was somewhat OK with it, but I never embraced the fact that any pet could be specced for any role. To me, that just seems silly. A moth should not be able to tank, and a turtle should not be able to dash, charge or put out as much DPS as a raptor. The changes to pet talents and abilities were gradual, and I’ve accepted them as they’ve been introduced, but now I’m going to be more critical of past changes as enough is enough already.
        </p>
        <p>
         With this latest change, which took away many of our pet’s defining abilities, they now have little more identity than a warlock’s summoned demon. In fact, they may almost be on par because at least a lock pet shows up with its own unique name.
        </p>
        <h3>
         And the Outright Unacceptable
        </h3>
        <p>
         No traps for MM or BM…are you kidding me?! For reals, this is ridiculous. Traps have been baseline since day one. I’m cool with SV having some additional tricks up its sleeve, but at the very least, please give BM and MM Frost and Freezing back. Come on, Blizzard!
        </p>
        <p>
         This cooldown they’re referring to as Aspect of the Cheetah…let’s get this fixed and returned to its former state, please.
        </p>
        <p>
         Give us back Master’s Call! Losing this ability altogether has been a tough one to handle. Master’s Call should again be a baseline ability for any hunter spec that doesn’t take Lone Wolf.
        </p>
        <p>
         Why is Camouflage now a talent and why is it in a tier competing with two CC abilities?! We don’t need to be full-invisible like rogues or druids, but come on… Camouflage should be baseline for the hunter
         <em>
          class fantasy
         </em>
         . Goddammit, Blizzard.
        </p>
        <p class="note">
         My assessment of our pruned and reimagined class is more about the feels, but if you want a more insightful critique of our current class mechanics and so forth, do yourself a favor and have a look at
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/40289/thoughts-7-0-3/#comment-529546">
          Denyel’s comment below
         </a>
         . It offers a practical analysis of the pre-patch hunter, highlighting both the good and the bad, but mostly bad.
        </p>
        <h3>
         So Now What?
        </h3>
        <p>
         After a few days on Garwulf, I realized I simply was not enjoying playing him as much anymore. And that sucks. It sucks bad. These days, I may not play as often as I once did, but no matter how much time I spent away from the game, and no matter what havoc Blizz wreaked on my beloved class throughout various patches and so forth, I always felt like I was spending time with an old familiar friend when I’d log on to do some huntering. No matter how much the class had morphed over the years, many things still felt the same.
        </p>
        <p>
         As of now, I don’t feel that’s the case anymore. The hunter class has lost its way due to Blizzard pushing on us what they feel the hunter class should be, and guess what, their idea of Hunter Class Fantasy is lame. Really lame.
        </p>
        <p>
         After a few days on Garwulf, I decided to turn my focus towards Paladin and Warrior – both of which feel a bit different, but not unrecognizable or broken. After that, I started playing my Disc Priest a bit and let me tell you, Blizzard nailed it with their class fantasy. Disc is a very exciting and engaging spec, albeit a bit stressful as a healer, but fun and rewarding.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then…I decided to do the unimaginable and roll a Warlock. For right now, if Blizz doesn’t untangle the mess they made of hunters, Warlock may be the new hunter for me. It’s hard for me to imagine that I wouldn’t main hunter in Legion, but for the first time ever, I’m not sure.
        </p>
        <h3>
         In Closing…
        </h3>
        <p>
         I do hate coming across as negative, but this time I am really disappointed with the path Blizzard has taken with hunters. Their idea of hunter class fantasy is, simply put…Bullshit.
        </p>
        <p>
         If they want to embrace this whole fantasy route and work at making each spec feel unique, then there are some ways they could fix this, although I feel like things may be too far gone at this point. I think Blizz may have shit the bed as far as hunter class design goes, and we just may have to live with it for one expansion.
        </p>
        <p>
         And did I also mention how much I lament the loss of Kill Shot? And my glaives. Glaive Toss was a fairly new ability, but I really enjoyed it for the short time it was around.
        </p>
        <p>
         I could go on and on, but I’ll stop for now. I just wanted to sound off a little bit and now I feel better.
        </p>
        <p>
         So…what do YOU think of Patch 7.0.3? Love it? Hate it? What do you think of the new focus regen mechanics? Do you miss mana?
        </p>
        <p>
         Let’s discuss it in the comments below!
        </p>

          Experiencing God Mode
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2016-06-01T01:29:33+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 1, 2016
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/author/craig/" itemprop="url" rel="author">

       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="fooboxshare_post_id" type="hidden" value="40265"/>
        <p>
         It’s been a really long time since I routinely paid careful attention to patch notes and things like that, so somewhere along the way I missed the bit about level scaling in lower level pvp. Many of you probably knew this, but I just discovered it tonight.
        </p>
        <p>
         What you may not know, however, is that if you’re toggling back and forth between dungeon and BG queues, like I was, there is a chance your pet’s level will stick at its scaled PvP version. Additionally, as long as you keep chain-queuing dungeons, there’s a good chance it will remain bugged out.
        </p>
        <p>
         I first noticed something was a bit odd when I came out blasting my way to 16K+ in Throne of the Tides at level 81. I started out thinking I was just having some exceptional RNG and a perfect group composition – allowing me to slay the meters. It’d been some time since I hit Cata dungeons on a hunter, so I wasn’t altogether sure that some 15k-20k burst wasn’t out of the ordinary. ‘Cause…well…Hunters, you know. We’re just that epic.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps.jpg">
          <img alt="throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40266" height="1125" sizes="(max-width: 2000px) 100vw, 2000px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps.jpg 2000w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/throne_of_the_tides_hunter_dps-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="2000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After the first dungeon, I finished with 15.8K average DPS at level 82, with my pet’s Kill Commands hitting for between 90 to 110K! The second dungeon I ran, I focused on my gameplay and managed 18.6K overall at level 83.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps.jpg">
          <img alt="wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40267" height="1125" sizes="(max-width: 2000px) 100vw, 2000px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps.jpg 2000w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug_dps-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="2000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Although I was really enjoying the godly DPS, I knew something was amiss. When we entered our 3rd dungeon, I paused to have a look at things and noticed my pet’s level was showing as 91 and my level was also showing one higher than it actually was.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_40268" style="width: 2010px">
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug.jpg">
          <img alt="This screenshot shows my level in chat as 83, which is correct, but then it also shows my level as 84 on the tooltip and my pet's level as 91!" class="size-full wp-image-40268" height="1125" sizes="(max-width: 2000px) 100vw, 2000px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug.jpg 2000w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/wow_cataclysm_hunter_pet_level_bug-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="2000"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          This screenshot shows my level in chat as 83, which is correct, but then it also shows my level as 84 on the tooltip, and my pet’s level as 91!
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         My
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/4485/arcturis/">
          Spirit Beast
         </a>
         got stuck at 89 after I left my BG to run a dungeon, then continued to level from there, all the way to 91 – proving way too much for the bosses of Throne of the Tides and The Stonecore. It was awesome. My party members were especially pleased with my performance, and we ended up smashing 6 dungeons in 1hr, 15mins, in which I went from 81 to half-way through to 86.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have a leveling hunter sitting around 80-85 (not sure that it works in Panda content), you gotta try this out. Enter a random BG and see if your pet’s level stays scaled after you exit. I Googled this bug and it seems to be something that’s been in-game for awhile, so it’s doubtful Blizzard cares too much about fixing it.
        </p>
        <p>
         It was certainly good for a few laughs this evening and I basically charged through Cataclysm in just over 90 minutes!
        </p>

          February 4, 2016

         By

        <input class="fooboxshare_post_id" type="hidden" value="40241"/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-40242">
          <img alt="the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40242" height="900" sizes="(max-width: 1600px) 100vw, 1600px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter.jpg 1600w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_wow_achievement_hunter-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1600"/>

        <p>
         My second time queueing for this instance and I won. My orc out-gears Garwulf 708 to 702, but the best I’ve managed on him is 3rd place. It must be the transmog. Folks were scared, I know it. No one, I mean…NO ONE wants the business end of my crossbow.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-40243">
          <img alt="highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40243" height="900" sizes="(max-width: 1600px) 100vw, 1600px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory.jpg 1600w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/highmaul_coliseum_gladiator_tournament_victory-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I would also like to thank all of the Death Knights and Ret Paladins that did not join this instance.
        </p>
        <p>
         This coliseum business is kinda fun. I only started doing it last week, so it’s still pretty new and exciting to me. Winning it was a thrill, considering I only ever see plate wearers come out victorious, but let that be a lesson…
        </p>
        <p>
         Do NOT underestimate the hunter in the loin cloth.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_arena_spoils.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-40244">
          <img alt="the_last_of_us_arena_spoils" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40244" height="900" sizes="(max-width: 1600px) 100vw, 1600px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_arena_spoils.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_arena_spoils.jpg 1600w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_arena_spoils-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_arena_spoils-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/the_last_of_us_arena_spoils-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          03/21/2016 Update:
         </strong>
         I’ve competed in this arena probably 20 times since this post, and managed to win it another 4 times. I’ve won it a total of 3 times now on Garwulf, twice on my Paladin, but I have yet to get a win on my orc BM hunter.
        </p>
        <p>
         I’ve had pretty good success on Garwulf, winning it 3 out of the 7 times I’ve queued for it. The first time I won felt largely due to luck, but when I won it straight away the 3rd time I queued, I figured I must be doing something right. My next win came on my 4th attempt this past week, so at 3 for 7, I feel like I’m doing alright – especially since this event so heavily favors hybrid classes and plate.
        </p>
        <p>
         My orc BM hunter is running pretty much the exact same spec as Garwulf, but I’ve never gotten past second place on him. Opposing players seem to gun for me, plus I feel like Garwulf has a very slight edge due to a few tricks up his sleeve.
        </p>
        <p>
         For one, I think Shadowmeld offers a huge advantage. After I run around tagging everyone in the arena, I usually will try to go invisible for 4 or 5 seconds, allowing me to escape some damage and get a few precious ticks of Spirit Bond in. If I’m really out of the fray and unnoticed, I can sometimes get some bandage ticks in as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         Next, I happen to have a supply of Baby Spice in my bank which I feel works very well for avoiding getting focused at the start. Lastly, I keep a hefty supply of Drums of Fury on hand at all times (since I’m a LW’er), which I pop right around the time Bestial Wrath is about to come off cooldown. The little extra haste buff helps me deal with the remaining 3-4 players that are left at that point. This week I’ll buy some drums off the AH for my orc to see if that little boost will do the trick.
        </p>
        <p>
         I may post a few more tips on how to tackle this event as a BM hunter, but in the meantime, feel free to ask me any questions and I’ll do my best to provide some answers!
        </p>
       </div>

         <a class="entry-title-link" href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/40223/taming-banthalos-at-level-100/" rel="bookmark">
          Taming Ban’thalos in Legion
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2016-02-03T13:16:05+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 3, 2016
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/author/craig/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Garwulf
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/40223/taming-banthalos-at-level-100/#comments">
           8 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="fooboxshare_post_id" type="hidden" value="40223"/>
        <p class="note">
         Post has been updated to reflect the hunter changes in Legion. The same basic strategy as before holds true, but since we no longer have Distracting Shot, we’ll use Concussive Shot to pull Ban’thalos instead.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-40224">
          <img alt="banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-40224" height="900" sizes="(max-width: 1600px) 100vw, 1600px" src="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne.jpg" srcset="https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne.jpg 1600w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne-300x169.jpg 300w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne-768x432.jpg 768w, https://huntsmanslodge.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/banthalos_spawn_above_sanctuary_of_malorne-1024x576.jpg 1024w" width="1600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Taming Ban’thalos has always been a bit of a challenge. He spawns up in the sky above the Sanctuary of Malorne, so you need to be on a flying mount to engage him. Second, he’s neutral, so you need to get his attention somehow. Third, you need to not die from fall damage once you do get his attention.
        </p>
        <p>
         At level 100, he poses another interesting challenge.
         <em>
          How do you aggro him without killing him?
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Back in Cataclysm,
         <a href="http://www.huntsmanslodge.com/9868/a-briefing-on-the-rare-pets-in-patch-4-2/">
          the taming challenge
         </a>
         involved a strategy which was basically: tag Ban’thalos by attacking him, then tame him without dying. At level 100, however, this strat no longer works because even if you take off all of your gear and punch him, there’s a chance that could still kill him. Instead, you need to use Concussive Shot
         <s>
          an ability which a lot of hunters may not even have on their action bars…
          <a href="http://www.wowhead.com/spell=20736/distracting-shot">
           Distracting Shot
          </a>
         </s>
         . This shot will cause Ban’thalos to get angry with you while not damaging him.
        </p>
        <h2>
         How to Tame Ban’thalos at Level 100
        </h2>
        <p>
         This process is surprisingly easy once you know what to do.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="more-link" href="https://huntsmanslodge.com/40223/taming-banthalos-at-level-100/#more-40223">
          [Read more…]
         </a>
        </p>
