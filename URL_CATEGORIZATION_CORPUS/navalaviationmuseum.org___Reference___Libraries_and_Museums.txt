          <div id="home-body-text">
           <h1>
            PRESERVING THE HISTORY AND HERITAGE OF NAVAL AVIATION
           </h1>
           <p style="margin-top: -15px;">
            The
            <strong>
             <span style="font-family: oswald; font-size: 1.25em;">
              National Naval Aviation Museum
             </span>
            </strong>
            is the world’s largest Naval Aviation museum and one of the most-visited museums in the state of Florida. Share the excitement of Naval Aviation’s rich history and see more than 150 beautifully restored aircraft representing Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard Aviation. These historic and one-of-a-kind aircraft are displayed both inside the Museum’s nearly 350,000 square feet of exhibit space and outside on its 37-acre grounds.
           </p>
           <p>
            There’s so much to see and do, you’ll want to stay all day! Admission is
            <strong>
             free!
            </strong>
            Hours of operation are
            <strong>
             9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
            </strong>
            daily. Closed Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day, and New Year’s. For more information, call
            <a class="phone" href="tel:+18003275002">
             <span style="font-family: oswald;">
              1-800-327-5002.
             </span>
            </a>
           </p>
           <div id="plan-visit-home-container" style="text-align: center; width: 100%; margin-top: 40px;">
            <a class="button" href="attractions/plan-visit" id="plan-visit-home">
             Plan Your Visit
            </a>
           </div>
           <div style="padding: 15px; margin-top: 40px; background: #e6f3ff;">
            <h4>
             Important Notes for Visitors
            </h4>
            <ul>
             <li style="margin-bottom: 10px;">
              Visitors (16 years and older) to the National Naval Aviation Museum must have valid photo identification and all bags are subject to search.
             </li>
             <li style="margin-bottom: 10px;">
              All visitors to the National Naval Aviation Museum, Fort Barrancas or the Pensacola Lighthouse, who do not possess a Department of Defense identification card or are not escorted by the holder of a Department of Defense identification card will be required to enter AND exit Naval Air Station Pensacola through the West Gate off Blue Angel Parkway.
              <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/dir//30.3359276,-87.344452/@30.3362923,-87.3433605,581m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m2!4m1!3e0?hl=en" target="_blank">
