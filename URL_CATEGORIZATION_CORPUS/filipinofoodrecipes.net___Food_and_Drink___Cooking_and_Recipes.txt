      <h1 style="margin: 10px; ">
       <font size="2">
        Filipino Recipes
       </font>
      </h1>
      <p align="justify" style="margin: 10px; ">
       <font size="2">
        Featured on 
			this site are free Filipino recipes that have become all time 
			Philippine favorites. Traditional Filipino food recipes that are 
			simple to cook and always a pleasure to serve and eat... dishes that 
			are always welcome on the dining table. Filipino food consist mostly 
			of vegetables, seafood, dairy, meat and rice. Like Philippine 
			fiestas, Filipino recipes are rich in flavor &amp; color.
       </font>
      </p>
      <div style="float: left; margin-left:10px; margin-top:5px; margin-right: 5px">
       <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">

      <p align="justify" style="margin: 10px; ">
       Filipino culinary arts is 
			greatly influenced by Chinese, European, American, Arab and Asian 
			cuisines. A fusion of various recipes adopted from earlier traders, 
			Asian immigrants and former colonizers.
      </p>
      <p align="justify" style="margin: 10px; ">
       Before the Spaniards 
			colonized the Philippines, the country's cuisine consisted of root 
			crops, game, vegetables and seafood. Filipino food recipes then was 
			almost always either boiled, roasted or broiled. Foreign trade 
			brought in all kinds of spices and plants to the Philippines. And 
			like the Filipino today, its cuisine is a gastronomic feast from 
			different countries and cultures - from east to west.
      </p>
      <p align="justify" style="margin: 10px; ">
       Filipinos have embraced as 
			their own cuisines form other countries like the noodles from the 
			Chinese, rice and meat dishes from the Spaniards, fast-food from the 
			Americans and even spaghetti from the Italians. All these now form 
			part of the Philippine cuisine - with the Filipino touch, of course.
      </p>
      <p align="center">
      </p>
      <p align="justify" style="margin: 10px; ">
       There are many Pinoy 
			viands from each region of the Philippines, with each region having 
			its own unique specialty. The Ilocanos from Northern Luzon are known 
			for their Pakbet - a simple but nutritious vegetable dish. While the 
			Bicolanos are famous for their Bicol Express a hot and spicy dish 
			simmered in coconut milk. Overall, nothing beats the fame of the 
			tasty pork and chicken Adobo, a dish the Philippines is known for 
			throughout the world. And that too is served in diffent variations.
      </p>
      <p align="justify" style="margin: 10px; ">
       Experience the tastes and 
			colors of Philippine food. A gastronomic delight that has been 
			savored through many generations. Enjoy the free Filipino food 
			recipes on this site. We will continue to add more recipes as we 
			develop the site. happy cooking and Mabuhay!
      </p>
