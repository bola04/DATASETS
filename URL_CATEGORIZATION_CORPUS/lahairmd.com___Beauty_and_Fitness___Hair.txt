                 <u>
                  <strong>
                   About Dr. Perrault:
                  </strong>
                 </u>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Dr. Gary Perrault is a board-certified physician and surgeon with a private practice in Beverly Hills, California and over twenty years experience, treating various hair loss related problems in men, women, and children. He specializes in restoration of the scalp and facial hair-bearing areas, including sideburn and eyebrow reconstruction. A large part of Dr. Perrault's practice involves treatment of patients with unsatisfactory hair restoration surgery results and the repair of hair loss related problems following cosmetic or reconstructive procedures.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Dr. Perrault has a vast experience as a practitioner and as an esteemed teacher, having trained prominent physicians throughout the United States in the art and science of hair restoration surgery. Dr. Perrault's practice is distinguished by a passion for artistic and technical detail that has produced numerous proprietary techniques involving microsurgical follicle dissection and the transposition of follicular unit grafts. When performing hair restoration surgery, painstaking attention is given to the many subtle variations of hair quality; this requires Dr. Perrault and his staff to invest an extravagant amount of effort and time into each patient.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  <u>
                   Board Certifications and Professional Affiliations:
                  </u>
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Dr. Perrault is a Diplomate of The National Board of Medical Examiners, The American Board of Surgery, and The American Board of Hair Restoration Surgery, a Fellow of The American Academy of Cosmetic Surgery, a Member of The California Academy of Cosmetic Surgery and The International Society of Hair Restoration Surgery.
                </p>

                <p>
                 <strong>
                  <u>
                   Approach:
                  </u>
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 When diagnosing and treating hair loss, Dr. Perrault believes that it is important to:
                </p>
                <p>
                 1. assess the patient's overall health, medical history, and relevant nutritional factors
                </p>
                <p>
                 2. and consider all
                 <strong>
                  effective
                 </strong>
                 medical, nutritional and surgical options
                </p>
                <p>
                 This requires a detailed history and a careful examination of the scalp and other areas of hair loss, using dermatoscopic and macrophotographic techniques. The initial consultation, with Dr. Perrault, requires a minimum of one hour in most patients.
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  <u>
                   Procedures:
                  </u>
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Scalp
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Treatment of male pattern hair loss
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Treatment of female pattern hair loss
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Repair of prior unsatisfactory hair restoration surgery results
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Repair of scarring and hair loss following traumatic injuries of the scalp, such as: motor vehicle accidents, neurosurgery, various plastic and reconstructive procedures, radiation injury, and burns
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Treatment of Aplasia Cutis Congenita
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Eyebrow
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Female eyebrow augmentation
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Male eyebrow augmentation
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Eyebrow repair, or restoration, following injury or surgery
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Sideburn
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Female sideburn area repair, or restoration following facelift surgery or injury
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Male sideburn area repair, or restoration following facelift surgery or injury
                </p>
                <p>
                 <strong>
                  Beard
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Beard augmentation
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Beard repair, following facial trauma or surgery
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Mustache area repair or restoration, following cleft lip/pallet surgery
                </p>
                <p>

                  Pediatric
                 </strong>
                </p>
                <p>
                 • Eyebrow reconstruction, following congenital melanocytic nevus excision
                </p>

                 • Eyebrow repair or restoration, following injury
                </p>
