       <td align="center" class="style14 style15" valign="middle">
        <div align="left" class="style17">
         "Hobbits delighted in such things, if they were accurate: they liked to have books filled with
         <br/>
         things that they already knew, set out fair and square with no contradictions."
        </div>
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td>
        <div align="right">
         from
         <span class="title-comment">
          Concerning Hobbits
         </span>
         by J.R.R. Tolkien
        </div>
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p align="justify">
      <a href="php/lotr.php">
       <img align="right" alt="Ring and Eye Device from the dust jacket of The Lord of the Rings" border="1" class="imageborder" height="414" src="images/other/ringandeye.gif" title="Ring and Eye Device from the dust jacket of The Lord of the Rings" width="300"/>
      </a>
      <span class="subtitles">
       TolkienBooks.net aims to list all British editions of the published writings of John Ronald Reuel Tolkien (author of
      </span>
      <span class="black-titles">
       The Hobbit
      </span>
      <span class="subtitles">
       and
      </span>
      <span class="black-titles">
       The Lord of the Rings
      </span>
      <span class="subtitles">
       ) and to illustrate changes to the texts and covers.
      </span>
     </p>
     <p align="justify">
      The site was put together to act as a companion piece to a book called
      <span class="black-titles">
       J.R.R. Tolkien: A Descriptive Bibliography
      </span>
      by Wayne G. Hammond.  This is a veritable mine of useful information for the Tolkien collector - if you donâ€™t own a copy, find one! Bibliography entries include page references to the bok.  The site also includes a few items that were unknown to Wayne, and points out a small number of possible errors and misinterpretations.
     </p>
     <p align="justify">
      Obviously,
      <span class="black-titles style6">
       <a href="./php/hobbit.php">
        The Hobbit
       </a>
      </span>
      and
      <span class="black-titles">
       <a href="./php/lotr.php">
        The Lord of the Rings
       </a>
      </span>
      sections are the largest areas of the bibliography, but there is a lot more to see - try the
      <span class="subtitles">
       <a href="./php/1938-1953.php">
        1938-1953
       </a>
      </span>
      section - it includes
      <span class="black-titles">
       <a href="./php/gallery.php?subsection=19">
        Farmer Giles of Ham
       </a>
      </span>
      as well as some pretty obscure and hard to find academic publications - or the
      <span class="subtitles">
       <a href="./php/slipcases.php">
        Boxed and Slipcased Sets
       </a>
      </span>
      section. There are also some very interesting items in the
      <span class="subtitles">
       <a href="./php/1910-1937.php">
        1910-1937
       </a>
      </span>
      section, ranging from his early poetry to some of his most important work on Old and Middle English. Each section is split into subsections, which appear as galleries of thumbnail images.
     </p>
     <p align="justify" class="subtitles">
      Just click on a thumbnail to see a larger image, details of the edition and any reprints.
     </p>
     <p align="justify">
      Books are presented in order of the year of first issue and are grouped together with later printings and editions following on from the first edition.  There is one image for most books, and articles are being added to fully illustrate the more important and hard to find editions. Bibliography entries give details of the edition, year of publication, publisher, format and ISBN (if applicable), together with details of any illustrations, later reprints and other handy snippets of information.
     </p>
     <p align="justify">
      As a general rule only British editions are listed, unless there isnâ€™t one of course! Where there is only a â€˜foreignâ€™ edition, it will be listed together with any other significant reprints and variants. For a few of the older items, that are particularly difficult to find, some non-British appearances are also listed.

       <br/>
      </span>
      <span class="subtitles">
       Devon Press, a fellow collector, has compiled an  excellent guide to American editions at
       <a href="https://www.tolkienbooks.us/">
        TolkienBooks.US
       </a>
       .
      </span>
