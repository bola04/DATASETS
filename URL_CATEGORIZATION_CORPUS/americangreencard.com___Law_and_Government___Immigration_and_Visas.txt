     <h2>
      <a name="anchor information">

      <font size="+1">
       General Information
      </font>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <font color="#000099">
       The US Diversity Visa Program  for this year 2013, is called American Green Card Lottery DV-2015.
      </font>
      <font color="#000099">
       Winners in the Green Card Lottery may receive an Immigrant Visa, and American Green Card, and the right to live and work in the United States of America.
      </font>
     </p>
     <p>
      This year's Green Card Lottery DV-2015 (for the year 2013) will begin  October 1, 2013 at  noon (EDT) (GMT-4) and will end  November 2, 2013 at  noon (EDT) (GMT-4).      
      Exact dates will be posted as soon as the U.S. Government releases that information.
     </p>
     <p>
      Each year the US Government, through its American Green Card Lottery Diversity 
                  Visa Program, makes available 55,000* immigrant visas to persons meeting 
      the eligibility requirements.
     </p>
     <p>
      Each eligible applicant must submit an entry form and digital photos.
     </p>
     <p>
      <font color="#ff0000">
       <strong>
        WARNING
       </strong>
      </font>
      <strong>
       :
      </strong>
      <u>
       Never
      </u>
      pay anyone to submit your official Green Card Lottery entry for you. It is easy to submit your own official entry for free.
        The US Government does
      <u>
       NOT
      </u>
      call Green Card Lottery winners, does
      <u>
       NOT
      </u>
      send email messages to Green Card Lottery winners, and does
      <u>
       NOT
      </u>
      mail letters to Green Card Lottery winners.
     </p>
     <p>
      Starting     October 1, 2013, you can go to the official Government Green Card Lottery entry site at
      <a href="http://www.dvlottery.state.gov/photo.aspx">
       http://www.dvlottery.state.gov/photo.aspx
      </a>
      and check the digital size of your photos. If they do not meet the requirements, you can email them to us at
      <font color="#000099">
       <a href="mailto:info@americangreencard.com">
        info@americangreencard.com
       </a>
      </font>
      . We will do one free quick check to see if they can be corrected. If we can make the needed corrections, we will let you know. If you wish for us to make those corrections, there is a $60 fee for each photo corrected.
     </p>
     <p>
      There is no fee to enter the Green Card Lottery. The only place where you can submit your
      <u>
       official
      </u>
      entry form is at
      <a href="https://www.dvlottery.state.gov/">
       www.dvlottery.state.gov
      </a>
      <a href="www.dvlottery.state.gov">
      </a>
      <a href="http://www.dvlottery.state.gov/application.aspx">
      </a>
      . Therefore, please go   there to submit your
      <u>
       official
      </u>
      entry and digital photo at no charge. Instructions   on how to submit your digital photo are on that same page.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="howenter.htm">
       Click here to go to
       <font size="+1">
        <br/>
        Instructions on How to Enter this year's American Green Card Lottery DV-2015 are posted here.
       </font>
      </a>
     </p>

     <hr/>
     <p>
      Applicants for the U.S. Diversity Program Immigration Visas are initially 
        chosen through a random computer-generated lottery drawing. Immigrant 
        visas are apportioned among six geographic regions, with a greater number 
        of visas going to regions with lower rates of immigration--and no visas 
        going to countries sending more than 50,000 immigrants to the U.S. in 
        the past five years. No one country can receive more than 3,500 diversity 
      program visas in any one year.
     </p>
     <p>
      <font color="#ff0000">
       The following countries  are NOT eligible to enter this year's Green Card Lottery DV-2015.
      </font>
      BANGLADESH, BRAZIL, CANADA, CHINA mainland-born (
      <u>
       Hong Kong SAR, Macau SAR and Taiwan
      </u>
      <strong>
       <u>
        were
       </u>
      </strong>
      <u>
       eligible
      </u>
      ), COLOMBIA, DOMINICAN REPUBLIC, ECUADOR, EL SALVADOR,  HAITI, INDIA, JAMAICA, MEXICO, NIGERIA, PAKISTAN, PERU, PHILIPPINES,   SOUTH KOREA, UNITED KINGDOM and UK dependent territories (
      <u>
       except Northern Ireland, which is
       <strong>
       </strong>
       eligible
      </u>
      ), and VIETNAM.
     </p>
     <p>
      If the winner files a successful application, he or she can enter the 
        U.S. and exchange the immigrant visa for a Green Card, issued by the Immigration 
        and Naturalization Service (INS). If the winner is already in the U.S., 
      he or she can get a Green card from INS without leaving the country.
     </p>
