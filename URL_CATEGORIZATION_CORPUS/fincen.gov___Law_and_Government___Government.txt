            <span class="field-content">
             <a href="/news/news-releases/fincen-directors-law-enforcement-awards-program-recognizes-significance-bsa" hreflang="en">
              FinCEN Director’s Law Enforcement Awards Program Recognizes Significance of BSA Reporting by Financial Institutions
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <div class="views-field views-field-field-date-release">
            <div class="field-content">
             <time datetime="2018-05-08T16:44:15Z">
              May 08, 2018
             </time>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="views-field views-field-body">
            <div class="field-content">
             <p>
              WASHINGTON— In a ceremony held at the U.S. Department of the Treasury today, the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) recognized the outstanding efforts of law enforcement agencies that successfully used Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) reporting in their criminal investigations. The BSA’s recordkeeping and reporting requirements help to create a financial trail that law enforcement agencies use to track criminals, their activities, and their assets.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="whats-new-items views-row col-md-4">
           <div class="views-field views-field-title">
            <span class="field-content">
             <a href="/news/news-releases/fincen-issues-advisory-human-rights-abuses-enabled-corrupt-senior-foreign" hreflang="en">
              FinCEN Issues Advisory on Human Rights Abuses Enabled by Corrupt Senior Foreign Political Figures and Their Financial Facilitators
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <div class="views-field views-field-field-date-release">
            <div class="field-content">
             <time datetime="2018-06-12T16:44:15Z">
              June 12, 2018
             </time>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="views-field views-field-body">
            <div class="field-content">
             <p>
              WASHINGTON—The Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) issued an
              <a href="https://www.fincen.gov/resources/advisories/fincen-advisory-fin-2018-a003">
               advisory
              </a>
              today to U.S. financial institutions to highlight the connection between corrupt senior foreign political figures and their enabling of human rights abuses. The use of financial facilitators is one way that corrupt senior foreign political figures access the U.S. and international financial systems to move or hide illicit proceeds and evade U.S. and global sanctions.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
