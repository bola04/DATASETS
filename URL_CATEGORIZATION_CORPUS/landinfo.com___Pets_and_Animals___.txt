       <h1>
        LAND INFO Satellite Imagery Solutions &amp; Digital Map Data
       </h1>
       <img alt="divider" src="images/div_750.gif"/>
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
        <tr valign="top">
         <td width="460">
          <p class="head7b">
           <strong>
            Complex Imagery and Map Data Projects Completed
            <br/>
            at Once; Impossible Ones Take Just a Bit Longer
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p class="bodycopy2">
           Founded in 1993, LAND INFO Worldwide Mapping, LLC (LAND INFO) is a leading provider of worldwide digital topographic map and nautical chart data (DRGs, DEMs &amp; vector layers), high-resolution aerial and satellite imagery (Pléiades-1A/1B, WorldView-1, WorldView-2, WorldView-3, WorldView-4, GeoEye-1, KOMPSAT, TripleSat, QuickBird &amp; IKONOS) and medium-resolution satellite imagery (SPOT 
			1-7, RapidEye, PlanetScope [Dove], Sentinel, Landsat, ALOS, ASTER &amp; 
			Deimos.)  We also improve imagery via a range of processing services  (ortho rectification, pan-sharpening, tonal balancing, mosaic output and wavelet compression.)
           <br/>
           <br/>
           A pioneer in the use of Object Based Image Analysis and other cutting-edge technologies, LAND INFO creates a number of value-added/derivative map data layers from imagery such as vector features (roads, buildings and hydrology), land-use/land-cover, vegetation, impervious surface mapping &amp; DEMs/contours. Our automated solution to extract and Z-attribute building polygons enables cost-effective solutions for applications requiring high-res coverage over large areas such as 5G wireless telco.  The company is an Authorized Distribution Partner with both leading commercial high-resolution imaging satellite operators (DigitalGlobe &amp; Airbus) and maintains one of the world's largest commercial archives of digital topographic map data (currently 237 countries).
          </p>
          <br/>

systems (GIS), academia, national security and defense, visualization/simulation, aviation, wireless communications and other utilities, mining, oil &amp; gas, conservation, hydrology, civil &amp; environmental engineering, conservation, humanitarian assistance, land surveying, development, fleet management, mass media and motion pictures, among others.
