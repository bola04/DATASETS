         <h2 class="lightblue">
          What is USMLE?
         </h2>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          The
          <strong>
           United States Medical Licensing Examination ®
          </strong>
          (USMLE®) is a three-step examination for medical licensure 
          in the United States and is sponsored by the
          <a href="http://www.fsmb.org">
           Federation of State Medical Boards (FSMB)
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="http://www.nbme.org">
           National Board of Medical Examiners® (NBME®)
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          The USMLE assesses a physician's ability to apply knowledge, concepts, and principles, and to demonstrate fundamental patient-centered 
          skills, that are important in health and disease and that constitute the basis of safe and effective patient care. Each of the three 
          Steps of the USMLE complements the others; no Step can stand alone in the assessment of readiness for medical licensure.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="/about/">
           Find out more about the USMLE »
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>

         <h2 class="lightblue">
          Upcoming Changes to USMLE
         </h2>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          A summary of
          <a href="/pdfs/Changes_to_USMLE_handout.pdf" target="_blank">
           planned changes over the next few years is available.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Composite Committee of the USMLE initiated a process to undertake a comprehensive review of the USMLE program in 2004. The Composite Committee establishes policy for the USMLE and is composed of representatives of the Federation of State Medical Boards (FSMB), National Board of Medical Examiners (NBME), the Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG), and the American public.
         </p>
         <p>
          This multi-year project to review and revise the USMLE sequence is called the Comprehensive Review of USMLE (CRU).
          <a href="/cru/">
           Additional information about CRU
          </a>
          is available.
