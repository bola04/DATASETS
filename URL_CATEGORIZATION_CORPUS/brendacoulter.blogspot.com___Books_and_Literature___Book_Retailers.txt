                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://brendacoulter.blogspot.com/2013/08/this-golden-oldie-nrjw-post-was.html">
                      Crazy in love with Japanese anemones
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-816472582163004829" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      (This "golden oldie" NRJW post was originally published on August 20, 2009.)
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_gQyIlCDGmAY/So3Xu6b8QZI/AAAAAAAAAqI/ntfj0OxHVBE/s1600-h/Anemone+2.JPG">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5372187131483275666" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_gQyIlCDGmAY/So3Xu6b8QZI/AAAAAAAAAqI/ntfj0OxHVBE/s320/Anemone+2.JPG" style="float: left; height: 240px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; width: 320px;"/>
                     </a>
                     I wonder why Ogden Nash never wrote a poem about Japanese anemones? The words roll trippingly off the tongue and suggest all kinds of delicious rhymes:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Japanese anemones.
                      <br/>
                      See them dancing in the breeze.
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gQyIlCDGmAY/So3Xt10iwKI/AAAAAAAAAp4/Glxs7AYJXrg/s1600-h/J.+anemones.JPG">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5372187113064415394" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gQyIlCDGmAY/So3Xt10iwKI/AAAAAAAAAp4/Glxs7AYJXrg/s320/J.+anemones.JPG" style="float: left; height: 246px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; width: 320px;"/>
                     </a>
                     They're my favorite late-summer flowers. Their strong, graceful stems rise high over lovely mounds of foliage to offer up unscented, nearly translucent flowers featuring adorable green-ball centers surrounded by egg-yolk-yellow ruffs. I mean, just
                     <i>
                      look
                     </i>
                     at these beauties.
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gQyIlCDGmAY/So3XuRtAvtI/AAAAAAAAAqA/i_5GVny_7dk/s1600-h/Anemone+1.JPG">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5372187120549019346" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_gQyIlCDGmAY/So3XuRtAvtI/AAAAAAAAAqA/i_5GVny_7dk/s320/Anemone+1.JPG" style="float: left; height: 254px; margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; width: 320px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      I must plant some more of these
                      <br/>
                      Japanese anemones.
                      <br/>
                      The whites are charming, but I think
                      <br/>
                      I should also have some pink.
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     [So far, I haven't planted any pink ones because I have so many pink roses in the front garden. But my white anemones have grown and spread and this year (2013) they're more delightful than ever.]
