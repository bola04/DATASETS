        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Welcome to the Virginia Woolf Society of Great Britain
         </h1>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <h4 style="text-align: left;">
          <span style="color: #cc99ff;">
           <strong>
            Honorary President: Dame Eileen Atkins
           </strong>
          </span>
         </h4>
         <hr/>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           <strong>
            The Virginia Woolf Society of Great Britain is a non-profit organisation that aims to raise the profile of Virginia Woolf and promote the reading and discussion of her works.
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          Formed in June 1998 the Society is supported by an Executive Council of elected volunteers (read more about the Society’s
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/message/constitution/">
           Constitution
          </a>
          ). An Editorial Committee produces the
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/virginia-woolf-bulletin/">
           <em>
            Virginia Woolf Bulletin
           </em>
          </a>
          three times a year (January, May and September), as well as a number of
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/society-publications/">
           other publications
          </a>
          .
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/events/">
           Woolf-related events
          </a>
          and talks, open to members only, are held throughout the year.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          The Society welcomes new members. If you are interested in joining, you can print off the form from the
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/membership/">
           Membership
          </a>
          page and send it with your payment to the address provided. At the present time the Society is only able to accept payments in pounds sterling or US dollars.
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          On this website you will find
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/message/">
           information about the Society
          </a>
          and information about Virginia Woolf herself. Under
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/resources/">
           Virginia Woolf Resources
          </a>
          you will find a wealth of Woolf information, including a short biography, bibliographies, articles, illustrations and links to related sites. If you want to know more about Woolf, her works and the Society, the best place to start is with the links below. The
          <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/useful-links/">
           Useful Links
          </a>
          area provides a guide to websites of other Woolf societies, archives, publishers and bookshops.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/resources/virginia-woolf-a-short-biography/">
            Virginia Woolf: A Short Biography
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/resources/the-principal-works-of-virginia-woolf-1882-1941/">
            Principal Works of Virginia Woolf
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/resources/selected-reference-works-about-virginia-woolf/">
            About Virginia Woolf
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.virginiawoolfsociety.org.uk/membership/">
            Membership details
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
