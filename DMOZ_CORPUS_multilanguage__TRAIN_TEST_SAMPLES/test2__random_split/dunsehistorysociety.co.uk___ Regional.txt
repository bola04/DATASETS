                 <p>

                 <p align="left">
                  <font size="3">
                   Duns and its people have a long rich history. The Dunse History Society was formed in 1993
 					 to encourage people to take an interest in that history and to promote and preserve this for future generations.
                  </font>
                 </p>
                 <p align="left">
                  Throughout the winter months the Society holds monthly meetings to which all
                      with an interest in local
 					  history are welcome. The format of the meeting  can vary 
                      but usually there is a guest speaker with a
 					   particular  interest in some aspect or period of history. 
 					There is normally also a subsequent question and answer session  to discuss the topic of the evening and
 					thereafter a chance to meet with the speaker and to socialise  with other members over some light
 					refreshments.
                 </p>
                 <table>
                  <td width="150">
                   <img border="0" height="225" hspace="5" src="coatofarms2.png" title="Duns Coat of Arms" width="150"/>
                   <font size="1">
                   </font>
                   <center>
                    Duns Coat of Arms
                   </center>
                  </td>
                  <td width="600">
                   <p align="left">
                    Meetings are held in the Dunse Social  Club,
                   41 Newton Street, Duns (a few doors along from the library and almost 
 					 diagonally opposite the
                    <a class="blue" href="http://www.duns.bordernet.co.uk/local/clark.html">
                     Jim Clark Rooms
                    </a>
                    ).
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <p align="left">
                    Also over the summer months there are outings to places of historic interest  (almost invariably with an expert)
 					and often to places or buildings which are  not normally open to the public.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <p align="left">
                    Over the years the Society has built up a substantial
                    <a href="archive.shtml">
                     archive
                    </a>
                    of books, documents and photographs with a bearing on the history
  of the area which can be made available for consultation by members and others.
                   </p>
                  </td>
                 </table>
