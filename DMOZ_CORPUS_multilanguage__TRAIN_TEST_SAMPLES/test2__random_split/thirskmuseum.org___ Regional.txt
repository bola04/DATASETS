      <p class="bodytext homepage">
       Experience the history of Thirsk
          in the house where Thomas Lord, founder of Lords Cricket Ground, was born in 1755. Explore rooms full of exhibits
          featuring local life and industry, cricketing memorabilia, farming equipment, furniture, costumes and toys. Marvel
          at the bones of our Saxon Giant and shudder at the legend of the Busby Stoop Chair!
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Thirsk Museum is in Kirkgate, just
          across the road from The World of James Herriot in the market town of Thirsk, North Yorkshire. We are open to the public
          from Easter to early November. Admission is free.
      </p>
      <div style="clear:both; margin:0; padding:0">

        Monday to Saturday, 10am to 4pm
       </p>

      <h2>
       Information for visitors
      </h2>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-weight:bold">
       Admission
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px">
       Adults and children: Free (donations welcome).
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:25px; max-width:400px">
       Special rates apply to group visits involving guided tours, please contact us for details.
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-weight:bold">
       Opening times for 2018
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px">
       26th March till 3rd November,
       <br/>
       Monday to Saturday, 10am to 4pm
       <br/>
       (last admission 3.30pm).
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:25px">
       Closed Sunday.
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-weight:bold">
       Facilities for disabled visitors
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:25px; max-width:400px">
       There is a ramped entrance to the ground floor but
          narrow stairs limit access for wheelchair users. A virtual tour of the Museum on DVD
          can be viewed in the reception area. Guide dogs are welcome.
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-weight:bold">
       Group visits
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:25px; max-width:400px">
       Groups and school parties of up to 30 can
          be accommodated (both during and outside normal opening times) by prior arrangement. Guided tours are
          available on request.
      </p>
      <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-weight:bold">
       Where to find us

        YO7 1PQ

       <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-style:italic">
        From the clock tower in the Market Place:
       </p>
       <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:25px">
        Kirkgate is the road to your right as you look towards the Nat West bank. As you follow Kirkgate, the Museum
             is on your right, opposite the Zillah Bell Gallery.
       </p>
       <p class="bodytext" style="padding-bottom:10px; font-style:italic">
        From the car park in Marage Road:
