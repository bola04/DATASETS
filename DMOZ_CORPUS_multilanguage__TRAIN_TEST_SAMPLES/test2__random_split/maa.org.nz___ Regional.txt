        What does MAA do?
       </h1>

        <article class="post-248 page type-page status-publish has-post-thumbnail hentry" id="post-248">
         <div class="hentry-wrapper">
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div dir="ltr">
            <p style="text-align:justify;">
             <strong>
              <span style="color:#000000;">
               Established in Christchurch (NZ) in 1967,  Medical Aid Abroad is a not-for-profit charitable trust which assists health work in developing countries, by donating surplus medical, surgical and pharmaceutical
              </span>
             </strong>
             <span style="color:#000000;">
              <span style="color:#0000ff;">
               <a href="https://maa.org.nz/supplies/" style="color:#0000ff;">
                supplies
               </a>
              </span>
             </span>
             <strong>
              <span style="color:#000000;">
               , from within New Zealand.
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
            <span style="color:#000000;">
             MAA has one part-time paid Store Manager, plus a dedicated team of volunteer workers who mostly come from a medical or pharmacy background. Click here to learn more.
             <a href="https://maa.org.nz/about/" style="color:#000000;">
              <span style="color:#0000ff;">
               Who are we
              </span>
              ?
             </a>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
            <span style="color:#000000;">
             Goods donated to MAA come from a wide variety of sources, typically: medical centres, pharmacies, hospitals, manufacturers, importers, wholesalers. Click here to see
             <span style="color:#0000ff;">
              <a href="https://maa.org.nz/supplies/" style="color:#0000ff;">
               What goods
              </a>
             </span>
             ?  These goods are received in our store, sorted for suitability and stored until requested.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
            <span style="color:#000000;">
             The majority of our goods go the South Pacific region, but we have sent supplies to countries much further afield – Asia, Africa and the Middle East.
             <span style="color:#0000ff;">
              <span style="color:#000000;">
               Click
              </span>
              <a href="https://maaorgnz.files.wordpress.com/2018/07/2-column-recipient-list.pdf" rel="noopener" style="color:#0000ff;" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              <span style="color:#000000;">
               for a recent list of countries that have received aid.
              </span>
             </span>
            </span>
           </p>
           <div style="text-align:justify;">
            <dl id="attachment_678">
             <dt>
              <img alt="1-bougainville" class="" height="512" src="https://maaorgnz.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/1-bougainville.jpg?w=682&amp;h=512" width="682"/>
             </dt>
             <dd>
              <span style="color:#000000;">
               Bougainville. Checking drugs donated by MAA
              </span>
             </dd>
            </dl>
           </div>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
            <span style="color:#000000;">
             MAA is non-political and is willing to offer help wherever it is needed.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
            <span style="color:#000000;">
             <span style="color:#ff0000;">
              <strong>
               Two RNZ Interviews:
              </strong>
             </span>
             In
             <strong>
              April 2012
             </strong>
             , Justin Gregory, from Radio New Zealand’s Spectrum programme came to our rooms and interviewed our team on the work of MAA.  If you would like to hear this interesting 23 minute documentary, click on this:
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
            <span style="color:#000000;">
             <!--[if lt IE 9]><script>document.createElement('audio');</script><![endif]-->
             <audio class="wp-audio-shortcode" controls="controls" id="audio-248-1" preload="none" style="width: 100%;">
              <source src="https://maaorgnz.files.wordpress.com/2017/01/rnz-spectrum-1apr2012.mp3?_=1" type="audio/mpeg">
              </source>
              <a href="https://maaorgnz.files.wordpress.com/2017/01/rnz-spectrum-1apr2012.mp3">
               https://maaorgnz.files.wordpress.com/2017/01/rnz-spectrum-1apr2012.mp3
              </a>
             </audio>
             – or you could click on this link
            </span>
            :
            <span style="color:#0000ff;">
             <a href="http://www.radionz.co.nz/national/programmes/spectrum/audio/2514034/spectrum-for-1-april-2012" rel="noopener" style="color:#0000ff;" target="_blank">
              RNZ Spectrum Documentary on MAA
             </a>
            </span>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align:justify;">
