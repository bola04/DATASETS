      <div class="mainheads">
       Welcome to the Chamber
      </div>
      The purpose of the Chamber of Commerce shall be to create a more favorable atmosphere for local business in harmony with the quality and character of the community.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      The Board of Directors meets the second Thursday of each month at 7:00 p.m.  
   
   All members are welcome to attend. Business owners interested in joining the
   Chamber are also invited to attend.
      <div class="krierhead">
       In This Month's Krier
      </div>
      <div style="padding:8px 0 8px 0">
       <div style="font-weight:bold; font-size:14px; padding-bottom:10px;">
        Lions Awards Night
       </div>
       <div class="imglt">
        <img alt="Killingworth Lions" class="bd" height="166" src="images/news/2018/Lions-GROUP250.jpg" width="250"/>
       </div>
       On June 6th  the Killingworth Lions Club held it's annual dinner meeting to install officers and present awards and honors.  President Thomas Keyes passed the torch to incoming President, David Beaudette,  and two new members were initiated.  New members are Michael Remiers and Joe Celarno.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <strong>
        Student Achievement Awards:
       </strong>
       Scott Elliott (Xavier) and Michael Callis (HKHS) were chosen because of their community service -  Scott received his award for creating "1BigCircle", an organization designed to help those with special needs  build friendships through fun  physical and social activities. The organization also administers a Special Olympics program called Unified Sports Fitness Club. Michael, an Eagle Scout, was recognized for his leadership and community service, especially with his fellow scouts. For his Eagle Scout project, Michael organized and guided a work crew that reblazed over 36 miles of Cockaponsett trails.

      <div class="krierhead clear">
       President's Message
      </div>
      <div class="imgrt">
       <img alt="Franco M. Piscitelli " class="bd" src="images/Franco_Head.jpg"/>
      </div>
      <strong>
       Hello Killingworth!
      </strong>
      <br/>
      June was a busy month for the Chamber.   We had our first Summer Concert at Parmelee Farm on June 10th.  The Car Cruise preceded our concert with a nice turn out.  Attendees came to see all the great looking hot rods from old Ford Trucks to brand new Corvettes, many staying on to watch KJ  Reimensnyder-Wagner, a fan favorite performing at Parmelee for her second year.  My firm, Barnum Capital Management was proud to sponsor the event.  If you are a business looking to sponsor a concert, please reach out to discuss details.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      On June 12th, we presented our annual President's Award scholarship to HK Senior Katherine Porter.   Finally, June's Outback Pub hosted our Business After Hours on the official start of summer, June 21st.  Members enjoyed some of June's famous snacks and appetizers.  The pub is a much loved Killingworth staple and we thank June  for her support of the Chamber and the Community over the years.
       We've continued our discussions with the Middlesex Chamber regarding a merge.  We are still in the initial phases but we target a completion date of April 2019.  Your feedback is welcome.  We have only received support for this change.  All are in agreement that a merger that expands business opportunities for our members to new markets while keeping our identity is ideal.  We will keep you posted on developments and hope to have another open forum later in the year.  Please join us again for the Car Show and Concert on July 8th at Parmelee Farm.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Hope you're enjoying the summer months, see you around town!
      <br/>
      <br/>
      <strong>
       Franco Piscitelli
      </strong>
