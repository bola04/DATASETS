         <div style="position: relative; margin-top: -9;">
         </div>

          <font face="arial" style="font-size: 12;">
           NOTE: The following was condensed from the original. Click
           <a class="blue" href="http://www.resume.writing-toronto.org/overview" style="text-decoration: none; font-size: 11; letter-spacing: 1;">

          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font face="arial" size="2">
           Resume writing has changed a great deal over the years. When I started writing my own resumes in Toronto in 1971, such documents typically listed jobs applicants had held, without giving any indication of how well they had performed. The employer had no way of knowing whether the applicant was a good risk. There were no good books on resume writing. There were no Certified Professional Resume Writers. In fact, there was no resume writing association.

      <font face="arial" size="2">
       By 1989, when I founded Résu-Card
       <font size="2">
        <small>
        </small>
        <b>
         ®
        </b>
       </font>
       , the situation had improved somewhat. Torontonians had begun to realize that if they wanted to get hired, they needed to give employers a reason to hire them. People had to start selling themselves by distinguishing themselves favourably from other candidates. In 1990, the Professional Association of Resume Writers was established. The industry evolved from an amorphous collection of amateurs to a body of professionals with standards and a commitment to serve with integrity.
       <p>
        When I started this business, the field of resume writing was not well-known. Most job seekers in Toronto did not even consider engaging the services of a professional. But as the job market tightened, the resume industry grew. More and more writers started advertising under "Resume Service" in the Yellow Pages
        <small>
        </small>
        <b>
         ®
        </b>
        , and Toronto bookstores began to devote entire sections to literature on how to score every imaginable kind of job. Resume writers in Toronto started to offer interview coaching, and their offices became a one-stop shop for job acquisition.
       </p>
       <p>
        Then, in the mid-90s, something happened that was more revolutionary than anything I have described so far. Computer geeks who had been posting messages to each other remotely on what were called "bulletin boards" realized it was possible to develop a common language whereby computers could communicate with each other. And the Internet was born.
       </p>
       <p>
        This changed the entire resume industry.

           <li>
            Prospective clients sometimes ask, "What is your success rate?" They want statistics because they see them advertised by other resume writers.
            <ul>
             <font style="font-size: 2;">
              <br/>
              <br/>
             </font>
             <li>
              First, statistics are usually misleading because no time frame is attached thereto. Anybody can get a job without a resume, through networking, given an inordinate amount of time. The function of a good resume is to speed up the process by increasing the likelihood each application will result in an interview.
             </li>
             <li>
              Second, although one often sees statistics, one never sees
              <i>
               audited
              </i>
              statistics. Anybody can say he or she has a 99% success rate. If you don't need the audit, then you trust the writer. If you already trust the writer, the statistics are redundant.
              <font style="font-size: 4;">
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </font>
             </li>
             <li>
              We use a different approach. We consult hiring authorities regularly to determine what they prefer to see in a resume. As evidence, we have uploaded an
              <a href="http://www.resume.writing-toronto.org/job">
               article
              </a>
              identifying and quoting Toronto recruiters.
             </li>
             <li>
              About half of the people who hire us to write their resumes are, themselves, hiring authorities. For example, we wrote a resume for a
              <a href="http://ohsofast.net/tim">
               certified human resources professional
              </a>
              experienced in all phases of recruitment at all levels, including senior management. The above-linked document is not merely a sample, but the resume of an actual, named individual.

        <h1>
         Resume Website
        </h1>
        If you like, we can create a resume website for you, based on your word-processed or paper resume. This can be done regardless of whether you engage our services to write a resume for you.
        <p>
         Take a look at our original, attractive
         <a href="http://www.resume.writing-toronto.org/samples/silmi.html">
          web design
         </a>
         . This sample shows how we sell the services of a graduating student to an employer. You will find a resume of a Landscape Technician who attended Humber College of Applied Arts and Technology, Toronto.
        </p>
        <p>
         She forwarded our resume and letter to the City of Toronto, which responded favourably, and she was hired in her chosen field. This sample illustrates the power of a professionally crafted resume and cover letter.
        </p>
        <p>
         Experts recommend that job-seekers acquire business cards with name, address, and phone number, for networking. Now, you can add your own, unique web address. Employers instantly see you capitalize on Internet technology, and you have an edge over the competition.
        </p>
        <p>
         Keep your resume up-to-date on the web, and prospective employers always have access to the latest version. When you're on the phone with people who can refer, recommend, or hire you, they are able to access your resume website within seconds. The benefits of such a site are limited only by your imagination. In addition, you can order a hard copy of your resume that matches your site.

        Our philosophy is
        <b>
         minimalist:
        </b>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="#sell">
           Sell yourself
          </a>
          as quickly and concisely as possible.
          <ul>
           <li>
            Employers do not spend more time than necessary reviewing applicants' information.
           </li>
           <li>
            If you waste employers' time during the hiring process, they'll expect you to do it later. Needless to say, there won't be a "later."
           </li>
           <li>
            If you can't sell yourself within the first half page, you can't sell yourself.
            <p>
            </p>
           </li>
           <li>
            Give employers information they need, but don't have.
           </li>
           <li>
            <a href="#sell-compet">
             Distinguish
            </a>
            yourself favourably from your competition.
           </li>
           <li>
            Don't list tasks typically performed by the people
            <br/>
            employers hire. They already have that information  they hire them.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           <a href="#parwcc-logo">
            <img align="right" border="0" height="149" hspace="11" src="images/cprw.gif" title=" Certified Professional Resume Writer since 1998 " vspace="0" width="183"/>
           </a>
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>

         <h1>
          Resume As Advertisement
         </h1>
         There was a time when a list of requisite qualifications would guarantee you a job, let alone an interview. Not anymore. Prospective employers have no end of resumes and no end of qualified people to hire. If you want to get interviews, you will have to distinguish yourself from others. You will
 have to stand out. You will have to give the employer a reason to notice you  and a
 reason to believe that you are likely to be an outstanding employee.
         <p>
          Most resumes consist of boring job descriptions. Ours don't. Résu-Card
          <font style="font-size: 6;">
          </font>
          <b>
           ®
          </b>
          features the
          <i>
           competitive
          </i>
          resume, an advertisement designed to get the employer's attention and sell your services.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a name="sell">
          </a>
         </p>
         <hr class="9"/>
         <h1>
          How We Sell Your Services To An Employer
         </h1>
         We put ourselves in the employer's shoes and ask ourselves, if we were going to pay you money to work
 for us, what would we want in return? Not, what qualities do you have? Not, what are your
 skills? Not, what did you do in past jobs? But what benefit do we derive from having you on
 staff?
         <p>
          <a name="sell-compet">
          </a>
          In our
          <i>
           competitive
          </i>
          resume, we demonstrate your ability to deliver benefit by showing how you were useful to current or previous employers. Not only do we guarantee your satisfaction,
          <font color="brown">
           we guarantee that we will distinguish you favourably from your competition.
          </font>
          It's our job to identify your strongest achievements and attributes by asking the right questions. Most people possess favourably distinguishing qualities. But very few know what they are. People are not accustomed to thinking in this way. Consequently, when it comes time to tout themselves, sell themselves, they feel uncomfortable and can't do it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our job is to sell you when you can't sell yourself  and
 help you discover valuable things about yourself that will aid you immensely at an interview.
         </p>
         <p>
          Click to read about our
          <a href="http://www.resume.writing-toronto.org/fees/#packages">
           competitive
          </a>
          resume packages.
         </p>
