          <a href="http://www.smartaboutmoney.org/">
           SmartAboutMoney.org
          </a>
          will help you draw a spending map so you don't get lost. In four short steps identify income, list expenses, compare income and expenses, and set priorities and make changes -- you can make sure your day-to-day expenses do not distract you from your long-term spending goals. One of the 10 basic steps to getting smart about money.
         </p>
         <p align="left">
          <strong>
           Need help balancing your checkbook?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Use this
          <a href="http://www.mindyourfinances.com/calculators/balance-checkbook">
           Balance Your Checkbook
          </a>
          calculator.
         </p>
         <p align="left">
          <strong>
           Do you have any emergency savings?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Having adequate emergency savings can make unforseen unemployment, auto repairs, medical emergencies, property damage and even legal issues more manageable. This
          <a href="http://www.forefieldkt.com/kt_dt/dt_trns.aspx?xd=Emergency&amp;il=ai">
           Emergency Savings Calculator
          </a>
          helps you determine how much emergency savings you may need and how you can begin saving toward this important goal.
         </p>

          </a>
          <strong>
           HOW TO TALK WITH YOUR KIDS ABOUT MONEY
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Is there a tool that  will help students learn the complexities of investing?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Visit the
          <a href="http://www.smgww.org/">
           Stock Market Game™
          </a>
          .  The Stock Market Game™ gives students the  chance to invest a hypothetical $100,000 in an on-line portfolio. They think  they're playing a game, but you know they're learning economic and financial  concepts that they'll use for the rest of their lives.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How can I help my children get realistic about managing money ?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.jumpstart.org/reality-check.html">
            Reality Check
           </a>
          </em>
          , is a free and easy to use tool provided by the Jump Start Coalition for Personal Financial Literacy. The tool is designed to give you a  general snapshot of your financial future and is not a precise budgeting tool.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How do I get my  teenager to become more financially savvy?
          </strong>
          <br/>
          The
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.moneytalks.ucr.edu/">
            Money Talks
           </a>
          </em>
          Web site, from the University of California Cooperative Extension, offers  teens, and their teachers, a fun and interactive opportunity to learn about  money management. Teens can play games, take quizzes, watch videos, read or  download colorful teen guides, and more. The Web site is available in English  or Spanish.
         </p>

          <a href="http://www.federalreserve.gov/pubs/foreclosure/default.htm">
           Foreclosure  Resources for Consumers
          </a>
          .

 		  :
         </p>
         <p align="left">
          Make an extra payment on your credit card, spend down that debt.
          <br/>
          Purchase U.S. government savings bonds which can be purchased directly from the Treasury's web site such as
          <a href="http://www.treasurydirect.gov/indiv/products/prod_ibonds_glance.htm">
           I Savings Bonds
          </a>
          .
          <br/>
          Open a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA.
         </p>
         <p align="left">
          <strong>
           Benjamin Bankes says:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Do you ever wonder where your money goes each month? Does it seem like you're   never able to get ahead? If so, you may want to
          <a href="http://www.forefieldkt.com/kt/trns.aspx?xd=BP-CORE-02&amp;il=a2&amp;xsl=content">
           establish a budget
          </a>
          to help you   keep track of how you spend your money and help you reach your financial goals.
         </p>

          <a href="http://www.360financialliteracy.org/Topics/Budgeting-Spending/Budgeting-and-Saving/Getting-started-establishing-a-financial-safety-net/(language)/eng-US">
           financial safety net
          </a>
          in place can ensure that you're protected when a   financial emergency arises. One way to accomplish this is by setting up a cash   reserve, a pool of readily available funds that can help you meet emergency or   highly urgent short-term needs.
