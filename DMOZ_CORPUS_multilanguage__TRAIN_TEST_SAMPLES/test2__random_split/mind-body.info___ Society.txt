       One of the most significant general insights of modern science has been the "recognition that life experience can shape brain chemistry in significant ways, and that experience and neurophysiology form a seamless web. Contrary to the expectation that there would be strong causation from genes, through neurochemistry, to behavior, it turns out that gene expression itself is profoundly influenced by experience."
       <sup>

       </sup>
       Recent studies argue further that problems in information processing within neural networks, rather than changes in chemical balance, might underlie certain psychological disorders such as depression
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       or psychosis
       <sup>
        3,4
       </sup>
       . It seems that mental health as well as mental illness are more a matter of information, rather than of chemistry.

But if the power of imagination can end a healthy life, imaginative healing methods can just as well - under the proper conditions - rescue life from even a deadly (mental or physical) illness.

      <h2>
       Gary Bruno Schmid
      </h2>
      <p>
       Gary Bruno Schmid, Ph.D., is an independent Mind-Body Health Care Professional with a psychotherapy and hypnotherapy practice licensed in Zurich, Switzerland since 1988. He has clinical experience in virtually all psychological, psychiatric and psychosomatic disorders including pair therapy, psychosis therapy and hypnotherapeutic work to improve the immunological, hormonal, nervous and metabolic processes involved in infectious, immune and autoimmune illnesses including cancer and chronic pain. His hypnotherapeutic work is based upon several decades of scientific research and clinical experience that has led to two medical textbooks on psychogenic death (
       <em>
        Tod durch Vorstellungskraft - Das Geheimnis psychogener Todesfälle
       </em>
       (2009, Springer-Verlag, Wien)) and on psychogenic healing (
       <em>
        Selbstheilung durch Vorstellungskraft
       </em>
       (2010, Springer-Verlag, Wien)). He has also published a book (
       <em>
        Biunity (Îkilibirlik
       </em>
       <em>
        )
       </em>
       (2008, Agarta Yayinlari, Ankara)) comparing the work of the quantum physicist John von Neumann (1903-1957), the psychiatrist C.G. Jung (1875-1961), and the sufi love mystic Ibn ‘Arabî (1165-1240), and is presently working on a fourth book entitled
       <em>
        Psychogenic Reality - The World by Imagination
       </em>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Dr. Gary Bruno Schmid was awarded his doctorate 1977 with a dissertation in quantum physics and received a diploma in depth psychology as Analytical Psychologist 1988 from the C.G. Jung-Institute in Küsnacht, Switzerland. He is an accredited supervisor, trainer and practitioner in medical hypnosis, recognized by the Swiss Medical Society of Hypnosis SMSH (www.smsh.ch) since the mid 1990's. In addition to his numerous professional publications in atomic physics, chaos theory and didactics of physics, he has also published many professionally refereed articles on the theory and therapy of psychosis and schizophrenia in leading physics, psychiatry and psychotherapy journals. Gary Bruno Schmid is fluent in both English and German.
      </p>

      <p>
       Gary Bruno Schmid, Ph.D., Physiker, Tiefenpsychologe und Hypnotherapeut arbeitet seit 1988 in eigner Praxis in Zürich, Schweiz. Er hat klinische Erfahrung mit nahezu allen psychologischen, psychiatrischen und psychosomatischen Störungen einschliesslich Psychosentherapie und bietet auch Paartherapie an. Er arbeitet hypnotherapeutisch, um die immunologischen, hormonellen, Nerven-und Stoffwechselvorgänge bei Infektionen, Immun- und Autoimmunerkrankungen einschliesslich Krebs und chronischen Schmerzen zu verbessern.
      </p>
      <p>
       Seine hypnotherapeutische Arbeit basiert auf jahrzehntelanger wissenschaftlicher Forschung und klinischer Erfahrung; nachzulesen in zwei medizinischen Lehrbüchern über den psychogenen Tod und der psychogenen Heilung:
       <em>
        Tod durch Vorstellungskraft - Das Geheimnis psychogener Todesfälle
       </em>
       (2009, Springer-Verlag, Wien)) bzw.
       <em>
        Selbstheilung durch Vorstellungskraft

Jung (1875-1961) und des Sufi-Liebesmystikers Ibn 'Arabi (1165-1240). Derzeit arbeitet er an einem vierten Buch mit dem Titel
       <em>
        Psychogenic Reality - The World by Imagination
       </em>
       (Psychogene Realität - Die Welt durch Imagination).
