   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./galleries/rockets/index.html">
      Cape
 Canaveral Rocket And Missile Photo Gallery
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    A one-of-a-kind chronological photo gallery of every rocket and missile
 variant ever launched from Cape Canaveral. It took over two years to compile
 these photos in honor of the 50th anniversary of the first rocket launch
 from the Cape.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./rocketsum.html">
      Cape Canaveral Rocket
 And Missile Program Fact Sheets
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Includes fact sheets and brief historical summaries of every rocket
 and missile program ever flown from Cape Canaveral,
    <br/>
    with vehicle classification, dimensions, dates of first and last launch,
 number launched, operational specifications and more.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <a href="./statistics/Cape_Canaveral_Launch_Chronology.html">
     <strong>
      Cape Canaveral Launch Chronology
     </strong>
    </a>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Spaceline, Inc. has completed an exclusive Cape Canaveral Launch Chronology
 for over 3,000 launches from July 24, 1950
    <br/>
    to the present, including launch dates, vehicle types, launch sites,
 payloads and flight results where known.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./statistics/Cape_Canaveral_Launch_Vehicles_Box_Score.html">
      Cape Canaveral Launch Vehicles Box Score
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Includes a complete list of every rocket and missile variant launched
 from Cape Canaveral presented in an easy-to-read
    <br/>
    table format, including vehicle type, dates of first and last launch,
 number launched, sponsors and vehicle type.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <font color="#FF0000">
      ***** CAPE CANAVERAL LAUNCH FACILITIES
 *****
     </font>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./launchsites/launchsiteindex.html">
      Cape Canaveral
 Launch Facilities Fact Sheets
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Includes fact sheets for every Cape Canaveral launch site, including
 launch pads, silos, other land sites, aircraft, ships and submarines. Dates
 of service, types of vehicles launched and a brief description of each
 launch site is featured.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./statistics/Cape_Canaveral_Launch_Sites_Box_Score.html">
      Cape_Canaveral_Launch_Sites_Box_Score
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Includes a complete listing of every Cape Canaveral launch site, listed
 in numerical or alphabetical order, presented
    <br/>
    in an easy-to-read table format. Features dates of service plus types
 and number of vehicles launched.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <font color="#FF0000">
      ***** HISTORY OF CAPE CANAVERAL *****
     </font>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./capehistory.html">
      History Of Cape Canaveral
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Tells the story of how 15,000 acres of sand and scrub became America's
 gateway to space. Includes an easy-to-read narrative explaining how Cape
 Canaveral was discovered, settled and developed as a missile test range
 and spaceport.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./lighthouse.html">
      History Of The Cape
 Canaveral Lighthouse
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Dedicated to one of the richest historical treasures in the State of
 Florida, this feature provides a brief account of the history of the Cape
 Canaveral Lighthouse, explaining why it was constructed in 1868 and how
 it has survived nearly intact to the present.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./rockethistory.html">
      History Of Rocketry
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    This feature presents an easy-to-read overview of the general development
 of rocketry through the ages, from Chinese fire arrows to the work of rocket
 pioneers Konstantin Tsiolkovsky, Robert Goddard, Hermann Oberth and Wernher
 von Braun that led to the rockets and missiles that spurred the development
 of Cape Canaveral as a missile test range and spaceport.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./bumper.html">
      Spaceline Commemorates
 The First Rocket Launch From Cape Canaveral
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Pieced together in honor of the 50th anniversary of the first rocket
 launch from Cape Canaveral, this feature provides a factual account and
 photos of the launch of Bumper #8, serving most of all to clear up many
 of the myths surrounding this launch.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./explorerchron.html">
      Chronology Of The
 Launch Of Explorer I - The First U.S. Satellite
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Originally prepared as an outline for a book commemorating the 40th
 anniversary of the launch of Explorer I, this feature provides an excellent
 overview of the major developments leading to the "space race" and the
 launch of the first U.S. satellite.
   </p>

   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./challenger.html">
      The Challenger Legacy
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Provides a detailed analysis of what caused the Challenger tragedy and
 what improvements were made to the Space Shuttle program as a result. Included
 is an exclusive eyewitness account of the tragedy written by Spaceline
 Founder and President Cliff Lethbridge, as well as a rarely seen list of
 Space Shuttle missions originally scheduled to follow mission STS-51L.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./rocketsum/shuttle-program.html">
      History
 Of The Space Shuttle Program
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    This is a detailed narrative of how the Space Shuttle program was conceived
 and developed, including information on early concepts for the vehicle
 that were never incorporated, such as a piloted fly-back liquid fueled
 booster engine.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./rocketsum/space-shuttle.html">
      Orbiter
 Fact Sheets And How The Space Shuttle Works
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    Includes brief fact sheets for Space Shuttle Enterprise, Columbia, Challenger,
 Discovery, Atlantis and Endeavour, plus a detailed description of the Space
 Shuttle integrated vehicle, its components, systems and operational characteristics.
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <b>
     <a href="./astronaut.html">
      U.S. Astronaut Selection
 Drafts And Qualifications
     </a>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    A complete list of U.S. astronaut drafts from the Mercury 7 through
 today, including astronaut qualifications for each draft. Also included
 is an account of the "Mercury 13" testing of female astronaut candidates
 and rarely seen lists of Air Force astronaut drafts for the X-20 "Dyna-Soar"
 and Manned Orbiting Laboratory, and names of the X-15 astronauts.
