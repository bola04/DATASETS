        <p>
         <strong>
          About MARSHALL COUNTY, ALABAMA
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p class="uk-text-left">
         Our great hotels, restaurants, and shopping make it a great vacation for everyone in the family. Experience arts, music, and community fun at area attractions and annual events. Interested in history? Marshall County has historical sites and many historical attractions.
        </p>
        <p class="uk-text-left">
         Discover
         <a href="/where-to-eat">
          local cuisine and enjoy the delicious food
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.julesjbertavineyards.com/">
          wine from our local winery and vineyard
         </a>
         . Visit Marshall County if you want to be in the great outdoors and hike, go camping, stay in a cabin, or go bass fishing.
        </p>
        <p class="uk-text-left">
         More about our
         <a href="/what-to-do/state-parks">
          state parks
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <p class="uk-text-left">
         One of the main attractions of
         <a href="http://www.alapark.com/lake-guntersville-state-park">
          Lake Guntersville State Park
         </a>
         soars high above all the others. Thanks to conservation efforts Alabama's nesting bald eagle population is increasing, and Guntersville has become a focal point of Eagle Awareness in the state. If you're a thrill-seeker, Lake Guntersville has a zip line called the Screaming Eagle.
        </p>
        <p class="uk-text-left">
         <a href="http://www.alapark.com/cathedral-caverns-state-park">
          Cathedral Caverns State Park
         </a>
         is an excellent field trip for school, church or other groups because the cave maintains a comfortable 60 degrees temperature year round! The park itself encompasses 493 acres, and there are marked hiking trails available for those wanting to enjoy the outdoors.
        </p>
        <p class="uk-text-left">
         Looking for spectacular bass fishing?
         <a href="http://www.alapark.com/bucks-pocket-state-park">
          Buck's Pocket State Park
         </a>
         is secluded in a natural pocket of the Appalachian Mountain foothills, the pocket itself is on an upstream tributary of Lake Guntersville. Seven miles downstream at Morgan’s Cove is a boat launch and fishing area on Lake Guntersville, noted for bass fishing.
        </p>
