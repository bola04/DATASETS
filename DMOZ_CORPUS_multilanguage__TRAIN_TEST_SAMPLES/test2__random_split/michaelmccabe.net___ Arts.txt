       <p>
        An independent, four-time Olivier Award-winning theatre producer in the UK and Tony® nominated on Broadway,
        <span class="show">
         Michael McCabe
        </span>
        has, since 2006, been represented by
        <span class="show">
         Wicked
        </span>
        (Olivier Audience Award, 2015 and 2010) at London's Apollo Victoria Theatre (where it is already the 15th longest running show in West End theatre history and the 7th longest running show currently playing in the West End). He is also currently represented by the show's 2018 UK &amp; Ireland Tour.
       </p>
       <p>
        Previous producing credits include:
        <span class="show">
         An American In Paris
        </span>
        (Olivier Award nomination, Best Musical), directed and choreographed by Christopher Wheeldon; the 2013-2017 UK/International Tour of
        <span class="show">
         Wicked
        </span>
        , directed by Joe Mantello;
        <span class="show">
         Sweeney Todd
        </span>
        (Olivier Award, Best Musical Revival), starring Michael Ball and Imelda Staunton and directed by Jonathan Kent; the 2011 West End production of
        <span class="show">
         Million Dollar Quartet
        </span>
        , directed by Eric Schaeffer with musical arrangements and supervision by Chuck Mead;
        <span class="show">
         Spring Awakening
        </span>
        (Olivier Award, Best Musical), starring Olivier Award-winners Aneurin Barnard and Iwan Rheon and directed by Michael Mayer, and, on Broadway, Rob Ashford's revivals of
        <span class="show">
         How To Succeed In Business Without Really Trying
        </span>
        (Tony® nomination, Best Musical Revival), starring Daniel Radcliffe, and
        <span class="show">
         Promises, Promises
        </span>
        , starring Sean Hayes and Kristin Chenoweth; the new musical
        <span class="show">
         Lysistrata Jones
        </span>
        , starring Patti Murin and Josh Segarra and directed and choreographed by Dan Knechtges and, and
        <span class="show">
         On a Clear Day You Can See Forever
        </span>
        , starring Harry Connick Jr. and Jessie Mueller and directed by Michael Mayer.
       </p>
       <p>
        In a career spanning 35 years, Michael has also become one of the theatre industry's most highly regarded marketing practitioners and was the original Marketing Director for Benny Andersson and Björn Ulvaeus'
        <span class="show">
         Mamma Mia!
        </span>
        (overseeing the first 14 international premieres).
       </p>
