                      <a href="http://tattoosday.blogspot.com/2018/07/dylan-and-black-pearl.html">
                       Dylan and the Black Pearl
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4496724884121551461" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       At the NY Empire State Tattoo Expo (
                       <a href="https://www.instagram.com/nyempirestatetattooexpo/?hl=en">
                        @nyempirestatetattooexpo
                       </a>
                       ) a couple weekends back, I met Dylan, who shared this amazing tattoo:
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-jh5kZPxGXas/W1STt2SLbsI/AAAAAAAA86A/C3Gv5WqZZkk8DIVNNOt0lIdDhb4zD8raACLcBGAs/s1600/empire%2Bpearl.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="585" data-original-width="282" height="640" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-jh5kZPxGXas/W1STt2SLbsI/AAAAAAAA86A/C3Gv5WqZZkk8DIVNNOt0lIdDhb4zD8raACLcBGAs/s640/empire%2Bpearl.jpg" width="308"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Dylan credited this work to
                      <a href="https://paultochluk.com/">
                       Paul Tochluk
                      </a>
                      (
                      <a href="https://www.instagram.com/tochlukdesigns/?hl=en">
                       @tochlukdesigns
                      </a>
                      ) at
                      <a href="https://abyssartstudio.com/">
                       The Abyss Fine Art and Tattoo Studio
                      </a>
                      (
                      <a href="https://www.instagram.com/abyssartstudiolbny/?hl=en">
                       @abyssartstudiolbny
                      </a>
                      ).
                      <br/>

                      This amazing black and gray work is a nod to pirates, as the ship at the top of the design is the
                      <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Pearl">
                       Black Pearl
                      </a>

                      <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Sparrow">

                     <meta content="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/--4tNTS0a5Nc/Wx1vgDK0ZUI/AAAAAAAA8G8/pCJhT9hjRFMivwlHBGuNDvmN_NibajdTwCLcBGAs/s640/6%2Bangela%2Bback.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://tattoosday.blogspot.com/2018/07/angela-peacock-and-sisterly-feathers.html">
                       Angela, a Peacock and Sisterly Feathers
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4173286696578858751" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Today is my sister Alicia's birthday and, although she doesn't have any tattoos, she loves peacocks, and this story involves a sister, so it seemed an appropriate post for the occasion.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Back in May, I was in Herndon, Virginia, for a work training and I was hanging out with one of my colleagues, Angela, who has lots of ink, including this lovely peacock and pair of butterflies on her back:
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/--4tNTS0a5Nc/Wx1vgDK0ZUI/AAAAAAAA8G8/pCJhT9hjRFMivwlHBGuNDvmN_NibajdTwCLcBGAs/s1600/6%2Bangela%2Bback.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="647" data-original-width="863" height="478" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/--4tNTS0a5Nc/Wx1vgDK0ZUI/AAAAAAAA8G8/pCJhT9hjRFMivwlHBGuNDvmN_NibajdTwCLcBGAs/s640/6%2Bangela%2Bback.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Angela credited Daniel Baker (
                       <a href="https://www.instagram.com/danielbakertattoos/?hl=en">
                        @danielbakertattoos
                       </a>
                       ) from
                       <a href="https://www.facebook.com/tattoorolo/">
                        Tattoorolo
                       </a>
                       in Corning, New York, with this lovely work.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       She also has the lovely feather on her leg:
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gb16RTG_-Hg/Wx1vgOHs69I/AAAAAAAA8G4/tMCJ26k_qDYxim44UoYzBuXuctWiNN47ACLcBGAs/s1600/6%2Bangela%2Bfeather.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="647" data-original-width="863" height="478" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gb16RTG_-Hg/Wx1vgOHs69I/AAAAAAAA8G4/tMCJ26k_qDYxim44UoYzBuXuctWiNN47ACLcBGAs/s640/6%2Bangela%2Bfeather.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Angela explained first about the feather, which she got inked up n Rochester:
                      <br/>
                      <blockquote class="tr_bq">
                       "My sister and I were getting matching tattoos and ... we went with a feather idea and she didn't care what feather and I said
                       <i>
                        well I'd really like just a peacock feather.
                       </i>
                       Since I got the peacock tattoo on my back ... people are inundating me with peacock stuff, family and friends ... I like it. So I was like
                       <i>
                        I just want an individual feather
                       </i>
                       .                       <a href="http://www.profetink.com/our-services.html">
                       Zach Smith
                      </a>
                      (
                      <a href="https://www.instagram.com/profet_ink/">
                       @profet_ink
                      </a>

                      <a href="http://www.profetink.com/">

                      </a>

                      <a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCEjRg3R3gTgqXKx5iM-XCzg">

                      </a>

                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I like the combination/juxtaposition of the skull, the rose, and the Roman numerals of time. It's just a really cool design and masterfully executed.

                       Remembering Chester
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1318447755016512529" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chester_Bennington">
                        Chester Bennington
                       </a>
                       (
                       <a href="https://www.instagram.com/chesterbe/?hl=en">
                        @chesterbe
                       </a>
                       ) from
                       <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linkin_Park">
                        Linkin Park
                       </a>
                       (
                       <a href="https://www.instagram.com/linkinpark/?hl=en">
                        @linkinpark
                       </a>
                       ) died a year ago today.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Earlier this month, I met Nicole at the Summer Vibe Tattoo Convention on Long Island and she shared this fascinating dot-work tribute to the singer:
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-upJrppDkg0k/W0Ixnubo_wI/AAAAAAAA8qg/HRS8DW53RzIHwHzz2Y2iWQCDVRay9G9LgCEwYBhgL/s1600/li%2B-%2Bchester.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="646" data-original-width="404" height="640" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-upJrppDkg0k/W0Ixnubo_wI/AAAAAAAA8qg/HRS8DW53RzIHwHzz2Y2iWQCDVRay9G9LgCEwYBhgL/s640/li%2B-%2Bchester.jpg" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Nicole was a huge fan of Linkin Park and Bennington and got this tattoo the night after he died. It's a huge thigh piece that appears differently, depending on the angle you;re viewing it. She credited the artist Ilya Cascad (
                      <a href="https://www.instagram.com/ilyacascad/?hl=en">
                       @ilyacascad
                      </a>
                      ) with the work, noting it was seven hours in the making.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Thanks to Nicole for sharing this amazng tribute with us here on Tattoosday!
