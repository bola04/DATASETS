       <div class="grid_10 alpha padRT">
        <h2 class="titlepad">
         30 years developing Deep Water Technology
         <br/>
         100 Millions lettuce heads produced
        </h2>
       </div>
       <div class="grid_15">
        <p align="justify" class="padbot">
         We are proud and pleased to see the 
           interest raised nowadays by Deep Water Technology (DWT). While producing 
           all those lettuces over that period of time we kept improving the system 
           and adapting it to different species under a wide variety of weather 
           conditions. We are offering you the opportunity to benefit from this 
           cumulated experience and expertise right from project design and throughout 
           the entire construction / commissioning process leading to a no-fault 
           production start-up.
        </p>
        <div class="lineH">
        </div>
        <h2 class="titlepad2">
         Our Expertise
        </h2>
        <div class="wrapper">
         <div class="grid_5 alpha padRT">
          <span class="img-box">
           <img alt="" src="2014/images/1page_img1.jpg"/>
          </span>
          <h5 class="color1 titlepad3">
           <font color="#006600">
            HYDROPONICS LETTUCES
           </font>
          </h5>
          <p align="left" class="padright2 padbot2">
           Our original expertise and 
               still our core business, the production of greenhouse lettuces has 
               developed steadily over the last 30 years. While Butterhead remains 
               the best lettuce type for production and market acceptance, constant 
               research is now bringing up the possibility to also produce a wider 
               base of lettuce types including Romaine COS, Curly, Lolo Biondo 
               and Lolo Rosso, Batavia, Oak Leaf green and red, as well as Leaf 
               green and Red. Average production cycles per year: 18
          </p>
          <a class="button1" href="2014/lettuce.html">
           more
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="grid_5 padRT">
          <span class="img-box">
           <img alt="" src="2014/images/1page_img2.jpg"/>
          </span>
          <h5 class="color1 titlepad3">
           HYDROPONICS BABY LEAFS
          </h5>
          <p align="left" class="padright2 padbot2">
           The challenge was to blend 
               the easiness and productivity of hydroponic Floating Rafts with 
               the seeding flexibility of soil direct seeding systems. It took 
               several years of trials to come up with a revolutionary boards and 
               highly mechanised system developed in cooperation with Italian growers 
               and researchers. Automation help limiting to 5 workers / Ha for 
               seeding, germination, irrigation, growing-up, harvesting &amp; boards 
               wash-up. Average production cycles per year: 20
          </p>
          <a class="button1" href="2014/hdbabyleafs.html">
           more
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="grid_5 omega">
          <span class="img-box">
           <img alt="" src="2014/images/1page_img3.jpg"/>
          </span>
          <h5 class="color1 titlepad3">
           <font color="#0066CC">
            AQUAPONICS
           </font>
          </h5>
          <p align="left" class="padright2 padbot2">
           Aquaponic is describing 
               a system where fish and vegetables grow in symbiosis, sharing same 
               water, and more or less feeding each other. HydroNov has developed 
               a different approach where water systems are kept separated, the 
               link between the 2 systems being made through a bio-digester, which 
               is producing plants food from fish wastes, opening the possibility 
               for a commercial organic hydroponic production. The system of the 
               future, available today.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <a class="button1" href="2014/aquaponics.html">
           more
          </a>
