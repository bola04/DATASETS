       <h1>
        Integration celebration
        <br/>
        is Saturday at Hoxie
       </h1>
       The 63rd anniversary of the Hoxie School integration and the 15th anniversary of the "Hoxie: The First Stand" documentary will be marked on Saturday from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. at Hoxie's Mustang Gymnasium.
       <p>
        On July 11, 1955, history was made in Hoxie, when the school voluntarily integrated by a unanimous decision of the school board.
       </p>
       <p>
        "The event set many legal precedents and is worthy of inclusion into historical context," Ethel Tompkins, a member of the Hoxie: The First Stand Committee and the first African-American graduate of HHS, said.
        <br/>
        <a href="freepages/2018-07-25/news/story1.php">
         continued...
        </a>
       </p>

       <h2>
        Mural graces downtown Walnut Ridge building
       </h2>
       <p>
       </p>
       <table align="right" border="0" class="picture" width="470">
        <tr>
         <td valign="top">
          <img src="freepages/2018-07-25/news/story2.jpg"/>
          Lily Watson paints a blue panel in a geometric mural designed by her husband, Stephen Watson. The mural was commissioned by Jon and Lesa Walter and is on the side of their building at 110 Northeast Front Street in Walnut Ridge.
          <br/>
          <i>
           Submitted Photo ~ Bill Jeffery
          </i>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
       <p>
        A new mural brightens up the side of Jon and Lesa Walter's building, located at 110 Northeast Front Street in Walnut Ridge.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lawrence County native Stephen Watson was commissioned by the couple to do the mural after Jon saw a similar mural online.
       </p>
       <p>
        "Stephen was commissioned to do one in Louisville the week prior," Jon said. "I saw it online, so I messaged him and asked if he could possibly do one here. He said it just happened he was in town, and he could do it that week."
        <br/>
        <a href="freepages/2018-07-25/news/story2.php">

       <h2>
        Interviews for Intermodal strategic plan underway
       </h2>
       Boyette Strategic Advisors completed their first round of interviews for the Northeast Arkansas Regional Intermodal Facilities Authority's strategic planning process July 10 and 11.
       <p>
        During the interviews, they met with several business and educational leaders, as well as elected officials in both Randolph and Lawrence counties.
        <br/>
        <a href="freepages/2018-07-25/news/story3.php">
         continued...
        </a>
       </p>

       <h2>
        Airport to move forward on rehab project
       </h2>
       Airport Manager Stacy Hoggard informed the Walnut Ridge Regional Airport Commission that additional supplemental funds have been made available through the Federal Aviation Administration.
       <p>
        During Monday night's meeting, Hoggard said that the Walnut Ridge airport is one of the few selected to receive these funds and proposed to move forward on a rehabilitation project originally slated for 2019.
        <br/>
        <a href="freepages/2018-07-25/news/story4.php">
         continued...
        </a>

       <h2>
        Wings of Honor receives grant
       </h2>
       The Wings of Honor Museum received a $2,500 grant from the Department of Arkansas Heritage, which will be used on the building's utilities.
       <p>
        "It couldn't come at a better time of the year because the utilities for the building get high during the summertime," president of the museum board Harold Johnson said. "It will give us money to put into more exhibits."
        <br/>
        <a href="freepages/2018-07-25/news/story5.php">
         continued...
        </a>

       <h2>
        Joint County-City effort improves Fulbright Ave.
       </h2>
       The main road into the Williams Baptist University campus has new shoulders and a new look, thanks to a cooperative effort by Lawrence County and the City of Walnut Ridge.
       <p>
        The project resulted in gravel shoulders on Fulbright Avenue from Hwy. 67 to the entrance of WBU, a distance of 7/10 of a mile.
