       Summer Reading Begins!
      </h1>
      <figure class="centered">
       <img alt="Summer Reading" src="https://bcpl.readsquared.com/images/Org/33/RP/Banners/266.png" style="border: 1px solid #ccc; padding: .25em; width: 98.5%;"/>
       <!--<figcaption style="font-size: 90%; font-weight: bold; font-style: italic;">Image (c) <a href="https://www.cincyplay.com/productions/rapunzel">Cincinnati Playhouse in the Park</a></figcaption>-->
      </figure>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Where:
       </strong>
       Boone County
       <br/>
       <strong>
        When:
       </strong>
       From June 1 to July 31
      </p>
      <p style="font-size: 135%;">
       <strong>
        <a href="https://bcpl.readsquared.com/">
         Register today or start logging if you've already registered!
        </a>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Boone County Summer Reading is a collaboration between Boone County Public Library, Boone County Schools, and Walton Verona Schools to promote literacy. For more information about program details check our
       <a href="/docs/summer-reading-2018.pdf">
        Summer Reading brochure
       </a>
       and watch our
       <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S_2-TZRtbeM">
        Video
       </a>
       !
      </p>
      <p>
       We offer three programs: 1) Preschool Program (birth to four years), 2) Youth Program (Kindergarten to 12th Grade), and 3) Adult Program. Join the one for you and start logging today!
      </p>

      <h1>
       More Than Big Bones
      </h1>
      <h2>
       12,000 Years of Human Life at Big Bone
      </h2>
      <div class="half-page">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Where:
        </strong>
        Scheben Branch
        <br/>
        <strong>
         When:
        </strong>
        June 28 at 7 p.m.
       </p>
       <p>
        Archaeologist Jeannine Kreinbrink will explore the history of Big Bone Lick State Historic Site. Humans arrived in the Big Bone Valley as early as 12,000 years ago. Learn about the Native Americans who lived and visited the area from Paleo to historic times.
       </p>
       <p>
        Bring your Native American artifacts as archaeologists will be present to identify them before and after the program.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Sponsored by Friends of Big Bone
        </strong>
        .
       </p>
      </div>
