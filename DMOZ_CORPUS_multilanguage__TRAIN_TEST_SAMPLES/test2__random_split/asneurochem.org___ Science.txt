The Brain Bee is a neuroscience<br/>competition for young students, 13 to 19 years of age. It was hosted by the American Psychological Association. The American Society for Neurochemistry also provides a<br/>$1,000 check and copy of the Basic Neurochemistry Textbook to the winner.<br/><br/><img alt="Elwin Brain Bee Winner" height="203" src="/images/meetings/2017/images/Elwin_Brain_Bee_Winner.png" style="margin: 5px 15px; float: left;" width="202"/><strong>IBBC 3rd Place and ASN Prize Winner: Elwin Raj</strong>
 <p>Elwin Raj, a student of Sekolah Menengah Kebangsaan (SMK) Seri Bintang Utara, Kuala Lumpur, did Malaysia proud when he secured <br/>third place in the International Brain Bee Championship (IBBC) 2017, which ended in Washington, D.C., yesterday.<br/><br/>Elwin, who was the champion of the national-level Neuroscience Competition, 'Malaysia Brain Bee Challenge' (MBBC) 2017, last July, <br/>was the country's sole representative, Joining 24 other champions from their respective countries for the five-day competition which <br/>ran from August 2 to 6, Harian Metro reported. Elwin, speaking to the press from Washington, said he did not expect to make the cut <br/>to the top three after being initially announced as the top five, midway throughout the competition.<br/>To be honest, I didnt set any targets. I was just motivated to help Malaysia be placed as among the nations which is also developed <br/>in the field of neuroscience. I also wanted to learn from other contestants and gain experience, as well as explore possible career <br/>opportunities in this field for the future. The other competitors provided a very stiff challenge, as they are also champions in their <br/>respective countries, he said.<br/>The third place win means Elwin brings home US$1,000 (RM4,281), an opportunity to undergo industrial training in the field of neuroscience at the University of Maryland, <br/>Baltimore, a trophy, a plaque and certificate.<br/><br/>The first and second spots went to Sojas Wagle, 15, from Arkansas, and Milena Malcharek from Poland, respectively.<br/><br/>Elaborating further, Elwin, who is the youngest of three, said he was grateful to god and both his parents, K Kunavathi, 57 and Paul Raj, 65, who worked tirelessly to encourage <br/>him to participate in the IBBC and MBB. "My deepest gratitude also to the Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM) Neuroscience Research Centre director Prof Datuk Dr Jafri Malin <br/>Abdullah, as well as USM medical lecturer and MBBC 2017 project leader, Prof Madya Dr Muzaimi Mustapha. I am also thankful to my mentors who helped me prepare for this <br/>competition, teachers, friends, parent-teacher association and family members who prayed for my success, he said.<br/><br/>IBBC 2017, organised by the American Psychology Association (APA), is geared towards motivating students aged between 13 to 19 to study the brain, and inspire them towards <br/>a career in basic and clinical neuroscience. Students are tested on knowledge of the human brain, covering subjects such as activity, emotion, memory, sleep, sight, hearing,<br/>Alzheimers disease, Parkinsons disease, schizophrenia, addiction and brain research. The competition involved oral tests, neuroanatomy laboratory examinations using actual<br/>human brains, neurohistologi examinations, and component diagnosis of patients.</p>
