        <h3>
         Our Beginnings
        </h3>
        <p>
         The idea of a baker's supply cooperative came from Frances E. Quigley. After serving in World War II, Fran became a bakery supply salesman in Neenah, WI.  In an effort to control rising costs, Quigley suggested that area bakers combine their purchasing power in a cooperative.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section>
        <div class="timeline-image" style="background-image: url(http://www.valleybakers.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/taking-root-image.png)">

         Taking Root
        </h3>
        <p>
         The idea took off and the co-op began a steady climb. VBCA has been offering a cost-effective method for members to buy supplies and stay competitive ever since.
        </p>
       </section>
       <section>
        <div class="timeline-image" style="background-image: url(http://www.valleybakers.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/service.png)">

         A RECORD OF SERVICE
        </h3>
        <p>
         VBCA membership has grown impressively over the years. Members include retail and wholesale bakeries, supermarket bakeries, donut shops, pizza restaurants, candy makers, institutional food service companies and others.
        </p>
