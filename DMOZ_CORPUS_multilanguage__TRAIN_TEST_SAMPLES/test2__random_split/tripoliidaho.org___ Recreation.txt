           <h2>
            Launch Report for June 2nd
           </h2>
           <dl class="article-info">
            <dt class="article-info-term">
             Details
            </dt>
            <dd class="modified">
             Last Updated: Sunday, 10 June 2018 17:07
            </dd>
           </dl>
           <div>
            <span style="color: #222222; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13px;">
             Thanks to all who attended and helped setup and tear down the launch equipment. Your help is greatly appreciated!
            </span>
            <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; color: #222222; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 13px;">
             Of course, thank you to Dave and Rick for getting the wavier setup and taken down.
             <br/>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              I also think we should take a minute to thank the rocket gods for allowing us to launch one more time before the dry/fire season. Yesterday was beautiful!
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              For those who did not attend it was clear skies and wind gusts where no more than 10mph ever! Best launch to end the season!
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              Launch stats:
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              Low Power - 9
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              H - 2
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              I - 4
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              J - 2
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              K - 2
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              We had two hybrid launches. One from TJ and one from Kelsey. Both successfully lit and got off the pad but had some unexpected mid flight events but all equipment was unscathed. yay!
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              We had 2 L1 attempts. Brennan and Tyler I believe. I look forward to seeing you in October ;)
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
             </div>
             <div style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px;">
              We had a couple new junior flyers. Welcome Alex and Katelyn. Good job with your successful flights. Look out HPR flyers, so far they have a better successful flight to failure ratio than us ;)
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div>
           </div>

           <h2>
            Next TIR Launch
           </h2>
           <dl class="article-info">
            <dt class="article-info-term">
             Details
            </dt>
            <dd class="modified">
             Last Updated: Saturday, 12 May 2018 13:55
            </dd>
           </dl>
           <p style="text-align: justify;">
            The next scheduled launch of the TIR 2018 spring launch season is
            <strong>
             Saturday, June 2
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Equipment set-up at 9:00a.m. with the 40,000' AGL waiver activated from 10am until 6pm or until there are no more rockets flying.  Port-a-potties will be on site.  Spectators are welcome to attend, but are forewarned that high-power rocketry is an inherently dangerous activity and observers assume all risk.
            <b>
             Please, no pets allowed, per BLM rules.
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Insured non-members*
            </b>
            are welcome to fly with us.  Those over 18 may pay a $5 launch fee (under 18 always fly for free).  If you are interested in flying rockets at our launches, but are not insured, please contact an officer of TIR and we can discuss your options for becoming an insured flyer.
           </p>
           <p>
            *All members of Tripoli and members of the National Association of Rocketry (NAR) are insured.
            <b>
             Boy Scouts as part of a scout activity, TARC teams, and sanctioned school groups are covered by Tripoli insurance.
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Rocketry is an inherently dangerous activity.  All participants are required to read and sign a
            </b>
            <strong>
             <a href="/images/stories/Release_and_Waiver_of_Liability.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
              release and waiver of liability
             </a>
            </strong>
            <b>
             .
            </b>
           </p>
