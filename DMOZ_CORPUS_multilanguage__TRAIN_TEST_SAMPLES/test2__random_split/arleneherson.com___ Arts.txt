     <p>
      <strong>
       Meet Arlene Herson.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Arlene has had a remarkable career
      <strong>
       interviewing celebrities
      </strong>
      on television on the award winning,
      <strong>
       <em>
        <a href="tvshow">
         The 
 		Arlene Herson Show
        </a>
       </em>
      </strong>
      , and on the radio on
      <strong>
       <em>
        <a href="radioshow">
         Florida Forum
        </a>
       </em>
      </strong>
      . Arlene produced
      <em>
       The Magic Flute
      </em>
      with
      <strong>
       Victor Borge
      </strong>
      conducting, 
 		and the musical
      <em>
       1776
      </em>
      with
      <strong>
       members of Congress
      </strong>
      playing our founding fathers, at the historic Constitution Hall in Washington, 
 		DC. She is also a public speaker and a journalist.
     </p>
     <p>
      Hundreds of nationally known personalities have been interviewed by Arlene, 
 		revealing meaningful,
      <strong>
       inspirational and personal stories
      </strong>
      , 
 		oftentimes never heard before. Sammy Davis, Jr., Tony Bennett, Julio Iglesias, 
 		Suzanne Somers, Danny DeVito, Chuck Norris, Placido Domingo, Paul Anka, 
 		Neil Sedaka, Linda Evans, Phyllis Diller, and Gloria Steinem are just a 
 		few Arlene's guests.
     </p>
     <p>
      Arlene has been honored by an appointment from
      <strong>
       President George 
 		W. Bush
      </strong>
      to the U.S. Holocaust Memorial Council,
      <strong>
       Governor 
 		Jeb Bush
      </strong>
      to the Florida Film &amp; Entertainment Advisory Council, 
 		and
      <strong>
       President George H. W. Bush
      </strong>
      to the Take Pride In America 
 		Advisory Board. She is one of the few non-lawyers appointed to the Florida 
 		Bar Grievance Committee.
     </p>
