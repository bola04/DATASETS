      <h1>
       Withypool
      </h1>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_Bridge.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       is a quiet unspoilt village situated in a shallow part of the Barle valley in the heart of the
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exmoor" target="_blank" title="Exmoor">
        Exmoor National Park
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_from_the_west_side.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       The village has a remote, upland character and is surrounded by open moorland, much of 			which is common land. Most of the village lies on the north side of the river and 			follows the narrow roads along the valley contours.
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_-_River_Barle_-_West_Side.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       The traditional buildings in the settlement generally consist of lime washed rendered 			stonewalls with slate roofs. It is these buildings and their setting that help 			contribute to
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       's character.
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_-_Shop.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       Along the banks of the River Barle grow the withies or willow trees that give
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       its name and, crossing the river is the characteristic six arched
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       Bridge. This was built around a hundred years ago to replace an old 				packhorse bridge that stood further up the river.
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_-_River_Barle_-_Downstream_from_Bridge.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       Within
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       , there are the local General Stores/Post Office, Olden World petrol 			pumps and Tearooms. The local pub is noted for good ale and bar/restaurant food. Also 			available are good B &amp; B's and holiday cottages.
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_Old_Petrol_Station_and_Cars.jpg"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       To the south is
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       Hill (1306 feet), With Superb views on the summit. 				On the south side of the hill is a Bronze Age stone circle.
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Tarr_Steps.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
      <p>
       There are excellent 		short and long walks in and around
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Withypool" target="_blank" title="Withypool">
        Withypool
       </a>
       including the Two Moors Way that passes 		through the village. One of the most popular walks is a 4mile riverside stroll to
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarr_Steps" target="_blank" title="Tarr Steps">
        Tarr Steps
       </a>
       , with wonderful scenery.
      </p>
      <div class="image">
       <img alt="" src="/media/medialibrary/2010/07/Home_-_Withypool_Famous_Dog_-_Pippa.JPG"/>
       <span class="caption">
       </span>
      </div>
