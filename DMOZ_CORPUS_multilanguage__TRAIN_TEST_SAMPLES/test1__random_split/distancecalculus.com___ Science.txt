           </h1>
           <img src="images2/slides-art/s4-tradvreform.png"/>
           <p>
            Two Main Types of Calculus Curriculum:
           </p>
           <b>
            Traditional
           </b>
           <span style="font-size:11px;">
            <br/>
            Classroom lectures, standard textbook, 
 						complete long homework problem sets "1-99 odd", emphasis on manual computations.
           </span>
           <br/>
           <b>
            Reform
           </b>
           <span style="font-size:11px;">
            <br/>
            Begun in the early 1990s, 
 						computational tools are required, just like in a chemistry or physics laboratory.  
 						While manual computation literacy is required, the primary goal of reform 
 						curricula is to understand the concepts - experimentally, numerically, 
 						graphically, algebraically, and narratively.
            <br/>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span style="font-size:9px;">
            <i>
             Distance Calculus utilizes one of the original Reform Calculus curricula:
             <br/>
             Calculus&amp;LiveMath (converted from Calculus&amp;Mathematica)
             <br/>
             from The Ohio State University &amp; University of Illinois at Champaign-Urbana
            </i>
           </span>

       </b>

       <br/>
