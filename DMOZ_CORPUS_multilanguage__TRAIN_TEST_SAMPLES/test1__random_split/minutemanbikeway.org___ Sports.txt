                   Bikeway 25th Anniversary
                  </strong>
                  <b>
                   .
                  </b>
                  Come celebrate the 25th anniversary of the Minuteman Commuter Bikeway on Saturday, September 29, 2018, when Arlington, Lexington, and Bedford will host a tri-town celebration of the popular rail-trail.  The bikeway was completed and dedicated by the three towns during 1992-93, and its success has inspired countless rail-trail and pathway projects in New England and beyond.  As we celebrate the past 25 years of
                  <i>
                   <b>
                    Going Places on the Minuteman Bikeway
                   </b>
                  </i>
                  , we look forward to an even better trail that is part of a robust regional bicycle transportation network.  For further information see the

               </span>
              </div>
              <div class="home-text">
               The Minuteman Bikeway passes through the historic area where the American Revolution began in April 1775. Today, the Minuteman Bikeway is one of the most popular and successful rail-trails in the United States, enjoyed for both healthy recreation and transportation.
              </div>
              <div class="home-text">
               <p>
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                The bikeway has become a new type of “Main Street” where neighbors and strangers alike come together while riding, walking, or skating on the path. In 2008, Rails-to-Trails Conservancy inducted the Minuteman Bikeway into the national Rail-Trail Hall of Fame.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                Built by the Commonwealth of Massachusetts on an inactive railroad right of way, the Minuteman Bikeway has become a treasured regional resource, used by local residents and visitors from near and far. Connecting to the Alewife “T” Station in Cambridge, the bikeway provides an easy way for bicyclists and pedestrians to travel to and from subway and bus lines, serving to reduce automobile traffic in the area.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                The bikeway is collectively managed and maintained by the four communities it passes through: Bedford, Lexington, Arlington, and Cambridge.
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="" style="display:block;clear:both;height: 0px;padding-top: 10px;border-top-width:0px;border-bottom-width:0px;">

              <div class="home-text">
               <h3>
                <strong>
                 Bikeway Dedications
                </strong>
               </h3>
               <p>
                In Lexington in 1993, the Minuteman Bikeway was dedicated to the memory of
                <strong>
                 Jack Eddison
                </strong>
                , who served many years as a Selectman and worked to champion the bikeway project. In Arlington in 2000, the bikeway was dedicated to
                <strong>
                 Donald Marquis
                </strong>
                , who served 34 years as Town Manager and was also intrumental in the bikeway's completion.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                Twenty years in planning, the Minuteman Bikeway might still be a dream if not for the tireless efforts of two community advocates:
                <strong>
                 Tom Fortmann
                </strong>
                of Lexington and
                <strong>
                 Alan McClennen, Jr.
                </strong>
                , of Arlington. Tom and Alan,
                <strong>
                 we thank you for making your dreams of a bikeway come true
                </strong>
                . We gratefully dedicate this website to you.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                We also applaud the efforts of
                <strong>
                 Cathy Buckley
                </strong>
                at Central Transportation Planning Staff (CTPS) for her efforts in the design and construction of the Minuteman Bikeway.
               </p>
