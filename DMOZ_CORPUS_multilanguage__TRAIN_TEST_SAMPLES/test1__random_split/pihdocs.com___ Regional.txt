         <p class="style14" style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Dispose of Needles, Syringes Properly
         </p>
         <p class="style11" style="margin-top:0;">
          No one would throw a ticking time bomb in the trash.  But some experts say that’s what happens when  people do not properly dispose of needles and syringes used for at-home injections.  More than 9 million syringes are used annually  for injections outside traditional U.S. healthcare facilities, and far too many  wind up in landfills and other places where contamination poses health  risks.
          <strong>
           PIH now offers patients a safe and easy way to dispose of  needles and syringes.
          </strong>
          Two container sizes are  available – 100 capacity ($9.99) and 300 capacity ($13.99).  Simply return the container to our office  when it is full and we’ll be sure it is properly incinerated.
         </p>

         <p class="style16" style="margin-top: 0;">
          <span class="style13">
           Partners in Health
          </span>
          is a medical group of seven 
                       doctors  and a certified nurse midwife practicing in the areas of Family Practice, Internal Medicine and Obstetrics/Gynecology.
         </p>
         <p class="style15">
          The  practice is located in downtown Lawrenceburg,   Indiana, a beltway community off  I-275 at Exit 16 on the west of the tri-state 
           Greater Cincinnati area. The group formed in 1999, though several doctors have been  established in the area much longer.
         </p>
         <p class="style15">
          The group practices in its own 30,000 square foot medical  complex that opened in 2005 and overlooks the Ohio River and Kentucky 
           hills.  The location and the offices, which sit above  a two-story parking garage, offer convenient access to patients.  
           The state-of-the-art facility allows doctors  to do on-site stress tests, echocardiograms, carotid doppler tests,  and 
           4-D ultrasounds.
         </p>
         <p class="style15">
          Doctors describe the group philosophy as “patient  first.”  The word
          <em>
           partner
          </em>
          is less about a group of 
           doctors in a practice and more  about how they establish relationships and work with patients.  A nursing and administrative 
           staff of nearly  fifty skilled and capable professionals supports our doctors.  Electronic medical recordkeeping and other  
           systems create efficiencies so our doctors can be doctors.
