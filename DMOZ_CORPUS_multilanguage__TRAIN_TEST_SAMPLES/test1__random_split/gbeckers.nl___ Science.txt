       <div class="itembox">
        Comparative cognitive neurobiology
       </div>
       <div class="descriptionbox">
        I am interested in neurophysiological processes underlying cognition, particularly 
                 those involved in auditory communication and sleep.
        <p>
         My comparative research focuses mostly, but not exclusively, on cognition in birds, 
                 using high-density electrical neuro-imaging techniques.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are many parallels between avian vocal communication and human speech, 
                 for example the ability to learn to produce specific vocalizations 
                 from other individuals. Because such parallels are lacking in most 
                 nonhuman mammals, and because many birds are relatively easy
                 to study in the lab, bird vocalization provides us with an interesting and
                 practical model system to study how perception and production of communicative 
                 behavior is enabled by neurocognitive and peripheral systems.
        </p>
        <p>
         In addition, I am interested in the evolution of mechanisms underlying vocal communication and language.
        </p>
        <p>
         In my research I integrate empirical techniques and 
                 theoretical perspectives from neuroscience, comparative and cognitive
                 psychology, evolutionary biology, physiology, and biophysics.
        </p>
        <p>
         My aim is to contribute to generating new insights
                 for those interested in biological principles, and, potentially, for those
                 building artificial devices, and fighting disease.
        </p>
        <p>
         I am an Assistant Professor at
         <a href="http://www.uu.nl/EN/Pages/default.aspx">
          Utrecht University
         </a>
         in the department Experimental Psychology / Cognitive Neurobiology (chair :
         <a href="http://testweb.science.uu.nl/behaviour/Bolhuis/">
          Johan Bolhuis
         </a>
         ).
        </p>
       </div>
