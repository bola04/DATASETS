          <p align="left" class="typesize">
           Welcome to Sawdustmaking 101,
                     it is my hope that this site will help novices better
                     understand the fundamentals of woodworking.
          </p>
          <p align="left" class="typesize">
           It is not necessary to spend
                     a fortune on tools to enjoy this hobby, many projects can be
                     done with a minimum investment.
          </p>
          <p align="left" class="typesize">
           I have included links to two
                     valuable resources, a
           <a href="GLOSSARY/glossary_of__woodworking_t.htm">
            Glossary
           </a>
           with basic definitions of the terms commonly used in
                     woodworking and an online reprint of
           <a href="#carpentryforboys">
            Carpentry
                       for Boys
           </a>
           , a book written for woodworking apprentices
                     in 1914.
          </p>
          <p align="right">
           <span class="typesize">
            Frank Campbell
           </span>

        <td valign="top" width="514">
         <blockquote>
          <p class="typesize">
           Try to select projects you feel capable of
                     doing but will also offer a bit of a challenge so you
                     continue to gain additional skills, many simple plans can be
                     found on the
           <a href="Projects/projects.html" target="_blank">
            Projects
           </a>
           page. Softwoods such as pine or spruce are good woods to
                     practice with, when you become more confident move on to the
                     more exotic woods.
          </p>
          <p class="typesize">
           Making shop fixtures and jigs is a great
                     way to hone your skills, many of them can be made from used
                     or recycled material, our
           <a href="jigs/jigs.html" target="_blank">
            Shop
                       Jigs and Fixtures
           </a>
           page has a lot of handy ideas.
          </p>
          <p class="typesize">
           Add tools as you need them, always buy the
                     best tool you can afford. There is usually more than one way
                     to accomplish the same result, tools are often a matter of
                     preference than necessity. Consider buying used tools, the
                     build quality of older tools is often superior to many of
                     the new tools on today's market.
          </p>
          <p>
           <font class="typesize" face="Arial" size="2">
            I have
                       included sections on
            <a href="#BasicToolBox">
             hand tools
            </a>
            and
            <a href="#PowerTools">
             power tools
            </a>
            with basic
                       information on using them along with links to suppliers
                       that can supply you with further information on specific
                       brands. Any purchase from those links helps keep this site
                       online.
           </font>
          </p>
          <p class="typesize">
           Never forget how dangerous a power tool
                     can be, if you have any hesitation as to the safety of a
                     situation stop and check out your options.
          </p>
          <p class="typesize">
           <span class="red">
            If it doesn't feel good
                       don't do it.
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="typesize">
           If you are looking for more plans and
                     ideas check my other site:
           <a href="http://absolutelyfreeplans.com" target="_blank">
            Absolutely
                       Free Plans
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <font class="typesize" face="Arial" size="2">
            If you
           </font>
           <span class="typesize">
            are still one of those among us that
                       likes to have an actual book in your hands for information
                       check out these free catalogues:
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
