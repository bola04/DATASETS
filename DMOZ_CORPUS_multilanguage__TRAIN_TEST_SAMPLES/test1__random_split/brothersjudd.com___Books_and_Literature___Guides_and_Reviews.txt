        <font color="008000">
         Brothers Judd QuasiDaily Bookmarks:

        <b>
         06/23/18
        </b>
        :
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.kirjasto.sci.fi/remarque.htm">
         Erich Maria Remarque
        </a>
        (1898-1970) was born in Osnabruck, GER on
        <b>
         June 22
        </b>
        , 1898. Read Orrin's review of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/701">
         All Quiet on the Western Front
        </a>
        (1928)
        <b>
         <i>
          (Grade: C)
         </i>
        </b>
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.kirjasto.sci.fi/haggard.htm">
         H. Rider Haggard
        </a>
        (1856-1925) was born in Norfolk, England on
        <b>
         June 22
        </b>
        , 1856. His book
        <a href="http://www.bibliomania.com/Fiction/Haggard/Solomon/">
         King Solomon's Mine
        </a>
        (1885) is one of the great adventure classics of all time.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.reelclassics.com/Directors/Wilder/wilder.htm">
         Billy Wilder
        </a>
        (1906-) was born in Sucha, Austria on
        <b>
         June 22
        </b>
        , 1906. Among his classic films are The Major and the Minor, Double Indemnity, The Lost Weekend, Stalag 17, Five Graves to Cairo and Sunset Boulevard
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.math.buffalo.edu/~sww/butler/butler_octavia_bio.html">
         Octavia Butler
        </a>
        (1947-) was born in Pasadena, CA on
        <b>
         June 22
        </b>
        , 1947. Read Orrin's review of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/10">
         Kindred
        </a>
        (1979)
        <b>
         <i>
          (Grade: A)
         </i>
        </b>
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0195103084/juddsbookreviews">
         Dom Casmurro
        </a>
        (1899)(
        <b>
         Joaquim Maria Machado de Assis
        </b>
        1839-1908)
       </p>
       <p>
        was born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil on
        <strong>
         June 21
        </strong>
        , 1839
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.childrensbookguild.org/Klause.html">
         Annette Curtis Klause
        </a>
        (1953-) was born in Bristol, ENG on
        <b>
         June 20
        </b>
        , 1953. Read Orrin's review of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/515">
         Blood and Chocolate
        </a>
        (1997)
        <b>
         <i>
          (Grade: A to F)
         </i>
        </b>
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        Henry Louis
        <a href="http://www.nmia.com/~browns/gehrig.htm">
         "Lou" Gehrig
        </a>
        (1903-1941) was born on
        <b>
         June 19
        </b>
        , 1903.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.salonmagazine.com/dec96/interview961216.html">
         Tobias Wolff
        </a>
        (1945-) was born in Birmingham, AL on
        <b>
         June 19
        </b>
        , 1945. His excellent memoir,
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0060972777/juddsbookreviews">
         This Boy's Life
        </a>
        (1989), describes growing up in Washington State with a brutal step-father.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://rylibweb.man.ac.uk/data1/dg/methodist/jwol1.html">
         John Wesley
        </a>
        (1703-91), founder of the
        <a href="http://www.methodist.org.uk/">
         Methodist Church
        </a>
        , was born in Epworth, England on
        <b>
         June 17
        </b>
        , 1703. He and his band of friends were derided at Oxford for their "methodism", methodical study of the Bible. After a conversion experience in 1738, he preached a message of salvation through faith in Christ alone.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        John Hersey (1914-93) was born in China on
        <b>
         June 17
        </b>
        , 1914. Read Orrin's reviews of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/1064">
         A Bell for Adano
        </a>
        (1944)
        <b>
         <i>
          (Grade: B+)
         </i>
        </b>
        and
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/1128">
         Hiroshima
        </a>
        (1946)
        <b>
         <i>
          (Grade: B)
         </i>
        </b>
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.poets.org/LIT/POET/Jwjohfst.htm">
         James Weldon Johnson
        </a>
        (1871-1938), who wrote the Black National Anthem--
        <a href="http://www.nku.edu/~diesmanj/johnson.html#lifteveryvoice">
         Lift Every Voice and Sing
        </a>
        , was born in Jacksonville, FL on
        <b>
         June 17
        </b>
        , 1871. Read Orrin's review of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/15">
         The Autobiography of an Ex-Colored Man
        </a>
        (1912) (Grade: B+)
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        At 2:30am on the morning of
        <b>
         June 17
        </b>
        , 1972, five men were caught breaking in to Democratic National Committee Headquarters in the Watergate Building. Read Orrin's reviews of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/851">
         Silent Coup: The Removal of a President
        </a>
        (1991)(
        <b>
         Len Colodny, Robert Gettlin
        </b>
        )
        <b>
         <i>
          (Grade: C+) and
         </i>
        </b>
        Leon Jaworski's
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/1024">
         The Right and the Power
        </a>
        (Grade: C), but also try G. Gordon Liddy's
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0312119151/juddsbookreviews/">
         Will
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0671894412/juddsbookreviews/">
         All the President's Men
        </a>
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <b>
         June 16th
        </b>
        is Bloomsday. Joyce fans all over the world commemorate the mindnumbing meanderings of Leopold Bloom through the streets of Dublin in 1904 (read
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/867">
         Orrin's review of Ulysses, Grade: F x Googleplex
        </a>
        ) But save yourself the trouble &amp; read
        <a href="http://www.bway.net/~hunger/ulysses.html">
         Ulysses for Dummies.
        </a>
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        Katharine Graham (1917-2001) was born in New York, NY on
        <strong>
         June 16
        </strong>
        , 1917. Read Orrin's review of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/470">
         Personal History
        </a>
        (1997) (
        <b>
         Katharine Graham
        </b>
        1917-2001)
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="http://www.nara.gov/exhall/charters/magnacarta/magmain.html">
         Magna Carta
        </a>
        was signed on
        <strong>
         June 15
        </strong>
        , 1215 by King John of England the agreement between John and several dozen English Barons that took away the King's total authority, and granted basic rights and liberties such as freedom of the church, no imprisonment without a jury trial, and the proper execution of the laws of the land.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <b>
         Jerzy Kosinski
        </b>
        (1933-91) was born in Lodz, POL on
        <b>
         June 14
        </b>
        , 1933. Read Orrin's review of
        <a href="index.cfm/fuseaction/reviews.detail/book_id/293">
         The Painted Bird
        </a>
        (1965)
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.emule.com/poetry/works.cgi?author=64">
         William Butler Yeats
        </a>
        (1865-1939) was born in Dublin, IR on
        <b>
         June 13
        </b>
        , 1865. His poem
        <a href="http://www.emule.com/poetry/dispoem.cgi?poem=2822">
         The Second Coming
        </a>
        seemingly predicted the course of the pre-Reagan 20th Century.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        President
        <a href="http://csdl.tamu.edu/bushlib/">
         George Herbert Walker Bush
        </a>
        (1924-) was born on
        <b>
         June 12
        </b>
        , 1924 in Milton, MA. In one of the Republics low points, he lost his reelection bid in 1992, but current polls give his presidency a 70+% approval rating. The Brothers Judd recommend
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0679746498/juddsbookreviews">
         What It Takes
        </a>
        , Richard Ben Cramer's extraordinary account of the 1988 presidential contenders.
       </p>
       <hr align="center" size="1"/>
       <p>
        <a href="http://search.eb.com/nobel/micro/315_74.html">

        </a>
        (1899-1972) was born in Osaka, JPN on
        <b>
         June 11
        </b>
        , 1899. Read Orrin's review of

        </a>
        (1949)(Grade: D)
