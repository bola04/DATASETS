        <h2 class="wsite-content-title">
         Tony Porter
        </h2>
        <span class="imgPusher" style="float:left;height:0px">
        </span>
        <span style="display: table;width:auto;position:relative;float:left;max-width:100%;;clear:left;margin-top:0px;*margin-top:0px">
         <a>
          <img alt="Picture" class="galleryImageBorder wsite-image" src="/uploads/1/9/0/2/19020775/published/tony-portrait.jpeg?1511801478" style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; margin-right: 10px; border-width:1px;padding:3px; max-width:100%"/>

         <span class="wsite-caption" style="display: table-caption; caption-side: bottom; font-size: 90%; margin-top: -10px; margin-bottom: 10px; text-align: center;">
         </span>
        </span>
        <div class="paragraph" style="display:block;">
         <span>
          I began my reflexology career in 1973 after deviating from my intended path of becoming an osteopath.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          Since that time I have been in full-time reflexology practice and also presenting reflexology seminars Internationally.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          It was through a meeting and later my association with Arthur Lincoln Pauls, a Canadian osteopath living in London, that I became interested in reflexology.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          Arthur is the Founder of Ortho-Bionomy, a wonderfully effective form of passive structural correction. It was through Arthur that I became interested in the concept of reflexology.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Eventually I became an assistant to Arthur at his Ortho-Bionomy seminars in America in the 1970s.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          It was through this that I was later to meet Dwight Byers, nephew of Eunice Ingham, the Mother of reflexology. This meeting lead me to working for Dwight in the International Institute of Reflexology headquarters in Florida.  From this came many years of Dwight and I travelling on a truly international scale to South Africa, Australia, Singapore, New Zealand, South America, Israel, and not forgetting most of the European countries including the UK.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          This travel went on for several years, and although very demanding, provided me with a vast amount of experience in reflexology. I was also later to visit other countries such as China, Mongolia and Malaysia and Thailand on my own with the aim of experiencing the reflexology methods which were in use in those countries.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          During this time I managed to keep a busy reflexology practice running in London, where I was also invited to work with some eminent consultants in the medical field who needed proof that reflexology had clinically demonstrable results.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          It was because of this that I realised that I needed to create a different type of approach and protocol to reflexology - an approach which gave positive and quantifiable results other than that of purely relaxation.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span>
          Drawing on my experience with the Ingham method of reflexology, (which I identify as classical reflexology)  and other techniques which I experienced during my travels. I devised a protocol which had the foundation of the classical techniques, combined with those which are more common in the oriental countries.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span>
          I called this combination and fusion of techniques A.R.T. first of all in recognition to my dear friend and mentor
