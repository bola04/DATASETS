           <div class="hidden-sm hidden-xs">
            <div class="col-md-6">
             <h4 display="none">
              <br/>
              The Welsh Sinfonia is a Cardiff-based professional chamber orchestra of
                 between 15 and 35 musicians, conducted by
              <a href="#meet">
               Mark Eager
              </a>
              , and led by
              <a href="#meet">
               Robin
                 Stowell
              </a>
              . After 20 years of serving Welsh choirs and choral societies, the Welsh
                 Sinfonia was relaunched in September 2009 with the vision of becoming the
                 National Chamber Orchestra of Wales.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              As a compact and flexible ensemble, the Welsh Sinfonia aims to bring chamber
                 music to the whole of Wales: reaching smaller venues and areas where live
                 classical music provision is limited. The orchestra delivers exciting and
                 vibrant performances, infusing classical greats with fresh contemporary
                 works, regularly commissioning new works from its Composer in Residence,
                 Charlie Barber.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Opportunities to hear regular professional performances of works for chamber orchestra
                 are rare in Wales and The Welsh Sinfonia is an important champion of this
                 rich and diverse repertoire, generously supported by the Arts Council of
                 Wales.
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="col-md-6">
             <h4 display="none">
              <br/>
              Cerddorfa siambr broffesiynol wedi ei leoli yng Nghaedydd  yw'r Sinffonia Gymreig, yn cael ei arwain gan
              <a href="#meet">
               Mark Eager
              </a>
              a'i flaenu gan
              <a href="#meet">
               Robin Stowell
              </a>
              .  Yn ensemble cryno a hyblyg, mae'r gerddorfa yn  anelu at ddod â cherddoriaeth siambr i Gymru gyfan, gan gyrraedd ardaloedd a lleoliadau  bychain, lle mae'r ddarpariaeth o gerddoriaeth glasurol yn brin.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Mae'r gerddorfa yn darparu perfformiadau cynhyrfus a bywiog, gan gyfuno'r mawrion Clasurol gyda gweithiau cyfoes ffres, gan gomisiynu  gweithiau newydd yn gyson gan eu Cyfansoddwr Preswyl, Charlie Barber.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Ar y cyd â' chyngherddau teithiol rheolaidd, mae'r Sinffonia Gymreig yn cynorthwyo ysgolion ar draws Cymru i ddatblygu  cerddorfeydd trwy gyfrwng prosiect Crescendo. Yn gynllun pum mlynedd ar gyfer pum ysgol, mae deunaw aelod o'r gerddorfa yn gweithio'n agos gyda chwaraewyr cerddorfaol ifanc  a chorau  ysgolion, gan  drefnu cyngherddau er mwyn cyflwyno'r disgyblion i bleser perfformio mewn ensemble mawr drwy repertoire difrifol ond cyraeddadwy.
             </h4>

             <img class="center-block centre-block img-responsive" src="Images/Crescendo logo 300dpi.png"/>
             <h3 class="text-left">
              Crescendo is a ground-breaking project, formed with the aim of developing
                   classical orchestras in secondary schools throughout Wales.
             </h3>
             <h4>
              Eighteen of the orchestra’s players are working with five schools over
                   the course of five years, in addition to performing in full orchestral
                   concerts in nearby venues. The young orchestral players are invited to
                   attend these local concerts to hear a full professional chamber orchestra,
                   and to introducing them to a wide range of serious but accessible music.
             </h4>
            </div>
            <div class="col-md-6">
             <div class="col-md-12">

               </ul>
              </h4>
              <blockquote>
               <p>
                "I loved the expresson on our youngsters' faces when they were introduced
                       to the audience and the project explained. There was an opportunity to
                       meet the orchestra during the evening and it was a delight to witness the
                       free flowing conversations between conductor, players, students and parents.
                       There was a real buzz on the bus on the way home for all the right reasons!"
               </p>

                Sally Marshall, Ysgol Bro Hyddgen, Machynlleth
