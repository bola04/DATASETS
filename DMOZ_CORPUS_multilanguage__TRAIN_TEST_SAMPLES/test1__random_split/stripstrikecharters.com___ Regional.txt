       <h1>
        Flats and Backcountry Fishing Charters and Guide Service in Marathon and Big Pine Key, Florida Keys
       </h1>
       <h2>
        Florida Keys Fishing at it's finest
        <br/>
        Fish the gin clear waters of the Florida Keys
       </h2>
       <p>
        Come               to the Middle Keys (Marathon and Big Pine Key) and discover the thrill of battling giant
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Tarpon.htm">
         Tarpon
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Bonefish.htm">
         Bonefish
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Permit.htm">
         Permit
        </a>
        on the gin clear flats of Florida Bay or embark               on a unforgettable trip into Everglades National Park to fish for Tarpon,
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Backcountry.htm">
         Snook,
        </a>
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Backcountry.htm">
         Redfish
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Backcountry.htm">
         Trout
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.stripstrikecharters.com/Shark_fishing.htm">
         Sharks
        </a>
        and much more. Fishing the Middle               Keys, I will ensure you have a fun and productive fishing               experience. The Middle Keys have many different types               of fishing for everyone!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Marathon
        </strong>
        is in the heart of the Florida Keys. Fishing can get simply incredible.   Away from the hustle and bustle of numerous fishing guides and a lot of   boat traffic. fishing in Marathon brings great Bonefishing, Tarpon and   Permit fishing.
       </p>
       <strong>
        Big Pine Key
       </strong>
       is located 15 minutes south of Marathon. It has some of the best flats fishing the Keys has to offer. If fly fishing for Permit is your game, Big Pine is the place to be. This area also has some incredible baby tarpon fishing especially in the summer time when the shrimp hatches occur.
       <strong>
        Big Pine Key
       </strong>
       is also home to the Bahia Honda bridge, which, in fact, is the best bridge for tarpon fishing in the entire Keys.

       <h1>
        Fly Fishing Instruction Available
       </h1>
       <h2>
        Learn how to cast and catch fish!
       </h2>
       <img height="119" src="images/fly_fishing.jpg" width="176"/>
       Fly Fishing Instruction available for all ages and experience levels. Fly Fishing trips are my specialty, I have guided many first time fly fisherman to their very first catch in the Keys.  Sometimes in as little as 15 minutes, I can hone your skills to tackle the challenge of catching your first fish on fly in the Florida Keys. Patience is key to your success. I also have to latest and greatest in fly tackle. All flies are custom tied by me. I know what the fish want and I know what they eat.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       <h1>
        Florida Keys Fly Fishing
       </h1>
       <h2>
        Fly Fishing for Silver King.
       </h2>
       <img alt="Florida Keys Tarpon Fishing - Fly Fishing" height="114" src="images/poon_fly3.jpg" width="176"/>
       Fly fishing for tarpon is one of my favorite things to do. Watching a 80 or a 100 lber take a fly   and soar through the air is priceless especially for the   first time tarpon angler. Honestly, it is not the easiest thing to do. Precise casting is   necessary to achieve this most sought after goal. Minimal false casts is essential. Getting the fish to eat is the hard part. With some help from me, we can get on the right track to catching your dream fish on fly.

      <h1>
       Florida Keys Fishing Report
      </h1>
      <div id="date">
       <strong>
        01/11/2017
       </strong>
       | Posted by: Captain Paul Fisicaro
      </div>
      <p>
       This last week has been the coldest of the year so far. Water temps plummeted in the the low 60's making fishing pretty darn tough. Before this front, the fishing was pretty good. Lots of Spanish Mackerel, big Yellow Jacks and a pleathora of Snapper. One the water temop gets back up there, we should return to normal. March is just around the corner though and Permit fishing isso close I can taste it. My most favorite time of year! Until the next time, tight lines!
      </p>
