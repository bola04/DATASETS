         <div class="home_main" style="width: 95%; padding-top: 10px; border-top: 1px solid #cccccc; margin-top: 25px;">
          <h2>
           <img alt="" class="pointer" height="14" src="https://www.jeffersonairplane.com/wp-content/themes/jeffair/links/images/pointer.png" width="8"/>
           Jefferson Airplane
          </h2>
          <p>
           <img alt="Jefferson Airplane" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-54" src="https://www.jeffersonairplane.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/large.jpg" style="float: left; margin-right: 5px; width: 200px;" title="Jefferson Airplane"/>
           Jefferson Airplane formed in 1965 in the San Francisco Bay area during the summit of the counter culture sweeping the country, and soon became became a household name with appearances on radio, television, and top selling albums.
          </p>
          <p>
           Jefferson Airplane’s debut show was on August 13, 1965 at the Matrix nightclub in San Francisco, then went on with several successful tours including performances at the Berkeley Folk Festival, Monterey Jazz Festival, Monterey Pop Festival, Woodstock, and Altamont.
          </p>
          <p>
           They recorded hit singles such as “White Rabbit,” “Volunteers,” “Embryonic Journey” and “Somebody to Love” and smash albums such as “Volunteers,” “Surrealistic Pillow,” “Bark” and “Takes Off.”
          </p>
          <hr/>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The band featured
           </strong>
           : Grace Slick (vocals), Marty Balin (vocals and guitar), Paul Kantner (vocals and guitar), Jorma Kaukonen (guitar), Jack Casady (bass), and Spencer Dryden (drums).
          </p>
          <p>
           Around 1973 they disbanded, forming a generation of music groups such as Jefferson Starship, Hot Tuna, and more. In 1989 a short reunion occurred, and in 1996 the band was inducted to the Rock &amp; Roll Hall of Fame in Cleveland, Ohio.
          </p>
          <hr/>
          <p>
           This website highlights the life and times of the famous Jefferson Airplane, one of history’s most influential rock bands of all time. Explore the biographies, read old posts from our (inactive) forums, and keep up with the band at Jefferson Airplane.com
          </p>
          <p>
