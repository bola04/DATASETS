             </h2>
             <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_5021" style="width: 310px">
              <a href="http://www.bartowel.com/news_beta/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/IMG_8822a.jpg" rel="lightbox[5017] attachment wp-att-5021">
               <img alt="Cass, Oliver &amp; Steve" class="wp-image-5021 size-medium" height="224" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.bartowel.com/news_beta/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/IMG_8822a-300x224.jpg" srcset="http://www.bartowel.com/news_beta/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/IMG_8822a-300x224.jpg 300w, http://www.bartowel.com/news_beta/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/IMG_8822a-768x573.jpg 768w, http://www.bartowel.com/news_beta/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/IMG_8822a-1024x763.jpg 1024w, http://www.bartowel.com/news_beta/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/IMG_8822a-600x447.jpg 600w" width="300"/>

             <p>
              Were thrilled to take a walk down beer memory lane with our newest episode of
              <a href="http://www.bartowel.com/podcasts">
               Bar Towel Radio
              </a>
              . This special episode was recorded live at the launch of
              <strong>
               <a href="https://www.hendersonbrewing.com/upper-canada-repatriation-brings-past-and-present-together/">
                Upper Canada Repatriation
               </a>
              </strong>
              , a recreation of the original Rebellion Lager by the famed Upper Canada Brewing Company, brought back by
              <a href="https://www.hendersonbrewing.com">
               Henderson Brewing
              </a>
              of Toronto in partnership with
              <a href="http://sleeman.ca">
               Sleeman Breweries
              </a>
              . We chat with
              <strong>
               Steve Himel
              </strong>
              , General Manager of Henderson and
              <strong>
               Oliver Dawson
              </strong>
              , former head of marketing at Upper Canada (and now of the
              <a href="https://www.beerloverstour.com">
               Beer Lovers Tour Co.
              </a>
              ) about how the brand originally came to be, what it was like to create craft beer brands in the 1980s and 1990s, and how the beerreturned in 2018.
             </p>

             <p>
              Yesterday at a gala event at
              <a href="http://www.beerbistro.com">
               beerbistro
              </a>
              , the 2017
              <strong>
               <a href="http://www.goldentap.com">

               </a>
              </strong>
              were announced. The Golden Tap Awards are Ontarios most democratic beer awards event, with all winners determined by public vote. The winners were as follows:
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Best brewery: Beaus All Natural
              </li>
              <li>
               Best bar for draught beer selection: Bar Hop
              </li>
              <li>
               Best bar for bottled and/or canned beer selection: Birreria Volo
              </li>
              <li>
               Best brewpub or tied house: Bellwoods Brewery
              </li>
              <li>
               Best regularly-produced craft beer: Beaus Lug Tread
              </li>
              <li>
               Best seasonal, limited release or specialty craft beer: Great Lakes Octopus Wants to Fight!
              </li>
              <li>
               Best craft cidery: West Avenue
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Beer Design and Branding: Collective Arts
              </li>
              <li>
               Most Innovative Brewery: Bellwoods Brewery
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Newcomer (Bar): Arabella Park
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Newcomer (Brewery): Merit Brewing
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Event: Cask Days
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Staff: Beaus All Natural
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Beer Writer: Ben Johnson
              </li>
              <li>
               Brewmasters Choice: Tooth and Nail Vim and Vigor
              </li>
              <li>
               Best Beer of the Festival: 555 Brewing Footprint
              </li>
              <li>
               Editors Circle Award: Ontario Beverage Network
              </li>
              <li>
               Editors Circle Award: Half Hours on Earth
              </li>
              <li>
               Editors Circle Award: Craft Beer Passport
              </li>
              <li>
