      <p>
       Heat sealing and vacuum packaging explained
      </p>
      <p>
       Vacuum packing is a process that entails getting rid of air from a container or a bag sealer. The key to successfully packing food products lies in choosing the right sealer for the job. Sealers work by projecting heat to a contact area using a heating bar to wield thermoplastics together. Vacuum packing, on the other hand, entails flushing air out while the product is still in its sealing package.  The most common vacuum packaging products include bottles, canisters, plastic bags and jars.
      </p>
      <p>
       Types of vacuum packaging machines
      </p>
      <p>
       The first variation is the Vacuum chamber machine. Here we have two types; single and double vacuum packaging machines.  Vacuum chambers sealers require the whole item to be placed in within the sealing equipment. Once the item is in the device, the lid is closed, and all the air is flushed out. The flushing is done by opening an automatic vent to the outside; the pressure generated from this process squeezes all the remaining air from the bag. These machines are ideal for low to medium volume products such as processed meat, chocolates and cheese.
      </p>
      <p>
       The advantages of heat and vacuum sealing
      </p>
      <p>
       Vacuum and heat sealing has numerous advantages the first being offering advanced protection against dehydration and to prevent entry of moisture. Vacuum packaging also vastly improves the shelf life of dry food items such as cereals, nuts, cheese, coffee, dried fish, meat and even potato chips. For items with a shorter shelf life such as vegetables, fresh meat and liquids, vacuum sealing helps to preserve their freshness while preventing the growth of fungi and bacteria.
