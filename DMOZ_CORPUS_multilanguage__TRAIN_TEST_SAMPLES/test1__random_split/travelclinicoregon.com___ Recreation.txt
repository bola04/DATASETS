        The Travel Clinic will likely close in December, 2018. Those who have 
 		plans to travel to subSaharan Africa or tropical South America in the 
 		next year or so may want to become familiar the yellow fever (YF) 
 		vaccine shortage issue.
        <a href="Travel-clinic-likely-close.htm">
         &lt;more&gt;
        </a>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Formats for service by The Travel Clinic:
        <br/>
        <strong>
         1.
        </strong>
        Currently we are only scheduling travelers for whom 
 		YF vaccine is an issue.
        <a href="Formats-for-Service-1.htm">
         &lt;more&gt;
        </a>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         2.
        </strong>
        With each traveler office visit there will be a 
 		discussion of all relevant issues (depending on itinerary) even if the 
 		traveler has already been seen by another practitioner for this trip.
        <a href="Formats-for-Service-2.htm">
         &lt;more&gt;
        </a>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         3. Usual office appointments:
        </strong>
        Our usual format is to 
 		see individual patients or small groups (ideally up to 3 travelers) at 
 		11:00 am, 12:30 pm or 2:00 pm on Tuesday or Thursday.
        <a href="Formats-for-Service-3.htm">
         &lt;more&gt;
        </a>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         4. Group visits
        </strong>
        with perhaps 10 or so participants with 
 		the same itinerary may be held in the office on an evening or weekend.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         5.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Weekend Group Meetings:
        </strong>
        We are 
 		beginning to offer YF vaccine in an off-hours group format among 
 		travelers who need Stamaril even though they may have different 
 		itineraries.
        <a href="Formats-for-Service-5.htm">
         &lt;more&gt;
        </a>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         YF vaccine shortage:
        </strong>

 		vaccine in the past year has become aware of the YF vaccine shortage and 
 		the difficulty accessing a practitioner who can supply this vaccine (see
        <a href="YF-vaccine-shortage.pdf">

        </a>
        Aug 17, 2017,
