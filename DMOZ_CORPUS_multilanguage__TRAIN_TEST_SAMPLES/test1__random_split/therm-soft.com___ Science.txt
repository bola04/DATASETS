        <header class="product-headline csc-header-n1">
         <h1 class="csc-firstHeader">
          NETZSCH
          <i>
           Advanced Software
          </i>
         </h1>
        </header>
       </div>
       <div class="csc-default">
        <a data-content-record="l10n_parent" id="c21577">
        </a>
        <p class="bodytext">
         <i>
          Advanced Software
         </i>
         package a set of four individual software for the advances analysis of thermoanalytical data and further predictions and simulations.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="col-sm-3">
      </div>
     </div>
     <a id="c278527">
     </a>
     <div class="row">
      <div class="col-sm-3">
       <div class="csc-default">
        <a data-content-record="l10n_parent" id="c278525">
        </a>
        <div class="csc-textpic csc-textpic-left csc-textpic-above">
         <div class="csc-textpic-imagewrap" data-csc-cols="1" data-csc-images="1">
          <figure class="csc-textpic-image csc-textpic-last">
           <a href="https://kinetics.netzsch.com/en/">
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="https://dcyd0ggl1hia3.cloudfront.net/fileadmin/_processed_/opt_kinetics_neo_logo_bb400bd415.png?1499237941"/>
           </a>
          </figure>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="col-sm-9">
       <a id="c1045">
       </a>
       <div>
       </div>
       <p class="bodytext">
        <a class="btn-primary" href="https://kinetics.netzsch.com/en/" target="_top">
         Kinetics Neo
        </a>
       </p>
       <p class="bodytext">
        Kinetics Neo is a software for the kinetic analysis of thermal measurements, including
        <em>
         <strong>
          model-free
         </strong>
        </em>
        and
        <em>
         <strong>
          model based
         </strong>
        </em>
        kinetic analysis. The kinetic analysis allows to find the set of kinetic parameters e.g. number of steps, contribution of each step to the total effect of the process such as step enthalpy or step mass loss; reaction type, activation energy and reaction order for each step. Then this information will be used for
        <em>
         <strong>
          predictions
         </strong>
        </em>
        of reaction progress for given temperature conditions or
        <em>
         <strong>
          optimization
         </strong>
        </em>
        of temperatures to get the desired reaction rate and product concentrations. The predictions can be done for one or several isothermal measurements, for dynamic measurements like heating, for isothermal or dynamic segment with daily temperature oscillations and for any sequence from such temperature segments.
       </p>
       <div>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="divider">
      <hr/>
     </div>
     <a id="c278528">
     </a>
     <div class="row">
      <div class="col-sm-3">
       <div class="csc-default">
        <a data-content-record="l10n_parent" id="c278526">
        </a>
        <div class="csc-textpic csc-textpic-left csc-textpic-above">
         <div class="csc-textpic-imagewrap" data-csc-cols="1" data-csc-images="1">
          <figure class="csc-textpic-image csc-textpic-last">
           <a href="/en/products-solutions/software/netzsch-advanced-software/thermal-simulations/">
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="https://dcyd0ggl1hia3.cloudfront.net/fileadmin/_processed_/opt_Thermal-Simulation_b3bdf2a79e.png?1456479324"/>
           </a>
          </figure>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="col-sm-9">
       <div class="csc-default">
        <a data-content-record="l10n_parent" id="c267873">
        </a>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p class="bodytext">
         <a class="btn-primary" href="/en/products-solutions/software/netzsch-advanced-software/thermal-simulations/" target="_top">
          <i>
           Thermal Simulations
          </i>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="bodytext">
         A program for simulation of the thermal behavior under conditions of both heat generation and heat conductivity. A finite element solution for simple geometries such as infinite plate, infinite cylinder and sphere is offered. The temperature dependence of cp, density and heat conductivity is taken into account.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="divider">
      <hr/>
     </div>
     <a id="c267891">
     </a>
     <div class="row">
      <div class="col-sm-3">
       <div class="csc-default">
        <a data-content-record="l10n_parent" id="c278529">
        </a>
        <div class="csc-textpic csc-textpic-left csc-textpic-above">
         <div class="csc-textpic-imagewrap" data-csc-cols="1" data-csc-images="1">
          <figure class="csc-textpic-image csc-textpic-last">
           <a href="/en/products-solutions/software/netzsch-advanced-software/peak-separation/">
            <img alt="" class="img-responsive" src="https://dcyd0ggl1hia3.cloudfront.net/fileadmin/_processed_/opt_Peak-Separation_70d651649d.png?1456479853"/>
           </a>
          </figure>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="clearfix">
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="col-sm-9">
       <div class="csc-default">
        <a data-content-record="l10n_parent" id="c267882">
        </a>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p class="bodytext">
         <a class="btn-primary" href="/en/products-solutions/software/netzsch-advanced-software/peak-separation/" target="_top">
          <i>
           Peak Separation
          </i>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p class="bodytext">
         If your experimental curve looks very complex with
         <em>
          <strong>
           several maxima
          </strong>
         </em>
         and seems to contain several
         <em>
          <strong>
           overlapping peaks
          </strong>
         </em>
         then our software helps to
         <em>
          <strong>
           separate
          </strong>
         </em>
         these peaks, presents experimental data as a
         <em>
          <strong>
           sum of peaks
          </strong>
         </em>
         , and analyzes each peak separately. The universal peak shape is used, which is the weighted mixture of Fraser-Suzuki and asymmetric Cauchy. The following peak types are the partial cases of the universal peak shape: Gaussian, Cauchy, Pseudo- Voigt (additive mixture of Gaussian and Cauchy), Fraser-Suzuki (asymmetric Gaussian), Laplace, asymmetric Laplace and asymmetric Cauchy.
        </p>
        <div>
        </div>
       </div>
