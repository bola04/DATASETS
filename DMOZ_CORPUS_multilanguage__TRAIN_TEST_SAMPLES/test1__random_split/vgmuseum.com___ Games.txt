               Man...
               <a href="/end/ps2/a/bouncer.htm">
                <u>
                 The Bouncer
                </u>
               </a>
               ... It has a bunch of ending variations depending on what character you use on specific missions. All the FAQs and info online are either wrong or incomplete, so I foolheartedly thought I could quickly figure out all the variations on my own, but it took me over 4 days of constant playing, testing, mixing and matching characters and missions. I believe I got a taste of what beta testers go through, and I think I hate the game now too. Had I known I was going to go through so much trouble, I wouldn't have even tried it. But whatever, it's done now.
               <br/>
               I usually like to mention what you need to do to trigger variations in the endings I post, but for this game it's not possible unless I put paragraphs of information. So, if you are one of the few The Bouncer fans, check out this
               <a href="http://www.gamefaqs.com/boards/196809-the-bouncer/74774303" target="_blank">
                <u>
                 thread I made on GameFAQs
                </u>
               </a>
               detailing all my tests and notes about how to get the ending variations. I left it there for posterity and archiving's sake in case a future someone needs that info.

               I finally finished getting the ending for
               <a href="/end/snes/a/femblem5.htm">
                <u>
                 Fire Emblem: Thracia 776
                </u>
               </a>
               (A.K.A. Fire Emblem 5). Unlike most of the other games in the series, this game doesn't have any crazy ending variations. As long as you have certain characters alive, you'll get their ending conversations.
               <br/>
               One thing to note though, the game was originally released through a service called Nintendo Power (no relation to the magazine) in which you downloaded the game from special kiosks into a rewritable cartridge. Eventually, the game was re-issued as a proper retail release, but some things were changed between versions, such as adding new ending ranks and shuffling/adding/removing some of the credits portraits. I included both credits versions, even though there's only one exclusive portrait for each version.
 
 Huge thanks to Eien Ni Hen for translating everything again!

               Nine years ago, in an Internet far far away... I came across the
               <a href="http://www.superpcenginegrafx.net/system_card_warning_warehouse.html" target="_blank">
                <u>
                 Super PC Engine Grafx Warning Warehouse
                </u>
               </a>
               where they showed a bunch warning messages you get when you try to load a
               <b>
                TurboGrafx CD
               </b>
               Super CD-ROM2 System Card game, on a console with a regular System Card. 
 What really peaked my interest was the myriad of games they
               <i>
                didn't
               </i>
               cover, and thus began this seemingly endless project.
               <br/>
               Shortly after starting, I realized that there were also warning messages for when the internal memory (Backup RAM) of the console was full. And then I thought, if TGCD games have these messages, then maybe Sega CD does too? It did. But the discoveries didn't stop there. After a few months I find that there are also warning messages for when the Backup RAM is corrupt, for both consoles! And then that there are also TurboGrafx-16 games with warning messages. And if all that wasn't enough, I also figure I might start collecting all the Super Game Boy borders, and the Game Boy Color, NGPC, WSC, warning screens too - which were already posted a few weeks ago.
               <br/>
               Anyway, after years of on-again off-again work, and almost 700 images with dozens upon dozens of time consuming animated GIFs... I bring you the complete collection of
               <a href="/features/warn2">
                <u>
                 System Card &amp; Backup RAM Warning Screens
                </u>
               </a>
               .
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Be aware that some of these images are
               <b>
                NOT SAFE FOR WORK
               </b>
               , as they contain partial nudity and overly cute characters.

               For many years I've been capturing the different warning screens you get when you try to insert a
               <a href="/features/warn">
                <u>
                 Game Boy Color
                </u>
               </a>
               ,
               <a href="/features/warn/ngpc.htm">
                <u>
                 Neo Geo Pocket Color
                </u>
               </a>
               , or
               <a href="/features/warn/wsc.htm">
                <u>
                 Wonderswan Color
                </u>
               </a>
               game into the monochromatic versions of those systems. Most of the time, the messages are generic, but other times you get some cool artwork to go alongside it. 
 Check the SNK Vs Capcom warning messages for the Neo Geo Pocket Color for some good stuff!
               <br/>
               As far as I know, these are all the warning screens possible for these handheld games, which total almost a thousand images worth!
