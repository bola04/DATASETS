                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7885502287222337352" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      When I was a kid I had a hand me down bunk bed that was my dad's when he was a kid.  The bed is made of solid oak and can be configured as two  separate beds or as on bunk bed.  The bed also has a ladder that simply hangs over the edge of the bed rail. The bed has a simple light colored finish (semi-transparent).
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I have had the bed in storage in my garage for the past few years and I knew I ultimately wanted to eventually restore the bed for my son.  Well, Tyler is definitely ready for a big-boy bed, so I started the process of restoring this bed two weeks ago.

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://www.ethanknepp.com/2010/09/state-college-celiac-support-group-with.html">
                       State College Celiac Support Group with Anne Roland Lee
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4945638211165361415" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      Casey, Hailey, and I attended last night's Celiac Support Group Meeting featuring guest speaker Anne Roland Lee from Schar Foods.  It was great to see such a huge turnout for this meeting too.  Thanks to all those that helped get the word.  Anne brought lots of free Schar Foods samples for the group to try out too.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Anne Roland Lee spoke about, “The Nutritional Aspects of the Gluten Free Diet".  She spoke about some really interesting topics that Casey and I haven't thought about, such as the topic of how a celiac might not be getting the nutrition that they need.  I didn't think about it, but she mentioned how many of the gluten-free flours aren't fortified like the glutenous ones, which are regulated by the FDA to be enriched.  This might be one reason Casey is still often fatigued... maybe Casey needs additional supplements to counter the less nutritious gluten-free flours... we'll see.

                  <div class="date-posts">

                    <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://www.ethanknepp.com/2010/08/anniversary-at-cayuga-lake.html">
                       Anniversary at Cayuga Lake
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1842167965717052430" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      Casey surprised me this year with a trip to the finger lakes in NY for our 8th anniversary. This time she found a lovely B &amp; B located in Senaca Falls, NY.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      The B &amp; B was
                      <a href="http://www.vancleefhomestead.com/">
                       VanCleef Homestead
                      </a>
                      owned and operated by Carol and Brent Gibbs.  The B &amp; B was very clean, relaxing and inviting.  Carol served up an unbelievable spread of delicious breakfast foods, including several items there were gluten-free.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Before hitting the trail and sampling wines, we ate lunch at the
                      <a href="http://www.wagnervineyards.com/TheGinnyLee">
                       Ginny Lee
                      </a>
                      located next to
                      <a href="http://www.wagnervineyards.com/">
                       Wagner Vineyards
                      </a>
                      .  This is a great place to catch a bite to eat, the food is great and the price is right!  Its also a great place to pair a Wagner wine or beer with some food.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Casey wanted us to visit some different wineries on the
                      <a href="http://www.cayugawinetrail.com/">
                       Cayuga Lake Wine Trail
                      </a>
                      .  We visited the following wineries on this trip:
                      <br/>
                      <ul>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.thirstyowl.com/">
                         Thirsty Owl Wine Company
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.lucasvineyards.com/">
                         Lucas Winery
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.knappwine.com/">
                         Knapp Winery
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://rednewt.com">
                         Red Newt Cellars
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.hazlitt1852.com/">
                         Hazlitt 1852 Vineyards
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.wagnervineyards.com/">
                         Wagners Vineyards
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://buttonwoodgrove.com/">
                         Buttonwood Grove Winery
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.cobblestonefarmwinery.com/">
                         Cobblestone Winery
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                       <li>
                        <a href="http://www.goosewatch.com/">
                         Goose Watch Winery
                        </a>
                       </li>
                       <br/>
                      </ul>
                      <br/>
                      For dinner on our first night in the NY finger lakes region, we ate at
                      <a href="http://www.suzannefrc.com/">
                       Suzanne's
                      </a>
                      .  This place is great; beautiful view of the sunset, excellent food, and great service (including staff that knows what gluten-free means).
