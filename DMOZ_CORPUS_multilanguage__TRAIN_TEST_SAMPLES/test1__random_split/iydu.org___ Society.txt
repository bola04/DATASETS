          <a href="http://www.iydu.org/iydu-freedom-forum-ecuador/" rel="bookmark" target="_blank" title="Permalink to REGISTRATION NOW OPEN: IYDU Freedom Forum Ecuador">
           REGISTRATION NOW OPEN: IYDU Freedom Forum Ecuador
          </a>
          <span class="separator">
           |
          </span>
          <span class="postDate">
           05 July 2018
          </span>
         </h3>
         <!--	<span class="entry-comments"></span>-->
         <p>
          We are pleased to announce that registrations are now open for the 2018 IYDU Freedom Forum, held jointly with the Union of Latin American Parties (UPLA).    The IYDU Freedom Forum will take place in Quito, Ecuador, 8-10 August 2018, following the UPLA Youth Summit from 5-7 August. IYDU delegates are also welcome to participate in the UPLA Youth Summit.   The UPLA registration form must be returned by 19 July for the Youth Summit and the IYDU registration form by 25 July to confirm your attendance to the Freedom Forum only.
         </p>
         <p>
          Registration Form:
          <a href=" http://www.iydu.org/wp-content/uploads/downloads/2018/07/Formulario-Encuentro-Juventud-UPLA-2018.doc">
           UPLA event (5-8 August)
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.iydu.org/wp-content/uploads/downloads/2018/07/IYDU-FFreg18.docx">
           IYDU Freedom Forum. (8-10 August)
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Draft programme:
          <a href="http://www.iydu.org/wp-content/uploads/downloads/2018/07/DraftAgendaEcuador.pdf">
           Click here to download
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          We look forward to seeing you in Ecuador.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Best regards,
         </p>
         <p>
          The IYDU Executive
         </p>

         <h3 class="entry-title">
          <a href="http://www.iydu.org/referendum/" rel="bookmark" target="_blank" title="Permalink to Referendum">
           Referendum
          </a>
          <span class="separator">
           |
          </span>
          <span class="postDate">
           14 June 2017
          </span>
         </h3>
         <!--	<span class="entry-comments"></span>-->
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The IYDU recognises the inalienable right of every person around the world to be free and to decide on their own governments, and supports a stable and democratic Iraq. The IYDU also recognises the aspirations of the Iraqi Kurdistan people and other minorities living in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq.
         </p>
         <p>
          The IYDU Executive looked into the non-binding independence referendum for Iraqi Kurdistan, which will be held on 25 September 2017, and anonymously believes it is a legitimate process.
         </p>
         <p>
          The IYDU calls on:
          <br/>
          • Recognising the struggle of the Kurds and other components including thousands of martyrs throughout the years of oppression under all kind of atrocities such as chemical attacks.
          <br/>
          • Recognising that the Kurds, Assyrians and other components suffered gravely under the dictatorship of Saddam Hussein, as it was documented throught the United Nations Security Council Resolution 688.
          <br/>
          • Acknowledging that the Kurds played a vital role in stopping the expansion of ISIS and created a precedent that lead to defeating ISIS in Mosul.
          <br/>
          • Recognising the sacrifices of the peshmerga and other groups made while the authorities were absent from playing their role.
          <br/>
          • IYDU is participating in an observation mission on the 25th of September.
          <br/>
          • Believing that any decision concerning the future of Iraq must take into account the constitutional provisions.
          <br/>
          • Supporting a referendum that takes into consideration the rights and freedoms of all ethnicities and religions living under the region of Kurdistan.
         </p>
        </li>

         <h3 class="entry-title">
          <a href="http://www.iydu.org/in-response-to-the-recent-attacks-in-jordan-turkey-germany-and-switzerland/" rel="bookmark" target="_blank" title="Permalink to In response to the recent attacks in Jordan, Turkey, Germany and Switzerland">
           In response to the recent attacks in Jordan, Turkey, Germany and Switzerland
          </a>
          <span class="separator">
           |
          </span>
          <span class="postDate">
           20 December 2016
          </span>
         </h3>
         <!--	<span class="entry-comments"></span>-->
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          In response to the recent attacks in Jordan, Turkey, Germany and Switzerland IYDU Chairman Bashir Wardini issued the following statement:-
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          These are dangerous times we live in; modern warfare in the guise of terrorism has left every major city vulnerable. Barely a day passes without an atrocity taking place. The repercussions of the conflicts of Syria, Iraq and Yemen have reverberated around the world.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Yet in many ways history, repeats itself. Yesterday, while watching the live broadcast of the assassination of Russia’s ambassador to Ankara, I was reminded of the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand – a major cause of WWI. We can only hope that our world leaders recognise what it is at stake and do not retaliate without considering the consequences.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Shortly after the assassination in Ankara, an attack took place at a Christmas market in the German capital, Berlin – a city that has opened its heart to hundreds of thousands of refugees fleeing conflict. The attack was reminiscent of the recent attack in Nice. While families, tourist and students gathered in celebration of the festive season, a truck traveling at high speed ploughed into them – killing 12 and injuring almost 50 more.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          In the shadow of both attacks, a shooting took place in Zurich, Switzerland which left three worshippers at an Islamic centre, severely injured. While on Sunday, gunmen carried out rare terrorist attack in Jordan, killing 10 civilians.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          After every major attack that takes place, the world stands back horrified, grappling to find answers. Each time we vow that such attacks will never happen again, but invariably they do, as more radicalised individuals and groups follow in the footsteps of those who proceed them. We must not live in fear or fall into the trap of sacrificing our values and personal freedoms in these difficult times – as this is the ultimate goal of the radicalised. However we must not be afraid to challenge the way we have been dealing with these situations and identifying their root causes. Until we find a deterant to radicalisation, these attacks against civilians will continue, indiscriminate of race, country and religion. We also ask the International Community to redefine its policy on foreign affairs by adopting a unified one that prioritises principles and eradicates radicalism at the source.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Our sympathy and solidarity is with the victims, our thoughts with the wounded, and our hearts with those who have lost friends or family in the horrific attack.
         </p>
        </li>

         <h3 class="entry-title">
          <a href="http://www.iydu.org/iydu-on-human-rights-day/" rel="bookmark" target="_blank" title="Permalink to IYDU on Human Rights Day">
           IYDU on Human Rights Day
          </a>
          <span class="separator">
           |
          </span>
          <span class="postDate">
           10 December 2016
          </span>
         </h3>
         <!--	<span class="entry-comments"></span>-->
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Chairman Bashir Wardini issued the following statement on Human Rights Day:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          2016 wasn’t exactly a kind year to millions around the globe; human rights regressed in countries that recognise the declaration and surprisingly, even, in advanced democracies. Fear denied millions from the rule of law, justice, opportunity, equality, love and basic rights. Even the United Nation’s human rights chief dubbed 2016 as a “disastrous year”. In some parts of the world, people are escaping war zones, creating an unprecedented influx of refugees, in other parts, people are facing systematic discrimination based on their ethnicity or religion and minorities are facing persecutions. In big parts of the world, people are still denied from their basic rights. It is hard to believe that in 2016, countries that helped draft the declaration of Human Rights just abolished a marriage rape law, even if it was not commonly executed. It is also hard to believe that child marriage still exists around the globe, or that in some countries, women are not allowed to drive and that you could receive the death penalty for love. It is even harder to accept that elected leaders are calling for manslaughter against drug abusers without a trial.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          This year proved that we are still afraid from one another and that our response to fear is repressive policies and laws that target civil society or even censor social media from fear that it will energize social and political movements.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If anything, this year unmasked that abuses are happening in our countries, under our own watch. It unveiled that the fight is still long and we are far behind in ending child marriage, corruption, poverty, racial disparities, abuses against women and girls. We are also far behind in reforming rights to health, labor, youth, people with disabilities, prisons and jail conditions among others.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Sometimes we forget that all people have the right to live in decency, under a roof, without famine, with access to education and freedom. Let’s make this coming year count by standing for others, as IYDU has been doing for the past decade.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
