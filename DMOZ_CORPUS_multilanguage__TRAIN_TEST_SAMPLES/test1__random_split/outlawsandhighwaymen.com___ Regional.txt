    <h1>
     The History of the Highwaymen
     <br/>
     and their Predecessors,
     <br/>
     the Medieval Outlaws
    </h1>
    <img alt="" height="20" src="images/blackfleuron.gif" style="margin-left: 100px; clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 10pt;" width="17"/>
    <br clear="all"/>
    <p class="header">
     The texts assembled on this site illustrate the history of the English highwaymen.
    </p>
    <!-- <br clear="all"> -->
    <div class="box1">
     <p style="margin-top: 25px;">
      Many are not easily available 
 elsewhere. They include:
     </p>
    </div>
    <div class="box1">
     <img alt="Figure of a highwayman from a Victorian toy theatre" height="270" src="images/tomking.gif" style="float: right; margin-top: 5pt; margin-left; 5pt; margin-bottom: 5pt;" width="388"/>
     <ul class="star">
      <li>
       street ballads
      </li>
      <li>
       songs
      </li>
      <li>
       extracts from pamphlet ‘lives’
      </li>
      <li>
       ‘merry tales’
      </li>
      <li>
       underworld exposés
      </li>
      <li>
       satires
      </li>
      <li>
       letters
      </li>
     </ul>
    </div>
    <br clear="all"/>
    <div class="box2">
     <p>
      I set up this website in 2001 to accompany the publication of my book
      <a href="book.htm" tabindex="60" title="book abstract">
       Outlaws and Highwaymen
      </a>
      , a social and cultural history of the English 
 highway robber. You will find here many of the sources that I cite in the text 
 and notes.
     </p>
    </div>
    <br clear="all"/>
    <div class="box1">
     <a href="book.htm" title="Outlaws and Highwaymen: details">
      <img alt="Cover of Outlaws and Highwaymen: thumbnail" height="350" src="images/tn_outlaws2.jpg" style="float: left; margin-right: 20pt; margin-bottom: 10pt;" width="230"/>
     </a>
     <p style="margin-top: 0;">
      But the website is meant for anyone who is interested, not just the people who have read my book.
     </p>
     <p>
      A good place to start is the outline
      <a href="history.htm" tabindex="70" title="short history of robbery in England">
       history
      </a>
      of the outlaws and highwaymen. On the other hand, you may prefer to go 
 straight to the
      <a href="contents.htm" tabindex="80" title="indexes to documents">
       contents
      </a>
      page, 
 to look up stories, ballads and other kinds of material. Or you can browse the new
      <a href="gallery.htm" tabindex="90" title="images of highwaymen">
       picture gallery
      </a>
      . I hope that you will find plenty to enjoy.
     </p>
