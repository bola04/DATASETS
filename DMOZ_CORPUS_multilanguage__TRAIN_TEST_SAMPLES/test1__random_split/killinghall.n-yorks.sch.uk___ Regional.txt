                 <p style="text-align: left;">
                  -
                  <span style="font-size: 16px; font-weight: bold;">
                   A word of welcome from the Headteacher - Mrs Sarah Bassitt
                   <span class="image-block caption-over-image" style="margin-left: 20px; float: right; width:125px;">
                    <img alt="" height="154" id="mce-592" src="attachments/Image/4E35F7D4FC.JPG" width="125"/>
                   </span>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 13px;">
                   Whether you are just beginning to think about your childs education or whether your child is already a partof the Killinghall Church of England Primary School community, we would like to take this opportunityto welcome you to our school. We hope that you will find our website interesting and informative. We area small, very friendly school with approximately 110 pupils.We are currently under going a building expansion with two new classrooms, this means that we have places in each year group at the moment.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 13px;">
                   Our ethos is based upon our Christian Values; our hard working and enthusiastic staff team place the individual needs of the children at the centre of their thoughts and actions.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 13px;">
                   Our school is anoutstanding church school that is rated by Ofsted as a good school, with outstanding behaviour and excellent Spiritual, Moral, Social, Cultural education -2012. Our June 2017 Ofsted Inspection confirmed the we continue to be a GOOD school.The recent success of our school is based upon the strongpartnershipsthat we have developed with pupils, parents, governors and the whole local community. Our School isa full strategic memberof the Harrogate and Rural Teaching School Alliance - a group of localschools who work collaboratively to improve outcomes for children.As a school we believe indeveloping a self-improving, self-sustaining school system to ensure that all our stakeholders realise their potential, and in turn create exciting opportunities and positive resultsfor our children.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <span style="font-size: 13px;">
                   Webelievethat our curriculum inspires and broadens childrens experiences and passion for learning. This belief drives what happens at our school. We are dedicated to ensuring that the children develop a real love of learning. We believe that we have a duty to help every child to become a rounded individual who is able to play their full part within an ever changing society. We were one of the schools involved in the local region in implementing the London Institute of Education and RSA 'Grand Curriculum Designs Programme'. Staff, parents, pupils, governors, and the Killinghall community were involved in the development of our curriculum.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p style="text-align: left;">
                  <span style="font-size: 13px;">
                   Our approach to education is holistic; we are concerned for the whole child  mind, body and spirit. We offer an exciting and challenging education for all. SMSC (Spiritual, Moral, Social, and Cultural) is threaded through all curriculum areas, activities and collective worship; we actively engage pupils in understanding fundamental British values. We encourage the children in our school to engage with our Christian Values and live these every day.
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p style="text-align: left;">
                  We celebrate our pupils achievements in all areas and recognise thatevery individualhas different strengths and talents. We encourage positive attitudes towards learning and behaviour, settinghigh aspirationsand challenging every child to be the best that they can be.
                 </p>
                 <p style="text-align: justify;">
                  The staff, governors and pupils are all proud to be a part of Killinghall Church of England Primary School and work hard to make it the special place it is. Visitors to school often remark on the caring and friendly atmosphere within it, and praise our children for their knowledge, manners andsupportive attitudetowards each other.
                 </p>
                 <p style="text-align: justify;">
                  For further information about admission to our school please follow the side link from the 'About us' tab. If you wish to visit or simply require further information about the school please ring the school office on 01423 506307 during normal school hours.
