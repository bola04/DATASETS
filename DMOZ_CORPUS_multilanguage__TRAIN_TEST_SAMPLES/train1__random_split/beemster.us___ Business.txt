                  <div class="sqs-block-content">
                   <h3 id="-where-can-i-buy-beemster-">
                    + Where can I buy Beemster?
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Beemster is available at grocery stores, specialty cheese shops and online retailers across the United States and Canada. Use the
                    <a href="/store-locator">
                     store locator
                    </a>
                    to find your nearest location.
                   </p>
                   <h3 id="-can-i-eat-beemster-cheeses-if-i-am-lactose-intolerant-">
                    + Can I eat Beemster cheeses if I am lactose intolerant?
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Most Beemster cheeses are safe to eat if you’re lactose intolerant. In the cheesemaking process, Beemster washes the curds multiple times, which removes much of the lactose. Remaining lactose breaks down and converts to lactic acid during natural ripening. So cheese that’s aged four months or longer tends to not bother people who are lactose intolerant.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Beemster cheeses that are aged at least four months include:
                    <a href="/products/classic">
                     <strong>
                      Classic
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    (aka Aged, 18 months),
                    <a href="/products/x-o">
                     <strong>
                      X - O
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    ( aka Extra Aged, 26 months),
                    <a href="/products/signature">
                     <strong>
                      Signature
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    (12 months),
                    <a href="/products/paradiso">
                     <strong>
                      Paradiso
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    ,
                    <a href="/products/vlaskaas">
                     <strong>
                      Vlaskaas
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    ,
                    <a href="/products/farmers-choice">
                     <strong>
                      Farmers’ Choice
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    (aka Medium, 6 months), and
                    <a href="/products/beemster-lite">
                     <strong>
                      Beemster Lite
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                    (aka 2% Milk, 5 months).
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Please note that Beemster flavored cheeses, such as our Mustard, Pumpkin Spice, and Red Pepper varieties do contain lactose. All Beemster cheeses contain milk.
                   </p>
                   <h3 id="-do-beemster-cheeses-contain-gluten-">
                    + Do Beemster cheeses contain gluten?
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Beemster cheeses are completely gluten free.
                   </p>
                   <h3 id="-if-cheese-gets-moldy-should-i-throw-it-away-">
                    + If cheese gets moldy, should I throw it away?
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Most mold is harmless, but to be safe, cut away one inch of cheese on all sides of any cheese that has visible mold and be sure to use the remaining cheese as quickly as possible. For more on mold, read the
                    <a href="http://1.usa.gov/1QnwJWU">
                     FDA’s guide to food safety
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <h3 id="-can-i-eat-the-rind-of-beemster-cheese-">
                    + Can I eat the rind of Beemster cheese?
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    Please don’t eat the rind! While it’s an essential part of the aging process and protects the cheese from dehydrating and molding, the rind partially consists of a plastic coating that isn’t digestible, so it’s best to always remove the rind before consuming Beemster cheeses.
                   </p>
