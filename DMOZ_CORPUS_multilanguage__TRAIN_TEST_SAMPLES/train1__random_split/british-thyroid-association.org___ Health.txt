           <div class="editor">
            <p>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             The British Thyroid Association is committed to promoting research and interest in the field of thyroidology and to the enhancement of clinical care to patients with thyroid disorders.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             A non profit making Learned Society, membership of the BTA is restricted toÂ professional clinical specialist doctors, retired consultants, academics, scientists, trainees, nurses and medical students in the UK who manage patients with thyroid disease and/or are researching into the thyroid and its disease in humans.
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             If you are working in this area we would encourage you to take out membershipÂ as this will provide fresh ideas, inspiration and new knowledge via yourÂ peers. It also offersÂ several benefits including:
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
            </p>
            <ul style="text-align: left;">
             <li>
              The opportunity to attend scientific meetings on the thyroid and thyroid disease
             </li>
             <li>
              The chance to share your abstract at
              <a href="http://www.british-thyroid-association.org/dont-miss">
               our annual meeting
              </a>
              each May and its subsequent publication in
              <a href="https://thyroidresearchjournal.biomedcentral.com/">
               Thyroid Research
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              The opportunity to submit a blog post aimed at patients
             </li>
             <li>
              Discounted registration fees for BTA and BES conferences
             </li>
             <li>
              A twice yearlyÂ newsletter which keeps you in touch with thyroid related issues
             </li>
             <li>
              Discounted membership of the BTF
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
             <a href="/sandbox/bta2016/new_member_application_2017.pdf" target="_blank">
              Please click here to download our membership form
             </a>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: left;">
            </p>
            <h3 style="text-align: left;">
             <strong>
              DON'T MISS
             </strong>
            </h3>
            <p style="text-align: left;">

               Please click here to read about thyroid related events and meetings which are held throughout the year

              <h3>
               <a href="/dont-miss">
                The BTF Research Award
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="offer-description editor ellipsis">
               <p>
                <span style="font-size: 8.5pt; font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; color: black;">
                 Since 1997 t
                </span>
                he British Thyroid Foundation (BTF) has offeredÂ an annual award to support a one-year research project into thyroid function or thyroid disorders. Known as the BTF Doris Godfrey Research Award, this yearâs award is up to Â£20,000 and has been funded by a legacy.
               </p>
               <p>
                Applicants uncertain of the suitability of their proposal may submit a 150-word overview of their proposal for informal feedback from the BTF prior to submission of a full application, by contacting the BTF at
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="mailto:research-award@btf-thyroid.org">
                 research-award@btf-thyroid.org
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Who can apply?
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                Researchers working on projects in the UK. The project must be based in the UK (i.e. the majority of the work must be carried out in the UK), supervised in the UK and publications arising from the research must be in an English language journal from a UK centre. The BTF must be acknowledged in any publication. The applicant must ensure that appropriate ethical approval is in place wherever research is carried out.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 How can the award be used?
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                The award can be used to supplement existing projects or to help get research ideas started. The funds are available for consumables, running costs and equipment but not personal computers. Any equipment purchased will belong to your institution after the project is completed. Support for staff costs/salaries will only be considered if they can be justified.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                <span style="font-size: 8.5pt; font-family: 'Verdana','sans-serif'; color: black;">
                 The application form, a short CV and a high resolution passport-sized photo should be submitted by
                 <strong>
                  midnight on
                 </strong>
                 <strong>
                  31 January 2018
                 </strong>
                 .

                  For full details click here
                 </a>
