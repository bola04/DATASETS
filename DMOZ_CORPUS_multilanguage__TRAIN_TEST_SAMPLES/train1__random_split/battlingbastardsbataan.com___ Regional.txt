     <span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:Verdana">

    <p style="margin-top:5.0pt;margin-right:.6in;margin-bottom:5.0pt;margin-left:
 .6in">
     <span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:Verdana">
      The picture above is of
 the Camp O'Donnell Memorial Monument. The memorial was built by the
 organization known as "The Battling Bastards of Bataan" to honor
 those American men who died at
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       <st1:placetype w:st="on">
        Camp
       </st1:placetype>
       <st1:placename w:st="on">
        O'Donnell
       </st1:placename>
      </st1:place>
      , while
 prisoners of the Japanese. The Cement Cross is a replica of the original cement
 cross built by the POWs.
      <o:p>
      </o:p>
     </span>
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top:5.0pt;margin-right:.6in;margin-bottom:5.0pt;margin-left:
 .6in">
     <span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:Verdana">
      The monument is
 located in the
      <span class="SpellE">
       Capas
      </span>
      National Shrine, in
      <span class="SpellE">
       Capas
      </span>
      ,
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       <st1:city w:st="on">
        <span class="SpellE">
         Tarlac
        </span>
       </st1:city>
       ,
       <st1:country-region w:st="on">
        Philippines
       </st1:country-region>
      </st1:place>
      ,
 adjacent to the memorial for the Philippine Army dead.
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       <st1:placetype w:st="on">
        Camp
       </st1:placetype>
       <st1:placename w:st="on">
        O'Donnell
       </st1:placename>
      </st1:place>
      was the first prison camp for the men who survived the "Death March".
 The picture was taken by James Litton.
      <o:p>
      </o:p>
     </span>
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top:5.0pt;margin-right:.6in;margin-bottom:5.0pt;margin-left:
 .6in">
     <span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:Verdana">
      The "Cross"
 was built as a memorial to the thousands who died in that camp. It is as much a
 part of
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      as the participants in that
 battle. The inscription on the base of the "Cross" reads "
      <span class="SpellE">
       Omnia
      </span>
      Pro Patria": All For Country. On the wall behind
 the "Cross" are inscribed the names of the men who died at
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       <st1:placetype w:st="on">
        Camp
       </st1:placetype>
       <st1:placename w:st="on">
        O'Donnell
       </st1:placename>
      </st1:place>
      .
      <o:p>
      </o:p>
     </span>
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top:5.0pt;margin-right:.6in;margin-bottom:5.0pt;margin-left:
 .6in">
     <span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:Verdana">
      The original
 "Cement Cross" is now on display in the National Prisoner of War
 Museum, at the Andersonville National Historic Site,
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       <st1:city w:st="on">
        Andersonville
       </st1:city>
       ,
       <st1:state w:st="on">
        GA.
       </st1:state>
      </st1:place>
      It was brought to this country by
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      survivors.
      <o:p>
      </o:p>
     </span>
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top:5.0pt;margin-right:.6in;margin-bottom:5.0pt;margin-left:
 .6in">
     <span style="font-size:16.0pt;font-family:Verdana">
      This page is dedicated
 to the brave warriors who served in the battle fields of
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       <st1:city w:st="on">
        Bataan
       </st1:city>
       ,
       <st1:country-region w:st="on">
        Philippines
       </st1:country-region>
      </st1:place>
      ,
 during World War II. We wish to provide all who enter this page with an
 accurate account of the American and Filipino heroisms in
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      ,
 the conditions endured during the "Death March" and their subsequent
 imprisonment in various prison camps. "Our purpose as an organization is
 quite simple. We wish to perpetuate the story of
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      and to leave to our descendants the truth as we knew it. We wish to correct the
 myths and outright lies concerning the events of
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      in 1941-1942. We want to provide information on the
      <st1:city w:st="on">
       Battle
      </st1:city>
      for
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      and it's subsequent fall on April
 9, 1942. This organization serves as a reminder that the precepts of courage,
 devotion to duty and sacrifice displayed by the men and women of
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      , both Filipino and American, have not and will not
 become outmoded. Our motto simply states: 'Keep the faith in our country: as
 exhibited by those men and women who fought in defense of
      <st1:place w:st="on">
       Bataan
      </st1:place>
      ,
 Philippine in 1942. Freedom is not free.'
      <o:p>
      </o:p>
     </span>
    </p>
