        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s7" style="left: 16px; top: -17.27px;margin:0;">
         <span class="Heading_1 xr_s8" style="">
          My name is Bill Nesbitt and I play and teach banjo in the central Little Rock area. I've played banjo
         </span>
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 93px; top: 4.73px;margin:0;">
         since I was in high school in the mid-70's. I teach 5-string, Scruggs-based bluegrass style banjo.
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 93px; top: 23.8px;margin:0;">
         On this site you will find banjo instructional videos, tablature for several popular banjo tunes,
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 93px; top: 42.86px;margin:0;">
         plus other tools and advice to help the beginner as well as the more experienced picker.
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 18px; top: 75.05px;margin:0;">
         I teach bluegrass banjo lessons in my home studio right here in Little Rock. I've taught retirees, waitresses,
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 16px; top: 94.12px;margin:0;">
         grade schoolers and college kids, doctors and lawyers... they all learned differently, but they all shared one
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 2px; top: 113.18px;margin:0;">
         thing in common: the same love for and fascination with the five-string bluegrass banjo that I've had ever since
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="top: 132.25px;margin:0;">
         I could remember.
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="top: 170.37px;margin:0;">
         I start with the basics. Is the banjo at least semi-decent to begin with? (Too many aren't.) Is it set up properly?
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="top: 189.44px;margin:0;">
         Do you have picks and know how to wear them properly? (Don't be ashamed, it's really counter-intuitive.) Then
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="top: 208.5px;margin:0;">
         it's on to open string rolls, a few basic chords and licks, and everybody’s starting-off tune, Cripple Creek! Still
        </h1>
        <h1 class="xr_tl Heading_1 xr_s8" style="left: 5px; top: 227.57px;margin:0;">
         one of my all-time favorites. You'd think I'd be sick of that song by now, but I love it.
        </h1>
