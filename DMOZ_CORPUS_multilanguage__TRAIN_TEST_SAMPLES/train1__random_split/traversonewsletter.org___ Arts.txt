          <div style="text-align: center;">
           <font style="font-size: 16px;">
            Welcome to the website of
            <b>
             <i>
              TRAVERSO
             </i>
            </b>
            , the quarterly newsletter for people interested in the history of the flute, its repertoire and performance practice. Each issue includes an article on a subject of interest to historical
 flutists, along with announcements about current events, new
 recordings and publications. The newsletter was founded by Ardal Powell in 1989, and is currently edited by Linda Pereksta.
           </font>
           <br/>
          </div>
          <br/>
          <font style="font-size: 16px;">
          </font>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <div style="text-align: justify;">
           <b>
            Linda Pereksta
           </b>
           has served as Chair of the National Flute
 Association’s Historical Flutes Committee, and was twice a major
 prizewinner in the NFA’s triennial Baroque Flute Artist Competition.
           <span>
           </span>
           An
 active recitalist and chamber musician, she has performed with
           <i>
            Washington Bach Consort,
            <i>
             Spire Baroque Orchestra
            </i>
            , Madison Bach Musicians
           </i>
           ,
           <i>
            Ensemble Musical Offering
           </i>
           ,
           <i>
            Tallahassee Bach
 Parley
           </i>
           ,
           <i>
            Baroque Southeast
           </i>
           ,
           <i>
            Belle Meade Baroque
           </i>
           , and others. She has written on subjects related to
 historical flutes for
           <i>
            The Flutist Quarterly
           </i>
           and
           <i>
            Flute Talk
           </i>
           , has performed and presented at numerous NFA conventions and
 regional flute festivals, and her doctoral
 treatise, “Twentieth-Century Compositions for the Baroque Flute,” was a
 winner of the NFA’s 2004 Graduate Research Competition.
           <span>
            Her main teachers were
           </span>
           Betty Bang Mather, Robert Willoughby and Colin St. Martin, and she has had additional studies with Christopher Krueger, Claire Guimond and Rachel Brown. Linda teaches Flute and directs the Early Music Ensemble at The
 University of Wisconsin Oshkosh.
