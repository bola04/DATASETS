      <p class="Body" style="line-height:125%">
       <font face="Bookman Old Style">
        <span style="line-height: 125%">
         Acupuncture uses solid “Dry” needles on 
     acupuncture points. AIT (Acupoint Injection Therapy)  is called ”Shui Zen” 
     meaning “Wet Needle” used on acupuncture points. Both techniques are 
     effective! Each has a therapeutic value that may be stronger with different 
     issues affecting the body.
        </span>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p class="Body" style="line-height:125%">

          Acupuncture
         </span>
        </b>
        <span style="line-height: 125%">
         is amazing for moving blood and energy and assisting the body with 
     constitutional issues, or issues that affect the whole functioning of the 
     body.
         <b>
          AIT
         </b>
         specializes old or new injuries causing pain or spasms. AIT 
     leaves gentle medicine derived from natural sources to nourish tissues and 
     nerves that have been compromised and are struggling to regain their normal 
     state of health. This medicine remains at the distressed site to assist the 
     starved tissues to heal and alleviate pain while accelerating healing.
