      <h3 style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 10px;">
       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
        <font color="#003c00" face="Arial Black">
         Shock Absorber Basics
        </font>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 10px;">
       <font color="#003c00">
        <img align="left" alt=" MX suspension setup for a twin chamber fork configuration with a custom ICS spring rate." border="0" height="319" src="Figs_Title/2-twin-chamber-fork.png" width="122"/>
        <span style="font-size: 12pt">
         Damping forces produced by a shock are the result of controlled fluid dynamic flow losses through the
       suspension circuits. Flow losses are controlled by the stiffness of the shim stack on the shock absorber valve.
       At low speed the shim stack is barely cracked open resulting in high jet velocities, high
       flow losses and a high damping force.
       As shaft speeds increase fluid pressure forces the stack to open further producing
       a progressively 
       larger flow area and controlled progression of the shocks damping force.
        </span>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 10px;">
       <font color="#003c00">
        <span style="font-size: 12pt">
         ReStackor uses multi-physics simulations to model that process combining the fundamentals of
         <a href="Physics/Stack_Stiffness/stack-stiffness.htm">
          structure mechanics
         </a>
         with
         <a href="Physics/Fluid_Dynamics/fluid-dyn.htm">
          fluid dynamics
         </a>
         to  numerically model
       shim
       stack stiffness, flow area, fluid dynamic flow losses and the resulting
       shock absorber damping force.
        </span>
       </font>
      </p>
      <p align="center" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 10px;">
       <img alt="A trail bike suspension setup needs shim stack taper changes to retune the low speed damping." border="0" height="200" src="Figs_Title/3-trail-bike-suspension-tuning.png" width="438"/>
      </p>
      <p align="left" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 10px;">
       <font color="#003c00">
        <span style="font-size: 12pt">
         Basing
       ReStackor calculations on the application of
         <a href="Physics/model-physics.htm">
          basic physics
         </a>
         makes the calculations equally applicable to
       tuning
       of
       motorcycle suspensions, 
       automotive racing shocks, trophy trucks or the setup of mountain bike
       suspensions.
        </span>
       </font>
      </p>

      <h3 style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 10px; margin-right: 5px;">
       <font face="Arial Black">
        <span style="font-size: 12pt">
         Suspension
       Response
        </span>
       </font>
      </h3>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       <span style="font-size: 12pt">
        ReStackor suspension response calculations compute
       the suspension link ratio from measurements of wheel and shock position. Suspension bottoming velocities are computed from
       inputs of weight and spring rate using the shocks damping force computed by
       ReStackor pro. Response outputs show the suspension velocities over the
       stroke, damping force and rebound response zeta coefficients. Response
       calculations are an add-on to ReStackor pro and run on the Response tab of
       ReStackor mid-valve spreadsheets.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       <img border="0" height="538" src="Figs_Title/5-response-screen.png" width="950"/>
      </p>
      <h3 style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       Baseline suspension setup
      </h3>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       Suspension tuning is a whole lot easier
       if you can start from a “good spot”. ReStackor suspension response
       calculations define the ballpark of where that “good spot” is using
       basic suspension tuning rules-of-thumb to define damping targets for compression
       and rebound:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
         <b>
          Rebound damping:
         </b>
         zeta values of 0.7 to
           0.75
        </p>
       </li>
       <li>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
         <b>
          Rebound/Compression damping ratio:
         </b>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
           Shock: 2. to 2.5:1
          </p>
         </li>
         <li>
          <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
           Fork: 3 to 3.5:1
          </p>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
         <b>
          Low speed compression:
         </b>
         Damping ratio of
           0.8:1 below suspension speed of 3 to 6 in/sec
        </p>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       <img border="0" height="324" src="Figs_Title/6-targets.png" width="918"/>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       Spring-mass-damper theory defines rebound
       damping zeta values of 0.7 to give the fastest possible suspension response with
       damping that is still stiff enough to suppress suspension resonance
       motions. That fact sets rebound damping for the baseline setup at zeta
       values of 0.7 to 0.75. Go stiffer and the slow rebound response causes the
       suspension to pack. Go softer and the suspension baby-buggies.
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       Over a series of bumps suspensions will
       either pack or jack. To prevent packing suspensions need low speed compression damping to be slighter
       stiffer than rebound. The
       tuning target is a rebound/compression damping ratio of 0.8:1 below
       suspension speeds of 3 to 6 in/sec. That keeps the
       suspension riding “high in the stroke” over small trail trash bumps.
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       <img alt="ReStackor suspension response calculations" border="0" height="336" src="Figs_Title/7-baseline-comp.png" width="918"/>
      </p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 150%; margin-left: 15px; 
 margin-right: 5px; margin-bottom: 12pt;">
       High speed compression damping is set
       by the ratio of rebound/compression damping. Baseline tuning sets peak compression damping forces
       to be approximately equal to
       rebound. That gives the suspension a consistent “feel” through the
       stroke. Outside that damping range you are better off going to a softer or stiffer spring. (
       <a href="Overview/response.htm">
        more
       </a>
       )
      </p>
