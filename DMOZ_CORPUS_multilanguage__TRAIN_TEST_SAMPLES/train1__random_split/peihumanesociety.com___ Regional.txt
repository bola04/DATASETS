          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           PEI's Only Shelter for Lost, Abandoned &amp; Homeless
          </h1>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           Companion Animals.
          </h1>
          <h1 style="text-align: center;">
           We love what we do!
          </h1>
          <hr/>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>

          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <strong>
            The PEIHS provides litter, food, toys and all supplies needed to all foster care homes caring for the influx of Mother cats and kittens during the year.  They stay in foster care for 12 weeks until they are socialized enough to be adopted.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <hr/>
          <h2>
           The PEI Humane Society (PEIHS) has eliminated mandatory fees for surrendering an owned pet.
          </h2>
          To cover one day of basic care, fees were previously $25 per animal to a maximum of $60. In order to help owners who struggle financially, the fees will now be voluntary. In 2017, 665 stray animals were brought to the shelter. The PEIHS will also have a mobile surrender day in Richmond, PEI starting on the last Wednesday of every month. The PEIHS hopes that with the removal of barriers like this, the shelter sees a reduction of abandoned animals and humane cases. Giving up a pet is a difficult decision and PEIHS staff provide a judgement free environment with the goal of healthy outcomes for companion animals in need.
          <strong>
           If an owner lives in or west of Summerside they may surrender their pet at the PEIHS mobile intake day. Staff will be located at the Ralph W. Maclellan Sports Centre, parking lot at 10:00 a.m., every fourth Wednesday of the month starting May 30th. Owners must make an appointment first by calling the shelter
          </strong>
          .

          <h2>
           KITTEN season is HERE!
 Do your part to help SAVE lives.
 SPAY &amp; NEUTER your pets!
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             🐾
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          <hr/>
          Kitten season is the time of year starting in spring and ending in fall ( April- October ) when cats give birth, flooding animal shelters and rescue groups across the nation with homeless litters. Sadly, many of these kittens never reach an animal shelter. Those who survive too often become feral as they reach maturity. These feral cats then reproduce, thereby re-initiating the cycle of suffering.
 
 Read on to learn how you can help homeless kittens this season!
          <h2>
           1)Spay and neuter your own pets
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             😸
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          Cats can reproduce as early as 4 months of age, and can become pregnant every 4 months thereafter. If you have felines four months of age or older on your property, make sure they are spayed or neutered as soon as possible and that they do not have any exposure to other cats of the opposite sex prior to the animal’s spay or neuter surgery.
          <h2>
           2) Look out for kittens
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             👀
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          Spring through fall, you are more likely to see small kittens living along roadsides,highways, porches, decks &amp; sheds etc. If you find a stray kitten on a roadside or elsewhere, and can safely transport the kitten to the PEI Humane Society, do so. Bear in mind that where there is one young kitten, there are likely more. If you can do so safely, search the vicinity of where the kitten was found for any siblings. You are very likely find more. If you cannot search the area safely, note the exact location where you saw the kitten (mile-marker, landmark, etc.) and contact the PEI Humane Society Animal Protection Officers.
 - 892-1190 or apo@peihumanesociety.com-
          <h2>
           3) Leave newborn kittens with their mothers.
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             🐣
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          Survival rates for newborn kittens without their mothers – even in a shelter environment – top out at about 50%.  If you find kittens, try and capture the mother along with them. If you cannot capture the mother along with the kittens, but the mother is supporting them,  wait until the kittens are at least 5 weeks of age before bringing them inside or to our shelter. If you find a kitten before 5 weeks of age without their mother, PLEASE bring them into the shelter immediately as they require specialized care!
          <h2>
           4)Low Cost Spay/Neuter Assistance Programs
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             💲
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          If your want to keep your pet, but need financial help with spaying or neutering, contact SpayAid PEI. SpayAid PEI is a volunteer non-profit organization dedicated to eliminating pet over-population on Prince Edward Island by providing a spay/neuter assistance program for Island residents most in need.
          <h2>
           5) Trap Neuter Release Programs (TNR)
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             🐈
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          The Cat Action Team of Prince Edward Island is an all-volunteer registered charitable organization working to care for and control the feral and stray cat population throughout the province.
 The mandate of the Cat Action Team’s trap-neuter release program is to reduce the number of reproducing feral or unowned cats within colonies, and to attempt to control infectious diseases.
          <h2>
           6)Know about “upper respiratory” infections.
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">
             🏥
            </span>
           </span>
          </h2>
          Upper respiratory infections (typically bacterial) affect many stray kittens. The most obvious symptom involves swollen, inflamed eyes. Often pus and discharge can be observed in the eyes and nose. When treated early, these infections can usually be cured with a prescription topical medicine. However, if left to fester, upper respiratory infections can result in permanent blindness, or even loss of one or both eyes. If you recover a kitten who appears ill, transport the kitten to the nearest vet clinic, Atlantic Vet College, or The PEI Humane Society.

           7) Donations to our shelter!
           <span class="_5mfr _47e3">
            <span class="_7oe">

 -Soft sided playpens &amp; carriers
