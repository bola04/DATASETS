         with Japanese and some American and European. But in China, you compete with everybody and

         compete with them. Matsushita, a Japanese microwave oven manufacturer find Chinese-brand
         microwave ovens sold in the department stores are identical to those Matsushita produces

          <td align="left" height="290" width="461">
           <font face="Arial" size="2">
            Yes it is true at
         most time but not at every time. Most foreign businesspersons in China know a Chinese
         word, "Guanxi", which means who you know or the contacts you have can play a key
         role to help you to accomplish your business smoothly in China. "Guanxi" is
         important everywhere in the world but not like the Chinese to give it too much emphasis.
         Sometime you may fall in the "Guanxi" trap and confuse yourself. When you try to
         get in the Chinese market, many people will come to you and tell you they have good
         "Guanxi" and can win the deal for you. For one target company, one person may
         told you he has "Guanxi" with manager A; another person may told you he has
         "Guanxi" with manager B; and on and on.  At the end you don't know who is
         decision maker.   This kind of harassment often makes you confuse and even upset.
         After hiring these people with good "Guanxi", many companies finally found out
         that they are not only incapable but also screw up their business. Some companies give up
         the open bid for a project because they think they have no "Guanxi" even the bid
         actually is fair and open. Please don't give up. You can contact your target company
         directly without referrals and you will surprise that you can establish so many useful
         "Guanxi" by yourself. What you need to do is just finding
