                      <h1>
                       OC Watershed
                      </h1>
                     </div>
                     <div class="edtdiv ">
                      <p>
                       OC Watersheds is a division in the Orange County Public Works department that coordinates
                       <a href="http://ocwatersheds.com/gov/pw/watersheds/programs/ourws" target="_blank">
                        watershed planning efforts
                       </a>
                       , collects water
                       <a href="http://ocwatersheds.com/gov/pw/watersheds/rainrecords/rainfalldata" target="_blank">
                        quality, hydrologic, and meteorological data
                       </a>
                       , develops large-scale
                       <a href="http://ocwatersheds.com/gov/pw/watersheds/programs/waterways/enveng/" target="_blank">
                        water quality projects
                       </a>
                       ,
                       <a href="http://ocwatersheds.com/gov/pw/watersheds/programs/waterways/tmdl/" target="_blank">
                        reduces pollutants
                       </a>
                       in local waterbodies, and ensures
                       <a href="http://ocwatersheds.com/gov/pw/watersheds/programs/waterways/stormwater/" target="_blank">
                        countywide compliance
                       </a>
                       with state and federal water quality regulations. For additional information on the drought, including turf removal rebate program, visit
                       <a href="http://www.overwateringisout.org/" target="_blank">
                        overwateringisout.org
                       </a>
                       .
                      </p>
                      <h2>
                       Mission Statement
                      </h2>
                      <p>
                       To develop regional management strategies to preserve, protect, and enhance coastal resources and surface waters throughout Orange County. A watershed approach considers the entire geographic area that a watercourse drains to address a broad range of issues.
                      </p>
