        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Public Sector Procurement can be longwinded and ludicrous, especially dealing with complex 
 			technological issues. The Scottish Parliament, with a selected supplier LogicaCMG, demystified 
 			the procurement process &amp; got to grips with competitive dialogue. Lynda illustrated how an 
 			innovative
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PS%20DEC%2007%20Mailshot%20LKT_NEW.pdf">
          service solution
         </a>
         had the Scots leading the way. These service solutions will be 
 			updated 2013 with more of Lynda's features in publications and for her events work.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Lynda met with Neil Couling - the then Jobcentre Plus Director for Benefits &amp; Fraud at Dept 
 			for Work &amp; Pensions who has been called an "inspirational leader" &amp; now reforming welfare 
 			in the UK. LKT found a tonic for the brain pain suffered in his previous Directorate in the 
 			thick of transformation and targets in the delivery of benefits to around 5million people 
 			and the UK's continuing fight against welfare fraud. This is an area Lynda will be following -
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/LKT_OCT07NeilCouling.pdf">
          find out more
         </a>
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Lynda discussed security for the 2012 London Olympics with Tarique Ghaffur when he was 
 			Metropolitan Police Assistant Commissioner &amp; Head of the Olympics Security Direcorate. 
 			Also with  British Transport Police's then Assistant Chief Constable David McCall. She discovered
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/LKTOlympicssecurityPSJune%2007.pdf">
          "Securing an Olympic Dream"
         </a>
         was more than protecting at least 33 venues, 10,500 athletes from 204 
 			nations, 250,000 accredited persons and 500,000+ daily spectators. The World in 2012 saw the 
 			'Made in Britain' Olympics Games excelled in gaining Gold - the ultimate challenge.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         General Sir Mike Jackson is Britain's most famous soldier of modern times. The outgoing 
 			UK's Chief of the General Staff, professional Head of the British Army known as  'Macho Mike' 
 			the author of 'Soldier'  (his outspoken account of a life lived through conflict)  
 			and Lynda
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PSSept07Gen%20Sir%20Mike%20JacksonLKT%20article.pdf">
          met a leader
         </a>
         from the front, who remains fierce in defence of causes 
 			across the globe and Whitehall.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Leadership - the Imbert way. In his days as Commissioner, Lord Peter Imbert  CVO, 
 			QPM, transformed London's Metropolitan Police Force into today's Metropolitan 
 			Police Service. Lynda worked alongside Peter Imbert's innovative challenges 23 
 			years ago - this feature saw Lynda meet again with the then Lord Lieutenant of 
 			London to discuss modern day policing and being an
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PSSept07Lord%20Imbert%20LKT%20article.pdf">
          'upfront'
         </a>
         leader when it all 
 			goes pear shape. Peter is working with Lynda on her next book and
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/Interviews%20in%20Public%20Servant.pdf">
          read some of 
 			his recent views on policing
         </a>
         which he discussed with Lynda during 2012.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Lynda looked at the future for the slimmed-down Home Office on the passing of 
 			yet another previous Home Secretary.  She discussed if the divided Home Office 
 			is
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PS%20June%2007%20Mailshot%20LKT.pdf">
          split-for-purpose
         </a>
         : and how one of its largest customers - the police - not 
 			to mention their public - are backlashing against a targets culture. Lynda 
 			will be re-visiting the Home Office during 2013 to see how they are progressing 
 			with policing &amp; the public rhetoric, reform &amp; reality.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         The establishment of a National Identiy Card scheme caused critical concerns &amp; contempt.  
 			Before he retired, Lynda sought out James Hall, the Home Office man then at the helm of 
 			an often controversial Government Department. She discovered  the strong private sector 
 			player who ran a key public sector role - the Identity and Passport Service. They 
 			discussed the
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/James%20Hall%20LKT%20PSApr07.pdf">
          brand reputation of individuals intimacy - their identity - tackling the 
 			problems of a modern world
         </a>
         .  Lynda will be progressing these issues soon with the
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.ukba.homeoffice.gov.uk/">
          Home 
 			Office UK Border Agency
         </a>
         as part of her new book in 2013 on the 'Future for Policing'.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         The British Transport Police (BTP) has recovered from a 'crumbling infrastructure' 
 			to meet the sternest of tests with distinction for all its customers. Lynda
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PSJan2007AndyTrotterLKT.pdf">
          interviewed 
 			their then Deputy Chief Constable Andy Trotter QPM
         </a>
         , - now the top man - and discovered 
 			why private profit and public safety can make uneasy bedfellows. Lynda interviewed Andy 
 			Trotter since becoming Chief Constable and is due to meet with him again to review a post 
 			Olympics BTP 2012 - both for her new book and for other policing communications and 
 			conferences she is involved with.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Lynda's unique
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PSJULY06%20LKT%20Bruce%20Mann%20LKT.pdf">
          interview with Bruce Mann
         </a>
         a senior British civil servant, who has 
 			been the official Head of the UK Civil Contingencies Secretariat of the Cabinet 
 			Office who says emergency planning is learning the lessons from past crises. 
 			Post Olympics 2012, Lynda will be updating her interviews for her new 2013 book 
 			on
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/newbook.htm">
          Managing Meltdown &amp; Controlling Chaos.
         </a>
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         Lynda's feature
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PSNov06London7article%20LKT.pdf">
          'Steps Forward from a Disaster'
         </a>
         on the lessons learned from London's 
 			response to terrorism post 7/7 will also be reviewed in 2013 for the above book.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/Tariquge%20Ghaffur%20Oct%202006%20art%20LKT.PS.pdf">
          Interview with London's then Metropolitan Police Assistant Commissioner Tarique Ghaffur 
 			CBE QPM
         </a>
         - a leadership role model for policing and the Muslim community. There will be 
 			an updated interview with Tarique in his new role since leaving the Metropolitan Police. 
 			He has since set up a Foundation to promote shared responsibility for security and safety 
 			amongst vulnerable communities in UK and is also a Chairman of Community Safety Development Global Ltd.
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="centre" class="rightColText ">
         <img border="1" src="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/images/Resilience%20banner.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         "Too costly, too hard, too frightening" Lynda King Taylor has heard every excuse for ignoring 
 			business continuity planning and risk management.  How are we doing post 2012 will be part of 
 			her new book
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/newbook.htm">
          Managing Meltdown &amp; Controlling Chaos
         </a>
         . Also
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/outlook.pdf">
          Read her 'Vision' article 'What are 
 			you scared of?' in the UK's Business Outlook magazine
         </a>
         . If interested in being part of this 
 			book please contact Lynda at
         <a class="rightColLink" href="mailto:LKTLondon@aol.com">
          LKTLondon@aol.com
         </a>
         or on +44 (0) 7775 658 067
        </p>
        <br/>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         When it comes to leadership and business excellence, Alex Linkston was the Chief Executive West 
 			Lothian Council in Scotland, constantly driving up the standards. He headed the only UK unitary 
 			Council to receive a corporate Charter Mark - the UK Government's gold award for excellence in 
 			customer service and also
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.thewowawards.co.uk/">
          the WOW award
         </a>
         . 
 			Alex discussed with Lynda
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.lyndakingtaylor.com/articles/PSFEB08Alex%20Linkston.pdf">
          their latest ambitions
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p align="right" class="rightColText ">
         During 2013-14 Lynda will be upgrading features &amp; case studies for more WOW work - not only 
 			that this excellent Council have established, also other organisations such as Housing Associations 
 			&amp; Partnerships, plus Police Constabularies  and others across all sectors who have achieved their
         <a class="rightColLink" href="http://www.thewowawards.co.uk/">
          WOW 'recognising great customer service ' award
         </a>
         standards.
        </p>
