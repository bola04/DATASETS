         <article>
          <h3>
           Responsive? What??  Did you say "template"?!
          </h3>
          <img alt="Responsive Web Design" class="imgrt" src="gfx/responsive.gif"/>
          <p>
           RWD.  Three letters that pack a mighty punch.  They stand for "Responsive Web Design".  Should you care?  Absolutely!  In a nutshell, a responsive design means one that looks and functions well across devices ~ desktop, laptop, tablet, and smartphone ~ one design that rescales content, navigation, and images to fit the viewer's device. You're on a responsive website right now based on the
           <a href="http://wrapbootstrap.com/preview/WB048M27G" target="_blank">
            Transition — V-card template
           </a>
           . Your eyes do not deceive you!  I just used the formerly reviled word "template", since it is the only feasible way to benefit from all that today's extensive RWD programming and HTML5 rich features have to offer.  Never fear ~ imagery, fonts, colors, and more can relay your brand and one-of-a-kind digital presence.
          </p>
          <p>
           Indeed, when ranking search results Google has begun factoring in adaptability to mobile.  Not sure if yours will pass?  Try
           <a href="https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/mobile-friendly/" target="_blank">
            Google's Mobile-Friendly Test
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Template.  There,  I said it again…
           <span class="gld">
            sol
           </span>
           <span class="rng">
            vibe
           </span>
           is there to help you once you recover from the shock.
          </p>
