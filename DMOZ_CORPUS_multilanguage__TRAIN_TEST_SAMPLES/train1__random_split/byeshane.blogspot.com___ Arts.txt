               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/2009/10/on-hiatus.html">
                On hiatus
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <p>
                I've been running this blog for eight months.  It's helped me with my writing, especially with writer's block, but sadly, it has become sort of a distraction from my other work.  Between the novel, three short stories, a few screenplays, a short film, two blogs and scraping money together for either film school or a literature degree, something has to give.  Unfortunately I'm making the difficult decision to put Come Back Shane! on hold.  It's been a fun eight months and hopefully I will be able to return to this eventually.  Thanks to all those who supported the blog.

                </i>

               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <p>
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/Ss5jzgCSUBI/AAAAAAAAFU0/l8aDqFddZ9s/s1600-h/nine-444.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">

                <a href="http://www.ropeofsilicon.com/article/breaking-nine-has-been-moved-to-december">

                </a>

                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Nine">

                <br/>
                <br/>
                I don't think the move helps
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Nine
                </span>
                's chances.  It would originally have had to deal with
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/The%20Road">

                </a>

                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Fantastic%20Mr.%20Fox">
                 Fantastic Mr. Fox
                </a>
                , and
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Me%20and%20Orson%20Welles">
                 Me and Orson Welles
                </a>
                .
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/New%20Moon">
                 New Moon
                </a>
                , which is a guaranteed hit is set to open on November 20, so perhaps
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Nine
                </span>
                is being moved to be saved from that.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Now
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Nine
                </span>
                has to contend with
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/A%20Single%20Man">
                 A Single Man
                </a>
                , which has now been set to open wide on Christmas Day,
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Avatar">
                 Avatar
                </a>
                (December 18),
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Sherlock%20Holmes">
                 Sherlock Holmes
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/The%20Young%20Victoria">
                 The Young Victoria
                </a>
                ,
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 It's Complicated
                </span>
                ,
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Squeakuel
                </span>
                , and
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/The%20Imaginarium%20of%20Doctor%20Parnassus">
                 The Imaginarium of Doctor Parnassus
                </a>
                .  I haven't even mentioned the opening of
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Invictus">
                 Invictus
                </a>
                or
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/The%20Lovely%20Bones">
                 The Lovely Bones
                </a>
                which open a week before
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Nine
                </span>
                's new limited release.  On paper this looks like a terrible idea, but I'm not really great at predicting box office and its effect on awards season.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Along with this news
                <a href="http://nine-themovie.blogspot.com/2009/10/cover-is-here.html">
                 Nine - the Movie
                </a>
                (via
                <a href="http://community.livejournal.com/ohnotheydidnt/39926763.html">
                 ONTD!
                </a>
                ) points us to the November cover of
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Vogue
                </span>
                with the women of
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Nine
                </span>

               <p>
                <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/Ssxk61rq6UI/AAAAAAAAFTc/iadVOppDvZI/s1600-h/adrien_brody_headshot-4729.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">

               <blockquote>
                Schwarzenegger he ain't, and I don't know if that had anything to do with the decision, but...wow...I don't want to say Brody's thin or anything, but I understand to get him on location in Hawaii to begin shooting the film, producers are just going to fax him to the islands.  [
                <a href="http://www.getthebigpicture.net/blog/2009/10/6/adrien-brody-of-all-people-to-hunt-predators.html">
                 Get the Big Picture
                </a>
                ]
               </blockquote>
               <br/>
               My being a Brody
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_0">
                fangirl
               </span>
               aside, I would seriously consider writing the dude an open letter.  His film choices have been fucking all over the place since he won that well deserved Oscar for
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                The Pianist
               </span>
               .  Some have been decent to good even though they've crashed and burned at the box office (
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                The Darjeeling Limited
               </span>
               ,
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                Cadillac Records
               </span>
               ,
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                The Brothers Bloom
               </span>
               , and
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Fantastic%20Mr.%20Fox">
                The Fantastic Mr. Fox
               </a>
               looks decent), but some of the choices have been pretty bad.
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                The Village
               </span>
               made me want to gouge my eyes out and
               <span class="blsp-spelling-error" id="SPELLING_ERROR_1">
                WTF
               </span>
               was
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                King Kong
               </span>
               ?  Yes, I know it's good to have a huge film on your filmography especially with all the smaller pictures, but that shit was nearly
               <span class="blsp-spelling-corrected" id="SPELLING_ERROR_2">
                unforgivable
               </span>
               .  Why do I get the feeling that this
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                Predators
               </span>
               business is going to be like
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                Kong
               </span>
               all over again?

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/2009/10/beyonce-could-be-wonder-woman.html">
                Beyonce could be Wonder Woman?
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <p>
                <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/SsvZ8LUJszI/AAAAAAAAFTA/N5QJr_o3hFI/s1600-h/Beyonce-Wonder+Woman.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                 <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5389641006931620658" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/SsvZ8LUJszI/AAAAAAAAFTA/N5QJr_o3hFI/s400/Beyonce-Wonder+Woman.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 371px;"/>
                </a>
                <br/>
                Well, this is disappointing.   According to
                <a href="http://www.comicbookmovie.com/wonder_woman/news/?a=10697">
                 Comic Book Movie
                </a>
                Beyonce might be considered to play Wonder Woman.  I suppose it would be "bold" to cast a black Wonder Woman (something
                <a href="http://www.getthebigpicture.net/blog/2009/10/6/beyonce-kind-of-confirms-shes-wonder-woman.html">
                 Get the Big Picture
                </a>
                points out Beyonce has said).  But now that I think of it, should anyone be surprised that Wonder Woman might not be white?  The TV version of Wonder Woman, Lynda Carter, is Hispanic - her mother was Mexican-American.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Anyway, I wish I was making the Beyonce part up:
                <br/>
                <span style="font-size:100%;">
                </span>
               </p>
               <blockquote>
                <span style="font-size:100%;">
                 Just got back from a press conference tonight with Beyonce and she spoke of a few upcoming movie projects.  The last on her list was Wonder Woman!
                </span>
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <span id="ctl00_ContentPlaceHolder1_m_contentLBL">
                 <span>
                 </span>
                 Yes, Wonder Woman!
                 <span>
                  She said she has to meet up with the writers and directors in a couple months and things will start to pick up by mid-2010 for a late 2011 release.  She said the
                  <span class="IL_SPAN">
                   <input name="IL_MARKER" type="hidden"/>
                   writers
                  </span>
                  were
                 </span>
                 <strong>
                  Stuart Beatti
                 </strong>
                 and
                 <strong>
                  David Elliot
                 </strong>
                 , the guys that wrote
                 <em>
                  GI Joe: Rise of Cobra
                 </em>
                 and the director was
                 <strong>
                  John Moore
                 </strong>
                 , the guy that directed Max Payne.
                </span>
               </blockquote>
               <br/>
               I think everything about this news is horrible.  Yes, Beyonce was surprisingly not bad in a movie like
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                Cadillac Records
               </span>
               , but she was in her comfort zone in that film.  In films where she's out of her musical element she just sort of dawdles in front of the camera.  And this thing is going to be written by the
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/GI%20Joe%3A%20The%20Rise%20of%20Cobra">
                GI Joe
               </a>
               guys, and directed by the guy who made
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                Max Payne
               </span>
               ?  That's even worse than the Beyonce news.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Now just because Warner Bros., hasn't reported Beyonce's involvement doesn't mean she isn't involved.  We were dangerously close to a
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/2009/07/justin-timberlake-being-considered-for.html">
                Justin Timberlake as the Green Lantern
               </a>
               so anything is possible.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               To be fair to Beyonce, I can't think of a really good Wonder Woman.
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Marion%20Cotillard">
                Marion Cotillard
               </a>
               is the only actress I can think of, but she'd be rejected by most.  Some of the other suggested actresses like
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Megan%20Fox">
                Megan Fox
               </a>
               , Angelina Jolie, and
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Jessica%20Biel">
                Jessica Biel
               </a>
               are either equally horrible or even worse.

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/2009/10/emily-blunt-talks-adjustment-bureau-and.html">
                Emily Blunt talks
                <i>
                 The Adjustment Bureau
                </i>
                and
                <i>
                 The Young Victoria
                </i>
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <p>
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/SsUwol5nh2I/AAAAAAAAFPo/dRR4mKyxymA/s1600-h/14.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                 <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5387766003145344866" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/SsUwol5nh2I/AAAAAAAAFPo/dRR4mKyxymA/s200/14.jpg" style="margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; float: left; cursor: pointer; width: 152px; height: 200px;"/>
                </a>
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/The%20Adjustment%20Bureau">
                 The Adjustment Bureau
                </a>
                which co-stars
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Matt%20Damon">
                 Matt Damon
                </a>
                is loosely based on Philip K. Dick's science fiction short story,
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Adjustment Team
                </span>
                .  The film is written and directed by George Nolfi who also wrote
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 The Bourne Ultimatum
                </span>
                and
                <span style="font-style: italic;">
                 Ocean's Twelv
                </span>
                e.  Damon plays David Norris, a congressman who falls fo a ballet dancer named Elise Sellas (
                <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Emily%20Blunt">
                 Blunt
                </a>
                ).  Their affair is affected by mysterious forces keeping the lovers apart.  Emily Blunt talked to
                <a href="http://moviesblog.mtv.com/2009/10/01/emily-blunt-on-the-modern-love-story-and-ominous-sci-fi-backdrop-in-the-adjustment-bureau/">
                 MTV
                </a>
                during a break from shooting in New York City:
                <br/>
               </p>
               <blockquote>
                "It's like a modern love story, but it's got an ominous sci-fi backdrop to it...It's going to be exciting and disconcerting and strange, which is what I like about [Dick's] work. It's very cool and clever. It's got a really tight script."
                <br/>
                <br/>
                "The term soulmates is used so casually, but in this case, in this film, it is true," Blunt explained. "They are sort of destined to be together and they fight fate to be together."
                <br/>
                <br/>
                "Matt's thrilled because this time it's me who's been thrust into the month of training, because I play a dancer in it, which is kind of arduous training seeing as I've never bloody danced before. And Matt is actually thrilled that for the first time it's me who has to do that kind of thing and he's got off scot free."
                <br/>
                <br/>
                "I really would love to do a movie where I kick ass!" she laughed. "I'm not doing any wire work or anything [in 'Adjustment Bureau'], so I'm bitterly disappointed!"
                <br/>
               </blockquote>
               <br/>
               <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/SsUulH-o8kI/AAAAAAAAFPY/Nnc_svYed2k/s1600-h/Young-Victoria-Albert.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5387763744550482498" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_bYfQu7o0iNU/SsUulH-o8kI/AAAAAAAAFPY/Nnc_svYed2k/s400/Young-Victoria-Albert.jpg" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 249px;"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               Blunt also
               <a href="http://moviesblog.mtv.com/2009/10/01/emily-blunt-on-donning-the-bonnet-in-royal-biopic-the-young-victoria/">
                discussed her role
               </a>
               as Queen Victoria.  She pointed out what we've known for years.  All British actresses have to do a period piece:
               <br/>
               <blockquote>
                "You have to don the bonnet at some point, otherwise you're simply not a British actress," she declared to MTV News.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                Across the pond, Victoria as a historical figure is known as something of a downer. After all, she spent a decade in self-imposed exile, mourning the death of her hubby Albert. "That was my impression of her – that she was repressed and in mourning and never smiled and waddled around with that dour-faced expression and a hanky on her head," laughed Blunt.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                "I loved that no one had seen that side to her – that young, passionate, fiery side," Blunt explained. "I was very surprised by the love story, which really did happen. When I read her diaries, I realized this wasn't just Hollywood sensationalizing this woman's life. It really was a meeting of souls when she met Albert."
                <br/>
                <br/>
                ...the actress approached Victoria as a woman first and a monarch second. She hopes viewers will do the same. "There are not that many people who know what's it like to be a queen, but I think everyone knows about falling in love for the first time," she said. "Everyone knows about a dysfunctional family. It's a really intimate portrait of her private life and what's it like for a girl who has a job and she feels in way over her head."
                <br/>
               </blockquote>
               <br/>
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/The%20Young%20Victoria">
                The Young Victoria
               </a>
               focuses on Victoria's years as a princess, her romance with Albert, and her struggle with royal life after becoming queen at 18.  The film though, is more love story than royal thriller.  It breezes along from Victoria's pampered beginning to a happily-ever-after ending.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It's a fluffy costume drama, but some of the performances are quite good, including the one from
               <a href="http://byeshane.blogspot.com/search/label/Rupert%20Friend">
                Rupert Friend
               </a>
               , whose Prince Albert is the real star of the whole show.  Blunt carries the film quite well with a lot of grace and innocence.  It difficult to make an audience feel sorry for a princess who has everything, but Blunt pulls it off.  It's in her eyes, I think.  Mark Strong, who plays the villain, is great as well, but he hovers in the background for too long to be a real threat to anybody.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I suppose it makes more historical sense for the film to be lighter fare - by the time Victoria became queen, monarchs had almost no political power. When the film treads into dark territory, most of that scene is pure fantasy that never took place.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <span style="font-style: italic;">
                The Young Victoria
               </span>
               opens in limited release in the US on December 18 and goes wide a week later.
