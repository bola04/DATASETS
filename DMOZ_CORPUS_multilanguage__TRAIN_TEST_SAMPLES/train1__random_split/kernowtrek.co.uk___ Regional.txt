             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Newquay Airport
              </strong>
              – 2 minutes by car, less than half a mile away. Car hire available.
              <strong>
               Newquay Train Station
              </strong>
              approx 3 miles – taxi’s and car hire available in Newquay town. For directions by road please scroll down to ‘How to Find Kernow Trek’.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Kernow Trek Lodge Self catering Apartments
              </strong>
              are minutes away from Cornwall’s most beautiful family beaches and stunning cliff walks. Great location, with easy access to the best surf spots and everything Cornwall has to offer. Just to name a few of our guests favourite places to visit ….. Watergate Bay; Mawgan Porth; St Mawgan village; Bedruthen Steps; Fistral Beach; Jamie Oliver’s Fifteen Restaurant; Eden Project; Padstow; St Ives; Port Isaac; Polzeath, Mevagissey; Polperro, St Agnes; Perranporth, St Michaels Mount; Truro …… there is always somewhere new to discover!
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              We are surrounded by beautiful beaches with golden sand and turquoise sea, rock pools and caves to explore. The best surf spots in the UK are right here on our door step. Take your pick from a host of great surfing beaches, from Fistral in Newquay, all along Cornwall’s North Coast to Harlyn Bay. Local cuisine is second to none – plenty of restaurants to choose between, from Jamie Oliver’s Fifteen at Watergate Bay, Rick Stein in Padstow, our local village pub, farm shop and tea rooms, to name but few! Peaceful countryside walks through fields, and dramatic cliff walks with breath taking views of our Cornish coastline here on our door step. There’s adventure for everyone of all ages and skill levels …..
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Local
              <strong>
               surf schools
              </strong>
              at Watergate Bay (
              <strong>
               West Country Surf School, Extreme Academy &amp; O’neils
              </strong>
              ) and Mawgan Porth
              <strong>
               (King Surf)
              </strong>
              beaches offer
              <strong>
               surf lessons
              </strong>
              with enthusiastic qualified instructors for all ages and ability, with wet suit and board hire to set you up for the whole day. For the more adventurous –
              <strong>
               Coasteering
              </strong>
              – exploring the rugged coast line from sea level, with experienced and qualified leaders.
              <strong>
               Sea safari’s
              </strong>
              and
              <strong>
               fishing trips
              </strong>
              allow you to see the beauty of our Cornish Coast from a different perspective, and where dolphins and seals are often spotted.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               <strong>
                Body boarding
               </strong>
              </strong>
              is very popular, and huge fun for everyone, both in the shallows and catching some of those bigger waves! Our local beaches are patrolled by fully trained and experienced life guards (usually from Easter until the end of September, between 9am and 6pm approx), so you and your family can feel safe in the water, following their guidelines.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Mawgan Porth
              </strong>
              Beach is an absolute favourite with families – a rolling expanse of golden sand as the tide goes out, revealing rock pools full of miniature sea life – pools large enough to swim in sometimes – rocks for climbing and a fun shallow stream leading in to the sea. Ideal for children of all ages –  surfing, body boarding, buckets, spades and fishing nets – all day family fun! King Surf surf school is based here for surf lessons.  Less than half a mile from Kernow Trek, approx 30 – 35 mins walk following the National Trust cliff path. Life guards are present Easter – early Autumn.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Bedruthen Steps
              </strong>
              beach is a real adventure, although unsuitable for visitors with limited mobility. The beach is accessed via steep steps cut in to the cliff face, with safety grab rails on the way down. Definitely not a bathing beach, but a dramatic feast of stacks and arches formed by the sea over the years, that beautiful clear sea and often undisturbed sand – never fails to captivate visitors. Watch the tide – it sweeps in very quickly here, so timing your visit for low tide is important.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Watergate Bay
              </strong>
              – well what can I say – the tide goes out to reveal a huge expanse of golden sand – it is what it is – an amazing beach! A surfers haven, also very popular with other sporting events including zap cats and beach polo. Jamie Oliver’s Fifteen restaurant  over looks Watergate Bay. Less than half a mile from Kernow Trek, approx 30 – 35 mins walk along the National Trust cliff path – amazing views! Life guards are present Easter – early Autumn. Surf lesson available here with O’neils surf school.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Amble as far as you like along the
              <strong>
               National Trust Coast Path
              </strong>
              – only 5 mins walk from Kernow Trek Lodge to join the coastal path, revealing stunning views (which ever direction you go!) of the North Cornwall Coast, hidden bays, flora and bird life.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Local
              <strong>
               Golf
              </strong>
              courses in Newquay, St Eval, Trevose and Perranporth.
              <strong>
               Kite surfing
              </strong>
              at Watergate bay,
              <strong>
               jet ski
              </strong>
              at Lusty Glaze in Newquay,
              <strong>
               paddle boarding
              </strong>
              at Harlyn Surf School,
              <strong>
               horse riding
              </strong>
              in and around Newquay –  including beach rides.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Cycle
              </strong>
              the Camel Trail, staring at Padstow or Wadebridge – a fantastic fun day out exploring the Camel Estuary, and beyond if you have the energy, with a Cornish Pasty picnic to keep you going! Bike hire available.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Discover the charm of local
              <strong>
               fishing harbours
              </strong>
              and
              <strong>
               villages
              </strong>
              , and indulge in the best local cuisine – from our village pub and farm shop, to
              <strong>
               Jamie Oliver’s Fifteen
              </strong>
              at Watergate Bay – just 2 minutes from Kernow Trek by Car – and Rick Stein Restaurants in Padstow. Enjoy Fun packed days at local
              <strong>
               theme parks and zoo’s
              </strong>
              , Minnack theatre,
              <strong>
               Cinema
              </strong>
              and
              <strong>
               bowling
              </strong>
              ,  country houses,
              <strong>
               Eden Project
              </strong>
              – or simply chill out on our sunny decking with a good book – there really is something for everyone!
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              On a more practical note, there are several
              <strong>
               Supermarkets in Newquay town
              </strong>
              (approx 3 miles), including Morrisons, Tesco Express, Sainsburys, Asda, Lidl and Aldi. On line shopping and delivery is available from selected stores. Newquay is also convenient for banks, cash points, numerous shops, cinema, bowling, cafe’s and restaurants offering a good choice of food to suit everyone’s taste.
              <strong>
               Newquay harbour
              </strong>
              is often a hive activity, with local fishing boats coming and going, gig races, sea safari’s and fishing trips. Also residence to a very lazy seal !
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Newquay hosts the bustling
              <strong>
               Boardmasters
              </strong>
              event in August, whilst the music festival takes place at Watergate Bay. Check out this years dates and line up on the Boardmasters web site.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Local shops in
              <strong>
               Mawgan Porth
              </strong>
              (less than half a mile) include Cornish Fresh Store, selling take away hot drinks, food, and local organic produce. Betty’s News Agent and store also in Mawgan Porth, with King Surf surf school, a selection of gift shops, mini golf, cafes, ‘Catch’ restaurant and Merrymore Inn. There is a cash machine, with charge for use. As you follow the coast road out of Mawgan Porth towards Kernow Trek, Bre-Pen Farm is on your right, selling delicious home made bread, cakes and scones, jams and chutney’s,  local farm produce including eggs and bacon for example.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              Highly recommended by all our guests over the years – the Travellers Rest Pub – right here in Trevarrian – fab food!
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              In the village of
              <strong>
               St Mawgan
              </strong>
              (just a 2 minute drive past Newquay Airport, or a beautiful 30 min walk through fields) there is a local gift shop, Post office and lovely tea rooms next to our local primary school. Great food at the Falcon Inn, and beautiful church over looking the village green and small children’s play area. A very peaceful place, lovely to sit in the tea rooms or on the village green and just listen to nature. Yet a very active community –  often a fun event happening here!
             </p>

               Padstow
              </strong>
              (approx 8 miles) offers a great seafood choice with a number of Rick Stein restaurants to choose from, tasty pasties and cream teas of course! A quaint, busy fishing harbour, with amazing views across the Camel Estuary. Bike hire is available here – a great fun day out, cycling as far as you wish along the camel trail.
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: justify;">
              <strong>
               Truro
              </strong>
              City is approx 17 miles, with its elegant cathedral, a good choice of shops, cinema and restaurants. Park and ride recommended.

            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             <strong>
              <em>
               Directions
              </em>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: justify;">
             It is easy to reach us by road, rail and air. Newquay Airport just is 2 mins by car, less than half a mile away. Newquay train station is approx 10-15 mins by road, with taxi’s readily available. Access by road is very straight forward, follow the signs to Newquay Airport from the A30. After passing the airport on your left, follow the road to a ‘T’ junction, and turn right (towards Mawgan Porth). Turn next left in to the center of Trevarrian, and we are on there on the left! Local bus services run from Trevarrian to Padstow and Newquay, with bus and rail links to other places of interest. However, use of a car is recommended – car hire is available at the airport and in Newquay.
