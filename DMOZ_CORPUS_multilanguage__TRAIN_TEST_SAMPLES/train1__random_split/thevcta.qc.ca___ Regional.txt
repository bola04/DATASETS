           News Bulletin 13, June 12, 2018
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="sep">
           Posted on
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/2018/06/14/news-bulletin-13-june-12-2018%e2%80%af/" rel="bookmark" title="3:44 pm">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2018-06-14T15:44:17+00:00">
            June 14, 2018
           </time>
          </a>
          <span class="by-author">
           <span class="sep">
            by
           </span>
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/author/vcta/" rel="author" title="View all posts by vcta">
             vcta
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Warning: La Capitale’s non-approved service providers
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          La Capitale has established a list of service providers which they believe are fraudulent.  They will no longer honour any receipts which we submit from them.  The list is available on our individual
          <strong>
           Espace client
          </strong>
          and will be updated regularly.
          <br/>
          See:
          <strong>
           Forms/ Medical and Paramedical.
           <br/>
          </strong>
          Please find the current list attached.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            New retirees
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          FNEEQ has a retirees Association: AREF.
          <a href="http://www.aref-neq.ca/">
           http://www.aref-neq.ca/
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you wish to avail yourself of the insurance plans (travel, health, life) you have 30 days from your official retirement date to do so.  You have to be an AREF member and maintain that membership.  Enrollment is optional but the decision to
          <u>
           not
          </u>
          enroll is irrevocable.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            The Bureau Federal
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          The Bureau Federal is the deliberative council of the FNEEQ Exective. At the last FNEEQ Congress, our own Eric Durling was elected for a three-year mandate.  We are especially pleased as this will not only allow us to be more informed, it will also give us a strong voice and vote at this table.  Congrats Eric!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Save the dates!
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          We have established the General Assembly dates for the 2018 fall semester.  As was the case this year, we are varying the times so as to accommodate members’ different schedules.  Please mark these in your calendars now so to be available to participate at our GAs.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Thursday, September 5 – 6 pm
          </li>
          <li>
           Wednesday, October 3 – UB
          </li>
          <li>
           Wednesday, December 19 – 2pm
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Estates General of Higher Education
           </u>
          </strong>
          <a href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-1.png">
           <img alt="" class="wp-image-2526 alignnone" height="121" src="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-1-300x62.png" width="533"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          During May 3 – 5, a few hundred participants of higher education gathered for the second Estates General in order to propose solutions to the concerns that have been gathered over the last year.  Various sectors of higher education attended, from support staff to students, professionals to university professors and cegep teachers.  Vanier had 5 teachers present who attended various panels and plenary sessions.  A summary was made that was presented to the political representatives from each provincial party.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Please find the full summary attached.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Of particular note to our members, the following items were addressed:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Work insecurity
          </strong>
          .  The difficulties of Cont Ed teachers were outlined and among key solutions proposed:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Teachers should get the same pay and working conditions for the same job           and responsibilities.
          </li>
          <li>
           Family and work balance should be recognized.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Financing.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           The PES (student weekly class period) formula as a mode of financing should be replaced so that the needs of the College and that of smaller programs are not negatively affected.
          </li>
          <li>
           Financing should be raised according to the numbers of disadvantaged students enrolled.
          </li>
          <li>
           The envelopes should be revised so to better respond to needs, e.g. more into F (fixed costs) and less into S (special projects).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Internationalization.
          </strong>
          While a lot of the university professors were in favour of expanding internationalization to bolster their revenues, a few college teachers spoke to the need to stop viewing students as revenue but to be committed to educating our society
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           International agreements should be envisioned so as to exchange knowledge and culture.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            FNEEQ Congress
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          The VCTA Executive together with members Stephanie Belmer, Hugo Casanova, Ingrid Mittmannsgruber and Janice Paquette went as voting delegates to the FNEEQ Congress in Sherbrooke (“Sherby”) from May 21-25.  We were extremely proud of our colleague Leila Bdeir who also came as an invited speaker May 23.
          <a href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-2.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="wp-image-2528 alignnone" height="227" src="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-2-300x152.jpg" width="439"/>
           <br/>
          </a>
          (photo credit Sheila Das)
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          The Congress, which takes place every three years, focussed on inclusion of marginalized groups, better communication and advertising strategies for the next negotiations, mobilization, employment instability and Cont Ed conditions and the budget.
         </p>
         <p>
          Importantly, the elections for the next FNEEQ Executive who will lead us into the negotiations in 2020 saw new members elected!  We have high hopes for a strong and effective team with (from left to right) :
         </p>
         <p>
          Richard Bousquet, vice-président responsable du regroupement université, Benoit Lacoursière, secrétaire général et trésorier, Caroline Quesnel, présidente, Josée Chevalier, vice-présidente responsable du regroupement cégep, Léandre Lapointe, vice-président responsable du regroupement privé
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-3.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2529" height="240" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-3-300x240.jpg" srcset="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-3-300x240.jpg 300w, http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/NB-13-JUNE-12-2018-3.jpg 374w" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Enjoy your well-deserved summer vacation !
          </strong>
         </p>

          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="sep">
           Posted on
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/2018/05/02/news-bulletin-12-may-2-2018/" rel="bookmark" title="4:18 pm">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            The Estates General for Higher Education
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          Starting
          <u>
           tomorrow
          </u>
          , Thursday May 3rd! Join in on the discussions with other key figures involved in higher education, from students to CEGEP teachers to contract teachers to university professors. The entire VCTA Executive is taking part and we warmly welcome any teacher to come with us to make a stand for higher education to be a priority for the upcoming provincial elections this October.  Main topics of discussion will be the financing and internationalization of higher education, administration and insecure or precarious employment.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Please see the attachment for the full agenda.
          </li>
          <li>
           Let us know if you would like to join us (at UQAM, Salle Marie-Gerin-Lajoie, Pavillon Judith-Jasmin).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            CEEC (
            <em>
             evaluation commission for college education
            </em>
            )
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          Our General Assembly met for a special assembly on April 4th and approved the FNEEQ motion on the CEEC.  Thus, Academic Council teacher representatives were mandated to vote against the “Self-evaluation Framework” for evaluating practices and tools here at Vanier, which they did to defeat the motion at Academic Council April 6th.  This action takes place in the context of quality assurance initiatives that are seeping into higher education across the globe that are transforming the objectives and the structures and procedures of higher education institutions.  In addition, the GA  motion asks of all teachers that:
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           a) No Vanier teacher participate in advisory committees related to the CEEC audit process or in other aspects of the process.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           b) Vanier teachers transmit only pre-existing information for any request for information connected to the CEEC after removing names from all documents that are transmitted and without any synthesis or revision of the documents
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           For further context, please see the FNEEQ powerpoint, here attached.
          </li>
          <li>
           For the full GA motion of April 4th, please refer to the message sent April 10th.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Happy Hour May 16th
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          Our end-of-year happy hour is coming up!  You are invited to what we hope will be a big party to celebrate the year.  Please remind your colleagues in your departments. We also want to celebrate our teachers who are retiring, so you are further invited to offer a few words in recognition and gratitude to anyone you know who is retiring this year.  Let us toast the year together!
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           See you May 16th at 4:30 in Jake’s Mall!
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/HAPPY-HR-MAY-16-2018.png">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2503" height="193" src="http://www.thevcta.qc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/HAPPY-HR-MAY-16-2018.png" width="262"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Science Program Petition
            <br/>
           </u>
          </strong>
          We are circulating the FNEEQ petition against the current science program revision proposal — which is suspended by the Ministry of Education until the end of the summer — in order to show our support with other colleges at FNEEQ and strengthen our collective voice.  Instead we are demanding a rewriting of the proposal to be developed by teachers and to take into consideration teachers’ concerns.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Add you name to the call by signing the petition!  Petitions are available in both union lounges, C-101 and N-127.
          </li>
         </ul>

          <strong>
           <u>

         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            General Offer of Service (GOS)
           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          All non-permanent teachers must sign a GOS by the end of April, which is around the corner.  If you miss this deadline,
          <u>
           you will lose your hiring priority
          </u>
          .  So don’t delay.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           See the GOS here attached.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            CRT Follow-up: PD as a Taxable Benefit
           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          We were informed at CRT of the College’s revised position concerning PD as a taxable benefit after we had previously submitted the materials from our preliminary investigation.  The Admin did not admit they made an error, but they did admit a more ample interpretation of the tax laws was possible. We will be following this up with further legal investigation.  However, for now:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Any activity done -in-house, that is using Vanier’s gym facilities or taking classes here
           <u>
            will not be taxed
           </u>
           , retroactive to Jan 1 2018 (not before, because the Admin does not admit error).
          </li>
          <li>
           For Phys Ed teachers, activities that they take that are shown to be directly related to their courses will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis, and if the link is demonstrated,
           <u>
            they should not be taxed
           </u>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           General fitness is not being deemed a requirement for Phys Ed teachers’ job and so outside gym facilities and general activities
           <u>
            will be taxed
           </u>
           .
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Funding for Master’s and Retraining
           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          The FNEEQ forms are now available to obtain funding towards a Masters degree (5-4.21) or retraining (5-4.22).  The deadline for submission is
          <u>
           May 15,
          </u>
          but it is recommended to get it in as early as possible.  You must download, and save the Excel file before using, then sign the forms and either email a scan (
          <a href="mailto:cpnc@education.gouv.qc.ca">
           cpnc@education.gouv.qc.ca
          </a>
          ) or mail in the paper copy.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Please find the forms here attached.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            The Estates General for Higher Education
           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is a rare opportunity to come together with the different key figures involved in higher education, from students to CEGEP teachers to contract teachers, to have our say and to make a stand that higher education be a priority for the upcoming provincial elections this October!    Main topics of discussion will be the financing of higher education, college governing structures, and insecure or precarious employment.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Join in the discussions May 3 (5pm), 4 and 5 (from 9am), at UQAM, Salle Marie-Gerin-Lajoie (Pavillon Judith-Jasmin), local J-M400.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Long-term Substitution
           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you have done substitution that exceeds 10 days and have not been paid entirely in CI during the last two years, please contact us!  This would be a change in practice and we would like more information in order to get the proper documentation for our investigation.

         <p>
          All non-permanent teachers must sign a GOS by the end of April.  If you miss this deadline, you will lose your hiring priority.  So do it as soon as possible and don’t get caught out!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <u>
            Domestic Workers Petition
           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          For decades now in Canada, domestic workers, most of whom are women, minorities and immigrants, have contributed to the Canadian economy, while their work goes by unseen, unrecognized and under-compensated.  Not to mention the appalling fact that many have working conditions that render them virtual slaves to the household, with on-site accommodation and extended and on-call working hours; and their position renders them particularly vulnerable to verbal abuse, sexual harassment and other abuse.
         </p>
         <p>
          Incredibly, Canada has not yet ratified the international convention for 2011 that would recognize and protect their rights as workers!  A campaign has been underway since March of this year to pressure the government to ratify the International Labour Organization’s Domestic Workers Convention, 2011 (No. 189).  Please take a moment to add your voice to this call:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="https://petitions.ourcommons.ca/en/Petition/Details?Petition=e-1568">
            https://petitions.ourcommons.ca/en/Petition/Details?Petition=e-1568
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>

           </u>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          We had a province-wide meeting of CEGEP science teachers and union representatives March 28 to discuss the proposed science revision. A Common Declaration was developed in response to this proposal.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Please see the attached document (in French).
          </li>
         </ul>

           </u>

         </p>
         <p>
          Fneeq has been organizing to protect to the Cegep system in the province by rallying teachers to the “DEC is a DEC” campaign.  Over the last years, quality assurance ideologies have seeped into the ministry’s view of education, which has led to subtle and overt changes that lead Cegeps into becoming “distinct”, “with their own local flavor,” and therefore competitive.  The downsides to this have become apparent during the discussions this winter about the proposed revision to the science program, where some colleges would suffer in enrollments, students would not graduate with the same qualifications for university, and adversity rather than collaboration would be fostered.  To show your support for a unified DEC, please add the campaign logo to your email signature for the remainder of the semester.
         </p>

          We are coming to that time of year when some dear colleagues, after a lifetime of teaching, decide to enjoy a new phase in life, away from the joys of marking and students, by going into retirement.   Let’s celebrate them!  Please know that the union has funds ($150 per retiree) that you can use to organize a party or towards a gift.  Just contact the VCTA with your request for the retiring teacher, and afterwards, provide us the original receipts.
