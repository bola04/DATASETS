               <h3>
                <a href="#">
                 <i class="icon-plus-sign">
                 </i>
                 What is “Double Swiping”?
                </a>
               </h3>
               <div>
                <div class="wpb_row vc_row-fluid vc_row standard_section " data-bg-mobile-hidden="" data-midnight="" id="fws_5b5c95604e3ed" style="padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; ">

                  </div>
                 </div>
                 <div class="col span_12 ">
                  <div class="vc_col-sm-12 wpb_column column_container col no-extra-padding" data-animation="" data-bg-color="" data-bg-opacity="1" data-delay="0" data-has-bg-color="false" data-hover-bg="" data-hover-bg-opacity="1" data-padding-pos="all">
                   <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                    <div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element ">
                     <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                      <p class="inner_content">
                       When a merchant swipes a card for the second time at their own point-of-sale (POS) or cash register after the card transaction has already been approved the first time.
                      </p>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="toggle extra-color-1">

                <a href="#">
                 <i class="icon-plus-sign">
                 </i>
                 Why are payment cards double-swiped?
                </a>
               </h3>
               <div>
                <div class="wpb_row vc_row-fluid vc_row standard_section " data-bg-mobile-hidden="" data-midnight="" id="fws_5b5c95604ec6c" style="padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; ">

                  </div>

                   <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                    <div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element ">
                     <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                      <p class="inner_content">
                       Merchants ‘double swipe’ to collect vital payment card details and cardholders’ personal data for their internal accounting and/or marketing purposes.
                      </p>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>

                 </i>
                 What vital information can be accessed by double-swiping?
                </a>
               </h3>
               <div>
                <div class="wpb_row vc_row-fluid vc_row standard_section " data-bg-mobile-hidden="" data-midnight="" id="fws_5b5c95604f4ae" style="padding-top: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; ">

                   <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                    <div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element ">
                     <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                      <p class="inner_content">
                       By double swiping, a merchant can access and store all payment card data, including sensitive information encoded on the magnetic strip of the payment card.
                      </p>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>

                </a>
               </h3>
               <div>
