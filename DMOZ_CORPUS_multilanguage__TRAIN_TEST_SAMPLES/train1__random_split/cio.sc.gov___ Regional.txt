                   <h4>
                    Division of Technology
                   </h4>
                   <p>
                    The Division of Technology is an operating unit under the Department of Administration, tasked with helping to facilitate the delivery of technology, security and privacy-related services and solutions to governmental entities throughout the state. The Division of Technology includes the following Divisions/Offices:
                   </p>
                   <ul>
                    <li>
                     <a href="/technology/information-security" target="_self" title="The Division of Information Security Website">
                      The Division of Information Security
                     </a>
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     <a href="/technology/technology-operations" target="_self" title="Division of Technology Operations Website">
                      The Division of Technology Operations
                     </a>
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     <a href="/technology/enterprise-privacy" target="_self" title="EPO Website">
                      The Enterprise Privacy Office
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                    </li>
                   </ul>
                   <h4>
                    Division News
                   </h4>
                   <ul style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px;">
                    <li style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px;">
                     <b>
                      Newly Released Statewide End-User Computing Devices Standard
                     </b>
                     <br style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px;"/>
                     The South Carolina Department of Administration recently released the first information technology (IT) standard for the statewide IT Shared Services initiative. This end-user computing device standard provides agencies with a set of common traits, characteristics and specifications for devices such as desktops, laptops, tablets and smartphones. Creating a common set of standards for end-user computing devices is expected to produce a wide range of benefits for the state as well as for individual agencies.
                     <a href="https://admin.sc.gov/files/End-UserComputingDeviceStandardFinal10.25.17.pdf" target="_blank" title="End-User Computing Device Standard (10.25.17)">
                      View the Standard
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                    </li>
                    <li style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px;">
                     <strong>
                      Information Security and Privacy Standards Released
                     </strong>
                     <br style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px;"/>
                     The Division of Information Security (DIS) and the Enterprise Privacy Office (EPO) have released version 1.5 of the standards for compliance with InfoSec and privacy policies. These standards provide additional guidance and clarification for implementation of previously published policies; an updated tool for performing gap analysis, implementation planning, and tracking of implementation progress; as well as a new matrix mapping FedRAMP cloud services to these standards.
                     <a href="http://admin.sc.gov/files/SCDIS-200-Information-Security-and-Privacy-Standards.xlsx" target="_blank" title="Information Security and Privacy Standards">
                      View the Standard
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                    </li>
                    <li style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px;">
                     <span style="font-weight: 700; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">
                      Beware of Disaster Scams
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">
                      The SC Department of Consumer Affairs has published a brochure, titled “Beware of Disaster Scams,” that is designed to help make you aware of some of the potential scams that could take place as a result of the recent flooding across South Carolina.
                      <a href="https://admin.sc.gov/files/Beware%20of%20Scams.pdf" target="_blank" title="Beware of Disaster Scams Brochure">
                       View the Brochure
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                    </li>
                    <li>
                     <strong>
                      Security Tips: Don't Take the Bait on Phishing Scams
                     </strong>
                     <div>
                      When it comes to phishing, the best line of defense is you. If you pay attention to potential traps and watch for the telltale signs of a scam, you can help minimize your risk of becoming a victim.
                      <a href="http://www.admin.sc.gov/files/DISMonthlySecurityTipsNewseltter%20-%20Don't%20Take%20the%20Bait%20on%20Phishing%20Scams%20May%202015.pdf" target="_blank" title="Security Tips Webpage">
                       View Full Article
                      </a>
                     </div>
                    </li>
                   </ul>
