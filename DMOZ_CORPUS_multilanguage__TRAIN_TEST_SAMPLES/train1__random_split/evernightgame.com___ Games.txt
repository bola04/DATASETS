                                     the player's borders rapidly expand.

      <div class="panel-heading text-center section-title">
       <h1>
        Gameplay
       </h1>
      </div>
      <div class="panel-body">
       <div class="row">
        <div class="col-sm-6 col-md-4">
         <div class="thumbnail">
          <a href="/Images/screenshots/gamelobby.JPG" target="_blank">
           <img alt="The War Room" class="img-responsive img-rounded" src="/Images/screenshots/gamelobby-3.JPG"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="details">
          <h3 class="">
           Game Lobby
          </h3>
          In a standard game the tick runs once every 24 hours, except on weekends and holidays, at a set time each day.
                                 This allows you approximately 24 hours to enter your orders for each cycle.
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="col-sm-6 col-md-4">
         <div class="thumbnail">
          <a href="/Images/screenshots/browsegame.JPG" target="_blank">
           <img alt="The War Room" class="img-responsive img-rounded" src="/Images/screenshots/browsegame-3.JPG"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="details">
          <h3 class="">
           The War Room
          </h3>
          The game interface a player sees is called the War Room.
 
                                 From here, armies are called forth to do battle against opponents
                                 and cast
          <a class="scrollto" href="#spells">
           devastating spells
          </a>
          with the power to change even the
          <a class="scrollto" href="#resources">
           landscape of the world
          </a>
          itself.
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="col-sm-12 col-md-4">
         <div class="thumbnail">
          <a href="/Images/screenshots/playerlobby.JPG" target="_blank">
           <img alt="The War Room" class="img-responsive img-rounded" src="/Images/screenshots/playerlobby-3.JPG"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="details">
          <h3 class="">
           Player Lobby
          </h3>
          Rankings are generally run in different groupings, includingRank, Games Finished / Games Played, Wins, Experience, and Skill Rating
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="panel-footer text-center">
       <p>
        Evernight is a turn-based game.
                         What that means is that you tell the game what you want your
        <a class="scrollto" data-interval="false" data-pause="hover" data-ride="carousel" data-slide-to="6" data-target="#carousel-forms" href="#entities">
         forces
        </a>
        to do (ie, move to a neighboring region).
                         Then, at set times, the game will process all player moves at the same time -- this includes fighting battles.
       </p>
       <p>
        When you log in after the turn has been processed, you will see the results of the previous turn's battles,
                         and be able to give your orders for the next turn.
       </p>
      </div>

       <h1 class="">
        Resources
       </h1>
       <p>
        There a two resources in Evernight.
        <a class="scrollto" href="#treasure">
         Treasure
        </a>
        generated from the various terrain types and
        <a class="scrollto" href="#fury">
         Fury
        </a>
        earned though Battles!
       </p>

      <div class="panel-body">
       <div class="row" id="treasure">
        <div class="col-md-5">
         <img alt="build structures and buy lessers" class="img-responsive img-rounded center-block" src="/Images/structures/citadel.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <div class="col-md-7">
         <h2>
          Treasure
         </h2>
         <p class="">
          Used to build structures and buy
          <a class="scrollto" data-interval="false" data-pause="hover" data-ride="carousel" data-slide-to="6" data-target="#carousel-forms" href="#entities">
           lessers
          </a>
          , the main combat force in Evernight.
                                 You start the game with 50 Treasure.
                                 Each turn, you receive Treasure according to the number and type of regions you control.
         </p>
         <p>
          Most of the region types have a special property and produce varying amounts of treasure.
         </p>
