     </font>

If you would like your transceiver programmed, please see David White, K6OG.  These frequencies were selected for operations from Estes Park (2-11-16).      <font face="sans-serif" size="4">
      WinLink 2000
     </font>
    </b>
    <font face="sans-serif">
     <br/>
     Our Global Radio E-Mail System is "on air" as of September 2, 2011.  We are 
   running Radio Mail Server (RMS) packet software on VHF, 145.030 MHz (1200 Baud).  The 
   call sign is N0FH-10.  We added another E-Mail System in September 2017. This is  a RMS server on UHF, 431.025 MHz (9600 Baud). For more information see,
     <a href="http://winlink.org" target="_blank">
      http://winlink.org
     </a>
     .
    </font>

    <b>
     <font face="sans-serif" size="4">
      Battery Backup
     </font>
    </b>
    <font face="sans-serif">
     <br/>
     Our two voice repeaters have a new DC (battery) backup system as of September 7, 
   2011.  It is more efficient than our previous inverter system.  We can 
   now provide about 20 hours of emergency power before we need to start the 
   generator.
    </font>
   </p>

     </font>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="left" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 1">
    <font face="sans-serif">
     Our IRLP connection is "on air" as of March 14, 2010.  The node is
     <b>
      <a href="http://status.irlp.net/?nodeid=7268" target="_blank">
       7268
      </a>
      .
     </b>
     Our thanks to Rocky Mountain  Ham Radio Club for their technical 
 assistance, hardware, and installation of the system.  See
     <a href="http://rmham.org" target="_blank">
      RM HAM
     </a>
     link for information on 
 their club.  Since our VHF and UHF repeaters are normally linked, the IRLP node 
 connects to both machines. The node is open (no passwords; touch tone only the 
 four digit node number where you wish to connect).  The disconnect is '73'.
    </font>

    <b>
     <font face="sans-serif" size="4">
      Simplex Frequency
     </font>
    </b>
   </p>
   <p align="left" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 1">
    <font face="sans-serif">
     The club at its April 21, 2012 meeting adopted 146.550 MHz as the club VHF 
 simplex frequency.  After initiating a contact on the National Calling 
 Frequency (146.520 MHz), please QSY to 146.550 MHz when practical.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p align="left" style="margin-bottom: 1">
    When in the Estes Valley, the Wilderness Protocol calling frequency is 146.520 MHz. Listening is the first five minutes at the top of the hour. See ARRL Repeater Directory for more information.
   </p>
