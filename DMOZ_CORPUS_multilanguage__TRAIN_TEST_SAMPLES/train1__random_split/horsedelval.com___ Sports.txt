               <h2 itemprop="name">
                Devon H.S. &amp; Country Fair enjoyed a banner year
               </h2>
               <dl class="article-info">
                <dt class="article-info-term">
                 Details
                </dt>
                <dd class="published">
                 <time datetime="2018-07-25T21:55:06+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
                  July 25, 2018
                 </time>
                </dd>
                <dd class="createdby" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                 By
                 <span itemprop="name">
                  SARA CAVANAGH
                 </span>
                </dd>
               </dl>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 DEVON, Pa--
                </strong>
                Both gross income and ticket sales were up despite a number of days of bad weather at the Devon Horse Show and Country Fair, which ran May 24 to June 3 this year.
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="wf_caption" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left; display: block; max-width: 300px; width: 100%;">
                 <img alt="McLain Ward on Clinta Devon" src="/images/news_/McLain_Ward_on_Clinta_Devon.jpg" style="margin: auto; float: left; width: 100%;" width="300"/>
                 <span style="display: block;">
                  McLain Ward on Clinta
                 </span>
                </span>
                There was a significant increase in the number of open jumper riders who are Olympic Games veterans, many of them also Olympic medalists, and that resulted in a number of thrilling finishes in the jumper classes.
               </p>
               <p>
                But it was once again McLain Ward who won the $250,000 Sapphire Grand Prix of Devon, this time on a very exciting new ride for Ward, the gray mare Clinta.
               </p>
               <p>
                Devon offered record prize money in 2017 that exceeded $850,000.
               </p>

               <h2 itemprop="name">
                U.S. dressage team second in Aachen Nations Cup
               </h2>
               <dl class="article-info">
                <dt class="article-info-term">
                 Details
                </dt>
                <dd class="published">
                 <time datetime="2018-07-22T15:41:58+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
                  July 22, 2018
                 </time>
                </dd>
                <dd class="createdby" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                 By
                 <span itemprop="name">
                  Staff Writers
                 </span>
                </dd>
               </dl>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 AACHEN, Germany--
                </strong>
                The U.S. Dressage team was leading after the Grand Prix, the first round of the Nations Cup in Aachen, but Germany came back strong in the second competition, the Grand Prix Special, to win the gold medal with the U.S. taking the silver medal on Saturday, July 21 at the World Equestrian Festival CHIO Aachen.
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="wf_caption" style="margin-left: 10px; float: right; display: block; max-width: 286px; width: 100%;">
                 <img alt="Kasey Perry glass dubletaa18brinkman7 21d 4381 large" src="/images/competitions/Kasey_Perry-glass_dubletaa18brinkman7-21d-4381_large.jpg" style="margin: auto; float: right; width: 100%;" width="286"/>
                 <span style="display: block;">
                  Kasey Perry-Glass on Goerklintgaards Dublet
                 </span>
                </span>
                Under the leadership of Chef d’Equipe Robert Dover, the U.S. team of Laura Graves, Adrienne Lyle, Kasey Perry-Glass, and Steffen Peters produced a final score of 459.371, just 4.98 points behind the German team.
               </p>
               <p>
                In the Grand Prix, Germany's top rider, Isabell Werth, had a bad day on Emilio and ultimately landed in 17th place, which left the path clear for the USA and giving the win to Laura Graves on Verdades.
               </p>
               <p>
                But then in the Grand Prix Special, it was Graves who had an uncharacteristically bad day on Verdades as he spooked at a camera and ended on a score of 74.085, while Werth came back with a 79.128 to clinch the win for Germany.
               </p>

               <h2 itemprop="name">
                Blackstone's colt by Street Sense sold for $350,000 at the Fasig-Tipton July sale that posted gains
               </h2>
               <dl class="article-info">
                <dt class="article-info-term">
                 Details
                </dt>
                <dd class="published">
                 <time datetime="2018-07-15T21:47:55+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
                  July 15, 2018
                 </time>
                </dd>
                <dd class="createdby" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                 By
                 <span itemprop="name">
                  Staff Writers
                 </span>
                </dd>
               </dl>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 LEXINGTON, Ky.--
                </strong>
                A Pa-bred colt by Street Sense bred by Blackstone Farm LLC brought $350,000 at Fasig-Tipton's July 10 sale in Lexington.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="catalog07102018thumb" src="/images/catalog07102018thumb.jpg" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left;"/>
                The colt, hip numbr 312, out of Mahkama, by Bernardiini, the Breeders' Cup nominated colt was consigned by Michael &amp; Julia O'Quinn Agent and bought by Godolphin.
               </p>
               <p>
                The July Sale posted gains across the board, with increases in gross, average, and median over last year’s sale and the highest average since 2007, while the gross and median were the highest since 2015.
               </p>
               <p>
                The average was $100,829 as opposed to the 2017 average of $93,645, with 196 sold this year with 86 not sold against 172 sold last year, when 72 were not sold.
               </p>
               <p>
                Seven Pa-breds went through the ring, with two not sold.
               </p>

               <h2 itemprop="name">
                Youngsters from Maryland, Pennsylvania and Virginia to participate in the 2018 The National Steeplechase Foundation Domestic Young Rider Trip.
               </h2>
               <dl class="article-info">
                <dt class="article-info-term">
                 Details
                </dt>
                <dd class="published">
                 <time datetime="2018-07-17T17:08:35+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
                  July 17, 2018
                 </time>
                </dd>
                <dd class="createdby" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                 By
                 <span itemprop="name">
                  Staff Writers
                 </span>
                </dd>
               </dl>
               <p>
                Edited from NSF Press Release
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
               <p>
                The National Steeplechase Foundation Domestic Young Rider Trip is a 5-day overnight experience structured towards providing the participant with an in-depth experience of what steeplechase racing in the USA is all about.
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="wf_caption" style="margin-left: 10px; float: right; display: block; max-width: 89px; width: 100%;">
                 <img alt="Janet Elliots sweater front" src="/images/news_/Janet_Elliots_sweater_front.jpg" style="margin: auto; float: right; width: 100%;" width="89"/>
                 <span class="wf_caption" style="margin-left: 10px; float: right; display: block; width: 100%;">
                  <span style="display: block;">
                   Janet Elliot, who will work with these six participants
                  </span>
                 </span>
                </span>
                It is for teenagers who are familiar with jump racing and who have a demonstrated interest in learning more about the sport.
               </p>
               <p>
                The ideal candidate will have expressed interest in being involved with steeplechase racing in the future.
               </p>
               <p>
                Activities include a hands-on visit to trainers’ barns in Maryland, Pennsylvania and Virginia; attending lectures and discussion sessions with trainers, jockeys, owners and stewards; and attending races at Saratoga Race Course in Saratoga Springs, New York.
               </p>
               <p>
                The 2018 participants include:
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>

                <span class="wf_caption" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left; display: block; max-width: 133px; width: 100%;">
                 <img alt="Charlie Fenwick Jack Fisher Willowdale" src="/images/racing/Charlie_Fenwick__Jack_Fisher_-_Willowdale.jpg" style="margin: auto; float: left; width: 100%;" width="133"/>
                 <span style="display: block;">
                  Charlie Fenwick and Jack Fisher on a dark and chilly day at Willowdale
                 </span>
                </span>
                Despite the cold and rain, there was a large and very enthusiastic crowd of spectators, with cars filling virtually every available parking space and kids riding stick horses and playing games.
               </p>
               <p>
                Doc Cebu, owned by Charles C. Fenwick, Jr., Michael Hankin and Charles Noell, trained by Jack Fisher and ridden by Hadden Frost, also won this race last year.
               </p>
               <p>
                Armata Stable's Grand Manon, trained by William Meister and ridden by Chris Gracie, went out to a big early lead, but he lost his rider on the second circuit of the three mile race, leaving the lead to Black and Blue Stable's Monstaleur, trained and ridden by Forrest Kelly.
               </p>
               <p>
                Doc Cebu took command after the horses emerged from the woods at the top of the hill for the last time, and Sycamore Run Farm's Pured It, trained by Katherine Neilson and ridden by Eddie Keating, moved up through the stretch to place second.
               </p>
               <p>
                "It was a shame Grand Manon lost his rider," said Fenwick. "Doc Cebu jumped well."
               </p>
               <p>
                When he first started his career as a jumper, Doc Cebu was owned solely by Fenwick, who is also in partnership with Hankin and Noell in Bruton Street-US.
               </p>
               <p>
                Fenwick said he decided last summer that, seeing as he was in the Bruton Street-US partnership, that Doc Cebu should also be owned in partnership, but that, instead of Bruton Street-US being named as owner, Doc Cebu would run in his name.
               </p>
               <p>
                "He's a riches to rags to riches story," said Fenwick of the 8-year-old by Hard Spun out of a Jade Hunter mare. "He was very highly thought of as a 2-year-old. "But, racing on the flat, he ended up at Charlestown, exhausted."
               </p>
               <p>
                That was when Fenwick got him and tried him over fences.
               </p>
               <p>
                "He's won seven races for us," said Fenwick. "He's a tough customer. He has a lot of personality. I love him, but I don't trust him. He'll bite you, and he might kick you,"
               </p>
               <p>
                "We tried to fox hunt him, but that didn't do," said Fenwick. "He seems to love this. He tries really hard."
               </p>

               <p>
                <br/>
                Polo has been termed the "sport of kings”.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="B Polo C Web Header" src="/images/real_estate/B_Polo_C_Web_Header.jpg" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left;"/>

               </p>
               <p>
                We are fortunate in this area to have the Brandywine Polo Club, which is one of the oldest clubs on the East Coast with a rich history and four tournament- size playing fields as well as a full-size playing arena.
               </p>
               <p>
                Brandywine Polo Club was started in 1950 by James McHugh, a former intercollegiate player at Yale.
               </p>

                <img alt="sunday polo sm" src="/images/news_/sunday-polo-sm.jpg" style="margin-left: 10px; float: right;"/>

               </p>

                Brandywine Polo Club is committed to making polo accessible to everyone.
               </p>
               <p>
                With a polo school that dates back to 1963, the Brandywine Polo School has continued to offer an introductory program that is perfect for anyone interested in pursuing polo regardless of age or experience level.
               </p>
               <p>
                It’s a relaxed environment that current and former students claim “is the most fun you can have on four legs!”.
               </p>
               <p>
                <img alt="learn to play" src="/images/news_/learn-to-play.jpg" style="margin-left: 10px; float: right;"/>

               </p>

               <h2 itemprop="name">
                Joe Davies swept the board at the Grand National Steeplechase, winning all three races including the featured Grand National
               </h2>
               <dl class="article-info">
                <dt class="article-info-term">
                 Details
                </dt>
                <dd class="published">
                 <time datetime="2018-04-24T16:04:39+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
                  April 24, 2018
                 </time>
                </dd>
                <dd class="createdby" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
                 By
                 <span itemprop="name">
                  SARA CAVANAGH
                 </span>
                </dd>
               </dl>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 BUTLER, Md.--
                </strong>
                Joseph Davies saddled 2016 Maryland Hunt Cup winner Senior Senator, ridden by Eric Poretz, to win the featured $50,000 Grand National Amateur Timber Stakes at the Grand National Steeplechase on April 21, and followed that up with wins in the next two races.
               </p>
               <p>
                <span class="wf_caption" style="margin-right: 10px; float: left; display: block; max-width: 241px; width: 100%;">
                 <img alt="senior senator last fence G N" src="/images/racing/senior_senator_last_fence_G_N.jpg" style="margin: auto; float: left; width: 100%;" width="241"/>
                 <span style="display: block;">
                  Senior Senator
                 </span>
                </span>
                "It was a good day," said Davies.
               </p>
               <p>
                Senior Senator is nominated for the Maryland Hunt Cup on April 28, but Davies said that he hadn't decided yet if they were going to run him back.
               </p>
               <p>
                "He had an amazing run yesterday," said Davies on Sunday about Senior Senator's race at the Grand National.
               </p>
               <p>
                Senior Senator has a history of acting up before a race, often having been permitted to actually saddle outside of the paddock, away from the other horses.
               </p>
               <p>
                "Some horses freeze before a race," said Davies, who said that wasn't the case with Senior Senator. "He's just exuberant. He wants to get on with it. Just getting him to a race requires expertise and a number of people to tack him up. It can be dangerous."
               </p>
