               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>

              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nwaz2LVqxcY/W1h_2vcqFJI/AAAAAAAAEDg/Op7V6qJndMoTJMn2E98gynazNllVwPt-ACLcBGAs/s1600/Peach-Berry-Crumble-Cake.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Peach-Berry Crumble Cake - a cake and a fruit crumble all in one / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="380" data-original-width="380" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nwaz2LVqxcY/W1h_2vcqFJI/AAAAAAAAEDg/Op7V6qJndMoTJMn2E98gynazNllVwPt-ACLcBGAs/s1600/Peach-Berry-Crumble-Cake.jpg" title="Peach-Berry Crumble Cake - a cake and a fruit crumble all in one / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>

               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Peach-Berry Crumble Cake is for those occasions when you’re in the mood for a crumble but you don’t have enough fruit. Or when half the family wants crumble, and the other half wants cake.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                People always ask me for this recipe, so that’s a good sign you’ll like it, too. And it’s so easy. No need to bust out the big stand mixer, just use a hand mixer or even a spoon.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                The most important part of any recipe that includes peaches is, of course, the peaches. A friend told me yesterday that she doesn’t buy peaches anymore because she never gets good ones.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                If you have that problem, just do what I do: go by color and smell. Look for yellow peaches with good color and no green shoulders (area around the stem). Then give each peach the sniff test. If it doesn’t smell peachy, it won’t have much flavor.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Are you a peach fan? What is your favorite summer fruit?
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-l2FNYVQZY8Y/W1iAbHjL_5I/AAAAAAAAEDo/p8REXt1R3W8q8RQ4rCnMVA9Xhe_MmB2_QCLcBGAs/s1600/Peach-Berry%2BCrumble%2BCake.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Peach-Berry Crumble - a cake and a fruit crumble all in one / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="570" data-original-width="380" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-l2FNYVQZY8Y/W1iAbHjL_5I/AAAAAAAAEDo/p8REXt1R3W8q8RQ4rCnMVA9Xhe_MmB2_QCLcBGAs/s1600/Peach-Berry%2BCrumble%2BCake.jpg" title="Peach-Berry Crumble Cake - a cake and a fruit crumble all in one / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>

               <br/>
               <h3>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 Peach-Berry Crumble Cake
                </span>
               </h3>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                (Makes one 8-inch/20-cm square cake, 8 or 9 servings)
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h4>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Crumble
                </span>
               </h4>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                3/4 cup (3 ounces/85 grams) rolled oats
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/2 cup (2.5 ounces/71 grams) unbleached all-purpose flour
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/4 packed cup (1.75 ounces/50 grams) dark brown sugar
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/4 cup (1 ounce/28 grams) finely chopped pecans
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/4 teaspoon salt
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                6 tablespoons (3 ounces/85 grams) unsalted butter, cold, coarsely shredded
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h4>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Fruit
                </span>
               </h4>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1 to 1 1/4 cups (about 6 ounces/170 grams) fresh blueberries
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1 cup chopped yellow peaches
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h4>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Cake
                </span>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </h4>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                2 teaspoons cider vinegar
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Enough milk to make 3/4 cup (6 fluid ounces/177 ml)
                <br/>
                1 1/2 dip-and-sweep cups (7.5 ounces/213 grams) unbleached all-purpose flour
                <br/>
                3/4 cup (5.25 ounces/149 grams) sugar
                <br/>
                1 teaspoon baking powder
                <br/>
                3/4 teaspoon baking soda
                <br/>
                1/2 teaspoon salt
                <br/>
                8 tablespoons (4 ounces/113 grams) unsalted butter, room temperature
                <br/>
                2 large eggs, room temperature
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  1
                 </b>
                 Preheat oven to 350F/180C/Gas4. Butter (or spray with cooking spray) an
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Pyrex-Square-Baking-Dish-Plastic/dp/B01E4BZ12W/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=kitchen&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1532526163&amp;sr=1-1&amp;keywords=pyrex+8-inch+square+baking+dish&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=4693a3d9278db649f6452b733d91f0a2&amp;language=en_US" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   8-inch square baking dish
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 . In small
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Pyrex-1085308-Essentials-6-Piece-Mixing/dp/B002FHSPAI/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=home-garden&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1532525869&amp;sr=1-19&amp;keywords=pyrex+mixing+bowls+with+lids&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=c8c7003a86a23e893c9a02b92564415e&amp;language=en_US" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   1.5-quart mixing bowl
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 , whisk together oats, flour, sugar, pecans, salt and cinnamon. With your fingers, rub the butter into the dry mixture, leaving some larger chunks of butter. Remove 1 cup of crumble from the bowl.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  2
                 </b>
                 Prepare fruit. I rarely peel peaches for anything, just scrub them well. Measure vinegar into 1-cup glass measure; add enough milk to make 3/4 cup. Stir and let stand about 5 minutes to thicken a bit while you proceed.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  3
                 </b>
                 In medium
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Pyrex-1085308-Essentials-6-Piece-Mixing/dp/B002FHSPAI/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=home-garden&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1532525869&amp;sr=1-19&amp;keywords=pyrex+mixing+bowls+with+lids&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=c8c7003a86a23e893c9a02b92564415e&amp;language=en_US" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   2.5-quart mixing bowl
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 , whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda and salt. Add the softened butter; with electric hand mixer, mix on low speed until cut in. Add soured milk, eggs and vanilla. Mix on low speed for 30 seconds to combine, then on medium speed for 1 minute.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
               </span>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  4
                 </b>
                 Pour into prepared pan. Scatter 1 cup of the crumble over the batter, add a layer of fruit, and top with remaining crumble. Bake for 45 to 55 minutes, or until golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
                </span>
                <br/>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  <br/>
                 </b>
                </span>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  5
                 </b>
                 Cool in pan on wire rack for 20 minutes to serve warm, or let cool completely and serve at room temperature.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
               <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/07/sunflower-seed-romesco-sauce.html">
                Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DnrPtYOm_Lg/W05d-8ntJXI/AAAAAAAAEDI/aQgPUWP1lVscCfLr4den3hRFYdNTVYciQCLcBGAs/s1600/Sunflower%2BSeed%2BRomesco%2BSauce.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce - good on absolutely anything! / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="380" data-original-width="380" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DnrPtYOm_Lg/W05d-8ntJXI/AAAAAAAAEDI/aQgPUWP1lVscCfLr4den3hRFYdNTVYciQCLcBGAs/s1600/Sunflower%2BSeed%2BRomesco%2BSauce.jpg" title="Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce - good on absolutely anything! / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Romesco is a nut and red bell pepper-based sauce that originated in Catalonia, Spain, and was served with the local catch. Served with seafood, escargot, poultry, lamb, vegetables, it’s good on just about anything!
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce is one of less than a handful of recipes on my blog that is not my own recipe. It was sent to me by my friend Jo who just made it for a dinner party where it was a huge hit. She can’t remember where she got it, but I think it may have been Sunset magazine.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Of course, I made a couple of changes. Was out of sherry vinegar, so I used red wine vinegar. Was out of flat-leaf parsley and had some cilantro I wanted to use up, so I used cilantro. I recommend it! And I changed some amounts. Oh and I left out the garlic; trust me, you won't even miss it.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                I used just 3 tablespoons of tomato paste, not wanting the sauce to be too tomato-y, and it wasn’t. Four tablespoons would probably be fine, too. This stuff is seriously good!
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">
                Today I put it on an organic grass-fed burger. Tomorrow? Chicken, roasted potatoes, vegetables, salad, sandwiches, pasta, a dip for crudites—I might just eat it by the spoonful right out of the jar!
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Takes no time at all to make. I made it in my blender (
                <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Blendtec-D1E28DC16HA-A1GP1D-Designer-Blender-Stainless/dp/B00KWM7DLA/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1531861418&amp;sr=8-5&amp;keywords=blendtec+725&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=b60f390a5fb0afb17f293017c96866d2" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Blendtec 725
                 </span>
                </a>
                ), but it can also be made in a food processor. Poured it into a pint jar. Now I'm wondering how well it would freeze. I'll freeze a small jar and let you know.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <i>
                 <br/>
                </i>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <i>
                 Update 07/22/18: I thawed the frozen jar overnight in the refrigerator and tried it this morning. It was wonderful! So I'm going to make another batch today to freeze in two half-pint jars.
                </i>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/--eS7u6w5IrY/W05dU9N1zLI/AAAAAAAAEDA/Rdmz9YbhHA05UVrIov_F5xQ0cA5WQKqLwCLcBGAs/s1600/Sunflower-Seed-Romesco-Sauce.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                  <img alt="Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce - good on absolutely anything you can think of! / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="570" data-original-width="380" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/--eS7u6w5IrY/W05dU9N1zLI/AAAAAAAAEDA/Rdmz9YbhHA05UVrIov_F5xQ0cA5WQKqLwCLcBGAs/s1600/Sunflower-Seed-Romesco-Sauce.jpg" title="Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce - good on absolutely anything you can think of! / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                 </a>
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span style="color: red;">
                  Don't forget to Pin it and share it!
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="text-align: center;">
                 <span style="color: red;">
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </div>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h3>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 Sunflower Seed Romesco Sauce
                </span>
               </h3>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 (Makes about 2 cups)
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/2 cup unsalted sunflower seeds, toasted in small dry skillet
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/2 packed cup jarred roasted red bell peppers
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/2 packed cup cilantro or flat-leaf parsley
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 3 or 4 tablespoons tomato paste
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/3 cup water
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar or sherry vinegar
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/4 teaspoon salt
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/8 to 1/4 teaspoon cayenne
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  1
                 </b>
                 Put all the ingredients in food processor or
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Blendtec-D1E28DC16HA-A1GP1D-Designer-Blender-Stainless/dp/B00KWM7DLA/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1531861418&amp;sr=8-5&amp;keywords=blendtec+725&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=b60f390a5fb0afb17f293017c96866d2" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   blender
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 ; process or blend until as smooth as you like it.
                </span>
                <br/>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  <br/>
                 </b>
                </span>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  2
                 </b>
                 Taste and adjust seasoning.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  3
                 </b>
                 Cover and chill for up to 5 days. Haven't tried it yet but am told it can be frozen for up to 3 months.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
               <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/07/ginger-snaps-ginger-nuts-or-ginger.html">
                Ginger Snaps - Ginger Nuts or Ginger Biscuits
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-iFaFNrnPRlw/W0ZWG7JSnNI/AAAAAAAAECs/f0lDkZYC5EAmAsQiFi-nzNtxxLTxdso5gCLcBGAs/s1600/Ginger-Snaps.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Ginger Snaps - Ginger Nuts or Ginger Biscuits - Stamped or Not / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="380" data-original-width="380" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-iFaFNrnPRlw/W0ZWG7JSnNI/AAAAAAAAECs/f0lDkZYC5EAmAsQiFi-nzNtxxLTxdso5gCLcBGAs/s1600/Ginger-Snaps.jpg" title="Ginger Snaps - Ginger Nuts or Ginger Biscuits - Stamped or Not / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                It was too hot to bake, but I was in the mood for some crunchy ginger cookies to have with my tea. AND I was eager to play with my
                <span style="color: black;">
                 cookie stamps
                </span>
                again! There was no recipe, so I did what I always do—made it up as I went.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Of course, if you don't have
                <a href="http://www.rycraft.com/our_products" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  cookie stamps
                 </span>
                </a>
                , you can just use something (bottom of a glass, bottom of a stainless steel measuring cup, your hand) to flatten the dough balls into 2-inch rounds. Maybe not as cute, but just as tasty!
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                For those of you who are dunkers—I am not, but if you are, please go right ahead—these are good dunking biscuits. Just the right size, crunchy and sturdy enough to hold up to a good dunking.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                As I mentioned, it was hot. So I was having a little trouble stamping my cookie dough, even chilled. But I came up with a trick I'm going to use even on cold days: a very, very light dusting of flour on the dough balls.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Just flatten the dough ball a bit, flour lightly, brush away the excess, proceed with stamping (step 5 below), and there you go, no sticking! Worked well with my stainless steel 1/4-cup measuring cup I used to make unstamped cookies.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Here are my
                <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/06/stamped-shortbread-cookies-rycraft.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Stamped Shortbread Cookies
                 </span>
                </a>
                . Sooo good with tea. Tell me, are you a dunker or a non-dunker?
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                PS Don't strain your eyes! The cookie in the middle in both photos is not stamped, just flattened with a flat-bottomed stainless steel measuring cup about 2 inches in diameter.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                PPS If you're a chewy cookie fan, you might like my
                <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2012/03/molasses-ginger-cookies-old-fashioned.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Molasses Ginger Cookies
                 </span>
                </a>
                .
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3JcES-4xw-I/W0ZWhBUcwJI/AAAAAAAAEC0/SNQQp3GesSYsjzjWLfVCNaMWr3pphLBNQCLcBGAs/s1600/Ginger%2BSnaps.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Ginger Snaps - Ginger Nuts or Ginger Biscuits - Stamped or Not / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="570" data-original-width="380" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3JcES-4xw-I/W0ZWhBUcwJI/AAAAAAAAEC0/SNQQp3GesSYsjzjWLfVCNaMWr3pphLBNQCLcBGAs/s1600/Ginger%2BSnaps.jpg" title="Ginger Snaps - Ginger Nuts or Ginger Biscuits - Stamped or Not / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h3>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 Ginger Snaps - Ginger Nuts or Ginger Biscuits
                </span>
               </h3>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                (Makes about 20 2-inch cookies)
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1 1/4 dip-and-sweep cup (6.25 ounces/177 grams) unbleached all-purpose flour
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/4 teaspoon salt
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 3/4 teaspoon cinnamon
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 3/4 teaspoon ginger
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/4 teaspoon allspice
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1 stick (4 ounces/113 grams) unsalted butter
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/4 cup (1.75 ounces/50 grams) sugar
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 2 tablespoons unsulphured molasses
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  1
                 </b>
                 In small bowl, whisk together flour, salt, cinnamon, ginger and cloves.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  2
                 </b>
                 In 1.5-quart mixing bowl, by hand or with electric hand mixer on lowest speed, cream the butter and sugar just until smooth, mixing in the molasses toward the end.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  3
                 </b>
                 Add flour mixture and mix well without beating a lot of air into it. If mixture is too dry to form a dough, mix in a teaspoon of water. Wrap dough in plastic wrap, forming a 4-inch disk; refrigerate for 20 to 30 minutes. If you overchill the dough, it will be difficult to work with.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  4
                 </b>
                 Using a measuring tablespoon, measure out level tablespoons of dough onto parchment-lined baking sheet. Roll the scoops into smooth balls. Cover and chill in the refrigerator for 15 minutes or so. This will make them easy to stamp, and the design will be very sharp. But if you overchill the dough balls, they will be difficult to stamp.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  5
                 </b>
                 To stamp, with dough ball in the center of the stamp, press the stamp evenly till the dough reaches the edge of the stamp. Gently lift the stamp away. Repeat. My technique is to lift the stamp with the dough attached and gently peel away at the edge. This works very well with the chilled dough. If you find the dough balls getting too warm before you’re finished stamping, put the baking sheet back in the refrigerator for 15 minutes.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  6
                 </b>
                 Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour. About 15 minutes before you’d like to bake the cookies, preheat the oven to 325F/165C/Gas3.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  7
                 </b>
                 Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until quite firm and edges are starting to brown. Cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes. Then transfer to wire rack to cool completely.
                </span>
               </div>

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
               <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/07/tomatillo-salsa-verde-for-streamlined.html">
                Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-F1ztK-w1r3Q/Wzb-jsb3z0I/AAAAAAAAECE/y4L9bn0dpQcRPk6YP6jAsuAsjxJp-S8tQCLcBGAs/s1600/Tomatillo%2BSalsa%2BVerde.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="380" data-original-width="380" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-F1ztK-w1r3Q/Wzb-jsb3z0I/AAAAAAAAECE/y4L9bn0dpQcRPk6YP6jAsuAsjxJp-S8tQCLcBGAs/s1600/Tomatillo%2BSalsa%2BVerde.jpg" title="Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                My Tomatillo Salsa Verde might be a little different from those meant to be used as a salsa. I developed this formulation to be used in my recipes. It’s such a timesaver having jars of this in the freezer for days when I’m craving
                <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2017/11/chili-verde-chile-verde-green-chili.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Chili Verde
                 </span>
                </a>
                , for example, but don’t have the time or energy to make it the regular way.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                As you might recall, I was crestfallen when Muir Glen discontinued their jarred crushed organic tomatillos that were such a timesaver. So my initial idea was to make my own and freeze it in jars. Then I thought how convenient it would be to have
                <i>
                 all
                </i>
                the ingredients except the pork and the cilantro garnish made ahead.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                So, working off my
                <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2017/11/chili-verde-chile-verde-green-chili.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Chili Verde
                 </span>
                </a>
                recipe, I came up with this recipe that makes just the right amount for two batches of Chili Verde. I divide it between two wide-mouth quart jars and pop them in the freezer, or use one now and freeze one.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Some of my favorite recipes can be quite time consuming, and I'm actually in the process of streamlining some of them so that we can enjoy them more often. So if that sounds good to you, be sure to follow me and stay tuned!
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-0uKWi4LGZfo/Wzb-8wVVYrI/AAAAAAAAECM/dxDJ7M1T-38ZDVFbmLV2PWRj8LMionzKgCLcBGAs/s1600/Tomatillo%2BSalsa%2BVerde%2BIngredients.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking (this photo - the raw ingredients in a saucepan) / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="380" data-original-width="380" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-0uKWi4LGZfo/Wzb-8wVVYrI/AAAAAAAAECM/dxDJ7M1T-38ZDVFbmLV2PWRj8LMionzKgCLcBGAs/s1600/Tomatillo%2BSalsa%2BVerde%2BIngredients.jpg" title="Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking (this photo - the raw ingredients in a saucepan) / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <br/>
               <h3>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 Tomatillo Salsa Verde
                </span>
               </h3>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                (Makes a little more than 6 cups)
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                2
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 pounds tomatillos, husked and quartered
                </span>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1 medium (about 12 ounces/340 grams) yellow onion, cut into 1-inch chunks
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1 7-ounce can diced or whole mild green chiles
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1 4-inch jalapeno pepper, halved lengthwise, seeds and ribs removed, coarsely chopped
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 teaspoon salt
                </span>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/2 teaspoon cumin
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/2 teaspoon oregano
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 cup (8 fluid ounces/237 ml) water
                </span>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <b>
                 1
                </b>
                In 3- to
                <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Creuset-Tri-Ply-Stainless-Saucier-3-5-Quart/dp/B00I9CZGAK/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1530545299&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=le+creuset+3.5-quart+saucier&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=93a2fe99ba57d1ae6dee56bb70ee828d" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  3.5-quart saucepan
                 </span>
                </a>
                , bring all ingredients to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 15 minutes or until tomatillos are tender and starting to fall apart. Let cool for 15 minutes.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <b>
                 2
                </b>
                Using an
                <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Cuisinart-CSB-179-Smart-Blender-Stainless/dp/B079NJTL29/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=home-garden&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1530545713&amp;sr=1-4&amp;keywords=cuisinart+stainless+steel+immersion+blender+csb-179&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=f19be10d529f6f621d8d8c077a377826" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  immersion blender
                 </span>
                </a>
                , blend mixture until it reaches desired texture. If you don’t have an immersion blender, let it cool more and then transfer to a blender or food processor.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <b>
                 3
                </b>
                Refrigerate, if using within a few days. To freeze, put in
                <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Ball-Mason-Mouth-Quart-Bands/dp/B00B80TLOM/ref=as_li_ss_tl?_encoding=UTF8&amp;pd_rd_i=B00B80TLOM&amp;pd_rd_r=8J5YBFTPNYYJ2HA0VF6K&amp;pd_rd_w=tVuG6&amp;pd_rd_wg=YcQpZ&amp;psc=1&amp;refRID=8J5YBFTPNYYJ2HA0VF6K&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=2d943ff7ee2cb64c7b2f2d2f8eede08a" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  wide-mouth quart jars
                 </span>
                </a>
                or other glass freezer containers. When ready to use frozen salsa, transfer from freezer to refrigerator about 24 hours before.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Z4NMX_RQcog/Wzb_fhR4JXI/AAAAAAAAECY/BXh8ZAjEYToQR4k46SAVYM9QbiQflK9wgCLcBGAs/s1600/Tomatillo-Salsa-Verde.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="570" data-original-width="380" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Z4NMX_RQcog/Wzb_fhR4JXI/AAAAAAAAECY/BXh8ZAjEYToQR4k46SAVYM9QbiQflK9wgCLcBGAs/s1600/Tomatillo-Salsa-Verde.jpg" title="Tomatillo Salsa Verde - For Streamlined Cooking / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>

               <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/06/raspberry-danish-braid-coffeecake.html">
                Raspberry Danish Braid - Coffeecake
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SGz2QJwMFuI/WzQzr-lT8WI/AAAAAAAAEBQ/E3Y_MPmcy90fKRl26TlGRi-Vj4IoZNsJACLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry-Danish-Braid%2Bcopy.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="380" data-original-width="380" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SGz2QJwMFuI/WzQzr-lT8WI/AAAAAAAAEBQ/E3Y_MPmcy90fKRl26TlGRi-Vj4IoZNsJACLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry-Danish-Braid%2Bcopy.jpg" title="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                Raspberry Danish Braid popped into my head when I was organizing my freezer and saw that I still had a piece of croissant dough in there. Of course, you can also make this braid with my
                <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2014/10/cinnamon-nut-coffee-cake-easy-danish.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Easy Danish Dough
                 </span>
                </a>
                or my
                <a href="https://www.delightfulrepast.com/2013/03/ruff-puff-quick-puff-pastry.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  Ruff Puff - Quick Puff Pastry
                 </span>
                </a>
                . Or even frozen puff pastry.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                But if you’d like to make it with croissant dough, here’s
                <a href="http://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/01/how-to-make-croissants-tutorial.html" target="_blank">
                 <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                  How to Make Croissants – A Tutorial
                 </span>
                </a>
                . The recipe makes enough dough for three braids, but I usually just make one at a time, using a third of the dough. You can use all sorts of things for the filling, but I’ve found a mere 5 tablespoons of raspberry jam works beautifully and is quick and easy.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                The finished braid is longer than my longest rectangular platters, so the ends are cut off in the photos. Also, I did not put a glaze on top of the braid pictured as I didn't want the added sweetness, but you might want to add it.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                It's been a bit hectic this week, so this wouldn't have been on the menu if not for my secret stash of dough in the freezer. I tell you,
                <i>
                 always always always
                </i>
                make more dough than you need and freeze it in tidy batches. It's great to be able to whip out a special homemade treat with practically zero effort on a busy day!
               </span>
               <br/>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6jxX7GyhUJI/WzRB3NgmucI/AAAAAAAAEBc/gwr4ow3e_B0Extm6tOyn-0HqU2Woa6b-QCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry-Danish-Braid-Coffeecake.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="570" data-original-width="380" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6jxX7GyhUJI/WzRB3NgmucI/AAAAAAAAEBc/gwr4ow3e_B0Extm6tOyn-0HqU2Woa6b-QCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry-Danish-Braid-Coffeecake.jpg" title="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <span style='color: red; font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>

               <h3>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                </span>
                <span style="color: red;">
                </span>
                <br/>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 Raspberry Danish Braid
                </span>
               </h3>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                </span>
                <br/>
               </span>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                (Makes one 16-inch braid, 6 to 8 servings)
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h4>
                <br/>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Dough
                </span>
               </h4>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/3 piece (9x4 inches) of finished
                <a href="http://www.delightfulrepast.com/2018/01/how-to-make-croissants-tutorial.html" target="_blank">
                 Croissants
                </a>
                dough
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h4>
                <br/>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Filling
                </span>
               </h4>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                5 tablespoons raspberry jam
               </span>
               <br/>
               <h4>
                <br/>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Egg Wash
                </span>
               </h4>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                1/2 beaten egg (save the rest to go in something else)
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1 teaspoon water
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <h4>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 The Glaze
                </span>
               </h4>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 2 tablespoons (1 ounce/28 grams) unsalted butter, melted
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                3/4 firmly packed cup (3 ounces/85 grams) unsifted powdered sugar
                <br/>
                1 tablespoon milk
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  1
                 </b>
                 Transfer the frozen dough to the refrigerator to thaw overnight.
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  2
                 </b>
                 After the 9x4-inch (one-third batch) piece of dough has thawed overnight in the refrigerator, bring it out of the refrigerator 20 minutes before you want to start rolling it out.
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
               </span>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  3
                 </b>
                 Line a heavy-duty
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Vollrath-1-Piece-Wear-Ever-Half-Size-Aluminum/dp/B0001MS3P6/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=kitchen&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1510021471&amp;sr=1-14&amp;keywords=half+sheet+pan&amp;&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=092add9a55c8abba464ee64a3ee337e0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   half sheet pan
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 with parchment paper.
                </span>
               </div>
               <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  4
                 </b>
                 On lightly floured surface, roll the dough into a neat 15x12-inch rectangle about 1/8 inch thick. With a short end of dough in front of you, lightly mark vertical thirds in the dough (with something like ruler or dull knife). Using a
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/OXO-Grips-4-inch-Cutter-Non-Stick/dp/B000AN0YYY/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=home-garden&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1530152084&amp;sr=1-2&amp;keywords=oxo+good+grips+plastic+pizza+wheel&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=07263fb920da56eb0539d8b9a3cbc636" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   plastic pizza cutter
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 , cut off the top two corners and cut slanted strips in the side sections.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <b>
                  Tip:
                 </b>
                 Don't make yourself crazy measuring the strips and getting them perfect. I just eyeballed it and it came out fine.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-npJxK4YmSX4/WzRClp8HwMI/AAAAAAAAEBk/Q7LLkH0Nb_0moPeXA1f_0vg94CYPmp0awCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img alt="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="253" data-original-width="380" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-npJxK4YmSX4/WzRClp8HwMI/AAAAAAAAEBk/Q7LLkH0Nb_0moPeXA1f_0vg94CYPmp0awCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B1.jpg" title="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  5
                 </b>
                 With
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Ateco-1385-Spatula-4-5-Inch-Stainless/dp/B000WG14FC/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=home-garden&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1530152212&amp;sr=1-3&amp;keywords=small+offset+spatula&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=6b130dab43b120fb770450c95f285691" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   small offset spatula
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 , spread the jam over the center third of the dough. Fold the strips over the center, alternating from side to side. Place on parchment-lined
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Vollrath-1-Piece-Wear-Ever-Half-Size-Aluminum/dp/B0001MS3P6/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=kitchen&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1510021471&amp;sr=1-14&amp;keywords=half+sheet+pan&amp;&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=092add9a55c8abba464ee64a3ee337e0" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   half sheet pan
                  </span>
                  .
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1vDLgms_uoc/WzRC3EzQgcI/AAAAAAAAEBs/FEifarMgvnUoYlZooVcZkoVBR8d3ipSqwCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img alt="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="253" data-original-width="380" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-1vDLgms_uoc/WzRC3EzQgcI/AAAAAAAAEBs/FEifarMgvnUoYlZooVcZkoVBR8d3ipSqwCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B2.jpg" title="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  6
                 </b>
                 Set the baking sheet in the cold oven and place a pan of boiling water (just 3 or 4 cups) on the bottom of the oven. This will keep the braid from drying out. Let rise until puffy, about 2 to 3, even 3 1/2 hours. Be sure to take it out before you preheat the oven!
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-O-KdLmyNIa4/WzRDQHkb7tI/AAAAAAAAEB0/uy4Jm1DWIjQQBxtOqFpkLTLA9ZMS2DemgCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img alt="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="253" data-original-width="380" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-O-KdLmyNIa4/WzRDQHkb7tI/AAAAAAAAEB0/uy4Jm1DWIjQQBxtOqFpkLTLA9ZMS2DemgCLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B3.jpg" title="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 OR If you have a
                 <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Nordic-Ware-Natural-Aluminum-Commercial/dp/B00008UA3Y/ref=as_li_ss_tl?s=home-garden&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1514735411&amp;sr=1-4&amp;keywords=half+sheet+pan+with+lid&amp;&amp;linkCode=ll1&amp;tag=deligrepas-20&amp;linkId=561a56d95a20dcee6ae94c7165a542c8" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                  <span style="color: #3d85c6;">
                   half sheet pan with a lid
                  </span>
                 </a>
                 and 4 little cups of some sort (these are 1.5 inch tall 2.5-ounce stainless steel sauce cups my mother got at a restaurant supply store decades ago), fill cups with boiling water and set them in the corners of the pan and put the lid on. Doesn't matter that the water cools off, it's just there to get some moisture, not heat, around the rising dough.
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-62T23OGOkLs/WzRDbaWsuzI/AAAAAAAAEB4/YQPL33Hi7JwwtEPs98H0kqF4r0gRKxDSACLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B4.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img alt="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com" border="0" data-original-height="253" data-original-width="380" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-62T23OGOkLs/WzRDbaWsuzI/AAAAAAAAEB4/YQPL33Hi7JwwtEPs98H0kqF4r0gRKxDSACLcBGAs/s1600/Raspberry%2BDanish%2BBraid%2B4.jpg" title="Raspberry Danish Braid / www.delightfulrepast.com"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  7
                 </b>
                 Preheat oven to 450F/230C/Gas8. In small bowl, whisk together the egg wash. Brush the braid with egg wash. As soon as you put the pan in the oven, reduce heat to 400F/205C/Gas6. Bake for about 20 minutes, until deep golden brown.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
               </span>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <b>
                  8
                 </b>
                 Allow to cool for 10 minutes before glazing. Whisk together the glaze and drizzle over the warm, not hot, braid. Serve warm or room temperature.
                </span>
               </div>
               <div>
                <span style='font-family: "verdana" , sans-serif;'>
                 <br/>
                </span>
               </div>

          About Me
