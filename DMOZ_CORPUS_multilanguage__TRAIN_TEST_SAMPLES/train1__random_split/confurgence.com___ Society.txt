Nonna Bunny and myself will be taking over as co-chairs of ConFurgence, and we are both looking forward to what we can bring to Australia’s furry fandom in the future.
         </p>
         <p>
          This letter is an opportunity for me to lay out my thoughts about the last convention, and give you all an update on our plans for the future.
         </p>
         <p>
          Firstly, I’d like to apologise for how long it has taken for this letter to be released to you all. I’m sure there are a variety of conspiracy theories in regards to the cause, but the reason is far more mundane. This year’s ConFurgence took a lot out of me, along with the rest of the staff, and I unfortunately went straight from recovering from ConFurgence into full time work, until mid-July. It’s only now, that I’ve had some time to reflect on the last convention.
         </p>
         <p>
          The last ten years has seen ConFurgence grow from a small event to one of the largest furry conventions in Australia, and with Pete stepping down after ten years of service, both Nonna and myself felt it was an appropriate time to pause and reflect on how we will take ConFurgence into the next ten years, and what changes need to be made, if any, to ensure that happens. We are also taking the opportunity to let our incredible staff take a break themselves, after years of wonderful work, as well as train new staff that we have coming through from the community.
         </p>
         <p>
          Therefore, I am officially announcing that ConFurgence in 2019 will be a smaller, single day event, held in Melbourne’s CBD, or nearby. The event will take place on a Saturday in early 2019. We are currently looking at appropriate venues, and hope to have a confirmed venue, as well as a confirmed date soon. We are planning a full day’s schedule of events, with most of the activities that the community expects at ConFurgence, including a fursuit walk through inner Melbourne, as well as a Saturday night dance party to end the convention. We also plan on a small Dealer’s Den as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          Alongside this one day event, we are also looking at running activities on the Friday night before the convention. This will most likely include a dinner somewhere in Melbourne’s CBD, as well as other activities, in a tribute to the MiDFur meets of old. Finally, we are looking at opening the Sunday after the convention up to the furry community, giving local groups in Melbourne the opportunity to plan and run activities that take advantage of furries visiting from across Victoria, as well as interstate and overseas.
         </p>
         <p>
          We are still committed to running a full-size, three day convention in 2020, although we cannot give any more details at this stage. Any in the community that have already made deposits towards registration for ConFurgence will have these deposits honoured, either for the one-day event in 2019, or the full-size event in 2020, or both, depending on circumstances.
         </p>
         <p>
          If there are any questions in regards to the above statements, please feel free to contact me.
         </p>
         <p>
          I look forward to seeing you all in the future, at the next ConFurgence, or elsewhere in the furry community.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Yours sincerely
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Nathan Cooper/StarShadow
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Co-Chair/Vice Chair
          <br/>
          ConFurgence
         </p>
