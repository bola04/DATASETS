        <td width="100%">
         <strong>
          Childrens
         </strong>
         stories and fables for
             the young at heart of all ages, all of them original, complete, and
             absolutely free; and none of them available anywhere else in the
             world except here
         <font color="#000000">
          .
          <br/>
          They do not depend on animated pictures, so you can print them off
             then read them anywhere you want: in bed, or curled up in an armchair,
             or on the bus, or wherever else is comfortable.
          <br/>
          There will be a new story from time to time; all of them are completely
             free, and there is nothing violent or cruel or nasty in any of them:
             that's a promise.
         </font>
         <p>
          <font color="#000000" size="3">
           <strong>
            These
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           short storybooks are about all the many little
                 creatures that have made their homes in
           <strong>
            the
           </strong>
          </font>
          <a href="meadow.htm">
           <font color="#000000">
            <img align="right" alt="The Meadow" border="0" height="145" hspace="0" src="field.jpg" width="222"/>
           </font>
          </a>
          <font color="#000000">
           <strong>
            meadow
           </strong>
           that lies next to the
           <strong>
            Old
                 Farmhouse
           </strong>
           .
           <br/>
           They are about
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Robert the
               Rabbit
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , known to everybody
               as
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Robbit
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , and his best friend
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Jonathan,
           </strong>
          </font>
          the smartest
               and cleverest snail in the whole meadow
          <font color="#000000">
           .
           <br/>
           As well as
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Robbit
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           and
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Jonathan
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , there are
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Millicent,
           </strong>
          </font>
          the harvest
               mouse
          <font color="#000000">
           ,
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Andrew
           </strong>
          </font>
          the ant
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            ,
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           and many, many more - not forgetting the animals'
               special friends,
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Farmer Jack
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , and his wife,
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Katie
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , who live in
          </font>
          the
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </font>
          <strong>
           Old Farmhouse
          </strong>
          <font color="#000000">
           .
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font color="#000000" size="3">
           <strong>
            The
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           <strong>
            Old Farmhouse
           </strong>
           is called that
                 because that is what it is - an old farmhouse.
           <br/>
           In fact, it's a very old farmhouse: so old that it's become all
               wobbly, just like a very old person.
           <br/>
           Its roof is wobbly and its walls are wobbly and, inside, its floors
               are wobbly as well.
           <br/>
           It's only a small farmhouse, but
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Farmer Jack
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , and his wife,
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Katie
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           , are very happy there and wouldn't live anywhere
               else in the whole, wide world.
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font color="#000000" size="3">
           <strong>
            The
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           <strong>
            meadow
           </strong>
           lies close by the
           <strong>
            Old
                   Farmhouse
           </strong>
           , on a hill that leads down to a tiny stream
                   and a pond.
           <br/>
           <strong>
            The meadow
           </strong>
           is small, just like the
           <strong>
            Old
               Farmhouse
           </strong>
           , but, in the summer, when it's full with flowers,
               it's so pretty that, sometimes,
          </font>
          <font color="#000080">
           <strong>
            Farmer Jack
           </strong>
          </font>
          <font color="#000000">
           and all meadow creatures sit there in the afternoon
               sunshine and think how happy they are just to be there.
          </font>
         </p>
