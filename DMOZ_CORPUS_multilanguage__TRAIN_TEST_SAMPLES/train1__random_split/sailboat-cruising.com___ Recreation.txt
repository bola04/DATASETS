        <h1 style="text-align: center">
         The Cruising Sailboat
         <br/>
        </h1>
        <h3 style="text-align: center">
         and the Lifestyle That Goes With It
        </h3>
        <!-- start: shared_blocks.5428#below-h1 -->
        <div class="" style="width: 100%; box-sizing: border-box">
        </div>
        <!-- end: shared_blocks.5428#below-h1 -->
        <p>
         <b>
          There's something rather special about a sailboat...
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          <span style="font-weight: normal;">
           But there's definitely something
           <em>
            really
           </em>
           special about a
           <em>
            cruising
           </em>
           sailboat. Size isn't it - large or small - they all have it. Neither do they have to be stunningly beautiful or hugely expensive.
          </span>
          <br/>
         </b>
        </p>
        <!-- start: shared_blocks.5423#below-paragraph-1 -->
        <!-- end: shared_blocks.5423#below-paragraph-1 -->
        <p>
         It's not about what they are; it's more about what they can deliver - simple pleasure, high adventure or genuine white-knuckle challenge.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Perhaps just a day sail on the lake or a trip along the coast. A voyage across an ocean maybe, or even a circumnavigation of the world. And best of all, by utilising nature's free and sustainable energy resource - the wind.
        </p>
        <p>
         But be warned - it's addictive. You could end up going the whole hog - getting rid of the shoreside assets, buying a suitable boat and
         <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/living-on-a-boat.html">
          living aboard
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Pure freedom.
        </p>
        <p>
         They are beguiling machines, sailing boats. Take this beauty for instance...
        </p>

             <span style="color: rgb(255, 3, 3);">
              <em>
               <b>
                Choosing a Cruising Sailboat
               </b>
              </em>
             </span>
             that's the
             <em>
              <b>
               <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/choosing-a-cruising-sailboat.html">
                Right One for You!
               </a>
              </b>
             </em>
            </p>
           </div>
           <!-- end: shared_blocks.202256644#Sloops, Cutters & Ketches -->
          </div>
          <div class="responsive_col-2 responsive_grid_block-202256641">
           <h3>
            A Sailboat for Offshore Cruising
           </h3>
           <p>
            Owning a sailing boat, particularly one designed for
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/offshore-sailboats.html">
             offshore and ocean sailing
            </a>
            , can change your life.
           </p>
           <p>
            With a few gallons of fuel, adequate food and water aboard she'll take you thousands of miles leaving a carbon footprint in her wake that would delight the most ardent environmentalist.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            Hey, you can
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/handline-fishing.html">
             catch your own fish
            </a>
            , make your own
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/marine-watermakers.html">
             drinking water from seawate
            </a>
            r (or collect nature's free offering with a
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/rain-catchers.html">
             raincatcher)
            </a>
            and charge your batteries with a
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/sailboat-wind-generator.html">
             windcharger
            </a>
            or
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/marine-solar-panel.html">
             solar panels
            </a>
            - all without using a drop of fuel!
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            And you don't need to be on the helm all day - a
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/windvane-self-steering.html">
             windvane self-steering gear
            </a>
            will keep you on the straight and narrow without drawing a single precious amp from your
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/deep-cycle-marine-battery.html">
             12volt battery bank
            </a>
            .
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            With
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/blue-water-sailboats.html">
             a bluewater boat
            </a>
            equipped in this way you could go the whole nine yards, sell your shoreside assets, cast off the shorelines together with the tedium of life ashore and set off on a
            <a href="https://www.sailboat-cruising.com/living-on-a-boat.html">
             cruising life
            </a>
            of freedom and adventure.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <p>
            A surprising number of people do just this, but let's not get ahead of ourselves.
           </p>
           <p>
            Take the accommodation below decks for example. What might look very attractive at a boat show could prove to be considerably less so when in a seaway.
           </p>
           <p>
            Take a look at the two alternative interior layouts below for instance...
           </p>

         </div>

        </div>

          'form should follow function'
         </em>
         and leave all the glitz and glamour to vehicles with wheels on.
        </p>
        <p>
         The interior layout of a cruising boat is all important. Take a look at these two similar - but different - interior layout options...
