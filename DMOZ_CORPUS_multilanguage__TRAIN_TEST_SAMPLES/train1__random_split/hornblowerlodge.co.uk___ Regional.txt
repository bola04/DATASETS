     <br clear="all"/>
     <p>
      Hornblower Lodge Cottage is a cosy, sunny, well equipped cottage for two people.  It is situated next to the Cleveland Way footpath and has stunning views towards Highlights Lighthouse and the cliffs beyond.
     </p>
     <div id="photos2">
      <img alt="Hornblower Lodge Cottage, Hawsker, Whitby, North Yorkshire, YO22 4JY  - Tel (0)1947 606247" height="335" hspace="10" src="images/cottage.jpg" vspace="10" width="450"/>
     </div>
     <p>
      The cottage stands next to the main property, in an acre of land with uninterrupted views out to sea.
     </p>
     <p>
      A private gate leads directly onto the Cleveland Way, where you can walk to Saltwick Bay beach or beyond into Whitby.  Whitby is famous for it's connections with Captain James Cook.  From the cottage it is only 7 minutes drive to the centre of Whitby.
     </p>
     <p>
      The cottage is ideally situated for walkers, having direct access onto the Cleveland Way.  If you want to be close to Whitby but away from the crowds this is the ideal location!
     </p>
     <p>
      The other direction is Robin Hood's Bay, which is a pretty little fishing village full of charm and character.
     </p>
     <h2>
      About Us &amp; Whitby Area
     </h2>
     <p>
      John and Janet Evans, and their daughter Megan moved to Hornblower Lodge in October 2007. The one acre of land was bought by Trinity House in 1858 and originally housed a lighthouse. It was not until  forty years later at the turn of the century that the lighthouse was demolished and replaced by the foghorn station. Although the foghorns are still present, they are no longer functional, which is probably a huge relief for the people of Whitby. The building has had a few nicknames in its time with the most well known being the 'Whitby Bull' or the 'Hawsker Bull.' Much to our amazement there is now a restuarant in America called the 'Whitby Bull.'
     </p>
