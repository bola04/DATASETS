                   <span>
                    25 July 2018
                   </span>
                  </h2>
                  <div class="date-posts">
                   <div class="post-outer">
                    <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                     <meta content="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RM2ve8YOJgM/W1AH20QsciI/AAAAAAAAIsc/mgqltavYOyMlPs2yxrshMaAQlnSvDTh6gCLcBGAs/s400/D%2526D_Shadow_Thief.png" itemprop="image_url"/>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://experimentalplayground.blogspot.com/2018/07/metatoy-system-rogue-class.html">
                       Metatoy System: Rogue Class
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1599660789506809006" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                       <tbody>
                        <tr>
                         <td style="text-align: center;">
                          <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RM2ve8YOJgM/W1AH20QsciI/AAAAAAAAIsc/mgqltavYOyMlPs2yxrshMaAQlnSvDTh6gCLcBGAs/s1600/D%2526D_Shadow_Thief.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                           <img border="0" data-original-height="1000" data-original-width="849" height="400" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RM2ve8YOJgM/W1AH20QsciI/AAAAAAAAIsc/mgqltavYOyMlPs2yxrshMaAQlnSvDTh6gCLcBGAs/s400/D%2526D_Shadow_Thief.png" width="338"/>
                          </a>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                         <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                          Art from Capcom's D&amp;D Shadow Over Mystara
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                       </tbody>
                      </table>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       Rogue or Thief is a specific class that is sneaky. For those who likes to do sneaky stuff, this class is for you.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        ROGUE
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       The sneaky, self-important, roguish character.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Starting class stats
                       </b>
                       : 2 to Agility or Intellect; 1, 1, 0 distributed however you wish.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Starting HP
                       </b>
                       : 1d6+6
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        HP Per Level / Resting
                       </b>
                       : 1d6+1
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Special Abilities
                       </b>
                       :
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Quick Reflexes
                       </b>
                       : Rogue is good at avoiding hits. Rogue can impose numbers of Disadvantage roll on attack against him per combat equal to his Level.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Roguish Stuff
                       </b>
                       : During non-combat scene, he gets Advantage roll for sneaking, hiding, climbing and opening locks.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Focus
                       </b>
                       : Once in combat, Rogue can focus on a target. When attacking the focused target, Rogue received Advantage attack roll.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Lucky
                       </b>
                       : Rogue gets +1 Luck Point and another +1 when he reaches level 5.
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>

                    <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                     <meta content="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-g7gYGQn9EtU/W01mCibKqNI/AAAAAAAAIqY/wd4TEfA3pLEjPUwsyFv9MM9UtV-IlfnPwCLcBGAs/s400/33d308d511d6f61a754335e05d426142.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://experimentalplayground.blogspot.com/2018/07/metatoy-system-warrior-class.html">
                       Metatoy System: Warrior Class
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6020005669271629765" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                       <tbody>
                        <tr>
                         <td style="text-align: center;">
                          <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-g7gYGQn9EtU/W01mCibKqNI/AAAAAAAAIqY/wd4TEfA3pLEjPUwsyFv9MM9UtV-IlfnPwCLcBGAs/s1600/33d308d511d6f61a754335e05d426142.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                           <img border="0" data-original-height="900" data-original-width="600" height="400" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-g7gYGQn9EtU/W01mCibKqNI/AAAAAAAAIqY/wd4TEfA3pLEjPUwsyFv9MM9UtV-IlfnPwCLcBGAs/s400/33d308d511d6f61a754335e05d426142.jpg" width="266"/>
                          </a>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                         <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                          Art by Akira Toriyama (Taken without permission)
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                       </tbody>
                      </table>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Today we are going talk about the meat of the game. Classes. there will be 3 basic classes in the game which can be found in many RPG. GMs are encouraged to modify thess basic classes to make up their character classes as they see fit in their game world.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       Today's class is the Warrior. A warrior can be a soldier, mercenaries, etc. Anything that warrior like can be a Warrior Class. Heck they could be Pure Human in a post apocalypse RPG.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        WARRIOR
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       The soldier, combative and physical character.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Starting class stats
                       </b>
                       : 2 to Might or Agility; 1, 1, 0 distributed however you wish.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Starting HP
                       </b>
                       : 1d6+8
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        HP Per Level/Resting
                       </b>
                       : 1d6+2
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Special Abilities
                       </b>
                       :
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Combat Superiority
                       </b>
                       : Warrior gets extra attack per round equal to difference between his level and the highest number of HD on the opponent side.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Heavy Hitter
                       </b>
                       : Warrior deals +1 melee damage. It increases to +2 when he reaches Level 5.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Favoured Weapon
                       </b>
                       : Choose 1 weapon. That weapon received Advantage attack roll. He can pick another weapon when he reaches Level 5.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Tough Guy
                       </b>
                       : Once per adventure upon reaching 0 HP, Warrior immediately gain 1d6+Level of HP.
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="clear: both;">
                      </div>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://experimentalplayground.blogspot.com/2018/07/metatoy-system-attributes-save-roll.html">
                       Metatoy System: Attributes, Save Roll, Action Roll and Luck Points
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7495734266840319913" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-FB4KNNvT9HA/W0SfYGUOQLI/AAAAAAAAIm8/QvroTxtEYnkrJzuhp0UZhoo8jeuLKDL_gCLcBGAs/s1600/markus%2Bpolo%2Bchar%2Bsheet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" data-original-height="962" data-original-width="800" height="400" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-FB4KNNvT9HA/W0SfYGUOQLI/AAAAAAAAIm8/QvroTxtEYnkrJzuhp0UZhoo8jeuLKDL_gCLcBGAs/s400/markus%2Bpolo%2Bchar%2Bsheet.jpg" width="331"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        ATTRIBUTES
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       Like Lara, Metatoy has 4 attributes. They are Might, Agility, Intellect and Willpower. See below for what they do in the game.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Might
                       </b>
                       : determines toughness, physical strength, resistance to substances.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Agility
                       </b>
                       : determines manual dexterity, hand-eye coordination and reflexes.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Intellect
                       </b>
                       : determines knowledge, awareness and ability to memorize.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Willpower
                       </b>
                       : determines tone of will, presence and ability to influence another.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        SAVE ROLL
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       A Save Roll is used to determine whether magic, poison, or various other types of attacks are effective against a character or monster.
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       When making saving roll, you make roll as normal using
 appropriate attribute against TN (Target Number) designated by the GM. Creature makes save roll
 by rolling 2d6 adding half of its HD (Hit Dice, will be explained later), rounded up. Use the table below for
 guidance.
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Might
                       </b>
                       : Physical harm that cannot be dodged. Poison, disease, radiation, death.
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Agility
                       </b>
                       : Physical harm that can be dodged.
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Intellect
                       </b>
                       : Deception and illusions.
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                       <b>
                        Willpower
                       </b>
                       : Charming effects. Psionic abilities
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                      </div>
                      <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        ACTION ROLL
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       There is one more mechanic used for special abilities (it
 could be mutation, etc). We called this the Action Roll. The special ability is
 scored 1 to 5, the higher the better. When asked to make an Action Roll, you
 roll 1d6. If you rolled
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        equal or less
                       </b>
                       than the special ability,
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        you can still use
 the special ability again next turn
                       </b>
                       . But, if you rolled
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        higher
                       </b>
                       than the special ability, it
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        decreases by 1
                       </b>
                       . When it reaches 0, it
 means the special ability is
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        Exhausted
                       </b>
                       .
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       There will be special instruction on how to replenish the
 value of the special abilities i.e maybe by taking a long rest (mutation), etc.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       P/S: I took this from Solar Blade &amp; Cosmic Spell with an okay from the author, Diogo Noguiera :) (you can see him in the list of SFK magic-users on the right). This is a d6 answer for Black Hack Usage Die. I really like the mechanic I add in Metatoy.
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: left;">
                      </div>
                      <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        LUCK POINT (OPTIONAL)
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                       <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                       You can use Luck Points to affect the outcome of a battle or
 situation and swing the odds to your favour. Every character starts with 3 Luck
 Points. They are not cumulative and must be use during a session. It will
 replenish again at the beginning of a new session. You can use 1 Luck Point to:
                      </div>
                      <div class="MsoNormal">
                      </div>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <span style="text-indent: -18pt;">
                         Make 1 reroll to any roll except damage roll.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <span style="text-indent: -18pt;">
                         Perform an action or movement in any round even when
 it’s not the character’s turn.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <span style="text-indent: -18pt;">
                         Recover HP by making recovery roll.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        <span style="text-indent: -18pt;">
                         Provide an Advantage to a single action.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="MsoListParagraph" style="mso-list: l0 level1 lfo1; text-indent: -18.0pt;">
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </div>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://experimentalplayground.blogspot.com/2018/07/metatoy-system.html">
                       Metatoy System: Core Rules
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1716827966283615394" itemprop="articleBody">
                      Hi there, I've been on and off with Experimental Playground not because I'm ditching it but I've been creating a new RPG mechanic which I called Metatoy System (previously Nara). It's an offshoot from LARA System and adding game mechanic that I like from old and new school RPG.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      So for every week after today (hopefully) I will make one post about Metatoy in this blog. Hopefully you would enjoy and even comment and/or critique on the new rpg system. Without wasting anymore time, read the core mechanic below:
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;">
                       <tbody>
                        <tr>
                         <td style="text-align: center;">
                          <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vbrad45PzTQ/WztY6H_7UfI/AAAAAAAAIjY/NX7RT4WNEVQ53kw58Gnj3nbDDbZmFO5uACLcBGAs/s1600/DnD-Kids-Featured.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                           <img border="0" data-original-height="545" data-original-width="970" height="224" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-vbrad45PzTQ/WztY6H_7UfI/AAAAAAAAIjY/NX7RT4WNEVQ53kw58Gnj3nbDDbZmFO5uACLcBGAs/s400/DnD-Kids-Featured.jpg" width="400"/>
                          </a>
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                         <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                          clipart taken from Wizard of the Coast (without permission)
                         </td>
                        </tr>
                       </tbody>
                      </table>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        THE CORE METATOY MECHANIC
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      Since I love 2d6 very VERY much, I take the basic mechanic of LARA and use that as a base to create Metatoy and just like LARA when a character needs to do something that is risky, they must roll 2d6 and add relevant attribute. Meeting or exceeding designated Target Number (TN) usually imposed by the GM means success. Simple right?
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        DIFFICULTY (TN)
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       Easy
                       <span style="white-space: pre;">
                       </span>
                       5
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       Moderate
                       <span style="white-space: pre;">
                       </span>
                       7
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       Hard
                       <span style="white-space: pre;">
                       </span>
                       9
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       Tough
                       <span style="white-space: pre;">
                       </span>
                       11
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       Extreme
                       <span style="white-space: pre;">
                       </span>
                       13
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        CRITICAL &amp; FUMBLE
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      You get a CRITICAL on a natural roll of 12 on 2d6; and a FUMBLE on a natural roll of 2 on 2d6. A CRITICAL is good and a FUMBLE is very bad. A CRITICAL HIT always deals double damage, obviously.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <b>
                        ADVANTAGE &amp; DISADVANTAGE ROLL
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      Well this is a mechanic that I saw first in Barbarian of Lemuria and many other rpg. It only became famous when D&amp;D introduced it in their game. Which means I will use it eliminating all future modifier in the game.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      When making Advantage roll, you roll 3d6 and drop the lowest. While, when making Disadvantage roll, you roll 3d6 and drop the highest. You don’t roll extra d6 if you have more Advantages, instead one Advantage neutralize one Disadvantage, so it better to have more Advantage than Disadvantage.
                      <div style="clear: both;">
                      </div>
                     </div>

                       Road Warrior
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7805683444067847740" itemprop="articleBody">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-fvn69aMpTlU/WR0ASMZD7_I/AAAAAAAAEus/Odzi8VumVcICf7zGmrWdAyPZ8qjaMCQhwCLcB/s1600/2017%2B-%2B1%2B%25281%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="400" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-fvn69aMpTlU/WR0ASMZD7_I/AAAAAAAAEus/Odzi8VumVcICf7zGmrWdAyPZ8qjaMCQhwCLcB/s400/2017%2B-%2B1%2B%25281%2529.jpg" width="221"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: justify;">
                       <b>
                        Road Warrior
                       </b>
                       <br/>
                       Pet Name given to character who can drive greatly using ground vehicle. They sacrifice their health just to modify their vehicle as good as possible.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <b>
                        HP
                       </b>
                       : 1d6+8
                       <br/>
                       <b>
                        Skill
                       </b>
                       : Pick 2
                       <br/>
                       <b>
                        Reserved skill
                       </b>
                       :
                       <br/>
                       [Ace Driver] Always roll advantage whenever manoeuvring ground vehicle. (If opponent vehicle is faster than pc vehicle, they still roll advantage).
                       <br/>
                       [HFS]* +1(+1 more) to any one ability. Ability score cannot go above the value of 3 for starting character.
                       <br/>
                       [Radiation Resistance] roll advantage whenever dealing with radiation roll.
                       <br/>
                       Tech Adapt: roll advantage whenever interacting or operating computer, ancient tech and robot.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <b>
                        Starting Equipment
                       </b>
                       : 15 VP vehicle, shotgun (3d6, always burst Ammo: Lots TN:5) and Light Armour (+1 DEF)﻿
                       <br/>
                       <br/>

                 <h2 class="title">
                  Introduction of sort
                 </h2>
                 <div class="widget-content">
                  <div style="font-weight: normal;">
                   Welcome to Experimental Playground (or EP) home of the print and play board games created by us. We believe in fun, simple, cheap and yet short game. Simplicity is our motto when making these print and play games. Hence, all of our games are simple, easily printed and usually uses a few tokens, dice, pencil and paper which is easily provided by you.
                  </div>
                  <div style="font-weight: normal;">
                   <br/>
                  </div>
                  <div style="font-weight: normal;">
                   Anyway, please enjoy our games and do feel free to give feedback, we love them :)
