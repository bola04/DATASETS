            <div allowdelete="false" class="ms-WPBody ms-WPBorder noindex ms-wpContentDivSpace " haspers="false" id="WebPartWPQ4" style="" webpartid="b7896aa0-8a20-4ac1-8fa2-547dd1126ff7" width="100%">
             <div class="ms-rtestate-field">
              <p>
               <strong>
                Find Your Reason to Quit!
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img _moz_resizing="true" alt="Stop_Smoking.jpg" src="/SiteAssets/Pages/default/Stop_Smoking.jpg" style="margin: 5px; width: 250px; height: 167px;"/>
               <br/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Whether its wanting to improve their health, save some money or helping your family, everyone has their own reasons for finally putting an end to their smoking habit. While getting ready to quit, think about why you are going through this process. Remind yourself of them every day and use them as inspiration.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               A few reasons to quit that you might want to consider 
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                Health &amp; Appearance
               </strong>
               <br/>
               - My chances of having cancer, heart attacks, heart disease, stroke, cataracts, and other diseases will go down
               <br/>
               - I will be less likely to get sick
               <br/>
               - I will breathe easier and cough less
               <br/>
               - My blood pressure will go down
               <br/>
               - My skin will look healthier, and I will look more youthful
               <br/>
               - My teeth and fingernails will not be stained
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                Lifestyle
               </strong>
               <br/>
               - I will have more money to spend
               <br/>
               - I can spend more time with family, catch up on work, or dive into my favorite hobby
               <br/>
               - I wont have to worry about when I can smoke next or where I can or cant smoke
               <br/>
               - My food will taste better
               <br/>
               - My clothes will smell better
               <br/>
               - My car home and kids wont smell like smoke
               <br/>
               - I will be able to smell food, flowers, and other things better
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                Your Loved Ones
               </strong>
               <br/>
               - I will set a great example for my kids; it takes a lot of strength to quit
               <br/>
               - My friends, family, co-workers, and other loved ones will be proud of me
               <br/>
               - I will protect my friends and family from the dangers of secondhand smoke
               <br/>
               - My children will be healthier
               <br/>
               - I will have more energy to do the things I love with friends and family
               <br/>
               - I will get healthy to make sure I am around to share in my familys special moments
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <br/>
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Health Milestones
               </strong>
               <br/>
               - Within 20 minutes, your heart rate and blood pressure drop
               <br/>
               - Within 12 hours, the carbon monoxide level in your blood stream drops to normal
               <br/>
               - Within 3 months, your circulation and lung function improves
               <br/>
               - After 9 months, you will cough less and breathe easier
               <br/>
               - After 1 year, your risk of coronary heart disease is cut in half
               <br/>
               - After 5 years, your risk of cancer of the mouth, throat, esophagus, and bladder are cut in half
               <br/>
               - After 10 years, you are one-half as likely to die from lung cancer, and your risk of larynx or pancreatic cancer decreases
               <br/>
               - After 15 years, your risk of coronary heart disease is the same as a non-smokers risk
              </p>
