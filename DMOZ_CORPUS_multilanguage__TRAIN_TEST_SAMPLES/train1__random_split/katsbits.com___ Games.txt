         </a>
         <em>
          in its

        <h3 class="smf_subject">
         <a href="https://www.katsbits.com/smforum/index.php?PHPSESSID=5116f8337e33e2ac2cce768941b1730e&amp;topic=992.0">
          General Data Protection Regulation and your consent to use a site or service
         </a>
        </h3>
        <div class="smf_date">
         May 21, 2018, 01:31:50 PM by
         <a href="https://www.katsbits.com/smforum/index.php?PHPSESSID=5116f8337e33e2ac2cce768941b1730e&amp;action=profile;u=1">
          kat
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="smf_body">
         In the coming weeks many online sites and services based in the Europe (and optionally elsewhere) will be emailing various
         <strong>
          notification of consent
         </strong>
         messages, or provide updated cookie consent pop-ups online, so as to be in compliance with the new EU
         <a class="bbc_link" href="https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/guide-to-the-general-data-protection-regulation-gdpr/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           General Data Protection Regulation
          </strong>
         </a>
         (come into effect 25 May), required to be issued for visitors (continued) use of a site or service.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         It's not exactly clear how this affects forums and other community or participatory services like KatsBits because
         <a class="bbc_link" href="http://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32016R0679" target="_blank">
          the regulations carry the implication
         </a>
         that if consent is not given,
         <em>
          <strong>
           any
          </strong>
         </em>
         content, user accounts or other material must be removed with immediate effect (
         <em>
          because consent for its continued use has not been/is no longer given
         </em>
         ). Needless to say the consequences of having to do this where more than one person is involved in a conversation potentially means/implies
         <strong>
          all
          <em>
           associated
          </em>
          content
         </strong>
         need be removed (and likely permanently deleted), which then obviously breaks context and continuity, of particular importance for technical support, fixing issues and getting appropriate degrees of community assistance.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         For updates on GDPR bookmark or subscribe to this post.
        </div>
