           The Necessity of Genius
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="sep">
           Posted on
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.futfanatico.com/2017/04/16/necessity-genius/" rel="bookmark" title="7:20 pm">

           <span class="sep">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.futfanatico.com/?p=23261">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-12150" height="203" src="http://www.futfanatico.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/200px-Manchester_United_FC_crest.svg_1.png" title="200px-Manchester_United_FC_crest.svg" width="200"/>
          </a>
          I wrote about
          <a href="http://www.futfanatico.com/2017/03/06/counting-conte/">
           Antonio Conte
          </a>
          a few months back. Basically, his courage in benching underperforming players and introducing a new tactical system impressed me. Then,
          <em>
           The Guardian
          </em>
          ran a profile of him with an interview from an assistant. The details divulged kinda ruined my enchantment, to be honest. And some things made more sense.
         </p>
         <p>
          First, he seems like a task master. LVG style, he stops training session games to talk with players and tell them where to stand. Secondly, his training sessions go on and on and on. That’s pretty Mou style. While I think Conte should lead Chelsea to a title this year, is he just benefiting from no European soccer (ala Leicester last year) and will he burn out his Starting XI ala Mourinho’s last time at Chelsea?
