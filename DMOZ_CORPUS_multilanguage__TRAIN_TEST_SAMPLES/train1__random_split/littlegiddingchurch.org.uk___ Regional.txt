                  <p>
                   Welcome
                  </p>
                  <p class="body1">
                   <span class="bodybold1">
                    Little
                                   Gidding Church is difficult to find, but unforgettable
                                   when found
                   </span>
                   . It is
                                 a secluded place in remote Huntingdonshire countryside
                                 which has borne witness to turbulent times over
                                 a thousand
                                 years with serenity, simplicity and peace.
                   <a href="lgchtmlfiles/lgpeople1.html" target="_self">
                    Nicholas
                                 Ferrar
                   </a>
                   and his family formed the first Anglican
                                 community here in 1625 after the religious changes
                                 of the English Reformation. King Charles I visited
                               in 1642, and sought refuge in 1646.
                  </p>
                  <p class="body1">
                   The poet
                   <a href="lgchtmlfiles/lgpeople2.html">
                    T.S.
                                   Eliot
                   </a>
                   visited in 1936, and was moved to
                                 reflect upon the Church and its history in the
                                 last of
                                 his Four Quartets: Little Gidding.
                  </p>
                  <p class="body1">
                   The Church is in the
                                   care of the Giddings PCC, and we invite you
                                 to explore this website, and to join us at one
                                     of the regular
                   <a href="lgchtmlfiles/lgservices1.html" target="_self">
                    services
                   </a>
                   when you can.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <strong>
                    The church is open every day
                    <br/>
                    between 10.00am and 4.30pm
                   </strong>
                  </p>
