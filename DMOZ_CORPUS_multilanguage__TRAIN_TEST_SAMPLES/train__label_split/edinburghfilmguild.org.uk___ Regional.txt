            <p>
             Here is an overview of our programme for the 2018-19 season:
            </p>
            <p>
             OCTOBER — NOVEMBER 2018
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Film Noir | USA
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Paul Fejos
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              May ’68 | 50th Anniversary
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Lino Brocka | Philippines
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Mexican Horror | 1950s-60s
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Special Screening: Hallowe’en
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              ‘Kimchi &amp; Noodle’ Westerns | Asia
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="textbox" dir="ltr">
             NOVEMBER — DECEMBER 2018
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Keisuke Kinoshita | Japanese War Time Films
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Soviet Cinema | ‘Thaw’ Period
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Ernst Lubitsch | Musicals
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              George Stevens | Comedy
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Keith’s 5-Star Favourites
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Anime | Japan
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Special Screening: Christmas/AGM
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="textbox" dir="ltr">
             JANUARY — FEBRUARY 2019
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Whodunnit? | Crime
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Documentaries | French Rural Life
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Pharaohs on Film
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Vintage British Thrillers
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Monster Movies
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="textbox" dir="ltr">
             FEBRUARY — MARCH 2019
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Complicated Women | Pre-code Hollywood
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Slovakian Cinema
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Fabián Bielinsky | Argentina
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Satyajit Ray | The Apu Trilogy
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Surreal | Fantasy
             </li>
             <li class="textbox" dir="ltr">
              Alt. Sci-Fi
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             The detailed programme will be available online shortly as will the brochure.
            </p>
            <p>
             Between October 2018 and March 2019 the Guild will screen over 60 films, three a week:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Sunday afternoon, usually starting at 4.30pm
             </li>
             <li>
              Sunday evening, starting at 7pm
             </li>
             <li>
              Friday evening, starting at 8pm
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             All the films we show are grouped into two, three or five film seasons. The films in a season are linked by genre (e.g. British Silent Films), director (e.g. Edward Yang), star (e.g. Michèle Morgan), or theme (e.g. Classics of Soviet Cinema).
            </p>
            <p>
             You can
             <a href="http://edinburghfilmguild.org.uk/wordpress/?page_id=769" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
              join online
             </a>
             , in person at any screening, or by completing and returning the
             <a href="http://edinburghfilmguild.org.uk/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/Membership-Form.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
              print form
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Membership starts at just £20.
            </p>
            <p>
             You can still look at
             <a href="http://edinburghfilmguild.org.uk/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/EFG_BROCHURE_2017_LOW_RES2.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
              the previous programme brochure here
             </a>
             , or browse through the individual mini-seasons
             <a href="http://edinburghfilmguild.org.uk/wordpress/?page_id=3369">
              here
             </a>
             (scroll down through the post and click on the images for more details).
            </p>

             Notice is hereby given that the 63rd Annual General Meeting of the Guild will be held in the Guild Cinema FilmHouse, 88 Lothian Road, Edinburgh EH3 9BZ on Sunday, 17th December 2017 at about 6.20pm immediately after the screening of our Christmas film,
             <em>
              Three Godfathers
             </em>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Nominations of candidates for council members and office bearers need to be submitted in writing to the EFG, signed by a proposer, a seconder and by the member to be proposed, confirming his/her willingness to serve on the council if elected. They should be submitted by the start of the AGM.
            </p>
            <p>
             Our meeting will be followed by our Christmas party with drinks and buffet.
