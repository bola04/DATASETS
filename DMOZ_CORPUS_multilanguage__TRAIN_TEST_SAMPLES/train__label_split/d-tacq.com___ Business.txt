      <h1>
       Intelligent Data Acquisition Boards and Systems
      </h1>
      <p>
       If you have a data acquisition requirement that calls for one or more of the following characteristics:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Top quality analog signal conversion.
       </li>
       <li>
        True simultaneous sampling with
        <span class="gloss" content="ADC device converts input voltage to digital word." title="Analog to Digital Convertor">
         ADC
        </span>
        per channel architecture.
       </li>
       <li>
        Simultaneous capture over large numbers of channels.
       </li>
       <li>
        High Throughput and scaleability.
       </li>
       <li>
        True Open System architecture.
       </li>
       <li>
        May need to operate autonomously.
       </li>
       <li>
        May need user specific field customisation.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Then please read on, because
       <a class="dtacq" title="D-TACQ High Performance Simultaneous Data Acquisition">
        D‑TACQ
       </a>
       's products and services may be ideal for you.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="dtacq" title="D-TACQ High Performance Simultaneous Data Acquisition">
        D‑TACQ
       </a>
       has a unique value proposition - fit a top quality analog signal conditioning array to state of the art
       <span class="gloss" content="ADC device converts input voltage to digital word." title="Analog to Digital Convertor">
        ADC
       </span>
       devices, and match up to a high performance, intelligent
       <span class="gloss" content="A large programmable logic device. The FPGA program image on D-TACQ products is loaded from flash memory at boot time, allowing for easy in-system upgrade." title="Field Programmable Gate Array">
        FPGA
       </span>
       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FPGA">
        <img alt="world" class="glossimg" src="./images/icon.world.gif"/>
       </a>
       based digital subsystem. Use a powerful but low power microprocessor for local data management. The microprocessor runs Linux for maximum flexibility and connectivity.
      </p>
      <p>
       So what is the benefit of using an intelligent data acquisition card? In a word, "Flexibility". Products act as networked data acquisition appliances. They talk TCP/IP, and, in most cases, no device driver is required. High level macros limit the level of user customization to a minimum.
      </p>
