                     AT 40:

                 2017
                       marked the 40th anniversary of coronary angioplasty
                 <i>
                  .
                 </i>

                       1977 in Zurich, Switzerland, a young physician named
                 <i>
                  <a href="http://www.ptca.org/archive/bios/gruentzig.html">

                       Gruentzig
                   </u>
                  </a>
                 </i>
                 inserted a catheter into a patient's
                       coronary artery and inflated a tiny balloon, successfully
                       opening a blockage and restoring blood flow to a human
                       heart.
