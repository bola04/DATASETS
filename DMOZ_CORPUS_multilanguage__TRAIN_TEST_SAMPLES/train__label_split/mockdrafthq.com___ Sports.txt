          <h1 style="font-size:200%">
           <center>
            2019 NFL Mock Draft
           </center>
          </h1>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            1. Cincinnati Bengals - Ryan Finley, QB, NC State
           </b>
           <br/>
           It actually could be the Browns or Cardinals that lock down the first selection of the 2019 NFL Draft with their young passers, but I expect this selection to be traded to a QB needy team.  The NFL has learned that the new secret to winning is drafting a first round QB and building a dominant defense before his 5 year rookie contract is over. That's exactly why I have the Bengals angling for Finley in this scenario.  Sorry, Mr. Dalton.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            2. Indianapolis Colts - Nick Bosa, DE, Ohio State
           </b>
           <br/>
           Some people may mock a QB here but not so fast.  From what I've read Andrew Luck has been throwing medicine balls so he should be good to go in 2018-2019.  Bosa would be a formidable pass rusher that Indy simply can't pass up.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            3. Buffalo Bills - Ed Oliver, DT, Houston
           </b>
           <br/>
           Another team with the QB part of the equation solved, now just for the defense.  Oliver has the talent of a #1 overall pick and could shoot up the board come draft day.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            4. Kansas City Chiefs - Justin Herbert, QB, Oregon
           </b>
           <br/>
           Chief fans are just going to have to be mad.  It's not that Mahomes isn't talented, but  this is a new era.  Young QBs have to win and win fast.  Mahomes is not the QB that won all of those games last year.  That guy plays in Washington now.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            5. New York Jets - Trey Adams, OT, Washington
           </b>
           <br/>
           If Sam Darnold is really the guy then you have to proect him.  This is a young QB that has turned the ball over at an alarming rate from inside the pocket.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            6. Washington Redskins - Jarrett Stidham, QB, Auburn
           </b>
           <br/>
           Yes, Alex Smith is here. But he's 34 and on a 4 year contract.  First round rookie contracts are 4 years with a 5th year option.  See what I see? Of course you do.  Smith is a good hand until whoever they draft is ready.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            7. Tennessee Titans - Dexter Lawrence, DT, Clemson
           </b>
           <br/>
           Why another DT? Because why not. Jurrell Casey is a beast but he can't do it all on his own.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            8. Seattle Seahawks - Andraez Williams, CB, LSU
           </b>
           <br/>
           This pick is all about bringing back the LOB.  I'm not sure if such a legendary time can be re-created but my theory is that when a franchise tastes success they will attempt to re-create it at some point.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            9. Arizona Cardinals - Jonah Williams, OT, Alabama
           </b>
           <br/>
           If the Cardinals are going to have success with Josh Rosen it will start up front.  Jared Veldheer has moved on to the Broncos, so his presence will be missed.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            10. Detroit Lions - Clelin Ferrell, DE, Clemson
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Lions are going to have to make a decision on Ziggy Ansah in 2018.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <center>
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">

          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            11. New York Giants - A.J. Brown, WR, Ole Miss
           </b>
           <br/>
           Is Odell Beckham Jr. getting traded?  We know at least one thing for now, he will begin the season as a New York Giant.  But will he end the season one?  Will some team break the bank and offer the Giants something they cannot refuse?
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            12. Baltimore Ravens - Josh Allen, LB, Kentucky
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Ravens got the QB of the future, now get the linebacker of the future.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            13. Miami Dolphins - Christian Wilkins, DE, Clemson
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Dolphins tried bringing in big money guys to fix their woes but it didn't work out.  Suh is gone.  Mario Williams is gone.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            14. Chicago Bears - David Edward, OT, Wisconsin
           </b>
           <br/>
           Another team with a young QB.  This is a vanilla pick but the Bears have a nice running game and a developing QB so it makes sense.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            15. Tampa Bay Buccaneers - Deandre Baker, CB, Georgia
           </b>
           <br/>
           Honestly I want to mock a QB here as I don't believe Jameis Winston's INTs will fly under the radar of national scrutiny much longer.  However Brent Grimes will have to get old eventually... right?!
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            16. Cleveland Browns - Rashan Gary, DE/DT, Michigan
           </b>
           <br/>
           Baker leads the Browns to a solid 8-8 record in this mock draft.  I know, that's hilarious depending on what side of the Big 12 you stand on but check this offensive roster again.  They have the talent.  Now just add more pieces on defense.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            17. Atlanta Falcons - Beau Benzschawel, OG, Wisconsin
           </b>
           <br/>
           This would have been a QB if Matt Ryan didn't just break the bank with a nine figure contract.  Matt Ryan has a nine figure contract.  Let that sink in for a minute.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            18. Dallas Cowboys - N’Keal Harry, WR, Arizona State
           </b>
           <br/>
           Tavon Austin and Allen Hurns are good pick ups.  I still believe Dallas will look to brining in additional pass catchers.  You can't lose two of your three top pass catchers in one year and just move on.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            19. Carolina Panthers - Anfernee Jennings, LB, Alabama
           </b>
           <br/>
           Thomas Davis had a great career but he's at the end of the road and serving a PED suspension.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            20. Los Angeles Chargers - Parris Campbell, WR, Ohio State
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Chargers were interested in adding OBJ but decided not to sweeten the deal enough for the Giants to do it.  That does not mean that they are done bringing in WRs.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            21. Denver Broncos - Bryce Love, RB, Stanford
           </b>
           <br/>
           Who is the long term answer here? Booker? Freeman? We'll see.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            22. San Francisco 49ers - Collin Johnson, WR, Texas
           </b>
           <br/>
           I honestly can't tell you who starts at WR for the Niners these days, but I do know Jimmy G is about to get a huge contract so you have to help him.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
          </p>
          <center>
           <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
           </script>

          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            23. Houston Texans - Raekwon Davis, DE/DT, Alabama
           </b>
           <br/>
           JJ Watt has flirted with retirement more than once.  I doubt he retires after the 2018 season but crazier things have happened.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            24. Green Bay Packers (via New Orleans Saints) - Dre’Mont Jones, DT, Ohio State
           </b>
           <br/>
           This should be a receiver or possibly a RB but there isn't anyone that outweighs the value of Jones here.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            25. Minnesota Vikings - Austin Bryant, DE, Clemson
           </b>
           <br/>
           One of the best defenses in the game but they cannot afford to pay everyone.  Danielle Hunter had 12.5 sacks in 2016, even with a down 2017 someone will pay him next summer.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            26. Oakland Raiders - Greg Little, OT, Ole Miss
           </b>
           <br/>
           Do I have to say it? Protect your franchise QB.  This honestly should be a RB with Lynch and Martin in contract years but I could easily see Gruden overthinking it and drafting Giacomini's replacement instead.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            27. Philadelphia Eagles - Rodney Anderson, RB, Oklahoma
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Eagles beat the Patriots with contributions from a number of RBs and the tradition will continue. Ajyai is in a contract year, Sproles is old, and Matt Jones may not survive the preseason if he hasn't fixed the fumbling issue.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            28. New England Patriots - Drew Lock, QB, Missouri
           </b>
           <br/>
           Tom vs. Time be damned, Bill will find a future franchise QB and get him in place sooner rather than later.  Danny Etling is not the guy nor is Brian Hoyer.  All good things have to come to an end.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            29. Los Angeles Rams - Joe Jackson, DE, Miami (FL)
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Rams can afford to spend some draft capital on defense.  If Arizona's Josh Rosen is a franchise guy, then that's Jimmy G, Russell Wilson, and him six times a year. When you really think about it, so much of this mock 2019 NFL Draft relies on whether Rosen plays well or not next year.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            30. Pittsburgh Steelers - Shaquille Quarterman, LB, Miami (FL)
           </b>
           <br/>
           We all wish Ryan Shazier well but he is a long way away from playing football again.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            31. Jacksonville Jaguars - Julian Love, CB, Notre Dame
           </b>
           <br/>
           The current best defense in football adds another corner to go with Ramsey, Hayden, and Bouye.  Ramsey will be looking for a massive contract in the Summer of 2019 and you never know what could happen.
          </p>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <b>
            32. Green Bay Packers - Chauncey Gardner-Johnson, S, Florida
           </b>
           <br/>
           The Pack don't win the Super Bowl in this projection.  It's more likely the Patriots do a bunch of moving around like the Bills did last year to make sure they secure a QB in the first before Jacksonville selects.  On topic, HaHa is a free agent next year and if he leaves Green Bay will have to do something about it.
          </p>
