      <div class="maintext" style="width:635px;">
       <span style="font-family: Arial">
        The Pittsburgh Diffraction 
 				Society (PDS) is 
 				a not-for-profit organization which promotes fundamental and 
 				applied diffraction and crystallographic research and the 
 				exchange of ideas and information concerning such research. 
 				The PDS was founded by Professor Surain S. Sidhu who organized 
 				the first Pittsburgh Diffraction Conference in 1943. 
 				Since that time the PDS has sponsored an annual conference which 
 				is held annually, returning to Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania every five years. 
 				Professor Sidhu is remembered and honored through
        <a href="http://www.pittdifsoc.org/sidhu_award.htm">
         the Sidhu Award
        </a>
        , given to an outstanding scientist who is within six years 
 				of having earned the Ph.D. or its equivalent. Other
        <a href="awards.htm">
         awards sponsored by the PDS to support and encourage young 
 				scientists
        </a>
        include
        <a href="http://www.pittdifsoc.org/george_jeffrey_award.htm">
         the George Jeffrey 
 				Award
        </a>
        , which provides 
 				travel assistance for students attending the triennial Congress 
 				of the International Union of Crystallography, and
        <a href="http://www.pittdifsoc.org/chung_soo_yoo_award.htm">
         the Chung Soo 
 				Yoo Award
        </a>
        given to the best student presenter at the annual 
 				Pittsburgh Diffraction Conference.
       </span>
