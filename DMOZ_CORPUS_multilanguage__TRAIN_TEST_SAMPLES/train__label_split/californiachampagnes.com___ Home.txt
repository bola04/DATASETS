                <div class="et_pb_slide_description">
                 <h2 class="et_pb_slide_title">
                  California Sparkling Wine &amp; Champagne
                 </h2>
                 <div class="et_pb_slide_content">
                  <p>
                   We are constantly adding to our site in an effort to bring you the most information possible about the California sparkling wine industry.
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <!-- .et_pb_slide_description -->
               </div>

          <div class="et_pb_section et_pb_section_1 et_pb_fullwidth_section et_section_regular">
           <div class="et_pb_module et_pb_fullwidth_slider_1 et_pb_slider et_pb_slider_no_arrows et_pb_slider_no_pagination et_pb_slider_parallax">
            <div class="et_pb_slides">
             <div class="et_pb_slide et_pb_slide_1 et_pb_section_parallax et_pb_bg_layout_dark et_pb_media_alignment_center et-pb-active-slide">
              <span class="et_parallax_bg" style="background-image: url(http://www.californiachampagnes.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/cover.jpg);">
              </span>
              <div class="et_pb_container clearfix">
               <div class="et_pb_slider_container_inner">
                <div class="et_pb_slide_description">
                 <div class="et_pb_slide_content">
                  <p style="text-align: left;">
                   Most California sparkling wines are made in the
                   <span style="color: #ccffcc;">
                    <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/methode-champenoise/" style="color: #ccffcc;" title="What is Méthode Champenoise?">
                     <em>
                      <strong>
                       Méthode Champenoise
                      </strong>
                     </em>
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   style with many of the wineries calling their product “sparkling wine” and some even use the
                   <span style="color: #ccffcc;">
                    <strong>
                     <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/champagne/" style="color: #ccffcc;" title="What is the Champagne region of France?">
                      Champagne
                     </a>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   designation.
                  </p>
                  <p style="text-align: left;">
                   You will find that many sparkling wine producers are found in the cooler California regions and the majority of these use the same grapes, primarily
                   <span style="color: #ccffcc;">
                    <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/pinot-noir/" style="color: #ccffcc;" title="What is Pinot Noir?">
                     <strong>
                      pinot noir
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   and
                   <span style="color: #ccffcc;">
                    <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/chardonnay/" style="color: #ccffcc;" title="What is Chardonnay?">
                     <strong>
                      chardonnay
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   with some
                   <span style="color: #ccffcc;">
                    <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/pinot-meunier/" style="color: #ccffcc;" title="What is Pinot Meunier?">
                     <strong>
                      pinot meunier
                     </strong>
                    </a>
                   </span>
                   as do their cousins in France.
                  </p>
                 </div>
                </div>

               </div>

           <div class="et_parallax_bg" style="background-image: url(http://www.californiachampagnes.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/southern-slide.png);">
           </div>
           <div class="et_pb_row et_pb_row_0 et_pb_equal_columns et_pb_gutters1 et_pb_row_fullwidth">
            <div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_2_3 et_pb_column_0 et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough">
             <div class="et_pb_module et_pb_text et_pb_text_0 et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_text_align_left">
              <div class="et_pb_text_inner">
               <p>
                The most popular designation for both sparkling wine and Champagne is
                <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/brut/" title="What does brut mean?">
                 <strong>
                  brut
                 </strong>
                </a>
                , a dry style that is usually a blend;
                <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/blanc-de-blanc/" title="What is Blanc de Blanc?">
                 <strong>
                  blanc de blancs
                 </strong>
                </a>
                indicates a wine made solely from chardonnay while a
                <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/blanc-de-noirs/" title="What is Blanc de Noirs?">
                 <strong>
                  blanc de noirs
                 </strong>
                </a>
                is made with either Pinot variety (or a blend thereof). There are also
                <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/what-is-rose/" title="What is Rosé?">
                 <strong>
                  rosés
                 </strong>
                </a>
                . We have also compiled a more
                <a href="http://www.californiachampagnes.com/grapes-used-in-sparkling-wine/" title="What Types Of Grapes Are Used To Make Champagne?">
                 <strong>
                  complete list of the grapes used in the production of sparkling wine
                 </strong>
                </a>
                from around the world.
               </p>
               <p>
                Unlike the still wine market, sales of sparkling wines are largely seasonal and are usually linked to celebratory occasions. This web site aims to change this by providing a large knowledge base on accessories, wineries, and information on how to purchase, store and drink fine California sparkling wine.
               </p>
              </div>
