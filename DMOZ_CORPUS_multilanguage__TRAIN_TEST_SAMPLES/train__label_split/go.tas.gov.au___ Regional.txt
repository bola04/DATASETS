           <p>
            The eGovernment website is managed by the Office of eGovernment
           </p>
           <p>
            The establishment of the Office of eGovernment reflects a new direction in progressing strategic issues in the use of information and communications technology (ICT) across the Tasmanian Government. The Office replaces the Inter Agency Policy and Projects Unit (IAPPU),
            <a href="http://www.dpac.tas.gov.au/">
             Department of Premier and Cabinet
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            The Office of eGovernment is responsible for:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             leading the development of an
             <a href="http://www.egovernment.tas.gov.au/ict/strategy">
              ICT Strategy
             </a>
             for Tasmanian Government
            </li>
            <li>
             the development of policies, standards and guidelines
            </li>
            <li>
             supporting key ICT projects across the Tasmanian Government
            </li>
            <li>
             supporting the governance of ICT (including the
             <a href="http://www.egovernment.tas.gov.au/about/modernising_ict_governance">
              ICT Policy Board
             </a>
             , created by government to advise the Premier on strategic directions for ICT within government)
            </li>
            <li>
             <span>
              building government statistical assets and capability through
              <a href="http://www.egovernment.tas.gov.au/stats_matter">
               Stats Matter
              </a>
             </span>
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.egovernment.tas.gov.au/about">
             Find out more about the Office of eGovernment
            </a>
            .
           </p>

             <h2>
              <a href="http://www.egovernment.tas.gov.au/ict">
               Information and communications technology (ICT)
              </a>
             </h2>
             <p>
              One of the major actions to support the ICT Strategy is to implement a common approach to data centre/storage. The next steps for Tasmanian Government data centres and managed services is to stabilise existing facilities and services. Short term activities are to meet immediate data centre and manage services needs and facilitate future transition. The development of a business case will determine medium term data centre and managed services requirements.

The Department of Treasury and Finance and the Department of Premier and Cabinet are working in partnership with the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) to develop Stats Matter.
