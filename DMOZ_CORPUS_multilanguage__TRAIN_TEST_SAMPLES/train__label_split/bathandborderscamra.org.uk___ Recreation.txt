         <h1 class="entry-title">
          The Campaign for Real Ale: Bath and Borders Branch
         </h1>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <h2>
          <img alt="" class="alignleft" height="80" src="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/style5.jpg" width="120"/>
         </h2>
         <h2>
          Welcome to the website of the Bath &amp; Borders branch of CAMRA, the Campaign for Real Ale.
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.camra.org.uk/about" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.camra.org.uk']);">
            CAMRA
           </a>
          </strong>
          is a national, independent, voluntary organisation campaigning for real ale, community pubs and consumer rights. Bath and Borders branch is just one of many throughout the UK with a total membership of over 185,000. Between us we put on dozens of beer festivals every year, campaign for all things beery, including fighting the closure of breweries and pubs, and, after selflessly visiting many hundreds of pubs and drinking thousands of pints (‘surveying’ is what we call it), we all contribute towards the annual
          <a href="http://www.camra.org.uk/gbg" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.camra.org.uk']);">
           Good Beer Guide
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Our branch covers a wide area, which encompasses not only Bath but also towns and villages in Wiltshire and Somerset. In Wiltshire these include Bradford-on-Avon, Trowbridge, Westbury and Warminster, and, in Somerset, Frome, Shepton Mallet, Wells, Midsomer Norton and Radstock. The city of Bath, one of the finest cities in the country for real ale, is the jewel in our crown and the
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/34th-camra-bath-beer-festival/" title="BATH BEER FESTIVAL 2012">
           Bath Beer Festival
          </a>
          , now held in November, is one of the longest running CAMRA events in the region. The whole branch area is a rich haven of real ale and splendid pubs, and it contains no less than
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/local_breweries.shtml">
           sixteen excellent breweries / brewpubs
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          For a link to a crawl of some Bath pubs click here:
          <a href="http://pubtrails.org.uk/#/bath-1/4593964555" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://pubtrails.org.uk']);">
           Pub  &amp; Beer Trails – Bath
          </a>
          . Note that this is not an official CAMRA site, or created by a local member.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          The 2018 CAMRA Bath Beer Festival, our third Winter Ales Fest, will be held on Friday the 9th and Saturday the 10th of November, this year, at the spruce new Widcombe Social Club. Full details can be found on our
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/34th-camra-bath-beer-festival/">
           Beer Festival page
          </a>
          .
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          On this site you will find lists and maps of
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/local-pubs/" title="Local Pubs">
           pubs in the branch’s area
          </a>
          , well over 300 in all, separated into areas based on cities, towns and villages. Each area includes a map and, where applicable, a link to the individual pub’s own website. Please note that, while we try to make these lists as accurate and comprehensive as possible, some mistakes and/or omissions may well occur as pubs close, open, and change in some way, so
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/contact-us/" title="Contact Us">
           please let us know
          </a>
          of any changes or updates.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can also follow us on
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Bath-Borders-CAMRA/697077707005806?fref=ts" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.facebook.com']);">
           Facebook
          </a>
          and
          <a href="https://twitter.com/BathCAMRA" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://twitter.com']);">
           Twitter
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="alignright" height="145" src="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/aboutale.jpg" width="150"/>
          Alongside the Bath Beer Festival the branch runs
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/events.shtml">
           events
          </a>
          on a more-or-less weekly basis. These include socials and meetings, brewery visits, minibus trips to outlying pubs, joint events with neighbouring branches, and even an annual weekend away. If you’re a CAMRA member in the area, or even just somebody interested in beer, please feel free to come to any of our events. Click on the
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/events/" title="Events">
           Branch Diary
          </a>
          link for full details. You can also email us via our
          <a href="http://www.bathandborderscamra.org.uk/contact-us/" title="Contact Us">
           contacts page
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
