     <h2 style="font-size: 1.375em;">
      Latest Wildlife Update — June 16, 2009
     </h2>
     <p>
      Since the arrival of the cold weather our office has received a steady stream of calls for mice, gray squirrels and also flying squirrels. We have been seeing a large number of juvenile gray squirrels this fall which is a bit unusual. Most gray squirrels have their young in the spring and a small percentage of females give birth to a second litter in the fall. In an average fall season we usually see 2 or 3 litters of juveniles.
     </p>

     <h2>
      Hopkinton Mice Removal
     </h2>
     <p>
      About a month ago I mouse proofed a 4000 square foot contemporary colonial home in Hopkinton Ma. The only spot in the entire house that the mice were using was where the pipe for the AC unit entered the siding of the house. The mice were climbing up the pipe on the foam covering and entering the hole where it went through the siding. This one hole allow dozens of mice to infest the basement of the house.
     </p>
