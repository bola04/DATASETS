        <h2 class="title">
         <a href="http://www.efede77.com/home-inspection-new-house/" rel="bookmark" title="Should I have a Home Inspection for My New House?">
          Should I have a Home Inspection for My New House?
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="post-meta">
         <img alt="" src="http://www.efede77.com/wp-content/themes/headlines/images/ico-time.png"/>
         August 15, 2017
         <span class="comments">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.efede77.com/wp-content/themes/headlines/images/ico-comment.png"/>
          <span>
           Comments Off
           <span class="screen-reader-text">
            on Should I have a Home Inspection for My New House?
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           <img alt="" class="wp-image-60 alignleft" height="392" sizes="(max-width: 588px) 100vw, 588px" src="http://www.efede77.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/home-insp.jpg" srcset="http://www.efede77.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/home-insp.jpg 1000w, http://www.efede77.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/home-insp-300x200.jpg 300w, http://www.efede77.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/home-insp-768x512.jpg 768w" width="588"/>
           One of the things you want to understand before doing anything is the reason or rather motivation behind it. A common question every person asks before being part of any project is, what’s in for me? People want to know the benefits they can reap or get after partnering with any type of service. It, therefore, means that your question and concern as to whether or not hire a home inspector is right.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           A house is one of the largest investments you’re going to make in your lifetime. It serves a number of purposes including the provision of shelter, protecting your family and possessions, and a place of comfort where you retire after a long day. Let’s just say this is the cornerstone of your life where you spend time with family creating lasting memories. A home inspection is important when buying a home. So, why is it one of the most highly recommended practice? Here are the reasons.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Peace of mind
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           We already indicated that a home is a huge investment and requires a lot of money. Home inspection primarily helps to ensure that the home you’re about to move in is very solid, habitable, and safe. It gives you the peace of mind of knowing that your home is safe. You will have nothing to worry about as far as the safety of your home is concerned. By not having the home inspected, you’ll be creating unnecessary anxiety and stress for yourself and family.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Uncover Safety Issues
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           The role of a home inspector is to thoroughly investigate and assess the home you intend to buy and determine if there are any safety issues or repairs needed. Whether it’s a newly built home or you want to buy an old home, a home inspection is important. Some of the issues that home inspection may uncover include, worn roofing, plumbing deficiencies, improper or faulty electrical wiring, and more. Of course, everything in the home doesn’t have to be perfect, but it’s important for any glaring safety issue to be identified.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Find Structural Issues
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           In addition to uncovering safety issues, a home inspector will help determine if the home has any structural problems that need fixing. We both know how costly structural problems can become if not fixed early. You don’t want to move in the new home only to discover there’s need for a major home improvement project costing thousands of dollars to correct an issue that was present at the time of purchase.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Forecast Potential Future Expenses
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           It’s important that you know the possible expenses you’re likely to have as far as owning that home is concerned. A home inspection will help you know the different parts and elements of the home that could be nearing the end of their life span or may need your attention in the near future. A home inspector can also determine the current state and condition of the home, its structure and the different elements and advice accordingly. Knowing all these will help you plan effectively.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Provides an Opportunity to Opt Out or Negotiate for Seller Repairs
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           Once you’ve seen the areas that have safety or structural issues, it may be appropriate to engage the seller and renegotiate depending on the agreed terms. Furthermore, you can request the seller to make necessary repairs before closing the deal. In most cases, when there’s no agreement on the renegotiations or repairs requested after the inspection, buyers tend to opt out and find a better offer.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-weight: 400;">
           So the answer to whether or not you need a home inspection for the new home is, yes. Note that a home inspector might miss out on a few issues when conducting the inspection. However, a home inspection will provide more insight on the condition of the house than when you or the real estate agent can do.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Home inspection process
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          It is important for the home inspector to take their time during the home inspection.  The home inspector will have a checklist they follow.  This is to be sure the home inspector does not miss any vital parts of the home during the home inspection process.  The entire home inspection process can take about two to three hours.  The home inspector will likely start on the outside of the home with any outside structures.  These would include garages, sheds, barns, etc.  They will also inspect any concrete or pavement.  While doing the inspection on the outside they will check the foundation of the home and the type of siding on the home.  They will also assess the roof structure and condition.
         </p>
         <p>
          When the home inspector is ready to inspect the inside of the home they usually work from the bottom to the top.  The inspector will start in the basement.  The basement it very important because they can many times assess the condition of the foundation.  The foundation is one of the most important parts of the home structure.  If the foundation is not in good condition this can greatly affect the purchase of a home.  Other important elements that are typically found in the basement are the heating/cooling unit, water heater, electrical panel, water/gas meter, sump pumps, etc.
         </p>
         <p>
          As the home inspector starts to inspect the first and second floors they will carefully assess the condition of the floors, walls, ceilings, kitchen, bathrooms, laundry room, all electrical, etc.   The last area the home inspector checks is the attic area.  This area is also very important because they can assess the insulation and check if there are any leaks in the roof.  They also can check the structure of the roof and surrounding walls.
         </p>
         <p>
          When the home inspector is finished they will fill out the home inspection checklist in detail and send it to the client.
         </p>
