                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://flagstamps.blogspot.com/2018/05/vexillology-study-of-flags-one-of.html">
                       Vexillology: Study of Flags; One of the Newest Pursuits
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2536657464711374315" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                       <div dir="ltr" style="background-color: #c0a154; color: #333333; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13.524px;" trbidi="on">
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
                         <span style='background-color: #e4eaef; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 14pt; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Vexillology: One of the Newest Pursuits
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
                         <strong style="color: #222222; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif; font-size: 11pt; text-align: justify;">
                          <span lang="EN-US" style='background: rgb(228 , 234 , 239); border: 1pt none; font-family: "arial" , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 24px; padding: 0cm;'>
                           Scholarly study of all aspects of flag is Vexillology. The term was coined in 1957 by Late    Dr Whitney Smith from the Latin word vexillum for flag. T
                          </span>
                         </strong>
                         <span lang="EN-US" style='background: rgb(228 , 234 , 239); color: #222222; font-family: "arial" , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;'>
                          he Flag Institute, UK defines Vexillology as the scientific study of flags and related emblems. It is concerned with research into flags of all kinds, both modern and historical, the creation of a body of practice for flag design and usage, and of a body of theory of flag development.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; color: #222222; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif; font-size: 11pt; line-height: 22px; margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span lang="EN-US" style='background: rgb(228 , 234 , 239); border: 1pt none; font-family: "arial" , sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 28px; padding: 0cm;'>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="MsoNormal" style="background-color: white; color: #222222; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif; font-size: 11pt; line-height: 22px; margin: 0cm 0cm 10pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;'>
                          Since time immemorial man has felt the need of a sign or symbol to distinguish himself, his family, his tribe, his country, and such symbols have taken many differing forms, of which one is the flag.
                         </span>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;'>
                          E
                         </span>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;'>
                          very country has a National flag. Today, there is no country in the world which does not have a National flag. It is an explicit outward symbolic expression of how a country sees itself.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Flags became wide spread symbols of national identity from early 19
                         </span>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 7.2pt; vertical-align: super; white-space: pre;'>
                          th
                         </span>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          century.
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Before the
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          French Revolution of 1789, it was monarchy and not the common people,
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          who had a flag.
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          With the advent of ‘Nation-State’ concept and the ensuing growth
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          of nationalism - flags have
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          become a globalised phenomenon.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Flags are ‘short hand of history’. The relevance of the adage of Harry Truman;
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          “
                         </span>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          the only
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; font-style: italic; white-space: pre;'>
                          thing
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          new in the world is the history you don’t know”
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          is amply established
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          in the
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          study of flags.
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          A
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          flag is something more than a coloured piece of cloth (bunting)
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          of which
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          it is made.
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          It incarnate
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          s something spiritual. Intrinsically a flag may be
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          valueless but
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          extrinsically a national flag is
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          priceless. In most countries, people
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          feel
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          that the national flag belongs to the citizens and,
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          therefore, they articulate this
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          by waving it in innumerable social settings which manifest the
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          existence and
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          glorification
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          of the country.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Nowadays, it has become a trend to wave the flag colours profusely to cheer their
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          favourite
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          teams in the Olympics and other international sports events.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Flags are truly inseparable from society. The serious study of flags is one of the
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          newest
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          pursuits available to those who take an intimate interest in the world
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          around them;
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          Study of a flag is A BIT LIKE BIRD WATCHING; is that it involves Spotting, identification,
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          classification, and some knowledge of backgrounds, type &amp; various other features
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          and
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          their functions.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          IT IS ALSO A BIT LIKE HISTORY; is that it includes some understanding of past events
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          and
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          how they came about. In fact, story of flags is mostly interwoven with historical events
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          reflecting our struggles, aspirations and hopes.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          IT IS A BIT LIKE GEOGRAPHY too; is that one has to know his way around the world,
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          his
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          own
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          country, and even obscure places.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          A vexillologist collects, preserves, organizes and publicises flag information. For him
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          the
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          flag
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          study is not just restricted on collecting the real flags only but from a plethora
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          of other
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          artifacts
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          and materials, namely, books, archival documents, news-papers, advertisements
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          and
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          many
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          other conventional/unconventional sources like; postage stamps, medals,
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          coins &amp;
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          currency notes, match box labels, propaganda/publicity leaflets, cigarette
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          cards, and so on
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          and so forth. In another word he embraces a rainbow of all
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          collecting arena.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <br/>
                        </div>
                        <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.8; margin-bottom: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          <img alt="H:\Flag foreign stamps\South Africa ICV.jpg" height="136" src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-fJzqWFKxYXnSt-28L8OCcoxlTeUcPgjcyKbEqFh_9SEeLLdAiiFtVcLq4hixOv3Yrc2ebwNh2cl4eQig9W9CxLcbOpcFmnFjMZA1zlP31DSLsZSBY88ou3f9bI5CPvS6q5S_LV1gPxokPQzMA" style="background: rgb(255, 255, 255); border: none; box-shadow: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.5) 1px 1px 5px; padding: 8px; transform: rotate(0rad);" width="258"/>
                         </span>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          International Congress of Vexillology (ICV)s
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          are held
                         </span>
                         <span style='color: black; font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre;'>
                          each
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          two years under the auspices of the International Federation of
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          Vexillological
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          Associations
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          (FIAV). The ICVs consist mainly of lectures, presentations
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          and workshops by
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          the leading flag
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          experts who have the opportunity to present
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          their research and activities to
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          their international
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          peers. The author represented
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          India in three consecutive ICVs, the only
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          Indian to do so, at
                         </span>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          Rotterdam (25th ICV, 2013),
                         </span>
                         <br/>
                         <span style='font-family: "arial"; font-size: 12pt; white-space: pre;'>
                          Sydney (26th ICV, 2015) and in London (27th ICV, 2017).
                         </span>
                        </div>

                        </span>
                        <i style="background-color: #c0a154; color: #333333; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13.524px; text-align: start;">
                         Flags and the Anniversaries of 2017: Myths, Mistakes, Misconceptions.
                        </i>
                        <span style='background-color: #c0a154; color: #333333; font-family: "arial" , "tahoma" , "helvetica" , "freesans" , sans-serif; font-size: 13.524px;'>
                         Christopher writes Harrington spoke briefly about flags that were changed due to the Socialist Red Scare, namely of the old red Oklahoma flag and a few others.  Harrington also gave an enchanting retelling of his youthful excitement in 1965 when the current Canadian flag was adopted, which he instantly loved.
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <br style="background-color: #c0a154; color: #333333; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13.524px; text-align: start;"/>
                        <span style='background-color: #c0a154; color: #333333; font-family: "arial" , "tahoma" , "helvetica" , "freesans" , sans-serif; font-size: 13.524px;'>
                         Harrington proposed that the some Canadian flags with the red and blue ensign may have never existed, and only came into existence due to assumptions and errors.  Essentially the Admiralty was making mistakes and assumptions, based upon the hypotheticals.  As of yet there is no direct evidence that some flags existed.  Perhaps the flags were proposed, but none were created to fly on a flag pole?

                         <span style='color: #595959; font-family: "lato" , "helvetica neue" , "arial"; font-size: 16px; letter-spacing: -0.5px;'>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://flagstamps.blogspot.com/2016/12/tibet-national-flag-and-its-symbolism.html">
                       TIBET NATIONAL FLAG AND ITS SYMBOLISM
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7441080685443359357" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                       <div class="_5pbx userContent" data-ft='{"tn":"K"}' id="js_19o">
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          National Flag of Tibet. A Priceless Gift From Sherap Gyatso of Lhadhan Chotrul Monlam Chenmo Trust, Tibetan Monastery. Sarnath.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-m4LjN77kjdM/WGZLfphBWGI/AAAAAAAAHuM/pVnhb_3rqOwWnZhf-1YpSNCxHGAmOZmzQCLcB/s1600/DSC01631.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="240" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-m4LjN77kjdM/WGZLfphBWGI/AAAAAAAAHuM/pVnhb_3rqOwWnZhf-1YpSNCxHGAmOZmzQCLcB/s320/DSC01631.JPG" width="320"/>
                          With Sherap Gyatso
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <br/>
                        </div>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-zBZKkspygjc/WGZJtCvqFLI/AAAAAAAAHuA/JK-PklOyTScRGCeqY7scVioRpI2NRFE8gCLcB/s1600/Tibetan%2Bflag.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="180" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-zBZKkspygjc/WGZJtCvqFLI/AAAAAAAAHuA/JK-PklOyTScRGCeqY7scVioRpI2NRFE8gCLcB/s320/Tibetan%2Bflag.jpg" width="320"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          On 30 November 2016 I was presented a historic Tibetan Flag by Sherap Gyatso, purported to have been flown 
 during the inauguration ceremony of the first Tibetan Monastery at 
 Sarnath, Varanasi by the founder Gan Thupten Jungney (Gan Gose La), the 
 most popular Tibetan Ranzen activist of his time, when His Holiness the 14th 
 Dalai Lama paid a visit to India to attend the 2500th Buddhist Jayanti 
 Celebration in 1956.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-rWtuO-UrThU/WGZPJS9UoNI/AAAAAAAAHuk/kzwbvVnCUC8rhJ2MgzSl_XktDf032LqcACLcB/s1600/4ac084fb2965ca8d2fd1bf984d4fd675.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="320" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-rWtuO-UrThU/WGZPJS9UoNI/AAAAAAAAHuk/kzwbvVnCUC8rhJ2MgzSl_XktDf032LqcACLcB/s320/4ac084fb2965ca8d2fd1bf984d4fd675.jpg" width="210"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        14th Dalai Lama
                        <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-uJuVvnRS-Xo/WGZO06SQAcI/AAAAAAAAHug/8uGRG0mcjL05tWtN0va8u0o4u2NK-tE4QCLcB/s1600/IMG_20161130_132325.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                         <img border="0" height="320" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-uJuVvnRS-Xo/WGZO06SQAcI/AAAAAAAAHug/8uGRG0mcjL05tWtN0va8u0o4u2NK-tE4QCLcB/s320/IMG_20161130_132325.jpg" width="240"/>
                        </a>
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                        </div>
                       </div>
                       <div class="_5pbx userContent" data-ft='{"tn":"K"}' id="js_19o">
                        <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cNofL4jBvKc/WGZMKcWsVlI/AAAAAAAAHuQ/CvDE5e6yr5k1Y3r8rrXVKbSh6LYL0uS8wCLcB/s1600/13thDalaiLama1910.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                         <img border="0" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-cNofL4jBvKc/WGZMKcWsVlI/AAAAAAAAHuQ/CvDE5e6yr5k1Y3r8rrXVKbSh6LYL0uS8wCLcB/s1600/13thDalaiLama1910.jpg"/>
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          The Snow Lion Tibetan Flag (Gangs Seng Dar Cha) was  adopted by
                          <b>
                           Thubten Gyatso -
                          </b>
                          the 13th Dalai Lama  in 1916.
                         </span>
                        </a>
                        <br/>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          The region of Tibet maintained its autonomy until 1951 when, following the
                          <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Chamdo" title="Battle of Chamdo">
                           Battle of Chamdo
                          </a>
                          , Tibet became
                          <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incorporation_of_Tibet_into_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Incorporation of Tibet into the People's Republic of China">
                           incorporated into the People's Republic of China
                          </a>
                          , and the previous Tibetan government was abolished in 1959 after a failed uprising.
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         China has made possessing the Tibetan flag illegal in Tibet since 1959. (Wikepedia).
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y6CYcFB3oPo/WGZaCNUKZPI/AAAAAAAAHvM/3sjHJzrkXrwnK_l6ImvIRKRb8M9txZycwCLcB/s1600/tibet%2Bleaflet.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-y6CYcFB3oPo/WGZaCNUKZPI/AAAAAAAAHvM/3sjHJzrkXrwnK_l6ImvIRKRb8M9txZycwCLcB/s320/tibet%2Bleaflet.jpg" width="211"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         However the Tibetan Flag is widely in use by the Tibetan people seeking freedom
  from Chinese Rule. The Flag is also used by the Tibetan Government in 
 Exile, based in Dharamsala, India and at all other Tibetan Monasteries.
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         <a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qJRyekmPQ1k/WGZYAKE2R_I/AAAAAAAAHu4/Erm-CMn7nfMLiMy1QSGeSxx0wPyzoUt7ACLcB/s1600/Cinderela.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="216" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qJRyekmPQ1k/WGZYAKE2R_I/AAAAAAAAHu4/Erm-CMn7nfMLiMy1QSGeSxx0wPyzoUt7ACLcB/s320/Cinderela.jpg" width="320"/>
                         </a>
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         A Cinderella stamp issued to commemorate the centenary of the UPU in 1974.
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <b>
                         THE SYMBOLISM OF THE TIBETAN FLAG
                        </b>
                        <br/>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                          <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                          </span>
                         </span>
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                             <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                              In a letter written to me in 1983,
                             </span>
                            </span>
                            Guru Tharchen of the
                            <b>
                             Tibetan Refugee Centre, Darjeeling
                            </b>
                            narrated the symbolisms of the Flag as follows;
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Snow Mountain
                            </b>
                            : Symbolise the geographical
  feature of Tibet surrounded by lofty Himalayas.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Pair of Lions
                            </b>
                            : the
  twin system of the Temporal and Spiritual power.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Wishing Gem
                            </b>
                            : in 
 the paws of the lions the rule of law based on the Law-principal of 
 cause and effect underlying the Ten Golden Precept and the Sixteen 
 Human-principals, which are the source of infinite benefit and peace.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Flaming Jewel
                            </b>
                            : over the wishing gem,devotion of the Three Gems 
 (
                            <i>
                             Triratna
                            </i>
                            ) who are endowed with twenty four transcendental attributes.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             Two 
 Flags in Red &amp; Blue
                            </b>
                            : unfailing support of the two guardian deities 
 known as MAR NAG NYIE.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Twelve Stripes in Red &amp; Blue
                            </b>
                            : the twelve 
 descendant of the six originals of Tibet.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Rising Sun
                            </b>
                            : freedom, 
 happiness and prosperity of the ideals of Buddhism.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Five Coloured Banner
                            </b>
                            : on the top of the mast, the victory of Golden Phodrang, the Central Government of Tibet, over all the spheres.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Double Edged Sword
                            </b>
                            : the
                            <i>
                             <b>
                              Vajra
                             </b>
                            </i>
                            and the L
                            <b>
                             otus
                            </b>
                            , on top of the banner , the incessant appearance of the incarnations of the three of the three
                            <b>
                             Budhisattavas
                            </b>
                            :
                            <b>
                             Avalokitesvara, Monjushri
                            </b>
                            and
                            <b>
                             Vajrapani.
                            </b>
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span style="font-size: large;">
                          <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                           <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                            <b>
                             The Un-bended and Un-ending Mast
                            </b>
                            :  the justice of the law without fear or favour.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <div style="text-align: justify;">
                         <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                          <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                           <span style="font-size: large;">
                            <b>
                             The Knot of the White Ribbon
                            </b>
                            : the noble tradition of the Dharma.
                           </span>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </div>
                        <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                         <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                          <br/>
                         </span>
                        </span>
                        <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                         <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                          <span style="font-size: large;">
                           However, we have also a simplified version of the Symbolism of the flag.
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </span>
                        <br/>
                        <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                         <a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-QwkLU8ImkYI/WGZY8K7bp7I/AAAAAAAAHvA/BQwnCfvcUToi4qVbKNSF5SRn2nEjeSLlwCLcB/s1600/tibet%2B111.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                          <img border="0" height="210" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-QwkLU8ImkYI/WGZY8K7bp7I/AAAAAAAAHvA/BQwnCfvcUToi4qVbKNSF5SRn2nEjeSLlwCLcB/s320/tibet%2B111.jpg" width="320"/>
                         </a>
                        </div>
                        <span data-ft='{"tn":"K"}'>
                         <span class="UFICommentBody _1n4g">
                         </span>
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         1. The white snow mountain in the center depicts the land of the:  great nation of Tibet.
                         <br/>
                         2. The six Red rays emanating from the sun  - the six original people of Tibet: the Se,Mu, Dong, Tong, Dru, and Ra.
                         <br/>
                         3. The blue rays- the commitment to spiritual and secular rule.
                         <br/>
                         4, The pair of snow-lions: the complete victory of the spiritual and secular government
                         <br/>
                         5. The three sided yellow border: the flourishing of the Buddha's 
 teachings. the fly-side without border :Tibet's openness to non-Buddhist
  thought
                         <br/>
                         6. The raised jewel :Tibet's reverence for the three Precious Gems: the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha.
                        </span>
                       </div>

                     Flags and Stamps
                    </a>
                   </dt>
                   <dd class="profile-textblock">
                    Sekhar Chakrabarti -  Vexillologist, author and a passionate collector of flag stamps and flag artifacts. 
 His name found a place in the "Limca Book of Records" for his collections of most stamps featuring the Indian National flag.
  Curated International exhibitions commemorating 125th Birth anniversary of Rabindranath Tagore in Kolkata and Cairo (Egypt) organised by the Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR), Govt of India in 2011.
 Had served as a jury member at several philatelic exhibitions organized by India Post and other philatelic organizations. 
 
 Curated a solo exhibition on “Indian National Flag” show casing myriad flag artifacts for the first time in India under the auspices of ICCR, Govt of India in 2014.
  First Indian delegate to present original research based papers on aspects of the Indian Flag at the 25th International Congress of Vexillology (ICV25) at Rotterdam, the Netherlands in 2013,  ICV26 Sydney Flag Congress, Australia in 2015 and at ICV27 in London Flag Congress in August 2017. 
 He lives in Kolkata, India. 
 Email ID:  sekhar.chakrabarti@gmail.com
