       <h1>
        Topknots and Toddlers
       </h1>
       An upcoming photography exhibition, “Topknots and Toddlers,” at Monongalia Arts Center (MAC) celebrates motherhood through photography. The exhibition features the portraits of motherhood captured by Lauren Webster, a lifestyle and documentary photographer from Morgantown. An opening reception is being held on Friday, July 6, from 6 – 8 pm. The exhibition will be on display in the Davis Gallery until August 4.
 
 Lauren Webster has always had a passion for photography. “I love documenting real moments that show my family’s, and my clients, true personality,” Webster says of her work. “Storytelling and documenting are my way of holding on to those memories, and I hope that my family and my clients can look back at the photos I’ve taken for years to come.”
 
 “Topknots and Toddlers” features portraits of Webster’s clients and a few self portraits, all celebrating the challenge and beauty of motherhood.
 
 MAC is located at 107 High Street, Morgantown, WV.  Inquiries should be directed to info@monartscenter.com or 304-292-3325.

       <h1>
        Millicent Scowlworthy
       </h1>
       In this 2006 play by Rob Handel, a child is found murdered in her adoptive parents’ basement on Christmas morning and a high school massacre soon follows in the same town. The murder-mystery is full of social commentary, but also offers up characters with playful names and eccentric personalities such as Jinx Porbeagle and Botho Spire. 
 
 The cast is made up of students in the Morgantown area, with ages ranging from 13 to 17. They attend University High, Morgantown High, and Westwood Middle. First-time director and rising University High Senior Bailey McCord plans on pursuing theatre in college, and is taking this project as a way to expand both his skill set and resume. “We have a few very seasoned performers and others who will be making their stage debut in this production,” McCord said. “It’s such an exciting experience getting to learn and grow as an artist with every rehearsal.”
 
 Tickets are now available for this performance. Admission is $10. Tickets may be purchased at the MAC Lobby Desk, by phone (304-292-3325), and online at www.monartscenter.com/shop. Millicent Scowlworthy contains mature themes of violence and death, and some mild language that may not be suitable for young children.
 
 The play will be performed at Monongalia Arts Center (MAC). The performances are August 10, 11, and 12. The Friday and Saturday performances will be at 8pm, and on Sunday there will be a matinee at 2pm.
