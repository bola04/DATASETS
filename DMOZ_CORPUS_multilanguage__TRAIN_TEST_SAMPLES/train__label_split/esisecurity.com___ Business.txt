         <br/>
         <b>
          Environmental Security International (ESI) is a  L3C* corporation whose mission is primarily a social benefit.
         </b>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         ESI provides consulting and training in Best Practices for 
 Environmental and Security Risk Assessments and integrated 
 Quality Management Systems (QMS)
         <br/>
         <br/>
         ESI experts conduct advanced investigations and provide technical 
 assistance to implement Environmental and/or Security Management 
 Systems (SMS) conforming to international standards such as 
 ISO 27000, 26000, and ISO 28000.  They are designed to meet 
 customer and regulatory requirements, as well as public policy goals.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         ESI provides facilitation and management services to assist 
 and integrate Emergency Preparedness, Response, Recovery and 
 Continuity plans with enterprise risk management objectives.
         <br/>
         <br/>
        </td>
        <td align="center" valign="top" width="20">
         <img border="0" height="1" src="images/spacer.gif" width="20"/>
        </td>
        <td align="left" class="facecontent" valign="top" width="410">
         <br/>
         ESI tests new technologies for socially beneficial and effective 
 applications to achieve organizational and community resiliency,
 assure compliance, and meet sustainability goals.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         ESI works with community organizations, stewards, defendants, and prosecutors to develop, broker, and implement Supplemental Environmental Projects 
 (SEP) to ensure meaningful and innovative outcomes to environmental enforcement.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         ESI offers educational programs in law, policy, standards, and best practices related to environmental security. ESI Senior Associates provide 
 litigation, government, and public relations support.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
          *
         </b>
         An L3C corporation is a designation in the tax code for low profit entities with a "socially beneficial" mission.
