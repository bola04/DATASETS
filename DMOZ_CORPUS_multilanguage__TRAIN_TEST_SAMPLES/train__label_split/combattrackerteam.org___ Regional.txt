               <span class="style9">
                MS of the Viet Nam War,
                <br/>
                w
               </span>
               <span class="style27">
                <span class="style16">
                 The COMBAT 
 			TRACKER TEAMS of the Viet Nam War,
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 were small, highly-trained units usually 
 			consisting of
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 five 
 			men and a Labrador Retriever. The purpose of
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 CTT was to: reestablish 
 			contact with the "elusive enemy";
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 reconnaissance of an area for possible enemy 
 			activities;
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 and 
 			locate lost or missing friendly personnel.
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 The methods used in 
 			completing the missions
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 were Visual and Canine Tactical Tracking. The 
 			unit
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 was usually 
 			supported by a platoon or larger force
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 and worked well ahead of them to maintain
                </span>
                <br class="style16"/>
                <span class="style16">
                 noise discipline and the 
 			element of surprise.
                </span>
