          <header class="entry-header">
           <h1 class="entry-title">
            <span class="hpt_headertitle">
             About UKLGIG
            </span>
           </h1>
          </header>
          <!-- .entry-header -->
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="pf-content">
            <div class="printfriendly pf-alignright">
             <a class="noslimstat" href="#" onclick="window.print(); return false;" rel="nofollow" title="Printer Friendly, PDF &amp; Email">
              <img alt="Print Friendly, PDF &amp; Email" src="https://cdn.printfriendly.com/buttons/print-button-gray.png" style="border:none;-webkit-box-shadow:none; box-shadow:none;"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            <p>
             <style media="all" type="text/css">
              .printfriendly {display: none!important;}
             </style>
            </p>
            <p style="text-align: center;">
             <strong>
              <span style="color: #bb4444; font-family: Verdana; font-size: 10pt;">
               £10 could pay for an LGBTQI+ asylum seeker
               <br/>
               to attend an appointment with our support worker
               <br/>
               Text LGIG01 £10 to 70070 to donate
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <table border="2" cellpadding="10" cellspacing="10" style="border: 2px solid #cccccc; background-color: #e2e6f2;">
             <tbody>
              <tr>
               <td style="width: 400px; vertical-align: middle;">
                <strong>
                 <a href="https://uklgig.org.uk/?page_id=1225">
                  STILL FALLING SHORT
                 </a>
                 :
                </strong>
                <br/>
                <strong>
                 The standard of Home Office decision-making in asylum claims based on sexual orientation and gender identity.
                 <a href="https://uklgig.org.uk/?p=2663">
                  Press Release 12th July 2018
                 </a>
                </strong>
                <br/>
                UKLGIG has published a
                <a href="https://uklgig.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Still-Falling-Short.pdf">
                 new research report
                </a>
                examining Home Office decision-making. The report finds that Home Office decision-makers are not applying the correct legal standard of proof of ‘reasonable likelihood’ in all asylum claims based on sexual orientation or gender.
               </td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
               <td style="width: 400px; vertical-align: middle;">
                <a href="https://uklgig.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/UKLGIG-on-HJ-Iran.pdf">
                 NEW BRIEFING PAPER:
                 <br/>
                 Applying HJ (Iran) and HT (Cameroon) to asylum claims based on sexual orientation.
                </a>
                <br/>
                This paper summarises the important guidance and framework set out by this case. It considers each of the four questions that lie at the core of the judgment and provides recommendations for decision-makers.
               </td>
              </tr>
             </tbody>
            </table>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             The
             <strong>
              UK Lesbian &amp; Gay Immigration Group (UKLGIG)
             </strong>
             supports LGBTQI+ people through the asylum and immigration process. Our vision is a world where there is equality, dignity, respect and safety for all people in the expression of their sexual or gender identity.
            </p>
            <p>
             We provide psychosocial and emotional support for LGBTQI+ asylum seekers to help improve their mental health and reduce isolation. We also provide legal information and advice. In addition, we advocate for improved policies and decision-making by governments in LGBTQI+ asylum claims.
            </p>
            <p>
             We also continue providing support and information to LGBTQI+ people seeking to live in the UK with their partners via our website, an online forum and a
             <a href="https://uklgig.org.uk/?page_id=80#partnership-meetings">
              monthly meeting
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             Around the world more than 70 countries consider homosexuality illegal, in five of these the death penalty can apply. Moreover, the persecution experienced by LGBQTI+ people goes well beyond any official prosecutions. The violence, humiliation, inequality and discrimination many LGBT people face, can be enacted by state officials, but is also often experienced within their own communities and families without any recourse to protection. UKLGIG assists LGBTQI+ people seeking a safe haven from persecution.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <br/>
              Our asylum related activities include:
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Supporting LGBT asylum seekers:
             </strong>
             providing emotional support &amp; legal information referring to solicitors; visiting detention centres and running other social support projects.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Research &amp; Policy:
             </strong>
             working to ensure LGBTQI+ people are treated equally and with dignity in the asylum process; seeking to improve the quality of Home Office decision making and seeking an end to the detention of LGBTQI+ people.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Training &amp; Information:
             </strong>
             providing training and information on LGBTQI+ asylum issues.
            </p>
           </div>
