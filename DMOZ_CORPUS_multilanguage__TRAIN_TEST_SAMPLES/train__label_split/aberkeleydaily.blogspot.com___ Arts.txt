       <h3 class="post-title">
        Portraits for a Purpose
       </h3>
       <div class="post-body">
        <div>
         <div style="clear:both;">
         </div>
         Here's the latest art project I'm leading. I would appreciate your help in making it a reality.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <div style="text-align: center;">
          <iframe frameborder="0" height="429px" scrolling="no" src="http://www.indiegogo.com/project/151742/widget/588619" width="224px">
          </iframe>
         </div>
         <br/>
         The
         <a href="http://www.indiegogo.com/portraits-of-family-house" target="_blank">
          Portraits of Family House
         </a>
         project is a series of portraits of the courageous people involved with Family House. This non profit organization provides free housing for the families of children being treated at UCSF Benioff Children's Hospital. Over the next two years, fine art portrait painter Seamus Berkeley will create 24 oil portraits of the people connected to Family House: the families, children, care givers, organizers, and health care professionals. The portraits will create a visual legacy of the faces and spirit of the people who transform Family House into a family's temporary home. Berkeley will gift the final collection of portraits to Family House that will benefit their mission and enhance the walls of the new Family House home.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Follow this
         <a href="http://www.indiegogo.com/portraits-of-family-house" target="_blank">
          link
         </a>
         to read more and be a part of creating art to benefit the mission of Family House.

         <a href="http://seamusberkeley.com/workshops-list/">
          Register here...
         </a>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Here's the class description:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         People have expressed a genuine need to connect with something other than their everyday routine of work and deadlines. Art can be just such an antidote for clearing space in the mind and re-orienting with the world again.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Using themed exercises each month, we will explore re-awakening direct visual experience as well as techniques for expressing the seen world on a two-dimensional surface.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Highlights:
         <br/>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Every Saturday from 10am to 1pm.
          </li>
          <li>
           Sign-up for one month at a time.
          </li>
          <li>
           Each month themed on a particular aspect of painting and drawing.
          </li>
          <li>
           First month will be to work with charcoal and explore seeing lights and darks and expressing them in there simplest shapes.
          </li>
          <li>
           Main intention is to increase awareness of seeing, that is, learning to see more attentively and practicing seeing deeply as a way to be more present and grounded in the moment.
          </li>
          <li>
           Month of February is four Saturdays, $80 total for the month.
          </li>
          <li>
           Sessions will include some instruction and demonstration. Much of the work will be focused on your work, while recognizing the internal dialogue taking place.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <br/>
         Details:
         <br/>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Where: Berkeley Fine Art Studio, 933 Parker Street, Berkeley CA 9410, between 8th and 9th Streets [
           <a href="http://seamusberkeley.com/content/contactmapberkeley">
            Map
           </a>
           ]
          </li>
          <li>
           When: Saturdays, 10am to 1pm
          </li>
          <li>
           Cost: $80 for four Saturdays-materials not included.
          </li>
          <li>
           Materials: In January, charcoal, drawing pad, kneaded eraser, soft cloth or chamois
          </li>
          <li>
           Class size: Limited to 12 students
          </li>
         </ul>

         <div style="clear:both;">

Approaches to seeing and deciding interesting compositions are the focus of this one day workshop, as well as learning how to see and how to get that scene onto your canvas.
