              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2627488264347002010" itemprop="description articleBody">
               <div style="text-align: center;">
                <b>
                 Sell Dubai Apartment
                </b>
               </div>
               <br/>
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-HiAYKWK5UNE/ViaGwVT0qLI/AAAAAAAABRk/_ba-TdTx7N0/s1600/Bermuda-Heights%252C-Dubai.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img alt="Sell Dubai Apartment" border="0" height="320" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-HiAYKWK5UNE/ViaGwVT0qLI/AAAAAAAABRk/_ba-TdTx7N0/s320/Bermuda-Heights%252C-Dubai.jpg" title="Sell Dubai Apartment" width="259"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: center;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                If you would like to sell a Dubai apartment you are not alone. Selling a property in Dubai has once again become relevant. A lot of people bought apartments in Dubai back at the start of the centure (it only became possible to purchase property there in 2002).
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                In 2015, however, developers around the Emirate were forced to
                <a data-mce-href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/dubai-property-registration-tax/" href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/dubai-property-registration-tax/">
                 contact property owners relating to an existing, but increased, 'Registration Tax'
                </a>
                introduced by the Dubai authorities in 2013. The 4% registration tax was to be paid before the end of June 2015, otherwise penalties would be imposed. This deadline was later extended to the end of October 2015.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                Quite a few recipients thought this was a request to complete on their properties. This took many of them by surprise as they had presumed that they would never receive a property in the Emirate.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                One way or the other, the communication has brought a new impetus for those who have put deposits on property in Dubai but who do not now wish to complete on them for one reason or another. Quite a few developments in Dubai were very popular with UK and Irish based property investors, such as
                <a data-mce-href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/selling-a-bermuda-views-dubai-apartment/" href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/selling-a-bermuda-views-dubai-apartment/">
                 Bermuda Views
                </a>
                ,
                <a data-mce-href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/selling-eagle-heights-dubai-apartment/" href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/selling-eagle-heights-dubai-apartment/">
                 Eagle Heights
                </a>
                and
                <a data-mce-href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/profile-residence-dubai-completion-email/" href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/profile-residence-dubai-completion-email/">
                 Profile Residence
                </a>
                in Dubai Sports City in particular.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                The market for property in Dubai, much like elsewhere in the world, dropped off a cliff after 2007, hence the lack of building activity. In fact, Dubai stayed in growth mode until 2008, but when the decline came it was spectacular. Since 2009, and particularly since 2013 there has been something of a pick-up, although there are signs that this is starting to tail off somewhat now with Dubai recording the
                <a data-mce-href="http://www.independent.ie/business/commercial-property/property-prices-in-dubai-plummet-by-more-than-12pc-31497631.html" href="http://www.independent.ie/business/commercial-property/property-prices-in-dubai-plummet-by-more-than-12pc-31497631.html" target="_blank">
                 largest fall in property prices
                </a>
                in the world, at 15.5% in the first half of 2015. For a more in-depth analysis read Diarmaid Condon's
                <a data-mce-href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/dubai-property-prices/" href="http://www.diarmaidcondon.com/dubai-property-prices/">
                 Dubai Property Prices
                </a>
                article.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                If you do need to sell a property in Dubai, whether it has already completed or you've just put a deposit on the unit, then we may be able to assist.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                We have contact with a number of investors in the region that are actively looking for property around Dubai. If they are interested in purchasing your unit or your option on a unit, then they are in a position to act quite quickly. This is a huge advantage, because under normal market conditions you could be waiting an awfully long time to sell your property or interest in a property.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                If you would like to see what you can achieve for your unit drop a mail to
                <a data-mce-href="mailto:info@diarmaidcondon.com" href="mailto:info@diarmaidcondon.com" target="_blank">
                 info@diarmaidcondon.com
                </a>
                stating the development, unit number, size and stage of completion (as far as you are aware) and whether you've paid the Registration Tax. We'll then see if we can find an interested buyer.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
                <br/>

                <strong>
                 How to Get Residency in the USA - The EB5 Visa
                </strong>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                If you are looking to live in the USA, with residency rights, then the
                <a data-mce-href="htt://www.centralfloridaeb5regionalcenter.com" href="htt://www.centralfloridaeb5regionalcenter.com" target="_blank" title="Central Florida Regional Center EB5 US Visa">
                 EB-5 Regional Center Investment Visa
                </a>
                is one of the fastest ways to achieve this result. There are significant benefits to residency in the US, not least not having to regularly re-apply for a visa as you do if you are not a citizen and wish to stay in the US - with no guarantee of being granted the visa.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                <br/>
               </div>

                <a data-mce-href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permanent_residence_(United_States)" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permanent_residence_(United_States)" target="_blank" title="Green Cards for Residency in the USA">
                 Green Cards
                </a>
                ) for you, your spouse and children under 21 by investing into a US government designated 'Regional Center' program.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                With the EB-5 visa you have the option to take a job, run a business or retire and live anywhere in the USA. As a permanent resident you have access to all benefits enjoyed by US citizens. Your children can attend schools or universities with the same rights as US citizens. These rights do not end after they turn 21, they stay with them for life.

                <br/>

                <br/>

               </div>

                1. Permanent residency for you and your family.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                2. Access to benefits enjoyed by US citizens.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                3. Freedom – no need to manage investment on a day to day basis.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                4. Flexible - no requirements for age, business experience, or language skills.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                5. No requirement to be constantly present in the United States.
               </div>
               <div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;">
                6. Maintain business and professional relations in your country of origin should you so wish.

               <a href="http://overseascafe.blogspot.com/2012/06/florida-market-continues-recovery.html">
                Florida Market Continues Recovery
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2097710928434474506" itemprop="description articleBody">
               <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FZae2cYB6Zw/T9BJwZM4zZI/AAAAAAAAAhk/YUzO4z4ukbw/s1600/Florida-Property-Sold.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                 <img border="0" height="199" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FZae2cYB6Zw/T9BJwZM4zZI/AAAAAAAAAhk/YUzO4z4ukbw/s320/Florida-Property-Sold.jpg" width="320"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <b>
                Florida Market Continues Recovery
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It's not going to be easy, or uniformly in one direction, but to all intents and purposes it appears that the Florida property market is showing signs that it may have bottomed out and is inclined to improve a little.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               This will come as somewhat of a relief for Irish owners in Florida, of whom they are many, although prices there are a long way off where most Irish purchasers entered the market. At least, for now anyway, prices seem to be headed in the right direction for owners.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Irishman, Gary Kenny, who runs
               <a href="http://www.feltrim.com/" target="_blank">
                Coldwell Banker Feltrim
               </a>
               in Davenport, Florida says the second home market, which his company services, has seen more international visitors coming back into central Florida which has helped the company sell more units in 2012 than the previous two years. There is actually, Kenny says, a shortage of good  property to promote and sell to an eager market.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <b>
                Investor Product Launch
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Within the next two weeks the company will announce details of an upcoming project just outside Tampa where it will release 50 units for sale, all producing return on investment to the buyer of
               <b>
                between 10 and 12% nett
               </b>
               . A decent return when you consider what you can get out of a bank these days.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <b>
                Market Improvement
               </b>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Orlando attracted more visitors last year than previously projected, beating expectations and setting a record for any U.S. travel destination, according to figures released last Tuesday.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Visit Orlando, the area's 'Convention and Visitors Bureau', said an estimated 55.1 million visitors spent at least a day in the Orlando area during 2011, an increase of 7.2 percent from the previous record. The visitors' bureau had earlier forecast a 2011 head count of 54.3 million people.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Paul Tang, chairman of Visit Orlando, termed the record year 'tremendously positive' news for the nation's theme-park capital.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Tang said: "We have seen a strong rebound in our visitation numbers over the past two years, led by increases in domestic and international travel, and we continue to be optimistic about the future of the destination. We are known worldwide as the top family destination, and we continue to be a top choice for inspiring meetings. Breaking the 55 million total visitation mark shows the strength of the Orlando brand."
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Putting the figures in perspective, Orlando was the first U.S. market to draw more than 50 million visitors in a single year in 2010 when it reached 51.5 million, beating New York to the milestone.

                  34. Upon information and belief, PCI and Vargas currently are marketing and
 attempting to sell PCI’s bonds to issuers and others in the United States and
 abroad.  In December 2010, PCI announced that financial guarantee bonds
 are a current growth area for the Company.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  35. The overall face value of the life insurance policies bonded by PCI – and
 sold through the above-identified issuers including A&amp;O and ASA – exceeds
 $670 million.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  36. PCI charged as a premium six to eleven percent of the total face value of
 each life insurance policy that it bonded.  These premium payments,
 including payments made by A&amp;O, ASA and other life settlement issuers,
 typically were sent to a U.S. dollar-denominated bank account in Costa Rica in
 the name of Relief Defendant Desarrollos Comerciales Ronim S.A.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  62. The first part of D&amp;B’s rating is an estimate of the company’s
 financial strength and is based on the company’s self-reported net worth.
  PCI was automatically assigned the “5A” rating because its materially
 false and misleading financial statements indicated that it had a net worth
 higher than $50 million.  D&amp;B assigns the first part of its rating
 based on company provided data and does not independently verify the accuracy
 of the data provided, which, in the case of PCI, was purportedly audited by
 Castillo
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  63. The second part of D&amp;B’s rating is a composite credit appraisal, but,
 because PCI is a service company and D&amp;B does not perform credit appraisals
 of service companies, PCI was automatically assigned an “S,” which the report
 explains means “Service -- Not Applicable – No Condition Assigned.”  PCI’s
 overall “5A-S” rating did not reflect any qualitative assessment by D&amp;B.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  67. Additionally, from at least October 2004 to the present, PCI and Vargas not
 only touted PCI’s D&amp;B rating, but also, they suggested to issuers and the
 public that the D&amp;B rating was a reflection of substantive analysis and
 review by D&amp;B.  PCI has stated on its website since October 2004,
 that:
                  <br/>
                  "As a private fully recognized insurance company, PCI has chosen to use
 the rating services of [D&amp;B] Internationally.  PCI’s strict
 underwriting guidelines [are] responsible for maintaining the highest rating
 attainable (5A) from D&amp;B indicating successful customer satisfaction and
 the ability
                  <br/>
                  to maintain one of the insurance industry lowest loss ratios." Contrary to
 PCI’s claim, the D&amp;B rating was unrelated to “customer satisfaction” and
 did not reflect low loss ratios or any appraisal of PCI’s credit-worthiness.
  It was based solely on PCI’s materially false and misleading financials.
                  <br/>
                  70. ... PCI did not and does not have reinsurance coverage or any other
 contractual relationship with the reinsurers listed in its sample bond.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <span style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-color: white; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt;">
                  75. ...  a North
 Carolina-based sales agent informed a customer that PCI had D&amp;B’s “highest
 rating” and was reinsured “by some of the biggest companies around,”
 including AIG and Swiss Re.  As a result, the customer invested $254,000
 in the A&amp;O offering.
                  <o:p>
                  </o:p>
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">

                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <span style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-color: white; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt;">
                  78. ... PCI and Vargas
 violated a cease-and-desist order issued by the Texas Department of Insurance
 in
                 </span>
                 <span style="background-color: white; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 15pt;">
                  2006 that prohibited PCI from
 engaging in unauthorized insurance business in Texas.  PCI and Vargas similarly ignored a Texas State Securities Board’s cease-and-desist order
 against PCI in 2008.
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <span style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-color: white; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt;">
                  81. "Recently, PCI has been
 forced to use incoming premiums on a new life settlement
                 </span>
                 <span style="background-color: white; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 15pt;">
                  offering to make partial payments
 on claims lodged against PCI bonds on investments in which
                 </span>
                 <span style="background-color: white; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 15pt;">
                  the policyholder outlived his or
 her life expectancy."
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 15.0pt; margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <br/>

                <span style="background-attachment: initial; background-clip: initial; background-color: white; background-image: initial; background-origin: initial; color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 10pt; line-height: 115%;">
                 In other words, the SEC is accusing PCI of
 running a Ponzi Scheme. Sound familiar?

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
               <a href="http://overseascafe.blogspot.com/2011/08/sao-nicolau-cape-verde-development.html">
                Sao Nicolau - Cape Verde Development
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-65089613387396060" itemprop="description articleBody">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--MSE0bWAI1E/TkpJnqfCI7I/AAAAAAAAADw/cvA4Iw-BQCg/s1600/San-Nicolau-Development.jpg">
                <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5641402429003146162" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/--MSE0bWAI1E/TkpJnqfCI7I/AAAAAAAAADw/cvA4Iw-BQCg/s320/San-Nicolau-Development.jpg" style="cursor: hand; cursor: pointer; float: left; height: 240px; margin: 0 10px 10px 0; width: 320px;"/>
               </a>
               <b>
                Sao Nicolau - Cape Verde Development
               </b>
               <br/>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                Have you invested in a hotel development with Cork based (although currently all out of the country)
                <a href="http://www.capeverdedevelopment.com/">
                 Cape Verde Development
                </a>
                on Sao Nicolau island in the Cape Verde Islands (also known as San Nicolau)? The development is known as
                <a href="http://www.imaginecapeverde.com/current-developments/monte-gordo.asp">
                 Monte Gordo
                </a>
                .
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                This project (pictured as it stood in 2006 - but it's not much different now) was allegedly purchased from a bankrupt developer - Sanilisa Empreendimentos S.A. It was to have a hotel added to the middle of the development to create the Hotel Monte Gordo Resort which would then be run by Extreme Hotels. The hotel was to be built between the rows of apartments, where a hole now stands (there is a foundation for something there at this stage, but that's as far as it ever got). In any case, the hotel never materialised and investors here are not one bit pleased as they feel this development was sold fraudulently.
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                If you were involved get in contact (
                <a href="mailto://info@overseascafe.com">
                 info@overseascafe.com
                </a>
                ), as we would like to hear from you. We have received some information about this development which may be of use to investors involved in the project.
                <br/>
                <br/>
                <b>

              <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
               <a href="http://overseascafe.blogspot.com/2011/05/where-to-invest-germany.html">
                Where to Invest - Germany
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2379029300618613978" itemprop="description articleBody">
               <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-cqKk_03CQUA/Tdnk1cxIh1I/AAAAAAAAADk/GmShbwL-Y9o/s1600/Brandenberg%2BGate" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5609766417773135698" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-cqKk_03CQUA/Tdnk1cxIh1I/AAAAAAAAADk/GmShbwL-Y9o/s320/Brandenberg%2BGate" style="float:left; margin:0 10px 10px 0;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 320px; height: 253px;"/>
               </a>
               With all the doom and gloom around the place you'd be forgiven for thinking that there's nowhere left in the world in which it's worth investing. In fact, you'd be very far from the truth. Just because Irish investors don't have the deep pockets (and reckless banks) they once had, doesn't mean that there are not countries out there worth a look. We're going to look at one of them today. Germany. Here are a few highlights.
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <div>
                 The German economy is performing exceptionally well with very exciting economic news in recent months. There are strong indications that the country is moving more and more centre stage as the main driver and, increasingly, perhaps the saviour of the Euro economy.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 Germany is the world’s second largest exporter and its third largest economy, driven by a population of over 82m people. Recent economic news points to excellent economic fundamentals - which is more than can be said for a lot of European economies.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 GDP  growth of c 3.5% in 2010 was exceptionally positive , and the official forecast for 2011 was recently increased form 2.3% to 2.6% on the back of excellent data elsewhere.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 German exports  experienced  their fastest growth rate in two decades during 2010 , with German trade surplus of €60.2 billion from Jan-May 2010. Compare this to the other 'powerhouses' of the European economy -France (-€20billion) and UK (-€40billion).
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 Business confidence measured by the German IFO index has climbed continuously over the past year from 95.9 in Jan 2010 to 110.3 in Jan 2011, another all time high in the 20 year period since re-unification. “The German economy has started the year with great vigour” (H-W Sinn , President IFO Institute Munich , 01/2011).
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 The German unemployment rate has fallen to 7.5% from 8.2% in 2009. This rate is forecast to drop further in 2011 to 7% with 300,000 new jobs anticipated. The number of Germans at work in 2012 is expected to reach an all-time high of 41 million .  In the former East German states, including Saxony, unemployment is at the lowest rate since 1991. Germany is one of the few countries where unemployment in now lower than before the 2008-2009 financial crisis.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 The German budget deficit is in control unlike other leading economies. The deficit is now forecast at 3.7% for 2010 reducing to 2.4% in 2011, well within the 3% EU target. The key German Markit purchasing managers survey shows that  both services and manufacturing are performing strongly with the index at 61.0 , the 2nd highest reading on record “yet another signal that
                 <span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space:pre">
                 </span>
                 Germany’s economy is pulling away from most of its  Eurozone competitors” (Irish Times 24/01/11).
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 “There are encouraging signs that domestic demand is beginning to emerge as a source of additional growth in the German economy.” ( Equinet Bank , Frankfurt 10/01/2011)
                </div>
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                So where can you get a bit of Germany? We'll be posting here with some info in the near future.
               </div>

                Maguire Claims to be Moving Forward
               </a>
              </h3>
              <div class="post-header">
               <div class="post-header-line-1">
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5693850650644837705" itemprop="description articleBody">
               <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-23HlsLjA28U/TY3K9klBqxI/AAAAAAAAAgs/rCeC_D-X2l8/s1600/Expresso%2Bdas%2Bilhas%2BMasthead%2Bcopy.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5588345871776459538" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-23HlsLjA28U/TY3K9klBqxI/AAAAAAAAAgs/rCeC_D-X2l8/s320/Expresso%2Bdas%2Bilhas%2BMasthead%2Bcopy.jpg" style="float:left; margin:0 10px 10px 0;cursor:pointer; cursor:hand;width: 243px; height: 96px;"/>
               </a>
               Mr. Maguire is back in the news again following claims in a Cape Verdean newspaper, Expresso das ilhas, that his Palm View Resort is now to be constructed on the site of another unfinished development. Courtesy of the
               <a href="http://boavistaexperience.phpbbweb.com/boavistaexperience-ftopic784-75.html">
                BoaVista Forum
               </a>
               the following rough translation of the newspaper article was offered:
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                <div>
                 "The Irish group Ciaran Maguire launched on the morning of Thursday, 24, in Boa Vista, the first stone for the construction of an apartment complex with 238 luxury apartments, casino, thalassotherapy center and restaurants.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 The initial investment Palm View Resort is 25 million and, according to prosecutors, all the apartments are already sold to Europeans, especially English and Irish. "The hotel, the restaurants will be complete later this year, and the other blocks will be complete next year," he told Radio in Cape Verde Ciaran Maguire, chairman of the Group.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 Palm View Resort is a luxurious beachfront resort, which initially will have three hundred employees. The project includes a seven-star boutique hotel, nine swimming pools, four restaurants, beach bars, bar, nightclub, tennis courts, soccer fields, outdoor theater, fully equipped gym and a health spa luxury and beauty. The design of the Palm View Resort Ciaran Maguire Group will be born in Praia Cabral, the former complex Sabi sands, FM Group, which went bust just over two years.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 In Cabral Beach Ciaran Maguire group wants to invest in the area surrounding Palm View Resort, rehabilitating the area and build a function room, a high school level and creating pedestrian promenade, with shops selling local handicrafts.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 It is recalled that in 2008, Ciaran Maguire organized a tour of the island of Boa Vista, and has taken him about 160 people, some of which would become investors in the transaction. But due to delays and uncertainty in implementing the project, some eventually require replacement funding.
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 The idea had been thought initially to Praia and then was transferred to Boa Vista."
                </div>
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                The original article in Portuguese is here -
                <a href="http://www.expressodasilhas.sapo.cv/pt/noticias/detail/id/23822">
                 http://www.expressodasilhas.sapo.cv/pt/noticias/detail/id/23822
                </a>
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                The BoaVista Facebook page is, however, quite adamant that much of the information in this article is false and misleading. It says:
               </div>
               <div>
                <br/>
               </div>
               <div>
                "The name "Palm View Resort" has again reared its head in relation to Boa Vista. This "Newspaper" article is riddled with the same old promises and has glaring inaccuracies. I have written to the newspaper and asked them to review this story in light of the damage that it will do to their credibility."
               </div>

                    <br/>
                   </div>
                   <div>
                    OC.
                   </div>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <b>
                  End of email tennis session.
                 </b>
                </div>
                <div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div>
                 This last one seems to have hit a nerve somewhere along the way as the email silence was renewed almost as quickly as it had been broken. It appears young Ciarán thought everybody would fall for the switch of the now abandoned Creola Sands development in the pictures (readily available on his blog
                 <a href="http://www.ciaranmaguireblog.com/2011/02/palm-view-london-cape-verde.html?spref=bl">
                  here
                 </a>
                 ) in which he stuck his flags for a few weeks, and take it as verification of his assertion that he has actually started work on the Palm View Resort. From reports received from those on the ground on Boa Vista it appears that there is currently no activity on the suggested Palm View site (which CMG has yet to prove it actually owns).
                </div>
