           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.accfcb.org/2018/07/sharing-drone-video-from-2018-14th-annual-teed-off-for-tots-golf-event.html">
             Sharing Drone Video From 2018 (14th Annual) Teed Off For Tots Golf Event
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q3vvwH6pFBA&amp;feature=youtu.be&amp;app=desktop" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="With Compliments of Correction Officer Terry Burns">
               Drone Video- 14th Annual "Teed Off For Tots" Golf Event
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              This Drone Video was taken at the 2018 Golf Tournament, the 14th annual to benefit the seriously ill children of our “Albany County Correctional Facility for Children’s Benefit” and the great work of the dedicated staff of the Albany County Correctional Facilities “Local 775”!
             </p>
             <p>
              We express our deep gratitude for this Terrific Video that was done by Correction Officer, Terry Burns, It was enjoyable to watch!! A great turnout with many familiar faces! We further wish to Thank you all of the sponsors, participants and the many volunteers for participating and continuing this wonderful tradition which highlights your generosity to our seriously ill children and your dedication to your community! We are beyond proud of your extraordinary effort, Congratulations!
             </p>
             <p>
              Special Thanks to:
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://accfcb.typepad.com/.a/6a01116861a05d970c022ad35a4336200c-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Local 775 Logo" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a01116861a05d970c022ad35a4336200c img-responsive" src="http://accfcb.typepad.com/.a/6a01116861a05d970c022ad35a4336200c-320wi" title="Local 775 Logo"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Most Gratefully Yours,
             </p>
             <p>
              Board of Directors
             </p>
             <p>
              (Albany County Correctional Facility for Children's Benefit)
             </p>

         <h2 class="date-header">
          October 01, 2017
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-category-eva_scotto entry-author-john_wojcik entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a01116861a05d970c01bb09c8c5dd970d">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.accfcb.org/2017/10/my-entry.html">
             We Welcome Eva Scotto, age 9 to ACCFCB's Family of Seriously Ill Children
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://accfcb.typepad.com/.a/6a01116861a05d970c01b7c925b03a970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Eva Scotto Photo" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a01116861a05d970c01b7c925b03a970b img-responsive" src="http://accfcb.typepad.com/.a/6a01116861a05d970c01b7c925b03a970b-320wi" title="Eva Scotto Photo"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Eva Scotto, age 9 Albany, NY
             </p>
             <p>
              Eva was diagnosed with Mitochondrial Disease, an inherited chronic incurable disease. It effects her autonomic nervous system further causing her a gastrointestinal disorder and is legally blind. Further symptoms of this disease include poor growth, loss of muscle coordination, muscle weakness, hearing problems, learning disabilities, heart disease, liver disease, kidney disease, respiratory disorders, neurological problems and dementia.
             </p>
             <p>
              Eva was born 4 months premature on 12/4/07, weighing just 18 ounces. Eva is currently unable to attend school. She alternates periods where she is fed via IV or through a tube in her intestines. She will receive physical and occupational therapy when she is feeling well enough. She receives palliative care at Albany Medical Center via a team of medical staff. She has undergone 13 surgeries and 30 hospitalizations. Eva does not tolerate the heat or cold very well and has issues regulating her vital signs. She tires easily and is totally incontinent.
             </p>
             <p>
              Some equipment Eva requires to keep her stable include: Feeding pump, nebulizer, cough assist machine, chest percussion therapy vest, oxygen compressor, G and J tubes.
             </p>
             <p>
              Mom said "she is into Mario, Luigi and Minions. Loves hand held devices as her vision is limited. We take each day for what it is....A Blessing. For the most part she is a happy girl but she has fear which causes outbursts at times when we go out."
             </p>
