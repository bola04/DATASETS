           Paramahansa
 Yogananda
          </a>
          was one of the first Indian spiritual teachers to
 reside
 permanently in the West. He taught the
          <a href="http://www.yogananda-srf.org/tmp/meditation_notitle.aspx?id=114" target="New_Window">
           techniques

          <a href="http://www.yogananda-srf.org/" target="New_Window">
           Self-Realization
 Fellowship
          </a>
          . He is the author of
          <a href="http://bookstore.yogananda-srf.org/c6/Autobiography-of-a-Yogi-c7.html" target="New_Window">
           Autobiography
 of a Yogi
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://bookstore.yogananda-srf.org/c6/c12/God-Talks-With-Arjuna-The-Bhagavad-Gita-p53.html" target="New_Window">
           The

 God-Realization
          </a>

 titling this work
         </i>
         The Second Coming of Christ,
         <i>
          I
 am not referring to a literal return of Jesus to earth....A thousand
 Christs sent to earth would not redeem its people unless they
 themselves become Christlike by purifying and expanding their
 individual consciousness to receive therein the second coming of the
 Christ Consciousness, as was manifested in Jesus....Contact with this
 Consciousness, experienced in the ever new joy of meditation, will be
 the real second coming of Christ — and it will take place
 right in the
 devotee's own consciousness."
         </i>
         —Paramahansa
 Yogananda

        <font size="+0">
         Earth not
 only spins, it wobbles. And over time, that wobbling -- called
 precession 
 -- affects the positioning of the sun relative to the constellations on
 the zodiac. For example, on the first day of spring (vernal equinox)
 5,000
 years ago, the sun was in Taurus. Today, the sun is in Pisces at the
 vernal
 equinox and in Taurus at the summer solstice. In about 600 years, the
 sun
 will enter "the age of Aquarius." Precession is commonly thought to be
 caused by the constant pull of the sun and moon on the bulge of the
 Earth's
 equator. It's a slow wobble, however, taking nearly 26,000 years to
 complete.
