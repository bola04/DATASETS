        <h2 class="title">
         About Me(ech)
        </h2>
        <div class="widget-content">
         <br/>
         <h6>
          <i>
           After some minor kicking and screaming, this is my official "coming out":
           <b>
            I'm a FLAMING PETHEAD
           </b>
           … which is a festive way of saying "mega Pet Shop Boys fan". If you want to read a bit more…
          </i>
         </h6>
         <br clear="all"/>
         <h5 align="right">
          [
          <a href="javascript:expandcollapse('faqs')">
           Ooh, I want to read a bit more!
          </a>
          ]
         </h5>
         <span class="posthidden" id="faqs" style="float:left;margin:70px 10px 10px 10px;">
          <b>
           WHAT IS THIS? WHERE AM I?
          </b>
          <br/>
          <i>
           These are sketches of
           <strike>
            the
           </strike>
           <a href="http://www.petshopboys.co.uk/" target="new">
            Pet Shop Boys
           </a>
           I've made for no particular reason other than a photo caught my eye.
          </i>
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <b>
           PET… SHOP… … HUH?…
          </b>
          <br/>
          <i>
           It's hard to nail down a tidy artistic description of Pet Shop Boys, but I think a "seething mass of elegant contradictions"
           <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/books-for-christmas--rock-music-the-crazy-shrine-of-king-tut-1507360.html">
            *
           </a>
           is just about perfect. For nearly 30 years they've amassed a pants-wettingly diverse and enduring archive of music — inspired by everything from political history &amp; dirty deeds, to existentialist introspection, hilarity, &amp; the general hopelessness of humanity… and fun and dancing and sex and all the other little snippets of life. Accompanying it all, Pet Shop Boys' visual catalog has maintained a peculiar integrity that manages to seem both orderly and completely bonkers.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            ARE YOU A GUY?
           </b>
           <br/>
           <i>
            Nope! The moustache is purely ornamental.
           </i>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            IS THERE ANYTHING SLEAZY OR VULGAR HERE?
           </b>
           <br/>
           <i>
            Nope! Your kids are totally safe.
           </i>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            THE PEOPLE LOOK DIFFERENT IN EACH DRAWING! OH, AND IN THE ONE SKETCH THERE'S THIS LITTLE BIT OF PAISLEY FABRIC SHOWING BEHIND NEIL'S ELBOW THAT WASN'T IN THE ORIGINAL IMAGE—
           </b>
           <br/>
           <i>
            Quite often the drawing doesn't turn out to look exactly like the image; after finding a lovable photo I just do what the pencil tells me to do. Also, I drew these with my feet, so I'm lucky they look like anything at all.
           </i>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            ARE YOU LOOKING FOR ATTENTION?
           </b>
           <br/>
           <i>
            Although I did unintentionally snag
            <a href="http://www.petshopboys.co.uk/pettexts/2761">
             15 minutes of fame
            </a>
            , I'm not looking for any particular attention. I just want to make some scribbly-doos and show them to people who might appreciate or make use of them. Ideally I'd be generating more Pet Shop Boys fans in some inexplicable way.
           </i>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            ARE THE BAND AWARE OF THIS ABUSE OF THEIR IMAGERY?
           </b>
           <br/>
           <i>
            Sketch Shop Boys is apparently a favorite brunch topic. They pop in every day, tea and sandwiches and all that. Chris:"I dearly wish there were a Sketch Shop Boys fan club." Neil:"We have it bookmarked on Internet Explorer™."
           </i>
