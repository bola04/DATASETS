                      <a href="https://luizstudio2.blogspot.com/2010/11/rising-moon-at-twilight.html">
                       Rising moon at twilight
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7081083466833561926" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <p align="left">
                       <a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/TO8GuiBGF1I/AAAAAAAAA1k/8ufhc3OB7vQ/s1600/moonrise+at+sunset.jpg">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5543657062791518034" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/TO8GuiBGF1I/AAAAAAAAA1k/8ufhc3OB7vQ/s400/moonrise%2Bat%2Bsunset.jpg" style="WIDTH: 228px; HEIGHT: 168px; CURSOR: hand"/>
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <p align="left">
                      </p>
                      <p align="center">
                       <a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/TPVDU4pXXPI/AAAAAAAAA1w/MMejdQNWxec/s1600/moonrise%2Bat%2Bdusk.jpg">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5545412542258961650" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/TPVDU4pXXPI/AAAAAAAAA1w/MMejdQNWxec/s400/moonrise%2Bat%2Bdusk.jpg" style="WIDTH: 240px; HEIGHT: 168px; CURSOR: hand"/>
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <p align="justify">
                       This is a scene from a nearby lake in my area. The first photo is my initial impression, however I reworked the painting by adding in more detail and color. I was unhappy with the clouds on the left as they were too light and threw the balance of the darks on the right in the water. I switched to a larger brush and made a mix of payne's gray and white,with a slight dab of cad red medium. I then softened the edges with my finger. I added in more highlights using white, cad yellow, and cad red, keeping the mix more to the white side, with just a hint of color. I then added in some of the dark cloud color into the water, highlighting the reflected sky with a medium values of phtalo blue grayed down with payne's gray. I then toned down the red along the horizon, adding in some white with cad red. Over all I'm much more pleased with the end result.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </p>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="https://luizstudio2.blogspot.com/2009/12/two-new-paintings.html">
                       Fallen tree in forest light
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4855458659891944057" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <p align="center">
                       <a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/TRvUyxfTlKI/AAAAAAAAA30/ieuuLb_-EsY/s1600/walk%2Bin%2Bthe%2Bforestc.jpg">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5556268534034830498" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/TRvUyxfTlKI/AAAAAAAAA30/ieuuLb_-EsY/s320/walk%2Bin%2Bthe%2Bforestc.jpg" style="WIDTH: 253px; HEIGHT: 320px; CURSOR: hand"/>
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div align="left">
                       Usually I fuss and fret and wind up spending days on end adjusting and readjusting my work, but for some reason I've allowed myself the freedom to let things just "be". I've tried a new technique on the "Fallen tree in Forest Light" taken from a scene I shot while hiking in the fall . I began painting in my shapes and colors with my reference photo and canvas turned to their side instead of upright. This allowed me to concentrate on getting the light right instead of focusing on "what" I was painting. Once I got that down, I tried to keep my paints thin, to retain transparent color in the areas of light, deepening in the shadow areas with slightly thicker paint as well as using thicker paint for the fallen tree.
                      </div>

                      <a href="https://luizstudio2.blogspot.com/2009/09/branch-brook-park.html">
                       Spring inspirations
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8254558381489512877" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <p align="center">
                       <a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/S6BdR3101LI/AAAAAAAAAp0/BnaxVkyB4sI/s1600-h/P1050421.JPG">
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <br/>
                      <p align="left">
                       <a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/S6BZlc95CeI/AAAAAAAAApo/w0pMpimjmcY/s1600-h/P1050423.JPG">
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <p align="left">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5377970490888599762" src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/SqJjqvHyuNI/AAAAAAAAAew/0MlLafuPzGY/s400/tina%27s+painting.jpg" style="TEXT-ALIGN: center; MARGIN: 0px auto 10px; WIDTH: 293px; DISPLAY: block; HEIGHT: 189px; CURSOR: hand"/>
                      </p>
                      <p align="center">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5449450151903614242" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/S6BWCm1p1SI/AAAAAAAAApU/uMikako9z28/s320/blossoms+in+the+parkb.JPG" style="WIDTH: 274px; HEIGHT: 183px; CURSOR: hand"/>
                      </p>
                      <p align="justify">
                       Every year, in the town of Belleville, NJ people from all over the Metropolitan area flock to see the Cherry Blossom Festival in Branch Brook Park. There are literally hundreds of these beautiful trees lining quaint little paths throughout the park, both a photographer's and artist's delight. I love walking the familiar paths, but still take hundreds of photos each and every Spring. Another favorite place of mine is Hudson County Park, in North Bergen, NJ. These paintings were inspired by scenes from these areas.

                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8908410421116086652" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/Sfi0bdO31JI/AAAAAAAAAVY/m32I0h_mB5o/s1600-h/recreation+area.JPG" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5330208542789457042" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/Sfi0bdO31JI/AAAAAAAAAVY/m32I0h_mB5o/s320/recreation+area.JPG" style="MARGIN: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px; WIDTH: 307px; FLOAT: right; HEIGHT: 217px; CURSOR: pointer"/>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I frequently travel between NJ and Pa and always pass through the Delaware Water Gap area. Just before the bridge, coming from NJ, is a cut off leading to a recreation area on the East side of Rt. 80. It offers a close up view of the Delaware River and Gap . During the past several years there has been severe flooding and the area has been renovated with a new welcome center. This painting represents the area before the new fence was installed, not that anyone would probably even notice, but just thought I'd make a notation on that for those who would remember. Oil on canvas 18x24.

                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6903363580026023002" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <p align="center">
                       <a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/S8W70FfyuuI/AAAAAAAAAu0/AoyXHf2OXp4/s1600/paintings1+008.jpg">
                        <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5459976626759711458" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_Icmq-qfkKwc/S8W70FfyuuI/AAAAAAAAAu0/AoyXHf2OXp4/s320/paintings1+008.jpg" style="WIDTH: 283px; HEIGHT: 292px; CURSOR: hand"/>
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      I've painted this scene several years ago when I first tried my hand with the newer water-based oils. It was taken from a photo reference found on the web, so I have no idea where these beautiful falls are located. If anyone recognizes them, please feel free to post a reply.
                      <br/>
                      In this painting I was concentrating on picking up the subtle variations of green in the foiliage and the feel of fast moving water.

                  Artist statement
                 </h2>
                 <div class="widget-content">
                  <a href="https://www.blogger.com/profile/03163047901691736051">
                   <img alt="My photo" class="profile-img" height="80" src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-jlJLC05h4LU/WycHVXLCw2I/AAAAAAAABgw/MOGcuV6s6gwXOJUT6aPsmaDTxykazKc0wCK4BGAYYCw/s80/1b.JPG" width="80"/>

                   </dt>
                   <dd class="profile-textblock">
                    Hi and welcome to my blog. As with many people interested in art, I'm a nature lover at heart and over the past 30 yrs. have sought to express that appreciation in photography and painting. 
 During summer months I love to go hiking, usually in State parks in the Poconos as well as spending time down the New Jersy shore. 
 These outings provide me with hundreds of photo references.
 
 I'm forever chasing sunsets and as so many others, am captivated by beautiful cloud formations. I also look forward to every change of season as it brings with it bold beautiful color, and water scenes have a magic of their own. 
 Indoors,I try to recreate some of those initial impressions through my artwork. For me, it's more about the process and joy I get from experimenting in paint.  Painting gives me a feeling of being in sync with nature, now part of the creation, not just an observer. 
 If you'd like to take a quick nature hike, please check out my photo links below.
 Thanks for looking, all comments welcome.
