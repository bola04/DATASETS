              <p class="auto-style137">
               <strong>
                <span class="auto-style10">
                 WE EXTEND A SPECIAL WELCOME
                </span>
               </strong>
               to those who are single, married, divorced, widowed, gay, confused, filthy rich, comfortable or dirt poor.
              </p>
              <p class="auto-style135">
               We extend a special welcome to wailing babies and excited toddlers.
              </p>
              <p class="auto-style135">
               We welcome you whether you can sing like Pavarotti or just growl quietly to yourself. You’re welcome here if you’re just browsing, just woken up or just got out of prison. We don’t care if you are more Christian than the Archbishop of Canterbury, or haven’t been to church since Christmas ten years ago.
              </p>
              <p class="auto-style135">
               We extend a special welcome to those who are over 60 but not grown up yet, and to teenagers who are growing up too fast. We welcome keep-fit mums, football dads, starving artists, tree-huggers, latte-sippers, vegetarians, junk-food eaters. We welcome those in recovery or still addicted. We welcome you if you are having problems, are down in the dumps or don’t like ‘organised religion’. (We’re not too keen on it either!)
              </p>
              <p class="auto-style135">
               We offer a welcome to those who think that the earth is flat, work too hard, don’t work, can’t spell, or are here because granny is visiting and wanted to come to church.
              </p>
              <div class="auto-style135">
               We welcome those who are inked, pierced, both or neither. We offer a special welcome to those who could use a prayer right now, had religion shoved down their throats as kids or got lost on the coastal path and wound up here by mistake. We welcome pilgrims, tourists, seekers, doubters ….. and you!
              </div>

               <h3 class="entry-title">
                <a href="http://stgermansparishes.com/the-food-bank-no-change-to-the-message-our-help-is-needed/" rel="bookmark" title="THE FOOD BANK  – no change to the message – our help is needed">
                 THE FOOD BANK  – no change to the message – our help is needed
                </a>
               </h3>
               <div class="entry-excerpt">
                <div class="vc_row wpb_row vc_row-fluid">
                 <div class="wpb_column vc_column_container vc_col-sm-12">
                  <div class="vc_column-inner ">
                   <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                    <div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element ">
                     <div class="wpb_wrapper">
                      <p>
                       <em>
                        <strong>
                         There are collecting boxes in all our four churches – please keep the contributions coming in………..
                        </strong>
                       </em>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       You will have read in the press that the need is greater than ever – so please keep the contributions coming in.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <span style="color: #008000;">
                        How can you help the Foodbank?
                       </span>
                       Currently (in end of June 2018). there are the following shortages:
                      </p>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        TINNED CARROTS
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        TINNED PINEAPPLE AND TINNED FRUIT COCKTAIL
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        FRUIT JUICE (LONG-LIFE)
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        RICE PUDDING (TINNED)
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        BISCUITS/SNACKS
                       </li>
                       <li>
                        TOILET ROLLS
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <p>
                       PLEASE – no baked beans, pasta, soup, teabags or cereals – the Foodbank has PLENTY of theses items.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       However,
                       <strong>
                        money
                       </strong>
                       is becoming a continuing issue, and it is becoming a common problem throughout all of Cornwall’s Foodbanks.   All are having to react to an increase in demand; and although food is being donated in increasing volumes covering the demand, the costs related to sustaining larger Foodbanks is becoming a challenge. So, the real requirement now is cash.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       If you could help through regular donations we would be extremely grateful. Setting up a Standing Order is ideal, but any donation would be wonderful. We can also take donations CAF and Stewardship schemes.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       If you can help please contact the Liskeard &amp; Looe Foodbank.
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Tel: 07512 011452. email:
                       <a href="mailto:liskeard.foodbank@gmail.com">
                        liskeard.foodbank@gmail.com
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       Thank you for your support.
                      </p>
                      <p>

              <h3>
               East Wivelshire
              </h3>
              <p>
               The area of the Diocese of Truro area is that of the county of
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cornwall">
                Cornwall
               </a>
               including the
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly">
                Isles of Scilly
               </a>
               . It was formed on 15 December 1876 from the
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Archdeacons_of_Cornwall">
                Archdeaconry of Cornwall
               </a>
               in the
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocese_of_Exeter">
                Diocese of Exeter
               </a>
               . It is therefore one of the younger dioceses.
              </p>
              <p>
               The diocese is divided into two
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archdeacon">
                archdeaconries
               </a>
               :
              </p>
              <ul>
               <li>
                the Archdeaconry of
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodmin">
                 Bodmin
                </a>
                (est. 1878): includes
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dean_%28religion%29">
                 Deaneries
                </a>
                of Trigg Minor and Bodmin,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wivelshire">
                 East Wivelshire
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stratton,_Cornwall">
                 Stratton
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triggshire">
                 Trigg Major
                </a>
                , and
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wivelshire">
                 West Wivelshire
                </a>
               </li>
               <li>
                the
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Archdeacons_of_Cornwall">
                 Archdeaconry of Cornwal
                </a>
                l: includes Deaneries of
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Austell">
                 St Austell
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnmarth">
                 Carnmarth North
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnmarth">
                 Carnmarth South
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerrier">
                 Kerrier
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penwith">
                 Penwith
                </a>
                ,
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Powdershire">
                 Powder
                </a>
                and
                <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pydarshire">
                 Pydar
                </a>
               </li>
              </ul>
              <h3>
               Rural deaneries
              </h3>
              <p>
               The names of the older deaneries (before 1875) are based on those of the ancient hundreds though the boundaries do not always correspond. The deaneries created in 1875 in the episcopate of
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Temple">
                Frederick Temple
               </a>
               were Bodmin, Stratton, St Austell, and Carnmarth. These remained unchanged until Carnmarth was divided; later still in the 1980s some alterations of boundaries occurred. Chronological Sequence:
              </p>
              <p>
               <em>
                Estwevelsire, 1259; Estwyvelschyre, 1291; Estwevelschire, 1366; Easte, 1672; East, 1847, 1980; East Wivelshire, 1997, 2009.
               </em>
              </p>
