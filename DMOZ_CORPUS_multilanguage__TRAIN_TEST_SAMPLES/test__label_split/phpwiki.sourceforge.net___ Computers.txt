       <h1>
        PhpWiki: a PHP WikiWikiWeb
       </h1>
       <p>
        A WikiWikiWeb is a web site where anyone can edit the pages through
       an HTML form. Linking is done automatically on the server side; all
       pages are stored in a database.
       </p>
       <p>
        This may sound rather simplistic, but a Wiki is a very unique way
       to collaborate on the Web. I, Steve Wainstead, set up a few while 
 	working at the
        <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/">
         New York Times on the Web
        </a>
        , 
       and the technical staff really took to 
       them in a big way. They wrote 500 pages in just a few months (all
       to document the internal systems).
       </p>
       <p>
        The addictive quality of a Wiki is that
        <i>
         making pages is as simple as
       making a link to them.
        </i>
        If they don't yet exist, the page link will
       be followed by a hyperlinked question mark; follow that link and you
       can define the new page.
       </p>
       <p>
        With this setup, you can update and create pages from anywhere there
       is a web browser handy.
