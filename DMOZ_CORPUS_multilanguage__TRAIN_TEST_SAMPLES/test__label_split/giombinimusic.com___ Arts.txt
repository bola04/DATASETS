       <div class="uk-width-1-1">
        <div class="uk-panel">
         <p>
          <strong>
           Pierluigi Giombini
          </strong>
          has composed and arranged various hit records from the 1980’s such as “
          <a href="http://giombinimusic.com/en/greatest-hits/masterpiece">
           <strong>
            Masterpiece
           </strong>
          </a>
          ” and “
          <a href="http://giombinimusic.com/en/greatest-hits/i-like-chopin">
           <strong>
            I Like Chopin
           </strong>
          </a>
          ” for Gazebo and “
          <a href="http://giombinimusic.com/en/greatest-hits/dolce-vita">
           <strong>
            Dolce Vita
           </strong>
          </a>
          ” for Ryan Paris, which have become
          <strong>
           "evergreens"
          </strong>
          .
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Pierluigi Giombini
          </strong>
          was born in Rome, in a family of musicians. His father,
          <a href="http://marcellogiombini.it/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            Marcello Giombini
           </strong>
          </a>
          , was a composer of numerous movie soundtracks, and sacred music. His grandfather,
          <strong>
           Arnaldo Giombini
          </strong>
          , was an oboe professor in the
          <strong>
           National Orchestra of Santa Cecilia
          </strong>
          , in Rome.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Pierluigi Giombini
          </strong>
          , being a great admirer of
          <strong>
           Keith Emerson
          </strong>
          , and of
          <strong>
           Wendy Carlos
          </strong>
          for her wonderful renditions of
          <strong>
           J.S. Bach
          </strong>
          , enthusiastically began to study and create new sounds with analog synthesizers, such as the Minimoog, Oberheim OB-Xa and OB-8, which he used to arrange and produce his hit records.
          <br/>
          During his career spanning decades, various productions of
          <strong>
           Pierluigi Giombini
          </strong>
          have been on the top record sales charts worldwide amounting to over twelve
          <strong>
           million copies sold
          </strong>
          .
          <br/>
          He now lives in Frankfurt am Main, where he continues to study classical music and create dance-music projects.
         </p>
