         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.hillison.com/?p=3294" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Niagara &amp; Toronto">
           Niagara &amp; Toronto
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p class="comment">
         </p>
         <p class="postinfo">
          Posted by Michelle on Jun 3, 2018 in
          <a href="http://www.hillison.com/?cat=3" rel="category">
           Hayley
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.hillison.com/?cat=5" rel="category">
           Jeff
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.hillison.com/?cat=4" rel="category">
           Michelle
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Had an amazing vacation to Pittsburgh, Niagara Falls and Toronto. I can’t believe we packed in everything we did in a week but FHEW.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pittsburgh was family stuff for me – seeing where my granddad Jim grew up and my great-grandparents graves. Stayed in this great hotel that used to be apartments and even had the old style buzzers. Great suites, can’t wait to stay there again. Then we did touristy stuff – Primanti Brothers for sandwiches with fries and slaw inside, the Warhol Museum which was great and even had his collaborations with Haring and Basquiat! Hayley picked up some prints for her dorm room next year.
         </p>
         <p>
          Niagara was more than I even expected, we did the Canadian side and it was great. Our hotel room had an amazing view of Niagara. The drinking AND gambling age was 19 so Hayley talked me into taking her to a casino to play and order a legal drink. Sheesh! She won $8 right quick on the slots and said she was done – that’s all it took. The boat ride to the Canadian and US Falls was amazing. We were definitely soaked but thrilled to be. My phone got damp because I couldn’t stop using it or keep it in the waterproof case, but thank goodness the iPhone X is water resistant and it was fine. The experience blew all three of us away. We dried off and had our first taste of the Canadian staple, poutine!
         </p>
         <p>
          Toronto, what a fun city. So friendly I felt like we were in the south. We had a great suite downtown and had a pass to hop on and off a tour bus looping around the city, so we did the tour and hopped off when we wanted. Hit their aquarium (duh, any vacation of ours always includes aquariums and art), their Chinatown and Hayley got to see a real life Banksy work, which was a dream come true for her – his work has decorated her room for years and was all over her dorm room. One of Jeff’s favorite authors was signing so he got to listen to her read and sign a book. Ate more Poutine and ate all sorts of fun stuff – pork dumplings, beef noodles and other stuff in Chinatown, Chinese pastries, liquid nitrogen ice cream, cookie dough served like ice cream and a waffle BBQ sandwich. Shopped and goofed off. Even had a movie night in the hotel. Found the amazing Graffiti Alley. Saw the CN Tower (tallest building in North America) in the sun and then obscured by fog. Met interesting uber drivers!
         </p>
         <p>
          Just a great week. Can’t wait to go back to Canada – I have family in BC and one of my grandmothers was from Nova Scotia.
         </p>
