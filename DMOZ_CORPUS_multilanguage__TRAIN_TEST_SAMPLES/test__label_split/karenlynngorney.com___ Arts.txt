              <br/>
              <p>
               Karen Lynn is best known as the romantic star of
               <strong>
                SATURDAY  NIGHT FEVER
               </strong>
               : the girl (Stephanie) who said "no" to  John Travolta after winning a dance contest with him; and the legendary TARA  MARTIN on ABC's
               <strong>
                ALL MY CHILDREN
               </strong>
               . She  is a winner of the People's Choice and European Bravo Awards. She holds a BFA  from Carnegie Mellon and an MFA from Brandeis University.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               In New York City, as member of famed Irish Rep. Company, Karen Lynn garnered  raves as "Mrs. Dandy McCabe" in
               <strong>
                The Field
               </strong>
               ; as  "Sister" in
               <strong>
                Late Night Catechism
               </strong>
               at Theatre at St. Lukes; and as  "Miss Ophelia St. Clare" in
               <strong>
                Uncle Tom's Cabin
               </strong>
               at the Mint Theatre.
              </p>
              Last season, Karen Lynn was directed by  -Austin Pendleton in
              <strong>
               Orpheus  Descending
              </strong>
              as "Eva Temple" at Theatre at  St. Christopher's; she also starred as Eyphah in the new farce, Under the  Knife, at Theatre for the New City and as Judy Garland in the new musical  comedy,
              <strong>
               Parker and Dizzys Fabulous Adventure
              </strong>
              at the Players Theatre in the Village.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              After a successful run at the West Side Theatre as Lady Belaria in  Shakespeares
              <strong>
               Cymbeline
              </strong>
              ,  and as "Marlene Dietrich" at the TRIAD, in "
              <strong>
               Marlene and Noel
              </strong>
              ,  Karen Lynn continued her work in films:
              <strong>
               <em>
                Late Phases
               </em>
              </strong>
              for Dark Sky Films;
              <strong>
               <em>
                The Imperialists are Still Alive
               </em>
              </strong>
              for Hi Jack Films and
              <strong>
               <em>
                Ripe
               </em>
              </strong>
              , for Tri-Mark Films.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              In the previous season, Ms. Gorney played "Mad Queen Margaret"  in
              <strong>
               Richard III
              </strong>
              with The Royal Shakespeare's Anatol  Yusef (Boardwalk Empire-Meyer Lansky) as "Richard" at the West End  Theatre; the Irish Arts Centre as "Francis Foley" in a new play,
              <strong>
               Monsterface
              </strong>
              ; and the  "Duchess of Windsor" in the comedy, "
              <strong>
               The Merry Wife of Windsor
              </strong>
              "  at the Sonnet Theatre.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Karen Lynn other films include:
              <strong>
               City of Thieves
              </strong>
              as "Hetty Green" (the  richest woman in the world in 1900) for Josh Carlebach (Drama Desk award); the  comic film,
              <strong>
               Cant Dance
              </strong>
              ; award  winners:
              <strong>
               Searching for  Bobby D., Thru the Wall;
              </strong>
              the Polish film,
              <strong>
               The Pale of Settlement
              </strong>
              ;
              <strong>
               Creating  Karma, Dear J., A Crime, with Harvey Keitel, 100 Years of Evil, Bronx  Paradise, and The Eight Faces of Jane: Kudzu.
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Television appearances include:"Anne Thornton" in
              <strong>
               FRANCIS KEY, AFTER THE SONG
              </strong>
              for PBS out this fall; "Judy" on the
              <strong>
               Sopranos
              </strong>
              ;  "Mary O'Malley" on
              <strong>
               Six Degrees
              </strong>
              ; and  "Iris Corman" and Monk on
              <strong>
               LAW and ORDER
              </strong>
              . She is  a founding member of The Frog and Peach Shakespeare Company:  "Constance" in
              <strong>
               KING JOHN
              </strong>
              ;  "Elizabeth" in
              <strong>
              </strong>
              <strong>
               RICHARD lll
              </strong>
              ;  "Gertrude" in
              <strong>
               HAMLET
              </strong>
              .
