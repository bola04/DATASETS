        <a href="http://www.tmz.com/person/alex-trebek/" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          Alex Trebek
         </strong>
        </a>
        can't host "Jeopardy!" forever -- in fact, he says there's a good chance he'll retire from the show soon -- and he's already got a replacement in mind ... with a very fitting name.
       </p>
       <p>
        Trebek talked to Harvey on the latest episode of "OBJECTified" Sunday night about his legendary career as a game show host. He says chances are more than 50/50 he'll leave "Jeopardy!" when his contract expires in 2020, and when he departs ... he thinks the LA Kings hockey announcer should take over.

        <strong>
         Alex Faust
        </strong>

       </p>

               <strong>
                Kanye West
               </strong>
              </a>
              's father,
              <strong>
               Ray West
              </strong>

             </p>
             <p>

                helped trigger his mental breakdown
               </strong>
              </a>
              in 2016.
             </p>

              <img src="https://images.tmz.com/2018/07/29/0729-kanyewest-raywest-x17swipe-3.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Ye and his pops were spotted grabbing lunch at Nobu Malibu in March ... a rare sighting of the father and son together.
             </p>
             <p>
              Ray and Kanye's late mother divorced when Ye was a young child, but the 2 remained amicable over the years.

            <a class="headline" href="http://www.tmz.com/2018/07/29/wwe-hall-of-famer-nikolai-volkoff-dead-dies-at-70/" rel="bookmark">
             <h1>
              <span class="hf1">
               WWE Hall of Famer
              </span>
              <span class="hf2">
               Nikolai Volkoff Dead at 70
              </span>
             </h1>
            </a>
            <h5 class="article-posted-date">
             <div class="inpost-byline-social">
              <div class="facebook-sharrre" data-text="WWE Hall of Famer Nikolai Volkoff Dead at 70 " data-title="Share" data-url="http://www.tmz.com/2018/07/29/wwe-hall-of-famer-nikolai-volkoff-dead-dies-at-70/">
              </div>
              <div class="twitter-sharrre" data-related="TMZ_Sports" data-text="WWE Hall of Famer Nikolai Volkoff Dead at 70 " data-title="Tweet" data-url="http://www.tmz.com/2018/07/29/wwe-hall-of-famer-nikolai-volkoff-dead-dies-at-70/" data-via="TMZ_Sports">
               <a class="box" href="#">
                <div class="share">
                 <span class="share-twitter-icon">
                 </span>
                 <span class="share-count" style="visibility: hidden;">
                  1
                 </span>
                </div>
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="comment-count">
               <a class="comment-link" href="http://www.tmz.com/2018/07/29/wwe-hall-of-famer-nikolai-volkoff-dead-dies-at-70/#disqus_anchor">
                <span class="comment-icon">
                </span>
                <span class="comment-text disqus-comment-count" data-disqus-identifier="aa05d22a-9353-11e8-bc9d-30342d354f50">
                </span>
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             7/29/2018 11:02 AM PDT
            </h5>
            <div class="article-content">
             <p>
              <img src="https://images.tmz.com/2018/07/29/0729-nikolai-volkoff-getty-3.jpg"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              WWE Hall of Famer
              <strong>
               Nikolai Volkoff
              </strong>
              died this weekend.
             </p>
             <p>
              His onetime ring partner,
              <a href="http://www.tmz.com/person/the-iron-sheik/" target="_blank">
               <strong>
                The Iron Sheik
               </strong>
              </a>
              -- with whom he won WrestleMania I's World Tag Team Championship in '85 -- confirmed in a tweet Sunday that Volkoff had passed away.
             </p>
             <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en">
              <p dir="ltr" lang="en">
               MY BEST FRIEND. MY PARTNER. MY BROTHER. WE GET MORE HEAT THAN ANYONE ON EARTH. I TRUST HIM WITH MY LIFE. HE TAKE CARE OF ME WHEN I WAS DOWN. I MISS YOU FOREVER 
               <a href="https://t.co/pp7ZgQRDnb">
                pic.twitter.com/pp7ZgQRDnb
               </a>
              </p>
               The Iron Sheik (@the_ironsheik)
              <a href="https://twitter.com/the_ironsheik/status/1023601009573031937?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">
               July 29, 2018
              </a>
             </blockquote>
             <script async="" charset="utf-8" src="https://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" type="text/javascript">
             </script>
             <p>
              Volkoff, who's real name was
              <strong>
               Josip Nikolai Peruzovi
              </strong>
              , wrestled in the WWWF from 1968 to 1971 where he won a tag team title in 1970. After that, he went on to wrestle singles ... and later returned to the WWWF between '74 and '80 and then again for a second time in '84.
             </p>
             <p>
              Volkoff had a successful run teaming up with Sheik in multiple wrestling events between '84 and '87 as well as other wrestlers in the years after. He went back and forth between foreign heel and hero through the '90s and eventually scaled back wrestling through the 2000s ... although he did make appearances in WWE.
             </p>
             <p>
              <iframe allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="410" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/xqQW6fPz1xo" width="728">
              </iframe>
             </p>
             <p>
              Although he was Croatian, Volkoff was portrayed as a Russian ... and would often rile up the crowd by singing the Russian National Anthem.
             </p>
             <p>
              WWE released a statement on Volkoff's passing, saying in part ... "As one of the greatest villains sports-entertainment had ever seen, Volkoffs infamous rendition of the Soviet National Anthem before his matches made him an instant icon in the eyes of the WWE Universe as a Superstar they truly loved to hate ... WWE extends its condolences to Peruzovis family, friends and fans."
             </p>
             <p>
              Volkoff was inducted into the WWE's Hall of Fame in 2005. He was 70 years old.
             </p>
             <p>
              RIP
             </p>
