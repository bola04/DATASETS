             <h4>
              <span style="color: #ff0000;">
               August 5, 2018
               <br/>
              </span>
              BARA Annual Picnic
             </h4>
             <p>
              <b>
               Sunday August 5th – 10:00am
               <br/>
               <a href="https://www.google.com/maps/place/Wood+Dale+County+Park/@41.0201032,-74.0330585,17z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m5!3m4!1s0x89c2ef5f5fb97ae3:0x78b73bb5c1d1bbf0!8m2!3d41.0201032!4d-74.0308698?hl=en" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
                Wood Dale County Park
               </a>
               <br/>
              </b>
              <b>
               Prospect Ave, Woodcliff Lake, NJ 07677
              </b>
             </p>
             <p>
              There is NO business meeting in August.  Please contact Ginny N2EYN if you haven’t already notified her of an item you will be bringing to the picnic. We will be gathering at the covered pavilion about 3/4 of the way around the park after you enter.
             </p>
             <p>
              There is no cost to attend and refreshments are available. All are welcome!The meeting will be held at:
              <br/>
              The Township of Washington Senior Center
              <br/>
              350 Hudson Ave
              <br/>
              Twp. of Washington, NJ 07676
              <br/>
              <a href="https://maps.google.com?daddr=350+Hudson+Ave+Twp+of+Washington+07676" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
               Directions to Meeting
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              <!-- Regular meeting end -->
             </p>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </tbody>
         </table>
         <p>
          Welcome to the Bergen Amateur Radio Association’s homepage! BARA is a general interest amateur radio club located in Bergen County, New Jersey. Club meetings are held at the
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=350+Hudson+avenue,Twp.+of+Washington+07676&amp;entry=gmail&amp;source=g" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
           Township of Washington Borough Hall Senior center
          </a>
          meeting room, on the first Sunday of every month. Guests and prospective members are always welcome. Meetings start around 7:30 PM. We also
          <a href="/bulletin/" title="Bulletin">
           publish a monthly club bulletin
          </a>
          to keep members who cannot attend meetings up-to-date. If you would like further information about BARA, or are interested in membership,
          <a href="/contact/" title="Contact">
           see the Contact page
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          If you can’t attend meetings you can still participate by
          <a href="/weekly-nets/" title="Weekly Nets">
           joining one of our weekly nets
          </a>
          . Check out the details for all our active nets on the
          <a href="/weekly-nets/" title="Weekly Nets">
           Weekly Nets page
          </a>
          . We encourage all Amateurs both members and non-members to join in these nets and keep the airways open and to promote friendly interaction.
         </p>
         <p>
          BARA is proud to be an ARRL Special Service Club and supports many activities:
         </p>
         <p>
          As a Special Service Club BARA has hosted several division conventions. Most recently we hosted the Hudson Division Dinner. The funds raised from this event were donated to the ARRL Spectrum Defense fund.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our Public Service committee provides communications for parades and other special events.
         </p>
         <p>
          We provide members with an outgoing QSL bureau headed by Warren Hager K2UFM. Each month Warren sends several pounds of cards to the ARRL for distribution.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our
          <a href="/ve-exams/" title="VE Exams">
           Volunteer Examiner (VE) program
          </a>
          is one of the oldest on the East Coast. Call Don Younger, W2JEK at 201-265-6583 or
          <a href="/ve-exams/" title="VE Exams">
           contact Don on the VE Exam page
          </a>
          for further information.
         </p>
         <p>
          Every Tuesday is “construction night”. We meet at the Westwood High School or Senior Center in the Township of Washington depending on the time of year. If you are interested in electronics projects or just enjoy the smell of molten solder you can
          <a href="/calendar/">
           join the fun at 7:00PM and check the calendar for the current location
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          BARA operates Field Day each year – see BARA Field Day for the latest info on our current location. Our “Weak Signal Group” participates in the January, June and September ARRL VHF Contests each year. For the Field Day and VHF contests we use the club call of K2BAR. In addition, we also encourage individual member participation in A.R.R.L. contests. When a contest has a “CLUB” category, our members submit their logs to be included.
         </p>
         <p>
          The
          <a href="/hamfest/" title="Hamfest">
           semi-annual Hamfests in May and October
          </a>
          are well-known throughout the Northeast. The next Hamfest will be at the Westwood Regional High School in the Township of Washington. Admission is only $5.00! Over 100 vendors and nearly 1000 buyers make this one of the areas largest ham radio events. Please visit our
          <a href="/hamfest/" title="Hamfest">
           Hamfest page for more info
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Still more opportunities for electronic bargains can be found at
          <a href="/auction/" title="Auction">
           our annual auction
          </a>
          held every March during our regular monthly meeting.
         </p>
         <p>
          So there you have it. At BARA you will find QRP enthusiasts, antenna specialists, key collectors, digital mode gurus and more. Our members have had articles published in QST and the “ARRL Antenna Compendium”. What ever your question is, technical or operating, at a BARA meeting you’ll generally find several experienced hams ready to field it.
         </p>
         <p>
          Why not come join us and have some Fun?
         </p>
         <p>
          Our
          <a href="/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/BARA_Membership.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
           membership form [PDF] is available online
          </a>
          and at all meetings.
         </p>
