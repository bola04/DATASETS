              </a>
             </h3>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Having reviewed thousands of rules that impact the business community since its inception, Ohio’s
              <a href="http://governor.ohio.gov/Priorities-and-Initiatives/Common-Sense-Initiative">
               <strong>

               </strong>
              </a>
              (CSI) has provided a model for addressing dated regulations.  Last Tuesday, the Greater Cleveland Partnership (GCP) hosted CSI’s third Small Business Advisory Council meeting of the year.  The goal?  Shine additional light on a necessary, but often overlooked part of government – state regulatory requirements – and support the Northeast Ohio business community through continued representation on the CSI Small Business Advisory Council.
             </p>

             <h3>
              <a class="title" href="/Stay Informed on News and Policy/Newsroom/News Highlights/2018/July/GCP Supports State Regulatory Reform Legislation">
               GCP Supports State Regulatory Reform Legislation
              </a>
             </h3>
             <p>
              The Greater Cleveland Partnership (GCP) has been a long-time advocate for regulatory reform and continues to be a strong supporter of Ohio’s
              <a href="http://governor.ohio.gov/Priorities-and-Initiatives/Common-Sense-Initiative">
               <strong>
                Common Sense Initiative
               </strong>
              </a>
              (CSI).
              <br/>
              Our members see value in
              <a href="https://www.legislature.ohio.gov/legislation/legislation-summary?id=GA132-SB-293">
               <strong>
                Senate Bill 293
               </strong>
              </a>
              (SB 293), legislation that would require state agencies to review and reduce regulatory restrictions by 30% over the course of three years.  On June 27th,
              <a href="~/media/D91D4C8B0D5C4860BD1ECCDCC2F32D32.ashx">
               <strong>
                GCP presented proponent testimony
               </strong>
              </a>
              on the bill.  The Ohio Senate passed SB 293 the same day.
              <br/>
