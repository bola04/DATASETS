    <b>
     <font color="#000099">
      <font size="+3">
       Who??
      </font>
     </font>
    </b>
    <br/>
    <b>
     <font size="+1">
      I
 am
 a middle-aged newspaper editor and former paramedic who lives in the isolated
 desert
 community of Ajo, Arizona. An animal lover in general, I am
 particularly enamored
 of cats and have been owned by some of the finest felines ever to
 rumble
 a purr.
     </font>
    </b>
    <p>
     <b>
      <font color="#000099">
       <font size="+3">
        What??
       </font>
      </font>
     </b>
     <br/>
     <a href="cats/cats.html">
      <img align="left" alt="Cat" border="0" height="113" hspace="6" nosave="" src="cats/bcat.gif" vspace="1" width="130"/>
     </a>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       I'm a cat lover
 first
 &amp; foremost. I can't remember a time when I didn't have a cat in my
 life. 
 At the moment, I'm owned by
       <a href="cats/cats.html">
        Ralph, Fred,
 Alice, Jane, Ricky, Mary, Judith, Claude, and Lilah Marie
       </a>
       ,
 a clowder who keep the house well covered with discarded
 fur. They aren't the only animals in my life, though. There are the
 dogs -- Bruno, Chuy, and visting corgi Naru -- plus assorted turtles and fish. They
 all demand, and sometimes get, their fair share of my attention.
       <br/>
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://www.cunews.info">
      <img align="right" alt="Ajo Copper News" border="0" height="139" hspace="6" src="paper.gif" vspace="1" width="154"/>
     </a>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       I'm al
       <font color="#000000">
        so a newspaper editor.  In
 1983, I succeeded my mother
 as editor of the
        <a href="http://www.cunews.info/index.html">
         Ajo
 Copper News
        </a>
       </font>
       ,
 a small rural weekly in southwestern Arizona,
       <font color="#000000">
        and
 also
 took over her column "Quacks".  I run the
       </font>
       paper with my
 brother
       <a href="http://clowder.net/hop">
        Hop,
       </a>
       who stepped into our
 father's
 shoes as publisher.  Our brother Joe managed to escape for many
 years but is now retired from his job in Alabama and back on the
 newspaper staff.  I
 sometimes
 wonder if we own the newspaper, or if it owns us.
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://ajoambulance.com">
      <img align="left" alt="Ambulance" border="0" height="106" src="ambu.gif" width="192"/>
     </a>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       My second job, until recently, was a
       <font color="#000000">
        s a paramedic. 
 I got started late -- I didn't
 become
 an EMT until 1991 when I was in my forties.   I worked two days
 a
 week for
        <a href="http://ajoambulance.com">
         Ajo Ambulance
        </a>
       </font>
       <a href="www.ajoambulnce.com">
       </a>
       , a non-profit company I helped
 start, until the summer of 2012 when sad circumstance dictated I retire from the field.  
 It was hard work, frequently dirty and often thankless, but I enjoyed
 it. I wish I could still do it.
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <img align="right" alt="Books" border="0" height="108" src="books.gif" width="75"/>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       Books
 have been part of
 my life almost as long as cats. My  childhood was spent in a
 family
 whose noses were all buried firmly in books (we didn't have a TV until
 I
 was about eight) and it seemed perfectly natural to me to lose myself
 between
 the covers of my favorite books. The earliest ones were The Bobbsey
 Twins
 and from there I went on to devour Nancy Drew, Cherry Ames, and other
 preteen
 fare. For most of my adult life I've read cozy mysteries, fantasy, and
 history
 -- though dips into other genres are the rule rather than the
 exception.
       <br/>
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       I've added breast
 cancer survivor to my list. I found the lump the day before
 Thanksgiving 2007. Diagnosis followed rapidly with a bilateral
 mastectomy in January 2008 and then six months of chemotherapy. It's a
 rough road to go down but at least I'm here to do it.
      </font>
     </b>
     <br/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="Family/album.html">
      <img align="left" alt="Family Album" hspace="6" src="album.gif" style="border: 0px solid ; width: 65px; height: 63px;" vspace="1"/>
     </a>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       I'm not yet an
 eccentric
 old lady surrounded by cats and books and shunning human
 companionship. 
 I have family who are a big part of my life: my daughter
 Paula with
 her husband Joey and my granddaughters Ashlee &amp; Danielle, my son
 Gery and his daughter Chloe,
 my
 brother Joe, my brother Hop &amp; his family. I am also
 researching my roots and am gradually building a
       <a href="Family/History/TreeLinks.html">
        family history
       </a>
       section for my
 pages.
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <a href="http://www.cunews.info/ajoinfo.html">
      <img align="right" alt="Where is Ajo?" border="0" height="125" hspace="2" src="AjoAZUSA.gif" width="194"/>
     </a>
     <b>
      <font color="#003399">
       <font size="+2">
        Where??
       </font>
      </font>
     </b>
     <br/>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       <a href="http://www.cunews.info/ajoinfo.html">
        Ajo
       </a>
       is a
 small
 town in southwestern Arizona, about 40 miles north of the Mexican
 border,
 in the middle of the great Sonoran Desert.  Ajo was a mining town
 for
 most of its life, then became a retirement community when the copper
 mine
 was closed in the early 1980s.
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      <font color="#000099">
       <font size="+2">
        Why??
       </font>
      </font>
     </b>
     <br/>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       That's an even smaller town about eleven miles southeast of Ajo. The
 highway branches there to head south to Mexico or east to Tucson.
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      <font color="#000099">
       <font size="+2">
        When??
       </font>
      </font>
     </b>
     <br/>
     <b>
      <font size="+1">
       I was born in 1947 to Richard F. &amp; Ann Hollister David.  After
 floating
 around for many years, I ended up in Ajo in 1980.   I plan to
 remain
 here a long, long time.
      </font>
     </b>
    </p>
