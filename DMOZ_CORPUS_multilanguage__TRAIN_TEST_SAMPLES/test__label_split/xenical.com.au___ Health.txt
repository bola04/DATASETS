     <h2>
      Does XENICAL (orlistat) have side effects?
     </h2>
     <p>
      As with any medication, there can be unwanted side effects with XENICAL.
     </p>
     <p>
      With XENICAL, these are usually related to the digestive system.  Tell your pharmacist or doctor if you notice any of the following and they worry you:
      <sup>
       1
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Increased flatulence (wind) with or without discharge.
      </li>
      <li>
       Abdominal pain.
      </li>
      <li>
       Urgent need to open the bowels.
      </li>
      <li>
       Oily, fatty or liquid stools.
      </li>
      <li>
       Oily discharge from anus.
      </li>
      <li>
       Rectal pain or discomfort.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      These symptoms are generally mild, occur at the beginning of treatment, go away after a short period of time and are particularly experienced after meals containing high levels of fat. Normally, these symptoms disappear if you continue treatment and keep to your recommended diet.
      <sup>
       1
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Tips to help avoid these side effects:
       <sup>
        1,2
       </sup>
      </strong>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Avoid meals that are very high in fat.
      </li>
      <li>
       Your daily intake of fat, carbohydrate and protein should be distributed over three main meals.
      </li>
      <li>
       Eat a nutritionally balanced diet, rich in fruit and vegetables, containing approximately 30% of calories from fat.
      </li>
      <li>
       Reduce your dietary saturated fats, while maintaining an adequate intake of unsaturated fats, including omega fatty acids.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Taking XENICAL doesn’t mean giving up everything you enjoy. You’ll soon learn the foods to avoid and how to eat less fat without giving up everything you enjoy.
     </p>
     <p>
      For more information on side effects of XENICAL, talk to your pharmacist or doctor, or refer to the XENICAL Consumer Medicine Information via the link on this page, or see the leaflet inside the XENICAL box.
     </p>
