              <h1 style="text-align: center;">
               About Saignon
              </h1>
              <h6 style="text-align: center;">
               <span style="color: #f6d284;">
                <strong>
                 Saignon is in the heart of the Luberon perched on the edge of the Luberon National Park
                </strong>
               </span>
              </h6>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               It’s perfectly placed as a base from which to tour the vineyards, the markets and the beautiful nearby villages of Bonnieux, Loumarin, Gordes, Lacoste and Menerbes. Within the village there’s a magnificent 12th century church, many Roman ruins and cobbled stoned streets, La Chapelle de St Jean as well as a bakery, general store and some small restaurants.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               While walking through the narrow streets you will discover ancient fountains, picturesque shady squares and beautiful old houses with intricate doorways.
               <br/>
               Easy access to the rock of Saignon Do not hesitate to climb to the top of the rock where there is a fabulous panoramic view of the plains and the Luberon mountain range. If the weather is fine, you can see Mont Ventoux, the Lure mountain, the Alpes de Haute Provence and even the heights of Avignon!
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               Thanks to its rock, Saignon played a key defensive role for the town of Apt, attacked through the centuries by numerous invasions… although not yet by tourists! Saignon is still relatively unknown so you can appreciate the beauty of the countryside and discover delightful walking trails in the surroundings and around Mont Luberon.
              </p>
