        Self Portrait – Sir Henry Raeburn
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p class="comments">
       <a href="https://stampsonline.wordpress.com/2010/01/17/self-portrait-sir-henry-raeburn/#respond">
        leave a comment »
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="main">
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" data-shortcode="caption" id="attachment_206" style="width: 389px">
        <a href="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-206" data-attachment-id="206" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="Self Portrait – Sir Henry Raeburn" data-large-file="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg?w=700?w=379" data-medium-file="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg?w=700?w=235" data-orig-file="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg?w=700" data-orig-size="379,483" data-permalink="https://stampsonline.wordpress.com/2010/01/17/self-portrait-sir-henry-raeburn/uk1-raeburn-1/" sizes="(max-width: 379px) 100vw, 379px" src="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg?w=700" srcset="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg 379w, https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg?w=118 118w, https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-raeburn-1.jpg?w=235 235w" title="Self Portrait - Sir Henry Raeburn"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Great Britain stamp issued 1973, Painted c.1815
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         Sir Henry Raeburn (1756-1823), the Scottish portrait painter, was born at Stockbridge, Edinburgh, where he was apprenticed to a goldsmith but taught himself to paint miniatures and then extended his practice to oil-painting.  His method was free and vigorous and he was not given to flattery.  Aiming at character, he succeeded in impressing it upon his canvas and excelled in male portraiture.  In 1815, he bacame a full member of the Royal Academy in London where he exhibited his paintings regularly.  He was knighted in 1822 and soon afterwards appointed His Majesty’s Limner for Scotland, but died the following year.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         This painting is one of the finest examples of his fully matured style.  It was painted c.1815 and is now in the National Gallery of Scotland
        </em>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Source: First Day Cover, British Post Office Philatelic Bureau.
       </p>

       <a href="https://stampsonline.wordpress.com/2010/01/17/self-portrait-sir-joshua-reynolds/" rel="bookmark">
        Self Portrait – Sir Joshua Reynolds
       </a>
      </h2>
      <p class="comments">
       <a href="https://stampsonline.wordpress.com/2010/01/17/self-portrait-sir-joshua-reynolds/#respond">
        leave a comment »
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="main">
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" data-shortcode="caption" id="attachment_197" style="width: 380px">
        <a href="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-197" data-attachment-id="197" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="Self Portrait – Sir Joshua Reynolds" data-large-file="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg?w=700?w=370" data-medium-file="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg?w=700?w=216" data-orig-file="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg?w=700" data-orig-size="370,515" data-permalink="https://stampsonline.wordpress.com/2010/01/17/self-portrait-sir-joshua-reynolds/uk1-reynolds-1/" sizes="(max-width: 370px) 100vw, 370px" src="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg?w=700" srcset="https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg 370w, https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg?w=108 108w, https://stampsonline.files.wordpress.com/2010/01/uk1-reynolds-1.jpg?w=216 216w" title="Self Portrait - Sir Joshua Reynolds"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Great Britain Stamp issued 1974, Painting completed 1773
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        The artist is portrayed in his robes as DCL Oxford, and the bust isof Michalangelo.  It was painted for the Royal Academy c.1773 and now hangs at the Royal Academy of Arts.
       </p>
       <p>
        Sir Joshua Reynolds, historically the most important figure of British painting was born at Plympton in Devonshire, but established in London from 1753.  The fundamental basis of his art was the deliberate allusion to the classical, and the aim was to become a scholar in his profession.  He was the first President of the Royal Academy, founded in 1768, and his Discourses to students are renowned for literary merit and art teaching.  Knighted in 1769 and made Doctor of Civil Law at Oxford in 1773, he died at the age of 68 and was buried in St Paul’s Cathedral.
       </p>
