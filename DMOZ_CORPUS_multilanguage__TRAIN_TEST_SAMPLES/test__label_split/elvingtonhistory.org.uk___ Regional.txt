   <p align="center">

     <b>
      YORKSHIRE
     </b>
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <font face="Century Schoolbook">
     Elvington is a neat parish 
 town, 7 miles from York, with a population of 405 persons.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <font face="Century Schoolbook">
     The Church is a Rectory, 
 dedicated to the Holy Trinity, and valued in the Liber regis at £5 17s 8and a 
 half pence. The Patronage is in the King.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <font face="Century Schoolbook">
     It is a modern edifice of 
 brick with stone quoins, and consists of a nave, chancel, and embattled west end 
 tower. The east end finishes with a semi-hexagon, and all the windows are 
 pointed in a semi-gothic style. The interior is neat, and has a gallery at the 
 west end. This church was erected in 1801 by the Rev A Cheap LLB, Rector.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <font face="Century Schoolbook">
     The village is small but neat, 
 and contains 2 chapels, one for the Wesleyan Methodists erected about 20 years 
 ago, and another for the Primitive Methodists, built in 1821.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <font face="Century Schoolbook">
     There is a school for the 
 education of 20 boys, the master of which has £20 per annum.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p align="center">
    <font face="Century Schoolbook">
     J Spence Esq is Lord of the 
 Manor
    </font>
   </p>
