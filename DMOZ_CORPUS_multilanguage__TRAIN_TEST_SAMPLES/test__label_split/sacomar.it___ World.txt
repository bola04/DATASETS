           <span class="Stile2">

           <span class="Stile2">
            Agency

           <span class="Stile2">
            island of Elba

           <h4>
            Pearls of Venus
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="clear">
          </div>
          <p>
           <span class="Stile2">
            The Arcipelago Toscano National Park
           </span>
           is the largest marine park in Europe.
           <br/>
           It includes the seven main islands of the Archipelago, some minor islands and
 								  cliffs. Like real pearls in a necklace, the islands differ from one another. Each
 								  though preserves traces of its history, each is unique; their common bond is the
 							    beauty of their natural setting.
           <br/>
           <span class="Stile2">
            Elba is the largest island of the Archipelago
           </span>
           : its sea, its beaches, the green of the
 								  countryside, the tiny fortress towns, archaeological treasures, chestnut forests
 								  and more have survived over the centuries and continue to flourish.
           <br/>
           On the island of Elba one gets the feeling of “good goods in small parcels” since
 								  one can travel through so may different landscapes and attractions, often in
 								  contrast with each other: areas scorched by sun and wind alongside others
 								  luxuriant and green with life, sandy and pebbled beaches, rocky coastlines and
 						      sheer cliffs.
          </p>

 The quays are equipped with supply of electricity and water, assistance during mooring.

              Rules and Regulation
             </h4>
             <br/>
             <p>
              Important rules regulating the access of the harbour:
             </p>
             <ul class="arrow_list">
              <li>
               Pleasure crafts must not hinder or obstruct manoeuvres or routes of ships entering and leaving the port as they always have precedence
              </li>
              <li>
               In
               <span class="Stile2">
                the access canal situated in the W part of the gulf
               </span>
               and shown on nautical maps, vessels leaving the port must keep lo the left.
              </li>
              <li>
               Prohibition of anchorage must be observed in the port area (W of the adjoining "Martello" Tower - The "Grotte" Point) anchorage is prohibited everywhere except in an area to the S.
              </li>
              <li>
               <span class="Stile2">
                Arriving from W
               </span>
               . to avoid the shallows of "Capo Blanco" about 1 mile WNN from the lighthouse,
               <span class="Stile2">
                go N of the cardinal light buoy positioned there
               </span>

          <h4 class="Stile1">
           The Marciana Marina Harbour
          </h4>
          <br/>
          <p>
           <stong>
            The little harbour has a sea-wall 330 metres long which shelters the port from high winds.
            <span class="Stile2">
             Marciana Marina
            </span>
            is an interesting departure point for the nearby
            <span class="Stile2">
             island of Capraia
            </span>
            (22 miles away) and for the coast of
            <span class="Stile2">
             Capo Corso in Corsica
            </span>
            ( 30 miles away).
            <br/>
           </stong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="Stile2">
            The touristic harbour of Marciana Marina
           </span>
           allows the mooring of
           <span class="Stile2">
            ships up to 50 m. of length
           </span>
           (even alongside).
          </p>
          The approach presents no particular difficulty. You recognize
          <span class="Stile2">
           Marciana Marina
          </span>
          from its long sea-wall with the round shaped yellow-ocre coloured
          <span class="Stile2">
           Medicean tower
          </span>
          dating from the 16th Century easily visible. The clear-coloured houses of the village with their red-tiled roofs follow along the whole of the southern water-front of the port.
          <p>
           Fresh vater and elettricity are available.
          </p>
