      <h2>
       Khiva - the City Museum
      </h2>
      During XI and XII centuries, Khiva was a small town-fortress. Like other towns of Khorezm, Khiva was destroyed by Mongolian invation. From the second half of XVI century as the capital of Khorezm, Khiva became one of the important cities of the Central Asia. In XVIII century Khiva was ruined over nomad's forays, ruinous intestine wars, and invasion of the Iranian Army.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      At the beginning of 19th century a new dynasty of rulers came to power. This period is characterized by a great construction works. Majolica, marble, paintings, carving was widely used in construction of many architectural monuments in Khiva: minarets, mosques, and madrassahs.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Skillful craftsmen were engaged in decoration of new buildings with carved doors and columns and beautiful ornaments.
 In accordance with the medieval architectural traditions, Khiva was divided into the inner town Ichan Kala, where Khan had his residence and were most of the monuments were constructed, and Dishan Kala, the outer town, where most of the citizens lived and worked.
