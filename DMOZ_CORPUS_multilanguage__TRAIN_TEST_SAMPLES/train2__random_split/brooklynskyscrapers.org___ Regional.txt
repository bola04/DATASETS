     <b>
      <font size="5">
       Established in 1936, the Brooklyn Skyscrapers 
     is one of the oldest model airplane clubs in the country. The club still has 
     a large membership, although not one single member resides in the borough 
     of Brooklyn, N.Y. The club has retained this name for two reasons, tradition 
     and chutzpah! Tradition, because of the clubs origin. And chutzpah because 
     of the clubs old battle cry, "Brooklyn voises da woild." Also known 
     as the most active
       <img align="RIGHT" height="198" naturalsizeflag="3" src="sky1.gif" width="185"/>
       club in the northeast, the clubs members still 
     travel to all parts of the country to compete in free flight model airplane 
     contests. Today the Skyscrapers have members living in all parts of the United 
     States, but the core members still live in the N.Y.C. Tri-state area. Photographs 
     of modeling activity from 1939 and 1957 can be seen below along with new photographs 
     showing today's members. Sort of a photographic history showing sixty years 
     of modeling's "good ole' days". Today the Skyscrapers fly in Wawayanda, 
     NY on the Bryton Barron Memorial Flying Field. Fellow Skyscraper member, Andrew 
     Barron purchased this amazing sod farm / flying field and has been kind enough 
     to let us all use it. The field is approximately 800 acres of flat sod and 
     some corn. This is probably the finest free flight flying site in the Northeast.
      </font>
