       <p>
        Spinning the Web brings together for the first time a unique collection of some 20,000 items from the libraries, museums and archives of North West England which tell the story of the Lancashire Cotton Industry. Spinning the Web invites users to search the collection (see
        <a href="help.php">
         Help
        </a>
        to find out how) or to explore these extraordinary times through a series of themes:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/overview/">
          Overview - an account of the period between 1760 to the
          <br/>
          present day
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/places/">
          Places - the impact of cotton on villages, towns and cities
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/people/">
          People - living and working in the mills
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/industry/">
          Industry - how cotton was made and sold
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/clothing/">
          Clothing &amp; products - uses of cotton
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        For learners there are a series of Learning Journeys - find out more about Local History Studies in
        <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/places/">
         Places
        </a>
        , Children in Victorian Times in
        <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/people/">
         People
        </a>
        or Industrialisation in
        <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/industry/">
         Industry
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Also you can
        <a href="http://www.spinningtheweb.org.uk/people/sendcard.php">
         Send a postcard
        </a>
        from our Leisure section.
       </p>
       <p>
        Spinning the Web is funded by the
        <a href="http://www.nof.org.uk/">
         New Opportunities Fund
        </a>
        and developed by
        <a href="http://www.manchester.gov.uk/libraries/">
         Manchester Library and Information Service
        </a>
        in partnership with local museums, libraries and archives. It is one of four textile related projects - see the
        <a href="http://www.textilehistory.org.uk">
         Consortium Web Page
        </a>
        for details.
       </p>
