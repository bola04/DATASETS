           Mifflinburg (PA) Brew Pub is Right on Track!
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          Posted by
          <span class="author vcard">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          It looks like things are starting to roll for the much-anticipated
          <strong>
           Rusty Rail Brewing Company
          </strong>
          (a.k.a. Rusty Rail Brew Haus) in Mifflinburg, PA. A logo has been released and the job of
          <a href="http://jobview.monster.com/Executive-Chef-Job-Mifflinburg-PA-US-130648449.aspx" target="_blank" title="Apply today!">
           Executive Chef
          </a>
          has been posted. But the thing I really want to know about is the beer!
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png">
           <img alt="RustyRail" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-1082" data-attachment-id="1082" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="RustyRail" data-large-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png?w=458" data-medium-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png?w=290" data-orig-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png" data-orig-size="458,474" data-permalink="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2014/02/26/rustyrail/rr1/" height="150" sizes="(max-width: 144px) 100vw, 144px" src="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png?w=144&amp;h=150" srcset="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png?w=144&amp;h=150 144w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/rr1.png?w=288&amp;h=298 288w" style="margin:5px;" width="144"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          I’m here to tell you I still know nothing, but thought I’d spread the word anyway. Feel free to comment on this post if you have insight to the brewer, the brewing styles or any other plans related to the brewhouse and brewing process.
         </p>
         <p>
          The location is a spacious three-story brick factory lined with windows and featuring exposed wood beams inside. It is located off 8th Street adjacent to the new Buffalo Valley Rail Trail on property owned by Ritz-Craft Corporation. The building was constructed in the late 1800’s and once used as a wooden truck body manufacturing facility. It looks perfect for a multi-story brewing facility and restaurant complex!
         </p>
         <p>
          Mifflinburg has a great transportation history. In 1825, Mifflinburg served as a major point on a Turnpike spanning from Bellefonte to Northumberland. But Mifflinburg, also known as “Buggytown, USA”, is most famous for the buggy manufacturing that boomed between 1840 and the early 1900’s. In 1899, multiple buggy works made more than 2,000 buggies, carriages and sleighs, which were sold all across the United States.
         </p>
         <p>
          The first railroad between Mifflinburg and Lewisburg was built in 1871. Elaine Wintjen of the Union County Historical Society reports that by 1885, the Lewisburg Center and Spruce Railroad Company ran to Laurelton, Spring Mills and Lemont, where it connected with the Bellefonte and Tyrone Railroad. Using the “cow catcher” of an old steam locomotive in the logo, Rusty Rail Brewing Company celebrates this often overlooked piece of Mifflinburg’s history.
         </p>
         <p>
          With all of this in mind, the operators indicate the theme of Rusty Rail is “turn of the century, industrial revolution, with a craft beer, eclectic feel.” They state that the restaurant will include quality craft beer and scratch-made food with entertainment in a fun, unique atmosphere. They have been in communication with Borough Council to review plans since at least July 2013, and locals tell me it will be at least a full year from then – maybe more – until it opens.
         </p>
         <p>
          I’m looking forward to learning more, and hoping Rusty Rail will prove to be another Susquehanna Valley destination brewery and pub!
         </p>

          Tonight I consumed a cider that reminded me of Willy Wonka’s Inventing Room gum that doubled as a three course meal (remember Violet Beauregarde?)

         <p>
          Well, close to it. If you like the sweet / dessert part of meals. Which I do!
         </p>
         <p>
          After trying a 50/50 blend of
          <a href="http://www.stbcbeer.com/black-water/choklat/" target="_blank" title="Choklat">
           <strong>
            Southern Tier
           </strong>
           Choklat
          </a>
          with
          <a href="http://www.ratebeer.com/beer/pizza-boy-coconut-cream-ale/240095/" target="_blank" title="Pizza Boy Coconut Cream Ale">
           <strong>
            Pizza Boy
           </strong>
           Coconut Cream Ale
          </a>
          (and I couldn’t stop singing “sometimes you feel like a nut…Almond Joy!”), I was pleased to catch a glass of a new cider from
          <strong>
           The Vineyard at Hershey
          </strong>
          . As you may or may not know, it is against the law in PA for a brewery to make a cider, but it is fine to do at a winery, so thank goodness
          <a href="http://vineyardathershey.com/the-winery/" target="_blank" title="The Winery at Hershey">
           The Vineyard at Hershey
          </a>
          /
          <a href="http://vineyardathershey.com/the-brewery/" target="_blank" title="The Brewery">
           The Brewery at Hershey
          </a>
          can pump out all three!
         </p>
         <p>
          I haven’t had much to say about The Brewery since it opened in March of this year (The Vineyard opened in 2012 and that was totally off my radar), but I also find myself blogging about every seven months, so don’t take my silence as not noticing.
         </p>
         <p>
          The folks at The Vineyard / The Brewery were integral in starting up
          <a href="http://www.hersheyharrisburgbeer.com/" target="_blank" title="Harrisburg Hershey Craft Beer Country">
           Hershey Harrisburg Craft Beer Country
          </a>
          – a marketing initiative to promote craft beer and beer education in the area (read more about it at
          <a href="http://www.abc27.com/story/23419475/local-breweries-band-to-promote-tourism" target="_blank" title='"Local breweries band to promote tourism"'>
           abc27
          </a>
          !) and prominent figures in the hosting of the
          <strong>
           Central Pennsylvania Oktoberfest at Penn National Race Course
          </strong>
          (can you find me
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/HersheyHarrisburgCraftBeerCountry/posts/665963846756662" target="_blank" title="Photos from the Oktoberfest on the HHCBC website">
           in these photos
          </a>
          ?) They also hosted a series of summer concerts, labeled the Decked-Out Live Entertainment Series, which we enjoyed on those rare Fridays at home. I can certainly appreciate their enthusiasm and willingness to organize people for education and entertainment.
         </p>
         <p>
          But here it is autumn…almost winter…and I’m looking for fall treats when this glass gets passed my way. It had about 4 remaining sips of
          <a href="https://twitter.com/VineyardHershey/status/405728900887904256/photo/1" target="_blank" title="Want to see their Twitter Pic? It's a good one!">
           <strong>
            The DeCider
           </strong>
          </a>
          <strong>
           – Peanut Butter Caramel Apple Cider
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Strange, but true, this is hard apple cider that starts off apple…then fills in with sweet, yummy caramel…and ends with roasty, rich peanut butter flavor. I’m serious when I say I felt like a three-course meal! What an interesting combination.
          <a href="https://twitter.com/VineyardHershey/status/403178185262915584" target="_blank">
           Their Twitter feed
          </a>
          accurately states, “The DeCider leads taste buds in moments of indecision &amp; crisis.” If you like
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/peanut-butter-chocolate/" title="PBC">
           peanut butter
          </a>
          as much as I do, you’ll enjoy this cider.
         </p>
         <p>
          My nephew is still too young to enjoy it with me, but the artwork did lead to a short talk on politics and history (and just a moment of wondering if his parents were sitting in The
          <em>
           Hall of Presidents
          </em>
          at Magic Kingdom while we ate pizza at home), and an even longer lesson in the differences between wine, beer, cider and distilled beverages.
         </p>
         <p>
          So he got an education and I got a treat. And a bottle to take home and try again!
         </p>
         <p>
          If you get a chance, I recommend it. I couldn’t survive on the stuff, but it certainly is an amalgamation of “yummmm” worthy of purchasing and sharing a bottle during this cider season. Keep being inventive, The Vineyard / The Brewery, and I’ll keep tasting the products as you roll them out!
         </p>

       <article class="post-991 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-beer-education category-craft-brewed-beers category-pennsylvania" id="post-991">
        <header class="entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title">
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2013/05/04/not-a-beer-week/" rel="bookmark">
           You Call That a “Beer Week”?
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          Posted by
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/author/pubcrawler/" title="View all posts by pubcrawler">
            pubcrawler
           </a>
          </span>
          in
          <span class="cat-links">
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/beer-education/" rel="category tag">
            Beer Education
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/craft-brewed-beers/" rel="category tag">
            Craft Brewed Beers
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/united-states/pennsylvania/" rel="category tag">
            Pennsylvania
           </a>
          </span>
          <a class="entry-date-link" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2013/05/04/not-a-beer-week/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to You Call That a “Beer Week”?">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-05-04T11:00:13+00:00" pubdate="">
            May 4, 2013
           </time>
          </a>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Three of the best beer weeks are on the horizon!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           May 25 – June 1
          </strong>
          is
          <a href="http://ashevillebeerweek.com/" target="_blank" title="Asheville Beer Week - YES!!!">
           Asheville Beer Week
          </a>
          . This annual event “celebrating a vital (and delicious) business (and pleasure) sector in WNC—craft beer; supported by main sponsor, Asheville Brewers Alliance – an all-volunteer committee of regional beer industry people” will be amazing. It is held in the town also known as Beer City, USA. And the week will include seminars, tastings, dinners, and special events with “beer celebrities” from around the country and the world.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           May 31 – June 9
          </strong>
          is
          <a href="http://www.phillybeerweek.org/" target="_blank" title="Philly Beer Week - the BEST!">
           Philly Beer Week
          </a>
          . This is the largest beer celebration of its kind in America, “featuring hundreds of festivals, dinners, tours, pub crawls, tastings and meet-the-brewer nights to area bars, restaurants and other locations throughout Greater Philadelphia.” It is organized and operated by Philly Beer Week Inc., a non-profit 501(c)(6) organization overseen by a board composed of brewery owners, distributors, restaurant owners and others, to promote Philadelphia’s beer and hospitality industries. It is the grand-daddy of all beer weeks.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           June 6 – June 16
          </strong>
          is
          <a href="http://pdxbeerweek.com/" target="_blank" title="Portland Beer Week - Yum!">
           Portland Beer Week
          </a>
          . Held in the city with more breweries per capita than any other in the US, Portland Beer Week “aims to celebrate and expand that culture, not just by promoting Portland brewers and breweries, but tap houses, restaurants, food, and underexplored beer styles. Craft beer culture is not just about the beer itself but about the people, food, camaraderie, and education that comes along with this art form.”
         </p>
         <h3>
          Harrisburg Beer Week, you say?
         </h3>
         <p>
          And now a Facebook ad tells me there is one coming to my area! But when I looked at the web site, I turned to my Best Beer Buddy and said, “You call THAT a Beer Week?”
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           June 10 – June 15
          </strong>
          is
          <strong>
           Downtown Harrisburg Beer Week
          </strong>
          . On the web site, it is described as “a week of beer sampling and entertainment…Enjoy great beer, food, promotions and entertainment all week at different bars in Downtown Harrisburg.”
         </p>
         <p>
          Really. Kamionka Entertainment Group?
         </p>
         <p>
          I hate to be negative about a Beer Week in my own backyard, but promoting nightly events
          <a href="http://downtownafterdark.com/" target="_blank" title='Kamionka"s Empire'>
           at four establishments that you own
          </a>
          , serving sample glasses of beer that are imported to the Harrisburg area does NOT make it a “Harrisburg Beer Week”. In naming it such, you have already tarnished future efforts of any reputable group of beer lovers that may be interested in promoting local breweries and beer culture to the area under the label “Harrisburg Beer Week.”
         </p>
         <p>
          Distributor(s) may be involved by association because they supply the beer, but I suspect the breweries themselves have never heard of Harrisburg Beer Week and are unknowing participants. I’d be curious to learn if any of them were involved in the planning or promotion of their beers for this “event”. Kamionka Entertainment Group attempts to give this “event” credibility by including an annual
          <a href="http://www.troegs.com/" target="_blank" title="Troeg's Brewing Company - Hershey, PA">
           Tröeg’s
          </a>
          Brewing Company event without any collaboration or credit to
          <a href="http://www.troegs.com/" target="_blank" title="Troeg's Brewing Company - Hershey, PA">
           Tröeg’s
          </a>
          see
          <em>
           Improper Recognition of Efforts
          </em>
          below.)
         </p>
         <h2>
          5 Reasons I won’t be at HBW
         </h2>
         <p>
          Here are just five of the many reasons, in no particular order, that I will not be attending the series of events I will now refer to as
          <strong>
           Kamionka Beer Week in Downtown Harrisburg
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <h3>
          Local Breweries Are Not Featured
         </h3>
         <p>
          Beer people in the Harrisburg area are proud to have nationally recognized award winning beers and brewers, yet none of them are part of Kamionka Beer Week.
         </p>
         <p>
          Stoudts and Yards, while great PA breweries, are the only Pennsylvania breweries being featured. Kamionka Entertainment Group choose to feature Sierra Nevada (CA), Lagunitas (CA) and Leinenkugel (born in WI, but a wholly owned subsidiary of Miller Brewing Company), as well as serving samples of the Coors products Batch 19, Blue Moon, and Third Shift at a location that proclaims to carry 70 craft beers.
         </p>
         <p>
          We’ve got
          <a href="http://www.abcbrew.com/" target="_blank" title="Appalachian Brewing Company - Harrisburg, PA">
           Appalachian
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.lbcharrisburg.com/" target="_blank" title="Lancaster Brewing Company - Harrisburg, PA location">
           Lancaster
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.millbock.com/" target="_blank" title="Millbock Brewing Co. - Linglestown, PA">
           Millbock
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://www.pizzaboybrewing.com/" target="_blank" title="Pizza Boy Brewing Co. - Enola, PA">
           Pizza Boy
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://www.troegs.com/" target="_blank" title="Troeg's Brewing Company - Hershey, PA">
           Tröegs
          </a>
          , all right here in our backyard.
          <a href="http://www.marketcrosspub.com/" target="_blank" title="Market Cross - Carlisle, PA">
           Market Cross
          </a>
          isn’t too far away in Carlisle.
          <a href="http://www.liquidhero.com/" target="_blank" title="Liquid Hero - York, PA">
           Liquid Hero
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://mudhookbrewing.com/" target="_blank" title="Mudhook Brewing - York, PA">
           Mudhook
          </a>
          are in York, and both
          <a href="http://www.ironhillbrewery.com/lancaster/" target="_blank" title="Iron Hill - Lancaster, PA">
           Iron Hill
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://springhousebeer.com/" target="_blank" title="Spring House - Brewed in Connestoga, PA; Taproom in Lancaster">
           Spring House
          </a>
          are in Lancaster County. Those are just the best known breweries nearby – there are certainly more!
         </p>
         <p>
          Seriously, think about serving local beers – ones that can be obtained without an EZ Pass – when you are planning a Beer Week! I’m pretty sure any given night at
          <a href="http://www.pizzaboybrewing.com/on-tap/" target="_blank" title="Al's of Hampden beer list on Pizza Boy Brewing Web Site">
           Al’s of Hampden
          </a>
          would trump the local selection presented during Kamionka Beer Week.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Improper Recognition of Efforts
         </h3>
         <p>
          Dating this “Beer Week” through June 15 to include
          <a href="http://www.troegs.com/" target="_blank" title="Harrisburg Brewers Fest® ">
           Tröegs 10th Annual Harrisburg Brewers Fest®
          </a>
          is a cheap trick. I’ve poked around, and it doesn’t sound like Tröegs Brewing Company was even
          <strong>
           asked
          </strong>
          to be involved in this little “Harrisburg Beer Week”. The link on the Kamionka Beer Week page doesn’t even go to the Harrisburg Brewers Fest® event – it is bookmarked to take the user back to the top of their own promotions. This Fest isn’t the culminating event to HBW, like Asheville’s 4th Annual
          <a href="http://www.ashevillebrewersalliance.com/beer-city-festival" target="_blank" title="Beer City Festival">
           Beer City Festival
          </a>
          is to the ABW. It is an event that Tröegs Brewing Company and the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation of Central Pennsylvania have worked 10 years to make into a don’t-miss event, and Kamionka Entertainment Group knew it was the only thing that would draw in true beer culture to the Week.
         </p>
         <p>
          My advice? To run a successful Beew Week, take the time to build beer partnerships, and give those partners the recognition they deserve.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tröegs, by the way, just completed the brewing of a collaboration beer for Philly Beer Week called
          <a href="http://jackcurtin.com/ldo/?p=8620" target="_blank" title="Jack Curtain's info on Brotherly Suds 4">
           Brotherly Suds 4
          </a>
          (as in “year #4” of making it) with Yards, Nodding Head, Flying Fish, Stoudts, Victory and Sly Fox. If Kamionka Entertainment Group can get a glass of THAT in my hand during HBW at any location in Dauphin County, I will rescind this entire post.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Does not Demonstrate Knowledge of Beer and Breweries
         </h3>
         <p>
          Knowing beer people, breweries and beer are key aspects to organizing a successful beer week. As I read about the sample glasses of beer that will be featured each night of Kamionka Beer Week (that’s right – between 3 and 5 sample glasses each night instead of pints from various kegs and firkins of rare, limited or special release beers), I was particularly stuck by the generic descriptions, clearly pulled straight from the brewery’s marketing materials.
         </p>
         <p>
          One friend’s response, when discussing the beers being served, made me giggle in agreement:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           I laugh at their description of Lagunitas as ‘A battle tested brewery that honors old world traditions’. WTF?! Lagunitas Brewing consists a bunch of California weed-lovin’ ass-kickers who stuff as many hops as possible into a beer! The only thing ‘battle tested’ was their ability to survive a Federal imposed shut down. And as far as honoring traditions? I think the good people of Lagunitas would say, ‘F#*&amp; tradition, I got a nice big fatty and a 150 ibu IPA right here’!
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          In full disclosure, Lagunitas actually self-describes the brewery as “battle- tested”, but not in the noble way presented on the HBW site. Lagunitas says:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           From our earliest days of striving to make consistently good beer, and instead making beer that ranged from vile, to barely drinkable, to wonderful, to elegant, to questionable-at-best. From being castigated by our West Marin neighbors to finally suffering an ‘eviction’ by our West Marin septic system. From landing in the welcoming arms of Petaluma, and actually getting our beer into bottles, onto the streets, and into the hands of sympathetic beer geeks, to steadily losing less money each month. From all this and more,
           <strong>
            Lagunitas Brewing Company is emerging as a battle-tested brewery capable of making great beer out of goat’s milk, brambles, and asphalt on the surface of the Moon, if need be
           </strong>
           .
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Having been to the Lagunitas pub many a time myself, I get this. They are not about “old world traditions”.  They are about making amazing, creative and flavorful beers. I haven’t had any made with the “asphalt on the surface of the moon” yet, but I have had Imperial Stout that was brewed with cacao nibs and white peppercorns, then aged in used oak whiskey barrels distillery, with brettanomyces and Sonoma County cherries added before being drawn from a firkin at 4:20 PM. I guess the firkin part was “old word tradition”.
         </p>
         <p>
          By the way, if Kamionka Entertainment Group can get a glass of anything like that in my hand during HBW at any location in Dauphin County, I will again rescind every word I’ve said.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Lack of Diversity
         </h3>
         <p>
          By planning a “Beer Week” only involving the establishments he owns, Mr. Kamionka has limited the places and pints that could be game-changers in the success of a beer week.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           A beer week should increase the visibility of local beers through programs, events, and consumer education.
          </li>
          <li>
           A beer week should involve the area’s breweries, restaurants, pubs, caterers, retailers, entertainers, and other businesses with ties to the craft beer community.
          </li>
          <li>
           A beer week should be a collection of curated events spanning multiple days and whose primary purpose is the promotion and celebration of independent craft beer.
          </li>
          <li>
           A beer week should
           <strong>
            not
           </strong>
           be a single corporation doing a sub par job of holding nightly events, and then slapping the label “Beer Week” on it.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          How much more fun would this week have been if just a few of the partnerships listed above had been asked to be involved?
         </p>
         <h3>
          HBW Events Do Not Promote Craft Beer
         </h3>
         <p>
          Essentially, we have a Blackjack tournament, a stuff-your-face crab leg dinner, a turkey leg dinner, a concert, a Burlesque show and a chili buffet, each with sample glasses of beer being handed out. In discussing the nightly “events”, one friend said,
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           I think the Burlesque show is particularly distasteful, and yet not surprising at all. I guess we should be glad he didn’t schedule the Little People Circus again or ask for people to bring panties to string across Second Street.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          I’ve got no problem with Blackjack or Burlesque, but these are the sorts of events that would be planned by an Entertainment Group, rather than craft beer enthusiasts. Philly Beer Week has so much going on that they just run the web site year round. There are so many events that, when one pub is too crowded, you can just walk down to the next one to find a comparable event. And they have an app for that!
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Bottom Line
         </h2>
         <p>
          “Harrisburg Beer Week” has a commercial web site listing a single corporation’s events for the week, which I purposely did not link to during this posting, because it really is just about profits for one corporation and not for the love of beer.
         </p>
         <p>
          I realize that Harrisburg is no Philadelphia, but there are so many people and resources that could be drawn upon. Breweries, pubs, homebrew clubs (like
          <a href="http://rehabrew.wordpress.com/" target="_blank" title="Regional Harrisburg Brewers - An Outlet for Harrisburg Homebrewers">
           ReHAB
          </a>
          ), distributors, restaurants, public transit companies and beer enthusiasts (such as the
          <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/hummelstownbeernutz/" target="_blank" title="Hummelstown Beer Nutz">
           Hummelstown Beer Nutz
          </a>
          ) throughout the area could have, and should have, been called upon to participate before this HBW label was so nonchalantly applied.
         </p>
         <p>
          Maybe next year we can try again?
         </p>
        </div>

           Much Joy over Terry’s Division with Farmer’s Cabinet
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          Posted by
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/author/pubcrawler/" title="View all posts by pubcrawler">

           </a>

          in

           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/craft-brewed-beers/" rel="category tag">

          <a class="entry-date-link" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2012/06/16/joy-division/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Much Joy over Terry’s Division with Farmer’s Cabinet">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2012-06-16T09:30:35+00:00" pubdate="">
            June 16, 2012
           </time>
          </a>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Very exciting news for residents of the Harrisburg, PA area is finally “out of the bag”! The already-fabulous
          <a href="http://pizzaboybrewing.com/index.php" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            Al’s of Hampden
           </strong>
          </a>
          , home of
          <strong>
           Pizza Boy Brewing Company
          </strong>
          , has signed on our beloved
          <strong>
           Terry Hawbaker
          </strong>
          to begin brewing late this summer. I couldn’t be more thrilled about this one! Last February, when
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/03/07/farmers-cabinet/">
           Terry left Bullfrog and Williamsport for Philadelphia and Farmer’s Cabinet
          </a>
          , I lamented that his beers would be harder for us to get – such small batches being brewed so far away. Pizza Boy was just getting started, but too late to get Terry. I predicted the situation may not be as ideal as it originally seemed, and was over the moon with joy when I learned a few weeks ago that
          <a href="http://lewbryson.blogspot.ca/2012/06/and-another-brewer-leaves-philadelphia.html" target="_blank">
           Terry will be leaving Philadelphia
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Pizza Boy had the
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/11/scott-morrison-brew/" target="_blank">
           consultation of Scott Morrison
          </a>
          for a short time after opening, but
          <a href="http://blog.pennlive.com/go/2012/03/out_with_sara_als_of_hampden_p.html" target="_blank">
           owner/brewer Albert Kominski has been handling the reigns from the start
          </a>
          (and doing very well, I must add!) However, he will surely feel the weight of so many working hours lifted by hiring a full-time brewer.
         </p>
         <p>
          Clearly area residents and visitors from afar cannot resist the best pizza (and soups and sandwiches, and occasional specials like perogies!) paired with the best beer selection in the region. Al’s of Hampden is always packed, and due to township regulations recently had to eliminate outside seating and reduce indoor seating. This resulted in a
          <a href="http://www.pennlive.com/midstate/index.ssf/2012/05/wertzville_road_corridor_in_ha.html" target="_blank">
           building project announced in May 2012, with expanded everything
          </a>
          , including a new 20-hectolitre BrauKon system (currently Al uses a 5-hectolitre
          <a href="http://www.braukon.de/braukon2/index.php?id=1&amp;L=1" target="_blank" title="BrauKon">
           BrauKon
          </a>
          brewing system.)
         </p>
         <p>
          With a full-time brewer in house, Al can concentrate on his new building and running the expanding business.            tight with Terry
          </a>
          ), but that’s okay with me as long as I get my fair share of the beer, too!

          <strong>
           <em>

           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" data-shortcode="caption" id="attachment_935" style="width: 221px">
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg">
           <img alt="River House Beer List (July 21, 2011)" class="size-medium wp-image-935" data-attachment-id="935" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"movlab10","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="River House Beer List (July 21, 2011)" data-large-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg?w=389" data-medium-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg?w=211&amp;h=300" data-orig-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg" data-orig-size="389,552" data-permalink="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/07/21/changing-taps/river-house/" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 211px) 100vw, 211px" src="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg?w=211&amp;h=300" srcset="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg?w=211&amp;h=300 211w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg?w=106&amp;h=150 106w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/07/river-house.jpg 389w" title="River House Beer List (July 21, 2011)" width="211"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           River House Beer List (July 21, 2011)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          It’s a little bit third hand, but three independent (and reliable) patrons of
          <a href="http://www.riverhousebrewpub.com/index.html" target="_blank" title="River House Brew Pub">
           River House Brew Pub
          </a>
          chatted with me this evening and said they were told this today while patronizing River House, and I see someone also
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2010/04/04/river-house-brew-pub-milton-pa/" title="Bart is gone :(">
           posted a comment to support it
          </a>
          . Funny. I just saw Bart at the
          <a href="http://www.selinsgrovebrewfest.org/" target="_blank" title="Hops, VInes and Wines">
           Selinsgrove Hops, Vines and Wines Festival
          </a>
          last weekend, and there wasn’t a hint that anything was amiss.
         </p>
         <p>
          I heard a bit of the story on what happened, but since it is all rumor, I’ll just let the image back up the fact that he’s gone. I’ll disagree with the poster who said, “Crap on tap is back,” – these are some very nice taps – but it’s disappointing to see that things didn’t work out.
         </p>
         <p>
          On the other hand, things didn’t work out back in April at
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/02/28/bullfrog-change-is-brewing/" target="_blank" title="Change is the only constant...">
           Bullfrog with Nick Micio
          </a>
          , but by May, it was all straightened out.  Although I haven’t had time to write about the fabulousness I discovered there in June, brewer
          <strong>
           Nate Saar
          </strong>
          has stepped in and stepped right up, brewing tasty treats like
          <strong>
           Somnus Viduata
          </strong>
          (coffe saison brewed with a variety of specialty malt and aged on 9 lbs of Colombian Magdalena coffee),
          <strong>
           Peche Panache
          </strong>
          (saison with bret, brewed with wheat and oats plus 80 lbs of peach puree),
          <strong>
           Stark Weiss
          </strong>
          (a German wheat with hints of chocolate covered banana), and
          <strong>
           Captain Drinkable
          </strong>
          (Czech-style unfiltered pilsner.)
         </p>
         <p>
          If things really come in 3’s, I’m really hoping that the
          <a href="http://mybeerbuzz-oneguybrewing.blogspot.com/2011/07/guy-hagner-leaving-berwick-brewing-re.html" target="_blank" title="Guy Hagner bringing back One Guy!">
           news out of Berwick
          </a>
          is the other story of change for our Susquehanna River based breweries.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Without change, there would be no butterflies. ~ Anonymous
            <br/>
           </em>
          </strong>

       <article class="post-907 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-craft-brewed-beers category-imperial-stout category-microbreweries category-pennsylvania" id="post-907">

         <h1 class="entry-title">
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/peanut-butter-chocolate/" rel="bookmark">
           You Got Peanut Butter in my Chocolate (Stout)!
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          Posted by
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/author/pubcrawler/" title="View all posts by pubcrawler">
            pubcrawler
           </a>
          </span>
          in
          <span class="cat-links">
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/craft-brewed-beers/" rel="category tag">
            Craft Brewed Beers
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/beer-styles/stout/imperial-stout/" rel="category tag">
            Imperial Stout
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/microbreweries/" rel="category tag">
            microbreweries
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/category/united-states/pennsylvania/" rel="category tag">
            Pennsylvania
           </a>
          </span>
          <a class="entry-date-link" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/peanut-butter-chocolate/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to You Got Peanut Butter in my Chocolate (Stout)!">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-05-17T22:55:20+00:00" pubdate="">
            May 17, 2011
           </time>
          </a>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Mmmm…Chocolate!   Mmmm…Peanut Butter!
           </em>
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" height="325" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/DJLDF6qZUX0?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;autohide=2&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" style="border:0;" type="text/html" width="523">
           </iframe>
          </span>
          <br/>
          Two great tastes that taste GREAT together – we’ve known that for a long time. But how often do you find them in a beer?  I have, a couple of times, but last night I had one that drove it right out of the ballpark!
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.springhousebeer.com/" target="_blank" title="Spring House Brewing Co.">
            Spring House Brewing Co.
           </a>
          </strong>
          was serving up
          <strong>
           Chocolate Peanut Butter Stout
          </strong>
          on nitro at the Taproom in Lancaster, PA, and it was outstanding!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" data-shortcode="caption" id="attachment_919" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg">
           <img alt="Beer List at the Taproom" class="size-medium wp-image-919 " data-attachment-id="919" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="Beer List at the Taproom" data-large-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg?w=523" data-medium-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg?w=300&amp;h=300" data-orig-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg" data-orig-size="720,720" data-permalink="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/peanut-butter-chocolate/pbtaproom/" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg?w=300&amp;h=300" srcset="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg?w=300&amp;h=300 300w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg?w=600&amp;h=600 600w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/pbtaproom.jpg?w=150&amp;h=150 150w" title="Beer List at the Taproom"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Photo Credit: Taproom by Spring House Brewing Co. Facebook
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Served in an Imperial pint glass, this beer was pitch black with a dense and creamy, thick tan head. It’s made with local Wilbur chocolate and what was described as a peanut butter powder (perhaps dehydrated peanut butter?), and the aroma off the glass was like splitting open a fresh peanut butter egg.
         </p>
         <p>
          The flavor was so well balanced – like the darkest chocolate cake blended with a savory peanut butter frosting. It has lingering chocolate flavor, blended with a light hop bitterness and subtle peanut butter sweetness that masks the 8% ABV, making for a perfectly drinkable pint (so good I could drink it again and again!)
         </p>
         <p>
          I did a little searching, and learned of another beer with this profile on tap this month.
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.bandwagonbeer.com/" target="_blank" title="BandWagon in Ithaca, NY">
            BandWagon Brew Pub
           </a>
          </strong>
          in
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg">
           <img alt="Chocolate Peanut Butter Stout" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-915" data-attachment-id="915" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="Chocolate Peanut Butter Stout" data-large-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg?w=500" data-medium-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg?w=200&amp;h=300" data-orig-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg" data-orig-size="500,750" data-permalink="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/17/peanut-butter-chocolate/wpid-imag0114-jpg/" sizes="(max-width: 200px) 100vw, 200px" src="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg?w=200&amp;h=300" srcset="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg?w=200&amp;h=300 200w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg?w=400&amp;h=600 400w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/wpid-imag0114.jpg?w=100&amp;h=150 100w" title="Chocolate Peanut Butter Stout"/>
          </a>
          Ithaca, NY currently has a
          <strong>
           <a href="http://bandwagonbeer.wordpress.com/bandwagon-beer-descriptions/" target="_blank" title="Peanut Butter Chocolate Stout at BandWagon">
            Peanut Butter Chocolate Stout
           </a>
          </strong>
          that is coming in at 6.8% ABV. Another one that is also currently obtainable is
          <strong>
           <a href="http://willoughbybrewing.com/beer-menu/" target="_blank" title="Willoughbys PB Coffee Porter">
            Peanut Butter Coffee Porter
           </a>
          </strong>
          at 5.5% ABV from
          <a href="http://willoughbybrewing.com/index.php?page=contact-us" target="_blank" title="Willoughby Brewing">
           <strong>
            Willoughby Brewing Co.
           </strong>
          </a>
          in Ohio. It would be fun to add these to my portfolio of consumed peanut butter beers.
         </p>
         <p>
          I’m pretty sure I had the
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.bostonphoenix.com/boston/food_drink/noshing/documents/03343479.asp" target="_blank" title="Boston Beer Works Peanut Butter Porter">
            Peanut Butter Porter
           </a>
          </strong>
          (5% ABV) while visiting
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.beerworks.net/html/locations_home.html" target="_blank" title="Boston Beer Works">
            Boston Beer Works
           </a>
          </strong>
          in 2006, and I know I drank an
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.shortsbrewing.com/beer/our-portfolio/specialty-beers/uber-goober-oatmeal-stout/" target="_blank" title="Uber Goober at Shorts">
            Über Goober Oatmeal Stout
           </a>
          </strong>
          (6.5% ABV) in 2009 at
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.shortsbrewing.com/" target="_blank" title="Short's Brewing">
            Short’s Brewing
           </a>
          </strong>
          in Bellaire, MI. These were both pretty good, but the Porter went way too light on the peanut butter flavor, and Uber Goober had sour untertones of stale legumes.
         </p>
         <p>
          In other peanut butter beer news, has anyone seen or had the
          <strong>
           Blue Moon Peanut Butter Blonde
          </strong>
          ? Did they make it outside GABF?
          <a href="http://www.femaleist.com/2010/09/26/gabf/" target="_blank" title="GABF - Peanut Butter Beer quote">
           A beer blogger there
          </a>
          made me laugh with her quote, “
          <em>
           I only tried this because it had peanut butter in it. It had a huge peanut butter nose, which was super interesting. Then I made the mistake and tasted it: wheat, peanut butter lager-y grossness
          </em>
          …”, but since she also didn’t like
          <strong>
           New Glarus Raspberry Tart
          </strong>
          , I’m not sure we’d have similar opinions.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you’ve ever doubted that peanut butter and chocolate belong together in a beer, get yourself to Lancaster and try this out. If you’re feeling really adventurous, blend it with
          <strong>
           <a href="http://springhousebeer.com/indexold.htm" target="_blank" title="Planet Bean">
            Planet Bean Coffee Stout
           </a>
          </strong>
          (
          <em>
           can you say cake and coffee?
          </em>
          ) I’ve been waiting for someone to brew a
          <strong>
           Peanut Butter
           <a href="http://www.padutchcountry.com/pennsylvania-dutch-country/lancaster-county-whoopie-pie.asp" target="_blank" title="Whoopie Pie">
            Whoopie Pie
           </a>
           Stout
          </strong>
          and name it “Carolyn’s Obsession”, but this may just fill that need without the personalization.
         </p>
         <p>
          If brewer Matt Keasey doesn’t keep this on as a regular, I don’t know what I’ll do – I’m in love with this beer – and if he doesn’t enter it in GABF, he’s crazy!  It is by far the best peanut butter beer ever.
         </p>
        </div>

          <a class="entry-date-link" href="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/11/scott-morrison-brew/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Scott Morrison and Al’s of Hampden">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2011-05-11T19:36:48+00:00" pubdate="">
            May 11, 2011
           </time>
          </a>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          If you’ve been drinking craft beer in Pennsylvania for a couple of years, then you know the name
          <strong>
           Scott Morrison
          </strong>
          . He is a highly respected brewer with four GABF medals under his belt from 2003 to 2006, and well known for his strong ales. He put McKenzies on the map, but left in 2006 after management pressured him to concentrate on more mainstream brews. Scott Morrison groupies have observed that he hasn’t dropped out of the brewing scene, rather has had his hands in happenings all over the east coast. Morrison’s name is associated with influence in places like Dock Street, Sly Fox and many other fine breweries, and rumors continue to fly about where and when he may open a brewery of his own.
         </p>
         <p>
          If you’ve been watching the beer scene in the Harrisburg (PA) area over the last couple of years, then you know the name
          <strong>
           Albert Kominski
          </strong>
          , who opened
          <a href="http://www.alsofhampden.com/" target="_blank" title="Al's of Hampden">
           Al’s of Hampden
          </a>
          in 2002, which has been exploding since he obtained his liquor license in 2008. On the surface, it may seem that Al is running a simple pizza shop. Get him talking about food and you’ll get an earful
          <a href="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png">
           <img alt="Al's of Hampden" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-891" data-attachment-id="891" data-comments-opened="1" data-image-description="" data-image-meta='{"aperture":"0","credit":"","camera":"","caption":"","created_timestamp":"0","copyright":"","focal_length":"0","iso":"0","shutter_speed":"0","title":""}' data-image-title="Al’s of Hampden" data-large-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png?w=180" data-medium-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png?w=180" data-orig-file="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png" data-orig-size="180,142" data-permalink="https://pubcrawlin.wordpress.com/2011/05/11/scott-morrison-brew/als/" height="118" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" src="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png?w=150&amp;h=118" srcset="https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png?w=150&amp;h=118 150w, https://pubcrawlin.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/als.png 180w" title="Al's of Hampden" width="150"/>
          </a>
          about his fresh and perfected recipes: his outstanding dough is made fresh every day and combine with signature sauces and toppings for remarkable pizza; the deli meats and cheeses are sliced and grated in-house and combine with freshly baked bread for amazing subs; the soups are started from scratch with hearty vegetables; the meatballs are made fresh from of a mix of ground veal, pork and Angus chuck, two styles of bread crumbs and eggs – no salt needed; the wings have outstanding sauces for a variety of palates.
         </p>
         <p>
          But get Al talking about beer, and your head might explode! At the moment, Al’s has up to 27 fresh-pour beers available – 3 beer engines, four nitro-taps and 20 CO2 draft lines which can be consumed at Al’s or taken out in growlers (which he can sell you in 64-ounce or 32-ounce sizes!) In addition to the taps, his four doors of coolers house approximately 350 varieties of single bottles, and you can take them home for a mix-a-six price. And it’s not just that he carries beer, but his passion for beer, that draws in such crowds. Al is absolutely a beer geek, and his concentration is on making sure the beers on tap are as fresh as possible and represent a bold spectrum of styles.
         </p>
         <p>
          So it shouldn’t be surprising to anyone that Al is taking this whole beer thing one step further – opening a brewery operation on premises! If it isn’t clear already, Al strives for no less than perfection, and doesn’t enter into this brewing venture lightly. He sought out an experienced and creative brewer for this project, and came up with a winning answer.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           <strong>
            Scott Morrison and Al Kominski will partner in the brewing operation at what will soon be known as Pizza Boy Brewing.
           </strong>
          </em>
          Scott’s expertise is sure to bring success to the brewing operation, and fulfill to the beer appetites of south-central Pennsylvania beer enthusiasts. We can expect high quality, pleasurable house beers, sure to have high ratings in the “overall balls” category!
         </p>
         <p>
          Al has been working with
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.braukon.de/braukon2/index.php?id=1&amp;L=1" target="_blank" title="BrauKon">
            BrauKon
           </a>
          </strong>
          of Truchtlaching, Germany to design and manufacture a 5 hectolitre brewing system (as a
          <a href="http://twitpic.com/4f2x2x" title="Share photos on twitter with Twitpic">
           <img alt="Getting started on the brewery!" class="alignleft" height="150" src="https://i2.wp.com/twitpic.com/show/thumb/4f2x2x.jpg" title="Getting started on the brewery!" width="150"/>
          </a>
          measurement reference, 1 barrel = 1.17 hectolitres = 117 litres = 244 U.S. pints), and in fact, will be returning to Germany in a few weeks to brew the test batch on his new system. The Braukon system will include everything from the malt mill to the storage tanks, using an energy saving boiling system (BrauKon EcoBoil) and a semi-automated brewing system.
         </p>
         <p>
          Just when I didn’t think things could get any better…  Keep your ears and eyes open, or better yet, follow Al’s of Hampden on
          <a href="http://twitter.com/alsofhampden" target="_blank" title="Al's of Hampden on Twitter">
           Twitter
          </a>
          or
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/home.php#!/pages/Als-Of-Hampden/10150097225655094" target="_blank" title="Al's of Hampden on Facebook">
           Facebook
          </a>
          to follow the whole story!
         </p>
        </div>
