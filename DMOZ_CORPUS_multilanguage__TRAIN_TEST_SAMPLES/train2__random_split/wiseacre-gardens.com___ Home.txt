      <span class="indent2">
       North of the Adirondacks
      </span>
      <br/>
      <span class="title">
       WiseAcre Gardens
      </span>
      <p>
       <span class="indent">
        I encourage you to bookmark now.
       </span>
       <br/>
       <span class="indent2">
        If you can view everything in one visit, you need to get a life.
       </span>
      </p>
      Over 10 years ago I threw some garden flower photos and a brief reference together to use as a quick reminder. I needed it, as a beginner flower gardener I often drew a blank about the name of that whatchamacallit or the conditions it prefered.
      <p>
       There's one thing certain about the
       <a href="plants/perennials.html">
        Garden Perennial Flowers
       </a>
       displayed on my North Country site. They all survive winters colder than my wife's feet.
      </p>
      <p>
       I also began my love affair with
       <a href="plants/wildflowers.html">
        Wildflowers of Northern New York
       </a>
       at the same time. This section isn't going to replace my
       <i>
        Peterson's Field Guide to Wildflowers
       </i>
       but it sure is pretty to look at.
      </p>
      <p>
       Of course I couldn't help but to add more photos to the site and shove them on you like a grandma with pics of the grandkids.
       <i>
        Oh my, you just have to see these, dear.
       </i>
       <a href="gardens/gardens.html">
        Gardens
       </a>
       and
       <a href="scenes/scenes.html">
        North Country Scenes
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       And even my Looney Tunes view of
       <a href="winter/winter.html">
        Winter
       </a>
       after the ice storm of '98
      </p>
      <p>
       And now I'm adding even more photos. The
       <a href="mushroom/mushroom.html">
        Mushroom
       </a>
       section is growing quickly. It's mainly a photo gallery and while I've identified most of the mushrooms not all are known. If you're looking for a cool background for your desktop you should check it out.
      </p>
      <p>
       Putting a website together was a pass-time and you'll notice I've passed a considerable amount of it away.
 A very large part of that was editing images. 
 So take one guess where the
       <a href="buttons/toons.html">
        Looney Tunes
       </a>
       stuff came from. 
 Hey I had to learn how to use Paint Shop Pro somehow. 
 Then once I had all those Looney Tunes images I had to do something with them. 
 With some "Quotes" (.wav to listen or download) added you might find those pages interesting for a couple of seconds.
      </p>
      <p>
       I've managed to put a real hodge-podge of a site together. 
 I didn't waste time - I'm just giving you an excuse to waste yours.
      </p>
      <p>
       If you'd rather spend your time on a forum than here then stop by one of the Avatar pages and pick one up before you leave.
 Unfortunately for you more serious types the only ones available are
       <br/>
       <a href="avatars_dead.html">
        Grateful Dead Avatars
       </a>
       or
       <a href="avatars.html">
        Looney Tunes Avatars
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       If you're looking to waste time too you came to the right spot. 
 I've got a collection of
       <a href="games/games.html">
        Flash Games
       </a>
       that are both quick and easy. 
 If you only have a few minutes to waste you better hurry, your times running out.
      </p>
