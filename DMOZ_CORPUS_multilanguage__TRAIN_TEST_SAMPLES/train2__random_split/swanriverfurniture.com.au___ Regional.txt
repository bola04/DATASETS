         <h3>
          Swan River Furniture
         </h3>
        </font>
        AT THE EDGE OF THE WORLD ~ BETWEEN THE RIVER AND THE SEA.....
        <p>
         <strong>
          SwanRiver Furniture
         </strong>
         specialises in solid JARRAH, MARRI and PINE Hand Crafted Furniture.
        </p>
        <p>
         Originally situated in Fremantle, Western Australia The Indian Ocean on one side ~ The Swan River on the other.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once known to the early settlers as The Swan River Colony.
        </p>
        <p>
         A region surrounded by once vast Eucalypt Forrest abundant with the remarkable hardwood Eucalyptus Marginata.
        </p>
        <p>
         In those days known as Swan River Mahogany.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now we know it as 'Jarrah'
        </p>
        <p>
         This beautiful Jarrah timber is now served well by people like me, who source it from various past lives ~old Warehouses and Bridges, Woolstores and Wharves, along with dressed milled timbers and Natural Edged Slabs from fallen trees ~ and craft it into items of distinctive furniture.
         <br clear="all"/>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <img align="left" alt="" border="0" height="180" hspace="10" src="images/SwanRiver.1.5.jpg" width="150"/>
        </p>
        <h4>
         <strong>
          THE NANNUP FURNITURE GALLERY
         </strong>
        </h4>
        SWANRIVER FURNITURE and PHIL'S WOODWORK SHOP have moved...
        <p>
         Now located in the small township of
         <strong>
          NANNUP
         </strong>
         in the very heart of the Southwest of Western Australia.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is where the tall Jarrah and Marri grow, and the Blackwood river winds its way to Augusta and the Southern Ocean.
        </p>
        <p>
         Although still maintaining the Swanriver Furniture and Phil's Woodwork Shop identities - both are now comfortably housed in my new Gallery/Showroom and Workshop ~ and even more committed to utilising our valuable resources in the most productive and value added way I can.
         <br clear="all"/>
