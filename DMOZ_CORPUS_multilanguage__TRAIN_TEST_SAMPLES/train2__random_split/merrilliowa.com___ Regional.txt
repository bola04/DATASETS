   <h3 align="center">
    MERRILL IOWA​​​REGULAR MEETING​​JUNE 18, 2018
   </h3>
   <h3 align="center">
    Mayor Husman  called the meeting to order at 7 pm.  Pledge of Allegiance was recited.   Council present: Norgaard, Hemmelman, Conley, Hoyt, Pierson.
    <br/>
    Motion by Conley 2nd by Norgaard to approve minutes of 5/21/18.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Conley to approve treasurer’s report.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Council and personnel answered questions from audience.
    <br/>
    Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hemmelman to close portion of Main Street  July 21 from 7 – noon for Fire Department sponsored water fights.  All  voted aye.  Fire Dept announced they will also have water slide at park  July 20.
    <br/>
    Maintenance reported flower pots are up on Main Street.   80 bushes were planted by ditch on north side and 8 trees planted at new  campground site.
    <br/>
    Nuisance properties were discussed again.
    <br/>
    Attorney explained status of 125 Main Street property.  Rapid Creek  would sell it to City for back tax paid by them.  Council decided to do  nothing at this time.
    <br/>
    Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hemmelman to set  rates for new campground for $15 since there is no sewer hookup at this  time.  Tent sites will be $10.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Pierson, 2nd by Conley to approve contract with Connections.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hoyt to give Economic Development  Committee permission to advertise City property south of fire station.   All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Hemmelman, 2nd by Pierson to accept credit/debit cards and charge a 3% convenience fee.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hoyt to approve liquor license renewal for Perera Lounge.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Norgaard, 2nd b Conley to approve building permits for Dittebenner for deck and garage.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Motion by Hemmelman, 2nd by Conley to allow bills as presented.  All voted aye.
    <br/>
    Meeting adjourned on motion by Hoyt, 2nd by Conley.
   </h3>

    <h3 align="center">
     MERRILL IOWA REGULAR MEETING​​ MAY 21, 2018
    </h3>
    <h3 align="center">
     Council met at  7:00 pm, Mayor Husman leading the Pledge of Allegiance.  Council  present: Norgaard, Pierson, Hemmelman, Conley, Hoyt.
     <br/>
     Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Conley to approve minutes of 4/16 &amp; 4/30.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Pierson, 2nd by Hoyt to approve treasurer’s report.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Department reports were given.
     <br/>
     Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hoyt to approve Resolution #2018-4 adopting  and approving tax compliance procedures relating to tax exempt bonds.   All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Mayor opened hearing on proposal to enter into a  Sewer Revenue Loan and Disbursement Agreement.  Attorney explained  procedure.  Hearing was closed.  Motion by Pierson, 2nd by Hemmelman to  approve Resolution 2018-5 authorizing and approving a Loan and  Disbursement Agreement and providing for the issuance and securing the  payment of $1,3000,00 Sewer Revenue Bonds, Series 2018.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Mayor opened hearing for 2017-18 budget amendment.  Hearing was  closed.  Motion by Hoyt, 2nd by Norgaard to approve Resolution 2018-6  amending 2017-18 budget.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Bulk water rates will remain at $35 regardless of load size.
     <br/>
     Street repair bids were considered.  Motion by Pierson, 2nd by Hoyt  to repair all streets on list with billing in two fiscal years.  All  voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hemmelman to amend Ordinance  raising garbage rates from $23.25 to $23.75 per quarter effective  August 1, 2018. All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion to waive 2nd and 3rd readings by Pierson, 2nd by Hemmelman.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hemmelman to approve business signs requested by Economic Development. All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Pierson, 2nd by Conley to approve Resolution #2018-7 declaring Kissinger Center chairs surplus.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Norgaard, 2nd by Hoyt to approve cigarette permit renewals for Caseys and Midwest Vape.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Pierson, 2nd by Norgaard to approve building permit for shed for M. Delutri.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Motion by Hemmelman, 2nd by Conley to approve bills.  All voted aye.
     <br/>
     Meeting adjourned at 9:10 on motion by Conley, 2nd by Hoyt.

    <p align="center">
     The Foundation of Merrill Iowa was formed to coordinate and provide philanthropic support for community betterment projects in the community of Merrill.  The Community Foundation of Merrill Iowa is an affiliate of the Iowa Community Affiliate Network, a collaboration of the Community Foundation of Greater Des Moines and the Iowa Area Development Group Community Foundation.  Each year the Foundation will make grant awards to worthwhile projects located in the community of Merrill.
    </p>
    <p align="center">
     <strong>
      Foundation of Merrill’s areas of emphasis include:
     </strong>
     <br/>
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <p align="center">
       <strong>
        Children, youth, and families:
       </strong>
       Programs 	that enhance the quality of life for children and through 	opportunities to enhance their intellectual, emotional, physical, 	and social development.
      </p>
     </li>
     <li>
      <p align="center">
       <strong>
        Community Betterment- Neighborhoods and 	community development:
       </strong>
       Programs that address community and 	neighborhood development, both social and economic.
      </p>
     </li>
     <li>
      <p align="center">
       <strong>
        Health and Human Services:
       </strong>
       Social 	service projects that benefit the underserved segments of the 	community
      </p>
     </li>
     <li>
      <p align="center">
       <strong>
        Education:
       </strong>
       Community education and 	service projects that serve all age groups and segments of the 	population
      </p>
     </li>
     <li>
      <p align="center">
       <strong>
        The Environment:
       </strong>
       Programs that address 	our natural resources and environment
      </p>
     </li>
     <li>
      <p align="center">
       <strong>
        Arts and Culture:
       </strong>
       Programs that 	provide or increase access to cultural and educational resources for 	theatre, music, arts, dance, and museums and that increase awareness 	of arts and culture.
      </p>
     </li>
    </ul>
    <p align="center">
     <br/>
     <u>
      <strong>
       Please note that we do not fund:
      </strong>
     </u>
    </p>
    <p align="center">
     Individuals * Fundraisers * Debt reduction or budget shortfalls * Politics/causes * Religious purposes (this does not exclude grants to religious organizations for non-religious purposes) * Equipment, unless it is essential for the program * Ongoing expenses, such as wages * Endowments.
     <br/>
