         <section class="entry">
          <h4>
           What is The Vernacular Architecture Society of South Africa?
          </h4>
          <p>
           <strong>
            It’s a voluntary organisation that aims to support the study and preservation of vernacular architecture in South Africa.
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <strong>
            It’s Cape Town based, not for profit, and run by members in their spare time.
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           You don’t have to be an architect to join!
           <br/>
           Just be curious about your own backyard, and open to exploring things.
           <br/>
           If you are interested in the built landscape you’ll have fun, and get to know ‘’as much about the Cape Dutch fantastic stuff as the simple things’’ according to
           <em>
            <a href="https://www.vassa.org.za/resources/james-walton/" title="Founder – James Walton">
             James Walton
            </a>
            ,
           </em>
           our founder
           <em>
            .
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <span style="font-style: normal;">
             Because our name is such a mouthful, we use the acronym ‘VASSA’ fairly interchangeably with ‘Vernacs’.
             <br/>
             Just so you know, as you read on …
            </span>
           </em>
          </p>
          <div class="twocol-one">
           <h4>
            What Does
            <em>
             Vernacular
            </em>
            Mean?
           </h4>
           <p>
            Building in indigenous styles, constructed from locally available materials, following traditional building practice and patterns, and not architect-designed.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Encyclopedia of Vernacular Architecture of the World defines it as …
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <span style="color: #808080;">
              <strong>
               ”Architecture built to meet specific needs, accommodating the values, economies and ways of life of the cultures that produce it”.
              </strong>
             </span>
            </em>
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="twocol-one last">
           <h4>
            What We Do
           </h4>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The Society arranges monthly talks
            </strong>
            on relevant topics, which are open to the public and are free.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             We organise monthly outings
            </strong>
            to areas and buildings of interest frequently with private access, so that members are introduced to local vernacular architecture first hand.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             We also arrange an annual weekend away
            </strong>
            .
            <br/>
            Usually a long weekend in September, to explore further afield.
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="clear">
          </div>
          <div class="twocol-one">
           <h4>
            Professional Perk
           </h4>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Earn CPD credits by becoming a member if you are a SACAP registered professional
            </strong>
            .
            <br/>
            VASSA is an
            <em>
             Accredited Learning Site (ALS)
            </em>
            validated through the Cape Peninsula University of Technology. This means you can earn accreditation points each year while enjoying activities and meeting like-minded peers.
            <br/>
            <a href="https://www.vassa.org.za/membership/sacap-members/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              <em>
               Get more information
              </em>
             </strong>
            </a>
           </p>
          </div>
          <div class="twocol-one last">
           <h4>
            What Else We Do
           </h4>
           <p>
            <strong>
             PUBLICATIONS
            </strong>
            <br/>
            We publish a bi-annual journal, as well as surveys, reports, guides for researchers etc. that are available for purchase and are downloadable.
            <br/>
            <strong>
             <em>
              <a href="/category/publications/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">
               See all
              </a>
             </em>
            </strong>
