      <h2>
       <a href="http://www.train99.com/00-gauge/nason-p5a-prr-electic-loco-in-oo-gauge-1934-40/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Nason P5A PRR electic loco in oo gauge 1934-40">
        Nason P5A PRR electic loco in oo gauge 1934-40
       </a>
      </h2>
      <small>
       May 9th, 2012
       <!-- by admin -->
      </small>
      <div class="entry">
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <img alt="Nason P5a" height="177" src="http://www.train99.com/oo-gauge/nasonp5a.jpg" title="Nason P5a" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        In 1934 Nason introduced a machined bronze kit to make the model of The Pennsylvania Rail Road’s P5A locomotive. The kit cost $37.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        I’ve had this loco for years, and it’s certainly my favorite OO loco.This is set up for outside 3rd rail, and has one large motor with field windings. The advertisement on the American OO site for this loco says it came with 2 motors in 1935. Here’s
        <a href="http://americanoo.blogspot.com/2010/10/1934-tale-of-two-bound-volumes-part-5.html" target="_blank">
         a link to the ad.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        When I got this loco it was wrapped in newspaper from 1957 so I’m pretty sure this is an unmolested model. I bought this from an elderly woman who’s dad had built the layout when she was a girl in the 1930s.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        I have lots of OO gauge trains assembled kits, and this loco along with the other locos I got from the same seller show the best workmanship of any OO trains.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        A few of the pieces of window glass  have fallen out over the years, and there are a few scratches that allow the bronze casing to show through, but otherwise this is in wonderful shape.
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Here’s another picture from the end:
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <img alt="Nason PRR P5a end view" height="335" src="http://www.train99.com/oo-gauge/nasonp5a1.jpg" title="Nason PRR P5a end view" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Here’s the top so you can see the details of the pantographs and the electrical stuff on the roof:
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <img alt="Nason PRR P5A Roof Top" height="500" src="http://www.train99.com/oo-gauge/nasonp5a2.jpg" title="Nason PRR P5A Roof Top" width="432"/>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        Lastly here’s a bottom view:
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center">
        <img alt="Bottom view Nason PRR P5a loco" height="335" src="http://www.train99.com/oo-gauge/nasonp5a1.jpg" title="Bottom view Nason PRR P5a loco" width="500"/>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        I think this engine was made between 1934 and 1940. Nason was in New Rochelle NY. Here’s
        <a href="http://americanoo.blogspot.com/2008/05/nason-railways-101.html" target="_blank">
         a bit of history on Nason.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
        <strong>
         This loco doesn’t run and I don’t have instructions for the kit. If you have the instructions for this loco, I’ll pay you for a photocopy. I don’t want to just start pulling out screws trying to get inside and end up damaging the pain needlessly. You can send me an email salesATnalrooDOTcom
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p align="left">
       </p>
