                <br/>
                <font size="2">
                 Our Great Lakes are picture perfect.  Endless shorelines, clear blue water, quaint towns and sand dunes are all within easy driving distance.  We love recreating on the Great Lakes but often times forget how utilitarian they are.  Throughout history, they have been a vital form of commerce.   Today, you’ll see freighters cruising from one port to another, delivering an array of products.  They take the same routes that wooden schooners did in days gone by. Occasionally, the trips went badly.  Many ships met their demise due to epic storms, blankets of heavy fog, hidden shoals and congested boat traffic.  There are literally thousands of shipwrecks resting on the bottom of the Great Lakes.   Today, Michigan has 14 areas zoned as Underwater Preserves.  The goal is simple, protect these shipwrecks and the historical artifacts found on them.  So, I challenge you to take advantage of this unique opportunity to dive a shipwreck this summer.  Yes, it is cold but you’re from Michigan so put on your big-boy wetsuit and come dive history with us!
