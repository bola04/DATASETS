                   <a class="contentpagetitle" href="http://oceanviewspeedway.co.nz/index.php?option=com_content&amp;task=view&amp;id=435&amp;Itemid=26">

                  <td colspan="2" valign="top">
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <b>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                      Cold night, hot action
                     </span>
                    </b>
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    By Tony
 Stuart
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     It was a
 cool autumn night, but the action at Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway
 on Saturday night was anything but cold.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     The biggest
 field of Superstocks seen at the track since the North Island Superstocks in
 2012 treated the big crowd to some fantastic racing action.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     First up was
 the Noel Kensington Memorial race. 1NZ William Humphries started on the outside
 of the front row, but it was the 599P car of Ron Tye which made the early
 running.
                     <span>
                     </span>
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Humphries
 was content to bide his time in the opening laps, but one he took the lead,
 there was no stopping him. He took a comfortable win over Wayne Hemi (591P)
 with Alex Bright (10N) storming home for third place.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     The action
 level ramped up for the George Podjursky Memorial. 56V Kerry Podjursky was
 highly motivated to win the race in memory of his late father, but a first-lap
 incident put him back in the pack.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Midway
 through the race Sjoerd Dykstra (38G) was turned sideways at the end of the
 front straight. Just as the red lights came on to remove his car, son Ricky
 Dykstra’s 28P car clipped his father’s bumper and rolled. Both father and son
 emerged uninjured, but their respective nights were over.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Scooter
 McIntosh held the lead until the very last corner, but was spun by Asher Rees
 (127C) and crossed the finish line backwards.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Brett Hyslop
 (9P) saw a gap and made a brave lunge to take the win from Rees, with McIntosh
 in third place. Only 3/10
                     <sup>
                      ths
                     </sup>
                     of a second separated the three cars.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     The final
 Superstock race was the Craig Heibner Memorial. Logan Sharpe (33S) got the
 early jump, but a first lap spin saw Asher Rees take a lead he would not
 relinquish. Hyslop finished second, whilst Humphries showed his class with a
 drive from near the back of the field through to third.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     The large
 field of Stockcars on hand included the 1NZ car of Ethan Rees, who was largely
 untroubled in winning the first race from 24V Wayne Wright and 98V Gerry
 Linklater.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Dion Mooney
 (6V) took out the second Stockcar heat from 87V Shane Kells, with Wright in
 third place.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     The action
 ramped up in the feature race, with Mooney being slowed early, sending him into
 battle mode. Among his targets was the 1NZ car of Rees. Linklater, a former 1NZ
 himself, drove a canny race to take the win from Rees, with Mark Johnston in
 third place in Trevor Greig’s 81V car.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     The Grand
 Slam saw the three Rees Superstocks starting almost a lap behind the Stockcars.
 Wright top the lead almost immediately, and despite his lap times being around
 a second a lap slower than the Superstocks, he held on to win by almost five
 seconds from Asher Rees, with Francis Potaka (52V) in third place.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Wins in the
 Adult Ministocks were shared between 86S Bradley Korff, 65W Caleb Madgwick and
 92V Hailey James, who took out the feature race.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Kyle Rowe
 (43P) took the opening Youth Ministock race with a last-corner pass of 26V
 Kaelin Mooney. Rowe was then unceremoniously tipped over in the second heat,
 won by 92V Jemma Barnes. 21P Cody Hodge took out the feature race.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     In the
 Production Saloons 71V Jason Pointon, 62V Jon Morrison and 7V Grant Loveridge
 took out a win apiece.
                     <span>
                     </span>
                     <span>
                     </span>
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     A very large
 field of Demolition Derby cars rolled out of the pits to face the starter, and
 chaos quickly ensured. After a large confrontation on the back straight, the
 82V car of Zeb Walker from the David Jones Motors team emerged as the winner.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     That brought
 the curtain down on a very successful speedway season. The crowd was the
 largest seen at Oceanview for several seasons. The turnout of visiting cars
 from eight different tracks in both islands, and the presence of the 1NZ cars
 in both the Superstock and Stockcar classes added prestige to the final meeting.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     Racing will
 resume in October, but for now Wanganui Stockcar and Speedway Club members,
 volunteers and officials will recharge their batteries over the winter break.
 The Club thanks everyone who has contributed to a memorable season.
                    </span>
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                   </p>
                  </td>

                   <a class="contentpagetitle" href="http://oceanviewspeedway.co.nz/index.php?option=com_content&amp;task=view&amp;id=434&amp;Itemid=26">
                    17 March review
                   </a>
                  </td>
                 </tr>

                <table class="contentpaneopen">
                 <tr>
                  <td colspan="2" valign="top">
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: 'arial black', 'avant garde'">
                     In a class
 of their own
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    By Tony
 Stuart
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Title
 sponsor Steve Heibner Distributors summed things up perfectly.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Presenting
 the Stevo’s Distributors North Island Sidecars trophies to winners Russell
 Stuart and Andrew Parker, Heibner commented that the 1NZ rider and swinger were
 in a class of their own.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     No one was
 at Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway on Saturday night would agree with
 the former New Zealand and North Island champion’s assessment.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Stuart and
 Parker are veterans of the New Zealand Sidecar community.
                     <span>
                     </span>
                     They won their first New Zealand title in
 2010-11, but until last night their best effort at the North Island event had
 been a third placing in the 2007-08 season.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Stuart, aged
 59, had planned to retire from racing at the end of the season, until he won
 the New Zealand title at Napier in January.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Winning two
 of the three Speedway NZ titles in the class though will see the pair race
 again next season.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     After two of
 the five rounds of heats Stuart and Parker and the local 3NZ team of John
 Hannan and Bryce Rose were the only unbeaten teams.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     In Round 3
 however Hannan could only manage one point in his heat, and the 3NZ team’s
 misery was compounded when the bike lost power on the last lap whilst leading
 their fourth race. Their night was over.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Going into
 the final round of heats, three teams could have won, and remarkably, they were
 all drawn together in the very last race of the night.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Mark
 Whye/Jason Cooper (96P) and Dylan Moohan/Tyler Berger (27A) were tied on 15 points,
 one behind Stuart/Parker.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Once again
 though, 1NZ got the jump from the start, and held on for an emphatic win.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Whye and
 Cooper came home in second, with the Auckland-based Moohan/Berger combination
 just behind in third place.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Local teams
 Aaron Rose/Bailee Ross (34V) and Craig Scott/Kristi Wackildene (62V) shared
 fourth place with Andrew McNamara/George Olsen (21B).
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Stuart was
 warm in his praise of the host club for a well-run event, and had some special
 words for Aaron Rose and Amelia Stanley, who had done a huge amount of work to
 run the event, including creating the unique trophies.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Wellington
 driver Tyler Lampp (992W) took out the Trev’s Concrete Stockcar Shootout. Lampp
 was the top qualifier, and came up against former 1NZ Gerry Linklater (98V) in
 the final.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Linklater,
 who qualified fourth, had earlier eliminated Francis Potaka (52V), Damon Baxter
 (7V) and Mark Johnston (driving Greig’s 81V car) to make the final, and was
 given the prize for the best challenge.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     It was a cool
 night at Oceanview Speedway, but for the good crowd the racing action was
 anything but. Spectators were treated to a clinical display of riding by the
 new North Island champions.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     The final
 meeting of the season will take place on Saturday 7
                     <sup>
                      th
                     </sup>
                     April at 7pm.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     That meeting
 will feature the Noel Kensington, George Podjursky and Craig Heibner Memorial
 races for Superstocks, along with a Stockcar teams race.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Also on the
 card is the Wanganui Stockcar and Speedway Club’s annual Demolition Derby, and
 the season will be brought to a close with fireworks.
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-size: small; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     In a season
 when there have been plenty of fireworks on the track, that will be a fitting
 conclusion!
                    </span>

                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <b>
                     <u>
                      <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                       Hermansen, Mooney shine at Oceanview
                      </span>
                     </u>
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </b>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                    By Tony
 Stuart
                    <o:p>
                    </o:p>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     All the
 elements were there.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     The pits
 were full to overflowing two hours before the start of the meeting, the weather
 was fantastic, and a good crowd was on hand.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     And the
 action at Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway lived up to the
 expectations, despite the two feature events being dominated by two drivers.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     1NZ
 Christian Hermansen proved his second New Zealand Minisprint title was no
 fluke. “The Hermanator” was in outstanding form, winning a third consecutive
 New Zealand Minisprint Grand Prix.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     A win and a
 second place in his heats put Hermansen on pole position for the 20-lap final.
 Alongside him was the impressive Lydia Dickinson (75S).
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     93P Karl
 McGill lowered his own lap record to 13.78 en route to the second row of the
 grid, which he shared with 44P Glen McCutcheon.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Hermansen
 blasted away from the start, and was quickly up to pace. His 3
                     <sup>
                      rd
                     </sup>
                     and
 4
                     <sup>
                      th
                     </sup>
                     laps both shattered the lap record, now 13.66 seconds, as he
 showed blistering speed whilst the track held some moisture.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     As the laps
 counted down there was a series of incidents. McGill got caught up trying to
 lap Cameron Hurley (82V) and almost rolled. His race was over.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     McCutcheon
 then fell by the wayside, and Ben Vaughn (57H) had to go to the back of the
 field while challenging for a podium position.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     After
 several restarts, Hermansen took the win from Dickinson, with 8P Nathan
 Jeffries in third place.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Hermansen
 had a number of issues with his new car early in the season, but has put those
 behind them. At prizegiving, he paid tribute to his crew chief father Mike,
 engines builders Bryan and Nelson Hartley and Mike Robins from Terminator Race
 Cars.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     A massive
 field of 29 Stockcars started the first heat of the Charlie Berntsen Trophy,
 and predictably, it didn’t take long for carnage to break out. 33V Trazarn
 Ryland-Annabell fired 61V Dion Black into the wall on the back straight rolling
 Black’s car.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Defending
 Charlie Berntsen Trophy-holder Francis Potaka was an early casualty, along with
 other good prospects including 15V Callum Sturzaker, 9V Chet Swan and 882P
 Brandon Jurgeleit. Less than half the field finished the first heat which was
 won by Dion Mooney (6V) from 78V Mark Johnston and 22V Scooter McIntosh.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     The second
 heat was a more orderly affair with Darryl Taylor taking the win in Trevor
 Greig’s 81V car from 53V Mason James and the ever-present Mooney.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     After two
 heats Mooney had a one point lead over Taylor. McIntosh was another two points
 further back, closely followed by James and Johnston.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     The third
 heat was another torrid affair. 19P Aidan Pond was left stranded against the
 wall at the southern end on the first lap, and as the leaders came round again,
 772P Kyle Lampp ran wide and slammed into Pond’s car.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     That set off
 a chain reaction which caught out Johnston. Clipping a car as he tried to avoid
 the mayhem, Johnston finished on his side, and out of the running. It was the
 second time in two meetings that Johnston had been rolled at that spot!
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     In the
 meantime Mooney drove a stealth mission to reach the front. Lampp killed
 James’s chances by blocking him for a lap and a half, and as the laps counted
 down Jurgeleit put in a massive hit on Taylor, slowing the 81V car.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Mooney took
 the race win, from McIntosh in second place and 7V Damon Baxter in third.
 Taylor limped home in fifth place.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     When the
 points were tallied Mooney had won The Charlie with 85 points. McIntosh
 finished second on 81 points and Taylor had done enough to finish third, a
 point further back.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Receiving
 the trophy from Berntsen’s widow Ann, Mooney told the crowd in the clubrooms it
 was his second time winning the event. He regards this one as more special
 however, given that Big Bad Charlie is no longer around.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     In the
 Superstocks, 29V Clint Hill, 4V Chad Ace and 471P Daniel Hole shared the
 victories. 86S Bradley Korff was dominant in the Adult Ministocks, with three
 wins from three.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     North Island
 Champion Grant Loveridge (7V) won the opening Production Saloon race, but had
 to bow to 71V Jason Pointon in the next two races.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     And in the
 Youth Ministocks, another Mooney starred. After coming a close second to 7P
 Louis Redshaw in the opening race, 26V Kaelin Mooney took the second race by a
 mere 0.02 seconds from 21P Cody Hodge, and added the feature race as well.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Given the
 hot weather conditions, the track was in superb condition, a credit to the
 track crew led by Mike Annabell. That allowed some fantastic racing in all
 classes, with the crowd going home happy.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Racing at
 Oceanview Family Speedway resumes on Sunday 11
                     <sup>
                      th
                     </sup>
                     March at 3pm with
 the running of the West Coast Sidecars, and the Heiby Memorial Youth
 Ministocks.
                    </span>

                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     <b>
                      <u>
                       <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                        Mooney wins at Oceanview
                       </span>
                      </u>
                     </b>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 8pt">
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                      By Tony Stuart
                     </span>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     The Mooney name is synonymous with Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway. And after two nights of torrid racing on Friday and Saturday nights, it was Dion Mooney who took the lion’s share of prize money put up by his brother Mike, owner of Elite Mechanical and Engineering.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     Although the field of Stockcars was slightly down on expectations due to a number of withdrawals, there was no lack of action. Two fine, warm evenings brought good crowds out to Oceanview, and they were treated to plenty of uncompromising racing.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     After qualifying on Friday night 24 cars faced the starter in the first Elite Mechanical Cup heat, and 89V Blair Lockett took the win from 28V Trent Palamountain.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     Mooney quickly got to the front in heat two, and was never headed. Former 1NZ Gerry Linklater (98V) came home in second, followed by 53V Mason James and 22V Scott McIntosh.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     Further back in the pack 52V Francis Potaka went on a mission seeking out visiting cars, and latched onto 172P Daniel Burmeister, one of the favourites. That battle continued to the end of the race, and spilled over into the final heat winning Potaka the JDT Engineering Stirrer prize.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     After two heats Mooney led on 44 points with McIntosh (42), and Linklater, 7V Damon Baxter and Palamountain all within striking distance on 40 points if Mooney slipped up.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     But there’s a good reason Mooney is rated as one of New Zealand’s best Stockcar drivers. Early in the final heat he got near the front of the race, and stayed out of trouble as others foundered.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     McIntosh and Palamountain both fell victim to mechanical gremlins, and whilst 992W Taylor Lampp took the win from Linklater, Mooney coasted home in fourth place.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     When the points were tallied, Mooney took the victory on 65, from Linklater (63) and Lampp (62), with Lockett and Baxter rounding out the top five.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     The second tier championship, sponsored by Fast Lane Spares was all action. 24P Stu Sowry took the money from 33V Trazarn Ryland-Annabell and 51V Peter Wapp, but the action came from 177P Jamie Tinetti and 78V Mark Johnston.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     Tinetti rolled Johnston at the southern end in heat two, with Johnston repaying the favour in the final heat. Tinetti exited his overturned car, went over and shook Johnston’s hand, and at one all, the battle was declared a draw!
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     The Karl Barritt Memorial Sidecars event was won by 96P Mark Whye/Jacob Cooper after a run-off with 1NZ Russell Stuart/Andrew Parker well after most of the crowd had left.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     The 96P bike was excluded from the final heat, but after lengthy discussions with the race officials 96P was reinstated, leaving the two combinations tied on points. 17P Tony Hislop/Matt Stewart finished third.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     So the first Elite Mechanical and Engineering Cup meeting was a terrific success. The Wanganui Stockcar and Speedway Club plans to make this an annual event.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: georgia, palatino">
                     Racing now takes a break during February, with a number of Speedway NZ titles being contested around the country. The next event at Oceanview is the New Zealand Minisprint Grand Prix on Saturday 3rd March.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                  </td>

                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Big Fields, Big Action
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    By Tony Stuart
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    What was originally scheduled pretty much as a club meeting took on a life of its own at Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway last night. The pits were full to overflowing, a good crowd was in hand, and the contact classes especially did not disappoint.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    So many Stockcars turned up for the Supercheap Stockcar Shootout that they had to be separated into two qualifying heats. The ten fastest laps over the two heats were used to pick the final field, with Dion Mooney taking the #1 position with as 17.65 second lap. 15P Luke Miers entered the contest in the second race, and in quick succession eliminated 42V Steve Read (who beat 52V Francis Potaka in the Shootout opener), 174V Brendon Learmonth, 24V Wayne Wright and 35P Matt Phippen. Miers then came up against 7V Damon Baxter in the race of the night.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    On the second lap Baxter spun Miers on the back straight, but couldn't get the nose of his Tank far enough ahead to complete the pass. Meanwhile Miers engaged reverse, and the two cars hugged the wall for more than half a lap in a furious battle. Baxter finally squeezed past Miers' car in sight of the finish line, but his momentum sent him sideways across the track. Miers was a fraction quicker to recover, and took a thrilling win. Both drivers were awarded prizes in the Charlie Berntsen Clubrooms afterwards for being the most entertaining drivers.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Miers was then eliminated by young Stratford driver William Hughes (99S), who looked destined to reach the final until he was punted out of the way by 81V Trevor Greig. In the money race, Mooney made short work of Greig, whose car left the track behind a tractor.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Mooney was in fine form, taking three wins in the Elite Mechanical Stockcars. Greig however had the last laugh taking out the PAUA Print Grand Slam. His new Rees Spank is right on the pace.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Eleven Superstocks fronted, although the brand-new 22S of Brent Pascoe and the 65V car being driven by Cameron Jurgens started all three heats off the back and were just looking for track time.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    9B Adam Groome took at hat-trick of wins, but it wasn't plain sailing. He won the first race from 9P Brett Hyslop and 41M Bruce Williams, leading most of the way.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Race two was a cracker, with Hyslop and 53V Morris Doyle trading the lead on sevral occasions. As the race counted down laps, Doyle tried to squeeze Hyslop into the pit straight wall but both cars spun, and Groome shot into the lead.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    The Sharp As Superstocks feature was another cracking race. Hyslop and 62S Regan Gernhoefer fell foul of 52B Regan O'Brien who did a masterly job blocking for his club-mate Groome. The 9B car crossed the line less than half a second ahead of Gernhoefer, with Hyslop and Doyle close behind.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Grant Loveridge (7V) has been the form driver in the Brian FM Production Saloons this season, and added the Ray Purdy Memorial title to the West Coast and Bay of Plenty championships he has won already. Loveridge had an easy win in the first heat from pole position, trailed 73V John Huijs for most of H2 before a late race pass, and was content to finish the final heat in second place to 62V Jon Morrison, ahead of Huijs, his closest rival.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Big fields of Youth and Adult Ministocks were on hand, with wins to 86s Bradley Korff (2), 91S Kyle Wilson and 26S Ella Sharpe in the NZME Adults. In the Youth grade, 76S Matthew Picard took out the first two races, with 43P Kyle Rowe winning the Remax Realty Feature. 35V Flynn Wild and 9W Cyrus Crook showed consistency, each placing in the top six in every race.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    The Youth Ministocks will be back in force next Saturday night for the second running of the Oceanview 5000. More than 60 cars will face the starter, in three separate age divisions. Supporting them will be Superstocks, Stockcars and the ever-popular Golden Oldies. Racing starts next Saturday at the earlier time of 6pm.
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: medium">
                    Thanks once again to all the drivers who travelled to support this meeting, and a special thank you to the track crew for their work over the Christmas break to recondition the track.
                   </p>

                  <td colspan="2" valign="top">
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <b>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                      All action at Oceanview
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </span>
                    </b>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     A cold
 south-easterly wind blew across Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway last
 night, but on the track, the action was red hot. A good crowd was on hand to
 watch the first two West Coast titles of the season decided, along with the
 opening round of the Oval Superstars Tour.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Sixteen cars
 fronted for the West Coast Stockcars title, a smaller than expected field due
 to early-season blues. Palmerston North driver Jackson Booth (12P) took the
 opening heat from Steve Read (42V) and Gerry Linklater (98V). Dion Mooney, clad
 in a Nelson Tigers body after damage last week, led early, but slipped back
 when his car went off-song.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Booth, a new
 entrant to the Stockcar class from Youth Ministocks, quickly discovered the
 reality of being the lone P car at Wanganui in the form of Mooney, who blocked
 him throughout the race. To Booth’s credit, he didn’t back away from the scrap.
 Linklater looked set to take a comfortable win until he suffered a fuel pump
 issue late in the race. That left Callum Sturzaker (15V) in the lead, and he
 took the win in just his second race in the Stockcar class, coming from the
 back of the grid. Trazarn Ryland-Annabell (33V) was second, with Shane Kells
 (87V) coming home in third.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     After two
 heats Sturzaker and Ryland-Annabell led with 27 points each. Two fourth places
 put Chet Swan (9V) one point back in third, with Read, Kells and Linklater
 still in contention.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Mark
 Johnston (78V) won the final heat from the front row, but the action was
 happening behind him. Ryland-Annabell was squeezed into the wall early in the
 race, pushing a wheelguard against his left rear tyre. He tried to continue but
 his title quest was over after six laps. Linklater chased hard, but couldn’t
 catch Johnston, whilst Swan drove a heady race for third. Read came home in
 fourth, ahead of Francis Potaka (52V) and Sturzaker.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     When the
 points were added up, Chet Swan took his first West Coast title, a reward for
 his consistency on the night. Callum Sturzaker’s Stockcar debut saw him finish
 second, with Steve Read completing an all-Wanganui podium.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Thirteen
 Superstocks fronted, with cars from Gisborne, Hawkes Bay, Palmerston North and
 Wanganui on hand. Scooter McIntosh (22V) took the first heat, holding off a
 charge from Wayne Hemi (591P) in the closing laps. Jordan Dare (2NZ) passed his
 team-mate Hemi on the line to grab second place.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Kenley Brown
 (17P) made no race of the second heat, beating home Hemi and young Wanganui
 driver Max Holloway (81V). McIntosh’s charge for the title stalled when he was
 walled at the northern end, and lost several places before recovering to finish
 9
                    </span>
                    <sup>
                     th
                    </sup>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     .
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Hemi had the
 points lead after two heats, two points ahead of Dare, with Holloway, McIntosh,
 Jake Baker (36P) and Rebecca Barr (34P) all tied three points further back.
 That set the scene for an exciting third heat, and it didn’t disappoint!
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Rebecca Barr
 leapt into the lead in Heat Three, and held on for an excellent win. Max
 Holloway’s race was over on the first lap when he clipped the wall on the back
 straight and rolled. He was the collected by Jayme Hemi in the 571P Tank,
 carried along for several seconds, then rolled again. Both Holloway and Hemi
 were uninjured, but their cars were parked.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     Later in the
 race Wayne Hemi slowed markedly. Baker came up behind Hemi, and sensing he was
 blocking, put in a huge shot that spun both cars around. Hemi responded by
 punting Baker’s car to the infield, and their battle continued for half a lap before
 Baker’s car expired. Hemi limped to the finish to pick up valuable points. Dare
 finished the race in second, with McIntosh showing good pace for third.
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Dare took
 the title on 33 points, with Hemi and Barr tied on 31, two ahead of McIntosh. A
 run off was held where Barr got the initial jump, then was spun by Hemi on Turn
 One. Hemi’s car also turned around, and Barr was the fastest to recover, going
 on to win and claim second place.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Seventeen
 cars entered for the opening round of the Oval Superstars Tour. A warm-up was
 as good as it got for defending series champion Christian Hermansen (1NZ) when
 his new car developed electrical gremlins on the grid, and his night ended.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Karl McGill
 (93P) took the first heat win, Nathan Jefferies (8P) took the second, and Shane
 Dewar (41P) took an incident-packed third heat which saw Glen McCutcheon’s 44P
 car tip over, then a few laps later a four car collision on the southern bend.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     With a win
 and a third place from his three heats, McGill earned pole position for the
 20-lap final, the Oval Open. Kuriger took the other place on the front row with
 Todd Phillips (37P), Lydia Dickinson (75S) and Jordan McLeod (97P) filling the
 next places on the grid.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Fifteen cars
 started the final, but it was a race of attrition with several yellows, and
 another multi-car pile-up at the southern end. McGill would build up a lead,
 only to see it erased each time the yellows came on. He showed composure
 however to nail the restarts, and he powered away to win by four seconds from
 Dewar, with Jefferies in third. In the process McGill broke Hermansen’s lap
 record, setting a new mark of 13.794, outstanding speed on a track that was
 starting to slick off at the end of the meeting.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     Grant
 Loveridge (7V) took a clean sweep of wins in the Brian FM Production Saloons,
 whilst Seth Hodge (12P) with two wins and Louis Redshaw (7P) were winners in
 the Remax Realty Youth Ministocks.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <o:p>
                     <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     </span>
                    </o:p>
                    <span style="font-size: medium">
                     A good field
 of sidecars turned on some terrific racing, with John Hannan/Bryce Rose (19V)
 almost taking a clean sweep, but not being able to catch Wingnut/Braden Rose
 (5P) in the final race after a slow start.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     <span style="font-family: Calibri, sans-serif">
                      It was an excellent night’s racing, and the
 Wanganui Stockcar and Speedway Club especially thanks all those competitors who
 travelled to compete. After a week off next weekend, racing will resume on
 Saturday 25
                     </span>
                     <sup style="font-family: Calibri, sans-serif">
                      th
                     </sup>
                     <span style="font-family: Calibri, sans-serif">
                      November at 7pm.
                     </span>
                    </span>
                   </p>

                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     <b>
                      Second time lucky
                     </b>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     The sun shone over Supercheap Auto Oceanview Family Speedway on Sunday. After the disappointment of the previous week, local racers and fans got their first taste of speedway action for the new season.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000">
                    <span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     The Elite Mechanical Stockcars provided plenty of action. Mark Johnston (78V) took the first heat, with Dion Mooney taking the second race. Mooney looked in control of the feature until a blown right rear tyre let Dennis Black (82V) through for a popular win.                      </o:p>
                     A number of
 drivers cruised the pole line, looking for victims. Berntsen’s son Brendon ucas fired Trevor Greig into the wall at the town end, getting revenge for a hit by
 Greig the race  before.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     Johnston was
 looking comfortable until he came under attack from Jason Wright, dropping him
 down the field.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     As Johnston
 faltered, McIntosh took the race win with Potaka close behind in second. That
 was enough to win the Charlie Berntsen Trophy. Johnston finished second, with
 Sandy Flett in  third place  overall.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     Potaka was
 over the moon. He told those in the Charlie Berntsen Clubrooms at the
 post-meeting presentations that Berntsen had been his mentor when he started
 racing over 20 years  ago.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     “This is the
 one I’ve always wanted to win” he said.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     The last
 title to be decided was the Zodiac Signs West Coast Minisprints, which was
 taken out by 1NZ Christian Hermansen.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     The superb
 track conditions saw almost half a second carved off the Minisprint lap record,
 first by Karl McGill, and then by Hermansen with a sizzling 13.95 second lap.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     The
 Minisprints have become a popular class for Oceanview spectators. The Wanganui
 Stockcar and Speedway Club was recognised by the organisers of the Oval
 Superstars Tour  winning the Best  Event of the 2016-17 OST season, an award
 voted by the series competitors.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     All that was
 left was the annual Demolition Derby, and that delivered lots of entertainment
 for the crowd as the season wound down. Joshua Annabell drove the last car
 moving after he  and Best Stirrer  Aaron Rose battered one another’s cars into
 submission.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    <span style="font-size: 10pt">
                     <o:p>
                     </o:p>
                     It was a
 fitting, metal-crushing end to an entertaining season of speedway at Oceanview.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">
                    For photo's of the race meeting please head over to the Oceanview Speedway facebook page
                   </p>
                   <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt">

                  <td colspan="2" valign="top">
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-size: medium">
                    <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Great night; great racing
                    </span>
                    <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-size: medium">
                    <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     A quick wrap-up of last night's action at Supercheap Autos Oceanview Family Speedway:
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-size: medium">
                    <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     * Congratulations to John "Handbag" Hannon and Bryce Rose (19V) for their win in the Karl Barritt Memorial Sidecars. Two wins and a second place in the heats put them in a run-off with the 21P combo of Glen Murray and Ann Plummer, which the 19V bike won comfortably, then blew a clutch before Handbag and Bryce could do a victory lap. * Who knew that the shaving off of Aaron Rose's beard would make such a difference to the weight of the front of the 34V machine. He pulled wheelstands every race off the start line, including in the run-off for third with Matthew and Derek Ramsay (6P), who took the final place on the podium. * Four wins from four for 2NZ Dion Mooney in the combined Stockcars and Superstocks. That included the Grand Slam, where Mooney bolted on the Hoosier tyres he will use in Palmerston North next weekend when he races for the Nelson Tigers. And we saw the debut of Scooter McIntosh's new Superstock; it looks and sounds awesome, and will be a real flyer once the bugs are ironed out. * Plenty of action (and a few relegations) in both the Youth and Adult Ministocks. Wins were well shared around. * A beautiful night at the Oval by the Ocean, with hardly any wind; great speedway weather. And a very poignant moment at the start of the meeting as competitors paid their own tribute to the late Karl Barritt. It was also great to have Karl's father Clive and his sister Anneka at the meeting, and making the presentations; thank you, and we hope we did Karl's memory justice.
                    </span>
                   </p>
                   <p style="color: #000000; font-size: medium">
                    <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif">
                     Lastly a big thanks to Pete from Go Slideways for taking on the infield photography role. Eugene was at a wedding, although he did make a cameo appearance later in the evening. Thanks for the awesome photos Pete, and we know there are a whole lot more to come.
                    </span>
                   </p>
