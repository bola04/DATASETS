    <br/>
    <strong>
     The Riva Yares Gallery
    </strong>
    , founded in 
 1964, with locations in
    <strong>
     Scottsdale, Arizona and Santa Fe, New 
 Mexico
    </strong>
    , offers two of the
    <strong>
     primary art spaces
    </strong>
    in the 
 country for the exhibition of
    <strong>
     museum-level modern and contemporary art.
    </strong>
    <br/>
    As the longest established contemporary gallery in the region, with 
 over 15,000 square feet of gallery space, Riva Yares has orchestrated 
 exhibitions for some of the most important modern and contemporary painters 
 including
    <strong>
     Milton Avery, Helen Frankenthaler, Adolph Gottlieb, Hans 
 Hofmann, Alex Katz, Yves Klein, Morris Louis, Kenneth Noland, Jules Olitski, 
 George Segal, Esteban Vicente, Tom Wesselmann
    </strong>
    , among others.
   </p>
   <p>
    Of the over fifty artists represented by Riva Yares at some juncture over the 
 past thirty-eight years, more than a dozen have international roots and include 
 Armand Arman, Fernando Botero, Carlo Maria Mariani, Roberto MÃ¡rquez, Roberto 
 Matta, and JesÃºs Soto.
   </p>
   <p>
    Several well-known sculptors showing at both locations are
    <strong>
     Fletcher 
 Benton, Johan Creten, Rico Eastman, Robert Graham, and Manuel Neri
    </strong>
    .
   </p>
   <p>
    The gallery publishes select catalogues and books that document the scope of 
 solo-exhibitions inclusive of critical essays, expansive color reproductions, 
 and concise biographies. Recent books include:
    <strong>
     Milton Avery: The Nude 
 and Other Subjects: 1929-1963 (64 pages with 50 color illustrations) and Esteban 
 Vicente: A Retrospective View: 1951-2000 (72 pages with 48 color 
 illustrations).
    </strong>
   </p>
   <p>
    <strong>
     The Riva Yares Gallery has been profiled in virutally every major art 
 publication including Art in America, ArtNews, Art &amp; Auction, as well as 
 Architectural Digest.
    </strong>
   </p>
   <p>
    <strong>
     The Riva Yares Gallery has been a member of the ADAA (Art Dealers 
 Association of America) for the past ten years.
    </strong>
    Founder and Director
    <strong>
     Riva Yares
    </strong>
    sits on the board of many arts-related entities and 
 is active within the full continuum of cultural affairs both in Santa Fe and in 
 Scottsdale. The Riva Yares Foundation has recently underwritten in part, the 
 current traveling Milton Avery Retrospective (Milwaukee Art Museum, The Norton, 
 Palm Beach, FL, UCLA's Hammer Museum, CA) and the educational programs for the 
 forthcoming Hans Hofmann exhibit at the Scottsdale Museum of Contemporary Art 
 featuring selections from the UC-Berkeley Museum collection.
   </p>
   <p>
    Scottsdale, Arizona and the Phoenix metropolitan area in general have grown 
 in population by over 40% over the past decade. Scottsdale, with more resorts 
 per capital than any other city in the country, offers a vast array of 
 recreational activities with clear skies and sunny days prevalent 95% of the 
 year.
   </p>
   <p>
    After having entered its fifth decade,
    <strong>
     The Riva Yares Gallery
    </strong>
    is committed in continuing to present the highest level of modern and 
 contemporary art in the country.
