           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/charlotte/2018/03/paper-flowers-for-springtime.html">
             paper flowers for springtime
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <h3>
              This weekend, I taught a workshop on how to make paper flowers at
              <a href="https://www.mohonk.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
               Mohonk Mountain House
              </a>
              for their Girlfriend's Getaway Weekend. In a little under an hour, everyone made at least one lovely, unique crepe paper flower—and many of the students made several.
             </h3>

             <h2>
              <a id="Supplies">
              </a>
              Paper Flower Supplies
             </h2>
             <p>
              For these paper flowers, we used special German-made crepe paper by Lia Griffith. This paper has unique properties, different from the crepe paper carried by most stores for streamer decorations. One pack of extra fine crepe paper gives you enough paper for hundreds of these flowers.
             </p>
             <p>

             <p>
             </p>

              You'll also need floral tape. Lia Griffith makes this tape in several colors, though any standard floral tape will do. For this project, we used roughly 12 inches of standard forest green tape per flower.
             </p>
             <p>
              <iframe frameborder="0" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="//ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;OneJS=1&amp;Operation=GetAdHtml&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;source=ac&amp;ref=tf_til&amp;ad_type=product_link&amp;tracking_id=housewrenstud-20&amp;marketplace=amazon&amp;region=US&amp;placement=B01JYSL6HE&amp;asins=B01JYSL6HE&amp;linkId=b563fbdbd267bbffe53b466bd7a10fa0&amp;show_border=true&amp;link_opens_in_new_window=true&amp;price_color=333333&amp;title_color=0066c0&amp;bg_color=ffffff" style="width: 120px; height: 240px;">

              </a>
              also yields roughly 100 flowers, because you can cut each 16-inch wire in half for an 8-inch stem.
             </p>
             <p>
              <iframe frameborder="0" marginheight="0" marginwidth="0" scrolling="no" src="//ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;OneJS=1&amp;Operation=GetAdHtml&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;source=ac&amp;ref=tf_til&amp;ad_type=product_link&amp;tracking_id=housewrenstud-20&amp;marketplace=amazon&amp;region=US&amp;placement=B010P9O04M&amp;asins=B010P9O04M&amp;linkId=b547e413c1b35eec47b66dd72cfeb901&amp;show_border=true&amp;link_opens_in_new_window=true&amp;price_color=333333&amp;title_color=0066c0&amp;bg_color=ffffff" style="width: 120px; height: 240px;">

              Needle-nose, or long-nose, pliers aren't essential for this project but they are good to have in your craft arsenal. If you plan to make lots of flowers, I recommend getting a pair. If you don't have a pair for this project, I've included a substitute trick in the instructions below.
             </p>

             <h2>
              <a id="Template">
              </a>
              Paper Flower Template
             </h2>

             <h2>
              <a id="Instructions">
              </a>
              Instructions
             </h2>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               To make a simple poppy, you'll need:
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <p>
              For the stem
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               One 8-inch floral wire
              </li>
              <li>
               One 12-inch strip of floral tape
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              For the petals
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Two strips of crepe paper, each 4 inches wide and 19.6 inches long (the full length of the paper). Cut these length-wise so the grain of the paper is vertical. I recommend choosing two complimentary colors, like light pink and peach or white and ivory.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              For the flower center (or stamen)
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               One strip of crepe paper, 1 inch wide and 4 inches long, cut length-wise so the grain of the paper is vertical. I recommend choosing a color that contrasts your petals, like plum or cranberry or rose red.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              For the leaves
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               One strip of green crepe paper, 4 inches wide and 8 inches long, cut vertically.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              Print-out of "Flower Patterns" template
             </p>
             <p>
              Scissors
             </p>
             <p>
              Glue (Tacky Glue or any white glue)
             </p>
             <p>
              Needle-nose pliers
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e364d4970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Paper-Flowers-Class-Instructions-Med - 2" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e364d4970c img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e364d4970c-320wi" title="Paper-Flowers-Class-Instructions-Med - 2"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               Assembly instructions:
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <ol>
              <li>
               From the "Flower Patterns" template above, cut out shapes A, C, and D.
              </li>
              <li>
               Fold both pieces of your petal crepe papers in half, then in half again.
              </li>
              <li>
               Lay shape A over your folded petal papers and either trace the shape in pen or just cut around it. You should end up with 8 petals (four in each color).
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09fc6074970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Petals " class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09fc6074970d img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09fc6074970d-120wi" title="Petals "/>
               </a>
              </li>
              <li>
               Holding one petal, put your thumbs in the center of and fingers behind it. The grain of the paper should run parallel with your thumbs. Press with your thumbs and stretch the paper outward with your fingers. This gives each petal a natural-looking, 3-D curve.
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e367b1970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Stretching petals" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e367b1970c img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e367b1970c-120wi" title="Stretching petals"/>
               </a>
              </li>
              <li>
               With your pliers, create a small loop at one end of the floral wire. It should resemble a miniature shepard's hook. If you don't have needle-nosed pliers, you can simply curl the wire around a pencil or some thin, round object.
              </li>
              <li>
               Lay shape D over your flower center strip of paper. Make small snips down the long side of the paper to create a fringe. (The template shape D shows how deep to make the cuts.)
              </li>
              <li>
               Dab a very small amount of glue along the opposite length of the flower center strip (the side you did not snip).
              </li>
              <li>
               Lay the hook of your floral wire stem at one end of the flower center strip, over the glued area.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09fc6332970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Fringe wrapping" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09fc6332970d img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09fc6332970d-120wi" title="Fringe wrapping"/>
               </a>
              </li>
              <li>
               Roll/wrap your flower center strip around the hook, pinching as you go to adhere the paper and glue. The fringed edge should remain free. When you finish, it should resemble a paper tassle or a stamen of a flower.
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9593134970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Finished fringe" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9593134970b img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9593134970b-120wi" title="Finished fringe"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </li>
              <li>
               Dab a scant amount of glue at the base of one petal. Add it to the outside of the stamen so that the petal extends a half an inch beyond the fringe. (It's okay to trim the stamen if you would like it to be shorter.)
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e367e6970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Adding petals" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e367e6970c img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2e367e6970c-120wi" title="Adding petals"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </li>
              <li>
               Adhere the next petal slightly to the right of the previous one and repeat these steps, spiraling around the stamen center, until you've used up all the petals. (You can choose to alternate colors or use one petal color on the inside and one petal color on the outside.)
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9592e79970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Paper-Flowers-Class-Instructions-Med - 1" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9592e79970b img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9592e79970b-120wi" title="Paper-Flowers-Class-Instructions-Med - 1"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </li>
              <li>
               Lay shape C over your green paper and cut out your leaves. I recommend three leaves. Adhere your leaves in a similar manner, applying them to the base of the flower you have just created. These look nice when placed asymmetrically: two on one side and one on the other, but be as creative as you like!
               <br/>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9592e97970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Paper-flowers-class-cynthia" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9592e97970b img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c9592e97970b-120wi" title="Paper-flowers-class-cynthia"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </li>
              <li>
               To finish the flower, place the top of the floral tape strip against the base of your flower. Wrap it around the stem at the top, using the warmth of your fingers to activate the sticky wax coating of the floral paper. Once it is sticky and adhering to the base of your flower and leaves, hold the tape downward at a 45-degree angle to the wire stem and then twirl and stretch the tape around the stem to cover the wire all the way to the bottom.The action is like making a candy cane and the tape should become sticky as you work with it to wrap the stem.
              </li>
              <li>
               Voila! Your flower is complete. You can bend, ruffle, and stretch the petals, leaves, and stamen more for a fuller, more natural look.
              </li>
             </ol>
             <p>
             </p>
             <h2>
              Ideas for Using Paper Flowers
             </h2>
             <p>
              Paper flowers are really cute on a wrapped gift, in an old glass bottle or bud vase, as a table arrangement at a bridal shower, tucked here and there behind a frame on a wall, as a Mother's Day Gift with a quote or message on a tag, or as part of a big bouquet for a hostess gift or get-well present.
             </p>
             <p>
              They would also be lovely wrapped around a ponytail-holder in your hair or tucked behind your ear with a barrette or bobby pin. You can clip the stem short and tuck it in a bun as well.
             </p>
             <p>
              The art and history of paper flowers goes back thousands of years, according to
              <a href="https://www.architecturaldigest.com/story/paper-flowers-the-global-ancient-roots-of-a-contemporary-maker-fixation" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
               a recent article in Architectural Digest
              </a>
              . The craft has been popular in China, Mexico, and Europe for centuries. It's an easy craft to update and the results are so pretty and gratifying.
             </p>

           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/charlotte/2018/01/well-hello-2018-.html">
             well, hello, 2018
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57d1c970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Doodles" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57d1c970d img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57d1c970d-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="Doodles"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Wishing you all a happy new year!
             </p>
             <p>
              I just updated my sidebar date and cannot believe this is year 12 for the blog! I'm going to try to be more playful and creative this year. But I think my word for the year will be Focus. Really. I want to be more attentive to the hours, to the days. It all goes by so fast.
             </p>
             <p>
              And I would like to be more present here on the blog.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca0ee970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Stitchnight" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca0ee970c img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca0ee970c-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="Stitchnight"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              With the cold weather, most of us are focused on warmth and comfort. I'm letting the holiday decorations linger because they do help with the deep darkness of Vermont in January. And this is the prettiest and freshest tree I've ever had.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57f00970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Tree" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57f00970d img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57f00970d-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="Tree"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Snow falls as I write this. So happy to be tucked inside with stitching, tea and twinkling lights.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca114970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Twinkles" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca114970c img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca114970c-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="Twinkles"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Over the holidays, my children and grandbabies were here for a visit. What a great gift to have them filling the house with giggles, stories and activity.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57d8f970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Betsy" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57d8f970d img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09e57d8f970d-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="Betsy"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              And little adventures!
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca143970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Snowwalk" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca143970c img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca143970c-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="Snowwalk"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Wow, it was 5 degrees or something and Vince could not wait to get out into the snowy banks. We walked to the library which is right across the street. Perfect escape. Still, it was intense out there. His dad and I were ready to get right inside again, but Vince loved being out there in it. Yes, he asked if he could drive one of the junkyard lawnmowers. Don't think any of them have moved even an inch in years. First chance he gets, he will probably repair one and drive it off down the street!
             </p>
             <p>
              Hoping you all are staying warm and keeping busy with all the things that make you happy to begin a new year. So many possibilities!
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2cca2af970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">

              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              That grand old poem called winter.
             </p>
             <p>
              ~ Henry David Thoreau
             </p>

             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2bf9bca970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">

             <p>
              Summer is going to be colorful and fun in Vermont!
             </p>
             <p>
              Meleen and I are hosting a painting workshop with
              <a href="http://www.jennifer-nelson-artists.com/jennifer-orkin-lewis" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
               August Wren
              </a>
              over the weekend of June 21-24. Jennifer Orkin Lewis is the noted illustrator known as August Wren. Her work is in Anthropologie, Kate Spade and a number of books including those with Chronicle publications. She will teach technique and lessons in painting with gouache and share our getaway weekend with guests. Before I moved to Vermont, Jen and I were neighbors. Recently, I visited her studio where I had a chance to admire her beautiful work with sneak peeks for future projects. We are so excited to have her join us as a guest teacher!
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2bf8de2970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">

             <p>
              Two weekends. Two Jennifers!
             </p>
             <p>
              We have added a second weekend with Jenn McGlon of
              <a href="https://www.etsy.com/shop/noodleandlou" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
               Noodle and Lou
              </a>
              . A maker of darling art dolls, she is one of our most favorite teachers. We always have the best time with her and she is coming back the weekend of June 14-17!
             </p>
             <p>
              With these bathing beauties!
             </p>
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b8d2bf9bd8970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">

             </p>

             </p>

              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01b7c935379c970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">

           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/charlotte/2017/06/fall-getaway-info.html">
             fall getaway info
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09a29e5e970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="VTSeptember2017" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09a29e5e970d img-responsive" src="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c991753ef01bb09a29e5e970d-400wi" style="width: 375px;" title="VTSeptember2017"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Registration is open for our fall retreat! We will be making papier mache bird plaques and stitched hoop art collages. Our guest artist is fabric and knit designer,
              <a href="http://betzwhite.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">
               Betz White
              </a>
              . Please send an email to fallvermontgetaway@gmail.com if you would like to attend or get more information.
             </p>
             <p>
              The event takes place in 2 charming, vintage 1850 farmhouses that are side by side in southern Vermont. Meleen and her husband just built an event barn which will be ready next fall - an extra bonus!
             </p>
             <div>
              We spend most of our time at the houses, making art, eating, laughing, catching up, getting to know each other. Lots of time on the covered porches or in front of the fire weather depending. It's a very cozy time of year, peak season in autumnal Vermont. We also look for local adventures and tag sales to scout out. Meleen and I have a number of artistic friends, artisan farmers, makers, growers nearby too.
             </div>
             <div>
             </div>
             <div>
              Meleen and Kana, our friend and assistant, prepare all the home-cooked food there at the house and it is delicious! There are several posts here on my blog about past retreats. (Use the sidebar link for Vermont Getaway.)
             </div>
             <div>
             </div>
             <div>
              Our super-fun spring event just wrapped up yesterday. What a great, memorable weekend! Lots of pictures to share with you in a post to follow.
             </div>
             <div>
             </div>
             <div>
              On IG you can follow us at #
              <wbr>
              </wbr>
              meleenandcharlottesvermontgeta
              <wbr>
              </wbr>
              way - and write to us with questions at fallvermontgetaway(at)gmail.com.
             </div>
