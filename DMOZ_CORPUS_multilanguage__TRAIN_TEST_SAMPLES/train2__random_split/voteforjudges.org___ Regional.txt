         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Welcome
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          March 21, 2018
         </p>
         <p>
          On behalf of VoteforJudges.org, I want to thank each of you who came to our website to learn about the judicial candidates listed on your ballot.  More than 55,000 people used VoteforJudges.org and we appreciate each of you.  It will be awhile before the results are official, but for the unofficial winners of the judicial races, please click
          <a href="http://www.voteforjudges.org/wp-content/uploads/UNOFFICIAL-JUDICIAL-RESULTS-2018.pdf">
           NEXT
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           In November 2018, you will be voting for dozens of judges seeking retention for another six-year term on the bench.  For some of you, you will be voting in a handful of contested judicial races.  VoteforJudges.org will provide information for prospective voters in all of these contests.  In the meantime, f
           <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
            or more information about promoting fair courts for all people, please visit the website of Chicago Appleseed Fund for Justice,
            <a href="http://www.chicagoappleseed.org">
             <span style="color: #0066cc;">
              www.chicagoappleseed.org
             </span>
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           Again, thank you.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           Malcolm Rich, Executive Director of the VoteforJudges.org Campaign..
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          February 27, 2018
         </p>
         <p>
          Welcome to www.VoteforJudges.org.  The Chicago Appleseed Fund for Justice created the www.Voteforjudges campaign to provide a nonpartisan effort aimed at judicial voter education.  The Committee to Elect Qualified Judges, a political action committee dedicated to educating voters about judicial elections, is proud to sponsor the www.voteforjudges.org campaign.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="background: white; color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           We provide this resource because judges make all kinds of decisions, from the very personal, such as child custody decisions, to those with much broader impact. The decisions that judges make affect our lives, both directly and indirectly, often for many years to come. We never know when a judge we vote for today will make a decision that will affect your life and the lives of your family and friends.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           The Cook County judicial system includes judges that sit on the Illinois Supreme Court, the Illinois Appellate Court, and trial courts that are divided into countywide and smaller “subcircuit” districts. Countywide judges are elected by the voters of the entire Cook County. Once elected, the candidate can be assigned to any division of the Circuit Court. In addition, judges can be elected through sub circuit elections.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           The 15 subcircuits are geographic areas that all lie within Cook County. Subcircuit candidates must reside within the geographic boundaries of the sub circuit, and they are selected by the voters of that subcircuit only. Once elected, however, the judges from the subcircuits have the same powers as judges elected countywide, and can also be placed in any division of the Circuit Court, as well. Each voter in Cook County will vote in countywide races and in a limited number of races to fill vacancies within their subcircuit.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           It is our responsibility to vote for judges we can trust to make impartial decisions based on fair consideration of the facts and law. In some cases, this means that judges may make unpopular or controversial decisions. We need to trust that our judges can make those right decisions without regard to political or economic pressure. That is why it is so important to vote for qualified people to be judges.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            The VoteforJudges.org website reports the results of 12 bar groups which receive information about candidates’ performance and recommend whether he or she is qualified to be a judge.  We also include the judicial endorsements of the Chicago Tribune. You can find those results by going to the
            <a href="http://www.voteforjudges.org/judicial-evaluations-results/">
             Judicial Evaluations and Results tab
            </a>
            .
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color: #444444; font-family: 'Helvetica',sans-serif; font-size: 10.5pt;">
           For more information about promoting fair courts for all people, please visit the website of Chicago Appleseed Fund for Justice,
           <a href="http://www.chicagoappleseed.org">
            www.chicagoappleseed.org
           </a>
           .
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Malcolm C. Rich
         </p>
         <p>
          Executive Director, VoteforJudges.org Campaign
         </p>
