             <h2>
              Did You Know?  From the Medical Nail Technician
             </h2>
             <ol>
              <li>
               The State of Texas has enforced strict rules for Pedicure and Manicure Spas due to at least 2 Deaths caused by infections through the unclean pedicure baths.
              </li>
              <li>
               Pedicure Baths must be washed and soaked for 10 minutes in an Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) approved disinfectant between every client. Or a liner may be used in a jetless bath.
              </li>
              <li>
               Every night the screens and jets must be unscrewed and soaked for one hour of all jet pedicure tubs.
              </li>
              <li>
               Stainless Steel implements should be sterilized in an autoclave.
              </li>
              <li>
               Sterilization is the only form of infection control to kill spores in viruses and bacteria, including staph infections.
              </li>
              <li>
               It is important to throw out all files, buffers and disposable implements to protect you, the technician and the next client.
              </li>
              <li>
               Cuticle Grooming-Cuticles serve an important function, they block bacteria from entering the skin and body.  Clip only when necessary and pushing them back is a SAFE option.
              </li>
              <li>
               Foot Care-Never shave your legs the day you have a pedicure as  germs can enter the body in freshly shaven pores.
              </li>
              <li>
               Always tell your nail technician if you are a diabetic, diabetics are more sensitive and skin can be broken more easily.
              </li>
              <li>
               Artificial Nails can cause damage to your nails that may take months to repair.  After Artificial Nails, Acrylics, or Solar Nails are removed they leave nails weak, brittle and split.
              </li>
              <li>
               Under an acrylic nail a fungus may be growing and you may not be aware of it.
              </li>
              <li>
               Onycomycosis, Fungal Nail is extremely contagious and is often passed along from client to client through unclean implements, unclean baths.
              </li>
              <li>
               Always look to see if your nail technician has gloves on, this will insure a safe, spa experience.
              </li>
             </ol>
