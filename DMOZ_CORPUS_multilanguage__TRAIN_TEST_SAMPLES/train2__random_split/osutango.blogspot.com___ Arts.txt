                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://osutango.blogspot.com/2016/08/the-philosophy-of-tango-dance.html">
                       The philosophy of  Tango Dance
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2611412742280491176" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      Buenos Aires, Argentina time showed at 17.00 a few hours again I will enjoy live tango dance. Chanta Cuatro in a restaurant in downtown Buenos Aires. Tango dance is a traditional dance from Argentina, the dance that combines compactness as well as exotic a movement being performed simultaneously. Every beat combined with typical accompaniment music, well a new spectacle in life and my experience. Interesting!!! I was so excited to watch this tango dance directly. Dance may be as long as I see through the glass screen. Immediately I rushed ... for the bath.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Exactly at 18.00 the car which takes me to the restaurant chanta cuatro has been waiting in the lobby of the Hilton hotel. I rushed down to immediately head to the restaurant. Carlos Gardel is the name of the area I was going to go, it was said in this area is the place to dance tango. Here is the headquarters of tango dancers who are experienced in Argentina.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Passing through city areas in Argentina gave a picture of a country that is so excited. Imagine in pinggiran2 café I watched so many people danced with such passion. Tarian2 tango can be seen around the streets of Buenos Aires. Replica Evita peron's former first lady of Argentina ever plastered building in downtown Buenos Aires. Finally we arrived at Chanta cuatro. The atmosphere of the restaurant is similar to the usual telenovela2 I see in the glass display. Not how long they start serving dinner entrees.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Along with the arrival of food, the curtains at the front desk we were open. Looks hand over some player music complete with its launch of the show. The beat and very typical tango music began to sound. While the dancers began to enter the stage! Wonderful, a sensational experience I was having. Music interspersed with dances that the longer the more adrenaline that makes my feet ever join the stamping. Sexy tango dancers began to perform the action. Combined with the typical steak and wine Argentina. I so enjoy a night full of new experiences for me.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      The end of the show, I am reliving a philosophy of tango dance. An idiom that says "it takes two to tango" reminds me of the sense of the importance of a co-operation in any case. As is the case in the tango dance is so enchanting and sensational. You need the cooperation of both parties. Such a beautiful night and a dance that was so riveting.

                     <meta content="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Fd62vOOp4FY/Udxp5PrwwfI/AAAAAAAAAlw/D59riXngw_A/s200/mike-eblen-tango.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://osutango.blogspot.com/2013/07/tango-series-in-newport-with-mike-eblen.html">
                       Tango Series in Newport with Mike Eblen
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8151644592289870375" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      Spread the word: Mike Eblen is back in Newport Oregon later this month to teach a series of classes withhis new partner Jeanine VanSise.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Fd62vOOp4FY/Udxp5PrwwfI/AAAAAAAAAlw/D59riXngw_A/s1600/mike-eblen-tango.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="mike-eblen-tango" border="0" height="200" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Fd62vOOp4FY/Udxp5PrwwfI/AAAAAAAAAlw/D59riXngw_A/s200/mike-eblen-tango.jpg" title="" width="178"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <b>
                        Check out these Argentine Tango lessons that will be offered there July 26, 27th and 28:
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         <b>
                          Class 1, Friday, July 26th 7:00pm
                         </b>
                         -  Nice Beginnings and Memorable Resolutions. In this class we will 
 find ways to expand our appreciation of when a dance begins and ends and
  how we can facilitate a more enjoyable experience for our partner.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         <b>
                          Class 2, Saturday July 27th 1:00pm
                         </b>
                         -  Finding My Axis, Your Axis and Our Axis. Start your Saturday with 
 exercises help strengthen the awareness that you have of your balance as
  well as your partner's.  We will explore how to maintain stability as 
 the dance becomes more challenging.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         <b>
                          Class 3, Saturday afternoon 2:30pm
                         </b>
                         -  Making Sense of Musicality. It's no secret that tango music is 
 rather complicated, with all the layers of sound, how can we begin to 
 dance with the music?  Mike and Mari will help make sense of this 
 challenging topic.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <ul>
                       <li>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         <b>
                          Class 4, Sunday 1:00pm
                         </b>
                         -  Taking Turns to the Next Level. What would a weekend class series be
  without covering what is arguably the hardest part of tango, turns!  
 There will be wisdom and guidance for all regardless of skill level.
                        </span>
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <ul style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <li>
                        <b>
                         Class 5, Sunday 2:30pm
                        </b>
                        -  Deepening Connection. Finish off 
 this weekend class series with Mike's favorite(and most popular) class. 
  We will be finding ways to make the steps we are familiar with feel 
 exponentially better.
                       </li>
                      </ul>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <b>
                        Classes are $10 a la carte, registering for the full weekend costs 
 only $40.  All classes will be accessible for dancers of any experience 
 level.
                       </b>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Classes are at Heather's house:
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       567 SE Vista Dr Newport
                       <br/>
                       for more information or to schedule a private please contact Heather :
                       <br/>
                       hjoyh at me dot com
                       <br/>
                       541-272-2019
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: medium;">
                        <b>
                         About Jeanine:
                        </b>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bFyhcDh6Th0/UdxpHCTrDyI/AAAAAAAAAlg/LjxJtHQQbMI/s1600/jeanine-vansise.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="jeanine-van-sise" border="0" height="254" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-bFyhcDh6Th0/UdxpHCTrDyI/AAAAAAAAAlg/LjxJtHQQbMI/s320/jeanine-vansise.jpg" title="" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Jeanine VanSise 
 began dancing tango, by accident, in 2007 and has not stopped since! She
  first studied Tango with Vicky Ayers and Elizabeth Wartluf at The 
 University of Oregon, but has since taken advantage of the chance to 
 study with teachers from all over the world. She feels most fortunate 
 for the chances she has had to study extensively with Dominic Bridge, 
 Rebecca Rorick-Smith, and Kara Wenham &amp; Javier Antar. Most of the 
 time you will find Jeanine DJing or dancing in Eugene or Portland, but 
 she did have the chance to spend a Summer studying and dancing in 
 Argentina.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        In 2009 
 Jeanine took on an active role in the Eugene tango community when she 
 took over both the Beginning and Intermediate tango classes at The 
 University of Oregon. Jeanine also was the DJ and co-host of Eugene’s 
 popular Milonga Entre Suenos with Carmela Hill-Burke for four years.
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       When teaching, 
 Jeanine emphasizes the chance to connect both to a partner, and to 
 music. While technique and strong fundamentals are emphasized in her 
 classes she finds that her strength is helping students find both a love
  for the dance, and a love for connecting with a variety of partners. 
 Jeanine is a teacher outside of tango, and because of that students 
 comment that her classes are accessible, full of passion, and create a 
 warm, comfortable, environment for learning.
                      </span>

                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3eeb3s5xod4/UavX9LBEFPI/AAAAAAAAAlA/Z3YAFqbg4nY/s1600/tango-catherine-liggett.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="145" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3eeb3s5xod4/UavX9LBEFPI/AAAAAAAAAlA/Z3YAFqbg4nY/s400/tango-catherine-liggett.jpg" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Very shortly after this party, Dedra and I will be moving to Washington DC to begin our new life there.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       The party is at 
 the home of Catherine and Gretchen, and has a large hard wood floor to 
 dance on. Heels are fine to wear, as long as they are non-scratching or 
 marking.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        It is, of course, totally free!
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       If you feel inspired, bring along some vegetarian goodies for everyone to snack on, and/or something to drink.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <b>
                       <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        Please invite your tango-dancing friends!
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       DJs TBA- Message Catherine (cliggett at facebook dot com) if you would be interested in contributing to the music for the night.
                      </span>

                    Monday, May 20, 2013

                  <div class="date-posts">
                   <div class="post-outer">
                    <div class="post hentry uncustomized-post-template" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                     <meta content="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-oA6c_GZkE9g/UZqx_QpTbDI/AAAAAAAAAko/xOyT_wqDbks/s200/tango-wedding-3.JPG" itemprop="image_url"/>

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://osutango.blogspot.com/2013/05/the-future-of-tango-at-osu.html">
                       The Future of Tango at OSU
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-794623387880075708" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       For those who're 
 behind on the plot - My wife Dedra Demaree and I started a Tango club at
  Oregon State University last year and, except for my trip back to South
  Africa, have been teaching there more or less ever since.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-oA6c_GZkE9g/UZqx_QpTbDI/AAAAAAAAAko/xOyT_wqDbks/s1600/tango-wedding-3.JPG" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="tango teachers" border="0" height="200" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-oA6c_GZkE9g/UZqx_QpTbDI/AAAAAAAAAko/xOyT_wqDbks/s200/tango-wedding-3.JPG" title="" width="133"/>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       We've always been
  partial to helping beginners find their feet, and so the "nothing 
 fancy, just good fundamentals" motto is what permeated our tenure here -
  but as things have worked out in 2013 - due to work and life 
 circumstances we will moving to Washington DC at the end of June.
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         OSU Tango will keep going
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Because we're going away (for now) that doesn't mean OSU is soon to dance its last tanda!
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        In
  fact, there are already 3 veterans from the community who have made 
 commitments to carry on instruction at the class - Rafeal Miranda who 
 more or less runs the Tango scene in Newport, Frank Davis who's well 
 known all round and a regular at the Portland Tango (both of whom have 
 taught at the club before), and Peter Gysegym who's had his own classes 
 and milongas going in Corvallis for years - with one or two other 
 experienced gentlemen waiting in the wings who want to help build Tango 
 in Corvallis.
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EJMEgUKf7cc/UZqybbiUZaI/AAAAAAAAAkw/wWFU0Qshy64/s1600/tango-class.jpg" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-student" border="0" height="320" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EJMEgUKf7cc/UZqybbiUZaI/AAAAAAAAAkw/wWFU0Qshy64/s320/tango-class.jpg" title="" width="240"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       There is also an 
 OSU faculty member ready to take the reigns on the club admin. So 
 classes should continue without issue after we've left.
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       </span>
                       <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        We hope that it'll remain free of charge - open to anyone with a good attitude that wants to get a start in Argentine Tango.
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       We
  largely started the club to help nurture the local community and expose
  OSU's ample ballroom, swing and blues fraternities to this art; which 
 we're happy to have been successful at.
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Our
  main concern at the moment is getting a few new students willing to 
 stand up as committee members for the club at the turn of the new 
 academic year. We trust that a few of you will raise your hands and help
  keep the doors open when the time comes. Please contact me directly 
 should you feel motivated to contribute and...
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         Work together to help the club grow.
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <b>
                       <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        For
  now, classes will run as usual, every monday evening from 7 - 9pm, and 
 will have one last milonga at our house some time in June.
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <div style="clear: both;">
                      </div>

                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-31880859126665633" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <b>
                        This weekend myself and an elite unit of local Cape Town tangueros 
 descended upon the Lifestyle shopping centre set in a trendy part of the
  city, for an Argentine Tango flash mob to celebrate my birthday.
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Despite a few hassles from secturiy, caused by a certain Tango neophyte 
 "someone" (a.k.a Mister 
 Let's-go-and-tell-security-about-the-mob-two-miniutes-before-we-start-and-have-Wikus-deal-with-the-fallout)
  we sprung the surprise on dozens of shoppers and passers by - much to 
 the annoyance of the guards who dutifully but gently shooed us from the 
 mall entrance to the amply-sized sidewalk.
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Alas even without the beneft of good acoustics, with our punchy Bose 
 sound system we had no shortage of volume to make our presence known.
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <b>
                        Our playlist of the day consisted of "Amor te sigo queriendo", 
 "Mariposita" and "Borges y Paraguay". A forth track -"A Media Luz" - was
  cut from the planned line-up to help ease a few bubbling frustration 
 levels.
                       </b>
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       The only misfortune was that the person who volunteered to film our 
 exploits had to withdraw from attendance shorly before the event,  so 
 unless something by one of the camera-wielding onlookers pops up on 
 youtube in due course, I sadly don't have any footage to share with you.
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">

                     <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                      <a href="http://osutango.blogspot.com/2012/12/33-tango-quotes-that-will-change-your.html">
                       33 Tango Quotes That Will Change Your Dancing
                      </a>
                     </h3>
                     <div class="post-header">
                      <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3643422578478137303" itemprop="description articleBody">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       Be inspired by to put poetry in motion with these
                       <a href="http://tangocritica.wordpress.com/2013/09/10/tango-quotes-moving-thoughts-that-will-reshape-your-dancing/" rel="nofollow">
                        Argentine Tango quotes
                       </a>
                       .
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6elqljdpVno/ULpTVIHm_dI/AAAAAAAAAWA/lZ5_IzRtPaA/s1600/argentine-tango-shadow.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-silhouette" border="0" height="214" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6elqljdpVno/ULpTVIHm_dI/AAAAAAAAAWA/lZ5_IzRtPaA/s320/argentine-tango-shadow.jpg" title="" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "
                       <b>
                        I believe that Tango has the potential to bring out the best in each of us, at least while in the embrace.
                       </b>
                       We surrender our egos; leave prickly personality traits at the table; 
 and cease to be CEOs, taxi drivers, engineers, unemployed. We replace 
 all our externals with a purity of spirit, a generosity of kindness, 
 splendid caring. And when these elements flow freely between partners, 
 it is... the joy." - Unknown
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <b>
                       <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      </b>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "
                       <b>
                        Warning: tango contains highly addictive ingredients
                       </b>
                       ,
  such as pain, pleasure, passion, excitement, connection, freedom, 
 torment, and bliss. In seven out of ten cases it takes over a person's 
 life." - Naomi Hotta
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         "There are no wrong moves in tango, only new ones." - VaroTango
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "The wealth of a 
 tango couple resides in that they both contribute to the choreographic 
 creation. The improvision, the emotion that each feels which is 
 materialized in steps.
                       <b>
                        It is ideal when both partners contribute what they feel to the music
                       </b>
                       ." - Guillermina Quiroga
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                        Javier: "When a man walks nicely, the woman dies in his embrace."
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                        Stella: "When a man walks badly, the woman wants to die." - Jaiver Rodriguez &amp; Stella Misse
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fc5AurXi_TM/ULpYM2HHaYI/AAAAAAAAAXA/JXBpPq-q8ZM/s1600/argentine-tango.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="317" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fc5AurXi_TM/ULpYM2HHaYI/AAAAAAAAAXA/JXBpPq-q8ZM/s320/argentine-tango.jpg" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       “A good dancer is
  one who listens to the music…We dance the music not the steps. Anyone 
 who aspires to dance never thinks about what he is going to do. What he 
 cares about is that he follows the music.
                       <b>
                        You see, we are painters. We paint the music with our feet.
                       </b>
                       ” - Carlos Gavito
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "
                       <b>
                        Other music exists to heal wounds; but the tango when sung and played is for the purpose of opening them
                       </b>
                       , for the purpose of sticking you finger in the wound and to tear them until they bleed." - Unknown
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "
                       <b>
                        The Tango is the ultimate communication between two people
                       </b>
                       .
  It begins with an embrace, an initial sharing of affection, yet 
 stresses individual balance... The mastering of one's individual balance
  is what allows two bodies to dance as one, along with a technique that 
 is clean and uncluttered, a form that is pure, a line that is classical 
 and an elegance that is sublime." - Miguel Angel Pla
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                        "The music goes in my ears, is filtered through my heart, and comes out through my feet." - El Flaco Dany Garcia
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "When someone begins he can be dazzled by things that are external;
                       <b>
                        the things of tango are internal
                       </b>
                       ... A dancer arrives at the roots of tango when he falls in love..." - Eduardo Arquimba
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Km5bvnx-qRA/ULpTraJDpwI/AAAAAAAAAWQ/iNI8RE86xwI/s1600/Bandoneon.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-bandoneon" border="0" height="250" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Km5bvnx-qRA/ULpTraJDpwI/AAAAAAAAAWQ/iNI8RE86xwI/s320/Bandoneon.jpg" title="" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "When you dance 
 tango with someone, you don’t need to know their entire history in order
  to get a glimpse of their more ‘raw’ self, their human warmth or lack 
 thereof, their ability to listen and participate in a dialogue, their 
 ability to enjoy the music, open themselves emotionally and show their 
 vulnerable side.
                       <b>
                        It’s harder to hide our ‘raw self’ if we are not able to use words to conceal.
                       </b>
                       " - Unknown
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                        </span>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         "It's
  not about fancy repertoire, flourishes and embellishments... At the 
 core it's about starting, stepping and pausing with the music. All the 
 rest is icing on the cake." - Chris Corby
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "Sssh. Don't tell
  anyone this. This is a secret. Imagine telling a beginner man he has to
  learn to find the rhythm of the music, watch out for navigational 
 hazards on the dance floor, develop a strategy on the spot for dealing 
 with them choosing from a repertoire of movements he has learned, then 
 lead the woman to move in the intended direction with the intended speed
  while maintaining the connection, and then... He has to follow the 
 woman's response to his lead to determine the next move (within a 
 millisecond, after all, this is not chess), and take responsibility for 
 whatever goes wrong. And we wonder why there aren't enough men in tango?
  Yet the surviving men keep trying.
                       <b>
                        It must be that the rewards of tango are greater than its obstacles.
                       </b>
                       " - Jay Rabe
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                        "What we need are more people with sensitivity and fewer exhibitionists." - Osvaldo Natucci
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DI6eNRO4KWM/ULpT3fC1wnI/AAAAAAAAAWY/aLcshRbqF-I/s1600/Tu_tango_gris_by_untangomas.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-sepia" border="0" height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DI6eNRO4KWM/ULpT3fC1wnI/AAAAAAAAAWY/aLcshRbqF-I/s320/Tu_tango_gris_by_untangomas.jpg" title="" width="225"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "The tango 
 trance. Seek it, and it will elude you. Talk about it in too much detail
  and it will haunt you evily. Live for it, and you will die many deaths.
  Treasure it, but don't hold onto it.
                       <b>
                        Dance with love and freedom and it
  will embrace you
                       </b>
                       . Be vulnerable, and feel it's power." - Dan Boccia
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "The tango is an 
 embrace in movement. A man and a woman enter a dialogue through their 
 bodies, guided by music which has an almost somber quality of yearning. 
 Of a passion that can that can never be fulfilled.
                       <b>
                        Of a sweet sadness. 
 Two strangers become one for the duration of the dance.
                       </b>
                       Two opposites 
 come together briefly to create the fantasy of a harmonious whole." - 
 Gisela Kirberg Mamone
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                        "Tango is not a race: there is no finish line." - Robin Thomas
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "For me, when I 
 think of
                       <b>
                        the best leaders I've danced with, I've noticed a commonality 
 between all of them which is an apparent lack of ego or bravado.
                       </b>
                       Instead
  of thinking about themselves, they just surrender to the music and to 
 the pleasure of having a woman in their arms, no matter if she's 
 experienced or not. They are the kind who would never let a woman feel 
 incompetent or humiliated but instead as though
                       <b>
                        it's been a sublime 
 privilege to share this wonderful thing called tango with them.
                       </b>
                       The 
 worst leaders are the ones who don't pay attention to a woman's skill 
 level and instead try to guide her into doing moves that are obviously 
 beyond her knowledge of the choreography, thus shaming and humiliating 
 her. They are the worst because they are only focusing on themselves 
 instead of making it a wonderful experience for them both. In other 
 words, they didn't surrender to the experience but to their own egos 
 with complete disregard for the women's." - Caroline Polack
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </span>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-olEEJ8FCQwA/ULpUC-Jv9BI/AAAAAAAAAWg/wIcX5eFjDdo/s1600/The+Tango+is+in+the+Heart-281.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-painting" border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-olEEJ8FCQwA/ULpUC-Jv9BI/AAAAAAAAAWg/wIcX5eFjDdo/s320/The+Tango+is+in+the+Heart-281.jpg" title="" width="256"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "Tango is a dance
  in which is it easy to become obsessed with perfection. The taste of 
 heaven that is found within tango may encourage some to seek perfection.
  Others may bring their own perfectionism to tango. But we should never 
 confuse heaven and perfection. They are very different.
                       <b>
                        The path of perfectionism often leads away from heaven
                       </b>
                       — as we find ourselves accompanied and driven forward by demons that 
 become all too familiar. If we pursue perfection in our practice, we are
  likely developing the demons that seek to keep us from effective 
 dancing." - Stephen Brown
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
                        "Tango is a a four legged animal with two beating hearts." - Unknown
                       </span>
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "When the tango 
 took hold of me, it was as if I had found the ultimate lover.
                       <b>
                        No single 
 experience can be as fascinating as this dance.
                       </b>
                       No single work of art is
  so replete with all the joy and sorrow and longing and tragi-comedy of 
 the human race, as is a tango danced between a man and a woman. It is 
 labyrinthine, yet so simple. Each lasts just a few moments, yet it is 
 eternal. There is a purity amidst all its complexities. The more one 
 searches for the meaning behind its mystery, the ever more elusive is 
 the tango... And yet, it is what it is, and we can see it, hear it, feel
  it, breathe it, live it, in the pleasure of its immediacy.
                       <b>
                        Those of us 
 it holds in its power - we want to shape our whole lives around it, its 
 cadences, its sweat, its subtle messages and surging desires.
                       </b>
                       The tango 
 changes us forever. It changed me forever. Never have I been so 
 intensely in love. Never had I felt so intensely alive. It helps me 
 forget. And it helps me remember sweetly." - La Nuit Blanche
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-uGt1akbHAJo/ULpWe5cmHzI/AAAAAAAAAWo/mE4Mgti2cj0/s1600/tango-embellishment.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-embellishment" border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-uGt1akbHAJo/ULpWe5cmHzI/AAAAAAAAAWo/mE4Mgti2cj0/s320/tango-embellishment.jpg" title="" width="212"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "
                       <b>
                        In tango, the relationship between lead and follower, man and woman, is so intense and all consuming,
                       </b>
                       that there is simply no time for small talk. The last man I danced 
 with, I know more intricately in many ways than his lover: I know that 
 he perspires in a tiny spot above his brow; that when the dance slows 
 and our connection is tight, his breathing almost stops; that when my 
 leg sweeps his, he arches his neck imperceptibly upwards; that when 
 another couple got too close he subconsciously enclosed me in a 
 protective embrace; and that his hand rested so delicately on the flesh 
 of my back. Yet all I know about him are his name and his country of 
 origin.
                       <b>
                        Small talk is a luxury not afforded to us tango addicts.
                       </b>
                       " - Anastasia Demaggio
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         "Tango is great for people who don't get enough opportunities to get rejected in everyday life." - Squeaky Frobisher
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "
                       <b>
                        In
  tango, heaven is found through the simple gift of grace.
                       </b>
                       That comes 
 from getting out on the dance floor with the person that happens to be 
 right for the moment, opening one's heart and falling in love again. The
  times that this happens, one is just happy to be in the arms of another
  at the end of the tanda." - Unknown
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "Tango is about feeling and sensitivity,
                       <b>
                        otherwise you are just doing gymnastics
                       </b>
                       . You can do all the steps but it has to have the feeling and sensitivity of authentic tango." - Miguel Zotto
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "Beginners
  want to dance like intermediates; intermediates want to dance like 
 advanced dancers; advanced dancers want to dance like the greats; but
                       <b>
                        the greats always go back to basics.
                       </b>
                       " - Unknown
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9a9SEYFDGZ0/ULpWq-fqIuI/AAAAAAAAAWw/eBjtFF8evsw/s1600/ClipArtTangoOversway.gif" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-street-scene" border="0" height="265" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-9a9SEYFDGZ0/ULpWq-fqIuI/AAAAAAAAAWw/eBjtFF8evsw/s400/ClipArtTangoOversway.gif" title="" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         "Tango can save your life, and it will break your heart." - Ms. Heartbreak Tango
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "When
  you dance with a partner you are close and the dance is very 
 suggestive, but it is not personal... Close is what the music inspires 
 you to become.
                       <b>
                        The embrace looks personal, but what we are actually 
 embracing is the music.
                       </b>
                       " - Carlos Gavito
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;"/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "To
  be a great lead, do not love the woman you dance with; rather,
                       <b>
                        listen 
 to the music and love it
                       </b>
                       ! Beautiful tango is a process of transference -
  your love for the music will be transferred to the follower, and she 
 will be enchanted." - John Vaina
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: medium;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                         "People
  talk about styles of tango, but there is only one tango. It 
 accommodates itself to every place and every era." - Eduardo Arquimbau
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wkgwFi8n9UA/ULpW9TKdFpI/AAAAAAAAAW4/cJToAD1ypvQ/s1600/tango-pivot.jpg" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="tango-pivot" border="0" height="194" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wkgwFi8n9UA/ULpW9TKdFpI/AAAAAAAAAW4/cJToAD1ypvQ/s320/tango-pivot.jpg" title="" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "However an excellent dancer you
  are as a man, even if you are able to dance with every woman, when you 
 dance with a woman who can’t follow you have more responsibility. You 
 must dance perfectly, which means you can’t relax, you can’t think about
  your posture, and you can’t rattle off a fine step:
                       <b>
                        what is the use of 
 being able to do everything if the woman can’t follow you?
                       </b>
                       " - Miguel 
 Zot
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       to
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       "Tango is a sad feeling that is danced." - Enrique Santos Discépoloo
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                      </div>
                      <div style="font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif;">
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-size: medium;">
                         "Please, just for me, forget the steps... Hold me, feel the music, and give me your soul. Then I can give you mine."
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </div>
