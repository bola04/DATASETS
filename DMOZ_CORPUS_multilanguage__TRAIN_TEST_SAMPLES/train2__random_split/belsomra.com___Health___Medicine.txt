 <section class="actualStickyFooterContentContainer">
 <div class="container-fluid padding0">
 <div class="row no-gutters">
 <div class="d-block col-lg-8 col-12 padding0 leftSide">
 <h1>Selected Risk Information</h1>
 <ul class="outerUl">
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> Do not take more BELSOMRA than prescribed.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> Do not take BELSOMRA unless you are able to stay in bed a full night (at least 7 hours) before you must be active again.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> Take BELSOMRA within 30 minutes of going to bed.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> <span class="bold">BELSOMRA may cause serious side effects that you may not know are happening to you. These side effects include:</span></li>
 <li>
 <ul class="innerUl">
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> sleepiness during the day</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> not thinking clearly</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> acting strangely, confused, or upset</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> “sleep-walking” or doing other activities when you are asleep like eating, talking, having sex, or driving a car.</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">Call your doctor right away if you find out that you have done any of these activities after taking BELSOMRA.</span></li>
 </ul>
 </li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> Do not take BELSOMRA if you fall asleep often at unexpected times (narcolepsy).</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> BELSOMRA is a controlled substance because it can be abused or cause dependence.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> <span class="bold">Before taking BELSOMRA, tell your doctor about all of your medical conditions, including if you:</span></li>
 <li>
 <ul class="innerUl">
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> have a history of depression, mental illness, or suicidal thoughts</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> have a history of drug or alcohol abuse or addiction</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> have a history of a sudden onset of muscle weakness (cataplexy)</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> have a history of falling asleep often at unexpected times (narcolepsy) or daytime sleepiness</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> have lung or breathing problems</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> have liver problems</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> are pregnant or plan to become pregnant</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> are breastfeeding or plan to breastfeed.</li>
 </ul>
 </li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> Medicines can interact with each other, sometimes causing serious side effects. Do not take BELSOMRA with other medicines that can make you sleepy unless your doctor tells you to.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> <span class="bold">Do not</span> drink alcohol while taking BELSOMRA. It can increase your chances of getting serious side effects.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> <span class="bold">Do not</span> drive, operate heavy machinery, do anything dangerous, or do other activities that require clear thinking after taking BELSOMRA.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> You may still feel drowsy the next day after taking BELSOMRA. <span class="bold">Do not</span> drive or do other dangerous activities until you feel fully awake.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> <span class="bold">BELSOMRA may cause serious side effects, including:</span></li>
 <li>
 <ul class="innerUl">
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">abnormal thoughts and behavior.</span> Symptoms include more outgoing or aggressive behavior than normal, confusion, agitation, hallucinations, worsening of depression, and suicidal thoughts or actions</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">memory loss</span></li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">anxiety</span></li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">temporary inability to move or talk (sleep paralysis)</span> for up to several minutes while you are going to sleep or waking up</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">temporary weakness in your legs</span> that can happen during the day or at night.</li>
 </ul>
 </li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> The most common side effects of BELSOMRA include drowsiness the next day after you take BELSOMRA.</li>
 <li><span class="fa-circle"> </span> How Should I Take BELSOMRA?</li>
 <li>
 <ul class="innerUl">
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> Take BELSOMRA exactly as your doctor tells you to take it.</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> Only take BELSOMRA 1 time each night, if needed, within 30 minutes of going to bed.</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> Only take BELSOMRA when you can get a full night’s sleep (at least 7 hours).</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> <span class="bold">Do not</span> take BELSOMRA if you drank alcohol that evening or before bed.</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> BELSOMRA may be taken with or without a meal. However, BELSOMRA may take longer to work if you take it with or right after meals.</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> Call your doctor if your insomnia (sleep problem) worsens or is not better within 7 to 10 days. This may mean that there is another condition causing your sleep problem.</li>
 <li><span class="grayBullet"> </span> If you take too much BELSOMRA, call your doctor right away or get emergency treatment.</li>
 </ul>
 </li>
 </ul>
