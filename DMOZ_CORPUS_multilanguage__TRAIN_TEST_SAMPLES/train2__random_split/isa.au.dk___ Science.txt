         <h1 class="borders">
          ASTRID2
         </h1>
         <h3>
          Current status
         </h3>
         <p>
          In 2017 ASTRID2 provided 36 weeks of user beam time to the 5 operational beam lines. In 2018, user beam provision continues with normal operation at 180 mA top-up, while machine physics studies are ongoing in order to improve stability and lifetime of the beam. A new beam line, SGM4, is currently under construction with commissioning expected in the second half of 2018.
         </p>
         <h3>
          A brief history of ASTRID2
         </h3>
         <p>
          In 2008
          <span class="colourRed">
           ISA
          </span>
          was awarded money to build a new high brilliance synchrotron storage ring, ASTRID2. Construction of the ring began in 2011 and by April 2012 the whole ring was 
 			under vacuum and ready for testing. 
 			The
          <a href="/facilities/astrid2/photos/20120514-MVD111_FirstLightinASTRID2.html">
           first beam
          </a>
          was injected into the ASTRID2 ring on
 			Monday 14th May 2012 and the
          <a href="/facilities/astrid2/photos/20120710-OneTurn_Astrid2.html">
           first full turn
          </a>
          of ASTRID2 was accomplished on Tuesday the 10th July 2012. On the 7th
 			of August electrons circulated the ring for almost 200 µs (
          <a href="/facilities/astrid2/photos/20120807-ASTRID2-MultiTurn.html">
           1300 turns
          </a>
          of the ring). On the 2nd of November
 			2012 a
          <a href="/facilities/astrid2/photos/20121102-1stStoredBeam-Scope_52uA.html">
           stored beam with RF
          </a>
          was acheived for the first time. Commissioning of ASTRID2 proceeded well in 2013 and on the 
 			13th of September 2013
          <b>
           200 mA of current was stored in ASTRID2 and top-up at 200 mA successful.
          </b>
          In December 2013 the
          <a href="news/1stExternalAstrid2user.asp">
           first external user
          </a>
          came to do experiments on the AU-UV beam line.
 			2014 saw the commissioning of the AU-SGM3, AU-Matline and
          <a href="http://www.isa.au.dk/facilities/astrid2/beamlines/AU-cd/AU-cd.asp">
           AU-CD
          </a>
          beam lines, with all beam lines fully operational by mid 2014. In early 2016 the newest beam line, AMOLine, was commissioned.
         </p>
         <h3>
          ASTRID2 Inauguration:
         </h3>
         <p>
          The Danish Minister for Research, Innovation and Higher Education, Morten Řstergaard,
          <a href="/news/astrid2inauguration.asp">
           officially opened the ASTRID2 facility
          </a>
          on Monday the 10th September 2012.
         </p>
