               <h1>
                Space Is Our Future!
               </h1>
               <p>
                <img alt="Apollo_11" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-1375" height="203" src="/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Apollo_11.jpg?dbdb4e&amp;dbdb4e" width="150"/>
                It is shortsighted of us as a collective humanity to think that we will be able to contain ourselves to this planet. It only takes a clear, starry night to prove to a person that there is so much more out there than this
                <a class="external external_icon" data-rel="lightbox nofollow" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wupToqz1e2g" rel="lightbox" title="">
                 “Pale Blue Dot”
                </a>
                , to quote the late Dr. Carl Sagan.
               </p>
               <p>
                The awe and wonder of seeing a Shuttle launch, watching old Apollo footage, or just staring up at the night sky prove to me, beyond a shadow of a doubt, that space exploration will be man’s last, best destiny.
               </p>
               <h3>
                The responsibility for maintaining man’s presence in space falls upon each and every one of us.
               </h3>
               <p>
               </p>
               <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1376" style="width: 260px">
                <img alt="STS-51 ACTS=TOS Deploy" class="size-full wp-image-1376" height="189" src="/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/STS-51_ACTS-TOS_Deploy.jpg?dbdb4e&amp;dbdb4e" width="250"/>
                <p class="wp-caption-text">
                 <span style="color: black;">
                  STS-51 ACTS-TOS Deploy
                 </span>
                </p>
               </div>
               <br/>
               If you haven’t done your part, it’s time to take action! NASA and the space exploration push as a whole is constantly under fire from organizations, politicians, and individuals who don’t realize the benefits from space exploration.
               <h3>
                It’s up to us to educate them!
               </h3>
               <p>
                As a nation and a planet, the exploration of space and other worlds will provide a unifying force… a sense of direction and purpose… that can only be a positive influence in this insane “me me me” lifestyle that seems to permeate society.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>

               <h1>
                Why am I so passionate about space exploration?
               </h1>
               <p>
                I was five years old when Apollo 11 landed on the Moon.
               </p>
               <p>
                That year, for Christmas, I received a spacesuit and helmet…
                <a alt="Roger - 1969 astronaut wannabe!" data-rel="lightbox-gallery-0" data-rl_caption="" data-rl_title="" href="/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Roger-as-an-astronaut-Christmas-1968.jpg?dbdb4e&amp;dbdb4e" title="">
                 I still have the picture
                </a>
                and remember how much I loved them.
               </p>
               <p>
                From that point on, I was determined to do something space-related.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="et_pb_column et_pb_column_2_3 et_pb_column_10 et_pb_css_mix_blend_mode_passthrough et-last-child">

               </p>
               <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1372" style="width: 219px">
                <img alt="Roger in the MCC" class="size-full wp-image-1372" height="200" src="/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Roger_84_2.jpg?dbdb4e&amp;dbdb4e" width="209"/>
                <p class="wp-caption-text">
                 <span style="color: black;">
                  Roger in the MCC
                 </span>
                </p>
               </div>
               I was fortunate enough to live my life-long dream and have a
               <a href="/career/">
                12 year career
               </a>
               with NASA as an Orbit, Deploy, and Rendezvous
               <a href="/fdo/">

                Mission Control Center (MCC)
               </a>
               . The FDO (pronounced “FDO”) is the primary MCC Flight Controller responsible for the overall trajectory of the manned vehicle and the target vehicle(s). When I was a FDO, that was the Space Shuttle and various payloads, including the Russian space station Mir.
               <p>
                I started work at NASA’s Johnson Space Center three weeks and two days prior to the STS-51L accident that resulted in the loss of Challenger and her crew.
               </p>
               <p>
                The period that followed left an indelible mark on my life as I was able to be a part of the recovery as the entire NASA community came together as a family, solved the problems, and returned to space.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>

                The links, below, cover a range of topics, from specific information on
                <a href="/fdo/">

                </a>

                <a href="/mcc/">

                </a>
