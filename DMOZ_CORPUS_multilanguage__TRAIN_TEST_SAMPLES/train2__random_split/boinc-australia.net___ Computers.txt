      <h2 style="text-align: left;">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <br/>
        BOINC@Australia - THE Aussie Alliance !
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <strong>
        BOINC
       </strong>
       @
       <strong>
        Australia
       </strong>
       is the top Australian team participating in BOINC distributed computing projects, giving a definite Aussie Presence. We have over 4600 (around 4300 regularly active) members and have generated over 7.9 BILLION credits  at this time.. that's a LOT of Science !
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        BOINC
       </strong>
       @
       <strong>
        Australia
       </strong>
       has a team in all the BOINC projects and is crunching actively in most of them. Join us in one or more project/s to help the advancement of science and advance an Aussie team in the ranks of contributors. If you have been crunching previously, any BBC Climate Project credits transfer with you, and any future crunching with us will help build and strengthen our fantastic team.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       There are a number of very good reasons to select BOINC@Australia as your team... we are the first and largest Aussie Alliane, with a fantastic member retentin and participation rate (better than MOST teams!) and with us you CAN compete against the world's Power-teams.
       <br/>
       While we are here for the Science, we also enjoy the fun side of it all... we have a team website, a very active forum, great team camaraderie and best of all, GREAT team members.
       <br/>
       Over the years, there have been a number of members from other overseas teams come and join us when they hear of our "Aussie Assaults" (quarterly "Attacks" on a democratically elected project) especially when they see how we compete against the large overseas teams... and a number of those overseas visitors have actually stayed on to become very valuable members of our team.
       <br/>
       We are open to new suggestions, new members and new science. Come and look us over - You can check out our web site by clicking on the team logo below and also have a look around our forum, although most of the topics are members only and not shown to guests..
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: center;">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         <br/>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h2 style="text-align: left;">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        Who can join our team ?
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Whether you are contributing a little to one project with one old computer or participate in several projects with a building full of the latest equipment, your contribution is always welcome.
      </p>
      <p>
       While our emphasis is on Australians living anywhere in the world (including Australia) and anyone living in, or having some connection with Australia,
       <strong>
        anyone
       </strong>
       is welcome to join us.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         <br/>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h2 style="text-align: left;">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        Helping Scientific Research
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a class="MIVA_AdLink" href="/home/../" id="MIVA_LINK_2_0_3" name="MIVA_LINK_2_0_3" target="_blank">
       </a>
       BOINC@Australia is a group of ordinary computer users with a strong interest in helping Scientific Research. By using the idle time on our computers we assist scientists in finding cures for
       <a class="MIVA_AdLink" href="/home/../" id="MIVA_LINK_1_0_1" name="MIVA_LINK_1_0_1" target="_blank">
       </a>
       cancer, influenza and other diseases, study global warming, discover pulsars, search for extraTerrestrial intelligence and help with numerous other scientific projects.
      </p>
      <p>
       Scientists use a program called BOINC (Berkeley Open Infrastructure for Network Computing) to create a volunteer computing project, giving them access to computing power from thousands of computers world wide. In some cases, the computing power they are able to access rivals that of supercomputers.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        <strong>
         <br/>
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: left;">
       <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
        Help us keep the momentum going
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       At last count there were well over 30,000 Australian participants in combined BOINC projects. There are over 1,100 Australian teams, with
       <strong>
        BOINC@Australia
       </strong>
       <a href="http://www.boincstats.com/stats/boinc_team_stats.php?pr=bo&amp;st=0&amp;to=10&amp;co=5">
        leading the pack
       </a>
       on both the total combined BOINC credits and recent average credit (RAC).
      </p>
      <p>
       We are by far the most successful Aussie team and are in the top 20 globally (18th Position - see
       <a href="http://www.boincstats.com/stats/boinc_team_stats.php?pr=bo&amp;st=0&amp;to=100">
       </a>
       BOINCStats) ... so why set up another Aussie team when our combined computing power can keep the best placed Aussie team in the top 20 (and hopefully BACK into the top 10 - we HAVE been at  #9 in the World !) worldwide?
      </p>
      <p class="call">
       As the song goes:
       <em>
        <strong>
         "Come on Aussie come on, come on".
        </strong>
       </em>
      </p>
