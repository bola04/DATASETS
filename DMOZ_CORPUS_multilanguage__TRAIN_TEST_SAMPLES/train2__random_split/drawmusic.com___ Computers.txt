       <h1 class="Dm17Title">
        Lesson 1: Introduction
       </h1>
       <b>
        Pop Music Theory
       </b>
       is a
       <b>
        free online music theory course
       </b>
       and
       <b>
        songwriting course
       </b>
       , covering:
       <ol>
        <li>
         <b>
          Basics:
         </b>
         Scales, chords, written notes.
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Analysis:
         </b>
         Studying
         <i>
          why
         </i>
         good music sounds good, to help you 
 		learn
         <b>
          how to write music
         </b>
         .
        </li>
       </ol>
       <br/>
       <b>
        Why Pop Music Theory?
       </b>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <b>
          Real pop music:
         </b>
         These lessons are about real, "popular" music 
 		(including rock, blues, R&amp;B, country, jazz, etc.).
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          We start at the beginning
         </b>
         and explain things one step at a time.
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Explanations and activities:
         </b>
         These are
         <i>
          lessons
         </i>
         , not just 
 		"theory facts". We
         <b>
          explain things
         </b>
         , 
 		
 		include
         <b>
          music theory games
         </b>
         , and give you tips to
         <i>
          use
         </i>
         the 
 		theory to
         <b>
          create your own music
         </b>
         .
        </li>
       </ul>
       <br/>
       Next, go to:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/music-theory/practicing-songwriting">
          <b>
           Lesson 2: Practicing Songwriting
          </b>
         </a>
         for 
 		some general advice,
         <i>
          or
         </i>
         :
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/music-theory/pitch-names">
          <b>
           Lesson 3: Pitch Names
          </b>
         </a>
         to jump 
 		straight into the music theory.
        </li>
       </ul>
