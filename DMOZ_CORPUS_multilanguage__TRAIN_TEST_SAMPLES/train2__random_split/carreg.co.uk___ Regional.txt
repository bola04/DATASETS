          <h2>
           DVLA Number Plates – DVLA Registrations
          </h2>
          <p>
           The
           <strong>
            DVLA is an executive agency, funded by the UK Government under the Department for Transport.
           </strong>
           The Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency holds over
           <strong>
           </strong>
           47 million driver records and over 39 million vehicle records.  They collect around £5.6 billion a year in Vehicle Excise Duty (VED) (Jan 2017) and are responsible
           <strong>
           </strong>
           for maintaining a database of driver and vehicles records in Great Britain &amp; Northern Ireland; previously its NI counterpart was called DVA.   The DVLA also issues Driving Licenses, organises the collection of vehicle excise duty (VED) (sometimes known as car tax and road fund license) and the DVLA also sell ‘unissued Government stock” number plates (that many refer to as private plates, personalised registrations, cherished numbers to name but a few).  DVLA began selling private plates in the late eighties holding high-value auctions run by auctioneers ’Christies’ and ‘Bonhams’ selling elite registrations like 1J, 1A, K1 NGS (£240,000).  The most expensive number sold at a DVLA personalised registrations auction is 25 0 which was sold for over £518,000 including VAT and auction fees.    51 NGH also raised £245,000 whilst S1 NGH “only” made £86,000 during a similar auction in 1998.   Since then the DVLA have played a major role within the personalised registrations industry releasing millions of new private plates (unissued Government stock) into the UK vehicle registrations market each year.   CarReg are proud to have attended some of the very first auction sales way back in 1989/90 and have been DVLA recognised resellers for many years, as well as selling number plates from our own stock portfolio and others on behalf of the trade &amp; public.  You can buy cheap registrations here.
           <a href="https://www.carreg.co.uk/dvla-number-plates/">
            More DVLA number plate information here…

        <h2>
         Vehicle Registrations
        </h2>
        Although the term number plates is the commonly used for what we sell, it is not 
 		actually the correct definition of our main product:
        <strong>
         Vehicle Registrations
        </strong>
        .
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Car number plates are merely the perspex plates fitted to your car bearing the registration mark of your vehicle. There are lots of names used for private registrations in the UK. Read the pages under 'Number plate Info' in the main menu to find out all about these; Personalised plates, Current plates, Prefix, Suffix and Dateless number plates. Or if you want to upgrade from your current private registration plate visit our
        <a href="https://www.carreg.co.uk/buy-number-plates/private-number-plates/" title="Number Plates">
         private number plates page
        </a>
        .
       </div>
       <div class="hpbt_content" id="box2">
        <h2>
         The DVLA
        </h2>
        The DVLA (Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency) is the branch of the UK Government 
 		responsible for managing driving licences and vehicle DVLA registrations within Great Britain 
 		(including maintaining a database of licenced drivers and registered vehicles).
        <br/>
        <br/>
        As well as issuing
        <b>
         DVLA number plates
        </b>
        , the DVLA administers driving endorsements and penalty points, transfers car registration numbers and organises the collection of vehicle excise duty (also known as road tax and road fund license).
       </div>

        <b>

        </b>
        private car registrations you can choose from with many prefix  and current style available for crazy low prices!
