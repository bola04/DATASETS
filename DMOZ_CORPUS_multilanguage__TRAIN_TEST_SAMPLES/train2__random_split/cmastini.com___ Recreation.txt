                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p>
                                   <img alt="Our Males" src="/images/Standard/standard1appearance.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="General Appearance"/>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    General Appearance:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   An ancient breed, rediscovered in Italy in the 1940's, the Neapolitan Mastiff is a heavy-boned, massive, awe inspiring dog bred for use as a guard and defender of owner and property.  He is characterized by loose skin, over his entire body, abundant, hanging wrinkles and folds on the head and a voluminous dewlap.  The essence of the Neapolitan is his bestial appearance, astounding head and imposing size and attitude.  Due to his massive structure, his characteristic movement is rolling and lumbering, not elegant or showy.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab2">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p>
                                   <img alt="Standard" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standard4.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="Size, Proportion, Substance" width="261"/>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Size, Proportion, Substance:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   Stocky, heavy boned dog, massive in substance, rectangular in proportion  Length of body is 10% -15% greater than height.  Height: Dogs 26-31 inches.  Bitches: 24-29inches.  Average weight of mature Dogs: 150 pounds; Bitches: 110 pounds; but greater weight is usual and preferable as long as correct proportion and function are maintained.  The absence of massiveness is to be so severely penalized as to eliminate from competition.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab3">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <img alt="Standard" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standard2.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="The Head" width="192"/>
                                   <strong>
                                    Head:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   Large in comparison to the body.  Differentiated from that of other mastiff breeds by more extensive wrinkling and pendulous lips which blend into an ample dewlap.  Toplines of cranium and the muzzle must be parallel.  The face is made up of heavy wrinkles and folds.  Required folds are those extending from the outside margin of the eyelids to the dewlap, and from under the lower lids to the outer edges of the lips.
                                   <strong>
                                    Severe Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Toplines of the cranium and muzzle not parallel
                                   </em>
                                   .
                                   <strong>
                                    Disqualifications:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Absence of wrinkles and folds.
                                   </em>
                                   <strong>
                                    Expression:
                                   </strong>
                                   Wistful at rest, intimidating when alert.  Penetrating stare.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab4">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <img alt="Standard" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standard5head.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="Head" width="192"/>
                                   <strong>
                                    Head Cont:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Eyes:
                                   </strong>
                                   Set deep and almost hidden beneath drooping upper lids.  Lower lids droop to reveal haw.
                                   <strong>
                                    Eye Color:
                                   </strong>
                                   Shades of amber or brown, in accordance with coat color.  Pigmentation of the eye rims same as coat color.
                                   <strong>
                                    Severe Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Whitish-blue eyes; incomplete pigmentation of the eye rims.
                                   </em>
                                   <strong>
                                    Ears:
                                   </strong>
                                   Set well above the cheekbones.  May be cropped or uncropped, but are usually cropped to an equilateral triangle for health reason.  If uncropped, they are medium sized, triangular in shape, held tight to the cheeks and not extending beyond the lower margin of the throat.
                                   <strong>
                                    Skull:
                                   </strong>
                                   Wide, flat between the ears, slightly arched at the frontal part, and covered with wrinkled skin.  The width of the cranium between the cheekbones is approximately equal to its length from the occiput stop.  the brow is very developed.  The frontal furrow is marked.  Occiput is barely apparent.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab5">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <img alt="Standard" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standard3.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="The Head" width="192"/>
                                   <strong>
                                    Head Cont:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Stop:
                                   </strong>
                                   Very defined, forming a right angle at the junction of muzzle and frontal bones, and the sloping back at the greater angle where the frontal bones meet the frontal furrow of the forehead.
                                   <strong>
                                    Nose:
                                   </strong>
                                   Large with well opened nostrils, and in color the same as the coat.  The nose is an extension of the topline of the muzzle and should not protrude beyond nor recede behind the front plane of the muzzle.
                                   <strong>
                                    Severe Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Incomplete pigmentation of the noze.
                                   </em>
                                   <strong>
                                    Muzzle:
                                   </strong>
                                   It is 1/3 the length of the whole head and is as broad as it is long.  Viewed from the front the muzzle is very deep with the outside borders parallel giving it a "squared" appearance.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab6">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Head Cont:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   The top plane of the muzzle from stop to tip of nose is straight, but is ridged due to heavy folds of skin covering it.
                                   <strong>
                                    Severe Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Top plane of the muzzle curved upward or downward
                                   </em>
                                   .
                                   <strong>
                                    Lips:
                                   </strong>
                                   Heavy, thick, and long, the upper lips join beneath the nostrils to form an inverted "V". The upper lips form the lower, outer borders of the muzzle, and the lowest part of these borders is made by the corners of the lips.  The corners turn outward to reveal the flews, and are in line with the outside corners of the eyes.
                                   <strong>
                                    Bite:
                                   </strong>
                                   Scissors bite or pincer bite is standard; slight undershot is allowed.  Dentition is complete.
                                   <strong>
                                    Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    More than 1 missing premolar.
                                   </em>
                                   <strong>
                                    Severe Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Overshot jaw; pronounced undershot jaw which disrupts the outline of the front plane of the muzzle; more than 2 missing teeth.
                                   </em>
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab7">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <img alt="Standard" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standard6.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="Neck, Topline and Body" width="261"/>
                                   <strong>
                                    Neck, Topline and Body:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Neck:
                                   </strong>
                                   Slightly arched,  rather short, stocky and well muscled, the voluminous and well - divided dewlap extends from the lower jaw to the lower neck.
                                   <strong>
                                    Disqualifications:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Absence of dewlap
                                   </em>
                                   .
                                   <strong>
                                    Body:
                                   </strong>
                                   The length of the dog, measured from the point of the shoulder to the point of buttock is 10 - 15% greater than the hieght of the dog measured from the highest point of the shoulder to the ground. Depth of the ribcage is equal to half the total height of the dog.  Ribs are long and well sprung.
                                   <strong>
                                    Chest:
                                   </strong>
                                   Broad and deep, well muscled.
                                   <strong>
                                    Underline and Tuckup:
                                   </strong>
                                   The underline of the abdomen in practically horizontal.  There is little or no tuckup.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab8">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   &lt;
                                   <strong>
                                    Neck, Topline and Body Cont.:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Back:
                                   </strong>
                                   Wide and strong.  Highest part of shoulder blade barely rising above the strong, level topline of the back.
                                   <strong>
                                    Loin:
                                   </strong>
                                   Well muscled, and harmoniously joined to the back.
                                   <strong>
                                    Croup:
                                   </strong>
                                   <strong>
                                    Tail:
                                   </strong>
                                   Set slightly lower than the topline, wide and thick at the root, tapering gradually towards the tip.  It is docked by 1/3.  At rest, the tail hangs straight or in slight "S" shape.  When in action, it is raised to the horizontal or a little higher than the back.
                                   <strong>
                                    Severe Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    Tail carried straight up or curved over the back. Kinked tail
                                   </em>
                                   .
                                   <strong>
                                    Disqualifications:
                                   </strong>
                                   Lack of tail or short tail.,which is less than 1/3 the length from point of insertion of the tail to the hock-joint. Wide, strong, muscular and slightly sloped.  The top of the croup rises slightly and is level with the highest part of the shoulder.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab9">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <img alt="Forequarters" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standardforequarters.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="Forequarters" width="133"/>
                                   <strong>
                                    Forequarters:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   Heavily built, muscular, and in balance with the hindquarters.
                                   <strong>
                                    Shoulder:
                                   </strong>
                                   Long, well muscled, sloping and powerful.
                                   <strong>
                                    Upper Arms:
                                   </strong>
                                   Strongly muscled, powerful.  In length almost 1/3 the height of the dog.
                                   <strong>
                                    Elbows:
                                   </strong>
                                   Covered with abundant and loose skin; held parallel to the ribcage, neither tied in nor loose.
                                   <strong>
                                    Forelegs:
                                   </strong>
                                   Thick, straight, heavy bone, well muscled, exemplifying strength.
                                   <strong>
                                   </strong>
                                   About the same length and the upper arms.  Set well apart.
                                   <strong>
                                    Pasterns:
                                   </strong>
                                   Thick and flattened from to back, moderately sloping forward from the leg.
                                   <strong>
                                    Dewclaws:
                                   </strong>
                                   Front dewclaws are not removed.
                                   <strong>
                                    Feet:
                                   </strong>
                                   Round and noticeably large with arched, strong toes. Nails strong, curved and preferably dark colored. Slight turnout of the front feet is characteristic.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab10">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <img alt="Hindquarters" height="200" src="/images/Standard/standardposteriors.jpg" style="border: medium solid rgb(50, 50, 50); margin-right: 15px; float: left;" title="Hindquarters" width="133"/>
                                   <strong>
                                    Hindquarters:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   As a whole, they must be powerful and strong, in harmony with the forequarters.
                                   <strong>
                                    Thighs:
                                   </strong>
                                   About the same length as the forearms, broad, muscular.
                                   <strong>
                                    Stifles:
                                   </strong>
                                   <strong>
                                    Legs:
                                   </strong>
                                   Heavy and thick boned, well-muscled.  Slightly shorter than thigh bones.
                                   <strong>
                                    Hocks:
                                   </strong>
                                   Powerful and long.
                                   <strong>
                                    Rear Pasterns:
                                   </strong>
                                   Heavy thick bones. Viewed from the side, they are perpendicular to the ground. Viewed from the rear, parallel to each other.
                                   <strong>
                                    Rear Dewclaws:
                                   </strong>
                                   Any dewclaws must be removed.
                                   <strong>
                                    Hindfeet:
                                   </strong>
                                   Same as the front feet but slightly smaller.Moderate angle, strong.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab11">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Coat:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   The Coat is short, dense and of uniform length and smoothness all over the body. The hairs are straight and not longer than one inch.  No fringe anywhere.
                                   <strong>
                                    Color:
                                   </strong>
                                   Solid coats of Grey(blue), black, mahogany and tawny, and the lighter and darker shades of these colors.  Some brindling allowable in all colors.  When present, brindling must be tan (reverse brindle).  There may be solid white markings on the chest, throat area from chin to chest, underside of the body, penis sheath, backs of the pasterns, and on the toes. there may be white hairs at the back of the wrist.
                                   <strong>
                                    Disqualifications:
                                   </strong>
                                   <em>
                                    White markings on any part of the body not mentioned as allowed.
                                   </em>
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab12">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Gait:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                   </strong>
                                   The Neapolitan mastiffs movement is not flashy, but rather slow and lumbering. Normal gaits are the walk, trot, gallop, and pace. The strides are long and elastic, at the same time powerful, characterized by a long push from the hindquarters and extension of the forelegs. Rolling motion and swaying of the body at all gaits is characteristic. Pacing in the showring is not to be penalized. Slight paddling movement of the front feet is normal. The head is carried level with or slightly above the back.
                                   <strong>
                                    Temperament:
                                   </strong>
                                   The Neapolitan Mastiff is steady and loyal to his owner, not aggressive or apt to bite without reason. As a protector of his property and owners, he is always watchful and does not relish intrusion by strangers into his personal space. His attitude is calm yet weary. In the show ring he is majestic and powerful, but not showy.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
                                </div>
                                <div class="roktabs-tab13">
                                 <div class="wrapper">
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   <strong>
                                    Faults:
                                   </strong>
                                  </p>
                                  <p style="text-align: center;">
                                   The foregoing description is that of the ideal Neapolitan mastiff any deviation from the described dog must be penalized to the extent of the deviation.
                                   <strong>
                                    Disqualifications:
                                   </strong>
                                   Absence of wrinkles and folds.  Absence of dewlap.  Lack of tail or short tail, which is less than 1/3 the length from the point of insertion of the tail to the hock.  White markings on any part of the body mentioned.
                                  </p>
                                 </div>
