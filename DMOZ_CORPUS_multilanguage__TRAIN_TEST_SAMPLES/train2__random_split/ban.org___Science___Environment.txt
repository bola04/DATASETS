                       <a class="summary-title-link" href="/news/2018/6/19/dell-and-basel-action-network-team-up-to-track-e-waste">
                        BAN Teams-Up with Dell to Track Electronic Waste
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <!-- Metadata (Below Title) -->
                      <div class="summary-metadata-container summary-metadata-container--below-title">
                       <div class="summary-metadata summary-metadata--primary">
                        <!-- Timestamp -->

                      <!-- Excerpt -->
                      <div class="summary-excerpt">
                       <p style="white-space: pre-wrap;">
                        <strong>
                         June 19, 2018. Seattle, WA. Austin, TX.
                        </strong>
                        Basel Action Network (BAN) has teamed up with Dell to use GPS trackers to verify where used Dell electronic scrap goes once it is collected from Dells U.S. consumer takeback programs. In this pilot project, announced today in Dell's 2018 Corporate Social Responsibility Report, Dell will be sending 40 electronic devices containing hidden trackers into their U.S. consumer disposition stream. Of these, 30 will be arranged by Dell and 10 provided to BAN to deploy independently. All of this is being done to see if things end up where they are supposed to -- in accordance with the law and
                        <a href="http://www.dell.com/learn/us/en/vn/corp-comm/e-waste" target="_blank">
                         Dell's strict no-export of e-waste policy
                        </a>
                        .
                       </p>

                      <div class="summary-excerpt">
                       <p style="white-space: pre-wrap;">
                        <strong>
                         May 24, 2018. Seattle, WA.
