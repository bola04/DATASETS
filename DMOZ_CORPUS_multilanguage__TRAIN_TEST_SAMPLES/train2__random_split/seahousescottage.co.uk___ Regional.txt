              </p>

              <h2>
               About Seahouses
              </h2>
              <p>
               Seahouses is situated on the Heritage coast in North East England and is affectionately known as ‘the village by the sea’.
              </p>
              <p>
               The North Northumberland Heritage coast stretches almost 50 miles from Cresswell in the south to Berwick-on-Tweed in the North. This coast is largely protected and much of it is under the control of the National Trust but is open to all.
              </p>
              <p>
               This coastal area is well known for its golden sandy beaches and the notable stretches are the nine mile long Druridge Bay in the south and the long stretch from Embleton Bay to Ross Sands at the mid-to-northern end. There are many delightful small coves and safe beaches in between, notably Warkworth, Alnmouth, Beadnell, Seahouses and Bamburgh.
              </p>
              <p>
               The area is a delightful mix of beaches, castles, hills, fells and natural history. The many castles in the area include Warkworth, Dunstanburgh, Etal (Battle of Flodden), Alnwick (still occupied by the Duke of Northumberland), Norham, Chillingham, (with its breed of rare wild white cattle), Lindisfarne and perhaps the best known of all, Bamburgh castle, standing sentinel on the beach and steeped in history, All can be visited and most but not all are under the custodianship of English Heritage or the National Trust.
              </p>
              <p>
               Among the many magnificent country houses are Cragside and gardens (Lord Armstrong) in Rothbury, Wallington House and Belsay Hall. Hadrian’s Wall is approx 40-50 mile to the south west.
              </p>
              <p>
               Seahouses is a very convenient centre for access to all activities. For boat trips to the Farne Islands for Puffins, Terns and Seals and trips to Lindisfarne Castle, Longstone Lighthouse and fishing trips, all can be booked from Seahouses harbour.
              </p>
              <p>
               For places of interest a little further afield, Wooler is a delightful market town nestling in the Cheviot Hills where fell walking can be undertaken and for the more energetic, a walk to the top of the Cheviot Mountain, at 816 metres, the highest point in Northumberland.
 Good restaurants abound and Seahouses is famous for its variety of fish and chip establishments.
              </p>
              <p>
               The 3 mile stretch of golden sand from Seahouses north beach to Bamburgh is perhaps the best place for leisure for all including families for active sports, swimming, sandcastles, kite flying etc, and for just relaxing or taking a leisurely walk. Parking is free and accessible and even on a busy day there is plenty of space for all.
              </p>
              <p>
               Seahouses is best accessed from the north by leaving the A1 road at Belford, (B1342) and following the road to Bamburgh and thence to Seahouses, approx 5 miles, or from the south by leaving the A1 road at North Charlton and following this road to Preston Tower, Chathill crossing and station, Beadnell and following the coast road to Seahouses. Approx 8 miles.
              </p>
