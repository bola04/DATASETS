      <h3>
       Underwater Hockey
      </h3>
      Underwater hockey is a very fast moving game that quickly builds 
 		swimming and free diving capability. It is played on the bottom of a 
 		swimming pool by two coed teams of three to six. Players wear fins, mask, snorkel, and a protective 
 		glove and headgear. The stick is short, approximately 1 foot long, the 
 		puck is heavy, around 3 lb., and the goal is 3 meters (9') long. The 
 		rules are "non-contact" and players generally cover "zones" around the 
 		puck. Success (scoring) ultimately depends on teamwork, since no single 
 		person can hold their breath forever. Individual strength is less of an 
 		advantage than it is in many other sports. The water nullifies pure mass 
 		advantage and emphasizes clever use of torque.
