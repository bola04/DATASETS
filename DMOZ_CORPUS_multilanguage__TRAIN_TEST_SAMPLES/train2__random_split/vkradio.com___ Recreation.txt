 interests:
     </strong>

   <p>
    <font size="4">
     VHF/UHF - I've spent the majority of my on air
 time (&gt; 90%) above 50 MHz. I play a little on HF and enjoy that too,
 especially while mobile on long trips.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <font size="4">
     Data communications - Computers and networking
 are my other technical interest, so it's natural to want to merge
 the two. I have been on packet in the past, and intend to get
 back on when circumstances permit (propagation here is roughly
 equivalent to that of a mineshaft, and antenna space is limited
 :( ). Hope to see, and take part in the development of a high
 speed, multi purpose data network, capable of carrying voice,
 data and other traffic. I am working on a
    </font>
    <a href="uwave_backbone.html">
     <font size="4">
      high speed point to
 point microwave link
     </font>
    </a>
    <font size="4">
     suitable for the
 data backbone. APRS via satellite is another potentially
 interesting data application.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <a href="pt.html">
     <font size="4">
      Public transport mobile
     </font>
    </a>
    <font size="4">
     - Yes, that means operating from a tram, train or bus!
 One way to pass the time spent travelling to work, and a great
 way to indulge in radio without intruding on one's home or work
 life! Also a challenge to develop suitable compact, yet effective
 antennas. This is my major mode of operation these days, I can be
 found on VK3RSE (439.375 MHz) or VK3RCC (438.100 MHz, 123Hz
 CTCSS) most weekday mornings and afternoons.
    </font>
   </p>

    <font size="4">

 and Remembrance Day contest (VHF and up, of course :) ). Also

 circumstances permit.

      Are you a
 user of Melbourne's amateur repeaters?
     </strong>
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <font size="4">
     If so, you may be interested in the VK3RSE
 users' mailing list. While primarily oriented towards Melbourne's
 VK3RSE repeater users, this list discussed repeater happenings
 and talk around Melbourne. Interesting contacts, QRM reports,
    </font>
    <a href="http://www.users.bigpond.net.au/vk3yng/lipd/">
     <font size="4">
      LIPDs
     </font>
    </a>
    <font size="4">
     , radio reviews and
 information, coming events and general chit chat between users of
 Melbourne's amateur repeaters. If you'd like to join, send an
 email to
    </font>
    <a href="mailto:vk3rse-request@lists.atctraining.com.au">
     <font size="4">
      vk3rse-request@lists.atctraining.com.au
     </font>
    </a>
    <font size="4">
     with the word 'subscribe' in the message body, and
 follow the instructions in the reply you receive. Existing
 subscribers will notice a new list address. There is no need for
 existing subscribers to re subscribe, existing subscribers have
 been migrated to the new list server. The old
 freeway.apana.org.au addresses now redirect mail to the new
 system, in case you forget. :-)
    </font>

   <p>
    <font color="#00FF00" face="Arial" size="5">
     <strong>
      CB
 Australia mailing list.
     </strong>
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <font size="4">
     Not licenced yet? Or prefer to use CB radio
 instead? Then CB-Australia is the mailing list for you.
 CB-Australia is a mailing list specifically for Australian CB
 radio operators and enthusiasts, and was created to address the
 lack of online forums for Australian CB operators to discuss
 their issues and exchange ideas. For too long, Aussie CBers have
 had to try a ham radio or scanner newsgroup and risk an "off
 topic" warning or other harassment. Topics on the list
 include anything to do with legal CB operation in Australia, as
 well as other "publicly accessible" radio networks,
 such as the RFDS HF frequencies. To subscribe, go to
    </font>
    <a href="http://www.onelist.com/community/cb-australia">
     <font size="4">
      http://www.onelist.com/community/cb-australia
     </font>
    </a>
    <font size="4">
     and log in (If you haven't used onelist before, you
 will need to register first). Alternatively, you can send email
 to
    </font>
    <a href="mailto:cb-australia-subscribe@onelist.com">
     <font size="4">
      cb-australia-subscribe@onelist.com
     </font>
    </a>
    <font size="4">
     . Some examples of topics relevant to CB-Australia
 include:
    </font>
   </p>
   <ul>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Radios, reviews and recommendations.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      27 MHz.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      UHF and repeaters.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Antennas, both purchased and home made.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Propagation ("skip",
         "troppo" and all that stuff :) ).
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      RFDS HF network - licensing, radios and
         information.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Issues (e.g. "is overseas DX
         legal"? "Are power mics legal"? etc).
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Clubs and nets.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      CREST.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      and more!
     </font>
    </li>
   </ul>

 Radio already on the Internet.

     <strong>
      VK3RTL Live on the
 Internet!
     </strong>
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <font size="4">
     After a long absence of streaming audio, the new repeater
 system here has been fitted with a streaming audio feed for Internet listeners
 to enjoy.Â  Like the previous streaming service, the VK3RTL feed uses
 streaming MP3 audio and can be played by most popular media players on most
 platforms.Â  So far, Windows, Mac and Linux systems have reported working
 well with the stream.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <font size="4">
     The audio content is basically a 24x7 feed of activity (both
 local and Internet linked) on VK3RTL.Â  Listeners in the western suburbs of
 Melbourne can also listen on 438.600 MHz if they have a scanner.
    </font>
   </p>
   <p>
    <font size="4">
     While there may be long periods of time where there is no
 audio on the feed, this just means that there is no activity on the
 system.Â  However, there are a number of scheduled nets that may be of
 interest.Â  As of March 2004, the schedule is:
    </font>
   </p>
   <ul>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Sunday 0000 UTC - IRLP Skywarn Net on IRLP reflector 9210
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Monday 2100 New York time (Tuesday 0100 UTC Apr - Oct, 0200
     UTC Nov - Mar) - EchoLink Linux and Open Source Net on *LINUX* and *VK3JED*
     conferences
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Tuesday 1930 Melbourne time (Tuesday 0930 UTC Apr - Oct,
     0830 UTC nov - Mar) - WANSARC Club Net, local RF users and Echolink on
     *VK3JED*
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Tuesday 1900 Houston time (Wednesday 0000 UTC Apr - Oct,
     0100 UTC Nov - Mar) - Central Texas Storm Spotters Net.
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Wednesday 2100 New York time (Thursday 0100 UTC Apr - Oct,
     0200 UTC Nov - Mar) - Echolink Help Forum on *N2LEN*
     </font>
    </li>
    <li>
     <font size="4">
      Friday 2000 Sydney time (Friday 1000 UTC Apr - Oct, 0900
     UTC Nov - Mar) - Aussie IRLP Virtual Pub on reflector 9500.
     </font>
    </li>
   </ul>

 player at:
