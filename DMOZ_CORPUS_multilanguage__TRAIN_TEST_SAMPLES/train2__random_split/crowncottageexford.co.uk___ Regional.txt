       <h1>
        About The Cottage
       </h1>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Crown Cottage
        </strong>
        in Exford is a picture postcard thatched self catering holiday cottage located in the centre of Exmoor National Park, on the border of West Somerset and North Devon.
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <b>
          1 double bedroom, 1 twin bedroom
         </b>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Lovely enclosed cottage garden
         </b>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Two excellent pub/restaurants
         </b>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Village shop/Post Office
         </b>
        </li>
        <li>
         <b>
          Lovely village green with children’s play area
         </b>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <b>
         The Cottage
        </b>
        has two bedrooms and accommodates up to 4 people:-
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Bedroom One
        </b>
        has a Victorian style double bed with luxury memory foam mattress, fitted wardrobe, bedside tables and a chest of drawers.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Bedroom Two
        </b>
        has twin beds, clothes rail, bedside table and built in drawers.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Shower Room
        </b>
        contains a large shower cubicle, WC, built in vanity unit with electric shaver point, towel radiator, and extractor fan.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         The Sitting Room
        </b>
        is very cosy, with a stone built fireplace and wood burning stove.
        <br/>
        There is a window seat which overlooks the village green. There is a smart TV,  Freesat set top box, DVD player and WiFi. A range of board games and DVDs are provided.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         The Dining Room
        </b>
        contains a pine table and four chairs.  A booster chair is available if required.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         The Kitchen
        </strong>
        contains all the equipment needed for a self catering holiday, including a full sized electric cooker, washing machine, and microwave.
       </p>
       <p>
        <b>
         Outside
        </b>
        there is a small enclosed cottage garden with a patio table and four chairs.
       </p>
       <p>
        There is plenty of on street parking in the village and there is a free public car park approx 100 yards away.
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <h1>
        Location
       </h1>
       <p>
        <img alt="" class="alignright size-small wp-image-10" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" src="http://www.crowncottageexford.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/Dark-Sky-logo-small-225x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.crowncottageexford.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/Dark-Sky-logo-small-225x300.jpg 225w, http://www.crowncottageexford.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/Dark-Sky-logo-small.jpg 404w"/>
        The cottage overlooks the village green in the centre of Exford. The village has a village shop/post office, tea rooms, garage,  the Crown Hotel and the White Horse Inn.
        <br/>
        Both these hotels have excellent restaurants/bars for non-residents.
       </p>
       <p>
        Exford is a walkers paradise with many miles of way marked paths down the valley following the river Exe, or over the moors of Exmoor to the coast, some 6 miles away.
       </p>
       <p>
        Exford is renowned for hunting, shooting, fishing and riding with the village hosting a horse show every August.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Exmoor coastal towns of Lynmouth, Porlock and Minehead are a short drive away whilst Taunton and the M5 are only 26 miles away.
       </p>
       <p>
        Exmoor is a beautiful place to visit. It is the home of Lorna Doone and Tarka the otter. It is a place of rolling hills, medieval villages, wild moors and a dramatic coastline. The moor does literally meet the sea with the highest sea cliffs in England.
       </p>
       <p>
        In 2011 Exmoor National Park was designated an International Dark Sky Reserve, the first place in Europe to achieve this prestigious award and only the second in the world.
       </p>
