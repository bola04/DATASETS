              <div class="et_pb_text_inner">
               <h2 style="text-align: center;">
                English Golden Retriever vs American Golden Retriever
               </h2>
               <p style="text-align: center;">
                Though formally recognized throughout our site as English Cream Golden Retrievers, the breed is also referred to as
                <strong>
                 English Golden Retriever
                </strong>
                s,
                <strong>
                 White Golden Retrievers
                </strong>
                ,
                <strong>
                 British Golden Retrievers
                </strong>
                , or
                <strong>
                 Cream Golden Retrievers
                </strong>
                . There are two distinct types of the golden retriever, the American-type Golden Retriever of the
                <a href="http://www.akc.org/dog-breeds/golden-retriever/">
                 American Kennel Club (AKC)
                </a>
                and the
                <strong>
                 British-type Golden Retriever
                </strong>
                of the
                <a href="https://www.thekennelclub.org.uk/">
                 Kennel Club of England (KC)
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                Though both are Golden Retrievers, they have some differences in structure and appearance. Our English Cream Golden Retrievers have characteristics based on the British-type of the KC. The most notable characteristic of the English Cream Retriever that we breed is a very light-colored coat, much sought after by Golden Retriever fanciers.
               </p>
              </div>

                 Females 20-22 in (51-56 cm) at the withers
                </li>
                <li style="text-align: left;">
