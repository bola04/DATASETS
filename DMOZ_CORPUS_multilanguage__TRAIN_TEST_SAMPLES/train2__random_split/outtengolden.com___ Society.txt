               <h2>
                Employment Lawyers for Employees and Executives
               </h2>
               <p>
                Our firm is dedicated to representing employees and other individuals (including executives, partners, professionals and talent), not employers, in all industries, across all professions, and at all employment levels. As advocates for workplace fairness, our passion and our profession is to help advance the goals of employees and protect their rights against injustices in the workplace.
               </p>
               <p>
                Our attorneys advise and represent clients in matters involving former employers, current employers, and potential future employers and partnerships. We also represent groups of employees collectively, as well as executive teams in transition. Our international practice reaches across borders to represent and protect multinational employees, executives and partners.
               </p>

                </em>
                ranked Outten &amp; Golden as “Best Law Firm” in Litigation - Labor &amp; Employment and Employment Law – Individuals.  In December 2014,
                <em>
                 Legal Leaders
                </em>
                ranked Outten &amp; Golden “New York Area’s Top Rated Lawyers for Labor &amp; Employment.”  Many of our lawyers are AV- rated by Martindale Hubbell and have been recognized by Super Lawyers in the field of “Employment Law – Employee.”  Legal 500 recognized O&amp;G as a leader in “Mass tort and class action: plaintiff representation – labor and employment.”

               Sandia Gender Discrimination
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="summary">
              <p>
               On February 7, 2017, Outten &amp; Golden LLP and our co-counsel, Lieff Cabraser Heimann &amp; Bernstein LLP, filed a gender discrimination class action lawsuit in federal court in New Mexico against Sandia National Laboratories, a Department of Energy research and development contractor operated by a wholly-owned subsidiary of Lockheed Martin. The case,
               <em>
                Kennicott v. Sandia National Laboratories
               </em>
               , was brought by three current and former employees on behalf of themselves and all current and former female employees employed by Sandia since December 17, 2008.

               Monaco Coach - $10 Million Class Settlement
              </a>
             </h3>
             <div class="summary">
              <p>
               <strong>
                UPDATE 7/6/2015
               </strong>
               : On March 6, 2009, Outten &amp; Golden filed suit against Monaco Coach Corporation ("Monaco Coach") seeking to recover 60 days wages and benefits for former employees of Monaco Coach under the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act (the WARN Act). We contend Monaco Coach ordered mass layoffs on or about March 2, 2009 without providing its employees with advance written notice. Generally, the WARN Act requires companies to provide their employees with 60 days written notice in advance of a mass layoff or plant closing.
