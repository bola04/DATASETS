            <td>
             <p class="menu-text">
              The food of Asia is popular 
 								the world over and it is no wonder why. The 
 								meals are simply prepared and have fantastic 
 								presentation with tastes that mingle with subtle 
 								complexity. Each country has its history cooked 
 								into its dishes. In Indonesia and Malaysia pork 
 								is rarely used due to the prevalence of Islam, 
 								the Vietnamese retain the flavors of centuries 
 								of French occupation while the arrival of the 
 								Portuguese across all of southeast Asia brought 
 								the chili from the Americas in the 16th century. 
 								All Asian cuisines maintain their own identities 
 								derived from their own distinctive cultures but 
 								they also have a great deal in common as their 
 								influences cross their boarders and share 
 								histories as well as many ingredients and 
 								methods of cooking.
             </p>
