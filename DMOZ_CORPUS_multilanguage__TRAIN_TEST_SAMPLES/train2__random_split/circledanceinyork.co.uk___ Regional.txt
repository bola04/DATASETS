        <div id="pageSynopsis">
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font face="Book Antiqua" size="5">
           Dances and Music from Around the World!
          </font>
         </p>
         <p>
          <font face="Book Antiqua" size="5">
           <em>
            Dance for fun, for fitness, for fulfilment...
           </em>
          </font>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p align="justify">
         <font size="4">
          <span style="color: #000000">
           <span style="color: #000000">
            <span style="font-family: Book Antiqua; font-size: 14pt">
             <strong>
              <em>
               Circle Dance
              </em>
             </strong>
             comes from the tradition of communities dancing together, a tradition which is timeless in origin and yet relevant today in its capacity to foster togetherness. Much of the
             <em>
              <strong>
               Circle Dance
              </strong>
             </em>
             repertoire comes from the Balkan countries: Bulgaria, Romania, Serbo-Croatian cultures, Macedonia and Greece. The dances are sometimes related to a particular time of year or festival but many are done at any gathering of people, at weddings, funerals, festivals or feast days. In fact, if you've ever seen Greek dancing, you already have a good idea of what circle dancing is!
            </span>
           </span>
          </span>
         </font>
        </p>
        <div align="center">
         <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.circledanceinyork.co.uk/userimages/leadingWHwebsmall.jpg"/>
        </div>
        <p align="right">
         <span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 12pt;">
          <em>
           Dancing on Cape Cod
          </em>
          , USA, Sept. 2017.
         </span>
         <span style="font-family: Georgia;">
          <em>
           Photo by Niccy Cordner
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         <span style="color: #000000">
          <span style="font-family: Book Antiqua; font-size: 14pt">
           In York, there are classes, workshops, weekends and special events for you to join in.
           <strong>
            <a href="http://www.circledanceinyork.co.uk/page9.htm">
             <strong>
              Karen Michaelsen
             </strong>
            </a>
            ,
           </strong>
           York based
           <em>
            Circle Dance
           </em>
           <em>
            Teacher,
           </em>
           has been leading all sorts of courses and events since 1997.
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         <span style="color: #000000">
          <span style="font-family: Book Antiqua; font-size: 14pt">
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="color: #000000">
          <span style="font-family: Book Antiqua">
           <span style="font-size: 14pt">
            Circle Dance could be called
            <em>
             <strong>
              International Dance for Wellbeing
             </strong>
            </em>
            as well, since dancing in a circle benefits mind, body and spirit.
            <em>
             <strong>
              Circle Dance
             </strong>
            </em>
            is a very accessible form of dance for people of all ages and abilities, with  the emphasis  on participation, not performance!
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.circledanceinyork.co.uk/userimages/groupphotoedit.jpg"/>
        </p>
        <p align="right">
         <span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 12pt;">
          <em>
           From Albania to Istanbul
          </em>
          residential weekend April 2018
          <em>
           Photo by Averil Rushton
          </em>
         </span>
         <em>
         </em>
        </p>
        <p align="justify">
         <span style="color: #000000">
          <span style="font-family: Book Antiqua">
           <span style="font-size: 14pt">
            <font size="4" style="font-size: 14pt">
             The dances vary in pace from slow to very fast and everything in between! The pace too, affects the mood, as slow dances are often more reflective and meditative while fast dances are expansive and energizing! Above all,
             <em>
              <strong>
               Circle Dance
              </strong>
             </em>
             is enjoyable and fun, and many people would say, life-enriching.
            </font>
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>

            Different music, different rhythms, different moves,
            <em>
             all this creates a unique experience of dancing.
            </em>
