        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Home">
          Home
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p class="post-info">
         Nov 16th, 2013 by
         <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by admin">
          admin
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="entry">
        <p>
         <strong>
          Started in 1864
         </strong>
         by Henry McClurg as the settlement of Park Place, Monroe experienced rapid growth in the early 1900s due to its rich farmlands and abundant timber, and its proximity to the Great Northern Railroad. Monroe is scenically set against the foothills of the Cascade Range, near where the Snoqualmie and Skykomish Rivers join to form the Snohomish.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008000;">
          *****************************************************************
          <span style="color: #008000;">
           ****************
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1120" style="width: 310px">
         <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Dishmaker-house.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-1120" height="187" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Dishmaker-house-300x187.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Dishmaker-house-300x187.jpg 300w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Dishmaker-house-150x93.jpg 150w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Dishmaker-house-768x477.jpg 768w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/06/Dishmaker-house-1024x637.jpg 1024w" width="300"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          George Dishmaker House
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <b>
          The 2018 Historic Homes Tour
         </b>
         will take place this summer to allow home owners to share their beautiful gardens and plantings with visitors, as well as their homes. So mark your calendars for Saturday, July 28 from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. Tickets are now on sale online and at the Monroe Historical Museum on Main Street. Tickets are $15 and serve as a donation to the Monroe Historical Society as well as admission to seven beautiful homes and the 1908 City Hall Building. Spend a Saturday touring some of Monroe’s beautiful old homes and meet some of the dedicated owners who are committed to restoring and preserving them. Online tickets available at
         <a href="http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/3440385">
          http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/3440385
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #cc99ff;">
          ***********************************************************
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/2018-Haji-scholarship-Dottie-Simoni-and-Kevin-Milne.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-1126" height="300" sizes="(max-width: 225px) 100vw, 225px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/2018-Haji-scholarship-Dottie-Simoni-and-Kevin-Milne-225x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/2018-Haji-scholarship-Dottie-Simoni-and-Kevin-Milne-225x300.jpg 225w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/2018-Haji-scholarship-Dottie-Simoni-and-Kevin-Milne-113x150.jpg 113w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/2018-Haji-scholarship-Dottie-Simoni-and-Kevin-Milne.jpg 720w" width="225"/>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <strong>
           Congratulations
          </strong>
          to our 2018 Haji Family Scholarship recipient, Kevin Milne. The Monroe Historical Society is proud to offer this annual scholarship to a graduating Monroe High School senior each year, thanks to a generous endowment from 1941 Monroe High School valedictorian Hiroko Haji. Society trustee and longtime Monroe Public School teacher, Dottie Simoni, on left, presented this year’s award to Kevin on May 31st at the high school’s award ceremony.
          <br/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #339966;">
          ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/new-sign.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-1056" height="275" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/new-sign-300x275.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/new-sign-300x275.jpg 300w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/new-sign-150x138.jpg 150w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/new-sign-768x705.jpg 768w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/new-sign-1024x940.jpg 1024w" width="300"/>
          </a>
         </b>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          It’s a sign!
         </b>
         After many years of watching our old museum sign deteriorate and hoping a new one would take its place we finally have a wonderful new sign gracing Old City Hall. The mounting bracket is topped by a metal cutout showing a steam locomotive and a load of logs. This attractive addition is thanks to a generous grant from the city of Monroe and the work of Pablo Guerrero of Amigo Arts in Monroe. Come by and take a closer look, and a big ‘thank you’ to all who made it possible. The city has promised to at least trim back the trees this fall which, as much as we like foliage, have begun to make building fronts hard to see.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #800080;">
          ***********************************************************************************
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Now you can donate to Monroe Historical Society simply by shopping! Fred Meyer stores donate a portion of the money you spend to a non-profit of your choice.
          <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/FredMeyerRewards.jpg">
           <img alt="FredMeyerRewards" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-747" height="85" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/FredMeyerRewards-150x85.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/FredMeyerRewards-150x85.jpg 150w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/FredMeyerRewards-300x170.jpg 300w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/FredMeyerRewards.jpg 960w" width="150"/>

          Simply go to
          <a href="http://www.fredmeyer.com/communityrewards">
           www.fredmeyer.com/communityrewards

          and link your rewards card to Monroe Historical Society – you can link by name or by our non-profit number [85404]. You still earn your rewards points, fuel points, and rebates. You can also sign up at the customer service desk.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Amazon will also donate a portion of sales to  the society, using AmazonSmile:
         <a href="http://www.smile.amazon.com">
          www.smile.amazon.com
         </a>
         and choosing Monroe Historical Society.  Every little bit helps!
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #008080;">
          *******************************************************************
          <span style="color: #008080;">
           ***************
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          <strong>
           <span style="color: #000000;">
            Currently at the museum:
           </span>
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/piano2.jpg">
          <img alt="piano2" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-754" height="225" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/piano2-300x225.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/piano2-300x225.jpg 300w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/piano2-150x113.jpg 150w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/piano2.jpg 778w" width="300"/>
         </a>
         The upright Hamilton piano that once provided music at the silent movie theaters of Monroe was installed at the museum in October. Nellie Johnson Blakeslee (photo on left side of piano) played the piano at various venues in town
         <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/theater2.jpg">
          <img alt="theater2" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-755" height="225" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/theater2-300x225.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/theater2-300x225.jpg 300w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/theater2-150x113.jpg 150w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/theater2-1024x768.jpg 1024w" width="300"/>
         </a>
         for many years. Recently tuned, it sounds beautiful!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Adjacent to this new addition to the museum Chris Bee, museum director, has been working on a display for the Avalon Theater. Originally the Monroe Theater, it opened in 1929 and showed the first “talkie” in town in 1930.
         <a href="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/schooldisplay2.jpg">
          <img alt="schooldisplay2" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-756" height="225" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" src="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/schooldisplay2-300x225.jpg" srcset="http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/schooldisplay2-300x225.jpg 300w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/schooldisplay2-150x113.jpg 150w, http://www.monroehistoricalsociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/schooldisplay2.jpg 778w" width="300"/>
         </a>
         The projector next to the piano was from the Avalon. The theater ended its run in 1966 and the site is now a parking lot.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Continuing along that wall visitors come to the recently completed pioneer school display. The heavy black slate on the wall is one from the old Wagner School which closed in 1956.
        </p>
